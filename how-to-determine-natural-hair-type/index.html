How To Determine Your Natural Hair Type A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Aug 15, 2023 ¬∑ 5 min read

Determining your natural hair type is essential in selecting the right hair care products and styling techniques. Your hair type is determined by the shape and structure of your hair strands. There are four main hair types: Type 1, Type 2, Type 3, and Type 4. In this article, we will discuss how to determine your natural hair type and provide tips and hair care advice for each type.

Table of Contents

  • Type 1 Hair
  • Type 2 Hair
  • Type 3 Hair
  • Type 4 Hair
  • FAQs

Type 1 Hair

Type 1 hair is straight and fine, and it lacks volume and body. It is the easiest hair type to manage and style. If your hair falls flat, lacks volume, and does not hold curls, you likely have Type 1 hair. Type 1 hair is further classified into three subtypes:

1A Straight

If your hair is very fine and has no curl pattern, you likely have 1A straight hair. This hair type is usually shiny and soft, but it can be difficult to style.

1B Straight

If your hair is slightly thicker and has a subtle wave, you likely have 1B straight hair. This hair type is more versatile than 1A hair and is easier to style.

1C Straight

If your hair is thick and has a natural wave, you likely have 1C straight hair. This hair type can hold curls and is the easiest to style among Type 1 hair.

Type 2 Hair

Type 2 hair is wavy and has a defined curl pattern. It is thicker than Type 1 hair and has more volume. If your hair has a defined S-shaped curl pattern, you likely have Type 2 hair. Type 2 hair is further classified into three subtypes:

2A Wavy

If your hair has loose, beachy waves and is easy to manage, you likely have 2A wavy hair. This hair type is the easiest to style among Type 2 hair.

2B Wavy

If your hair has more defined waves and is thicker than 2A hair, you likely have 2B wavy hair. This hair type is more difficult to style and requires more maintenance.

2C Wavy

If your hair has defined S-shaped waves and is frizzy, you likely have 2C wavy hair. This hair type requires more maintenance and styling than 2A and 2B hair.

Type 3 Hair

Type 3 hair is curly and has a well-defined curl pattern. It is thicker than Type 2 hair and has more volume. If your hair has tight, spiral curls, you likely have Type 3 hair. Type 3 hair is further classified into three subtypes:

3A Curly

If your hair has loose, defined curls and is easy to manage, you likely have 3A curly hair. This hair type is the easiest to style among Type 3 hair.

3B Curly

If your hair has tight, springy curls and is prone to frizz, you likely have 3B curly hair. This hair type requires more maintenance and styling than 3A hair.

3C Curly

If your hair has tight, corkscrew curls and is very thick, you likely have 3C curly hair. This hair type is the most difficult to manage and requires the most maintenance and styling among Type 3 hair.

Type 4 Hair

Type 4 hair is kinky and has a tightly coiled curl pattern. It is the most fragile and delicate hair type and requires the most care and attention. If your hair is tightly coiled and has a Z-shaped curl pattern, you likely have Type 4 hair. Type 4 hair is further classified into two subtypes:

4A Kinky

If your hair has tightly coiled curls and is soft and fragile, you likely have 4A kinky hair. This hair type requires gentle handling and regular moisturization.

4B Kinky

If your hair has a Z-shaped curl pattern and is very tightly coiled, you likely have 4B kinky hair. This hair type is the most fragile and requires the most care and attention among Type 4 hair.

FAQs

How do I determine my natural hair type?

You can determine your natural hair type by examining the shape and structure of your hair strands. Type 1 hair is straight and lacks volume, Type 2 hair is wavy, Type 3 hair is curly, and Type 4 hair is kinky.

What is the most common hair type?

The most common hair type is Type 1 straight hair.

How do I care for my natural hair?

You can care for your natural hair by using gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, moisturizing regularly, and avoiding heat styling and harsh chemicals.

What products should I use for my hair type?

You should use products that are specifically formulated for your hair type. For Type 1 hair, use lightweight, volumizing products. For Type 2 hair, use products that enhance and define waves. For Type 3 hair, use moisturizing products that define and enhance curls. For Type 4 hair, use products that provide intense moisture and nourishment.

How often should I wash my natural hair?

You should wash your natural hair once a week or every two weeks, depending on your hair type and styling routine. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause dryness and breakage.

Determining your natural hair type is the first step in achieving healthy, beautiful hair. By understanding your hair type and using the right products and styling techniques, you can enhance your natural beauty and achieve the hair of your dreams.
