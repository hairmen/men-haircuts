How To Get Dreads With Short Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you have short curly hair and want to achieve the look of dreadlocks, you may be wondering if it's even possible. The good news is that it is! With the right technique and a little patience, you can have beautiful, natural-looking dreads that are perfect for any occasion. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of getting dreads with short curly hair, along with tips and advice for maintaining them.

How to Get Dreads with Short Curly Hair

The first step to getting dreads with short curly hair is to make sure your hair is long enough. While it's possible to get dreads with hair as short as two inches, it's generally easier to work with hair that's three to four inches long. If your hair is shorter than that, you may need to wait a few months before attempting to get dreads.

Next, you'll need to decide on a method for getting your dreads. There are several different methods to choose from, including backcombing, twisting, and the natural method. Each method has its own pros and cons, so it's important to do your research and choose the one that's right for you.

Once you've chosen your method, it's time to start preparing your hair. This may involve washing and conditioning your hair, as well as combing out any tangles or knots. You may also want to trim your hair to ensure that it's all roughly the same length.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a basic step-by-step guide to getting dreads with short curly hair:

1. Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a residue-free shampoo. This will help to remove any product buildup or oils that may interfere with the dread-making process.

2. Section your hair

Use a comb to section your hair into small, even sections. The size of the sections will depend on your hair type and the method you're using to make your dreads.

3. Backcomb or twist

Use a backcombing or twisting method to create your dreads. This may involve using a comb, your fingers, or a special tool designed for dread-making.

4. Secure your dreads

Once you've created a dread, use a rubber band or a piece of string to secure it in place. Be careful not to make the bands too tight, as this can damage your hair.

5. Repeat

Continue creating and securing your dreads until your entire head is covered. You may need to touch up your dreads periodically as they settle and mature.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Once you've created your dreads, it's important to take good care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips for maintaining your dreads:

1. Wash your hair regularly

Contrary to popular belief, you do need to wash your hair when you have dreads. However, you should use a residue-free shampoo and avoid over-washing, as this can cause your dreads to unravel.

2. Avoid using products

Avoid using hair products like gels, waxes, or pomades, as these can leave residue in your hair and make your dreads look greasy.

3. Be gentle

Be gentle when washing and drying your hair to avoid damaging your dreads. Use a gentle shampoo, and pat your hair dry with a towel instead of rubbing it.

4. Get regular maintenance

Visit a professional stylist regularly to have your dreads touched up and maintained. This will help to keep them looking neat and tidy.

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to get dreads with short curly hair?

A: The time it takes to get dreads with short curly hair will depend on the method you're using, as well as the length and texture of your hair. Generally, it can take anywhere from several hours to several weeks to create dreads.

Q: Can you get dreads with relaxed hair?

A: Yes, it's possible to get dreads with relaxed hair. However, you may need to wait until your hair has grown out some before attempting to make dreads.

Q: Can you undo dreads?

A: Yes, it's possible to undo dreads, but it can be a time-consuming and labor-intensive process. If you're not sure you want to commit to dreads, it's best to try a temporary method first.

Hair Care for Dreads

When you have dreads, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for your hair while you have dreads:

1. Keep your scalp clean

Use a residue-free shampoo to keep your scalp clean and healthy.

2. Moisturize your hair

Dreads can be drying to your hair, so it's important to moisturize regularly. Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair hydrated.

3. Protect your hair

Avoid exposing your hair to excessive heat or sunlight, as this can damage your dreads.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, natural-looking dreads with short curly hair. Whether you're looking for a new style or just want to try something different, dreads can be a fun and unique way to express yourself.
