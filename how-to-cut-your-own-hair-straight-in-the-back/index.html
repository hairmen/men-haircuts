How To Cut Your Own Hair Straight In The Back A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can seem like a daunting task, especially when it comes to the back. But with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to achieve a straight, even cut without having to visit the salon. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting your own hair straight in the back, along with some tips and FAQs to help you get the best results.

Table of Contents

  • Preparing Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror

It's important to use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut and to avoid damaging your hair. Clippers can be useful for trimming the hair at the nape of your neck, but they're not necessary.

Preparing Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, so make sure it's completely dry before you start. Comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair will make it easier to cut evenly. Start by parting your hair down the middle of your head, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, part your hair horizontally from one ear to the other, creating two sections. Pin the top section of hair up and out of the way with a hair tie or clip.

Next, divide the bottom section of hair into two equal parts, one on each side of your head. Pin one side up and out of the way, so you can focus on cutting the other side.

Cutting Your Hair

Take a small section of hair from the bottom of the unclipped side, and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and slide your fingers down to the length you want to cut. Use your scissors to cut across the hair, keeping your fingers straight and level.

Continue taking small sections of hair and cutting them to the same length, working your way up the side of your head. Use a mirror to check your progress and make sure the hair is even. Repeat on the other side of your head.

Blending the Layers

Once you've cut both sides of your hair, it's time to blend the layers together. Start by unclipping the top section of hair, and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to trim any uneven pieces, blending them into the bottom layers.

If you want to add layers or texture to your hair, you can do so at this point. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle, or use thinning shears to remove bulk.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up, rather than cutting too much at once.
  • Use the mirror to check your progress frequently.
  • If you're not sure about a certain length or style, start with a longer cut and work your way down.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it will appear longer when it dries.
  • Be patient and take your time. It's better to take longer and get it right than rush and make mistakes.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair straight in the back without help?

Yes, it's possible to cut your own hair straight in the back using the techniques outlined in this article. Just make sure you have the right tools and take your time.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

No, regular scissors are not sharp enough to cut hair cleanly and can cause damage. Use sharp scissors specifically designed for cutting hair.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends, and consider using hair masks or oils to nourish and moisturize your hair.

Cutting your own hair can be a great way to save money and achieve the look you want without leaving your home. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a straight, even cut in the back and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
