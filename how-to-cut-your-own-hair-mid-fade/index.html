How To Cut Your Own Hair Mid Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a fresh new haircut but don't want to break the bank by going to a professional barber, you might be wondering how to cut your own hair mid fade. It may seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a great-looking mid fade haircut on your own. In this guide, we'll go over everything you need to know to get started.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • The Cut
  • Cleanup
  • Tips for a Great Mid Fade
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After the Cut

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to gather some essential tools to achieve the perfect mid fade haircut:

  • Electric hair clippers
  • Clipper guards in various sizes
  • Comb
  • Scissors
  • Hand mirror
  • Haircutting cape or towel

Once you have all of your tools, you're ready to get started!

Preparation

Before you begin cutting your hair, it's important to prepare yourself and your workspace:

  • Start with clean, dry hair
  • Choose a well-lit area to work in
  • Cover your clothing with a haircutting cape or towel
  • Set up your tools within reach

Once you're prepared, it's time to start cutting.

The Cut

Follow these steps to achieve a great mid fade:

  1. Start by using the longest clipper guard to trim the top of your hair. Run the clippers from front to back, following the natural shape of your head.
  2. Next, switch to a shorter guard and trim the sides and back of your hair. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using an upward motion to blend the hair as you go.
  3. Use the scissors to trim any longer hairs that the clippers missed, especially around the ears and neckline.
  4. To create the fade, switch to an even shorter clipper guard and use it to trim the hair around the sides and back of your head, leaving the hair on top longer. Use a blending technique to create a smooth transition between the shorter and longer hair.
  5. Repeat the process with an even shorter clipper guard, trimming slightly higher up the head each time, until you achieve the desired fade.
  6. Use the comb to check for any uneven spots and make adjustments as needed.

Cleanup

Once you've finished cutting, it's time to clean up:

  • Remove the clipper guards and use the clippers to trim any stray hairs.
  • Clean your clippers and other tools with a brush or cloth.
  • Shower or wash your hair to remove any loose hairs.

Tips for a Great Mid Fade

Follow these tips to achieve the best possible mid fade:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down gradually to achieve a smoother, more natural-looking fade.
  • Use a light touch when cutting to avoid creating choppy or uneven spots.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Cutting your own hair can be tricky, especially if you're new to it.

FAQ

Here are some commonly asked questions about cutting your own hair mid fade:

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

While you can technically use regular scissors to cut your hair, it's not recommended. Haircutting scissors are specifically designed to create clean, precise cuts, while regular scissors can cause split ends and uneven spots.

How long does a mid fade haircut last?

A mid fade haircut can last anywhere from 2-4 weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain the style.

Hair Care After the Cut

Once you've achieved your perfect mid fade haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking great:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a styling product like gel or pomade to keep your hair in place.
  • Visit a professional barber every few months to maintain the style and keep it looking sharp.

Now that you know how to cut your own hair mid fade, you can achieve a great-looking haircut anytime, anywhere. With a little practice and patience, you'll be a pro in no time!
