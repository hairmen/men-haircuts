Should I Cut My Own Hair Short A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Sep 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're considering cutting your own hair short, you're not alone. With the rise of DIY haircuts during the Covid-19 pandemic, many people are wondering whether they should take the plunge and chop off their locks. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of cutting your own hair short and provide you with some tips and tricks to help you achieve a salon-worthy look.

Description

Before we dive into the nitty-gritty of cutting your own hair short, let's first define what we mean by "short." Generally speaking, a short haircut is one that falls above the shoulders. This can include anything from a pixie cut to a bob or lob (long bob). Short haircuts can be low-maintenance, trendy, and flattering for a wide range of face shapes and hair types.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Short

If you've never cut your own hair before, it's important to proceed with caution. Cutting short hair requires a bit more precision than cutting long hair, so it's a good idea to start with small snips and work your way up. Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting your own hair short:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools on hand. This includes sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a handheld mirror.

Step 2: Choose Your Style

Decide on the length and style you want to achieve before you start cutting. Look up pictures online or consult with a hairstylist for inspiration.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. Start at the back and work your way forward, making sure each section is even.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Using your scissors, start cutting small sections of hair at a time. Cut vertically, using the comb as a guide. Remember to cut less than you think you need to â you can always go back and trim more later.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

Use the handheld mirror to check the back of your head and make sure everything looks even. Make any necessary adjustments.

Step 6: Finish with Styling

Once you're happy with your new haircut, style it as desired using a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Short

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with these tips, you can achieve a professional-looking result:

  • Start small and work your way up. It's easier to cut more hair than to try and fix a mistake.
  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. Dull scissors can lead to split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Watch tutorials online for guidance. There are plenty of hairstylists who have posted tutorials on cutting short hair at home.
  • Be patient. Cutting your own hair takes time and practice, so don't expect to get it perfect on the first try.

FAQ

Is it a good idea to cut my own hair short?

If you're comfortable with the idea of cutting your own hair and have the right tools and guidance, cutting your own hair short can be a great way to save money and achieve a trendy, low-maintenance look.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting your own hair, don't panic. Depending on the severity of the mistake, you may be able to fix it with some additional trimming or by styling your hair differently. If all else fails, you can always visit a professional hairstylist for help.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Once you've cut your hair short, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and shine. Here are some tips for caring for short hair:

  • Wash your hair every other day to prevent it from getting too oily.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't weigh down your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Cutting your own hair short can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it's important to take your time and proceed with caution. With the right tools, guidance, and patience, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home.
