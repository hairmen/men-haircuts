How To Thicken Your Baby s Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter Oct 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a new parent, you may be wondering how to thicken your baby's hair. While it may seem like a trivial concern, hair thickness can be an indication of overall hair health. Additionally, thick hair can be easier to style and manage than thin hair. In this article, we'll cover some tips and tricks for how to thicken your baby's hair.

How to Thicken Your Baby's Hair: Step by Step

Step 1: Keep Your Baby's Scalp Clean

One of the most important things you can do to promote hair growth and thickness is to keep your baby's scalp clean. Use a gentle baby shampoo to wash your baby's hair every few days. Avoid using adult shampoos or harsh chemicals that could irritate your baby's scalp.

Step 2: Massage Your Baby's Scalp

Massaging your baby's scalp can help stimulate blood flow to the hair follicles, which can promote hair growth. Use gentle circular motions to massage your baby's scalp for a few minutes each day.

Step 3: Use a Soft Brush

When brushing your baby's hair, use a soft brush with natural bristles. Avoid using plastic or synthetic bristles, which can be harsh on your baby's scalp. Brush your baby's hair gently, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.

Step 4: Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid putting your baby's hair in tight hairstyles, such as braids or ponytails. These styles can pull on the hair and cause breakage, which can lead to thinning hair over time.

Tips for Thicker Baby Hair

Tip 1: Use Natural Oils

Natural oils, such as coconut oil and olive oil, can help promote hair growth and thickness. Massage a small amount of oil into your baby's scalp and hair, then rinse with warm water after a few minutes. Be sure to avoid getting oil in your baby's eyes.

Tip 2: Feed Your Baby a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can help promote hair growth and thickness. Make sure your baby is getting enough vitamins and minerals, such as vitamin D, iron, and zinc. Consider adding foods rich in these nutrients, such as leafy greens, nuts, and whole grains, to your baby's diet.

Tip 3: Don't Overwash Your Baby's Hair

While it's important to keep your baby's scalp clean, overwashing can strip the hair of its natural oils and lead to dryness and breakage. Aim to wash your baby's hair every few days, or as needed.

Tip 4: Be Gentle When Styling Your Baby's Hair

When styling your baby's hair, be gentle to avoid breakage. Use soft hair ties and avoid pulling the hair too tightly. Consider using a detangling spray or leave-in conditioner to make styling easier.

FAQs About Thicker Baby Hair

Q: Can I use adult hair products on my baby's hair?

A: No, it's best to use products specifically designed for babies. Adult hair products can be too harsh for your baby's delicate scalp.

Q: How often should I brush my baby's hair?

A: Aim to brush your baby's hair once a day, using a soft brush with natural bristles.

Q: Will cutting my baby's hair make it thicker?

A: No, cutting your baby's hair won't make it thicker. However, trimming the hair regularly can help prevent split ends, which can lead to breakage and thinning hair over time.

Hair Care for Thick Baby Hair

Tip 1: Use a Gentle Shampoo

When washing your baby's thick hair, use a gentle shampoo that won't strip the hair of its natural oils. Look for a shampoo specifically designed for babies with thick hair.

Tip 2: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When combing your baby's hair, use a wide-tooth comb to avoid tangles and breakage. Start at the ends of the hair and work your way up to the roots.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as blow dryers and flat irons, on your baby's hair. Heat can damage the hair and lead to breakage and thinning over time.

Tip 4: Be Patient

Remember that hair growth takes time, so be patient as you work to thicken your baby's hair. With consistent care and attention, you can help promote healthy, thick hair for your little one.
