How To Sleep Better With Long Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter Sep 01, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have long hair, you know how difficult it can be to get comfortable at night. Tossing and turning can cause tangles, knots, and breakage. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks to help you sleep better with long hair. From the right pillowcases to the right bedtime routine, we've got everything you need to know.

Invest in a Silk Pillowcase

A silk pillowcase is a great investment for anyone with long hair. Unlike cotton, silk doesn't create as much friction against your hair, which can cause tangles and breakage. Plus, silk is less absorbent than cotton, so it won't suck the moisture out of your hair while you sleep.

Tie Your Hair Up

Tying your hair up before bed is a great way to prevent tangles and knots. Just make sure you use a hair tie that won't damage your hair, like a scrunchie or a soft elastic band. Avoid using tight hair ties or metal clips, which can cause breakage and damage.

Brush Your Hair Before Bed

Brushing your hair before bed can help to detangle any knots and prevent breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles to gently work out any tangles. Be gentle when brushing, and try not to pull or tug on your hair too much.

Consider a Sleep Cap

If you're really struggling with tangles and knots, you might want to consider wearing a sleep cap. A sleep cap can help to keep your hair in place and prevent it from getting tangled while you sleep. Look for a cap made from a soft, breathable material like silk or satin.

Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Using a leave-in conditioner before bed can help to moisturize and protect your hair while you sleep. Apply a small amount of conditioner to the ends of your hair, and then gently comb it through. Avoid using too much conditioner, as this can make your hair greasy or heavy.

Try a Silk Hair Wrap

A silk hair wrap is a great way to protect your hair while you sleep. Simply wrap your hair up in the silk scarf, and tie it at the back of your head. This will help to keep your hair in place and prevent tangles and knots.

Avoid Sleeping on Wet Hair

Wet hair is more prone to breakage and damage, so it's best to avoid sleeping on wet hair if possible. If you must go to bed with wet hair, try wrapping it up in a microfiber towel or a t-shirt to help absorb some of the moisture.

Use a Satin Scrunchie

Using a satin scrunchie can help to prevent breakage and damage to your hair. Satin is a smooth material that won't create as much friction against your hair as cotton or other materials. Plus, a scrunchie is a gentle way to tie your hair up without causing damage.

Get a Haircut

If you're really struggling with tangles and knots, it might be time for a haircut. Getting regular trims can help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Plus, a shorter style might be easier to manage at night.

Establish a Bedtime Routine

Establishing a bedtime routine can help you get better sleep and protect your hair. Make sure you're getting enough rest each night, and try to stick to a consistent sleep schedule. Take a few minutes each night to brush your hair and tie it up, and consider using some of the other tips we've shared to protect your hair while you sleep.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: How can I prevent my hair from getting tangled at night?

A: There are several things you can do to prevent tangles and knots. Try tying your hair up, using a silk pillowcase, and using a leave-in conditioner before bed. You could also consider wearing a sleep cap or a silk hair wrap.

Q: Should I sleep with my hair up or down?

A: It's generally best to tie your hair up before bed to prevent tangles and knots. Just make sure you use a gentle hair tie and don't tie your hair too tightly.

Q: Can sleeping on a silk pillowcase really help my hair?

A: Yes! Silk pillowcases are much gentler on your hair than cotton or other materials. They create less friction against your hair, which can help to prevent tangles and breakage.

Hair Care Tips

Remember, taking care of your hair is important for both its health and appearance. Try to avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools, and make sure you're getting enough nutrients and hydration to support healthy hair growth. If you're struggling with hair loss or other hair issues, don't hesitate to consult a professional stylist or dermatologist for advice.
