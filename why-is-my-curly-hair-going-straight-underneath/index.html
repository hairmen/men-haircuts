Why Is My Curly Hair Going Straight Underneath

Written by Bardi Aug 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it's beautiful and unique, it can also be difficult to manage. One of the most frustrating things that can happen to curly hair is when it starts going straight underneath. This can happen for a variety of reasons, but don't worry, there are steps you can take to get your curls back.

Description

When curly hair starts going straight underneath, it means that the curls are losing their shape and definition. The hair may look frizzy and limp, and it can be difficult to style. This can be caused by a number of factors, including genetics, damage, and products.

How to Determine the Cause

The first step in getting your curls back is to determine the cause of the problem. Here are some common causes:

Genetics

If your hair has always been curly but is starting to go straight underneath, it may be due to genetics. Some people have hair that is naturally more prone to losing its curl pattern, especially as they age.

Damage

If you've been using heat styling tools or chemical treatments on your hair, it can cause damage that leads to straightening. This can also happen if you've been rough with your hair while brushing or combing.

Products

The products you use on your hair could also be causing your curls to go straight. Some products contain ingredients that can weigh down your hair or strip it of its natural oils, causing it to lose its curl pattern.

How to Fix It

Once you've determined the cause of your straightening curls, you can take steps to fix the problem. Here are some tips:

Reduce Heat Styling

If heat styling is the cause of your problem, try to reduce how often you use hot tools. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Avoid Chemical Treatments

If you've been getting chemical treatments like relaxers or perms, stop for a while and give your hair a break. These treatments can cause damage that leads to straightening.

Use the Right Products

Choose products that are formulated for curly hair and avoid those that contain harsh chemicals. Look for products that contain natural oils and ingredients that will help define your curls.

Detangle Carefully

When you're combing or brushing your hair, be gentle and use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush. Don't pull on your hair or use harsh movements that could damage it.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for your curly hair:

  • Don't wash your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent breakage.

FAQ

Q: Can I still straighten my hair if it's curly?

A: Yes, you can still straighten your curly hair, but it's important to use the right products and tools to prevent damage. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It's best to wash your hair every 2-3 days, as washing too often can strip it of its natural oils.

Q: How do I know if my hair is damaged?

A: If your hair is dry, brittle, and prone to breakage, it may be damaged. You may also notice split ends and a lack of shine.

Conclusion

If your curly hair is going straight underneath, don't panic. By identifying the cause and taking steps to fix the problem, you can get your curls back in no time. Remember to be gentle with your hair, use the right products, and avoid heat styling and chemical treatments as much as possible.
