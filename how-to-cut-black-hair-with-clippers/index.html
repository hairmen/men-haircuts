How To Cut Black Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Oct 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to cut black hair with clippers, it can seem like a daunting task. However, with the right tools and techniques, it can be a simple and satisfying process. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to cut black hair with clippers, from the tools you'll need to the steps to follow to achieve a great cut.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • Cutting Black Hair with Clippers: Step-by-Step
  • Tips for Cutting Black Hair with Clippers
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Cutting Black Hair with Clippers

Tools You'll Need

Before you begin, you'll need to gather a few tools to get started. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers (with guards)
  • Comb
  • Scissors (for detailing)
  • Hair clips or ties (to section off hair)
  • Cape or towel (to protect clothing)
  • Barber brush or soft-bristled brush
  • Clipper oil and cleaning brush (for maintenance)

Make sure your clippers have guards that are appropriate for the length of hair you'll be cutting. For shorter cuts, use a smaller guard, while longer cuts will require a larger guard.

Preparation

Before you start cutting, it's important to prepare the hair. Follow these steps:

  1. Wash and dry the hair thoroughly.
  2. Section off the hair using clips or ties.
  3. Comb through each section to remove any knots or tangles.
  4. Start with the longest guard and work your way down to shorter guards, as needed.

By preparing the hair, you'll ensure that it's easy to work with and that you achieve the desired length.

Cutting Black Hair with Clippers: Step-by-Step

Now that you have your tools and have prepared the hair, you're ready to start cutting. Follow these steps:

  1. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up to the crown of the head, using the longest guard.
  2. Move from the crown to the front of the head, continuing to use the longest guard.
  3. Switch to a shorter guard to trim around the ears and the hairline.
  4. Blend the hair by using a guard that's one size shorter than the previous one, and run it along the line where the two guards meet.
  5. Use scissors to detail any areas that need extra attention, such as the sideburns or the back of the neck.
  6. Repeat the process with each section of hair until you've achieved the desired length.

Remember to use a comb to keep the hair taut as you cut, and to move the clippers in a smooth, fluid motion to avoid uneven cuts.

Tips for Cutting Black Hair with Clippers

Here are a few extra tips to keep in mind as you cut:

  • Start with a larger guard, and work your way down to a shorter guard to avoid cutting too much off at once.
  • Use a mirror to check your work as you go.
  • If the hair is particularly thick or curly, consider using a smaller guard to avoid leaving lines or uneven spots.
  • Be sure to clean and oil your clippers regularly to keep them in good working order.

FAQ

Here are a few common questions people have about cutting black hair with clippers:

Can I cut black hair with clippers if it's natural?

Yes, you can cut natural black hair with clippers. Simply follow the same steps outlined above.

What if I accidentally cut the hair too short?

If you accidentally cut the hair too short, you can either leave it as is or try blending it in with longer hair using scissors or a smaller guard.

How often should I cut black hair with clippers?

How often you cut hair with clippers will depend on how quickly the hair grows and how short you want it to be. As a general rule, you'll want to cut hair every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care After Cutting Black Hair with Clippers

After you've finished cutting the hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash and condition the hair regularly to keep it healthy.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep the hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heating tools or chemicals that can damage the hair.
  • Trim the hair regularly to maintain the desired length.

By following these tips, you'll be able to keep the hair looking great between cuts.

Conclusion

Cutting black hair with clippers doesn't have to be intimidating. By following these steps and tips, you'll be able to achieve a great cut and keep the hair looking healthy and beautiful. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and have fun with it!
