How To Style Shaved Hair Growing Out Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Sep 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're one of the many people who decided to take the plunge and shave your hair, you may be experiencing the awkward stage of growing it back out. While it may seem like there's nothing you can do to make your hair look good during this phase, there are actually a few tricks and tips that can help you style your hair and make the most of the growing-out process. Read on to learn more.

How to Style Shaved Hair Growing Out

The first step in styling your shaved hair as it grows out is to keep it healthy. This means that you should be washing your hair regularly and using a good conditioner to keep it soft and hydrated. You should also be using a wide-toothed comb or brush to detangle your hair, as this will help prevent breakage.

Once your hair is clean and detangled, you can begin to experiment with different styles. One popular option is to slick your hair back with gel or pomade. This works especially well if you have short hair that is just starting to grow out. Another option is to use a headband or hair tie to pull your hair back into a ponytail or bun. This can help keep your hair out of your face and give it a more polished look.

Step by Step: Styling Shaved Hair That's Growing Out

Here's a step-by-step guide to styling your shaved hair as it grows out:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will help remove any dirt and oil that may be weighing your hair down.

Step 2: Apply a styling product

Apply a small amount of gel, pomade, or other styling product to your hair. Use your fingers to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Comb or brush your hair

Use a wide-toothed comb or brush to detangle your hair and style it as desired. You may want to slick your hair back or create a more textured look.

Step 4: Add accessories

If you want to add a little something extra to your hairstyle, consider using a headband, hair tie, or other accessory. This can help keep your hair in place and give it a more polished look.

Tips for Styling Shaved Hair That's Growing Out

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you style your shaved hair that's growing out:

  • Experiment with different products to find the one that works best for your hair type and desired style.
  • Be patient as your hair grows out. It may take several weeks or months for your hair to reach the length you want.
  • Consider getting regular trims to help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Try different accessories, such as scarves or hats, to add some variety to your hairstyle.

FAQ: Styling Shaved Hair That's Growing Out

Here are a few frequently asked questions about styling shaved hair that's growing out:

Q: Can I use heat tools on my shaved hair as it grows out?

A: It's generally best to avoid using heat tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, on hair that's growing out. These tools can damage your hair and make it harder to style in the long run.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: The frequency with which you should wash your hair depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people may need to wash their hair daily, while others can go several days between washes. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Q: How can I make my hair grow faster?

A: While there's no guaranteed way to make your hair grow faster, there are a few things you can do to promote healthy hair growth. This includes eating a balanced diet, getting enough sleep, and avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling tools.

Hair Care Tips for Shaved Hair That's Growing Out

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair as it grows out. This means using a gentle shampoo and conditioner, avoiding harsh chemical treatments, and getting regular trims to prevent split ends. You may also want to consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair soft and hydrated.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make the most of your shaved hair as it grows out and create a variety of stylish looks along the way. Remember to be patient and experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you.
