How To Cut Hair With Thinning Shears A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 27, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're looking for a way to add texture and volume to your hair, you may want to consider cutting your hair with thinning shears. Thinning shears are a type of scissors that have teeth on one or both blades, which helps to remove bulk from the hair without creating a blunt edge. In this article, we'll show you how to cut hair with thinning shears in a few easy steps.

Description

Thinning shears are a valuable tool for hairdressers and home hair enthusiasts alike. They provide a quick and easy way to remove bulk from the hair without creating a harsh line. Thinning shears come in a variety of sizes and styles, so it's important to choose a pair that's suitable for your hair type and the desired outcome.

How to Cut Hair with Thinning Shears

Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting hair with thinning shears:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to wash and dry it. This will help to remove any dirt or product buildup and make it easier to work with.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will help you to work on one section at a time and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Hold the thinning shears at a slight angle and start cutting the hair in small sections. Work from the bottom of the hairline upwards, taking care to cut only a small amount of hair at a time.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

After you've cut a section of hair, use a comb to blend the cut hair with the uncut hair. This will help to create a natural-looking finish and prevent any harsh lines.

Step 5: Repeat

Continue cutting and blending your hair until you've achieved your desired look. Remember to take breaks and step back to assess your progress.

Tips for Cutting Hair with Thinning Shears

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your hair with thinning shears:

  • Choose the right type of thinning shears for your hair type
  • Work in small sections to avoid taking off too much hair at once
  • Take breaks and step back to assess your progress
  • Remember to blend the cut hair with the uncut hair for a natural finish

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use thinning shears on any type of hair?

Yes, thinning shears can be used on any type of hair. However, it's important to choose a pair of thinning shears that's appropriate for your hair type.

How often should I use thinning shears on my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. For most people, using thinning shears every 6-8 weeks is sufficient.

Can I use thinning shears to create layers in my hair?

Thinning shears can be used to create subtle layers in the hair, but they're not as effective as regular scissors for creating dramatic layers.

Hair Care after Cutting with Thinning Shears

After cutting your hair with thinning shears, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends

Cutting your hair with thinning shears is a great way to add texture and volume to your hair without creating a harsh line. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a natural-looking finish that's easy to maintain.
