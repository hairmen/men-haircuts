Why Is My Hair Curly Underneath But Wavy On Top

Written by Oliver Jul 13, 2023 · 4 min read

If you are someone who has curly hair, you may have noticed that your hair is curly underneath but wavy on top. This may have left you wondering why this is the case. In this blog post, we will explore the reasons behind this phenomenon and provide tips on how to care for your curly hair.

Description

Curly hair can be a challenge to manage, especially when it has different curl patterns in different parts of the head. One common issue that many curly-haired people face is having curly hair underneath but wavy hair on top. This can make styling and maintaining the hair difficult, as the two sections of hair require different care and attention.

How to

If you have curly hair that is curly underneath but wavy on top, there are a few things you can do to manage it:

1. Identify Your Curl Pattern

Before you can effectively care for your curly hair, you need to identify your curl pattern. There are different types of curls, ranging from loose waves to tight coils. Once you know your curl pattern, you can choose the right products and techniques to care for your hair.

2. Use the Right Products

Curly hair needs moisture to stay healthy and defined. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl-enhancing shampoos, conditioners, and styling products. Avoid products that contain harsh chemicals, sulfates, and alcohol, as these can dry out your hair and make it frizzy.

3. Style Your Hair Strategically

If you want to enhance the curls underneath your hair, consider styling it in a way that allows the curls to shine. For example, you could wear your hair in a half-up, half-down style that shows off the curls at the back of your head. Alternatively, you could use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your curls while keeping the top of your hair wavy.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to care for curly hair that is curly underneath but wavy on top:

Step 1: Identify Your Curl Pattern

Take a good look at your hair and try to identify your curl pattern. Is it loose waves, tight coils, or something in between? Knowing your curl type will help you choose the right products and techniques to care for your hair.

Step 2: Use the Right Products

Invest in products that are specifically designed for curly hair. Look for shampoos, conditioners, and styling products that are free from sulfates, alcohol, and other harsh chemicals. These products will help keep your hair moisturized and defined.

Step 3: Style Your Hair Strategically

Experiment with different hairstyles to find one that shows off your curls underneath while keeping the top of your hair wavy. You could try a half-up, half-down style, a messy bun, or a braid. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your curls without disturbing the top of your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for curly hair:

  • Don't wash your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair, as this can disrupt the curl pattern.
  • Invest in a satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

FAQ

1. Is it normal to have curly hair underneath but wavy on top?

Yes, it is normal to have different curl patterns in different parts of your hair. This is often due to genetics and can be managed with the right products and styling techniques.

2. Can I straighten my hair if it is curly underneath but wavy on top?

You can straighten your hair if you wish, but be aware that this can damage your hair and cause breakage. If you do choose to straighten your hair, use a heat protectant spray and a low heat setting on your straightener.

Hair Care

Caring for curly hair that is curly underneath but wavy on top can be a challenge. By identifying your curl pattern, using the right products, and styling your hair strategically, you can keep your curls defined and healthy. Remember to be patient and experiment with different techniques until you find a routine that works for you.
