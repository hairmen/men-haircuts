Will Layering Hair Make It Curly

Written by Petter Nov 07, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of your straight hair and wishing for some luscious curls? Well, you might have heard that layering your hair can help you achieve those curls. But is that really true? In this article, we will discuss whether layering hair will make it curly or not.

Description

Layering is a common hair cutting technique that involves cutting the hair at different lengths to create volume and texture. It is usually done on straight or wavy hair to add movement and dimension. However, many people believe that layering can also make their hair curly.

How to Layer Hair

Layering hair requires some skill and knowledge. It is best to get it done by a professional stylist. However, if you want to do it yourself, here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Towel-dry your hair and let it air-dry until it is about 80% dry.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips. Start by parting your hair in the middle and then creating two sections on either side of your head.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Take a section of your hair and comb it straight. Hold it between your fingers and cut the hair at an angle. Repeat this process for all the sections of your hair.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you have cut all the sections, blend the layers by combing them with a fine-tooth comb. This will ensure that your hair looks natural and not choppy.

Tips for Layering Hair

Layering hair can be tricky, but with these tips, you can achieve the perfect look:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends
  • Start with small sections of hair
  • Don't cut too much hair at once
  • Keep the layers long and subtle for a natural look

FAQ

1. Will Layering Hair Make it Curly?

No, layering hair will not make it curly. Layering can add volume and texture to straight or wavy hair, but it cannot change the natural hair texture.

2. Can Layering Damage Hair?

If done incorrectly, layering can damage hair. It is important to use sharp scissors and not cut too much hair at once. It is also best to get it done by a professional stylist.

3. How Often Should I Layer My Hair?

It depends on your hair type and length. If you have long hair, you can get it layered every six months. If you have shorter hair, you can get it layered every three months.

Hair Care After Layering

After layering your hair, it is important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat tools too often
  • Get regular trims to avoid split ends
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine

Conclusion

Layering hair can add volume and texture, but it cannot make it curly. If you want to achieve curly hair, you can use curling tools or get a perm. However, if you want a natural look, layering can be a great option. Just make sure to get it done by a professional stylist and take good care of your hair afterwards.
