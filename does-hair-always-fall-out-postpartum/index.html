Does Hair Always Fall Out Postpartum

Written by Daniel Sep 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Postpartum hair loss is a common phenomenon where new moms experience hair shedding after giving birth. This hair loss is not permanent and usually resolves on its own. However, many women still wonder if hair always falls out postpartum. In this blog post, we will discuss this topic in detail and provide some tips on how to deal with postpartum hair loss.

Description

Postpartum hair loss, also known as telogen effluvium, is a condition where a woman experiences an excessive amount of hair shedding several months after giving birth. This condition is caused by hormonal changes during pregnancy and childbirth. During pregnancy, the body produces high levels of estrogen, which prevents hair shedding. However, after giving birth, estrogen levels drop significantly, causing hair to enter the resting phase of the hair growth cycle. This phase is known as the telogen phase, and it lasts for about three months. After this phase, the hair falls out, and new hair begins to grow.

How to Deal with Postpartum Hair Loss

Postpartum hair loss is a natural process, and there is no need to panic. However, there are some ways to deal with postpartum hair loss, such as:

1. Be Gentle with Your Hair

During postpartum hair loss, your hair may become weaker and more fragile. Therefore, it is essential to be gentle with it. Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools that can damage your hair further.

2. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can help promote hair growth and prevent hair loss. Make sure to include foods rich in vitamins and minerals, such as iron, zinc, and biotin, in your diet.

3. Take Supplements

If you are not getting enough nutrients from your diet, you can take supplements to support hair growth. Biotin, vitamin D, and iron supplements can help promote healthy hair growth.

4. Manage Your Stress

Stress can also contribute to hair loss. Therefore, it is essential to manage your stress levels. You can try relaxation techniques such as meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises to reduce stress.

FAQ

What is the normal amount of hair loss postpartum?

On average, a woman can lose up to 100 hairs per day. However, during postpartum hair loss, it is normal to lose up to 300 hairs per day.

How long does postpartum hair loss last?

Postpartum hair loss usually lasts for about six months. However, some women may experience hair shedding for up to a year.

Can I prevent postpartum hair loss?

Postpartum hair loss is a natural process, and it is difficult to prevent it. However, you can take steps to minimize hair shedding, such as eating a healthy diet, managing your stress levels, and being gentle with your hair.

Hair Care Tips

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that is free of harsh chemicals and sulfates. These ingredients can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause further damage.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids, buns, and ponytails. These styles can pull on your hair, causing damage and breakage.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will help prevent breakage and minimize hair shedding.

4. Limit Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons. These tools can damage your hair and cause it to break.

Conclusion

Postpartum hair loss is a common phenomenon that affects many new moms. While it can be alarming, it is a natural process that usually resolves on its own. By following the tips and hair care advice outlined in this blog post, you can minimize hair shedding and promote healthy hair growth.
