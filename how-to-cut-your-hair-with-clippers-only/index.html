How To Cut Your Hair With Clippers Only

Written by Petter Aug 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our step-by-step guide on how to cut your hair with clippers only. With the current pandemic, many people have started cutting their hair at home to save money and avoid going to the salon. Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a breeze. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your hair with clippers only, and answer some of the most frequently asked questions about hair cutting.

Description

Cutting your hair with clippers only is a simple and affordable way to maintain your hairstyle at home. Clippers are electric hair cutting tools that come with different guard sizes, which determine how short or long the hair will be cut. Clippers can be used to cut hair on the sides and back of the head, as well as to trim the top of the hair. By following our step-by-step guide, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home.

How to Cut Your Hair with Clippers Only

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You will need:

  • Clippers with various guard sizes
  • A comb
  • Scissors
  • A mirror
  • A towel

Once you have all of your tools ready, follow these steps:

  1. Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it.
  2. Choose the guard size you want to use. For a shorter haircut, use a shorter guard size, and for a longer haircut, use a longer guard size.
  3. Attach the guard to the clippers and turn them on.
  4. Starting at the nape of your neck, run the clippers up towards the crown of your head. Repeat this until all of the hair in the back of your head is cut to the desired length.
  5. Move to the sides of your head and cut the hair to the same length as the back.
  6. Use the comb to lift the hair on the top of your head and cut it to the desired length with the scissors.
  7. Blend the sides and the top of your hair by using a longer guard size and running the clippers over the area where the sides and top meet.
  8. Trim the hair around your ears and the back of your neck with scissors or a smaller guard size.
  9. Use the mirror to check for any uneven spots and make any necessary adjustments.
  10. Finally, wash your hair again to remove any loose hairs.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair:

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down until you achieve the desired length.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process.
  • Use the comb to lift the hair and make sure you cut it evenly.
  • Trim your hair when it's dry so you can see the length better.
  • Watch tutorial videos to get a better understanding of the process.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting hair with clippers only:

Can I cut my own hair with clippers?

Yes, you can cut your own hair with clippers. However, it may take some practice to get the hang of it.

What guard size should I use to cut my hair?

The guard size you use depends on the length of hair you want to cut. For a shorter haircut, use a shorter guard size, and for a longer haircut, use a longer guard size.

Can I use clippers to cut my bangs?

Clippers are not recommended for cutting bangs. Use scissors instead.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you want to keep it. On average, people cut their hair every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is important to maintain a healthy scalp and hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often.
  • Eat a balanced diet to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Cutting your own hair with clippers only can save you time and money. With our step-by-step guide and tips, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. Just remember to take your time, choose the right guard size, and follow the proper hair care tips to keep your hair healthy and looking great.
