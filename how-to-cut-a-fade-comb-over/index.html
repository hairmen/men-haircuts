How To Cut A Fade Comb Over A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Nov 10, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and versatile hairstyle, you can't go wrong with a fade comb over. This classic cut features short sides that gradually blend into longer hair on top, creating a sleek and modern look that can be dressed up or down. However, achieving the perfect fade comb over requires some skill and technique. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting a fade comb over, from choosing the right tools to finishing touches.

Description

A fade comb over is a men's hairstyle that features short hair on the sides and back of the head that gradually blends into longer hair on top. The fade can be low (near the ears) or high (near the temples), and the hair on top can be styled in various ways, such as slicked back, side-parted, or textured. This hairstyle is popular among men of all ages and can be adapted to different face shapes and hair textures.

How to Cut a Fade Comb Over: Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you'll need to have the right tools on hand. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers with guards of different lengths (1, 2, 3, 4, etc.)
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair spray or pomade
  • Mirror

Step 2: Choose the Right Length

Decide how short you want the sides and back of your hair to be. This will determine the initial guard size you use. If you want a low fade, start with a #1 or #2 guard. If you want a high fade, start with a #3 or #4 guard. Remember, you can always go shorter, but you can't go longer.

Step 3: Create the Fade

Start by using the clippers with the initial guard size to trim the hair on the sides and back of the head. Make sure to use smooth, even strokes and follow the natural curve of the head. As you move up the head, switch to a shorter guard size (e.g., #2 to #1, #4 to #3) to create the fade. Blend the hair between the different guard sizes using a clipper-over-comb or scissor-over-comb technique. Use the mirror to check your work from all angles.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Next, cut the hair on top of the head to the desired length. The length can vary depending on your preference, but a good rule of thumb is to leave about 2-3 inches of hair. Use scissors to cut the hair, taking small sections and cutting vertically to create texture. You can also use a razor or thinning shears to remove bulk and add texture.

Step 5: Style the Hair

Finally, style the hair on top of the head using hair spray or pomade. You can slick it back, part it to the side, or create a messy, textured look. Use the comb to shape the hair and the mirror to check the final result.

Tips for Cutting a Fade Comb Over

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down gradually to create a smoother transition.
  • Use the mirror to check your work from all angles and make sure the fade is even.
  • Take your time and don't rush. It's better to cut slowly and methodically than to make a mistake.
  • Practice makes perfect. If you're new to cutting hair, start with a simple fade before trying more advanced techniques.
  • Ask for feedback from friends or family members to see if they like the final result.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut a fade comb over on myself?

A: While it's possible to cut your own hair, it's recommended to have someone else do it for you, especially if you're new to cutting hair. It's easier to see the back of your head and make sure the fade is even when someone else is cutting your hair.

Q: How often should I get a haircut to maintain a fade comb over?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how neat you want to keep the hairstyle. On average, you should get a haircut every 3-6 weeks to maintain a fade comb over.

Hair Care for a Fade Comb Over

To keep your fade comb over looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a hair styling product, such as pomade or gel, to hold the hair in place and add shine.
  • Trim the hair on the sides and back of the head every 2-3 weeks to maintain the fade.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers or straighteners, as it can damage the hair and cause frizz.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or using a hair sunscreen.

Now that you know how to cut a fade comb over, you can experiment with different styles and techniques to create a look that suits your personality and lifestyle. With a little practice and patience, you can master this timeless hairstyle and rock it with confidence.
