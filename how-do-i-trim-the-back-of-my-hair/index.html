How Do I Trim The Back Of My Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Sep 23, 2023 · 4 min read

Trimming your hair at home can be a daunting task, especially when it comes to the back of your head. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy trim without ever leaving your home. In this post, we’ll walk you through the steps of how to trim the back of your hair in relaxed English language.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You’ll Need
  • Preparation
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Trimming Your Hair
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for a Successful Trim
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Your Trim

Tools You’ll Need

Before you begin, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Scissors designed for hair cutting
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Mirror (preferably a full-length mirror)
  • Good lighting

It’s also a good idea to have a towel or cape to protect your clothing from hair clippings.

Preparation

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will not only make it easier to work with, but it will also ensure a more accurate trim. Next, comb through your hair to remove any tangles or knots. If your hair is particularly long or thick, consider using a detangling spray or conditioner to make the process easier.

Sectioning Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections to make the trimming process easier. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then create a horizontal part from ear to ear. Clip or tie the top section of your hair out of the way, leaving the bottom section loose.

Next, create a vertical part down the center of the back of your head, dividing the loose section in half. Clip or tie one half out of the way, leaving the other half loose. Repeat this process on the other side of your head.

Trimming Your Hair

Take a small section of hair from the bottom half of your hair and comb it straight down. Hold the ends of the hair between your fingers and snip off the desired amount, making sure to trim straight across. Repeat this process on all the sections of the bottom half of your hair.

Once you’ve finished trimming the bottom half, release the top half and repeat the same process.

When you reach the back of your head, it can be helpful to use a second mirror to ensure you’re trimming evenly. Alternatively, you can ask a friend or family member to help.

Finishing Touches

After you’ve finished trimming, comb through your hair to make sure it’s even. If you notice any uneven sections, snip off small amounts of hair until it’s even. Be careful not to overdo it, as it’s better to have slightly uneven hair than to accidentally cut too much off.

Tips for a Successful Trim

  • Start with small snips and work your way up. It’s better to cut too little than too much.
  • Don’t rush the process. Take your time and make sure you’re happy with each section before moving on.
  • If you’re unsure about how much to cut off, always err on the side of caution and cut less than you think you need.
  • Invest in high-quality hair cutting scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create uneven cuts.

FAQs

Q: Can I trim my hair when it’s wet?

A: It’s generally best to trim your hair when it’s dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may lead to uneven cuts.

Q: How often should I trim the back of my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, it’s a good idea to trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Your Trim

After you’ve finished trimming, it’s important to take good care of your hair. This includes regular washing and conditioning, as well as using a heat protectant if you use heat styling tools. Additionally, consider using a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and nourished.

Trimming the back of your hair at home can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it’s definitely doable. By following these simple steps and taking good care of your hair, you can achieve a beautiful, salon-worthy trim without ever leaving your home.
