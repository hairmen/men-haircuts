How To Cut Front Hair Flicks At Home Step By Step

Are you tired of paying a fortune at the hair salon just to get your front hair flicks trimmed? With a little bit of practice and patience, you can easily learn how to cut your own front hair flicks at home. Not only will this save you money, but it will also give you more control over your hairstyle. In this article, we will guide you through the step-by-step process of cutting front hair flicks at home.

How to Cut Front Hair Flicks at Home

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. It is also recommended to have a mirror in front of you and a second mirror behind you to see the back of your head.

When cutting front hair flicks, it's important to start slowly and only cut a little bit at a time. You can always trim more later, but you can't put hair back once it's cut. It's better to be cautious and take your time.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wet Your Hair

Spray your hair with water to make it easier to comb and cut. Wet hair is easier to control and helps prevent split ends.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Use a comb to detangle your hair and make sure it's smooth. This will help you see where the hair naturally falls and where to cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Take the front section of your hair and pull it forward. This will be the section you will cut for your front hair flicks. The rest of your hair should be tied back or clipped away.

Step 4: Cut Your Hair

Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to cut at an angle, starting from the middle of the hair and moving towards the end. Cut a little bit at a time and check to see if you like the length before cutting more.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Put down your scissors and check your work in the mirror. Make sure both sides are even and the length is what you wanted. You can always trim more if needed.

Tips for Cutting Front Hair Flicks at Home

  • Start with a little bit of hair and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and make clean cuts.
  • Comb your hair before cutting to make sure it's smooth and tangle-free.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time and be patient.
  • Check your work in the mirror frequently to make sure both sides are even.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair flicks?

Yes, you can cut your own hair flicks. With a little bit of practice and patience, you can easily learn how to do it at home.

What kind of scissors should I use?

It's important to use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and make clean cuts. Hair cutting scissors are recommended, but if you don't have any, sharp household scissors will do.

How do I know if my hair flicks are even?

Use a mirror to check your work frequently. Make sure both sides are even and the length is what you wanted. If you notice any unevenness, trim a little bit more until it's even.

Hair Care After Cutting Front Hair Flicks at Home

After cutting your hair flicks, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent split ends and maintain the length. Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage. Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the length and prevent split ends. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and use a heat protectant spray when you do. With proper care, your hair flicks will look great and last a long time.

Now that you know how to cut front hair flicks at home step by step, you can save money and have more control over your hairstyle. Remember to start slow and be patient, and you'll have beautiful hair flicks in no time.
