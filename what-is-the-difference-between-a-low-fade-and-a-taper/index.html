Low Fade Vs Taper What s The Difference

Written by Frank Oct 30, 2023 · 4 min read

When it comes to haircuts, there are many different styles to choose from. Two popular styles that are often confused with one another are the low fade and the taper. While they may look similar, there are some key differences between the two styles. In this blog post, we'll explore the differences between a low fade and a taper, and provide some tips for caring for both styles.

What is a Low Fade?

A low fade is a type of haircut that gradually fades from short hair at the nape of the neck to longer hair at the top of the head. The fade should be very subtle, with no visible lines or harsh transitions between the different lengths. This style is often worn with longer hair on top that can be styled in a variety of ways, such as slicked back or parted to the side.

What is a Taper?

A taper is a type of haircut that involves gradually decreasing the length of the hair from the top of the head down to the neckline. Unlike the low fade, a taper usually involves a more noticeable change in length, with a distinct line between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair at the back and sides of the head. A taper can be worn with a variety of different hair lengths and styles, from short buzz cuts to longer, textured hair.

What's the Difference?

The main difference between a low fade and a taper is the way that the hair is blended. In a low fade, the hair gradually blends from short to long with no visible line of demarcation. In a taper, there is usually a distinct line between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair at the back and sides of the head.

Another difference is the length of the hair on top. A low fade is typically worn with longer hair on top, while a taper can be worn with a variety of different hair lengths and styles.

How to Get a Low Fade or Taper

If you're interested in getting a low fade or taper, the best way to start is by finding a skilled barber who can help you achieve the look you want. Make sure to bring in a photo or a clear description of the style you're looking for, so that your barber can understand your vision.

Once you're in the chair, your barber will use clippers and scissors to gradually cut your hair to the desired length. They may also use a straight razor to clean up the neckline and around the ears.

Tips for Caring for Your Low Fade or Taper

Once you have your new haircut, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking fresh and stylish. Here are a few tips:

  • Visit your barber regularly for touch-ups and trims
  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and healthy
  • Style your hair with a pomade or wax to add texture and hold
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage
  • Experiment with different styles and hair products to find what works best for you

FAQ

Which is better: a low fade or a taper?

There is no right or wrong answer to this question, as both styles can look great depending on your hair type and personal style. It's best to talk to your barber about which style would work best for you.

How often should I get a touch-up?

This will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain your style. As a general rule, it's a good idea to visit your barber every 4-6 weeks for a touch-up and trim.

Can I style my low fade or taper in different ways?

Absolutely! Both styles are versatile and can be styled in a variety of different ways. Experiment with different products and styling techniques to find what works best for you.

Hair Care

Regardless of which style you choose, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and stylish. This means using high-quality hair products, avoiding excessive heat and chemical treatments, and visiting your barber regularly for touch-ups and trims.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can enjoy a low fade or taper that looks great and makes you feel confident and stylish.
