How Do I Keep My Hair From Falling Out After Covid

Written by Christine Oct 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're like many people, you may have noticed that your hair has been falling out more than usual since you recovered from COVID-19. This is a common side effect of the virus, and it can be frustrating and alarming. However, there are things you can do to minimize hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.

Description

COVID-19 can cause a variety of symptoms, including fever, cough, and shortness of breath. However, many people also experience hair loss as a result of the virus. This can be due to a variety of factors, including stress, inflammation, and changes in hormone levels. While hair loss can be upsetting, it's important to remember that it's usually temporary, and there are steps you can take to support healthy hair growth.

How to Keep Your Hair from Falling Out After COVID

Step 1: Take Care of Your Scalp

Your scalp is the foundation of your hair, so it's important to keep it healthy. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to cleanse your scalp without stripping it of its natural oils. Avoid using hot water, as this can dry out your scalp and make hair loss worse. Instead, use lukewarm water to wash your hair.

Step 2: Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a balanced, nutrient-rich diet can support healthy hair growth. Make sure to include plenty of protein, which is essential for building strong, healthy hair. You can get protein from sources like lean meats, fish, eggs, and beans. Also, make sure to eat plenty of fruits and vegetables, which are rich in vitamins and minerals that are important for hair health.

Step 3: Manage Stress

Stress is a common cause of hair loss, so it's important to manage it as much as possible. Try to find ways to relax, such as practicing yoga or meditation, taking a warm bath, or listening to calming music. Also, make sure to get enough sleep, as lack of sleep can increase stress levels and worsen hair loss.

Tips for Keeping Your Hair Healthy

In addition to the steps above, there are other things you can do to support healthy hair growth. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or hair dye
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or a brush with soft bristles to avoid pulling on your hair
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can put pressure on your hair, such as braids or ponytails
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective spray

FAQ

Q: Will my hair grow back after COVID?

A: Yes, in most cases, hair loss due to COVID is temporary and your hair will grow back over time.

Q: How long will it take for my hair to grow back?

A: It varies from person to person, but most people will start to see new hair growth within a few months of recovering from COVID.

Q: Can I take supplements to promote hair growth?

A: Some supplements, such as biotin, have been shown to support healthy hair growth. However, it's important to talk to your doctor before taking any supplements, as they can interact with medications and have side effects.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is an important part of supporting healthy hair growth. Make sure to use gentle, nourishing products that don't contain harsh chemicals. Also, avoid using hot styling tools, as heat can damage your hair and make hair loss worse. Instead, let your hair air-dry whenever possible, and use a heat protectant spray if you do need to use a styling tool.

Conclusion

Hair loss can be a frustrating and unsettling side effect of COVID, but it's usually temporary. By taking care of your scalp, eating a healthy diet, managing stress, and following the tips above, you can support healthy hair growth and minimize hair loss. If you're concerned about your hair loss, talk to your doctor or a dermatologist, who can provide additional guidance and support.
