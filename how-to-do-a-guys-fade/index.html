How To Do A Guy s Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Oct 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and versatile haircut, you can't go wrong with a guy's fade. This classic men's hairstyle features short hair on the sides and back, which gradually fades into longer hair on top. Whether you want a low fade, high fade, or something in between, this haircut can be tailored to suit your preferences and face shape.

Description of a Guy's Fade

A guy's fade is a haircut that features short hair on the sides and back, which gradually fades into longer hair on top. This hairstyle can be customized to suit your preferences, with options for low fades, high fades, and everything in between. The key to a successful fade is blending the hair seamlessly, so there are no harsh lines or abrupt transitions.

How to Do a Guy's Fade

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need clippers, scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. You may also want to use hair clips to section off the hair and make the process easier.

Step 2: Choose Your Fade

Decide how high you want the fade to be. A low fade will start near the ears and gradually taper up, while a high fade will start near the temples and taper up to the crown of the head. You can also choose a mid fade or drop fade, depending on your preferences.

Step 3: Start with the Sides

Using your clippers, start with the shortest guard and work your way up. Start at the bottom of the head, near the nape of the neck, and work your way up to the desired length. Use the longest guard near the top of the head, so the hair blends smoothly into the longer hair on top.

Step 4: Blend the Fade

Once you've created the initial fade, use your scissors or clippers without a guard to blend the hair between the shorter and longer sections. Work in small sections, using the comb to lift the hair and blend it seamlessly.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Use your scissors to cut the hair on top to the desired length. You can use texturizing shears to add volume and texture, or keep it simple with a blunt cut. Style as desired.

Tips for a Perfect Guy's Fade

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down, so you don't accidentally cut too much hair.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp and easier to cut.
  • Take your time and work in small sections, so you can ensure the hair is blended seamlessly.
  • Use hair clips to section off the hair and make the process easier.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help or visit a professional barber if you're unsure.

FAQ About Guy's Fades

What face shapes suit a guy's fade?

A guy's fade can suit a variety of face shapes, but it's especially flattering on those with square or oval faces. If you have a round face, consider a high fade to elongate your features.

How often should I get a guy's fade?

Most guys get a fade every 2-4 weeks, depending on how quickly their hair grows and how well they maintain the style. Regular trims can help keep the fade looking fresh and sharp.

Can I style a guy's fade differently?

A guy's fade is a versatile hairstyle, and you can experiment with different styling products and techniques to achieve different looks. Try using a matte pomade for a messy, textured look, or a high-shine pomade for a sleek, polished finish.

Hair Care Tips for a Guy's Fade

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers or flat irons, which can damage the hair and make it harder to style.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or styling product to keep the hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals like bleach or hair dye, which can damage the hair and affect the fade.
  • Visit a professional barber regularly for trims and touch-ups.

With these tips and techniques, you can create a stylish and versatile guy's fade that suits your face shape and personal style. Whether you prefer a low fade, high fade, or something in between, this classic hairstyle is sure to turn heads.
