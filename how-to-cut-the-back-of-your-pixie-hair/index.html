How To Cut The Back Of Your Pixie Hair

Written by William Sep 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you a fan of short haircuts? Pixie haircuts are a popular choice for those who want to keep their hair short and stylish. However, maintaining a pixie haircut requires some effort, including regular trimming. In this post, we’ll guide you through how to cut the back of your pixie hair. Keep reading to learn more!

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut the Back of Your Pixie Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Pixie Hair
  • FAQs

How to Cut the Back of Your Pixie Hair

Cutting the back of your pixie hair can be challenging, especially if you don’t have any experience. That’s why we’ve prepared a step-by-step guide to help you achieve a professional-looking cut.

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair and Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Also, prepare the necessary tools, including a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, section your hair into two parts. Create a horizontal parting at the back of your head, just above the nape of your neck. Clip the top section of your hair, so it doesn’t interfere with the cutting process.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Using your scissors, start cutting the hair at the back of your head. Start with small sections and work your way up, making sure to cut evenly. Keep in mind that it’s better to cut less than more, as you can always trim more later.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

After you’ve finished cutting, check your progress in a mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that the back of your hair is even.

Step 5: Blend the Sides

To ensure a smooth transition between the back and sides of your hair, use your scissors to blend the sides with the back. Take small sections of hair and cut at an angle, blending the hair together.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a more detailed step-by-step guide to help you cut the back of your pixie hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Brush your hair with a comb to remove any tangles.
  3. Section your hair into two parts, with a horizontal parting at the back of your head.
  4. Clip the top section of your hair, so it doesn’t interfere with the cutting process.
  5. Start cutting the hair at the back of your head, working from the bottom up.
  6. Take small sections of hair and cut evenly.
  7. Check your progress in a mirror and make any necessary adjustments.
  8. Blend the sides with the back to ensure a smooth transition.
  9. Repeat the process as needed to achieve your desired length.
  10. Finish by styling your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Your Pixie Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect pixie haircut:

  • Invest in a good pair of scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up.
  • Take your time and don’t rush the process.
  • Check your progress in a mirror regularly.
  • Ask for help if you’re unsure.

FAQs

Can I cut the back of my pixie hair myself?

Yes, you can cut the back of your pixie hair yourself, but it's recommended to ask for help from a friend or family member. Alternatively, you can visit a professional hairstylist for a more polished look.

How often should I cut the back of my pixie hair?

You should trim the back of your pixie hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and style.

Can I style my pixie haircut differently?

Yes, you can style your pixie haircut in various ways, including adding texture, using hair accessories, or changing your hair color.

Hair Care Tips for Pixie Haircuts

Here are some hair care tips to keep your pixie haircut looking its best:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use a hairspray or pomade to style your hair as desired.
  • Visit a hairstylist regularly to maintain your haircut.

Now that you know how to cut the back of your pixie hair, you can achieve a stylish and low-maintenance look. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and ask for help if needed. Happy cutting!
