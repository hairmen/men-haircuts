How To Take Red Tones Out Of Blonde Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Jul 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a blonde bombshell, you know how frustrating it can be to deal with red tones in your hair. Whether you're a natural blonde or have dyed your hair, red tones can be stubborn and difficult to eliminate. But fear not! With the right products and techniques, you can take red tones out of blonde hair and achieve the perfect shade of blonde.

How to

The first step in removing red tones from blonde hair is to choose the right product. Look for a toner that is specifically designed to neutralize redness. You can find toners at most beauty supply stores or online. Once you have your toner, follow these steps:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or residue. This will help the toner to penetrate your hair more effectively. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel-dry it.

Step 2: Apply the Toner

Put on gloves and mix the toner according to the instructions on the package. Apply the toner evenly to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the tips. Make sure to saturate all of your hair with the toner.

Step 3: Wait

Leave the toner on your hair for the recommended amount of time, usually around 20-30 minutes. Check the color of your hair every few minutes to make sure it's not getting too light. You can also do a strand test before applying the toner to your whole head to see how it will react.

Step 4: Rinse

Once the toner has been on your hair for the recommended amount of time, rinse it out thoroughly with cool water. Make sure to get all of the toner out of your hair.

Step 5: Condition

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair and leave it on for a few minutes to help restore moisture and shine. Rinse your hair thoroughly.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you take red tones out of your blonde hair:

  • Use a purple shampoo or conditioner in between toning sessions to help maintain your blonde color
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause damage
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can dry out and damage your hair
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about taking red tones out of blonde hair:

How long will the toner last?

The length of time the toner lasts depends on a variety of factors, including how often you wash your hair and how much sun exposure your hair gets. Expect the toner to last anywhere from a few weeks to a few months.

Can I use toner on natural blonde hair?

Yes, toner can be used on natural blonde hair to help remove red tones and enhance the color.

Will toning my hair damage it?

No, toning your hair should not cause any damage as long as you follow the instructions carefully and use a high-quality product.

Hair Care

To keep your blonde hair looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help protect your hair from damage and keep it moisturized
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently

By following these tips and using the right products, you can take red tones out of your blonde hair and achieve the perfect shade of blonde. Happy toning!
