How To Cut Men s Medium Length Curly Hair

Curly hair can be a challenge to cut, especially if the length is medium. However, with the right tools and techniques, cutting men's medium length curly hair can be done easily. In this article, we'll go over a step-by-step guide on how to cut men's medium length curly hair, including tips, FAQs, and hair care.

How to Cut Men's Medium Length Curly Hair

Before we start, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a good pair of scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. It's also helpful to have a mirror and a cape to protect your client's clothes.

Follow these steps to cut men's medium length curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition the Hair

Make sure the hair is clean and conditioned. Curly hair can be prone to dryness, so use a moisturizing conditioner to keep the hair hydrated.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Use a comb to section the hair into manageable sections. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up to the crown.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Use your scissors to cut the hair in small sections. Start at the back and work your way to the sides and the top. Cut the hair to the desired length, but be careful not to cut too much at once.

Step 4: Check the Length

Once you've cut all the sections, check the length of the hair. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that the hair is even.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Use your scissors to blend any harsh lines. This will ensure that the haircut looks natural and seamless.

Step 6: Style the Hair

Use a styling product to define the curls and add texture. You can use a gel, mousse, or cream, depending on the client's preference.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to cut men's medium length curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition the Hair

Start by washing the hair with a moisturizing shampoo. Curly hair can be prone to dryness, so it's important to use a shampoo that won't strip the hair of its natural oils. Follow up with a moisturizing conditioner to keep the hair hydrated.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Use a comb to section the hair into manageable sections. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up to the crown. Pin the sections that you're not working on out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Starting at the back, take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. Be careful not to cut too much at once. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you've completed the back section.

Move on to the sides and the top, following the same technique. Cut the hair in small sections to ensure that you're not cutting too much at once.

Step 4: Check the Length

Once you've cut all the sections, check the length of the hair. Use your scissors to make any necessary adjustments to ensure that the hair is even.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Use your scissors to blend any harsh lines. Comb the hair straight and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut into the ends of the hair, creating a soft, blended look.

Step 6: Style the Hair

Use a styling product to define the curls and add texture. You can use a gel, mousse, or cream, depending on the client's preference. Apply the product to damp hair and scrunch the curls with your hands. Allow the hair to air dry or use a diffuser to dry the hair gently.

Tips for Cutting Men's Medium Length Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting men's medium length curly hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. Cut small sections to ensure that you're not cutting off too much length.
  • Blend the hair to create a natural, seamless look.
  • Use a light touch when cutting curly hair. Curly hair can bounce back and shrink when it dries, so cutting too much can result in a shorter haircut than intended.
  • Use a mirror to check the haircut from all angles.

FAQs

How often should men with medium length curly hair get a haircut?

It depends on the client's hair growth and the desired length. Generally, men with medium length curly hair should get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Can you cut curly hair when it's wet or dry?

It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry. Curly hair can shrink when it dries, so cutting it when it's wet can result in a shorter haircut than intended.

What's the best way to style curly hair?

It depends on the client's hair type and the desired look. A gel, mousse, or cream can be used to define the curls and add texture. Experiment with different products to find the one that works best for your client's hair.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and hydrated. Here are some tips for caring for curly hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair hydrated.
  • Avoid brushing curly hair when it's dry. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle the hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to add moisture to the hair.
  • Avoid using heat tools, such as straighteners and curling irons, as they can damage the hair.
  • Protect the hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a swim cap.

With these tips and techniques, cutting men's medium length curly hair can be done easily. Remember to use a light touch, cut small sections, and blend the hair to create a natural, seamless look. With the right care and styling, your client's curly hair will look and feel healthy and hydrated.
