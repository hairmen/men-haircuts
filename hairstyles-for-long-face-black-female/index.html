Hairstyles For Long Face Black Female Tips Tricks And Styles

Written by Oliver Nov 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a black woman with a long face, you know how difficult it can be to find the right hairstyle. You want something that flatters your features and complements your natural beauty, but it can be challenging to know where to start. Fortunately, there are plenty of options that can work for you, whether you prefer short, medium, or long hair. In this article, we'll explore some of the best hairstyles for long face black females so you can feel confident and beautiful every day.

Description

A long face is characterized by a high forehead and a narrow chin. If you have this face shape, you want to choose a hairstyle that balances out these features and adds width to your face. This can be achieved with layers, bangs, and volume around the cheeks and ears. You also want to avoid hairstyles that are too long or too straight, as they can make your face appear even longer.

How To: Determine Your Face Shape

If you're not sure whether you have a long face, there are a few simple steps you can take to find out. First, take a look at yourself in the mirror and measure the length of your face from your hairline to your chin. Then, measure the width of your forehead, cheeks, and jawline. If your face is longer than it is wide and your forehead and chin are both prominent, then you likely have a long face shape.

Step By Step: Hairstyles for Long Face Black Female

1. Short Pixie Cut

A short pixie cut is a great option for black women with long faces. This style is short on the sides and back, with longer layers on top that can be styled in various ways. A side-swept fringe can help balance out a high forehead and add width to the face.

2. Shoulder-Length Bob

A shoulder-length bob is another flattering hairstyle for long face black females. This style falls just below the chin and can be styled straight, wavy, or curly. Layers around the face can add volume and create a more balanced look.

3. Curly Updo

If you have natural curls, consider an updo for a special occasion. This style can be achieved by pulling the hair up into a bun or ponytail and leaving some tendrils around the face. This creates a more symmetrical look and adds volume to the sides of the face.

4. Long Layers

If you prefer longer hair, consider adding layers to create movement and volume around the face. You can also opt for a side-swept fringe or bangs to balance out a high forehead.

Tips

When choosing a hairstyle for a long face, consider the following tips:

  • Add volume to the sides of the face to balance out a high forehead and narrow chin
  • Avoid hairstyles that are too long or too straight
  • Consider side-swept bangs or a fringe to add width to the face
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you

FAQ

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a long face?

Avoid hairstyles that are too long or too straight, as they can make your face appear even longer. Instead, opt for styles with layers, curls, or volume around the cheeks and ears.

Can I wear bangs if I have a long face?

Yes, bangs can be a great way to balance out a high forehead and add width to the face. Consider a side-swept fringe or choppy bangs that fall just below the eyebrows.

How do I know if a hairstyle will flatter my face?

Consider your face shape and features when choosing a hairstyle. Look for styles that balance out your prominent features and add width to your face. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, be sure to follow a regular hair care routine. This includes washing and conditioning your hair regularly, using a heat protectant when styling with heat tools, and protecting your hair from UV rays and environmental damage. Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.

With these tips and styles, you can find the perfect hairstyle for your long face and feel confident and beautiful every day.
