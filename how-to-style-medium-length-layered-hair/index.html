How To Style Medium Length Layered Hair

Written by William Dec 02, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have medium length layered hair and are looking for some styling ideas, you’ve come to the right place. In this post, we’ll share some tips and tricks on how to style your hair in a way that suits your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. Whether you prefer a sleek and straight look or loose waves, there’s a style for everyone.

Description

Medium length hair is versatile and easy to manage. When you add layers to it, you create movement, texture, and volume. Layered hair can be styled in many different ways, including updos, half-up styles, and loose waves. The key is to find a style that complements your face shape and hair texture.

How to

Before you start styling your hair, make sure it’s clean and dry. Apply a heat protectant spray if you plan on using heat styling tools. Here are some styling ideas:

Sleek and Straight

If you want a polished and sophisticated look, try straightening your hair. Use a flat iron to smooth out any kinks or waves. Finish with a shine serum or hairspray to keep your hair in place.

Loose Waves

To create loose waves, use a curling wand or curling iron. Wrap small sections of hair around the barrel, leaving the ends out. Hold for a few seconds, then release. Once all your hair is curled, brush it out with a soft bristle brush to create soft waves. Finish with a texturizing spray to add volume and hold.

Half-Up Styles

If you want to keep your hair out of your face but still show off your length and layers, try a half-up style. Tease the crown of your hair, then pull back the top section and secure with bobby pins. Leave the rest of your hair down or style it in loose waves.

Step by Step

Here’s a step-by-step guide to styling your medium length layered hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray.
  3. Straighten or curl your hair, depending on the look you want to achieve.
  4. Tease the crown of your hair for extra volume.
  5. Create a half-up style or leave your hair down.
  6. Finish with hairspray or texturizing spray to hold your style in place.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you style your medium length layered hair:

  • Invest in a good flat iron or curling wand to make styling easier.
  • Use a texturizing spray to add volume and hold.
  • Try different partings to create different looks.
  • Accessorize with headbands, clips, or scarves to add some extra flair.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about styling medium length layered hair:

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair texture and scalp condition. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it every other day. If you have dry hair, you can wash it once or twice a week.

Q: What products should I use to style my hair?

A: It depends on the look you want to achieve. For sleek and straight hair, use a flat iron and a shine serum. For loose waves, use a curling wand and a texturizing spray. For a half-up style, use bobby pins and hairspray.

Q: Can I style my hair without heat?

A: Yes, there are many heat-free styling options, such as braids, buns, and twists. You can also use rollers or flexi rods to create curls without heat.

Hair Care

To keep your medium length layered hair healthy and shiny, here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a swim cap.

Now that you know how to style your medium length layered hair, it’s time to experiment and have fun. Whether you’re going for a casual or formal look, there’s a style for every occasion. Remember to take care of your hair and use quality products to keep it healthy and beautiful.
