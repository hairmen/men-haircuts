How To Stop Hair Fall After Bleaching A Complete Guide

Are you experiencing hair fall after bleaching your hair? You're not alone. Bleaching can cause damage to your hair, leading to hair fall and breakage. However, there are ways you can prevent hair fall and maintain healthy hair after bleaching. In this guide, we'll show you how to stop hair fall after bleaching.

Reasons for Hair Fall After Bleaching

Bleaching your hair can cause damage to the hair cuticles, leading to hair fall and breakage. The bleaching process involves the use of chemicals that can weaken your hair and cause it to become brittle. Here are some of the reasons why you may experience hair fall after bleaching:

  • The chemicals used in the bleaching process can damage the hair shaft, causing it to break.
  • Bleaching can cause the hair to become dry and brittle, making it prone to breakage.
  • Over-bleaching can lead to hair fall and damage to the hair follicles, which can affect hair growth.

How to Stop Hair Fall After Bleaching

To prevent hair fall after bleaching, you need to take special care of your hair. Here are some steps you can take to stop hair fall and maintain healthy hair:

1. Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning your hair can help to restore moisture and prevent breakage. Use a deep conditioning treatment at least once a week to keep your hair healthy and strong.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can further damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers as much as possible.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush. A wide-tooth comb is gentler on your hair and reduces the risk of breakage.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly can help to remove split ends and prevent breakage. Aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and strong.

5. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help to promote healthy hair growth. Eat foods such as salmon, nuts, and leafy greens to support healthy hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to stop hair fall after bleaching:

  1. Deep condition your hair once a week.
  2. Avoid heat styling as much as possible.
  3. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  4. Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks.
  5. Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals.

Tips to Prevent Hair Fall After Bleaching

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair fall after bleaching:

  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair and make it more prone to breakage.
  • Wash your hair with lukewarm water instead of hot water, as hot water can further damage your hair.
  • Avoid over-brushing your hair, as it can cause breakage and damage to the hair cuticles.

FAQs

Can hair fall after bleaching be prevented?

Yes, hair fall after bleaching can be prevented by taking special care of your hair. Follow the steps outlined in this guide to maintain healthy hair after bleaching.

How long does it take for hair to stop falling out after bleaching?

The time it takes for hair to stop falling out after bleaching varies depending on the extent of the damage. However, with proper hair care, you should start to see a reduction in hair fall within a few weeks.

Hair Care After Bleaching

After bleaching your hair, it's important to take special care of it to prevent hair fall and breakage. Here are some tips to care for your hair after bleaching:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to protect your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as flat irons and curling irons.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as it can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish and strengthen your hair.

By following these tips and taking special care of your hair, you can prevent hair fall after bleaching and maintain healthy, beautiful hair.
