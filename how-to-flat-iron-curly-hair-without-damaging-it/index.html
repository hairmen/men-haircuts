How To Flat Iron Curly Hair Without Damaging It

Written by Oliver Sep 18, 2023 · 5 min read

Flat ironing curly hair can be a quick and easy way to get a sleek and straight look, but it can also cause damage if not done properly. In this article, we’ll show you how to flat iron curly hair without damaging it. We’ll cover everything from preparing your hair to prevent damage, to using the right products and techniques to ensure your hair stays healthy and beautiful.

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start flat ironing your curly hair, there are a few things you need to do to prepare it. First, make sure your hair is clean and dry. If your hair is damp, it can cause damage when you apply heat. You should also detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb or a brush designed for curly hair. This will help prevent breakage and make it easier to flat iron your hair.

Next, you’ll want to apply a heat protectant to your hair. This will help prevent damage from the heat of the flat iron. You can find heat protectant sprays, creams, and serums at most drugstores and beauty supply stores.

Choosing the Right Products

When it comes to flat ironing curly hair, it’s important to use the right products. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, as they will help protect your hair from damage and keep it moisturized. You should also avoid products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair and make it more prone to damage.

Some good products to use on curly hair include leave-in conditioners, curl creams, and oils. These products will help keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

Using a Heat Protector

As mentioned earlier, using a heat protector is essential when flat ironing curly hair. Heat protectants work by creating a barrier between your hair and the heat of the flat iron, helping to prevent damage.

To use a heat protector, simply apply it to your hair before you start flat ironing. Make sure to apply it evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends, which are the most prone to damage.

Sectioning Your Hair

When flat ironing curly hair, it’s important to work in small sections. This will help ensure that all of your hair gets straightened evenly and will prevent you from having to go over the same section multiple times, which can cause damage.

To section your hair, use clips or hair ties to divide it into manageable sections. Start at the bottom and work your way up, making sure to straighten each section before moving on to the next one.

Flat Ironing Your Hair

When flat ironing curly hair, it’s important to use a flat iron with adjustable temperature settings. This will allow you to customize the heat to your hair type and prevent damage.

Start at the roots and slowly glide the flat iron down the section of hair. Make sure to keep the flat iron moving to prevent heat damage. Repeat this process until all of your hair is straightened.

Cooling Down Your Hair

After flat ironing your hair, it’s important to let it cool down before styling it. This will help set the style and prevent damage from heat styling tools. You can use a cool shot button on your blow dryer to cool down your hair or simply let it air dry for a few minutes.

Finishing Touches

Once your hair has cooled down, you can add some finishing touches to complete your look. You can use a hair serum or oil to add shine and prevent frizz. You can also use a hairspray or hair gel to help hold your style in place.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to help keep your curly hair healthy:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using hair ties with metal clasps, as they can cause damage to your hair.

FAQs

Can I flat iron my curly hair every day?

No, it’s not recommended to flat iron your curly hair every day. Heat styling tools can cause damage to your hair if used too often. Try to limit your use of heat styling tools to once or twice a week.

Can I flat iron my hair when it’s wet?

No, it’s not recommended to flat iron your hair when it’s wet. Wet hair is more prone to damage from heat styling tools. Make sure your hair is completely dry before flat ironing.

How can I keep my hair straight after flat ironing?

You can keep your hair straight after flat ironing by using a hairspray or hair gel to hold the style in place. You can also use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent your hair from getting tangled while you sleep.

Conclusion

Flat ironing curly hair can be a great way to get a sleek and straight look, but it’s important to do it properly to prevent damage. By preparing your hair, choosing the right products, and using the right techniques, you can flat iron your hair without damaging it. Remember to take care of your hair by using hair care tips and to limit your use of heat styling tools to keep your hair healthy and beautiful.
