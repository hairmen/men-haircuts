How To Get Permanent Natural Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you've always wanted natural curly hair but have been struggling with your straight locks, we've got some good news for you. You can get permanent natural curly hair with a few simple steps. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to get permanent natural curly hair. We'll also share some tips on how to maintain your curls and answer some frequently asked questions about curly hair.

How to Get Permanent Natural Curly Hair

Before we get started, it's important to note that getting permanent natural curly hair requires a chemical treatment. If you're not comfortable with using chemicals on your hair, this may not be the best option for you.

The chemical treatment involves breaking the bonds in your hair and then reforming them in a curly pattern. This process is called a perm. Perms have come a long way since the 80s and 90s, and the chemicals used today are much gentler on your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get permanent natural curly hair:

Step 1: Choose a Professional Stylist

Getting a perm is not something you should attempt at home. It's important to choose a professional stylist who has experience with curly hair. Look for someone who specializes in perms and has a good reputation.

Step 2: Consultation

Before you get a perm, you'll need to have a consultation with your stylist. They'll examine your hair and determine if it's healthy enough for a perm. They'll also discuss the type of curl you want and the size of the rods they'll use.

Step 3: Prep Your Hair

On the day of your appointment, your stylist will wash your hair and apply a pre-treatment solution. This solution will protect your hair from the chemicals in the perm solution.

Step 4: Apply the Perm Solution

Your stylist will apply the perm solution to your hair, making sure to saturate each section evenly. They'll then wrap your hair around rods to create the curl pattern you want.

Step 5: Rinse and Apply Neutralizer

After the perm solution has been on your hair for the designated amount of time, your stylist will rinse your hair thoroughly. They'll then apply a neutralizer to stop the chemical reaction and set the curls.

Step 6: Style Your Curls

Your stylist will remove the rods from your hair and style your curls. They may use a diffuser to dry your hair or let it air dry.

Tips for Maintaining Your Curls

Once you've got your new natural curly hair, it's important to take care of it. Here are some tips for maintaining your curls:

  • Use sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry to prevent frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Apply a curl-defining product to enhance your curls.
  • Protect your curls while you sleep by sleeping on a silk pillowcase or using a silk headscarf.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about curly hair:

How often should I wash my curly hair?

Curly hair tends to be dry, so you don't need to wash it as often as straight hair. Aim to wash your hair once or twice a week.

Can I straighten my curly hair?

Yes, you can straighten your curly hair, but it's important to use a heat protectant and avoid using a high heat setting.

How can I prevent frizz?

To prevent frizz, avoid touching your hair too much and use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires a different hair care routine than straight hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using a brush on your hair when it's dry.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls moisturized.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to avoid frizz.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and a little bit of effort, you can keep your natural curly hair looking its best. Enjoy your new curls!
