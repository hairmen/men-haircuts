Best Short Haircuts For Pear Shaped Faces

Written by Frank Jun 20, 2023 · 2 min read

Are you a pear-shaped beauty looking for the perfect short haircut? Look no further! We’ve compiled a list of the best short haircuts for pear-shaped faces to help you find your perfect look.

Description

Pear-shaped faces are characterized by a wider jawline and narrower forehead. The goal of a short haircut for this face shape is to balance out the proportions and draw attention to the upper half of the face.

How to

The key to finding the perfect short haircut for a pear-shaped face is to focus on hairstyles that add volume to the top of the head. This can be achieved through layers or a shorter cut on top with longer pieces on the sides.

Step by Step

1. Start by determining your face shape and identifying your best features.

2. Research different short haircuts for pear-shaped faces.

3. Consult with a hairstylist to determine the best cut for your face shape and hair texture.

4. Experiment with different styling techniques to find the perfect look.

Tips

- Avoid heavy bangs that draw attention to the jawline.

- Consider adding volume to the crown of the head with layers or a shorter cut on top.

- Experiment with different hair products to find the perfect styling routine.

FAQ

Q: Can I still have bangs with a pear-shaped face?
A: Yes, but avoid heavy or blunt bangs that draw attention to the jawline. Consider side-swept or wispy bangs instead.

Q: Should I avoid short haircuts altogether if I have a pear-shaped face?
A: No, but focus on adding volume to the top of the head to balance out the proportions.

Hair Care

Maintaining a short haircut for a pear-shaped face requires regular trims and styling. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and nourished. Experiment with different products to find the perfect styling routine for your hair type and texture.

With these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to find the perfect short haircut for your pear-shaped face. Whether you opt for a pixie cut or a bob, remember to focus on adding volume to the top of the head to balance out your proportions.
