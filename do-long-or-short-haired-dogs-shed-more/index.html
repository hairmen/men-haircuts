Do Long Or Short Haired Dogs Shed More

Written by Bardi Sep 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As dog owners, we all know that shedding is a part of life when it comes to our furry friends. However, the question remains: do long or short haired dogs shed more? In this article, we will explore the answer to this question and provide some tips for managing your dog's shedding.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Dogs come in all shapes and sizes, and with that comes varying lengths of hair. While it may seem like common sense that long haired dogs would shed more, the truth is that it actually depends on the breed. Some long haired breeds, such as the Afghan Hound, actually shed less than their short haired counterparts. On the other hand, some short haired breeds, such as the Beagle, shed quite a bit.

How to

If you're wondering whether a particular breed sheds more or less, you can do some research online or consult with a veterinarian or breeder. It's important to keep in mind that every dog is different, so even within a breed, there can be variations in shedding.

Step by step

If you already have a dog and are struggling with shedding, there are some steps you can take to manage it. Here are some tips:

Brush your dog regularly

Regularly brushing your dog can help remove loose hair before it has a chance to fall out. This can help reduce shedding and keep your home cleaner. Use a brush that is appropriate for your dog's coat type.

Bathe your dog

Bathing your dog can help remove loose hair and dirt that can contribute to shedding. Be sure to use a dog-specific shampoo and follow the instructions carefully. Over-bathing can actually strip your dog's coat of natural oils, which can lead to more shedding.

Feed your dog a healthy diet

A healthy diet can help keep your dog's coat healthy and reduce shedding. Look for a high-quality dog food that contains protein, vitamins, and minerals. Avoid food that contains fillers or artificial ingredients.

Keep your home clean

Vacuuming regularly can help remove loose hair from your home. Be sure to also wash your dog's bedding and any other areas where they spend time.

Tips

  • Invest in a good vacuum cleaner designed for pet hair
  • Consider using a de-shedding tool to remove loose hair
  • Talk to your veterinarian if you're concerned about excessive shedding

FAQ

Do all dogs shed?

Yes, all dogs shed to some extent. Some breeds shed less than others, but every dog will lose some hair at some point.

Is shedding a sign of a medical problem?

Excessive shedding can be a sign of a medical problem, such as a skin condition or thyroid issue. If you're concerned about your dog's shedding, talk to your veterinarian.

Hair Care

Caring for your dog's coat is an important part of managing shedding. Here are some tips:

Regular grooming

Regular grooming can help keep your dog's coat healthy and reduce shedding. This includes brushing, bathing, and trimming as needed.

Use the right grooming tools

Using the right grooming tools for your dog's coat type can help keep shedding under control. Talk to a professional groomer or veterinarian for advice.

Monitor your dog's skin and coat

Regularly checking your dog's skin and coat can help you identify any potential issues early on. Look for signs of dryness, irritation, or excessive shedding.

In conclusion, the answer to whether long or short haired dogs shed more is not a straightforward one. It depends on the breed and each individual dog. However, by following some simple tips for managing shedding, you can help keep your home cleaner and your dog's coat healthy.
