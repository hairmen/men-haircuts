Why Do Ocean Waves Change Direction

Written by Eveline Nov 16, 2023 · 3 min read

Have you ever been to the beach and noticed that the waves seem to change direction? It can be confusing, especially if you’re trying to swim or surf. Understanding why ocean waves change direction can help you navigate the water more effectively. Here’s everything you need to know:

Table of Contents

  1. What are Ocean Waves?
  2. How Do Waves Form?
  3. Wind Direction
  4. Ocean Currents
  5. Shoaling
  6. Beach Topography
  7. Tides
  8. Conclusion

What are Ocean Waves?

Ocean waves are disturbances in the ocean’s surface that are caused by wind, tides, and other factors. They can vary in size, speed, and direction.

How Do Waves Form?

Waves form when wind blows over the surface of the ocean. The stronger the wind, the larger the waves will be. The direction of the wind also affects the direction of the waves.

Wind Direction

When wind blows over the ocean, it creates a force that pushes the water in the same direction. This creates a wave. The direction of the wave will be perpendicular to the direction of the wind. So, if the wind is blowing from the east, the waves will be moving from north to south.

Ocean Currents

Ocean currents can also affect the direction of waves. If there is a strong current moving in a certain direction, it can push the waves in that direction as well. This can cause the waves to change direction or angle as they move across the ocean.

Shoaling

Shoaling occurs when waves move into shallow water. As the depth of the water decreases, the wave’s speed decreases as well. This causes the wave’s energy to become concentrated, which can cause the wave to change direction or break.

Beach Topography

The shape and slope of the beach can also affect the direction of waves. If the beach is steep, the waves will break closer to shore and in a more predictable direction. If the beach is shallow, the waves may break further out and in a less predictable direction.

Tides

Tides can also affect the direction of waves. When the tide is coming in or going out, it can cause the waves to move in a different direction. This is because the tide is moving large amounts of water in a certain direction, which can affect the direction of the waves.

Conclusion

There are many factors that can cause ocean waves to change direction. Understanding these factors can help you navigate the water more effectively and stay safe while swimming or surfing. Remember to always be aware of your surroundings and the conditions of the ocean before entering the water.
