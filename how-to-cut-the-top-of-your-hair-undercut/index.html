How To Cut The Top Of Your Hair Undercut

Written by William Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, an undercut can be an edgy and stylish choice. However, maintaining an undercut requires regular trims, especially on the top portion of your hair. If you're wondering how to cut the top of your hair undercut, this guide will walk you through the process step-by-step.

Description

An undercut is a popular hairstyle where the sides and back of the head are shaved while leaving the top portion of the hair longer. This creates a contrast between the two lengths, which can be styled in various ways. However, the top portion of the hair needs to be trimmed regularly to maintain its shape and length.

How to Cut the Top of Your Hair Undercut

Here are the steps to follow when cutting the top of your hair undercut:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see the length and shape of your hair, and it will also prevent any unnecessary tugging or pulling.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Separate the top portion of your hair from the sides and back. Use a comb to create a horizontal parting from ear to ear, and clip the rest of the hair out of the way.

Step 3: Determine the Desired Length

Decide how much you want to trim off the top portion of your hair. It's best to start with small cuts and work your way up gradually to avoid cutting too much off at once.

Step 4: Cut Your Hair

Start cutting the hair in small sections. Use scissors to trim the hair at a slight angle, cutting less at the front and more at the back. This will create a layered effect and prevent the hair from looking too blunt.

Step 5: Check the Length

After cutting a section of hair, check the length by pulling it straight up. Make sure it's even with the rest of the hair and adjust if necessary.

Step 6: Repeat the Process

Continue cutting small sections of hair until you've trimmed the entire top portion. Be careful not to cut too much off or create any uneven layers.

Step 7: Blend the Sides and Back

Once you've finished trimming the top, use clippers or scissors to blend the sides and back into the top portion. This will create a seamless transition between the lengths.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting the top of your hair undercut:

  • Use professional hair cutting scissors for best results.
  • Start with small cuts and work your way up gradually.
  • Don't cut too much off at once to avoid any mistakes.
  • Check the length frequently to ensure it's even.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head.
  • Consider using a comb or brush to help guide your scissors.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting the top of your hair undercut:

How often should I cut the top of my hair undercut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the top portion loses its shape. Generally, it's recommended to trim the top every 4-6 weeks.

Can I cut the top of my hair undercut myself?

Yes, you can cut the top of your hair undercut yourself with the proper tools and technique. However, it's always best to consult with a professional stylist for the best results.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you accidentally cut too much off or create an uneven layer, don't panic. You can always visit a stylist to fix the mistake or wait for the hair to grow out.

Hair Care

To maintain your undercut hairstyle, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat styling tools. Regular trims will also help keep the shape and length of your hair.

Cutting the top of your hair undercut may seem daunting, but with these steps and tips, you can achieve a stylish and edgy look. Remember to start with small cuts, check the length frequently, and blend the sides and back for a seamless transition. Happy cutting!
