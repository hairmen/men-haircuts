How Do You Thin Long Thick Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by William Nov 18, 2023 · 4 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing or a curse, depending on how you look at it. While it can give you an enviable volume and texture, it can also be a challenge to manage and style. One way to make it more manageable is by thinning it out. In this article, we will discuss how do you thin long thick hair, the tools you need, and some tips and tricks to make the process easier.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Thin Long Thick Hair
  2. Tools You Need
  3. Tips and Tricks
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care After Thinning

How to Thin Long Thick Hair

Thinning long thick hair involves cutting some of the hair strands to reduce the volume and weight. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to do it:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Then, towel-dry it and use a blow dryer to dry it completely. Make sure your hair is tangle-free before you start thinning it.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Using a comb, divide your hair into small sections. Clip each section of hair that you are not working on to keep it out of the way.

Step 3: Thin Your Hair with Thinning Shears

Take a section of hair and hold it at a 45-degree angle away from your head. Use thinning shears to cut about 1/4 to 1/2 inch of hair at a time. Start by snipping the ends of your hair and work your way up to the middle of the section. Repeat the process on the other sections of your hair.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

After thinning each section, check your progress by running your fingers through your hair. If it still feels too thick, repeat the process until you achieve the desired thickness.

Tools You Need

To thin long thick hair, you will need the following tools:

  • Thinning shears
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Volumizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Blow dryer

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to make the process of thinning long thick hair easier:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up to avoid over-thinning your hair.
  • Use thinning shears that have teeth of different sizes to achieve a natural look.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet as it will look shorter when it dries.
  • Don't over-thin your hair as it can lead to split ends and damage.

FAQs

How often should you thin your long thick hair?

You can thin your long thick hair every 6-8 weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows and how much thickness you want to remove.

Can you thin your hair with regular scissors?

No, you should not thin your hair with regular scissors as they can damage your hair and create uneven layers.

Can thinning your hair make it grow faster?

No, thinning your hair does not affect its growth rate. However, it can make your hair look healthier and more manageable.

Hair Care After Thinning

After thinning your long thick hair, you should take extra care of it to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a leave-in conditioner to moisturize your hair and prevent dryness.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain its shape.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote hair growth and health.

Now that you know how do you thin long thick hair, you can achieve a more manageable and stylish look. Just remember to use the right tools and follow the steps and tips we've mentioned above.
