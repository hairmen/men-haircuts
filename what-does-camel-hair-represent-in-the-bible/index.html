Camel Hair in the Bible

Camel hair is mentioned several times in the Bible, mainly in reference to clothing and fabrics. It was a common material used for making tents, clothing, and blankets in ancient times.

For example, in Exodus 26:7, God commands Moses to make the tabernacle curtains out of "fine linen, and blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine twined linen, with cherubims of cunning work." In the next verse, God instructs Moses to make the tent covering out of goat hair.

Another example is in the book of Job, where he describes his own clothing: "I put on righteousness, and it clothed me: my judgment was as a robe and a diadem. I was eyes to the blind, and feet was I to the lame. I was a father to the poor: and the cause which I knew not I searched out. And I brake the jaws of the wicked, and plucked the spoil out of his teeth. Then I said, I shall die in my nest, and I shall multiply my days as the sand. My root was spread out by the waters, and the dew lay all night upon my branch. My glory was fresh in me, and my bow was renewed in my hand. Unto me men gave ear, and waited, and kept silence at my counsel. After my words they spake not again; and my speech dropped upon them. And they waited for me as for the rain; and they opened their mouth wide as for the latter rain. If I laughed on them, they believed it not; and the light of my countenance they cast not down. I chose out their way, and sat chief, and dwelt as a king in the army, as one that comforteth the mourners." (Job 29:14-25)

What Does Camel Hair Represent?

In the Bible, camel hair represents humility, simplicity, and poverty. It is often associated with prophets and ascetics who have renounced worldly possessions and comforts in order to devote themselves fully to God.

Camel hair is also a symbol of endurance and resilience. Camels are known for their ability to survive in harsh desert environments, and their hair is similarly tough and resilient. This quality is often associated with spiritual strength and the ability to persevere through difficult times.

The Camel Hair Coat of John the Baptist

One of the most famous references to camel hair in the Bible is in relation to John the Baptist. In Matthew 3:4, it is written: "And the same John had his raiment of camel's hair, and a leathern girdle about his loins; and his meat was locusts and wild honey."

John the Baptist was a prophet who lived a simple life, wearing a coat made of camel hair and eating a diet of locusts and wild honey. His message was one of repentance and preparation for the coming of the Messiah.

Hair Care Tips for Camel Hair

If you own clothing or blankets made of camel hair, it is important to take good care of them to ensure they last a long time. Here are some hair care tips for camel hair:

1. Hand-wash only

Camel hair is delicate and should be hand-washed only. Use a mild detergent and lukewarm water and gently agitate the fabric. Rinse thoroughly and press out excess water.

2. Dry flat

Do not wring or twist the fabric, as this can damage the fibers. Instead, lay the item flat on a clean towel and reshape it as it dries.

3. Store properly

Store camel hair items in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Avoid hanging them, as this can cause stretching and distortion.

FAQ:

Q: Is camel hair expensive?

A: Camel hair is generally more expensive than other types of wool due to its rarity and the difficulty of harvesting it.

Q: Can camel hair be dyed?

A: Yes, camel hair can be dyed, but it is important to use a dye that is appropriate for the delicate fibers.

Q: Is camel hair warm?

A: Yes, camel hair is warm and insulating, making it a popular choice for winter clothing and blankets.

Q: What is the difference between camel hair and cashmere?

A: Camel hair and cashmere are both soft and luxurious fibers, but camel hair is coarser and more durable, while cashmere is finer and more delicate.

Overall, camel hair represents humility, simplicity, and endurance in the Bible. If you own clothing or blankets made of camel hair, be sure to take good care of them to ensure they last a long time.
