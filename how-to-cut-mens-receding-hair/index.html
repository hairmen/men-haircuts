How To Cut Men s Receding Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a man with receding hair, you may be wondering how to cut your hair in a way that looks stylish and hides your thinning areas. Don't worry- cutting men's receding hair can be done, and with a few tips and tricks, you can make it look great. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to cut men's receding hair, along with some helpful tips and hair care advice.

How to Cut Men's Receding Hair: Step by Step

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you begin cutting your hair, make sure it is clean and dry. This will help you get a more accurate cut and prevent any snags or pulls in your hair. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to cleanse and moisturize your hair before you start.

Step 2: Determine Your Hair Length

Next, decide how long you want your hair to be. This will depend on your personal style and hair type. If you have thinning hair, cutting it shorter may help to hide the thinning areas. However, if you prefer longer hair, consider using hair products or styling techniques to add volume and texture to your hair.

Step 3: Choose Your Cutting Tool

When it comes to cutting men's receding hair, you have a few options. You can use scissors, clippers, or a combination of both. Scissors are best for longer hair, while clippers work well for shorter styles. If you're not sure which tool to use, consult with a professional stylist or barber.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Before you start cutting, section your hair into manageable sections. Use clips or hair ties to separate your hair into sections, starting at the back of your head and working your way forward. This will help you get a more even cut and prevent any uneven spots.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Using your chosen cutting tool, begin cutting your hair starting at the back of your head and working your way forward. Make sure to cut small sections at a time and check your progress after each cut. This will help you make sure you're getting an even cut.

Step 6: Blend Your Hair

Once you've cut your hair to the desired length, use your cutting tool to blend your hair. This will help remove any harsh lines and create a more natural-looking cut. Use a comb or your fingers to guide your cutting tool and blend your hair evenly.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Finally, use hair styling products to add volume and texture to your hair. This will help create a fuller appearance and hide any thinning areas. Use a hair dryer, round brush, or other styling tools to create your desired look.

Tips for Cutting Men's Receding Hair

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when cutting men's receding hair:

  • Keep your cutting tool sharp to prevent snags and uneven cuts
  • Use hair products to add texture and volume to your hair
  • Consider a shorter style to hide thinning areas
  • Get regular trims to maintain your style

FAQ: Cutting Men's Receding Hair

Q: Can I cut my own hair?

A: Yes, you can cut your own hair, but it's important to use the proper tools and techniques. Consider consulting with a professional stylist or barber before attempting to cut your own hair.

Q: What tools do I need to cut men's receding hair?

A: You will need a cutting tool, such as scissors or clippers, as well as hair clips or ties to section your hair.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain your style and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Men's Receding Hair

Along with cutting your hair, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent further thinning and damage. Here are a few tips for hair care for men's receding hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Take hair supplements, such as biotin or folic acid, to promote hair growth
  • Wear a hat or sunscreen to protect your scalp from UV rays

Cutting men's receding hair can be done with a little practice and patience. By following these steps and tips, you can create a stylish and flattering haircut that makes you feel confident and comfortable.
