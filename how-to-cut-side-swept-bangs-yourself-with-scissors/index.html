How To Cut Side Swept Bangs Yourself With Scissors

Written by Frank Nov 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending money on salon appointments every time you need to trim your side swept bangs? Cutting your own bangs can be intimidating, but with the right technique and tools, it can be a money-saving and empowering experience. In this article, we'll guide you through the steps of cutting your own side swept bangs with scissors.

Description

Side swept bangs are a popular hairstyle that can flatter any face shape. They can be short or long, depending on your preference, and they add a touch of glamour to any look. However, maintaining the perfect length of side swept bangs can be a challenge, especially if you don't have the time or budget to visit a salon every few weeks. With a few simple tools and techniques, you can trim your own side swept bangs at home.

How to Cut Side Swept Bangs Yourself with Scissors

Step 1: Gather your tools

Before you start cutting your side swept bangs, you'll need to gather a few tools:

  • Sharp hair-cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut straight lines.

Step 2: Decide on the length

Decide on the length of your side swept bangs before you start cutting. You can use the length of your current bangs as a guide or choose a new length. It's important to remember that you can always cut more, but you can't add length back once it's cut off.

Step 3: Section your hair

Section off the hair you want to cut. Use a comb to create a triangular section at the front of your hairline, starting from the center of your forehead and ending at the arch of your eyebrows. Clip or tie the rest of your hair back to keep it out of the way.

Step 4: Cut the hair

Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and use your scissors to cut the hair in a straight line. Start by cutting a little bit at a time, and check the length frequently. Remember to cut at an angle to create the side swept effect.

Step 5: Check the length

Check the length of your bangs frequently by looking in the mirror and comparing each side. Make sure the length is even and adjust as needed. It's better to cut less than to cut too much, as you can always cut more.

Step 6: Style your bangs

Once you're happy with the length, style your side swept bangs by blow-drying them with a round brush or using a flat iron to add a slight curve. You can also add some texturizing spray or pomade to add volume and texture.

Tips

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Cut a little bit at a time to avoid cutting too much off.
  • Check the length frequently to ensure it's even.
  • Style your bangs after cutting them for the best results.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own bangs, practice on a wig or mannequin head first.

FAQ

Can I cut my own side swept bangs with regular scissors?

You can, but sharp hair-cutting scissors are recommended for a clean and even cut.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my bangs?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim a little more or visit a salon to fix the mistake.

How often do I need to trim my side swept bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want your bangs to be. On average, you'll need to trim your side swept bangs every two to four weeks.

Hair Care

To keep your side swept bangs looking their best, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat when styling your hair. You can also use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

Now that you know how to cut your own side swept bangs with scissors, you can save money and feel empowered to take control of your own hairstyle. With a little practice and patience, you can achieve salon-worthy results from the comfort of your own home.
