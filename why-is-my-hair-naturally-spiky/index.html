Why Is My Hair Naturally Spiky

Written by Aprilia Jul 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered why your hair has a tendency to stick up and spike out at odd angles? While some people might struggle to achieve this hairstyle with gel and other styling products, others are simply born with naturally spiky hair. In this article, we’ll discuss the science behind why some people have naturally spiky hair and how you can care for your hair to make the most of this unique trait.

Description

Naturally spiky hair is a genetic trait that is passed down from generation to generation. It is characterized by hair that grows in a direction that causes it to stand up and out from the head, rather than lying flat against the scalp. This can give the hair a wild and untamed appearance, but it can also be styled in a variety of ways to create an edgy and modern look.

How to Determine If You Have Naturally Spiky Hair

If you’re not sure whether your hair is naturally spiky or if it’s just a result of your styling routine, there are a few things you can look for to determine the answer. First, take a look at your hair when it’s wet and freshly washed. Does it still stick up and out from your head, even when it’s not styled? If so, you likely have naturally spiky hair. Additionally, you may notice that your hair has a wiry texture and is difficult to comb or brush into a smooth style.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Naturally Spiky Hair

If you’re looking to embrace your naturally spiky hair and create a trendy and modern hairstyle, there are a few steps you can follow to achieve the look you want. Here’s a quick guide:

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Begin by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair thoroughly and allow it to air dry completely before styling.

Step 2: Apply Styling Product

Apply a small amount of styling product to your fingertips, such as a hair wax or pomade. Work the product through your hair, focusing on the ends and the roots to create volume and texture.

Step 3: Style Your Hair

Using your fingers or a comb, style your hair into the desired shape. You can create spikes all over your head or focus on specific sections, such as the front or the crown of your head. Experiment with different shapes and styles until you find the one that works best for you.

Tips for Caring for Naturally Spiky Hair

If you have naturally spiky hair, it’s important to care for it properly to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

Tip 1: Use a Gentle Shampoo and Conditioner

Choose a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for your hair type. Look for products that are gentle and won’t strip your hair of its natural oils.

Tip 2: Avoid Overstyling Your Hair

While it can be tempting to style your hair into spikes every day, this can be damaging to your hair over time. Try to limit the amount of styling you do and give your hair a break whenever possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I straighten my naturally spiky hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your hair using a flat iron or other styling tool. However, keep in mind that this can be damaging to your hair over time, so it’s best to limit the amount of heat styling you do.

Q: Is naturally spiky hair more prone to frizz?

A: Yes, because of the texture of naturally spiky hair, it can be more prone to frizz and flyaways. Combat this by using a smoothing serum or oil on your hair before styling.

Hair Care for Naturally Spiky Hair

In conclusion, naturally spiky hair is a unique and edgy trait that can be styled in a variety of ways to create a modern and trendy look. By caring for your hair properly and using the right products and styling techniques, you can make the most of this unique characteristic and create a look that is all your own.
