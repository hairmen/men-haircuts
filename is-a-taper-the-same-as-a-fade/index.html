Is A Taper The Same As A Fade

Description

When it comes to men's hairstyles, there are a lot of terms that can be confusing for those who are not familiar with them. Two of the most common terms that are often used interchangeably are taper and fade. So, is a taper the same as a fade?

The short answer is no, but they are similar. Both styles involve cutting the hair shorter on the sides and back, while leaving the hair on top longer. The main difference between the two is how the hair is faded or tapered from the top to the sides and back.

How to

If you're looking to get a taper or a fade, it's important to communicate with your barber or hairstylist to make sure you get the look you want. Here are some tips on how to achieve both styles:

Taper

A taper involves gradually cutting the hair shorter from the top to the sides and back, creating a blended look. To get a taper, follow these steps:

  1. Decide on the length you want for the top of your hair.
  2. Ask your barber or hairstylist to use clippers to cut the sides and back of your hair, starting with a longer guard and gradually switching to a shorter one.
  3. Blend the hair between the top and sides using scissors or clippers.

Fade

A fade involves cutting the hair very short on the sides and back, gradually fading the hair to a longer length on the top of the head. To get a fade, follow these steps:

  1. Decide on the length you want for the top of your hair.
  2. Ask your barber or hairstylist to use clippers to cut the sides and back of your hair at a shorter length than the top.
  3. Gradually fade the hair from the sides and back to the top using a shorter guard on the clippers.

Step by Step

If you're still unsure about the difference between a taper and a fade, here is a step-by-step guide for each:

Taper

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Decide on the length you want for the top of your hair.
  3. Use clippers to cut the sides and back of your hair, starting with a longer guard and gradually switching to a shorter one.
  4. Blend the hair between the top and sides using scissors or clippers.
  5. Use scissors to trim any longer pieces of hair that are sticking out.
  6. Style your hair as desired.

Fade

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Decide on the length you want for the top of your hair.
  3. Use clippers to cut the sides and back of your hair at a shorter length than the top.
  4. Gradually fade the hair from the sides and back to the top using a shorter guard on the clippers.
  5. Use scissors to trim any longer pieces of hair that are sticking out.
  6. Style your hair as desired.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting a taper or a fade:

  • Bring a picture of the style you want to show your barber or hairstylist.
  • Communicate clearly about the length and style you want.
  • Be patient - a good haircut takes time.
  • Take care of your hair by using quality products and getting regular trims.

FAQ

What is a taper?

A taper involves gradually cutting the hair shorter from the top to the sides and back, creating a blended look.

What is a fade?

A fade involves cutting the hair very short on the sides and back, gradually fading the hair to a longer length on the top of the head.

Can I get a taper and a fade at the same time?

Yes, it is possible to combine a taper and a fade for a unique look.

Hair Care

After getting a taper or a fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the style. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a styling product to keep your hair in place.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.
  • Avoid over-styling your hair with heat tools, as this can cause damage.
