How To Set Hairstyle According To Face A Comprehensive Guide

How to Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in setting a hairstyle according to your face shape is to determine your face shape. You can do this by standing in front of a mirror and using a tape measure to measure the following:

  • Forehead: Measure the width of your forehead at the widest point (usually halfway between your eyebrows and your hairline).
  • Cheeks: Measure the width of your cheeks at the widest point (usually across your cheekbones).
  • Jawline: Measure the width of your jawline at the widest point (usually below your ears).
  • Face Length: Measure the distance from the center of your hairline to the tip of your chin.

Once you have these measurements, you can use the following guide to determine your face shape:

  • Round: If your face length and width are almost equal, and your jawline is rounded, you have a round face.
  • Square: If your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are all about the same width, and your jawline is square, you have a square face.
  • Oval: If your face length is longer than your forehead and your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, you have an oval face.
  • Heart-Shaped: If your forehead is wider than your jawline, and your chin comes to a point, you have a heart-shaped face.
  • Long: If your face length is the longest measurement, and your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are all about the same width, you have a long face.
  • Diamond-Shaped: If your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, and your forehead and jawline are narrower, you have a diamond-shaped face.

Hairstyles for Round Faces

If you have a round face, you want to create the illusion of length and angles. To do this, you should opt for hairstyles that add height and volume at the crown of your head, and avoid styles that add width to the sides of your face.

Some great hairstyles for round faces include:

  • Long layers
  • Side-swept bangs
  • High ponytails
  • Top knots
  • Asymmetrical bobs

Hairstyles for Square Faces

If you have a square face, you want to soften your strong jawline and create the illusion of length. To do this, you should opt for hairstyles that add volume to the top of your head and length to your face.

Some great hairstyles for square faces include:

  • Long layers
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Loose waves
  • Shoulder-length bobs
  • Center parts

Hairstyles for Oval Faces

If you have an oval face, you are lucky! Oval faces are considered the "ideal" face shape and can wear almost any hairstyle. However, you want to avoid hairstyles that add too much height at the crown of your head, as this can make your face look longer.

Some great hairstyles for oval faces include:

  • Long layers
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Pixie cuts
  • Bobs of all lengths
  • Updos

Hairstyles for Heart-Shaped Faces

If you have a heart-shaped face, you want to minimize the width of your forehead and create balance between your forehead and chin. To do this, you should opt for hairstyles that add volume to your jawline and chin.

Some great hairstyles for heart-shaped faces include:

  • Long layers
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Chin-length bobs
  • Messy updos
  • Beachy waves

Hairstyles for Long Faces

If you have a long face, you want to create the illusion of width and balance. To do this, you should opt for hairstyles that add volume to the sides of your face and avoid styles that add height at the crown of your head.

Some great hairstyles for long faces include:

  • Side-swept bangs
  • Chin-length bobs
  • Wavy or curly hair
  • Half-up, half-down styles
  • Low ponytails

Hairstyles for Diamond-Shaped Faces

If you have a diamond-shaped face, you want to minimize the width of your cheekbones and create balance between your forehead and chin. To do this, you should opt for hairstyles that add volume to the top of your head and the sides of your face.

Some great hairstyles for diamond-shaped faces include:

  • Side-swept bangs
  • Long layers
  • Updos
  • Bob haircuts
  • Curly or wavy hair

Tips for Choosing the Right Hairstyle

Now that you know the best hairstyles for your face shape, here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Consider your hair texture and thickness
  • Think about your lifestyle and how much time you want to spend styling your hair
  • Bring pictures of hairstyles you like to your stylist
  • Experiment with different styles and don't be afraid to try something new!

FAQs

Q: Can I wear any hairstyle regardless of my face shape?

A: While you can technically wear any hairstyle you want, certain styles may not flatter your face shape as well as others. By choosing a hairstyle that complements your face shape, you can enhance your natural beauty and look your best!

Q: How often should I get my hair cut?

A: It's recommended to get a haircut every 6 to 8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I change my face shape with a different hairstyle?

A: While a hairstyle can create the illusion of a different face shape, it won't actually change the shape of your face.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you know how to choose the right hairstyle for your face shape, here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water for healthy hair from the inside out

By following these tips and choosing a hairstyle that complements your face shape, you can look and feel your best every day!
