How To Cut Your Own Curtain Bangs A Step By Step Guide

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle without committing to a major chop, curtain bangs might be the perfect solution. This versatile style frames your face and can be customized to suit any face shape or hair type. But, going to the salon can be expensive, and sometimes you just want to take matters into your own hands. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your own curtain bangs at home.

What are Curtain Bangs?

Curtain bangs are a type of fringe that are longer on the sides and shorter in the middle. They're called "curtain" bangs because they frame your face like a pair of curtains. This style has been popular for decades, and it's easy to see why: it's versatile, low-maintenance, and can be styled in many different ways.

How to Cut Your Own Curtain Bangs

While we always recommend going to a professional stylist for major haircuts, cutting your own curtain bangs is definitely doable. All you need is a pair of sharp scissors and a steady hand. Here's how to do it:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb out any tangles and clip the rest of your hair back, leaving only the section you want to cut as your bangs.
  2. Decide how long you want your bangs to be. For most people, the sweet spot is around the middle of the nose, but you can go shorter or longer depending on your preference.
  3. Section off a small triangle of hair in the middle of your forehead. This will be the shortest part of your bangs.
  4. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and twist it once or twice. This will create a natural-looking point to cut.
  5. Using sharp scissors, cut the hair at an angle, following the shape of the twisted section. Be sure to cut slowly and carefully, checking your progress frequently.
  6. Release the twist and comb the hair straight down. Check to make sure the length is even, and make any necessary adjustments.
  7. Now it's time to create the "curtain" effect. Take a small section of hair on each side of the middle section and angle the scissors upward as you cut. This will create longer pieces on the sides, which will frame your face.
  8. Continue cutting small sections of hair on each side, making sure to keep the length gradually longer as you move away from the center.
  9. When you're happy with the length and shape of your bangs, use a comb to style them into place.
  10. Voila! You now have gorgeous curtain bangs.

Tips for Cutting Curtain Bangs

Cutting your own bangs can be intimidating, but with these tips, you'll be a pro in no time:

  • Start with longer hair than you think you want. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Use sharp scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. Dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Don't cut your bangs straight across. This can create a harsh, unflattering look. Instead, cut at an angle to create a softer, more natural shape.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting hair can be tiring, and it's important to stay focused and steady.

FAQ

Can I cut my own bangs if I have curly hair?

Absolutely! Just be sure to cut your bangs when your hair is dry and in its natural state. This will help you see how your curls fall and ensure that your bangs are the right length.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! It's always better to err on the side of caution and cut less than you think you need. If you do make a mistake, wait a few days for your hair to grow out a bit, and then try again.

How often should I trim my curtain bangs?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how long you want your bangs to be. As a general rule, aim to trim your bangs every six to eight weeks to keep them looking fresh and healthy.

Hair Care for Curtain Bangs

Once you've cut your curtain bangs, you'll want to take good care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your bangs clean and free of oil and buildup.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't weigh down your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, which can damage your hair and make your bangs look frizzy.
  • Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to your bangs to keep them smooth and shiny.

Cutting your own curtain bangs can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to achieve the perfect look for your face shape and style. Plus, you'll save money and time by doing it yourself. Give it a try, and see how curtain bangs can transform your look!
