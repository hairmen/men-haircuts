Hairstyles For Thin Faces Over 60 Tips And Tricks

Written by Luffy Sep 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have a thin face and are over the age of 60, finding the right hairstyle can be challenging. As we age, our hair thins out, and our faces lose volume, which can make it difficult to choose a flattering hairstyle. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll take you through some of the best hairstyles for thin faces over 60. So let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Choose the Right Hairstyle
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Hairstyle
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

How to Choose the Right Hairstyle

When choosing a hairstyle for a thin face over 60, there are a few things to keep in mind:

1. Consider the Length

Short haircuts are often the best option for women with thin faces over 60. Short hairstyles can add volume to thinning hair and draw attention away from the face. However, if you prefer longer hair, consider a layered cut to add volume.

2. Look for Volume

Hairstyles with volume are ideal for thin faces over 60. Consider adding layers or using hot rollers to add volume to your hair. Avoid flat hairstyles that can make your face appear even thinner.

3. Take Your Face Shape into Account

When choosing a hairstyle, it's essential to consider your face shape. If you have a thin face, avoid hairstyles that are too long or too short. Look for hairstyles that add width to your face, such as a bob or a layered cut.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Hair

Once you've chosen the right hairstyle for your thin face over 60, it's time to style your hair. Follow these steps for a sleek and stylish look:

1. Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products designed for thinning hair. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and fullness to your hair.

2. Blow Dry Your Hair

Once you've washed your hair, gently towel dry it and blow-dry it using a round brush. Use the brush to lift your hair at the roots, creating volume and fullness.

3. Use Hot Rollers

If you want to add more volume to your hair, consider using hot rollers. Roll your hair in small sections, starting at the crown of your head, and work your way down to the ends. Leave the rollers in for 10-15 minutes before removing them.

4. Style and Finish

Once you've added volume to your hair, you can style it as desired. Use a hairspray to hold your style in place and add shine to your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hairstyle

Now that you've chosen the perfect hairstyle for your thin face over 60, it's important to maintain it. Follow these tips to keep your hair looking its best:

1. Use the Right Products

Use products designed for thinning hair, such as volumizing shampoo and conditioner, to add body and fullness to your hair. Avoid heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.

2. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular haircuts are essential for maintaining a healthy and stylish hairstyle. Visit your hairstylist every six to eight weeks to keep your hair in shape.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools, such as a curling iron or straightener, to protect your hair from damage.

FAQs

Q: What are the best hairstyles for thin faces over 60?

A: Short hairstyles, such as a bob or a pixie cut, are often the best option for women with thin faces over 60. Look for hairstyles with volume and avoid flat hairstyles that can make your face appear even thinner.

Q: How can I add volume to my thinning hair?

A: You can add volume to your hair by using hot rollers, blow-drying your hair with a round brush, or using volumizing products, such as mousse or hairspray.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

Thin hair requires special care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Follow these hair care tips to keep your thin hair in great shape:

1. Use the Right Products

Use products designed for thinning hair, such as volumizing shampoo and conditioner, to add body and fullness to your hair. Avoid heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.

2. Be Gentle When Brushing Your Hair

Be gentle when brushing your hair, as thin hair is more fragile and prone to breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools, such as a curling iron or straightener, to protect your hair from damage.

With these tips and tricks, you can find the perfect hairstyle for your thin face over 60 and keep your hair looking its best. Remember to choose a hairstyle with volume, use the right products, and protect your hair from damage.
