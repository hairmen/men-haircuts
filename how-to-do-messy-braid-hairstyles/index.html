How To Do Messy Braid Hairstyles

Written by Eveline Nov 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a fun and easy way to switch up your hairstyle, then messy braid hairstyles are the way to go. Not only are they effortless and chic, but they're also perfect for any occasion. Whether you're heading to work, a night out with friends, or a wedding, messy braid hairstyles can be dressed up or down to suit any outfit.

How to do Messy Braid Hairstyles

The key to perfecting messy braid hairstyles is to embrace the imperfections. Unlike traditional braids, messy braids are meant to be a little rough around the edges, so don't worry about making it perfect. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start braiding, make sure your hair is clean and dry. If you have curly or frizzy hair, it's best to straighten it first to make it easier to work with. Apply a small amount of texturizing spray or mousse to add volume and texture to your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into three parts, starting at the crown of your head. Use clips or hair ties to secure the other sections so they don't get in the way.

Step 3: Start Braiding

Take the section of hair closest to your face and divide it into three equal parts. Cross the right section over the middle section, then cross the left section over the middle section. Continue this pattern, adding more hair from each section as you go, until you reach the nape of your neck.

Step 4: Mess it Up

Once you've reached the nape of your neck, you can start to mess up your braid. Gently tug on the sections of your braid to loosen them up and create a messy, undone look. Don't worry about making it perfect - the messier, the better!

Step 5: Secure Your Braid

When you've finished braiding, secure the end of your braid with a hair tie. To make your braid look even messier, gently pull on the strands of your braid to loosen them up.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you perfect your messy braid hairstyle:

  1. Prep your hair with texturizing spray or mousse.
  2. Section your hair into three parts.
  3. Start braiding, adding more hair from each section as you go.
  4. Mess up your braid by gently tugging on the sections to create a messy, undone look.
  5. Secure the end of your braid with a hair tie.

Tips for Perfecting your Messy Braid

Here are some tips to help you perfect your messy braid hairstyle:

  • Use a texturizing spray or mousse to add volume and texture to your hair.
  • Start with clean, dry hair to make it easier to work with.
  • Don't worry about making it perfect - messy braids are meant to look a little rough around the edges.
  • Loosen up your braid by gently tugging on the sections to create a messy, undone look.
  • Accessorize your braid with hairpins, flowers, or ribbons to add some extra flair.

FAQs

Q. Can messy braid hairstyles be worn for formal events?

A. Yes, messy braid hairstyles can be dressed up or down to suit any occasion. For formal events, you can add some extra accessories like flowers or jewels to your braid for a more glamorous look.

Q. How long does it take to do a messy braid hairstyle?

A. It usually takes around 10-15 minutes to do a messy braid hairstyle, depending on the length and thickness of your hair.

Q. Will a messy braid hairstyle work on short hair?

A. Yes, messy braid hairstyles can work on short hair too. Just make sure to use hairpins or clips to secure any loose strands in place.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful:

  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can damage the strands and cause split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, like bleach or hair dye, as they can cause damage and breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to perfect your messy braid hairstyle in no time. Whether you're running errands or attending a fancy event, this versatile hairstyle is perfect for any occasion. So go ahead and embrace the imperfections - messy braids are the new chic!
