How To Maintain Edges With Crochet Braids Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Oct 17, 2023 · 3 min read

Crochet braids are a popular protective hairstyle that involves adding extensions to the natural hair using a crochet needle. One of the biggest concerns when it comes to crochet braids is how to maintain the edges, as they are the most delicate part of the hair. In this article, we’ll share some tips and tricks on how to maintain edges with crochet braids.

Table of Contents

  • How to Maintain Edges with Crochet Braids
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Crochet Braids

How to Maintain Edges with Crochet Braids

The key to maintaining edges with crochet braids is to be gentle and avoid putting too much tension on the hairline. Here are some general tips to keep in mind:

  • Don’t pull the braids too tight
  • Use a light hold edge control or gel to smooth down the edges
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products on the edges
  • Protect the edges at night by wearing a satin bonnet or using a satin pillowcase

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to maintain edges with crochet braids:

  1. Start by gently brushing the edges with a soft-bristled brush or a toothbrush.
  2. Apply a small amount of edge control or gel to the edges and smooth down with your fingers or a brush.
  3. Use a scarf or headband to hold the edges down while the product dries.
  4. Once the edges are dry, remove the scarf or headband and style as desired.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help maintain edges with crochet braids:

  • Avoid using heavy oils or products on the edges, as this can weigh down the hair and cause breakage.
  • Be gentle when brushing or styling the edges, as they are more delicate than the rest of the hair.
  • Protect the edges at night by wearing a satin bonnet or using a satin pillowcase.
  • If you notice any signs of breakage or thinning, take a break from crochet braids and focus on hair care and growth.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about maintaining edges with crochet braids:

Q: Can I wear crochet braids with thinning or damaged edges?

A: It’s best to avoid wearing crochet braids if you have thinning or damaged edges, as the added weight and tension can exacerbate the problem.

Q: How often should I wash my crochet braids?

A: It’s best to wash your crochet braids every 2-3 weeks to avoid buildup and maintain scalp health.

Q: How long can I wear crochet braids?

A: Crochet braids can be worn for up to 8 weeks, but it’s important to pay attention to how your hair is reacting and remove them if you notice any signs of damage or breakage.

Hair Care for Crochet Braids

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy edges with crochet braids. Here are some tips:

  • Moisturize regularly with a lightweight leave-in conditioner or oil
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products on the scalp, as this can cause buildup and clog pores
  • Protect the hair at night by wearing a satin bonnet or using a satin pillowcase
  • Take breaks between styles to allow the hair to rest and recover

By following these tips and tricks, you can maintain healthy edges while enjoying the versatility and convenience of crochet braids. Remember to be gentle, avoid putting too much tension on the hairline, and prioritize hair care and maintenance.
