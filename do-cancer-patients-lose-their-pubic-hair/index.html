Do Cancer Patients Lose Their Pubic Hair

Written by Oliver Jun 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

When it comes to cancer, one of the most common questions patients have is whether or not they will lose their hair. While hair loss is a well-known side effect of many cancer treatments, there is less information available about whether or not pubic hair is affected. In this article, we will explore the question "Do cancer patients lose their pubic hair?" and provide tips for managing hair loss during cancer treatment.

What Causes Hair Loss During Cancer Treatment?

Before we dive into whether or not cancer patients lose their pubic hair, it's important to understand why hair loss occurs during cancer treatment. Chemotherapy drugs are designed to target rapidly dividing cells, which are found in cancer cells but also in hair follicles. This means that chemotherapy can damage hair follicles and cause hair to fall out. Radiation therapy can also cause hair loss in the area being treated.

Do Cancer Patients Lose Their Pubic Hair?

The short answer is yes, cancer patients can lose their pubic hair. Just like the hair on the scalp, pubic hair is also made up of rapidly dividing cells and can be affected by chemotherapy or radiation therapy. While some patients may not experience pubic hair loss, it is a possible side effect of treatment.

How to Manage Pubic Hair Loss During Cancer Treatment

While losing pubic hair may not be as noticeable as losing hair on the scalp, it can still be a distressing side effect for many cancer patients. Here are some tips for managing pubic hair loss during cancer treatment:

Consider hair removal before treatment

If you are about to start cancer treatment and are concerned about losing your pubic hair, you may want to consider removing it before treatment begins. This can make hair loss less noticeable and can also make it easier to manage as it grows back.

Use gentle hair removal methods

If you do decide to remove your pubic hair before treatment, be sure to use gentle hair removal methods such as shaving or waxing. Avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive methods that can irritate the skin.

Keep the area clean and dry

During treatment, it's important to keep the pubic area clean and dry to avoid skin irritation or infection. Use a mild soap and pat the area dry with a soft towel.

Avoid tight clothing

Tight clothing can rub against the skin and cause irritation, which can be especially uncomfortable if you are experiencing pubic hair loss. Wear loose-fitting clothing to avoid this.

FAQs

Does everyone lose their pubic hair during cancer treatment?

No, not everyone will experience pubic hair loss during cancer treatment. However, it is a possible side effect.

Will my pubic hair grow back after treatment?

Yes, in most cases pubic hair will grow back after cancer treatment is finished. However, it may take several months for hair to regrow.

Is there anything I can do to prevent pubic hair loss during cancer treatment?

Unfortunately, there is no guaranteed way to prevent hair loss during cancer treatment. However, some patients have had success with scalp cooling systems or other methods that aim to reduce the amount of chemotherapy that reaches the hair follicles.

Pubic Hair Care After Cancer Treatment

Once your cancer treatment is finished and your pubic hair begins to grow back, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for pubic hair care after cancer treatment:

Avoid harsh chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your pubic hair, such as hair dyes or bleach. These can irritate the skin and damage the hair.

Use a gentle shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo to cleanse your pubic hair. Look for a product that is formulated for sensitive skin.

Avoid tight clothing

As mentioned earlier, tight clothing can rub against the skin and cause discomfort. Opt for loose-fitting clothing to avoid this.

Moisturize the skin

Keep the skin in the pubic area moisturized to avoid dryness and irritation. Use a fragrance-free moisturizer to avoid further irritation.

Conclusion

While losing pubic hair may not be the most well-known side effect of cancer treatment, it is a possibility for some patients. If you are concerned about pubic hair loss, there are steps you can take to manage it and make it less noticeable. Remember to take care of your pubic hair properly after treatment to ensure it grows back healthy and strong.
