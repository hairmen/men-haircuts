How Do You Cut Long Layers Into Your Own Hair

Written by Oliver Jun 18, 2023 · 4 min read

Long layers are a popular hairstyle that can add volume, movement, and texture to your hair. However, getting a haircut at a salon can be expensive, and scheduling an appointment can be time-consuming. If you want to give yourself a long layer haircut, you can do it at home with the right tools, technique, and patience. In this blog post, we will discuss how to cut long layers into your own hair.

How to Cut Long Layers into Your Own Hair

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have never done it before. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can give yourself a long layer haircut at home. Here are the steps to follow:

Step by Step Guide

  1. Wash and dry your hair: Start by washing and drying your hair. It's easier to cut clean and dry hair than wet or dirty hair.
  2. Brush your hair: Brush your hair to detangle and remove any knots.
  3. Divide your hair into sections: Use a comb to divide your hair into four equal sections - two in the front and two in the back.
  4. Clip the sections: Use hair clips to secure each section of hair to prevent them from mixing up while cutting.
  5. Decide on the length of layers: Decide on the length of the layers you want. It's important to have a clear idea of the length you want before cutting to avoid mistakes.
  6. Cut the first section: Start with one of the front sections. Take a small section of hair, hold it between your fingers, and cut it at the desired length. Repeat this process with the other sections.
  7. Blend the layers: After cutting each section, use a comb to blend the layers together. This will help to create a seamless and natural look.
  8. Check the length: Once you have cut all sections, check the length of your hair in the mirror to make sure it's even.
  9. Style your hair: Style your hair as desired. You can use a blow dryer, curling iron, or straightener to create your desired look.

Tips for Cutting Long Layers into Your Own Hair

Cutting long layers into your own hair may take some practice, but with these tips, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut:

  • Use sharp scissors: Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut. Use sharp scissors specifically designed for cutting hair.
  • Start with small sections: It's easier to control the length and shape of your layers with smaller sections of hair.
  • Cut less than you think you need: It's better to cut less hair than you think you need, and then go back and cut more if necessary. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Don't rush: Take your time when cutting your hair. It's better to take breaks and come back to it later than rush and make a mistake.

FAQs

Can I cut long layers into my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can still cut long layers into curly hair. However, it's important to follow the same steps as cutting straight hair and take into account the natural curl pattern of your hair.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always go to a salon and have a professional fix it. Alternatively, you can try to fix it yourself by cutting less hair or blending the layers better.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Hair Care after Cutting Long Layers into Your Own Hair

After cutting long layers into your own hair, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the health and style of your haircut. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the health of your hair and prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently give yourself a long layer haircut at home. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't hesitate to seek professional help if necessary.
