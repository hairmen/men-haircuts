How To Cut Your Hair With Scissors

Written by Eveline Aug 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our blog post on how to cut your hair with scissors. If you're looking to save money on haircuts or want to experiment with a new hairstyle, cutting your own hair with scissors can be a great option. In this post, we'll go through the steps of how to cut your hair with scissors, tips to make it easier, and answer some frequently asked questions. Let's get started!

How to Cut Your Hair with Scissors

The first step to cutting your hair with scissors is to gather your supplies. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips or ties to section off your hair. If you have long hair, you may also want to consider investing in hair cutting shears, which are specifically designed for cutting hair.

Once you have your supplies, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help make it easier to cut and ensure that your hair is clean and free from any styling products.

Step 2: Section Off Your Hair

Use your comb and hair clips or ties to section off your hair. Start by separating the top section of your hair from the bottom section. Then, divide the top section into smaller sections and clip or tie them up so they're out of the way.

Step 3: Trim the Ends

Take a small section of hair from the bottom section and comb it straight down. Hold the scissors vertically and snip off the ends. Repeat this process with small sections of hair until you've trimmed all the ends of the bottom section.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Release a section from the top section of your hair and comb it straight down. Hold the scissors at a 45-degree angle and snip into the hair to create layers. Repeat this process with each section of the top section until you've blended the layers.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your hair, take a look in the mirror and check your work. You may need to make some additional snips to even out any uneven sections.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair with Scissors

Cutting your hair with scissors can be tricky, especially if you're not used to doing it. Here are some tips to help make the process easier:

  • Use sharp scissors: Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut.
  • Take your time: Don't rush the process. Take your time to ensure that you're cutting your hair evenly.
  • Start with small snips: It's easier to take more hair off than to add it back on, so start with small snips and work your way up.
  • Don't cut wet hair: Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so it's best to cut your hair when it's dry.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair with regular scissors?

You can cut your own hair with regular scissors, but it's best to use scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair. Hair cutting shears are sharper and have a better grip, which makes them easier to use.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and what style you're going for. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic! You can always go to a professional stylist to fix it. Alternatively, you can try to fix it yourself by trimming the sections that are uneven.

Hair Care Tips

Cutting your hair with scissors can be a great way to save money and experiment with new hairstyles, but it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly: This will help keep your hair clean and free from any styling products.
  • Use a conditioner: Conditioner can help keep your hair soft and prevent tangles.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools: Heat styling tools can damage your hair, so it's best to avoid them if possible.
  • Get regular trims: Getting regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

That's it for our post on how to cut your hair with scissors. We hope you found this helpful and are ready to give it a try! Remember to take your time, start with small snips, and always use sharp scissors. Happy cutting!
