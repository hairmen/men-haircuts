How To Cut My Hair Around My Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Dec 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle or simply give your hair a fresh look, cutting your hair around your face can be a great option. However, it can be intimidating to cut your own hair, especially if you're not a professional stylist. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about how to cut your hair around your face, from the tools you need to the steps to follow. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  1. Tools Needed
  2. Section Your Hair
  3. Start Cutting
  4. Blend the Layers
  5. Tips for Cutting Your Hair Around Your Face
  6. FAQs
  7. Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

Tools Needed

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips to section your hair
  • A mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp so they can cut your hair evenly. You can find hair-cutting scissors at your local beauty supply store or online.

Section Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Once it's dry, use a comb to part your hair where you want to cut it. If you're not sure where to part your hair, a good rule of thumb is to part it where you usually wear it. Use clips to section off the hair you're not cutting.

Next, take a small section of hair from the front of your face. Start with a small section so you can see how it looks before you cut more. Comb the hair straight down in front of your face.

Start Cutting

Using your scissors, make small cuts at an angle, starting from the bottom of the hair and working your way up. Take your time and make small cuts until you've reached your desired length. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.

Once you're happy with the length, move on to the next section of hair. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you've cut around your entire face.

Blend the Layers

To make sure your layers blend together seamlessly, take a small section of hair from the side of your face and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. This will help blend the layers together.

Repeat this process on the other side of your face, then check to make sure the layers are even. If not, go back and make small cuts until everything looks even.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair Around Your Face

Here are a few tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with a small section of hair and make small cuts to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it will look shorter when it dries.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress as you go.
  • If you're not confident in cutting your own hair, consider asking a friend or family member to help.

FAQs

Here are a few frequently asked questions about cutting hair around the face:

Q: How much hair should I cut?

A: It depends on your personal preference. Start with a small section and make small cuts until you've reached your desired length.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to change up your look. Most people get their hair cut every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

After you've cut your hair around your face, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and avoid pulling or tugging on it.

Now that you know how to cut your hair around your face, you can give yourself a fresh new look anytime you want. Just remember to take your time and make small cuts, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
