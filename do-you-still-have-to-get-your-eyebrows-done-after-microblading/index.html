Do You Still Have To Get Your Eyebrows Done After Microblading

Written by Aprilia Jul 30, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you considering getting microblading done for your eyebrows? Or have you already had the procedure done and are wondering if you still need to get your eyebrows done regularly? Here’s a guide to help you navigate through the world of microblading and eyebrow maintenance.

Description

Microblading is a semi-permanent tattooing technique where a small handheld tool made up of tiny needles is used to deposit pigment into the top layer of the skin. This results in the appearance of fuller, more defined eyebrows. The procedure typically lasts anywhere from 1-3 years before a touch-up is needed.

How to

The microblading process typically involves a consultation to determine the desired shape and color of the eyebrows. The technician will then use the handheld tool to create small, hair-like strokes that mimic the natural growth of eyebrow hair. The process can take anywhere from 1-2 hours depending on the technician and the complexity of the desired look.

Step by Step

1. Consultation with technician to determine desired shape and color of eyebrows

2. Numbing cream is applied to the area to minimize discomfort

3. Technician uses a handheld tool to create small hair-like strokes of pigment into the top layer of the skin

4. Aftercare instructions are provided to ensure proper healing and longevity of the procedure

Tips

It is important to do your research and find a reputable and experienced technician to perform the microblading procedure. Also, be sure to follow the aftercare instructions provided by the technician to ensure proper healing and longevity of the results.

FAQ

How long does microblading last?

The results of microblading typically last anywhere from 1-3 years before a touch-up is needed.

Does microblading hurt?

Numbing cream is applied to the area prior to the procedure to minimize discomfort. Some clients may still experience mild discomfort, but it is typically well-tolerated.

Can I still get my eyebrows done after microblading?

While microblading can result in fuller, more defined eyebrows, some clients may still choose to get their eyebrows done regularly to maintain a desired shape or color.

How do I care for my eyebrows after microblading?

Aftercare instructions typically include avoiding getting the eyebrows wet for the first few days, avoiding sun exposure, and avoiding picking or scratching at the area. It is also important to apply the provided aftercare ointment as directed.

Hair Care

It is important to note that microblading can cause some temporary hair loss in the eyebrow area due to the trauma of the procedure. However, this is typically minimal and hair growth typically returns within a few months. It is also important to avoid waxing or threading the eyebrow area for at least 2 weeks prior to the procedure and to avoid these methods of hair removal during the healing process.

In conclusion, while microblading can result in fuller, more defined eyebrows, some clients may still choose to get their eyebrows done regularly to maintain a desired shape or color. It is important to do your research and find a reputable and experienced technician to perform the procedure and to follow the aftercare instructions provided to ensure proper healing and longevity of the results.
