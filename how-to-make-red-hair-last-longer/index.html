How To Make Red Hair Last Longer Tips And Tricks

Written by Eveline Jun 22, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're one of the lucky ones with fiery red hair, you know that it can be a bit of a challenge to maintain that vibrant color. Between fading and the natural growth of your roots, it can be difficult to keep your locks looking their best. The good news is, there are plenty of ways to make red hair last longer. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for keeping your red hair looking fabulous for as long as possible.

How to Make Red Hair Last Longer

Tip #1: Use Color-Safe Shampoo

One of the most important things you can do to make your red hair last longer is to use a color-safe shampoo. Regular shampoos can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause your color to fade more quickly. Look for shampoos specifically designed for colored hair, and avoid shampoos that contain sulfates or harsh chemicals.

Tip #2: Avoid Hot Water

When you wash your hair, be sure to avoid hot water. Hot water can open up your hair cuticles and cause your color to fade more quickly. Instead, use lukewarm or cool water to rinse your hair.

Tip #3: Use a Deep Conditioner

Red hair can be particularly prone to dryness and damage, so it's important to use a deep conditioner regularly. Look for conditioners that contain ingredients like argan oil or shea butter, which can help to nourish and protect your hair.

Tip #4: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can be very damaging to your hair, and can cause your color to fade more quickly. Try to avoid using heat styling tools like curling irons and straighteners as much as possible. If you do need to use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray.

Tip #5: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can be very damaging to your hair, and can cause your color to fade more quickly. If you're going to be spending time in the sun, be sure to wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner that contains SPF.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Red Hair Last Longer

Step 1: Choose the Right Shampoo

Start by choosing a color-safe shampoo that's specifically designed for red hair. Look for shampoos that contain gentle, nourishing ingredients like argan oil or keratin.

Step 2: Condition Your Hair Regularly

Use a deep conditioner at least once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished. Look for conditioners that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, or olive oil.

Step 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. If you do need to use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage to your hair.

Step 4: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

If you're going to be spending time in the sun, be sure to wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner that contains SPF to protect your hair from damage.

Tips for Maintaining Red Hair

Tip #1: Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Tip #2: Use a Color-Boosting Treatment

If you're noticing that your color is starting to fade, try using a color-boosting treatment. These treatments can help to enhance the vibrancy of your color and make it last longer.

Tip #3: Avoid Chlorine

Chlorine can be very damaging to your hair, and can cause your color to fade more quickly. If you're going to be swimming in a pool, be sure to wear a swim cap or use a clarifying shampoo to remove any chlorine buildup.

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my red hair?

A: It's generally best to wash your hair every 2-3 days, depending on how oily your scalp is. Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils and cause your color to fade more quickly.

Q: Can I dye my hair red at home?

A: While it's possible to dye your hair red at home, it's generally best to visit a professional stylist. Red can be a difficult color to get right, and a stylist can help to ensure that you get the perfect shade for your skin tone and hair type.

Q: Will using a purple shampoo help to make my red hair last longer?

A: No, purple shampoo is designed for blonde or gray hair, and won't have any effect on red hair. Stick to using a color-safe shampoo that's specifically designed for red hair.

Hair Care Tips for Redheads

Tip #1: Protect Your Hair from the Elements

Red hair can be particularly prone to damage from wind, sun, and other environmental factors. Be sure to protect your hair by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner that contains SPF.

Tip #2: Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Try to avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or peroxide. These chemicals can be very damaging to your hair, and can cause your color to fade more quickly.

Tip #3: Embrace Your Natural Texture

Red hair can be particularly beautiful when left to its natural texture. Embrace your curls or waves, and use products that enhance your natural texture rather than trying to straighten or smooth your hair.

Conclusion

Red hair can be a bit of a challenge to maintain, but with the right care and attention, you can keep your locks looking fabulous for as long as possible. Follow these tips and tricks, and you'll be able to make your red hair last longer and look its best.
