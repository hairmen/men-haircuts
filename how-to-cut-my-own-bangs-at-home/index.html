How To Cut My Own Bangs At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 18, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of having to go to the salon every time you need to trim your bangs? Or maybe you're just looking for a way to save some money on haircuts? Whatever your reason may be, cutting your own bangs at home is a great skill to have. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process step-by-step, so you can achieve the perfect bangs every time.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Bangs
  • Step-by-Step Guide

  • FAQ

How to Cut Your Own Bangs

Before you start cutting your bangs, there are a few things you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Once you have these items, you're ready to start!

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own bangs:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you get a better idea of how your bangs will fall naturally.

Step 2: Section Off Your Bangs

Use a comb to section off your bangs. Make sure they're even and straight.

Step 3: Wet Your Bangs

Use a spray bottle filled with water to wet your bangs. This will help them stay in place while you're cutting.

Step 4: Decide on the Length

Decide on the length you want your bangs to be. It's always best to start off longer and gradually cut more if needed.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Hold your scissors vertically and cut small sections at a time. Make sure you're cutting straight across.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

After each cut, comb your bangs down to check your progress. If they're too long, continue cutting small sections until you reach the desired length.

Step 7: Finish Up

Once you're happy with the length, use the scissors to add texture to your bangs. This will help them blend in better with the rest of your hair.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Bangs

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your own bangs:

  • Always start off longer than you think you want. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Don't cut your bangs straight across. Add texture to make them look more natural.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring!

FAQ

Can I cut my bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can still cut your own bangs if you have curly hair. Just make sure you cut them when they're dry, so you can see how they'll naturally fall.

What should I do if I mess up?

If you mess up, don't panic! You can always pin your bangs back or wear a headband until they grow out. Or, if you're really unhappy with the results, you can always go to a salon and have them fix it.

Hair Care After Cutting Bangs

Once you've cut your own bangs, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Use a heat protectant if you're going to use heat tools on your bangs.
  • Don't touch your bangs too much. The oils from your fingers can make them look greasy.
  • Get regular trims to keep your bangs looking fresh.

Cutting your own bangs can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's totally doable. Just remember to take your time, start off longer than you think you want, and use sharp scissors for best results. Good luck!
