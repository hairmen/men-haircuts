How To Contour Inverted Triangle Face Tips Step By Step Guide And Hair Care

Written by Robby Sep 18, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you struggling with how to contour an inverted triangle face? This face shape is characterized by a wider forehead and narrower chin. Fortunately, with the right techniques and tools, you can create a balanced, symmetrical look. In this post, we'll show you how to contour an inverted triangle face shape, including tips, step by step guide and hair care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Contour an Inverted Triangle Face

What is an Inverted Triangle Face?

An inverted triangle face is characterized by a wider forehead and narrower chin. The cheekbones may also be prominent, giving the face a triangular shape. This face shape is also known as heart-shaped, due to the resemblance to the shape of a heart when turned upside down. If you have an inverted triangle face, you can use contouring to create a more balanced look.

How to Contour an Inverted Triangle Face

Contouring an inverted triangle face involves using makeup to create the illusion of a more balanced, symmetrical face shape. The goal is to create shadows and highlights that draw attention away from the wider forehead and towards the chin and jawline.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide on how to contour an inverted triangle face:

  1. Start by applying foundation to your face, using a shade that matches your skin tone.
  2. Apply a darker shade of foundation or contour powder to the temples and the sides of the forehead, blending well.
  3. Apply the same darker shade of foundation or contour powder to the area under the cheekbones, blending well.
  4. Apply a highlighter or lighter shade of foundation to the center of the forehead, down the bridge of the nose, and the chin.
  5. Blend the highlighter into the rest of the foundation, making sure there are no harsh lines.
  6. Use a contour brush to blend the darker shade of foundation or contour powder into the hairline, temples and under the cheekbones.
  7. Apply a neutral blush to the apples of your cheeks, blending upwards towards the temples.
  8. Finish with setting powder to keep the contouring in place.

Tips for Contouring an Inverted Triangle Face

Here are some tips to help you contour an inverted triangle face:

  • Use a matte contour powder, rather than a shimmery highlighter, to create the illusion of shadows.
  • Blend well to avoid harsh lines and make the contouring look natural.
  • Choose a blush that complements your skin tone and the contouring shades you've used.
  • Use a smaller brush to apply contouring shades to the sides of the nose, if desired.
  • Practice makes perfect! Experiment with different shades and techniques to find what works best for you.

Hair Care for Inverted Triangle Face

The right hairstyle can also help balance an inverted triangle face. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid hairstyles that add volume to the forehead, such as bangs or high buns.
  • Choose hairstyles that add volume to the chin and jawline, such as layered cuts or side-swept bangs.
  • Avoid hairstyles that are too long or flat, as they can make the face look longer.
  • Consider a center part, as it can help create the illusion of a more symmetrical face shape.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find what works best for your face shape and hair type.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can contouring change the shape of my face permanently?

No, contouring is a makeup technique that creates the illusion of a more balanced face shape. It does not permanently change the shape of your face.

2. What are some other tips for contouring an inverted triangle face?

Some other tips include using a light hand when applying contouring shades, using a blending sponge or brush to blend well, and choosing shades that complement your skin tone and natural features.

3. Can I use cream contour products instead of powders?

Yes, cream contour products can work well for an inverted triangle face shape. Just be sure to blend well and use a setting powder to keep the contouring in place.

With these tips and techniques, you can create a more balanced, symmetrical look for your inverted triangle face shape. Happy contouring!
