How Long Does Postpartum Hair Loss Last

Written by Aprilia Jun 08, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Postpartum hair loss is a common occurrence for new moms. This condition is a result of hormonal changes that happen during and after pregnancy. It can be quite alarming to see clumps of hair falling out, but rest assured, it is a normal part of the postpartum process. In this article, we will discuss how long postpartum hair loss lasts and what you can do to manage it.

Causes of Postpartum Hair Loss

During pregnancy, the hormones in a woman's body change to support the growth and development of the baby. These hormonal changes can also affect hair growth. The increased levels of estrogen during pregnancy can cause hair to remain in the growth phase for longer, resulting in thicker, fuller hair. However, after giving birth, the levels of estrogen in the body drop, causing the hair to enter the resting phase and eventually fall out. This is why postpartum hair loss typically occurs a few months after giving birth.

Symptoms of Postpartum Hair Loss

The symptoms of postpartum hair loss are pretty straightforward. You may notice more hair falling out when you brush or wash your hair. You may also notice thinning hair, especially around the temples and crown of your head. In some cases, you may even experience bald patches.

Tips for Managing Postpartum Hair Loss

While postpartum hair loss is a natural part of the process, there are some things you can do to manage it:

  • Be gentle with your hair. Avoid pulling it back tightly or using harsh chemicals.
  • Consider a shorter haircut. Shorter hair can make it look fuller and thicker.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet. Proper nutrition can help support hair growth.
  • Get enough rest. Lack of sleep can contribute to hair loss.
  • Consider taking a hair supplement. Biotin and other vitamins can help support healthy hair growth.

Hair Care Tips for Postpartum Hair Loss

In addition to the tips above, there are some things you can do to care for your hair during postpartum hair loss:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates.
  • Avoid heat styling tools. Blow drying, flat ironing, and curling can damage hair and make it more prone to breakage.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet. Wet hair is more fragile and can break more easily.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush. A wide-tooth comb is gentler on hair and can help prevent breakage.

FAQs About Postpartum Hair Loss

How long does postpartum hair loss last?

Postpartum hair loss typically lasts for about 6 months to a year. However, it can vary from person to person.

Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

Postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented, but there are things you can do to manage it. See the tips above for more information.

Will my hair grow back after postpartum hair loss?

Yes, your hair will grow back after postpartum hair loss. It may take some time, but with proper care and nutrition, your hair should return to its pre-pregnancy state.

Postpartum hair loss can be alarming, but it is a normal part of the postpartum process. By following the tips above and taking care of your hair, you can manage this condition and help your hair grow back healthy and strong.
