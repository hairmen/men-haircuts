How To Make A Chignon With Medium Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 10, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're looking for an elegant and sophisticated hairstyle that's perfect for any occasion, a chignon might be just what you need. This classic updo is ideal for medium-length hair and can be easily styled at home. In this guide, we'll show you how to make a chignon with medium hair, step by step. Whether you're a beginner or a pro, you'll be able to create a stunning chignon in no time!

Table of Contents

  • How to Prep Your Hair
  • How to Create a Basic Chignon
  • How to Add a Twist to Your Chignon
  • Tips for Styling a Chignon
  • FAQs About Chignons
  • Hair Care Tips for Medium-Length Hair

How to Prep Your Hair

Before you start styling your chignon, it's important to prep your hair properly. Start by washing your hair and applying a lightweight conditioner to keep your locks smooth and manageable. Once your hair is dry, brush it thoroughly to remove any tangles or knots.

Next, apply a small amount of styling mousse or gel to your hair to add texture and hold. This will help your chignon stay in place throughout the day. If you have particularly fine or thin hair, you may want to tease the hair at the crown of your head to add volume.

How to Create a Basic Chignon

Now that your hair is prepped and ready, it's time to create your chignon. Follow these steps:

  1. Gather your hair into a low ponytail at the nape of your neck.
  2. Twist the ponytail until it starts to coil around itself.
  3. Wrap the twisted ponytail around the base of the ponytail to create a bun.
  4. Secure the bun with bobby pins, making sure to tuck in any loose ends.
  5. Use hairspray to set the style in place.

Voila! You now have a beautiful, basic chignon. If you want to add a little extra flair to your hairstyle, read on to learn how to create a twisted chignon.

How to Add a Twist to Your Chignon

A twisted chignon is a stylish and modern take on the classic updo. Here's how to create it:

  1. Start by following steps 1-3 from the previous section to create a basic chignon.
  2. Take a section of hair from one side of your head and twist it tightly.
  3. Wrap the twisted section of hair around the bun, tucking the end into the bun to secure it.
  4. Repeat on the other side of your head with another section of hair.
  5. Use bobby pins to secure the twisted sections of hair in place, making sure they blend seamlessly into the bun.
  6. Finish with a spritz of hairspray to keep your twisted chignon in place.

That's it! You now have a beautiful, twisted chignon that's perfect for any occasion.

Tips for Styling a Chignon

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when styling a chignon:

  • Experiment with different placements for your chignon – it can be positioned high on your head, at the nape of your neck, or off to one side.
  • Use hair accessories like decorative pins or headbands to add some extra flair to your chignon.
  • If you're having trouble getting your chignon to stay in place, try using a hair donut or padding to create a larger base for your bun.
  • Don't be afraid to use hairspray liberally – it will help keep your chignon in place all day long.

FAQs About Chignons

Here are some frequently asked questions about chignons:

What type of hair is best for a chignon?

Medium-length hair that is at least shoulder-length is ideal for a chignon. However, you can also create a chignon with shorter hair by using hair extensions or padding to add volume and length.

How do I keep my chignon from falling out?

Make sure to use plenty of bobby pins and hairspray to secure your chignon in place. You can also use a hair net or hairpins to keep any loose strands in place.

Can I wear a chignon to a formal event?

Yes! Chignons are a classic and elegant hairstyle that are perfect for formal occasions. Dress up your chignon with decorative pins or headbands for a more glamorous look.

Hair Care Tips for Medium-Length Hair

Here are a few tips to keep your medium-length hair looking healthy and beautiful:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent it from becoming too dry.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling wands too often – they can cause damage and breakage.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide to creating a chignon, you'll be able to create a beautiful and elegant hairstyle that's perfect for any occasion. So go ahead and give it a try – you might just discover your new go-to look!
