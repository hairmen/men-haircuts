What To Wear Hiking In 60 70 Degree Weather A Comprehensive Guide

Are you planning a hiking trip in 60-70 degree weather? It's important to dress properly so you can enjoy your hike comfortably and safely. In this guide, we'll go over everything you need to know about what to wear hiking in this temperature range.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Dress for Hiking in 60-70 Degree Weather
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Hiking in 60-70 Degree Weather
  • FAQ

Description

60-70 degree weather is considered mild to cool, but it can still feel warm when you're hiking. The temperature can change quickly, so it's important to be prepared with layers. You'll want to dress in a way that keeps you comfortable while also protecting you from the sun, wind, and rain.

How to Dress for Hiking in 60-70 Degree Weather

Dressing in layers is key for hiking in 60-70 degree weather. You'll want to wear moisture-wicking clothing that keeps you cool and dry as you sweat. Start with a base layer that's made of a breathable material, such as polyester or merino wool.

Next, add a mid-layer that provides insulation, such as a fleece or down jacket. This layer should be easy to remove if you start to get too warm.

Finally, add an outer layer that protects you from the elements. Choose a waterproof and windproof jacket that's breathable so you don't overheat.

What to Wear

  • Base Layer: Moisture-wicking shirt and pants
  • Mid-Layer: Fleece or down jacket
  • Outer Layer: Waterproof and windproof jacket
  • Footwear: Hiking boots or shoes with good traction
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, hat, sunscreen, and insect repellent

What Not to Wear

  • Cotton clothing (it holds onto moisture and can make you cold)
  • Sandals or flip flops
  • Heavy or bulky clothing

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to dress for hiking in 60-70 degree weather:

  1. Start with a moisture-wicking base layer that's made of polyester or merino wool.
  2. Add a mid-layer that provides insulation, such as a fleece or down jacket.
  3. Choose a waterproof and windproof jacket for your outer layer.
  4. Wear hiking boots or shoes with good traction.
  5. Accessorize with sunglasses, a hat, sunscreen, and insect repellent.
  6. Bring a backpack to carry extra layers, water, and snacks.
  7. Check the weather forecast and adjust your layers accordingly.
  8. Remember to remove or add layers as needed during your hike.
  9. Stay hydrated and take breaks when needed.
  10. Enjoy your hike!

Tips for Hiking in 60-70 Degree Weather

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when hiking in 60-70 degree weather:

  • Check the weather forecast before you go and prepare for changes in temperature.
  • Wear comfortable and well-fitting clothing to avoid chafing and blisters.
  • Break in your hiking boots or shoes before your trip.
  • Bring a map, compass, and/or GPS device to navigate on the trail.
  • Pack plenty of water and snacks to stay fueled and hydrated.
  • Be aware of wildlife and follow Leave No Trace principles.
  • Tell someone your hiking plans and when you expect to return.

FAQ

Q: Can I wear shorts while hiking in 60-70 degree weather?

A: Yes, you can wear shorts, but make sure they're made of a moisture-wicking material and that you're comfortable with the temperature. Just remember to bring extra layers in case the temperature drops.

Q: Do I need a hat while hiking in 60-70 degree weather?

A: It's a good idea to wear a hat to protect your face and scalp from the sun. Choose a hat that's made of a breathable material and has a wide brim for maximum coverage.

Hair Care

Don't forget to protect your hair while hiking! Here are some tips:

  • Wear a hat or headband to protect your scalp from the sun.
  • Tie your hair back to keep it out of your face and prevent tangles.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to prevent damage from the wind and sun.
  • Bring a brush or comb to smooth out tangles.
  • Pack dry shampoo or baby powder to absorb oil and sweat.

Now that you know what to wear hiking in 60-70 degree weather, you're ready to hit the trail! Remember to dress in layers, wear comfortable footwear, and bring plenty of water and snacks. Stay safe and have fun!
