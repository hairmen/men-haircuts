How Do You Cut Your Own Hair At The Back

Written by Christine Aug 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to save some money and cut your own hair at home, one of the most challenging areas to tackle is the back of your head. It can be difficult to see what you're doing, and mistakes can be costly. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-quality look from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your own hair at the back, step by step.

Description

Many people are hesitant to cut their own hair, especially at the back. However, with the rising costs of salon visits, more and more people are choosing to take matters into their own hands. Whether you're looking to trim your ends or give yourself a complete haircut, this article will provide you with the information you need to achieve great results.

How To

The first thing you'll need to do is gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a handheld mirror. You may also want to invest in a trimmer or clippers, depending on the style you're going for. Once you have your tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Clean Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see what you're doing and ensure a smooth cut.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair

Using the comb, divide your hair into sections. Start by parting it down the middle and then creating a horizontal part at the back of your head. Clip the top section out of the way.

Step 3: Use the Mirror

Hold the handheld mirror up to the back of your head so you can see what you're doing. It may take some practice to get the hang of this, but it's essential for achieving a good cut.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Using the scissors, start cutting your hair. Begin with small sections and work your way up. Be sure to hold the hair between your fingers and cut at an angle for a more natural look.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After each section, check your progress in the mirror. This will help you ensure that you're cutting evenly and not missing any spots. If you notice any mistakes, don't panic. You can always trim it up later.

Step 6: Repeat

Repeat this process until you've cut all the sections. If you're going for a more layered look, you may need to create additional horizontal parts and repeat the process.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair:

  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to take small sections and work your way up.
  • Be patient. Cutting your own hair can be challenging, so take your time and don't rush.
  • Invest in quality scissors. This will make a big difference in the outcome of your cut.
  • Watch tutorials. There are plenty of tutorials online that can help you learn new techniques and styles.
  • Ask for help. If you're struggling to see the back of your head or need some guidance, ask a friend or family member to help you.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair:

Is it hard to cut your own hair?

It can be challenging, especially if you're new to it. However, with practice and patience, you can achieve great results.

What tools do I need to cut my own hair?

You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a handheld mirror. Depending on the style you're going for, you may also want to invest in a trimmer or clippers.

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Cutting curly hair can be more challenging, but it's still possible. Be sure to cut it when it's dry and take small sections to avoid any unevenness.

Hair Care

After you've finished cutting your hair, it's essential to take care of it properly. Be sure to wash and condition it regularly, use a heat protectant if you're using hot tools, and trim it every few months to keep it healthy.

Cutting your own hair at the back can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve great results. Remember to be patient, take your time, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. With practice, you'll be able to master the art of cutting your own hair and save yourself some money in the process.
