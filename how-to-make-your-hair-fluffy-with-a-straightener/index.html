How To Make Your Hair Fluffy With A Straightener

Written by Bardi Nov 21, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of limp, lifeless hair? Do you want to add some volume and bounce to your locks? Look no further than your trusty hair straightener. With a few simple tricks and techniques, you can use this versatile tool to give your hair the fluffy, full-bodied look you crave. In this article, we'll show you how to make your hair fluffy with a straightener, step by step.

  • How to Make Your Hair Fluffy with a Straightener
  • Step by Step
  • Tips

Step by Step

Before we get started, it's important to prep your hair properly. Start by washing and conditioning your hair as you normally would. Then, towel-dry your hair until it's just damp. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair to prevent damage from the straightener.

1. Section your hair: Divide your hair into small sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't miss any spots.

2. Straighten your hair: Starting from the roots, clamp the straightener down on each section of hair and gently pull the straightener down to the ends. Repeat this process on each section until all of your hair is straightened.

3. Flip your hair: Once your hair is straightened, flip your head upside down and shake your hair out. This will create some volume and texture in your hair.

4. Backcomb your hair: Take a small section of hair from the crown of your head and backcomb it by teasing it with a comb. This will create some lift and volume at the roots.

5. Finish with hairspray: To make your fluffy hair last all day, finish with a light mist of hairspray. This will help hold the volume and prevent your hair from falling flat.

Tips

- Use a straightener with ceramic plates, which will distribute heat evenly and prevent hot spots that can cause damage to your hair.

- Don't use too much product, as this can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.

- Experiment with different techniques, such as curling your hair with the straightener, to create different textures and styles.

FAQ

Q: Can I make my hair fluffy with a straightener if I have short hair?

A: Yes, you can still use a straightener to create volume in short hair. Just be sure to use small sections and backcomb the hair at the roots for added lift.

Q: How often should I use a straightener on my hair?

A: It's best to limit the use of a straightener to once or twice a week, as frequent heat styling can damage your hair and cause breakage.

Hair Care

Remember to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. This includes regular trims to prevent split ends, using a deep conditioning treatment once a week, and avoiding excessive heat styling and chemical treatments. With proper care and a little help from your straightener, you can achieve the fluffy, voluminous hair of your dreams.
