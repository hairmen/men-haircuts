How To Cut A Choppy Bob Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Nov 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

In need of a new haircut that's both trendy and easy to style? Look no further than the choppy bob. This short and layered haircut has been a favorite among celebrities and fashionistas for years, and for good reason. Not only is it versatile, but it's also low maintenance and perfect for any hair type.

How to Cut a Choppy Bob Haircut

A choppy bob is all about layers and texture, so it's important to have the right tools and technique. If you're not confident in your cutting abilities, it's always best to visit a professional stylist. However, if you're feeling brave and want to give it a try yourself, here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Sectioning clips
  • Comb
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prep the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair. Comb through any tangles and make sure the hair is completely dry. Then, use your spray bottle to dampen the hair slightly. This will help you to see the natural texture and movement of the hair.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Using your comb, create a center part and then section the hair into four equal parts. Clip each section out of the way, leaving one section at the back of the head to work on first.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Starting at the back of the head, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut into the hair, creating a choppy and textured effect. Continue working your way up the head, taking small sections and cutting into them until you reach the crown of the head.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

Next, move on to the sides of the head. Take a small section of hair from the front of the head and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut into the hair, creating a choppy and textured effect. Continue working your way back, taking small sections and cutting into them until you reach the back of the head.

Step 5: Add Finishing Touches

Once you've finished cutting all the hair, go back through and make any final adjustments. Use your fingers to create more texture and movement if needed, and trim any pieces that look too long or out of place.

Tips for Cutting a Choppy Bob

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up. It's easier to add more texture and layers as you go than to try and fix a mistake later on.
  • Use a point-cutting technique to create a more textured and choppy look.
  • Don't be afraid to leave some pieces longer than others. The uneven look is what makes the choppy bob so trendy.
  • When in doubt, leave it to the professionals. A good stylist can give you the perfect choppy bob while keeping your hair healthy and damage-free.

FAQs

Can I cut a choppy bob on curly hair?

Absolutely! In fact, a choppy bob can look great on curly hair. Just be sure to cut the hair when it's dry so you can see the natural curl pattern.

What face shape does a choppy bob suit?

A choppy bob can suit most face shapes, but it's especially flattering on those with a heart-shaped or oval face.

How often do I need to trim a choppy bob?

It's recommended to get a trim every 8-10 weeks to keep your choppy bob looking fresh and healthy.

Hair Care Tips for a Choppy Bob

Now that you've got your choppy bob, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as this can damage your hair and make it look frizzy.
  • Invest in a good texturizing spray or dry shampoo to enhance the choppy effect and make styling easier.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your choppy bob looking fresh and healthy.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to give yourself a trendy and low-maintenance choppy bob haircut in no time. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun with it!
