How To Pick A Hairstyle For Your Face Female

Written by Eveline Dec 02, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you looking for a new hairstyle but not sure which one will suit your face shape? Don’t worry, we’ve got you covered! In this article, we’ll guide you through the process of picking a hairstyle that flatters your face shape and enhances your natural beauty. Let’s get started!

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in picking a hairstyle is to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes:

  • Round
  • Oval
  • Square
  • Heart
  • Diamond
  • Rectangle

To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and take a close-up selfie or stand in front of a mirror and trace the outline of your face with a dry-erase marker. Look at the overall shape of your face and compare it to the descriptions above.

Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, your face is equal in width and length with soft, curved lines. Your cheeks are the widest part of your face.

Oval Face Shape

Oval face shapes are longer than they are wide with a gentle curve at the jawline. The forehead and jawline are about the same width.

Square Face Shape

Square face shapes have a strong, angular jawline and forehead with a width that is almost equal to the length of the face.

Heart Face Shape

Heart face shapes have a wide forehead and a narrow chin with a pointy jawline. The face is wider at the temples than at the jawline.

Diamond Face Shape

Diamond face shapes are widest at the cheekbones and have a narrow forehead and jawline.

Rectangle Face Shape

Rectangle face shapes are longer than they are wide with a strong, square jawline and forehead. The face is about the same width at the temples and jawline.

Hairstyle Ideas for Different Face Shapes

Now that you’ve determined your face shape, let’s look at some hairstyle ideas that will flatter your features.

Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, choose hairstyles that add height and volume to the crown of your head. Long layers and side-swept bangs will elongate your face and make it appear slimmer. Avoid chin-length cuts and blunt bangs.

Oval Face Shape

Oval face shapes can pull off almost any hairstyle, so have fun with it! Experiment with short, long, straight, or curly hair. A middle part will emphasize your symmetry, while side-swept bangs will soften your features.

Square Face Shape

If you have a square face shape, choose hairstyles that soften your angular features. Soft, layered cuts that fall below the jawline will flatter your face. Side-swept bangs and center parts will also soften your features.

Heart Face Shape

If you have a heart face shape, choose hairstyles that balance your features. Chin-length bobs and long, layered cuts with side-swept bangs will soften your forehead and jawline. Avoid short, blunt cuts and full bangs.

Diamond Face Shape

If you have a diamond face shape, choose hairstyles that balance your features. Long, layered cuts with side-swept bangs and chin-length bobs will flatter your face. Avoid short, blunt cuts and full bangs.

Rectangle Face Shape

If you have a rectangle face shape, choose hairstyles that add width to your face. Soft, layered cuts with side-swept bangs and chin-length bobs will flatter your features. Avoid long, straight hair that will elongate your face.

Tips for Choosing the Right Hairstyle

Here are some tips to keep in mind when choosing a hairstyle:

  • Consider your hair texture and thickness
  • Think about your lifestyle and how much time you have to style your hair
  • Bring pictures of hairstyles you like to your stylist
  • Be open to your stylist’s suggestions
  • Consider getting a consultation with a stylist who specializes in your hair type and face shape

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Hairstyle

Once you’ve picked a hairstyle, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair with a shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Protect your hair from environmental factors such as sun, wind, and pollution

With these tips in mind, you’re ready to pick a hairstyle that flatters your face shape and enhances your natural beauty. Happy styling!
