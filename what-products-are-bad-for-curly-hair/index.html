What Products Are Bad For Curly Hair

Written by Aprilia Aug 21, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that it can be challenging to find the right products that work for your hair type. Unfortunately, some hair products can do more harm than good, causing dryness, breakage, and frizz. In this blog post, we will discuss what products are bad for curly hair, and how you can avoid them.

Table of Contents

  • Silicones
  • Sulfates
  • Alcohol
  • Heat Tools
  • Brushes
  • Tips for Curly Hair Care

Silicones

Silicones are commonly found in hair products and are used to make hair appear smooth and shiny. However, they can be very damaging to curly hair. Silicones create a barrier around the hair shaft, preventing moisture from penetrating the hair. This can lead to dryness, breakage, and frizz. Look for products that are silicone-free, or use a clarifying shampoo to remove buildup from silicone-based products.

Sulfates

Sulfates are a type of detergent that are commonly found in shampoos. While they do a great job of cleaning hair, they can strip curly hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Look for sulfate-free shampoos that are gentle on curly hair.

Alcohol

Alcohol is commonly found in hair products and can be very damaging to curly hair. It can dry out hair, causing it to become brittle and prone to breakage. Look for products that are alcohol-free or contain low levels of alcohol.

Heat Tools

Heat tools such as flat irons and curling irons can be damaging to curly hair. The high heat can cause dryness and breakage, leading to frizz and split ends. If you must use heat tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using them every day.

Brushes

Brushes can be damaging to curly hair, as they can cause breakage and tangles. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair. This will help prevent breakage and keep your curls looking their best.

Tips for Curly Hair Care

Here are some tips for taking care of your curly hair:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Using a sulfate-free shampoo can help prevent dryness and breakage, keeping your curls looking healthy and shiny.

2. Deep condition regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help restore moisture to your hair, preventing dryness and breakage.

3. Use a diffuser

A diffuser attachment for your hair dryer can help prevent frizz and enhance your natural curls.

4. Avoid over-washing

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and breakage. Aim to wash your hair no more than twice a week.

5. Sleep on a satin pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help prevent frizz and breakage, as it is gentle on your hair.

FAQ

What ingredients should I avoid in hair products?

You should avoid silicones, sulfates, and alcohols in hair products. These ingredients can be very damaging to curly hair.

How often should I wash my curly hair?

You should aim to wash your curly hair no more than twice a week. This will help prevent dryness and breakage.

What can I do to prevent frizz?

You can prevent frizz by using a diffuser attachment for your hair dryer, sleeping on a satin pillowcase, and using hair products that are gentle on curly hair.

Conclusion

Curly hair requires special care and attention, and using the wrong products can be very damaging. By avoiding silicones, sulfates, and alcohols, and following the tips we've provided, you can keep your curls looking healthy and beautiful.
