How To Hide A Grown Out Undercut Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter Sep 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for ways to hide a grown out undercut? You're not alone. Many people who have tried this trendy hairstyle eventually get tired of it and want to grow it out. However, the process of growing out an undercut can be challenging. It can take months or even years for your hair to grow back to its original length. In the meantime, you may struggle to find ways to style your hair and hide the undercut. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to hide a grown-out undercut.

How to Hide a Grown Out Undercut: The Basics

Before we get into the tips and tricks, let's go over the basics of what an undercut is and why it can be challenging to grow out. An undercut is a hairstyle where the sides and back of your hair are shaved or cut very short, while the top is left long. This creates a dramatic contrast between the two lengths. While undercuts can look cool and edgy, they require a lot of upkeep. You need to get them trimmed regularly to maintain the short length, which can be expensive and time-consuming.

When you decide to grow out your undercut, you'll need to be patient. It can take several months for your hair to grow back to its original length. During this time, you may experience some awkward stages where your hair looks uneven or choppy. But don't worry, there are ways to hide these stages and make the transition smoother.

How to Hide a Grown Out Undercut: Step by Step

Here are some step-by-step tips on how to hide a grown-out undercut:

Step 1: Try a New Part

One of the easiest ways to hide an undercut is to try a new part. If you usually part your hair on the side, try parting it down the middle or on the opposite side. This will help to cover up the shaved section and create a new look.

Step 2: Use Hair Accessories

Hair accessories can be your best friend when it comes to hiding a grown-out undercut. Try using headbands, clips, or bobby pins to hold your hair in place and cover up any shaved sections. You can also experiment with scarves, hats, and beanies for a more stylish look.

Step 3: Use Hair Extensions

If you're really struggling to hide your undercut, consider using hair extensions. Extensions can add length and volume to your hair, making it easier to style and conceal any shaved sections. There are many different types of extensions to choose from, including clip-ins, tape-ins, and sew-ins.

Step 4: Experiment with Hairstyles

When you're growing out an undercut, it's essential to experiment with different hairstyles. Try braids, updos, and ponytails to see what works best for your hair type and length. You can also try curling or straightening your hair to create a more polished look.

Tips for Hiding a Grown Out Undercut

Here are some additional tips for hiding a grown-out undercut:

Tip 1: Be Patient

As we mentioned earlier, growing out an undercut takes time and patience. Don't get discouraged if your hair doesn't look perfect right away. Keep experimenting with different styles and be open to trying new things.

Tip 2: Get Regular Haircuts

While you're growing out your undercut, it's important to get regular haircuts. This will help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. It will also help to maintain a more polished look as you transition to longer hair.

Tip 3: Invest in Quality Hair Products

Investing in quality hair products can make a big difference when you're trying to hide a grown-out undercut. Look for products that are designed for your hair type and texture. Consider using a volumizing mousse or texturizing spray to add volume and texture to your hair.

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to grow out an undercut?

A: It can take several months to a year or more to grow out an undercut, depending on how short the shaved sections are and how fast your hair grows.

Q: Can I dye my hair while growing out an undercut?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair while growing out an undercut. However, be aware that dyeing your hair can make it more prone to breakage and damage.

Hair Care

As you grow out your undercut, it's essential to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

Tip 1: Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair regularly to remove dirt, oil, and product buildup. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type.

Tip 2: Use Heat Protectant

If you're using heat styling tools like curling irons or straighteners, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

Tip 3: Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles like braids or ponytails that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.

In conclusion, hiding a grown-out undercut can be challenging, but it's not impossible. By following these tips and tricks, you can make the transition to longer hair smoother and more manageable. Remember to be patient, experiment with different styles, and take good care of your hair.
