How To Get Short Layers On Long Hair

Written by Christine Jul 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to add some dimension and movement to your long hair, getting short layers can be a great option. Short layers can help to remove some of the weight and bulk from long hair, making it easier to manage and style. In this article, we'll go over how to get short layers on long hair, step-by-step, so you can achieve the look you want.

Description

Short layers on long hair can add volume and texture, creating a more dynamic and interesting look. They can also help to frame the face and bring attention to certain features. Whether you want a subtle change or a more dramatic transformation, getting short layers is a great way to switch up your hairstyle.

How to Get Short Layers on Long Hair

Step 1: Decide on the Length

The first step in getting short layers is to decide on the length you want them to be. Depending on the current length of your hair and the look you're going for, you may want to opt for shorter or longer layers. Talk to your stylist about your options and what will work best for your hair type and face shape.

Step 2: Find a Good Stylist

Once you've decided on the length and style you want, it's important to find a good stylist who can help you achieve the look you're after. Look for a stylist who has experience working with long hair and who can show you examples of their work.

Step 3: Schedule a Consultation

Before you get your hair cut, schedule a consultation with your stylist to discuss your desired look and any concerns you may have. This is a good opportunity to ask questions and get advice on how to maintain your new hairstyle.

Step 4: Prepare for Your Appointment

Before your appointment, make sure to wash and dry your hair so your stylist can see its natural texture and length. If you have any pictures or inspiration for your new hairstyle, bring them to your appointment to show your stylist.

Step 5: Get Your Hair Cut

During your appointment, your stylist will cut your hair into short layers. They may use scissors or a razor to create texture and movement. Be sure to communicate with your stylist throughout the process to ensure you're happy with the length and style.

Step 6: Style Your New Look

Once your hair is cut, your stylist will likely style it to show off your new layers. They may use products like mousse or hairspray to add volume and hold. If you're not sure how to style your new look at home, ask your stylist for tips and advice.

Tips

  • Consider getting a trim every six to eight weeks to maintain your short layers.
  • Use heat protectant products when styling your hair to prevent damage.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • If you have thin hair, be cautious about getting too many short layers, as this can make your hair look even thinner.

FAQ

Can I get short layers if I have curly hair?

Absolutely! Short layers can work well with curly hair, as they can help to define your curls and create more volume. Talk to your stylist about your options and how to style your curly hair with short layers.

Will short layers make my hair look thinner?

If you have thin hair, getting too many short layers can make your hair look even thinner. However, if you have thick hair, short layers can help to remove some of the bulk and make your hair easier to manage.

How do I maintain my short layers?

To maintain your short layers, get regular trims every six to eight weeks. Use high-quality hair products and avoid using too much heat when styling your hair. Talk to your stylist about any products or techniques that can help you maintain your new look.

Hair Care

Getting short layers can be a great way to switch up your hairstyle and add some dimension and movement to your long hair. However, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Use high-quality hair products, avoid using too much heat, and get regular trims to keep your hair looking its best.
