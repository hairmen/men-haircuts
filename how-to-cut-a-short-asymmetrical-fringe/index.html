How To Cut A Short Asymmetrical Fringe A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're in the mood for a bold new hairstyle, why not try cutting a short asymmetrical fringe? This edgy look is perfect for those who want to shake things up without committing to a full haircut. Here's everything you need to know about how to cut a short asymmetrical fringe.

Description

A short asymmetrical fringe is a hairstyle where the bangs are cut at an angle, creating an uneven look. This style is perfect for those who want to add a bit of edge to their hair without committing to a full haircut. It's also a great way to frame your face and draw attention to your eyes.

How to Cut a Short Asymmetrical Fringe

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you'll need a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean, as this will make the cutting process much easier.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Before you start cutting, you'll need to decide on the length of your fringe. Use the comb to section off the hair you want to cut, and use the mirror to get a better view of your face. Once you've decided on the length, mark it with your fingers.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Using the scissors, start cutting at the point you marked with your fingers. Cut at a slight angle, following the line of your eyebrows. Make sure you cut small sections at a time, as this will give you more control over the length.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

After you've cut a few sections, check your progress in the mirror. Make sure the length is even and that the angle is consistent. If you need to make any adjustments, do so gradually to avoid cutting too much off.

Step 5: Finish the Cut

Once you're happy with the length and angle, use the comb to comb out any tangles or uneven sections. If you want a more textured look, you can use the scissors to make small snips along the edges of the fringe.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you get the perfect short asymmetrical fringe:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut it shorter. This will give you more control over the length.
  • Use the mirror to get a better view of your face and the angle of the fringe.
  • Don't cut too much off at once. It's better to make small adjustments gradually.
  • Use the scissors to make small snips along the edges of the fringe for a more textured look.

FAQ

What face shape is best for a short asymmetrical fringe?

A short asymmetrical fringe can work with any face shape, but it's particularly flattering on those with oval or heart-shaped faces.

Can I cut a short asymmetrical fringe on my own?

While it's possible to cut a short asymmetrical fringe on your own, it's always best to consult with a professional hair stylist to ensure the best results.

Hair Care

Once you've cut your short asymmetrical fringe, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage the ends.
  • Use a small amount of hair oil to keep your fringe looking smooth and shiny.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the length and shape of your fringe.

Cutting a short asymmetrical fringe is a bold and edgy choice for those who want to shake up their hairstyle. With a few basic tools and some patience, you can achieve this look at home. Just remember to take your time and make small adjustments gradually, and you'll have a stunning new hairstyle in no time!
