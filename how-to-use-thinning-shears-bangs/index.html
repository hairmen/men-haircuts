How To Use Thinning Shears Bangs A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Oct 27, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Thinning shears are a valuable tool for hair stylists and enthusiasts alike. They can be used to add texture and volume to hair, as well as to thin out thick hair. Thinning shears are especially useful when it comes to bangs. In this article, we will go over how to use thinning shears on your bangs, step-by-step.

Description

Bangs can be a tricky hairstyle to master. They require constant maintenance and can easily become too thick or too thin. Thinning shears can help you achieve the perfect bangs by adding texture and volume, while also thinning out any areas that may be too thick. They are especially useful for those with thick hair or for those who want to achieve a more natural, effortless look.

How to Use Thinning Shears Bangs: Step by Step

Step 1: Prep Your Bangs

Before using thinning shears, make sure your bangs are dry and styled as you normally would wear them. This will help you see any areas that need to be thinned out and will give you a better idea of how much hair you can safely remove.

Step 2: Section Your Bangs

Starting at the center of your bangs, use a comb to section the hair you want to thin out. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and clip the rest of your bangs away from the section you will be working on.

Step 3: Use the Thinning Shears

Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle and make small, quick cuts into the section of hair. Start at the top of the section and work your way down, making sure to keep the shears at the same angle throughout. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven bangs.

Step 4: Repeat on the Other Side

Once you have thinned out one side of your bangs, repeat the process on the other side. Make sure to keep your bangs even by comparing the thickness of each side as you work.

Step 5: Blend Your Bangs

Once you have finished thinning out your bangs, use a comb to blend the thinned out sections with the rest of your bangs. This will create a natural, seamless look.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Bangs

- Start with small cuts and work your way up to larger ones. This will help you avoid cutting too much hair at once.

- Use the thinning shears sparingly. Thinning out too much hair can lead to uneven and sparse-looking bangs.

- Keep the shears at a 45-degree angle to achieve a natural, textured look.

- Use a comb to blend the thinned out sections with the rest of your bangs.

FAQ

Can you use thinning shears on any type of bangs?

Yes, thinning shears can be used on any type of bangs. However, they are especially useful for those with thick hair or for those who want to achieve a more natural, effortless look.

Do thinning shears damage hair?

Thinning shears do not damage hair if they are used correctly. However, it is important to use them sparingly and to make small, quick cuts.

Hair Care

After thinning out your bangs, it is important to maintain them with regular trims. This will help you avoid any uneven or sparse-looking areas. It is also important to use a heat protectant when using heat styling tools, as excessive heat can damage your hair.

In conclusion, thinning shears are a valuable tool for achieving the perfect bangs. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a natural, textured look that will enhance your overall hairstyle.
