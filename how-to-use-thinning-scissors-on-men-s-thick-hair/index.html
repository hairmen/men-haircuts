How To Use Thinning Scissors On Men s Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jun 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning scissors are a game-changer when it comes to taming men's thick hair. Thinning scissors are designed to remove bulk from the hair, creating a more natural and textured look. If you're struggling to manage your thick hair, thinning scissors might be the solution you're looking for. In this article, we'll go over how to use thinning scissors on men's thick hair in a simple step-by-step guide.

Description

Thinning scissors are a type of scissors that have teeth on one blade and a sharp edge on the other. The teeth are designed to remove hair, while the sharp edge cuts the hair. They are commonly used for thinning out thick hair, creating texture and removing bulk. Thinning scissors can be used on any hair type, but they are especially useful for thick hair as it can be difficult to manage and style.

How to Use Thinning Scissors on Men's Thick Hair

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start using thinning scissors, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Thinning scissors work best on dry hair, so make sure your hair is completely dry before you start.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable sections, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Use hair clips to keep the sections separate.

Step 3: Hold the Thinning Scissors Correctly

Hold the thinning scissors in your dominant hand with the teeth facing down. Hold the section of hair you want to thin with your other hand and position the scissors at a 45-degree angle.

Step 4: Begin Thinning Your Hair

Start at the bottom of the section and work your way up, using the thinning scissors to remove hair. Make sure to hold the scissors at a 45-degree angle and only cut small sections of hair at a time. It's better to cut less hair than too much, as you can always go back and thin more if needed.

Step 5: Repeat on Other Sections

Repeat the process on the other sections of your hair, working your way up to the top. Make sure to blend the sections together as you go, to create a natural and textured look.

Tips

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Take breaks if your hand gets tired, as using thinning scissors can be a bit more intensive than regular scissors.
  • Don't thin your hair too much, as it can create an uneven and choppy look.
  • Make sure to regularly clean and oil your thinning scissors to keep them in good condition.

FAQ

Can thinning scissors be used on any hair type?

Yes, thinning scissors can be used on any hair type, but they work especially well on thick hair.

How often should I use thinning scissors?

It depends on the individual and how fast their hair grows. Generally, it's recommended to use thinning scissors every 4-8 weeks.

Is it easy to use thinning scissors?

It can take some practice to get the hang of using thinning scissors, but with some patience and practice, anyone can use them.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair after using thinning scissors. Make sure to use a quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can damage it. Regular trims and maintenance can help keep your hair looking healthy and styled.

Thinning scissors can be a great tool to help manage men's thick hair. With some practice and patience, anyone can use them to create a natural and textured look. Remember to take care of your hair and use thinning scissors in moderation to avoid over-thinning and damage.
