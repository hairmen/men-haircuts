How To Flat Iron Your Hair With Volume

Written by Eveline Oct 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Flat ironing your hair can give you a sleek and polished look, but it often comes at the cost of volume. If you're someone who loves having voluminous hair, don't worry! You can flat iron your hair while still maintaining its volume. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks to help you achieve flat, straight hair with volume.

Table of Contents

  • How to Flat Iron Your Hair with Volume
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Keep in Mind
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Flat Ironed Hair

How to Flat Iron Your Hair with Volume

Before we dive into the guide, let's talk about some products you'll need to achieve voluminous, flat ironed hair:

  • Heat protectant spray or serum
  • Flat iron
  • Round brush
  • Root lifting spray or mousse

Now that you have your products ready, let's get started!

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

It's important to start with clean hair to avoid any buildup or residue that can affect the final look of your hair. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to give your hair some extra body.

Step 2: Apply a Heat Protectant

Before using any heat tools on your hair, it's crucial to apply a heat protectant spray or serum to protect your hair from damage. Spray the heat protectant all over your hair, making sure to cover each strand.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a round brush, blow dry your hair until it's completely dry. Make sure to lift your roots as you blow dry to create volume.

Step 4: Apply a Root Lifting Spray or Mousse

Once your hair is dry, apply a root lifting spray or mousse to the roots of your hair. This will give your hair some extra lift and volume.

Step 5: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable parts. This will make it easier to flat iron your hair and ensure that you don't miss any spots.

Step 6: Flat Iron Your Hair

Using your flat iron, straighten your hair in small sections. Make sure to pull the flat iron away from your scalp to create volume at the roots. Don't forget to use a comb or brush to help guide the flat iron through your hair.

Step 7: Finish with Hairspray

Once you've flat ironed your hair, finish with a light hold hairspray to keep your style in place.

Tips to Keep in Mind

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when flat ironing your hair with volume:

  • Don't flat iron your hair when it's wet. Make sure your hair is completely dry before using any heat tools.
  • Use a lower heat setting on your flat iron to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don't flat iron the same section of hair multiple times. This can lead to damage and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to detangle your hair before flat ironing.

FAQs

Can I flat iron my hair every day?

While it's not recommended to flat iron your hair every day, you can do it occasionally as long as you use a heat protectant and avoid using a high heat setting.

How long will my flat ironed hair last?

Your flat ironed hair can last anywhere from a few days to a week, depending on your hair type and how well you maintain it.

Can I flat iron my hair without using a heat protectant?

No, it's not recommended to flat iron your hair without using a heat protectant. Heat protectant sprays and serums help to minimize damage and breakage caused by heat styling tools.

Hair Care for Flat Ironed Hair

Now that you've flat ironed your hair with volume, it's important to take care of your hair to avoid any damage or breakage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid friction and breakage while you sleep.

With these tips and tricks, you can flat iron your hair while still maintaining its volume. Remember to use a heat protectant, section your hair, and use a root lifting spray or mousse for the best results. Happy styling!
