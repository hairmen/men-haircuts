How To Cut Thick Curly Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have thick, curly, and long hair, you know how difficult it can be to manage. And when it comes to cutting your hair, finding the right stylist who can handle your hair type can be a challenge. But don't worry, with a few tips and tricks, you can learn how to cut your thick curly long hair at home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process step-by-step.

Description

Thick curly long hair can be challenging to cut, but with the right techniques, you can achieve a beautiful haircut at home. In this guide, we'll provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to cut your hair, as well as tips on hair care and maintenance.

How to Cut Thick Curly Long Hair

Step 1: Gather your materials

Before you begin cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary materials. You'll need a pair of hair cutting scissors, hair clips, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. It's essential to use hair cutting scissors as they are sharper and will ensure a clean cut.

Step 2: Wash and detangle your hair

Wash your hair and let it air dry or use a diffuser to dry it gently. Once it's dry, detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb. This step will help you see the natural shape of your curls before you start cutting.

Step 3: Section your hair

Using hair clips, section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. This step will make it easier to cut your hair evenly and prevent you from cutting too much hair at once.

Step 4: Start cutting

Begin by cutting the ends of your hair. Take a small section of hair and twist it to create a defined curl. Use your scissors to cut the ends of the hair. Continue this process throughout all four sections of your hair, making sure to cut each section evenly.

Step 5: Check for symmetry

After you've trimmed all four sections, check for symmetry. Make sure both sides are even, and if not, go back and make adjustments.

Step 6: Blend the layers

If you want to add layers, use the same technique and cut small sections of hair at a time. Make sure to blend the layers into the rest of your hair to avoid a choppy look.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts, as it's easier to cut more hair than to add it back.
  • Take breaks to ensure you're not cutting too much hair.
  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors.
  • Don't be afraid to seek professional help if needed.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair while it's wet?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as it's easier to see the natural shape of your curls. However, if you must cut your hair while it's wet, make sure to cut less hair than you would if it were dry.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. It's best to leave it alone and seek professional help if needed. Remember, hair grows back, and mistakes can be fixed.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's essential to take care of it properly. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and avoid heat styling tools as much as possible. Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair and invest in a silk pillowcase to prevent breakage.

Cutting your thick curly long hair may seem daunting, but with these tips and tricks, you can achieve a beautiful haircut at home. Remember to take your time, start with small cuts, and seek professional help if needed. Happy cutting!
