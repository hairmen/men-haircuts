Starting Dreads With Shoulder Length Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Aug 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're thinking about starting dreads with shoulder length hair, congratulations! Dreads are a great way to express your individuality and style. However, you may be feeling overwhelmed by the process. Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll give you all the information you need to start your dreads with confidence.

How to Start Dreads with Shoulder Length Hair

There are many ways to start dreadlocks, but we recommend the backcombing method for shoulder length hair. Here are the steps you need to follow:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Clean hair will make the dreading process easier. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or excess oil.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using small hair ties or rubber bands. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the dreads you want.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Starting at the roots, backcomb each section of hair using a metal comb. This will create knots and tangles that will eventually form into dreads.

Step 4: Twist and Secure

Twist each section of hair clockwise and then wrap it around itself to create a dread. Secure the end with a small hair tie or rubber band. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Tips for Starting Dreads with Shoulder Length Hair

Here are some tips to make the dreading process easier and more successful:

Tip 1: Use a Dreadlock Wax

A dreadlock wax will help your hair lock together faster and make the process smoother. Be sure to choose a wax that is specifically designed for dreadlocks.

Tip 2: Be Patient

Dreads take time to form, especially with shoulder length hair. Be patient and avoid over-manipulating your hair.

Tip 3: Protect Your Dreads

Once your dreads start to form, be sure to protect them from damage. Avoid excessive heat and chemical treatments and sleep with a satin or silk pillowcase.

FAQs About Starting Dreads with Shoulder Length Hair

Can I Start Dreads with Relaxed Hair?

Yes, you can start dreads with relaxed hair. However, relaxed hair may take longer to lock and you may need to use additional products to help the process.

How Long Will it Take for My Dreads to Form?

The time it takes for your dreads to form will depend on your hair type and how well you take care of your hair. On average, it can take anywhere from six months to a year for dreads to fully form.

Can I Wash My Dreads?

Yes, you can wash your dreads. However, it's important to use a residue-free shampoo and avoid over-washing, as this can cause your dreads to unravel.

Hair Care for Dreads

Once your dreads have formed, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are some tips for keeping your dreads healthy:

Tip 1: Wash Your Dreads

Use a residue-free shampoo to wash your dreads once or twice a week. Be sure to rinse thoroughly to avoid buildup.

Tip 2: Avoid Using Products

Avoid using products that contain oils or heavy ingredients that can cause buildup and attract dirt.

Tip 3: Keep Your Scalp Healthy

A healthy scalp is important for healthy dreads. Massage your scalp regularly to promote blood flow and use a tea tree oil or peppermint oil spray to soothe any irritation.

Starting dreads with shoulder length hair may seem daunting, but with the right tools and knowledge, you can achieve the look you desire. Follow these tips and tricks and be patient, and you'll have beautiful dreads in no time.
