How To Do A High Skin Fade Haircut

Written by Bardi Oct 26, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and modern haircut, a high skin fade might be just what you need. This haircut is characterized by a gradual taper from the top of the head to the sides and back, with the hair getting shorter and shorter until it blends with the skin. In this article, we'll show you how to do a high skin fade haircut step by step, with tips and frequently asked questions to help you get the best results.

Description

A high skin fade is a popular haircut that features a short cut on the sides and back of the head, gradually blending into longer hair on top. The fade starts high on the head and tapers down to the skin, creating a sharp and clean look. This haircut is versatile and can be styled in many ways, from a textured crop to a slicked-back undercut.

How to

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need clippers with different guard sizes, scissors, a comb, and hair products like pomade or gel. Here's how to do a high skin fade haircut:

Step 1: Prep the Hair

Start by washing the hair and towel-drying it. Comb the hair to remove any tangles and make sure it's easy to work with. If the hair is too long, use scissors to trim it to a manageable length.

Step 2: Clipper the Sides

Attach a guard to your clippers and start by buzzing the hair on the sides and back of the head. The guard size will depend on how short you want the fade. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one as you move up the head. Remember to blend the hair as you go, using a slight flicking motion with the clippers.

Step 3: Blend the Fade

Once you've buzzed the hair with the clippers, it's time to blend the fade. Switch to a shorter guard and work your way up the head, leaving a smooth transition between the shorter hair and the longer hair on top. Use the comb to lift the hair and create a guide for where to blend the fade.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Now it's time to cut the hair on top of the head. Use scissors to create the desired length and style, whether it's a textured crop, a slicked-back undercut, or another trendy look. You can also use clippers to trim the hair around the ears and the neckline.

Step 5: Style the Hair

Finally, style the hair with your preferred products, whether it's pomade, gel, or wax. Use a comb or your fingers to create the desired look, whether it's a messy fringe, a side part, or a quiff. You can also add some hairspray to keep the style in place.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the best results with your high skin fade haircut:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Blend the fade gradually, taking your time to create a smooth transition between the shorter and longer hair.
  • Use scissors to create texture and movement on the top of the head, whether it's a choppy cut or a layered style.
  • Experiment with different hair products to find the right one for your hair type and desired style.
  • Visit a professional barber or hairstylist if you're not confident in doing a high skin fade haircut yourself.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about high skin fade haircuts:

What is a high skin fade?

A high skin fade is a haircut that features a short cut on the sides and back of the head, gradually blending into longer hair on top. The fade starts high on the head and tapers down to the skin.

How do you ask for a high skin fade?

To ask for a high skin fade, tell your barber or hairstylist that you want the hair on the sides and back of your head to be short and gradually blend into longer hair on top. Specify how high you want the fade to start and how short you want the hair to be.

What products should I use for a high skin fade?

For a high skin fade, you can use pomade, gel, wax, or hairspray to style the hair. Choose a product that works well with your hair type and desired style, and apply it evenly throughout the hair.

Hair Care

To maintain a high skin fade haircut, it's important to keep the hair clean and well-groomed. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are suitable for your hair type, and avoid over-washing or over-styling the hair. Visit your barber or hairstylist regularly for touch-ups and trims, and ask for advice on how to style and care for your hair.

By following these tips and steps, you can easily do a high skin fade haircut at home or visit your barber for your next appointment. This popular haircut is a stylish and versatile choice for men of all ages and hair types, and can be adapted to many different looks and styles. Experiment with different textures, lengths, and products to find the right high skin fade for you.
