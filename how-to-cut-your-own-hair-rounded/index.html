How To Cut Your Own Hair Rounded A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of going to the salon every few weeks just to maintain your rounded haircut? Why not try cutting it yourself at home? Not only will it save you time and money, but it can also be a fun and rewarding experience. In this article, we'll provide you with a comprehensive guide on how to cut your own hair rounded, including tips, tricks, and frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Rounded
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Successful Haircut
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After the Cut

How to Cut Your Own Hair Rounded

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's discuss the tools you'll need. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a handheld mirror. It's also helpful to have a larger mirror so you can see the back of your head.

Next, choose a comfortable and well-lit area to cut your hair. Make sure you have enough space to move around and that the floor is easy to clean.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start by washing and drying your hair. Comb through it to remove any tangles and knots.

2. Separate the top section of your hair from the bottom section using hair clips. This will make it easier to work on each section separately.

3. Take a small section of hair from the bottom section and comb it straight down.

4. Hold the scissors at an angle and start cutting the hair in a rounded shape. Be sure to cut small sections at a time and check your progress in the mirror.

5. Continue cutting the hair in small sections until the bottom section is complete.

6. Unclip the top section of your hair and repeat steps 3-5.

7. Next, blend the top and bottom sections together by cutting any uneven pieces.

8. Check your hair in the mirror to ensure it's the desired length and shape.

9. Finally, style your hair as desired.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

- Start with small sections of hair and cut a little bit at a time. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.

- Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and a jagged cut.

- Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and stressful, so it's important to rest when needed.

- Be patient and take your time. Cutting your own hair requires precision and attention to detail.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I cut my hair rounded if it's curly?

A: Yes, you can still achieve a rounded haircut with curly hair. It's important to cut it when it's dry and to use a diffuser when blow-drying to maintain the curls.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: If you make a mistake, don't panic. It's just hair and it will grow back. You can always go to a salon to have it fixed if needed.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. On average, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care After the Cut

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its shape and health. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type and texture. Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair. Lastly, be sure to get regular trims to keep your hair looking its best.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve a rounded haircut that looks salon-worthy. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
