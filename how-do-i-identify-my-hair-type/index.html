How Do I Identify My Hair Type

Written by Eveline Aug 30, 2023 ¬∑ 4 min read

If you are struggling to figure out what your hair type is, you are not alone. Many people are unaware of their hair type and what products work best for them. Identifying your hair type is important because it can help you choose the right hair care products, styling techniques and treatments to keep your hair healthy and beautiful.

Table of Contents

  • How to Identify Your Hair Type
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Identifying Your Hair Type
  • FAQs

How to Identify Your Hair Type

The first step in identifying your hair type is to understand the different types of hair. There are generally four types of hair: straight, wavy, curly, and kinky. Each hair type has its own unique characteristics that can help you determine which type of hair you have.

Straight Hair

Straight hair is characterized by its lack of curl or wave. It tends to be smooth and shiny, and is often the easiest hair type to manage. If your hair falls flat and lacks volume, you may have straight hair.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair has a slight curl or bend, and tends to be thicker and more voluminous than straight hair. If your hair has a natural wave or tends to curl slightly, you may have wavy hair.

Curly Hair

Curly hair has a distinct curl pattern and tends to be dry, frizzy, and prone to breakage. If your hair has a defined curl pattern, you likely have curly hair.

Kinky Hair

Kinky hair is tightly coiled and often has a coarse texture. It tends to be the driest hair type and requires a lot of moisture to stay healthy. If your hair has a tight curl pattern or appears to be tightly coiled, you likely have kinky hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you identify your hair type:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Examine your hair in the mirror and try to determine its natural curl pattern.
  3. Run your fingers through your hair to feel its texture. Is it coarse, fine, or somewhere in between?
  4. Take note of how your hair reacts to humidity. Does it frizz easily or stay relatively smooth?
  5. Consider how your hair responds to different products. Does it respond well to heavy oils and butters, or does it become weighed down and greasy?

Tips for Identifying Your Hair Type

Here are some additional tips to help you identify your hair type:

  • Get a second opinion from a hair stylist or friend who is knowledgeable about hair types.
  • Look for inspiration from people with similar hair types to yours. You can find pictures online or on social media.
  • Experiment with different hair care products to see what works best for your hair type.
  • Consider your hair's overall health. If your hair is dry, brittle, and prone to breakage, you may have kinky or curly hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about identifying hair types:

Q: Can my hair type change over time?

A: Yes, your hair type can change over time due to hormonal changes, aging, or chemical treatments.

Q: What if my hair has multiple curl patterns?

A: It is common for people to have multiple curl patterns on their head. Try to identify the predominant curl pattern to determine your hair type.

Q: Is it important to know my hair type?

A: Yes, identifying your hair type can help you choose the right hair care products and styling techniques to keep your hair healthy and looking its best.

Hair Care for Different Hair Types

Once you have identified your hair type, it is important to choose the right hair care products and treatments to keep your hair healthy and looking its best. Here are some general tips for caring for different hair types:

Straight Hair

Straight hair tends to be the easiest hair type to manage, but it can still benefit from regular deep conditioning treatments to keep it healthy and shiny.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair requires more moisture than straight hair to keep its natural curl pattern defined. Use a lightweight leave-in conditioner or curl cream to keep your waves looking their best.

Curly Hair

Curly hair requires a lot of moisture to stay healthy and defined. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and deep condition your hair regularly to keep it hydrated.

Kinky Hair

Kinky hair requires the most moisture of all hair types. Use a heavy moisturizing leave-in conditioner or hair butter, and avoid using heat styling tools that can dry out your hair.

By following these tips and identifying your hair type, you can choose the right products and treatments to keep your hair healthy and looking its best.
