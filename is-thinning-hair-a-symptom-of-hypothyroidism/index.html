Is Thinning Hair A Symptom Of Hypothyroidism

Written by Bardi Jun 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're experiencing hair loss, you may be wondering if it's related to an underlying medical condition. One condition that can cause hair loss is hypothyroidism, a condition in which your thyroid gland doesn't produce enough hormones. In this article, we'll explore the link between hypothyroidism and hair loss, and what you can do about it.

What is Hypothyroidism?

Hypothyroidism is a condition in which your thyroid gland doesn't produce enough hormones. These hormones are responsible for regulating your body's metabolism, which affects many bodily functions, including hair growth.

Hypothyroidism is more common in women than men, and it often develops after pregnancy or menopause. It can also be caused by an autoimmune disorder, radiation therapy, or surgery to remove the thyroid gland.

Is Thinning Hair a Symptom of Hypothyroidism?

Yes, hair loss is a common symptom of hypothyroidism. When your thyroid gland doesn't produce enough hormones, it can cause your hair to become thin, dry, and brittle. This is because the hormones that regulate hair growth are affected by the lack of thyroid hormones.

How Does Hypothyroidism Cause Hair Loss?

When you have hypothyroidism, your body's metabolism slows down, which affects many bodily functions, including hair growth. The hair follicles require a constant supply of nutrients and oxygen from the blood to grow and maintain healthy hair. When your metabolism slows down, the blood flow to the hair follicles decreases, which can lead to hair loss.

In addition, the lack of thyroid hormones can cause the hair follicles to enter a resting phase, which means they stop growing new hair. This can lead to hair thinning and hair loss.

How to Treat Hair Loss Caused by Hypothyroidism

If you're experiencing hair loss due to hypothyroidism, the first step is to see a doctor. Your doctor can perform a blood test to check your thyroid hormone levels and determine if you have hypothyroidism.

If you are diagnosed with hypothyroidism, your doctor may prescribe thyroid hormone replacement therapy. This involves taking a synthetic thyroid hormone to replace the hormones your thyroid gland isn't producing. Over time, this can help improve your hair growth and reduce hair loss.

In addition to thyroid hormone replacement therapy, there are other steps you can take to improve your hair health:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth. Make sure you're getting enough protein, iron, and zinc, as these nutrients are essential for healthy hair.

2. Manage Stress

Stress can contribute to hair loss, so it's important to manage your stress levels. Try relaxation techniques like yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises to help reduce stress.

3. Avoid Harsh Hair Treatments

Avoid harsh hair treatments like chemical relaxers, hot oil treatments, and excessive heat styling. These can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage and hair loss.

FAQ about Thinning Hair and Hypothyroidism

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after starting thyroid medication?

A: It can take several months for hair to grow back after starting thyroid medication. However, with treatment, most people see an improvement in their hair growth over time.

Q: Can hypothyroidism cause permanent hair loss?

A: In some cases, hypothyroidism can cause permanent hair loss. However, with treatment, most people see an improvement in their hair growth.

Q: Can hypothyroidism cause hair to turn gray?

A: Hypothyroidism doesn't directly cause hair to turn gray. However, it can cause premature graying in some people.

Hair Care Tips for People with Hypothyroidism

Here are some hair care tips for people with hypothyroidism:

1. Use a Mild Shampoo

Use a mild shampoo that's gentle on your hair and scalp. Avoid shampoos that contain harsh chemicals that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Condition Your Hair

Use a conditioner that's designed for your hair type to help keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Avoid excessive heat styling, as this can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage and hair loss. If you do use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to help minimize damage.

4. Brush Your Hair Gently

Use a soft-bristled brush to gently detangle your hair. Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.

5. Get Regular Haircuts

Getting regular haircuts can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Conclusion

Thinning hair can be a symptom of hypothyroidism, a condition in which your thyroid gland doesn't produce enough hormones. If you're experiencing hair loss, it's important to see a doctor to determine the underlying cause. With treatment, most people see an improvement in their hair growth over time.
