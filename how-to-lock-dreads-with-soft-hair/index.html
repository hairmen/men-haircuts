How To Lock Dreads With Soft Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking to lock your dreads but have soft hair? Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to lock dreads with soft hair. From the basics to expert tips, we'll help you achieve the perfect set of locks that you've been dreaming of.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Dreadlocks, commonly known as dreads, are a popular hairstyle that involves sectioning off hair and encouraging it to form into matted, rope-like strands. However, for those with soft hair, locking dreads can seem like a daunting task. Soft hair is typically finer and more fragile than other hair types, making it more difficult for the hair to lock on its own. But don't worry, with the right technique and care, you too can rock a head full of beautiful dreads.

How To

Before we dive into the step-by-step process, let's take a look at some of the tools and products you'll need to lock your dreads:

  • Dread wax or locking gel
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair bands
  • Shampoo and conditioner

Step by Step

Step 1: Section Your Hair

The first step to locking your dreads is to section your hair into small, even pieces. The size of the sections will depend on your hair type and the desired size of your dreads. Use clips or hair bands to keep each section separate.

Step 2: Apply Dread Wax or Locking Gel

Apply a small amount of dread wax or locking gel to each dread section. This will help to hold the hair together and encourage it to lock. Be sure to use a product that is specifically designed for locking hair, as regular hair products may not have the same effect.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Using a comb, begin to backcomb each section of hair. This involves combing the hair in the opposite direction of its natural growth, which will cause it to tangle and knot together. Be gentle with your hair, as too much force can cause breakage or damage.

Step 4: Roll Your Hair

Once the hair is backcombed, roll each section between your palms to encourage the hair to lock together. This will also help to smooth out any loose hairs or bumps in the dread.

Step 5: Continue the Process

Repeat these steps for each section of hair until your entire head is covered in dreads. This process may take several hours, depending on the length and thickness of your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect set of dreads:

  • Be patient - locking your hair can take time, so don't get discouraged if your dreads don't look perfect right away.
  • Avoid using too much product - using too much dread wax or locking gel can cause build-up and make your dreads look greasy or dirty.
  • Maintain your dreads - once your dreads are locked, be sure to maintain them by washing and conditioning them regularly. This will help to keep them clean and healthy.

FAQ

Can I lock my dreads without using product?

While it is possible to lock your dreads without using product, it can be more difficult, especially if you have soft hair. Using a locking gel or dread wax can help to hold the hair together and encourage it to lock more easily.

How often should I wash my dreads?

You should wash your dreads at least once a week to keep them clean and healthy. However, be sure to use a shampoo and conditioner that is specifically designed for locking hair, as regular hair products may cause build-up or damage.

Hair Care

Locking your dreads can be a great way to achieve a unique and stylish look. However, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo and conditioner that is specifically designed for locking hair.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can cause build-up and make your dreads look greasy or dirty.
  • Be gentle when combing or styling your hair, as too much force can cause breakage or damage.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a natural oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil, to keep it soft and healthy.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve the perfect set of dreads, even if you have soft hair. Remember to be patient and take care of your hair, and you'll be rocking a head full of beautiful locks in no time.
