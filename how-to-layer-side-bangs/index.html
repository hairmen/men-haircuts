How To Layer Side Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Nov 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to switch up your hairstyle with some side bangs? Layered side bangs can add volume and texture to your hair, framing your face and giving you a fresh new look. But how do you achieve the perfect layered side bangs? In this article, we'll guide you through the process step by step, including tips and tricks along the way.

How to Layer Side Bangs

Step 1: Choose the Right Type of Bangs

Before you start layering your side bangs, it's important to choose the right type of bangs for your face shape. Round faces look best with longer, side-swept bangs, while square faces benefit from shorter, choppy bangs. Heart-shaped faces can pull off almost any type of bangs, while oval faces can experiment with different lengths and styles.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair before you begin layering your side bangs. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid weighing down your hair, and blow-dry your hair with a round brush to add volume and shape to your bangs.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Separate your hair into three sections - the top half, the bottom half, and the section that contains your side bangs. Clip the top and bottom sections out of the way so you can focus on your bangs.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Begin by cutting your bangs to the desired length. For layered side bangs, start with a longer length and gradually work your way shorter. Use scissors to create small, angled cuts to create texture and movement in your bangs.

Step 5: Check the Length

It's important to check the length of your bangs as you go to avoid cutting too much off. Use a mirror to check the length from different angles, and remember that your hair will bounce up slightly when it dries, so leave a little extra length to account for this.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you've achieved the desired length, it's time to blend the layers. Use thinning shears to create soft, feathered edges in your bangs. This will help them blend seamlessly with the rest of your hair.

Step 7: Style Your Bangs

With your layered side bangs complete, it's time to style them. Use a round brush to blow-dry your bangs away from your face, creating volume and shape. You can also use a flat iron to straighten your bangs for a sleek, polished look, or use a curling iron to add some waves and texture.

Tips for Layering Side Bangs

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect layered side bangs:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually work your way shorter to avoid cutting too much off.
  • Use thinning shears to create soft, feathered edges in your bangs.
  • Blow-dry your bangs away from your face to create volume and shape.
  • Experiment with different styles to find the perfect look for you.

FAQ About Layered Side Bangs

Here are some frequently asked questions about layered side bangs:

How often should I trim my side bangs?

You should trim your side bangs every 6-8 weeks to keep them looking fresh and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my own side bangs?

While it's possible to cut your own side bangs, it's recommended to visit a professional stylist to ensure a clean, even cut.

What if I don't like my side bangs?

If you don't like your side bangs, don't worry - they'll grow out in a few weeks. In the meantime, you can experiment with different styles to find the perfect look for you.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Side Bangs

Here are some hair care tips to keep your layered side bangs looking their best:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid weighing down your hair.
  • Blow-dry your hair with a round brush to add volume and shape to your bangs.
  • Use a flat iron or curling iron to style your bangs, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.
  • Trim your bangs every 6-8 weeks to keep them looking fresh and prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve the perfect layered side bangs in no time. Experiment with different styles and lengths to find the perfect look for you, and don't be afraid to visit a professional stylist if you need some extra help. Happy styling!
