How To Repair Damaged Hair Due To Bleaching A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 04, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Bleaching your hair can be a great way to achieve a bold, new look. However, the process can be quite damaging to your hair, leaving it dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. If you're dealing with damaged hair due to bleaching, don't worry. With the right care and attention, you can repair your hair and restore it to its former glory. In this guide, we'll show you how.

How to Repair Damaged Hair Due to Bleaching

The first step in repairing damaged hair due to bleaching is to assess the extent of the damage. If your hair is severely damaged, you may need to trim off the affected areas and start fresh. However, if the damage is mild to moderate, you can try the following steps:

1. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help to strengthen and rebuild damaged hair. Look for a treatment that contains hydrolyzed keratin or collagen, as these ingredients can penetrate the hair shaft and improve its elasticity. Apply the treatment to clean, damp hair and leave it on for the recommended time before rinsing it out.

2. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning is essential for repairing damaged hair. Use a conditioning treatment that is specifically formulated for damaged hair, and leave it on for at least 30 minutes. For best results, cover your hair with a shower cap or towel to trap in heat and help the treatment penetrate the hair shaft.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can further damage already fragile hair. If possible, avoid using these tools altogether while you're repairing your hair. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

4. Limit Shampooing

Shampooing too often can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it more susceptible to damage. Try to limit your shampooing to once or twice a week, and use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that is designed for damaged hair.

5. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help to remove damaged ends and prevent further breakage. Aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks, or as needed.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're ready to start repairing your damaged hair, follow these steps:

Step 1: Assess the Damage

Take a close look at your hair and determine the extent of the damage. If your hair is severely damaged, you may need to trim off the affected areas and start fresh. If the damage is mild to moderate, you can proceed with repairing your hair.

Step 2: Use a Protein Treatment

Apply a protein treatment to clean, damp hair. Leave the treatment on for the recommended time before rinsing it out.

Step 3: Deep Condition

Apply a conditioning treatment to your hair and leave it on for at least 30 minutes. For best results, cover your hair with a shower cap or towel to trap in heat and help the treatment penetrate the hair shaft.

Step 4: Limit Shampooing

Try to limit your shampooing to once or twice a week, and use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that is designed for damaged hair.

Step 5: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools while you're repairing your hair. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Step 6: Get Regular Trims

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks, or as needed, to remove damaged ends and prevent further breakage.

Tips for Repairing Damaged Hair

  • Be patient. It can take time to repair damaged hair, so be prepared to stick with your hair care routine for several weeks or even months.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as hair dyes and relaxers, while you're repairing your hair.
  • Wear protective styles, such as braids or twists, to help protect your hair from further damage.
  • Eat a healthy diet that is rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

FAQ

Can damaged hair be repaired?

Yes, damaged hair can be repaired with the right care and attention.

How long does it take to repair damaged hair?

The length of time it takes to repair damaged hair can vary depending on the extent of the damage. Be prepared to stick with your hair care routine for several weeks or even months.

Can I bleach my hair again after repairing it?

It's generally best to avoid bleaching your hair again until it has fully recovered from the previous bleaching process.

Hair Care Routine for Damaged Hair

If you're dealing with damaged hair, use the following hair care routine:

  • Shampoo your hair once or twice a week with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that is designed for damaged hair.
  • Apply a protein treatment to your hair every 4-6 weeks to help rebuild and strengthen damaged hair.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week for at least 30 minutes.
  • Avoid heat styling tools while you're repairing your hair.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks, or as needed, to remove damaged ends and prevent further breakage.

With the right care and attention, you can repair your damaged hair and restore it to its former glory. Be patient, be gentle with your hair, and stick to a consistent hair care routine, and you'll be on your way to healthy, beautiful hair in no time.
