What Shedding Of Hair Means And How To Deal With It

Written by Oliver Jul 20, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're someone who is experiencing hair loss or noticing more hair on your brush or in the shower, you may be wondering what shedding of hair means. Shedding of hair is a natural process that happens to everyone, but when it becomes excessive, it can be a sign of an underlying issue. In this article, we'll discuss what shedding of hair means, how to deal with it, and provide some tips for managing hair loss.

What is Shedding of Hair?

Shedding of hair is a natural process where your hair falls out and is replaced by new hair. It's a part of the hair growth cycle and happens to everyone. On average, a person can lose up to 100 hairs per day. However, when the rate of shedding exceeds the rate of hair growth, it can lead to thinning hair or baldness.

How to Deal with Shedding of Hair

If you're experiencing excessive shedding of hair, there are a few things you can do to help manage it:

1. Identify the Underlying Cause

There are many reasons why shedding of hair can occur, including stress, hormonal imbalances, medication, and nutrient deficiencies. Identifying the underlying cause can help you determine the best course of action for managing hair loss.

2. Practice Good Hair Care

Practicing good hair care can help reduce shedding of hair. This includes using a gentle shampoo, avoiding harsh chemicals or heat styling, and using a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair. Additionally, avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet that is rich in nutrients like vitamins A, C, and E, and biotin can help promote hair growth and reduce shedding of hair. Additionally, drinking plenty of water and staying hydrated can help keep your hair healthy.

Step by Step Guide to Dealing with Shedding of Hair

Follow these steps to help manage shedding of hair:

Step 1: Consult with a Doctor or Dermatologist

If you're experiencing excessive shedding of hair, the first step is to consult with a doctor or dermatologist. They can help determine the underlying cause of your hair loss and recommend the best course of action for managing it.

Step 2: Identify the Underlying Cause

Identifying the underlying cause of your hair loss is crucial for finding the right treatment. This may involve a blood test or scalp biopsy to determine if there are any nutrient deficiencies, hormonal imbalances, or other medical conditions that may be contributing to your hair loss.

Step 3: Practice Good Hair Care

Practicing good hair care is essential for reducing shedding of hair. This includes using a gentle shampoo, avoiding harsh chemicals or heat styling, and using a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair.

Step 4: Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet that is rich in nutrients like vitamins A, C, and E, and biotin can help promote hair growth and reduce shedding of hair. Additionally, drinking plenty of water and staying hydrated can help keep your hair healthy.

Tips for Managing Shedding of Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing shedding of hair:

1. Use a Hair Growth Supplement

There are many hair growth supplements available that can help promote healthy hair growth and reduce shedding of hair. Look for supplements that contain biotin, vitamins A, C, and E, and iron.

2. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help improve blood flow and promote healthy hair growth. Use your fingertips to gently massage your scalp for a few minutes each day.

3. Avoid Stress

Stress can cause hair loss, so it's important to find ways to manage stress in your life. This may include exercise, meditation, or talking to a therapist.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is shedding of hair normal?

A: Yes, shedding of hair is a natural process that happens to everyone.

Q: How much hair loss is normal?

A: On average, a person can lose up to 100 hairs per day.

Q: What causes shedding of hair?

A: Shedding of hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including stress, hormonal imbalances, medication, and nutrient deficiencies.

Conclusion

Shedding of hair is a natural process that happens to everyone, but when it becomes excessive, it can be a sign of an underlying issue. If you're experiencing excessive shedding of hair, consult with a doctor or dermatologist to determine the underlying cause and the best course of action for managing it. Additionally, practicing good hair care, eating a healthy diet, and managing stress can all help reduce shedding of hair and promote healthy hair growth.
