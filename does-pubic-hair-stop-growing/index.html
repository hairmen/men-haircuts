Does Pubic Hair Stop Growing

Pubic hair growth is one of the most common concerns among both men and women. Many people believe that pubic hair stops growing at a certain age or after a certain period, but is it true? In this blog post, we will discuss everything you need to know about pubic hair growth and answer the question, "Does pubic hair stop growing?"

Pubic Hair Growth Process

Pubic hair growth is a natural process that occurs during puberty. As hormones start to change, hair will grow in the pubic area, underarms, and other parts of the body. The hair that grows in the pubic area is thicker and coarser than the hair that grows on other parts of the body.

The growth of pubic hair begins with the appearance of small, fine hairs around the base of the penis or vulva. These hairs will slowly become thicker and darker over time. Eventually, the hair will cover the entire pubic area, including the scrotum or labia.

Factors that Affect Pubic Hair Growth

Pubic hair growth is influenced by various factors, including age, genetics, and hormones. Genetics play a significant role in determining the thickness, color, and texture of pubic hair. Hormones such as testosterone and estrogen also play a crucial role in the growth of pubic hair.

Age is another factor that affects pubic hair growth. During puberty, the body produces more hormones, which leads to the growth of pubic hair. As a person ages, the production of these hormones decreases, resulting in less pubic hair growth.

Does Pubic Hair Stop Growing?

The answer to this question is no. Pubic hair does not stop growing. However, the growth rate of pubic hair can slow down over time. As a person ages, the production of hormones decreases, resulting in slower hair growth. This is why older people tend to have less pubic hair than younger people.

It's also important to note that pubic hair growth can vary from person to person. Some people may experience faster or slower hair growth rates than others.

Pubic Hair Growth During Pregnancy

During pregnancy, a woman's body goes through many changes, including an increase in hormone production. As a result, many women may experience faster pubic hair growth during pregnancy. However, this growth is usually temporary and will slow down after childbirth.

How to Care for Pubic Hair

Caring for pubic hair is essential to maintain good hygiene and prevent infections. Here are some tips on how to care for pubic hair:

  • Trim pubic hair regularly to keep it neat and tidy.
  • Use a mild soap or cleanser to wash the pubic area.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on pubic hair.
  • Avoid wearing tight clothing that can irritate the pubic area.

FAQs About Pubic Hair Growth

1. Does shaving or waxing affect pubic hair growth?

No, shaving or waxing does not affect pubic hair growth. However, it may make the hair appear thicker and darker as it grows back.

2. Can stress affect pubic hair growth?

Yes, stress can affect pubic hair growth. Stress can disrupt the production of hormones, which can lead to slower hair growth.

3. Is it normal to have less pubic hair as you get older?

Yes, it's normal to have less pubic hair as you get older. This is because the production of hormones decreases with age, resulting in slower hair growth.

4. Can pubic hair growth be stimulated?

No, pubic hair growth cannot be stimulated. However, maintaining a healthy diet and lifestyle can help promote overall hair growth.


In conclusion, pubic hair does not stop growing. The growth rate of pubic hair may slow down over time, but it will continue to grow. Factors such as age, genetics, and hormones can affect pubic hair growth. It's important to care for pubic hair properly to maintain good hygiene and prevent infections.
