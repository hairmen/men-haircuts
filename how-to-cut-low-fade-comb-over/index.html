How To Cut Low Fade Comb Over A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Oct 30, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking for a trendy and stylish haircut, the low fade comb over is a great option. This hairstyle is perfect for men who want a polished and professional look without sacrificing their personal style. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut a low fade comb over in a few easy steps.

Description

The low fade comb over is a popular hairstyle that combines a classic comb over with a modern low fade. This hairstyle has a tapered cut on the sides that gradually blends into longer hair on top. The comb over is styled by sweeping the longer hair to one side, creating a sleek and polished look. This hairstyle works well for men who have thick or thin hair, and it's versatile enough to be styled in different ways.

How to Cut a Low Fade Comb Over

Step 1: Preparation

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have a towel or cape to protect your client's clothes from hair clippings.

Step 2: Start with the Sides

Using the clippers, start cutting the hair on the sides of the head. Begin with a longer guard and gradually work your way down to a shorter guard. The key is to create a gradual taper that blends into the longer hair on top. Make sure to blend the hair around the ears and the back of the head.

Step 3: Cut the Top

Once you've finished the sides, move on to the top of the head. Use the scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. The length will depend on your client's preference, but a good rule of thumb is to leave at least two inches of hair on top. Cut the hair in a straight line, starting from the front and working your way back.

Step 4: Blend the Top and Sides

Next, use the clippers to blend the hair on the top and sides of the head. Use a longer guard to blend the hair around the temples and a shorter guard to blend the hair around the crown. Make sure to blend the hair evenly to create a seamless transition from the top to the sides.

Step 5: Style the Hair

The final step is to style the hair. Use a comb and some hair product to create the comb over. Start by applying the product to the hair on top, and then use the comb to sweep the hair to one side. You can also add some texture to the hair by using your fingers to create a messy look.

Tips for Cutting a Low Fade Comb Over

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to create a gradual taper.
  • Blend the hair evenly to create a seamless transition from the top to the sides.
  • Leave at least two inches of hair on top to create a polished look.
  • Use hair product to create the comb over and add texture to the hair.

FAQ

How often should I get a low fade comb over?

A low fade comb over typically needs to be maintained every two to four weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows.

Can I cut a low fade comb over at home?

If you have experience cutting hair, you can cut a low fade comb over at home. However, it's recommended that you visit a professional barber or stylist for the best results.

Hair Care for a Low Fade Comb Over

To maintain a low fade comb over, it's important to keep your hair clean and well-groomed. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type, and avoid using too much product, which can weigh down your hair. Regular trims can also help keep your hair looking neat and polished.

Now that you know how to cut a low fade comb over, you can rock this stylish and trendy hairstyle with confidence. With a little practice and the right tools, you can create a sleek and polished look that's perfect for any occasion.
