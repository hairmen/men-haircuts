Why Does Hair Shed So Much After Braids

Description

Have you ever noticed that after removing braids, your hair sheds more than usual? This is a common phenomenon that many people experience. The reason behind this excessive hair shedding is not complicated, but it can be frustrating to deal with. In this article, we will discuss why hair sheds so much after braids and what you can do to prevent it.

How to

Before we delve into the reasons why hair sheds after braids, let's first talk about how to properly remove braids. It's important to do it correctly to minimize hair damage and shedding.

Step 1: Cut the Braids

Start by cutting the braids close to your scalp. Be careful not to cut your natural hair in the process. Use scissors or a blade to cut the braids, but be gentle to avoid any accidental cuts.

Step 2: Detangle the Hair

After cutting the braids, use a detangling comb to gently separate the strands. Make sure to start from the ends and work your way up to the roots. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause breakage and shedding.

Step 3: Wash Your Hair

Once you have detangled your hair, wash it with a mild shampoo and conditioner. Use warm water to help remove any dirt or buildup that may have accumulated during the time you had the braids in. Make sure to rinse your hair thoroughly to avoid any product buildup.

Step by step

Now that we know how to properly remove braids, let's discuss why hair sheds so much after braids. There are several reasons why this happens:

Reason 1: Tension

When you wear braids, your hair is under tension. The tightness of the braids can cause your hair to become weak and brittle, leading to breakage and shedding. This is especially true if you keep the braids in for a long time.

Reason 2: Dryness

Braids can also cause your hair to become dry and brittle. This is because the braids can prevent your natural oils from reaching the ends of your hair, leading to dryness and breakage. The longer you keep the braids in, the more your hair will suffer from dryness.

Reason 3: Hair Products

Using hair products while wearing braids can also contribute to hair shedding. This is because the products can build up on your scalp and clog your hair follicles, leading to hair loss. It's important to choose hair products that are lightweight and won't weigh your hair down.

Tips

To minimize hair shedding after braids, here are some tips:

Tip 1: Keep the Braids in for a Short Time

Try to keep your braids in for no longer than six weeks. This will minimize the tension on your hair and reduce the risk of breakage and shedding.

Tip 2: Moisturize Your Hair

Make sure to moisturize your hair regularly while wearing the braids. Use a lightweight oil or leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and prevent dryness.

Tip 3: Avoid Heavy Products

Avoid using heavy hair products while wearing braids. This will prevent product buildup and clogged hair follicles, which can lead to hair loss.

FAQ

Q: Is it normal for hair to shed after braids?

A: Yes, it is normal for hair to shed after braids. This is because the tightness of the braids can cause your hair to become weak and brittle, leading to breakage and shedding.

Q: How long should I keep braids in?

A: Try to keep your braids in for no longer than six weeks. This will minimize the tension on your hair and reduce the risk of breakage and shedding.

Q: What can I do to prevent hair shedding after braids?

A: To prevent hair shedding after braids, make sure to keep your hair moisturized, avoid heavy hair products, and limit the amount of time you keep the braids in.

Hair Care

Braids can be a great protective style for your hair, but it's important to take care of your hair while wearing them. Make sure to keep your hair moisturized, avoid heavy hair products, and limit the amount of time you keep the braids in. With proper care, you can minimize hair shedding after braids and keep your hair healthy and strong.
