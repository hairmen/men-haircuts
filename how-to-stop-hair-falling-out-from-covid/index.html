How to Stop Hair Falling Out from Covid

If you are experiencing hair loss after having Covid-19, you are not alone. Many people have reported hair loss as one of the long-term effects of Covid-19. In this article, we will discuss how to stop hair falling out from Covid-19 and provide you with some tips to prevent further hair loss.

Table of Content

  • Description
  • How to Stop Hair Falling Out from Covid
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

Description

Hair loss after Covid-19 is a common problem that many people are facing. This condition is called Telogen Effluvium, which is a type of hair loss that occurs due to stress on the body. Covid-19 can cause stress on the body and can result in hair loss. However, the good news is that this condition is temporary and your hair will grow back once your body recovers from the stress.

How to Stop Hair Falling Out from Covid

The best way to stop hair falling out from Covid-19 is to take care of your body and reduce stress. Here are some tips to help you:

Step by Step

  1. Eat a healthy diet that includes protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  2. Get enough sleep and rest.
  3. Reduce stress by practicing relaxation techniques such as meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises.
  4. Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair such as dyes, perms, or relaxers.
  5. Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair such as braids, ponytails, or buns.
  6. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type.
  7. Avoid over-styling your hair with heat tools such as blow dryers, flat irons, or curling irons.
  8. Massage your scalp regularly to improve blood flow and promote hair growth.
  9. Take supplements that are rich in vitamins and minerals that are good for hair health such as biotin, iron, and zinc.
  10. Consult with your doctor if you are experiencing severe hair loss or if you have any underlying health conditions that may be causing hair loss.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent hair loss:

  • Avoid using tight hats or headbands that can pull on your hair.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair as it can damage your hair and scalp.
  • Avoid smoking and drinking alcohol as they can contribute to hair loss.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

FAQ

  1. Can Covid-19 cause permanent hair loss?
    No, hair loss caused by Covid-19 is temporary and your hair will grow back once your body recovers from the stress.
  2. How long does it take for hair to grow back after Covid-19?
    It can take several months for your hair to grow back after experiencing hair loss due to Covid-19.
  3. What type of doctor should I see for hair loss?
    You can see a dermatologist or a trichologist for hair loss.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important for preventing hair loss and promoting hair growth. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair as it can damage your hair and scalp.
  • Avoid over-styling your hair with heat tools such as blow dryers, flat irons, or curling irons.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or brush to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to improve blood flow and promote hair growth.
  • Take supplements that are rich in vitamins and minerals that are good for hair health such as biotin, iron, and zinc.
