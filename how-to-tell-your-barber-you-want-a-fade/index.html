How To Tell Your Barber You Want A Fade

Description

A fade is a popular hairstyle where the hair gradually gets shorter towards the bottom. It's a versatile and stylish look that can be customized to fit your personal style. However, if you're not familiar with the terminology or how to communicate with your barber, it can be tough to know how to ask for a fade. This article will provide you with tips and step-by-step instructions on how to tell your barber you want a fade.

How to

Before you head to the barbershop, it's important to decide what type of fade you want. There are different variations of fades, such as high fades, low fades, and skin fades. You should also consider the length you want on top and the overall look you're going for.

When you arrive at the barbershop, greet your barber and let them know the type of fade you want. Be specific and use terminology that your barber will understand. For example, you might say, "I want a low fade with a number two on top."

If you're unsure of the terminology, you can bring in a picture of the hairstyle you want. This can help you and your barber get on the same page and ensure you get the look you want.

Step by Step

Here are the steps to follow when telling your barber you want a fade:

  1. Decide on the type of fade you want.
  2. Consider the length you want on top and the overall look you're going for.
  3. Greet your barber and let them know the type of fade you want.
  4. Be specific and use terminology your barber will understand.
  5. Show a picture if you're unsure of the terminology.
  6. Ask your barber for their opinion or recommendations.
  7. Stay still and avoid moving your head too much during the haircut.
  8. Communicate any concerns you have during the haircut.
  9. Pay attention to the haircut and let your barber know if you're happy with the results.
  10. Talk to your barber about any maintenance or styling tips for your new haircut.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Be patient and allow your barber to take their time.
  • Don't be afraid to ask questions or clarify any terminology.
  • Bring in a picture if you're unsure of the terminology or want to show the barber the look you're going for.
  • Consider your face shape and hair texture when deciding on the type of fade you want.
  • Communicate any concerns or changes you want during the haircut.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting a fade haircut:

What's the difference between a low fade and a high fade?

A low fade starts the fade lower on the head, while a high fade starts the fade higher on the head.

What's a skin fade?

A skin fade is a type of fade where the hair is cut extremely short towards the bottom, creating a "skin" effect.

How often should I get a fade?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you want to maintain the look. Typically, fades should be maintained every two to four weeks.

Hair Care

To maintain your new fade haircut, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using products that contain alcohol, which can dry out your hair. Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized. Also, be sure to schedule regular haircuts to keep your fade looking fresh.

By following these tips and communicating clearly with your barber, you can get the perfect fade haircut that fits your personal style.
