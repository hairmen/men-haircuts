How to Cut Curly Hair Yourself Brad Mondo

Brad Mondo is a hairstylist with over 6 million subscribers on YouTube. He's known for his tutorials on cutting and coloring hair, and his tips are highly sought-after by those looking to save money on salon visits. Here's how to cut curly hair yourself using Brad Mondo's methods:

1. Invest in Good Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. Don't use kitchen scissors or dull scissors - they'll only make the job harder. Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors, and make sure they're sharp.

2. Start with Clean, Dry Hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and let it air dry or use a diffuser on low heat. Don't brush or comb your hair after washing - this can cause breakage and damage. Instead, use your fingers to gently detangle your curls.

3. Section Your Hair

Use hair clips to section your hair into four equal parts - two on top and two on the bottom. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly. Use a comb to create a straight part down the middle of your head, and then create a part from ear to ear. Clip each section out of the way.

4. Start Cutting

Take a small section of hair from the bottom, and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers, and use your scissors to cut off the ends. Cut a little at a time, and keep checking to make sure the hair is even. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working from the bottom to the top.

5. Check for Evenness

Once you've finished cutting your hair, check for evenness. Take a small section of hair from each section, and compare the lengths. If they're not even, trim the longer pieces until they match. Don't worry if your hair isn't perfect - curly hair is forgiving, and small imperfections won't be noticeable.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some tips for cutting curly hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair. It's easier to cut more later than to try to fix a mistake.
  • Don't use a razor. Razors can cause frizz and damage to curly hair.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Curly hair shrinks when it dries, so it's important to cut it when it's dry.
  • Don't cut your hair too short. Curly hair tends to bounce up when it dries, so it's better to err on the side of caution.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair:

Can I cut my own curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair. Just make sure you have the right tools and follow the steps carefully.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended that you cut your curly hair every 3-4 months to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic if you make a mistake! Curly hair is forgiving, and small mistakes can be fixed with a little bit of styling.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for taking care of your curly hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's dry. Instead, use your fingers to detangle your curls.
  • Use a diffuser on low heat to dry your hair, or let it air dry.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

With these tips and Brad Mondo's methods, you can cut your curly hair yourself and save money on salon visits. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow the steps carefully. Happy cutting!
