How To Properly Thin Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Oct 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it looks gorgeous and voluminous. On the other hand, it can be heavy, difficult to style, and even uncomfortable to wear. If you have thick hair and are looking for a way to make it more manageable, then thinning it out might be the solution for you. In this article, we'll show you how to properly thin thick hair in a relaxed English language.

How to Thin Thick Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's first talk about what thinning hair actually means. Thinning hair is the process of removing some of the bulk from your hair to make it lighter and more manageable. This can be done in a few different ways:

  • Thinning shears: These are special scissors that have teeth on one blade and a straight edge on the other. They're used to cut into the hair and remove some of the bulk.
  • Razor cutting: This technique involves using a straight razor to slice into the hair and remove some of the weight.
  • Texturizing scissors: These scissors have teeth on both blades and are used to create texture and remove bulk from the hair.

Now that you know the different techniques, let's move on to the step-by-step guide.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. If you have curly hair, it's best to let it air dry so you can see how it naturally falls. If you have straight hair, you can blow dry it straight.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips. Start by sectioning off the top half of your hair and clipping it out of the way.

Step 3: Thin the Bottom Half

Using thinning shears, razor cutting, or texturizing scissors, start thinning the bottom half of your hair. Take small sections and cut into them, removing some of the bulk. Be careful not to cut too much or you'll end up with uneven hair.

Step 4: Thin the Top Half

Once you've thinned out the bottom half, unclip the top half of your hair and repeat the process. Take small sections and cut into them, removing some of the bulk.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've thinned out both the top and bottom half of your hair, it's time to blend the layers. Take a comb and comb through your hair, blending the layers together. This will create a seamless look and prevent any choppy or uneven parts.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

After you've blended the layers, check your hair for evenness. If you notice any areas that are too thick or too thin, you can go back in and make adjustments.

Tips for Thinning Thick Hair

  • Be careful not to over-thin your hair. It's better to go slow and make small adjustments than to go too far and end up with uneven hair.
  • Always use sharp scissors or shears. Dull blades can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • If you're not comfortable thinning your own hair, consider going to a professional stylist.
  • Thinning your hair can make it more prone to breakage, so be sure to use a good quality conditioner and avoid using heat tools too often.

FAQs About Thinning Thick Hair

Can I thin my hair if it's already damaged?

It's not recommended to thin your hair if it's already damaged. Thinning it out can make it more prone to breakage and further damage.

How often should I thin my hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick it is. Generally, it's recommended to thin your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Does thinning my hair make it grow back thicker?

No, thinning your hair does not make it grow back thicker. It simply removes some of the bulk and weight.

Hair Care for Thicker Hair

  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove any buildup from styling products.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often. When you do use them, use a heat protectant spray.
  • Invest in a good quality conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or oil to help detangle your hair and make it more manageable.

By following these tips and using the step-by-step guide, you can properly thin your thick hair and make it more manageable. Remember to go slow, be careful, and take your time. With a little patience and practice, you'll have beautiful, healthy, and manageable hair.
