How To Cut Long Thick Hair To Shoulder Length

Written by Robby Jul 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you have long, thick hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, cutting your hair to shoulder length can be a simple and easy process. In this article, we’ll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut long thick hair to shoulder length, along with tips, frequently asked questions, and hair care advice.

Tools You’ll Need

Before you begin cutting your hair, gather the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Fine-tooth comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Large mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your long thick hair to shoulder length:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your hair straight and remove any tangles.
  2. Divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to secure each section.
  3. Begin with one of the back sections. Take a small section of hair from the bottom and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut straight across with the scissors. Repeat this process, working your way up the section of hair.
  4. Once you’ve finished cutting the first back section, repeat the process with the other back section.
  5. Move on to the front sections. Take a small section of hair from the bottom and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut at an angle towards the back of your head. This will create layers and add volume to your hair.
  6. Continue cutting small sections of hair at an angle, working your way up the front sections.
  7. Once you’ve finished cutting both front sections, check your hair in the mirror to make sure it’s even. If necessary, make small adjustments.
  8. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Long Thick Hair

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting long thick hair:

  • Take your time and work in small sections to avoid making mistakes.
  • Don’t cut your hair too short. It’s better to cut less initially and make small adjustments as needed.
  • If you’re unsure about cutting your hair, consider getting a friend or family member to help.
  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it difficult to achieve a clean cut.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting long thick hair to shoulder length:

Q: Can I cut my hair to shoulder length if it’s curly?

A: Yes, you can cut curly hair to shoulder length. However, keep in mind that curly hair can appear shorter when dry, so be sure to cut it longer than you want it to be.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It’s recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair growth.

Q: Can I cut my hair if it’s wet?

A: It’s best to cut your hair when it’s dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may lead to uneven cuts.

Hair Care after Cutting Long Thick Hair

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it to maintain healthy and strong hair growth:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, or use them sparingly and with a heat protectant spray.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals to promote hair growth.

With these tips and techniques, cutting long thick hair to shoulder length can be a simple and easy process. Just remember to take your time, work in small sections, and invest in a good pair of scissors. Happy cutting!
