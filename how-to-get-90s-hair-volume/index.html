How To Get 90S Hair Volume A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 24, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to add some major volume to your hair, then you're in luck. The 90s were all about big, bold hairstyles, and the good news is that you can still achieve that same look today. In this article, we're going to show you how to get 90s hair volume in a few simple steps.

Description

The 90s were all about big hair. From the voluminous curls of Whitney Houston to the spiky, textured hair of Gwen Stefani, there was no shortage of bold hairstyles during this era. If you're looking to recreate that same look, then you're going to need some volume. In this article, we're going to show you how to get 90s hair volume with some simple tips and tricks.

How to Get 90s Hair Volume

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

The first step to achieving 90s hair volume is to start with clean hair. This means washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo that will remove any buildup or residue. This will help to create a clean slate for you to work with.

Step 2: Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Once your hair is clean, use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. These products are designed to add volume and thickness to your hair, so they're perfect for achieving that 90s look.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

When blow drying your hair, flip your head upside down. This will help to create volume at the roots, which is where you need it most. Use a round brush to lift your hair up and away from your scalp as you blow dry.

Step 4: Tease Your Hair

To really amp up the volume, you're going to need to tease your hair. Take small sections of hair and backcomb them at the roots using a fine-tooth comb. This will create height and fullness.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

Once you've teased your hair to your desired level of volume, finish with a strong hold hairspray. This will help to keep your hair in place and prevent it from falling flat throughout the day.

Tips for Getting 90s Hair Volume

- Use a volumizing mousse or spray before blow drying your hair for even more volume.
- Avoid using heavy styling products like oils or serums, as these can weigh your hair down.
- If you have naturally curly hair, use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your curls and add volume.
- Experiment with different partings to create more volume at the crown of your head.
- Don't be afraid to go big - the 90s were all about bold, statement hairstyles.

FAQ

What if I have thin hair?

If you have thin hair, you can still achieve 90s hair volume. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, and be sure to tease your hair at the roots. You can also try using clip-in hair extensions to add extra volume and thickness.

Can I achieve 90s hair volume with short hair?

Absolutely! You can still achieve 90s hair volume with short hair. Use a volumizing mousse or spray before blow drying, and tease your hair at the roots. You can also try using a texturizing spray to add more fullness and body.

Hair Care Tips

Remember to be gentle with your hair, especially when teasing it. Use a fine-tooth comb and avoid pulling too hard, as this can cause damage and breakage. Be sure to also use a heat protectant spray when blow drying your hair to prevent damage from heat styling.

Now that you know how to get 90s hair volume, it's time to give it a try. With these simple tips and tricks, you'll be rocking a bold, statement hairstyle in no time.
