How To Stop Telogen Effluvium Hair Loss

Written by Robby Aug 30, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you've been experiencing hair loss, you may have heard of telogen effluvium. Telogen effluvium is a type of hair loss that occurs when the hair follicles enter a resting phase, leading to excessive shedding. In this article, we'll explore how to stop telogen effluvium hair loss through proper hair care and lifestyle changes.

Description

Telogen effluvium is a common type of hair loss that is usually temporary. It can be caused by a variety of factors, including stress, hormonal changes, medications, and nutritional deficiencies. The hair loss typically appears as excessive shedding, with more hair falling out than usual. Telogen effluvium can affect both men and women, and it can occur at any age.

How to Stop Telogen Effluvium Hair Loss

The best way to stop telogen effluvium hair loss is to identify and address the underlying cause. This may involve making lifestyle changes, such as reducing stress or improving your diet, or adjusting your medication regimen. In some cases, topical treatments or medications may be recommended to stimulate hair growth and prevent further shedding.

Identify the Cause

The first step in stopping telogen effluvium hair loss is to identify the underlying cause. If you suspect that stress is a contributing factor, try incorporating stress-reducing activities into your daily routine, such as yoga or meditation. If you think your diet may be lacking in essential nutrients, consider adding more protein, iron, and vitamins to your meals. If you suspect that medication is causing your hair loss, talk to your doctor about alternative treatments.

Reduce Stress

Stress is a common cause of telogen effluvium hair loss, so it's important to take steps to reduce stress in your life. This may involve practicing relaxation techniques, such as deep breathing or mindfulness meditation, or engaging in physical activity, such as yoga or running. You may also find it helpful to seek support from friends or a mental health professional.

Improve Your Diet

A healthy diet is essential for maintaining healthy hair. Make sure you're getting enough protein, iron, and vitamins, such as vitamin D and vitamin B12. Foods that are rich in these nutrients include lean meats, fish, eggs, leafy greens, and dairy products. You may also want to consider taking a multivitamin or other supplements to ensure you're getting all the nutrients your body needs.

Adjust Your Medication Regimen

If you suspect that medication is causing your hair loss, talk to your doctor about alternative treatments. In some cases, switching to a different medication or adjusting the dosage may help reduce hair loss. However, it's important to talk to your doctor before making any changes to your medication regimen.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you stop telogen effluvium hair loss:

  1. Identify the underlying cause of your hair loss.
  2. Reduce stress through relaxation techniques or physical activity.
  3. Improve your diet by eating more protein, iron, and vitamins.
  4. Talk to your doctor about adjusting your medication regimen if necessary.
  5. Consider using topical treatments or medications to stimulate hair growth.
  6. Be patient and consistent with your hair care routine.

Tips for Preventing Telogen Effluvium Hair Loss

Here are some tips to help prevent telogen effluvium hair loss:

  • Avoid harsh styling products or treatments, such as perms or hot oil treatments.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid washing your hair too frequently.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles, such as braids or ponytails, that can pull on your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too frequently.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or scarf.

FAQs

How long does telogen effluvium last?

Telogen effluvium hair loss is usually temporary and typically lasts for six months to a year. However, in some cases, it can last longer.

Can telogen effluvium be cured?

Telogen effluvium hair loss can often be treated and cured by addressing the underlying cause. However, it's important to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine.

What are the symptoms of telogen effluvium?

The main symptom of telogen effluvium is excessive shedding, with more hair falling out than usual. This shedding may be accompanied by thinning hair or bald patches.

Hair Care for Telogen Effluvium Hair Loss

If you're experiencing telogen effluvium hair loss, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips to help you care for your hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's free of harsh chemicals.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as this can further damage your hair.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair too vigorously, as this can lead to hair breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too frequently.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner to nourish your hair and promote growth.

By following these tips and making lifestyle changes to address the underlying cause of your hair loss, you can stop telogen effluvium hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.
