What Kind Of Dogs Shed Less A Guide To Low Shedding Breeds

Written by Bardi Sep 13, 2023 · 3 min read

If you're looking for a furry companion but don't want to deal with excessive shedding, you're in luck! There are plenty of breeds out there that are known for their low-shedding coats. In this article, we'll take a look at some of the most popular breeds that shed less, as well as some tips for keeping their coats healthy and shiny.

Why Do Dogs Shed?

Before we dive into the low-shedding breeds, let's take a moment to talk about why dogs shed in the first place. Shedding is a natural process that helps dogs get rid of old or damaged fur and make way for new growth. Some dogs shed more than others, depending on their breed, age, health, and other factors.

Low-Shedding Breeds

Now, let's get to the good stuff! Here are some of the most popular breeds that are known for shedding less:

Poodle

Poodles are a popular choice for people with allergies or who want a low-shedding dog. Their curly, hypoallergenic coat doesn't shed much, but it does require regular grooming to prevent matting and keep it looking neat and tidy.

Bichon Frise

The Bichon Frise is another breed that is known for its curly, low-shedding coat. These friendly little dogs are great for families and don't require a lot of exercise, but they do require regular grooming to keep their coat looking its best.

Yorkshire Terrier

The Yorkshire Terrier, or "Yorkie," is a small breed that is known for its silky, low-shedding coat. These dogs are great for people who live in apartments or who don't have a lot of space, but they do require regular grooming to keep their coat from matting.

Shih Tzu

The Shih Tzu is a small, affectionate breed that is known for its long, flowing, low-shedding coat. These dogs are great for families and are very social, but they do require daily grooming to keep their coat looking its best.

Tips for Keeping Low-Shedding Coats Healthy

While low-shedding breeds require less maintenance than high-shedding breeds, they still require some grooming to keep their coats healthy and shiny. Here are some tips for keeping your low-shedding dog's coat looking its best:

  • Brush your dog's coat regularly to remove loose hair and prevent matting.
  • Bathe your dog as needed, but be careful not to over-bathe, as this can dry out their skin and coat.
  • Feed your dog a high-quality diet that is rich in essential nutrients and vitamins.
  • Provide your dog with plenty of fresh water to keep their skin and coat hydrated.

Frequently Asked Questions About Low-Shedding Dogs

Here are some common questions people have about low-shedding dogs:

Do low-shedding dogs still shed?

Yes, all dogs shed to some extent. However, low-shedding breeds shed much less than high-shedding breeds.

Do low-shedding dogs require less grooming?

No, low-shedding dogs still require regular grooming to keep their coat healthy and shiny.

Are low-shedding dogs hypoallergenic?

While no dog is completely hypoallergenic, low-shedding breeds like Poodles and Bichon Frises are less likely to cause allergic reactions in people with allergies.

Conclusion

If you're looking for a furry companion that won't leave your home covered in hair, a low-shedding breed may be the perfect choice for you. By choosing a breed like a Poodle, Bichon Frise, Yorkshire Terrier, or Shih Tzu, you can enjoy all the benefits of having a dog without the hassle of excessive shedding. Just remember to keep their coat healthy and shiny with regular grooming and a high-quality diet.
