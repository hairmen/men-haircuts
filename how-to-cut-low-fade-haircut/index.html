How To Cut Low Fade Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Low fade haircut is a popular men's hairstyle that involves shaving the hair on the sides and back of the head, leaving longer hair on top. This style is versatile and can be customized to suit different face shapes and hair types. In this article, we'll guide you on how to cut low fade haircut in easy steps. We'll also provide some tips on hair care and answer frequently asked questions about this hairstyle.

Table of Contents:

  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Low Fade Haircut

  5. Hair Care for Low Fade Haircut

How to Cut Low Fade Haircut

The low fade haircut involves tapering the hair on the sides and back of the head, gradually blending it into the longer hair on top. Here are the basic steps to cut low fade haircut:

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Start by preparing your tools. You'll need a pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, and a mirror.
  2. Decide on the length of the fade you want. The length can vary from a skin fade to a longer fade, depending on your preference and hair type.
  3. Begin by using the clippers to shave the hair on the sides and back of the head. Start with a longer guard and gradually switch to a shorter guard as you move upwards.
  4. Use the scissors to blend the hair on the sides and back into the longer hair on top. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it at an angle to create a seamless blend.
  5. Repeat the process on the other side of the head, making sure to keep the length and angle consistent.
  6. Trim the hair on top to your desired length, and style it as desired.
  7. Check the haircut in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Low Fade Haircut:

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect low fade haircut:

  • Start with a longer guard and gradually switch to a shorter guard as you move upwards to create a seamless blend.
  • Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it at an angle to create a natural-looking fade.
  • Check the haircut in the mirror regularly to ensure that both sides are even.
  • Use hair clippers with a sharp blade to ensure a smooth and clean cut.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time to ensure a perfect cut.

FAQ:

Here are some frequently asked questions about low fade haircut:

  • What face shape is best suited for low fade haircut? Low fade haircut is suitable for most face shapes, but it looks particularly good on square and oval faces.
  • How often should I get a low fade haircut? It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain the style. On average, you should get a touch-up every 2-3 weeks.
  • Can I style my hair differently with a low fade haircut? Yes, low fade haircut is versatile and can be styled in different ways, such as slicked back, side part, or messy fringe.

Hair Care for Low Fade Haircut:

Here are some tips for maintaining your low fade haircut:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping away natural oils.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and soft.
  • Avoid using too much heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Visit a professional barber regularly to maintain the style and keep it looking sharp.

Cutting low fade haircut is easy with the right tools and technique. Follow our step-by-step guide and tips to achieve the perfect low fade haircut. Don't forget to maintain your hair with proper care and regular touch-ups. Happy styling!
