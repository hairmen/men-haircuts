Why Is Only The Bottom Of My Hair Wavy

Written by Luffy Jul 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you ever wonder why only the bottom of your hair seems to be wavy, while the rest of it remains straight? It can be frustrating to have uneven waves in your hair, especially if you're trying to achieve a certain style or look. In this article, we'll explore why this happens and what you can do to fix it.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Fix It
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

The main reason why only the bottom of your hair is wavy is because of the weight of your hair. The top layers of your hair are lighter and therefore straighter, while the bottom layers are heavier and more prone to becoming wavy. Another reason could be the way you style your hair. If you frequently use heat styling tools on the bottom layers of your hair, this can cause the waves to set in and become more prominent.

How to Fix It

If you want to achieve more even waves throughout your hair, there are a few things you can try:

1. Change Your Haircut

One way to even out your waves is to get a haircut that adds more layers to the top of your hair. This will help to distribute the weight of your hair more evenly, and can result in more uniform waves throughout your hair.

2. Use a Curling Iron

If you're looking for a quick fix, you can use a curling iron to add waves to the top layers of your hair. By curling your hair in the opposite direction of your natural waves, you can create more uniform waves throughout your hair.

3. Try a Perm

If you're looking for a more permanent solution, you can try a perm. This involves using chemicals to curl your hair, and can result in more even waves throughout your hair. However, keep in mind that perms can be damaging to your hair, so it's important to use caution and follow proper hair care guidelines.

Step by Step

If you're using a curling iron to create more even waves in your hair, follow these steps:

1. Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair as usual. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair to protect it from heat damage.

2. Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable sections using hair clips. Start with a section at the top of your head, and work your way down.

3. Curl Your Hair

Take a small section of hair and clamp it in your curling iron. Hold the curling iron vertically and wrap your hair around the barrel, away from your face. Hold for a few seconds, then release the curl. Repeat this process on all sections of your hair.

4. Finish and Style

Once all of your hair is curled, use your fingers to gently separate the curls and create more volume. Finish with a light hold hairspray or texturizing spray, and style as desired.

Tips

  • Use a heat protectant spray or serum to protect your hair from heat damage when using a curling iron or other heat styling tool.
  • Try using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your natural waves.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment regularly to keep your hair healthy and strong.

FAQ

Q: Can genetics play a role in why only the bottom of my hair is wavy?

A: Yes, genetics can play a role in the texture and wave pattern of your hair. If other members of your family have similar hair, it's possible that genetics could be a factor.

Q: Can I use a straightening iron to straighten out the bottom of my hair?

A: Yes, you can use a straightening iron to straighten out the waves at the bottom of your hair. However, be sure to use a heat protectant spray or serum to protect your hair from heat damage.

Hair Care

Keeping your hair healthy and strong is important for achieving and maintaining even waves throughout your hair. Here are a few tips for taking care of your hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as this can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment at least once a week to help nourish and strengthen your hair.
  • Avoid over-brushing your hair, as this can cause breakage and frizz.

By following these tips and using the techniques outlined in this article, you can achieve more even waves throughout your hair and enjoy a healthy, beautiful mane.
