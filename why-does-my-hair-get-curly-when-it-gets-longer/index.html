Why Does My Hair Get Curly When It Gets Longer

Written by Aprilia Nov 29, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever noticed that your hair starts to get curly as it gets longer? This phenomenon is more common in people with naturally curly or wavy hair, but it can also happen to those with straight hair. In this article, we’ll explore the reasons why your hair gets curly as it grows longer, and provide tips on how to care for your curly locks.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

When hair is short, the weight of the hair helps to keep it straight. However, as it grows longer, the weight of the hair becomes too heavy for the scalp to support, and the hair starts to bend and curl. This is because the hair follicles are not perfectly straight, but instead are slightly curved. As the hair grows longer, the weight of the hair pulls the hair follicles out of their straight shape, causing the hair to curl.

How to

If you want to embrace your curly hair, there are a few things you can do to enhance your curls:

1. Use the right products

Curly hair requires moisture to stay healthy and defined. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl-enhancing shampoos, conditioners, and styling products.

2. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons can damage curly hair, making it frizzy and dry. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting low.

3. Use a diffuser when blow-drying

A diffuser attachment on your blow dryer can help to enhance your curls and reduce frizz. It works by distributing the heat evenly throughout your hair, rather than concentrating it in one spot.

Step by Step

If you’re new to caring for curly hair, here are some step-by-step instructions to help you get started:

1. Shampoo and condition your hair

Use a curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner to cleanse and moisturize your hair. Avoid using hot water, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Apply a leave-in conditioner

A leave-in conditioner will help to keep your hair hydrated and defined. Apply it evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair

Using a wide-tooth comb, gently detangle your hair from the ends to the roots. Avoid brushing your hair, as it can cause frizz.

4. Apply styling products

Apply a curl-enhancing styling product, such as a curl cream or gel. Scrunch your hair gently to encourage your curls.

5. Diffuse your hair

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair. Hold the diffuser close to your scalp and move it in a circular motion to enhance your curls.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you care for your curly hair:

  • Don’t over-wash your hair. Curly hair can become dry and brittle if it’s washed too frequently.
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase to reduce frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.

FAQ

Why does curly hair get frizzy?

Curly hair is prone to frizz because the hair cuticle is lifted, which allows moisture to enter and cause the hair to swell. This can result in frizz.

Can you straighten curly hair?

Yes, you can straighten curly hair using heat styling tools such as flat irons. However, it’s important to use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting low to avoid damaging your hair.

What causes curly hair?

Curly hair is caused by genetics. The shape of the hair follicle determines whether hair is straight, wavy, or curly.

Hair Care

Caring for curly hair can be challenging, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your curls looking healthy and defined. Remember to avoid heat styling tools, use a diffuser when blow-drying, and apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated. With a little bit of care, you can embrace your curly hair and rock those beautiful curls!
