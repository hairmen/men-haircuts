Why Is My Curly Hair Turning Wavy Understanding The Science Behind It

Written by Bardi Nov 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair is a blessing and a curse. On good days, it can make you feel like a goddess with bouncy, defined curls. On bad days, it can be a wild mess that refuses to be tamed. But what happens when your curly hair starts to turn wavy? It can be a confusing and frustrating experience, but there are reasons why it happens. In this article, we'll explore why your curly hair is turning wavy and what you can do about it.

Definition: What is Curly Hair?

Before we can understand why curly hair turns wavy, we need to define what curly hair is. Curly hair is hair that forms tight or loose coils, spirals, or ringlets. The shape of the hair follicle and the angle at which the hair grows out of the scalp determine the curl pattern. Curly hair can range from wavy to kinky, and it can be fine, medium, or coarse.

Why is My Curly Hair Turning Wavy?

There are several reasons why curly hair can turn wavy:

  • Hormonal changes: Hormones can affect the shape and texture of your hair. For example, during puberty, many people's hair becomes curlier or wavier. Similarly, during pregnancy, some people's hair becomes curlier. After pregnancy or menopause, hormones can cause hair to become less curly.
  • Heat damage: Using heat styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers can cause heat damage to the hair. This can cause the curl pattern to loosen and become wavier.
  • Chemical damage: Chemical treatments like relaxers, perms, and color can cause damage to the hair. This damage can change the curl pattern and make it wavier or straighter.
  • Natural aging: As we age, our hair can become less curly and more wavy or even straight. This is due to changes in the hair follicles and the texture of the hair.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

Before you can properly care for your wavy hair, you need to determine your hair type. There are three main types of hair: straight, wavy, and curly. Within each type, there are subcategories based on the size of the wave or curl. To determine your hair type, start by washing your hair and letting it air dry without any products. Then, examine the shape of your hair strands and the pattern of your hair.

Step-by-Step: How to Care for Wavy Hair

Here are some steps you can take to care for your wavy hair:

  1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for wavy hair.
  2. Avoid using hot water, which can strip the hair of natural oils.
  3. Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  4. Use a leave-in conditioner or styling product to enhance your waves and reduce frizz.
  5. Avoid using heat styling tools whenever possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and a low heat setting.
  6. Avoid using harsh chemicals or treatments on your hair, as these can cause damage and change the curl pattern.

Tips for Managing Wavy Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing your wavy hair:

  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz and disrupt the curl pattern.
  • Consider getting a haircut specifically designed for wavy hair, such as long layers or a shag cut.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find what works best for your hair.
  • Be patient with your hair and embrace its natural texture. Wavy hair can be beautiful and unique!

FAQs About Curly to Wavy Hair

Q: Can I make my curly hair more wavy?

A: While you can't change your natural curl pattern, there are styling techniques and products that can enhance your waves and make them more defined.

Q: Can my hair go from wavy to curly?

A: It's possible, especially during hormonal changes or natural aging. However, it's more common for hair to become less curly over time.

Q: Can cutting my hair make it more wavy?

A: Yes, getting a haircut that's specifically designed for wavy hair can enhance the curl pattern and make it more defined.

Conclusion

If you're wondering why your curly hair is turning wavy, there are several possible reasons. Hormonal changes, heat damage, chemical damage, and natural aging can all affect the curl pattern of your hair. By understanding your hair type and taking steps to care for your wavy hair, you can embrace its natural beauty and feel confident in your unique look.
