What Is A Fade In Barbering

Written by Eveline Sep 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Table of Contents: Description | How To | Step By Step | Tips | FAQ | Hair Care

Description

A fade in barbering is a hairstyle that involves gradually cutting the hair shorter from the top of the head down to the sides and back. The term "fade" refers to the gradual transition from longer hair at the top to shorter hair at the sides and back. This type of haircut can be achieved with clippers or scissors and is a popular choice for men of all ages.

There are many variations of the fade, including high fades, low fades, skin fades, and bald fades. The type of fade chosen will depend on personal preference and the style of the haircut.

How To

To achieve a fade in barbering, it is important to have the right tools. You will need a pair of clippers with various guard sizes, scissors, and a comb. You may also want to have a straight razor or trimmer for detailing.

The first step in creating a fade is to determine the length of the hair on top of the head. This will be the longest length of hair in the haircut. Use scissors to cut the hair to the desired length and blend any layers.

Next, select the guard size for the clippers and start at the bottom of the head, working your way up. Use the clippers to create a line around the head, leaving the hair longer at the top and gradually cutting shorter as you move down the sides and back.

Continue to switch to smaller guard sizes and blend the hair to create a smooth transition from longer to shorter hair. Use scissors to blend any harsh lines and detail the hair around the ears and neckline.

Step By Step

Here are the step-by-step instructions for achieving a fade in barbering:

  1. Determine the length of the hair on top of the head and cut to desired length with scissors.
  2. Select the guard size for the clippers and start at the bottom of the head, working your way up.
  3. Use the clippers to create a line around the head, leaving the hair longer at the top and gradually cutting shorter as you move down the sides and back.
  4. Switch to smaller guard sizes and blend the hair to create a smooth transition from longer to shorter hair.
  5. Use scissors to blend any harsh lines and detail the hair around the ears and neckline.

Tips

Here are some tips for achieving a great fade:

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a comb to hold the hair in place and cut in the direction of the hair growth.
  • Blend the hair in small sections to create a natural-looking fade.
  • Take your time and be patient. A great fade takes practice and skill.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about fades in barbering:

What is the difference between a high fade and a low fade?

A high fade starts at the temples and gradually blends into longer hair at the top of the head. A low fade starts just above the ears and gradually blends into longer hair at the top of the head.

What is a skin fade?

A skin fade is a type of fade where the hair is cut down to the skin at the sides and back of the head.

Can I get a fade with curly hair?

Yes, fades can be achieved with all hair types, including curly hair. It is important to have a barber who is skilled with cutting curly hair to achieve the best results.

Hair Care

After getting a fade in barbering, it is important to take care of the hair to maintain the style. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair clean and moisturized. Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down the hair and make it look greasy. Visit your barber regularly to maintain the shape of the haircut and keep the fade looking fresh.
