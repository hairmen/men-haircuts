How To Cut Bangs Around Your Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 28, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of the same old hairstyle and looking for a change? Cutting bangs around your face is a great way to switch things up and add some personality to your look. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut bangs around your face in a few easy steps. But before we dive into the how-to, let's talk about why you should consider cutting bangs and some tips to keep in mind.

Why Cut Bangs Around Your Face?

There are many reasons why you might consider cutting bangs around your face. For one, it's an easy way to change up your look without drastically altering your hair length. Bangs can also help frame your face and highlight your best features. Plus, they're a fun way to experiment with different styles and trends.

Tips to Keep in Mind

Before you start cutting your bangs, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Start with clean, dry hair
  • Use sharp scissors specifically designed for cutting hair
  • Take it slow and cut small sections at a time
  • Don't cut your bangs too short – you can always trim more later
  • Consider your face shape and hair type when choosing a bang style

How to Cut Bangs Around Your Face

Now that you know why you should cut bangs and some tips to keep in mind, let's get to the how-to. Follow these steps to cut bangs around your face:

  1. Section off the hair you want to cut into bangs. You can use a comb to create a straight line or a triangular shape depending on the style you want.
  2. Hold the hair straight out from your head and use your scissors to cut across the hair in a straight line. Start with small snips and work your way across the entire section.
  3. Once you've cut the initial line, use the point of your scissors to create texture and blend the edges. This will help your bangs look more natural and less blunt.
  4. If you want your bangs to be shorter, continue cutting small sections until you achieve the desired length. Remember, you can always cut more but you can't add hair back!
  5. Once you're happy with the length, use your fingers to comb through your bangs and make sure they're even on both sides. If not, make small adjustments as needed.
  6. Style your bangs as desired. You can blow dry them straight, curl them, or even add some texture with a sea salt spray.
  7. Enjoy your new look!

Tips for Maintaining Your Bangs

Now that you've cut your bangs, how do you keep them looking great? Here are some tips:

  • Get regular trims to keep your bangs looking fresh and prevent them from getting too long
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much – the oils from your hands can make them greasy
  • Use a dry shampoo to absorb excess oil and keep your bangs looking clean
  • Consider using a heat protectant spray when styling your bangs with hot tools

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some of the most common questions people have about cutting bangs:

How do I know if bangs will look good on me?

Consider your face shape and hair type when choosing a bang style. If you have a round face, for example, you might want to avoid blunt bangs and opt for side-swept bangs instead.

How often do I need to trim my bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want your bangs to be. Generally, you should plan on getting a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

Can I cut my bangs myself or should I go to a salon?

You can cut your bangs yourself if you feel confident and comfortable doing so. However, if you're unsure or want a more complex style, it's best to go to a salon and have a professional cut them for you.

Final Thoughts

Cutting bangs around your face is a fun and easy way to change up your look. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve the perfect set of bangs that highlight your best features and complement your face shape. Remember to take it slow and always trim less than you think you need – you can always cut more later!
