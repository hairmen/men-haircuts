Description

Blonde hair is available in many different shades, ranging from cool ash to warm honey. To choose the right shade for you, itâs important to consider factors like your skin tone, eye color, and natural hair color.

If you have cool-toned skin with pink or blue undertones, you may suit cooler blonde shades like platinum or ashy blonde. If your skin has warm undertones with yellow or peachy tones, you may prefer warmer blonde shades like golden or honey blonde.

Your natural hair color can also impact which blonde shade suits you. If you have naturally dark hair, it may be more challenging to achieve a lighter blonde shade without causing damage. In this case, it may be best to gradually lighten your hair over time or consider a darker blonde shade.

How to

The first step to picking the right blonde color for your hair is to determine your skin tone. You can do this by looking at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If they appear blue or purple, you have cool-toned skin. If they appear green, you have warm-toned skin.

Next, consider your natural hair color. If you have light blonde or naturally light hair, you may be able to achieve a range of blonde shades easily. If you have darker hair, you may need to lighten your hair gradually over time or consider a darker blonde shade.

Itâs also important to consider your eye color. If you have blue or green eyes, you may suit cooler blonde shades. If you have brown or hazel eyes, you may prefer warmer blonde shades.

Step by Step

  1. Determine your skin tone by looking at the veins on the inside of your wrist.
  2. Consider your natural hair color.
  3. Think about your eye color.
  4. Choose a blonde shade that complements your skin tone, natural hair color, and eye color.
  5. Visit a professional hairstylist to achieve the desired shade and to avoid damage to your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing the right blonde shade for your hair:

  • Consider the maintenance required for each shade. Lighter blonde shades may require more frequent touch-ups to maintain the color.
  • Try on wigs or use virtual makeover tools to get a sense of how different blonde shades may look on you.
  • Consult with a professional hairstylist for advice on which blonde shade will suit you best.
  • Use color-safe shampoo and conditioner to maintain your blonde color and prevent fading.
