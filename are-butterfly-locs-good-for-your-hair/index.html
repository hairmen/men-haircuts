Description

Butterfly locs are a type of crochet faux locs that are characterized by their loose and wavy texture. They are created by wrapping hair extensions around a small section of your natural hair using a crochet hook. The result is a bohemian-inspired look that is both stylish and low-maintenance.

Butterfly locs are typically made with synthetic hair extensions, which can come in a variety of colors and textures. They can last for up to six weeks with proper maintenance, making them a great choice for those who want a long-lasting protective hairstyle.

How to Get Butterfly Locs

If youâre interested in getting butterfly locs, you can either visit a hairstylist or do them yourself at home. Here are the steps to follow:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will help to remove any dirt or product buildup and prepare your hair for the installation process.
  2. Next, section your hair into small, equal parts using a rat-tail comb. The sections should be about the same size as the hair extensions you plan to use.
  3. Take a small section of your natural hair and braid it tightly to the scalp. Repeat this process until all of your hair is braided.
  4. Using a crochet hook, insert the hook through the braid and pull a loop of hair extension through. Tie a knot at the end of the hair extension to secure it in place.
  5. Repeat this process until all of your braids have been covered with hair extensions.
  6. Trim the hair extensions to your desired length and style the locs as desired.

Step-by-Step Guide to Installing Butterfly Locs

If you need more guidance on how to install butterfly locs, here is a step-by-step guide:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will help to remove any dirt or product buildup and prepare your hair for the installation process.
  2. Next, part your hair into small, equal sections using a rat-tail comb. The sections should be about the same size as the hair extensions you plan to use.
  3. Take a section of your natural hair and split it into two parts. Insert the crochet hook through the center of the section and pull a loop of hair extension through.
  4. Loop the hair extension around the two parts of your natural hair to create a butterfly shape. Tie a knot at the end of the hair extension to secure it in place.
  5. Repeat this process until all of your hair has been covered with butterfly locs.
  6. Trim the hair extensions to your desired length and style the locs as desired.

Tips for Maintaining Butterfly Locs

Here are some tips for maintaining your butterfly locs and keeping them looking their best:

  • Keep your scalp moisturized by applying oil or a leave-in conditioner regularly.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your locs and cause them to unravel.
  • Protect your locs while sleeping by wearing a satin bonnet or using a satin pillowcase.
  • Avoid exposing your locs to excessive heat, such as from a blow dryer or flat iron.
  • Be gentle when washing your locs to avoid causing frizz or damage.

Hair Care Tips for Butterfly Locs

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep your butterfly locs looking their best:

  • Wash your locs every 1-2 weeks with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or styling products that can cause buildup and weigh down your locs.
  • Retwist your locs every 2-3 weeks to maintain their shape and prevent frizz.
  • Trim the ends of your locs regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Protect your locs from sunlight and excessive heat by wearing a hat or scarf when outdoors.
