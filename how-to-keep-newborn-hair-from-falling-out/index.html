How To Keep Newborn Hair From Falling Out Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Nov 15, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're a new parent, you may have noticed that your baby's hair seems to be thinning or falling out. This can be a normal part of the newborn phase, but there are things you can do to help keep your baby's hair healthy and full. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for how to keep newborn hair from falling out.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Keep Newborn Hair from Falling Out
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Keeping Newborn Hair Healthy
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care for Newborns

How to Keep Newborn Hair from Falling Out

First, it's important to know that some hair loss is normal for newborns. This is because they have been surrounded by amniotic fluid in the womb, which can cause hair to become thin and fall out. However, if you notice excessive hair loss or bald patches, it's important to speak to your pediatrician.

Here are some things you can do to help keep your baby's hair healthy:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

When washing your baby's hair, make sure to use a gentle shampoo that is specifically formulated for newborns. Avoid using adult shampoos, which can be too harsh for your baby's delicate scalp. Look for shampoos that are free from sulfates, parabens, and fragrances.

2. Don't Over-Wash

While it's important to keep your baby's scalp clean, you don't want to over-wash their hair. This can strip away natural oils that are important for keeping hair healthy. Aim to wash your baby's hair once or twice a week, or as needed.

3. Be Gentle

When washing your baby's hair, be gentle. Use a soft washcloth or your fingertips to massage the scalp and rinse thoroughly with warm water. Avoid using hot water, which can be too harsh for your baby's skin.

4. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid putting your baby's hair in tight hairstyles, such as ponytails or braids. These can pull on the hair and cause it to break or fall out. Instead, opt for loose hairstyles or leave your baby's hair down.

5. Use a Soft Brush

When brushing your baby's hair, use a soft brush with natural bristles. Start at the roots and work your way down, being gentle and careful not to pull on the hair. Brushing can help distribute natural oils and stimulate the scalp.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for how to keep newborn hair from falling out:

  1. Use a gentle shampoo
  2. Don't over-wash
  3. Be gentle
  4. Avoid tight hairstyles
  5. Use a soft brush

Tips for Keeping Newborn Hair Healthy

Here are some additional tips for keeping your baby's hair healthy:

  • Protect your baby's scalp from the sun by using a hat or shade
  • Avoid using hair products, such as gels or hairspray
  • Massage your baby's scalp to stimulate hair growth
  • Make sure your baby is getting enough nutrition, as a healthy diet can contribute to healthy hair

FAQs

Is it normal for newborns to lose hair?

Yes, it is normal for newborns to lose some hair in the first few months of life. This is because of the hormonal changes that take place after birth, as well as the friction caused by lying on the back.

When will my baby's hair grow back?

Most babies will start to grow new hair by the time they are six months old. However, the texture and color of the hair may be different than before.

Hair Care for Newborns

While it's important to take care of your baby's hair, it's also important to remember that hair care is not the most important thing in a newborn's life. Make sure to prioritize your baby's overall health and well-being, and don't stress too much about their hair.

If you have concerns about your baby's hair loss, speak to your pediatrician. They can help determine if there are any underlying issues that need to be addressed.

Overall, by using gentle products, being careful when washing and brushing, and avoiding tight hairstyles, you can help keep your baby's hair healthy and full.
