Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in figuring out what hairstyle suits you is to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. Knowing your face shape can help you choose a hairstyle that flatters your features and enhances your natural beauty.

To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back. Take a close look at your face and compare it to the following descriptions:

Oval Face Shape

If you have an oval face shape, your forehead is slightly wider than your jaw, and your cheekbones are the widest part of your face. This face shape is considered to be the most versatile, as most hairstyles look good on it.

Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, your face is almost as wide as it is long, and your cheekbones are the widest part of your face. To make your face appear slimmer, choose a hairstyle that adds height and volume on top.

Square Face Shape

If you have a square face shape, your forehead, cheekbones, and jaw are all roughly the same width. To soften your features, choose a hairstyle that adds volume on top and has soft, wispy layers around your face.

Heart Face Shape

If you have a heart-shaped face, your forehead is wider than your jaw, and your chin is pointed. To balance out your features, choose a hairstyle that adds volume to the lower half of your face, such as long layers or a chin-length bob.

Diamond Face Shape

If you have a diamond-shaped face, your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, and your forehead and jaw are both narrow. To balance out your features, choose a hairstyle that adds volume on top and has soft, wispy layers around your face.

Oblong Face Shape

If you have an oblong face shape, your face is longer than it is wide, and your forehead, cheekbones, and jaw are roughly the same width. To add width to your face, choose a hairstyle that has volume on the sides and soft, wispy layers around your face.

Once you've determined your face shape, you can start looking for hairstyles that complement your features. But before you do that, there are a few more things to consider.

Identifying Your Hair Type

Another important factor to consider when choosing a hairstyle is your hair type. There are four basic hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and kinky. Knowing your hair type can help you choose a hairstyle that works with your hair's natural texture and helps you achieve the look you want.

If you're not sure what your hair type is, take a close look at your hair when it's clean and dry. Here are some general characteristics of each hair type:

Straight Hair

Straight hair is naturally smooth and shiny, and it tends to be thin and fine. If you have straight hair, you can wear it long or short, and you have a wide range of styling options.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair has a natural texture that falls somewhere between straight and curly. It tends to be thicker than straight hair, and it may have a tendency to frizz. If you have wavy hair, you can wear it in a variety of styles, from loose waves to tight curls.

Curly Hair

Curly hair has a spiral or corkscrew texture that can range from loose to tight. It tends to be thicker than straight or wavy hair, and it may be prone to dryness and frizz. If you have curly hair, you can wear it in a variety of styles, from loose waves to tight curls.

Kinky Hair

Kinky hair has a tightly coiled or zigzag texture. It tends to be very thick and coarse, and it may be prone to dryness and breakage. If you have kinky hair, you can wear it in a variety of styles, from tight coils to loose waves.

Once you've identified your hair type, you can start looking for hairstyles that work with your hair's natural texture. But before you do that, there's one more thing to consider.

Choosing the Right Hair Length

The length of your hair can also have a big impact on how it looks and how easy it is to style. Here are some general guidelines for choosing the right hair length:

Short Hair

Short hair is generally considered to be anything above shoulder length. It can be easier to style than longer hair, and it can also help you look more youthful and energetic. Short hair works well for most face shapes, but it's especially flattering on those with oval or heart-shaped faces.

Medium Hair

Medium hair is generally considered to be anything between shoulder length and mid-back length. It can be versatile and easy to style, and it works well for most face shapes. Medium hair is a good choice if you want a low-maintenance hairstyle that still looks polished and put-together.

Long Hair

Long hair is generally considered to be anything below mid-back length. It can be harder to style than shorter hair, but it can also be very versatile and dramatic. Long hair works well for most face shapes, but it can be especially flattering on those with oval or oblong faces.

Once you've chosen the right hair length for you, it's time to consider the final factor: hair color.

Picking the Perfect Hair Color

Your hair color can have a big impact on how your hairstyle looks and how it complements your skin tone. Here are some general guidelines for choosing the right hair color:

Dark Hair

If you have dark hair, you can choose from a range of shades, from deep brown to black. Dark hair can be very dramatic and striking, and it works well for most skin tones.

Blonde Hair

If you have blonde hair, you can choose from a range of shades, from pale blonde to golden blonde. Blonde hair can be very feminine and youthful, and it works well for most skin tones.

Red Hair

If you have red hair, you can choose from a range of shades, from deep auburn to bright copper. Red hair can be very bold and eye-catching, and it works well for most skin tones.

Highlights and Lowlights

Another option to consider is adding highlights or lowlights to your hair. Highlights are lighter strands of hair that are woven into your natural color, while lowlights are darker strands of hair that are woven into your natural color. Both can add depth and dimension to your hair and help you achieve a more natural-looking hairstyle.

Once you've chosen the right hair color for you, it's time to start looking for hairstyle ideas.

Hairstyle Ideas for Different Face Shapes

Now that you've determined your face shape, hair type, length, and color, it's time to start looking for hairstyle ideas that complement your features. Here are some ideas to get you started:

Oval Face Shape

For oval faces, almost any hairstyle works well. You can try a short pixie cut, a long layered cut, or even a trendy bob.

Round Face Shape

For round faces, try a hairstyle that adds height and volume on top, such as a long layered cut with side-swept bangs or a short pixie cut with a voluminous crown.
