How To Tone Down Bright Orange Hair

Description

Bright orange hair can be a bold and adventurous choice, but it's not always the look you're going for. If you've ended up with orange hair after dyeing or bleaching, you may be wondering how to tone down the color. There are several methods you can try at home to reduce the intensity of your orange hair, whether you want to go for a more natural-looking shade or just a less vibrant orange. Keep reading to learn how to tone down bright orange hair.

How to

There are several methods to tone down bright orange hair, including:

  • Using a toner
  • Applying a color corrector
  • Using a purple or blue shampoo
  • Using a vinegar rinse
  • Applying a hair mask

Step by Step

Using a Toner

A toner is a product that helps neutralize unwanted tones in your hair. To use a toner to tone down bright orange hair, follow these steps:

  1. Choose a toner with ash or cool undertones.
  2. Mix the toner with a developer according to the instructions on the package.
  3. Apply the toner to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  4. Leave the toner on for the recommended amount of time, usually around 20-30 minutes.
  5. Rinse the toner out of your hair with cool water.

Note that using a toner can be damaging to your hair, so be sure to follow up with a deep conditioning treatment.

Applying a Color Corrector

A color corrector is a product that helps neutralize specific tones in your hair. To use a color corrector to tone down bright orange hair, follow these steps:

  1. Choose a color corrector with blue or green undertones.
  2. Mix the color corrector with a developer according to the instructions on the package.
  3. Apply the color corrector to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  4. Leave the color corrector on for the recommended amount of time, usually around 20-30 minutes.
  5. Rinse the color corrector out of your hair with cool water.

Again, be sure to follow up with a deep conditioning treatment.

Using a Purple or Blue Shampoo

Purple or blue shampoos are products that help neutralize unwanted warm tones in your hair. To use a purple or blue shampoo to tone down bright orange hair, follow these steps:

  1. Wet your hair in the shower.
  2. Apply the purple or blue shampoo to your hair, focusing on the parts that are the most orange.
  3. Massage the shampoo into your hair and scalp, working up a lather.
  4. Leave the shampoo on your hair for a few minutes.
  5. Rinse the shampoo out of your hair with cool water.

Repeat this process every time you wash your hair until you achieve the desired tone.

Using a Vinegar Rinse

A vinegar rinse can help remove excess orange tones from your hair. To use a vinegar rinse to tone down bright orange hair, follow these steps:

  1. Mix equal parts water and apple cider vinegar in a spray bottle.
  2. Shampoo your hair as usual.
  3. Spray the vinegar rinse onto your hair, focusing on the parts that are the most orange.
  4. Leave the vinegar rinse on your hair for a few minutes.
  5. Rinse the vinegar rinse out of your hair with cool water.

Follow up with a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture to your hair.

Applying a Hair Mask

A hair mask can help nourish and hydrate your hair while toning down the orange color. To use a hair mask to tone down bright orange hair, follow these steps:

  1. Choose a hair mask with purple or blue undertones.
  2. Apply the hair mask to your hair, focusing on the parts that are the most orange.
  3. Leave the hair mask on for the recommended amount of time, usually around 10-20 minutes.
  4. Rinse the hair mask out of your hair with cool water.

For best results, use a hair mask once a week.

Tips

  • If you're unsure which method to try, start with a purple or blue shampoo.
  • Be patient - it may take several treatments to achieve the desired tone.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Consider seeking the help of a professional stylist if you're not comfortable dyeing or correcting your hair at home.

FAQ

Can I use lemon juice to tone down orange hair?

Lemon juice can be used to lighten your hair, but it's not recommended for toning down orange hair. Lemon juice can actually make orange hair more vibrant, so it's best to avoid it if you're trying to tone down the color.

Will toning down my orange hair damage it?

Using toner or color corrector can be damaging to your hair, so it's important to follow up with a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture and prevent breakage.

How long will it take to tone down my orange hair?

The amount of time it takes to tone down your orange hair will depend on the method you choose and the intensity of the color. It may take several treatments to achieve the desired tone.

Hair Care

To maintain healthy hair, be sure to:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and methods, you can successfully tone down your bright orange hair and achieve your desired look.
