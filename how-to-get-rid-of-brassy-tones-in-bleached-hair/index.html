How To Get Rid Of Brassy Tones In Bleached Hair

Written by William Oct 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have bleached hair, chances are you've experienced brassy tones at one point or another. Brassy tones can be caused by a variety of factors, including environmental factors, heat styling, and even the water you use to wash your hair. In this article, we'll go over how to get rid of brassy tones in bleached hair and keep your locks looking their best.

Description

Brassy tones in bleached hair occur when the hair takes on a yellow or orange hue. This is especially common in those with naturally dark hair who have bleached their hair to a lighter shade. The cause of brassy tones can vary, but it's often due to exposure to environmental factors like the sun, as well as heat styling tools like curling irons and straighteners. Additionally, the minerals in your water can contribute to brassy tones, especially if you have hard water.

How to Get Rid of Brassy Tones in Bleached Hair

There are several ways to get rid of brassy tones in bleached hair, including:

1. Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is specifically designed to counteract brassy tones in blonde, silver, and gray hair. The purple pigments in the shampoo neutralize the yellow and orange tones, leaving your hair looking brighter and cooler. Use the shampoo once or twice a week, leaving it on your hair for several minutes before rinsing it out.

2. Try a Toning Treatment

A toning treatment can also help eliminate brassy tones in bleached hair. These treatments are specially formulated to neutralize yellow and orange tones and can be used in place of your regular conditioner. Apply the treatment to your hair and leave it on for several minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Use a Hair Mask

A hair mask can help nourish and hydrate bleached hair while also helping to eliminate brassy tones. Look for a mask that is specifically formulated for blonde or bleached hair, and use it once a week or as needed. Apply the mask to your hair and leave it on for the recommended amount of time before rinsing it out.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get rid of brassy tones in bleached hair:

Step 1: Use a Purple Shampoo

Start by using a purple shampoo once or twice a week. Wet your hair and apply the shampoo, making sure to massage it in from root to tip. Leave the shampoo on for several minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 2: Try a Toning Treatment

If the purple shampoo isn't enough to eliminate your brassy tones, try a toning treatment. Apply the treatment to your hair after shampooing and leave it on for several minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 3: Use a Hair Mask

Once a week, use a hair mask to nourish and hydrate your bleached hair while also eliminating brassy tones. Apply the mask to your hair and leave it on for the recommended amount of time before rinsing it out.

Tips to Prevent Brassy Tones in Bleached Hair

Here are some tips to help prevent brassy tones in bleached hair:

1. Use a Water Softener

If you have hard water, consider using a water softener to help prevent mineral buildup in your hair. This can help prevent brassy tones and keep your hair looking its best.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools can contribute to brassy tones in bleached hair, so try to avoid using them as much as possible. If you must use a heat styling tool, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can also contribute to brassy tones in bleached hair, so protect your locks by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

FAQs

Q: Can I use regular shampoo on bleached hair?

A: Yes, you can use regular shampoo on bleached hair. However, using a purple or toning shampoo can help prevent brassy tones.

Q: How often should I use a hair mask?

A: You can use a hair mask once a week or as needed, depending on how damaged your hair is.

Q: Can I use a toning treatment instead of a purple shampoo?

A: Yes, a toning treatment can be used in place of a purple shampoo to help eliminate brassy tones.

Hair Care for Bleached Hair

Bleached hair requires special care to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for bleached hair:

1. Use a Sulfate-Free Shampoo

Sulfates can be harsh on bleached hair, so use a sulfate-free shampoo to minimize damage.

2. Hydrate Your Hair

Bleached hair can be prone to dryness, so use a hydrating conditioner or hair mask to keep it moisturized.

3. Protect Your Hair from Damage

Try to avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, and use a heat protectant spray when you do use them. Additionally, protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

With these tips and tricks, you can keep your bleached hair looking its best and eliminate brassy tones for a cooler, brighter look.
