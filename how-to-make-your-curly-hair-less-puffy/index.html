How To Make Your Curly Hair Less Puffy Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Aug 08, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how frustrating it can be to deal with frizz and puffiness. Luckily, there are many ways to tame your curls and get the smooth, defined look you want. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to make your curly hair less puffy.

Table of Contents

  • How to Make Your Curly Hair Less Puffy
  • Step-by-Step Process
  • Tips for Curly Hair Care
  • FAQs

How to Make Your Curly Hair Less Puffy

Here are some tips to help you make your curly hair less puffy:

1. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Switching to a satin pillowcase can make a big difference in how your curls look in the morning. Cotton pillowcases can cause friction and breakage, leading to frizz and puffiness. Satin pillowcases are gentler on your hair, reducing the risk of damage and keeping your curls smooth and defined.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Using a leave-in conditioner after washing your hair can help keep your curls moisturized and reduce frizz. Look for a lightweight formula that won't weigh your hair down or make it greasy. Apply the conditioner to wet hair, working it through with your fingers or a wide-tooth comb.

3. Use a Diffuser

A diffuser attachment on your hair dryer can help distribute heat evenly and reduce frizz. Use it on a low setting to dry your hair, scrunching the curls as you go. Avoid using a high heat setting, which can cause damage and make your hair more prone to puffiness.

4. Try a Hair Gel

Using a hair gel can help define your curls and keep them in place throughout the day. Look for a gel that is specifically designed for curly hair, with ingredients like aloe vera or glycerin to help moisturize and reduce frizz. Apply the gel to wet hair, scrunching it in with your fingers, and let it air dry or use a diffuser.

Step-by-Step Process

Here is a step-by-step process for making your curly hair less puffy:

1. Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to rinse thoroughly to avoid leaving any residue that could weigh your hair down.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help keep it moisturized and reduce frizz. Work the conditioner through your hair with your fingers or a wide-tooth comb.

3. Use a Diffuser

Attach a diffuser to your hair dryer and use it on a low setting to dry your hair. Scrunch your curls as you go to help define them and reduce frizz.

4. Apply a Hair Gel

After your hair is dry, apply a hair gel to help define your curls and hold them in place. Use a gel that is specifically designed for curly hair, and apply it by scrunching it in with your fingers.

Tips for Curly Hair Care

Here are some additional tips for caring for your curly hair:

1. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to frizz. Try washing your hair every other day or every few days, depending on how oily your scalp is.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Using a wide-tooth comb can help detangle your hair without causing breakage or frizz. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up, gently combing through any knots or tangles.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Using heat tools like flat irons or curling irons can cause damage and make your hair more prone to puffiness. Try air-drying your hair whenever possible, or use a diffuser on your hair dryer to dry your curls gently.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a regular towel to dry my curly hair?

A: Regular towels can cause friction and damage to your curls, leading to frizz and puffiness. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently blot your hair dry.

Q: How often should I get my hair trimmed?

A: Getting regular trims can help keep your curls healthy and reduce split ends. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks, or as needed to keep your hair looking its best.

Q: Can I brush my curly hair?

A: Brushing curly hair can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make your curly hair less puffy and achieve the smooth, defined curls you've always wanted.
