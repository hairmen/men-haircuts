What Does A Low Fade Haircut Look Like

Written by Daniel Nov 28, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you are looking to switch up your hairstyle, you may be considering a low fade haircut. This popular style is characterized by a gradual taper from the hair on the sides and back of the head to the longer hair on top. In this article, we will go over what a low fade haircut looks like, how to get it, and how to take care of it.

Description

A low fade haircut is a style where the hair on the sides and back of the head gradually tapers down to the skin. The transition from the longer hair on top to the shorter hair on the sides and back is seamless and smooth. The hair on top can be styled in a variety of ways, from a classic crew cut to a messy textured look.

How to Get a Low Fade Haircut

If you want to get a low fade haircut, we recommend going to a professional stylist or barber. They will be able to assess your hair type and face shape to determine the best length and style for your hair. The process typically involves using clippers to gradually taper the hair on the sides and back of the head down to the desired length. The hair on top is then styled as desired.

Step by Step

1. Start by washing your hair and towel drying it.

2. Communicate with your stylist or barber about the length and style you want.

3. They will use clippers to gradually taper the hair on the sides and back of your head down to the desired length.

4. The hair on top is then styled as desired.

5. After the cut, ask your stylist or barber for any styling tips or product recommendations.

Tips

- Bring a picture of the style you want to your stylist or barber to ensure clear communication.

- Consider the maintenance required for the style before getting it. A low fade haircut may require regular touch-ups to maintain the seamless transition.

- Use quality hair products to keep your hair looking and feeling its best.

FAQ

What is the difference between a low fade and a high fade?

A low fade starts the taper near the ear and gradually tapers down to the skin. A high fade, on the other hand, starts the taper higher up on the head and tapers down to the skin.

How often should I get a low fade?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you want to maintain the style. Generally, a low fade should be touched up every 2-4 weeks.

Hair Care

To maintain a low fade haircut, it is important to keep your hair clean and healthy. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner and avoid using hot tools like hair dryers or straighteners too often. Use styling products like pomade or wax to keep your hair in place and looking great.

Overall, a low fade haircut is a stylish and versatile option for anyone looking to switch up their hairstyle. With proper care and maintenance, you can keep your low fade looking fresh and stylish for weeks to come.
