Why Do Polynesians Have Curly Hair

Written by Daniel Aug 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Polynesians are known for their beautiful curly hair. For many people, curly hair is a symbol of beauty and uniqueness. But have you ever wondered why Polynesians have curly hair? In this article, we will explore the reasons behind this unique feature and provide some tips for taking care of your curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • What causes curly hair?
  • Polynesian genetics and curly hair
  • How to take care of curly hair
  • FAQ about Polynesian curly hair

What causes curly hair?

Curly hair is caused by the shape of the hair follicle. If the hair follicle is oval, the hair will be straight. If the hair follicle is round, the hair will be curly. The shape of the hair follicle is determined by genetics, so curly hair is often an inherited trait.

Polynesian genetics and curly hair

Polynesians have a unique genetic makeup that contributes to their curly hair. They are descended from the Austronesian people who migrated to the Pacific Islands over 3,000 years ago. These people had a genetic mutation that caused their hair follicles to be more round than oval, resulting in curly hair.

In addition to genetics, environmental factors can also contribute to the texture of Polynesian hair. The warm, humid climate of the Pacific Islands can cause hair to become frizzy and more curly.

Step by step guide to get curly hair like Polynesians

If you want to achieve the Polynesian curly hair look, here are some steps you can take:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to prevent frizz and add moisture to your hair.
  3. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair while it is wet.
  4. Apply a curl-enhancing product, such as a curl cream or gel, to define your curls.
  5. Scrunch your hair with a microfiber towel or cotton t-shirt to remove excess water and enhance your curls.
  6. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on a low heat setting.
  7. Once your hair is dry, use a light oil or serum to add shine and reduce frizz.

How to take care of curly hair

Curly hair requires a different approach to hair care than straight hair. Here are some tips for taking care of your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to add moisture to your hair.
  • Detangle your hair gently with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  • Avoid using brushes or fine-tooth combs, which can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Avoid heat styling tools, which can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a microfiber towel or cotton t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel, which can cause frizz.
  • Apply a curl-enhancing product to define your curls.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, which can cause frizz.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQ about Polynesian curly hair

Q: Can Polynesians have straight hair?

A: Yes, Polynesians can have straight hair. While curly hair is a common trait among Polynesians, not all Polynesians have curly hair. The texture of a person's hair is determined by genetics and can vary within a population.

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your curly hair using heat styling tools such as a flat iron or blow dryer. However, it's important to use a heat protectant and to avoid overusing these tools, as they can cause damage to your hair.

Q: What is the best hairstyle for curly hair?

A: The best hairstyle for curly hair is one that allows your curls to shine. Many people with curly hair opt for a layered haircut, which can help define their curls and prevent frizz.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It's generally recommended to wash curly hair every 2-3 days, as over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause dryness.

Q: Can I dye my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can dye your curly hair. However, it's important to use a dye specifically formulated for curly hair and to follow the instructions carefully to avoid damage.

Conclusion

Polynesians have curly hair due to a genetic mutation that causes their hair follicles to be more round than oval. Taking care of curly hair requires a different approach than straight hair, but with the right products and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, defined curls like Polynesians.
