How To Cut Your Own Mohawk Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Nov 30, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking to rock a bold and edgy hairstyle, a mohawk fade might be just what you need. This haircut features a shaved or buzzed strip of hair down the center of your head, with shorter hair on the sides that gradually fades into a longer length at the top. It's a versatile look that can work for any hair type or texture, and you don't even need to go to a salon to achieve it. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut your own mohawk fade like a pro.

What You'll Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the following tools on hand:

  • Clippers with different guard sizes
  • Scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror (or two)
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Hair gel or wax (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and towel-drying your hair. Then, comb it out to remove any tangles or knots. If your hair is longer than 1 inch, you may need to trim it down with scissors or clippers before you start the fade.

Step 2: Determine the Length of the Fade

Decide how short you want the sides to be. A typical mohawk fade will start with a clipper guard size of 1 or 2 on the sides and gradually blend into a longer length at the top. Make sure you have the appropriate guard sizes available.

Step 3: Buzz the Sides

Starting with the shortest guard size, buzz the hair on the sides of your head. Use upward strokes, moving from the bottom to the top of your head. Repeat this step with progressively longer guard sizes until you reach the desired length at the top of the fade. Make sure to blend the hair smoothly so there are no harsh lines.

Step 4: Trim the Top

Using scissors or clippers, trim the hair on the top of your head to the desired length. Keep in mind that the top should be longer than the sides to achieve the classic mohawk shape.

Step 5: Create the Mohawk Strip

Using clippers or scissors, create a strip of hair down the center of your head. This strip should be about 2-3 inches wide, depending on your preference. Make sure it's centered and straight.

Step 6: Blend the Mohawk Strip

Using the same guard size as the top of the fade, blend the hair on the sides of the mohawk strip. This will create a seamless transition from the shorter sides to the longer top. Be careful not to cut into the strip itself.

Step 7: Refine the Fade

Use a smaller guard size to refine the fade and remove any remaining lines or unevenness. Pay close attention to the area around the ears and the nape of the neck.

Step 8: Style Your Mohawk

Apply hair gel or wax to the top of your hair, and style it into a point or spike. You can also try different variations, such as a messy or textured look.

Tips for a Perfect Mohawk Fade

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down gradually. It's always easier to take off more hair than to add it back.
  • Use multiple mirrors to check the back of your head and make sure the fade is even.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time and be patient.
  • Practice makes perfect. If you're not happy with your first attempt, keep trying until you get the hang of it.

FAQ

Can I cut a mohawk fade on any hair type?

Yes, a mohawk fade can work on any hair type or texture, from straight to curly to wavy. Just make sure you have the right tools and follow the steps carefully.

Do I need to buzz my hair all the way down to the skin?

No, you can choose the length of the fade based on your personal preference. A mohawk fade can range from a 1 or 2 guard size to a longer length at the top.

How often do I need to trim a mohawk fade?

This depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain the look. Generally, you'll need to trim the sides every 1-2 weeks and the top every 3-4 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for a Mohawk Fade

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it healthy and clean.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers or flat irons, as this can damage the hair and make it harder to style.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or using a hair product with SPF.

Cutting your own mohawk fade can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it does take some practice and patience. By following these steps and tips, you'll be able to achieve a stylish and edgy look that's sure to turn heads.
