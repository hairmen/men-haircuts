How To Do A 60 S Flip Hairstyle A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you feeling nostalgic for the good old days of the 1960s? One of the most iconic hairstyles of that era was the "flip" - a bouncy, voluminous hairstyle that was popularized by stars like Mary Tyler Moore and Jackie Kennedy. If you want to recreate this retro look, keep reading for a step-by-step guide.

Description

The 60's flip hairstyle was a popular hairdo in the 1960s. It was characterized by a flipped outwards curl that started from the chin level and was styled with a lot of volume. It was an iconic look that was worn by many women during that era, including famous personalities like Mary Tyler Moore and Jackie Kennedy.

How to Do a 60's Flip Hairstyle

Here's a step-by-step guide to recreating the 60's flip hairstyle:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Begin by washing and drying your hair. You want to start with a clean slate so that your hair is easier to style. Blow-dry your hair until it's completely dry.

Step 2: Section your hair

Section your hair into three parts: the top, the sides, and the back. Clip the top section away so that it's out of the way.

Step 3: Tease the crown

Take the back section of your hair and tease it at the crown. This will create volume and lift for your flip. Use a teasing comb to backcomb your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down. Once you've teased the entire section, smooth it out with a brush.

Step 4: Curl the ends

Using a large barrel curling iron, curl the ends of your hair outwards. Hold the curling iron vertically and wrap the ends of your hair around the barrel, curling away from your face. Hold the curl for a few seconds, then release it. Repeat this process for the entire back section of your hair.

Step 5: Brush it out

Once you've curled the entire back section of your hair, use a brush to gently brush out the curls. This will create the "flip" effect.

Step 6: Repeat on the sides

Repeat steps 3-5 on the side sections of your hair. Tease the roots, curl the ends, and brush it out to create the flip effect.

Step 7: Style the top section

Take down the top section of your hair and style it as desired. You can leave it straight or add some curls for extra volume.

Step 8: Finish with hairspray

To help your flip stay in place, finish with a light mist of hairspray.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when recreating the 60's flip hairstyle:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot styling tools to protect your hair from damage.
  • Don't be afraid to tease your hair - the more volume, the better!
  • For an extra retro touch, add a headband or scarf to complete the look.

FAQ

What hair length is best for the 60's flip hairstyle?

The 60's flip hairstyle looks best on shoulder-length or shorter hair. If your hair is longer, you can still achieve the look by creating a faux bob.

How do I maintain my 60's flip hairstyle?

To maintain your 60's flip hairstyle, avoid touching or brushing your hair too much throughout the day. You can also use dry shampoo to help absorb excess oil and keep your hair looking fresh.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, make sure to use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner. You should also avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair over time. If you do use hot tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to recreate the iconic 60's flip hairstyle in no time. Whether you're dressing up for a retro-themed party or just want to add some vintage flair to your everyday look, this hairstyle is sure to turn heads!
