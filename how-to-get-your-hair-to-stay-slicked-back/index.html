How To Get Your Hair To Stay Slicked Back A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Sep 10, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re going for a sleek and polished look, slicked back hair is a great option. However, getting your hair to stay in place can be a challenge. In this guide, we’ll go over step-by-step instructions on how to get your hair to stay slicked back, as well as some tips and tricks to help maintain the look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Your Hair to Stay Slicked Back
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Slicked Back Hair

How to Get Your Hair to Stay Slicked Back

Before we dive into the step-by-step instructions, let’s first discuss the basics of getting your hair to stay slicked back. One of the key components is using the right product. A strong-hold pomade or gel is essential to keep your hair in place. You’ll also want to make sure your hair is clean and dry before applying any product.

Another important factor is the direction of your hair. You want to make sure you’re combing your hair in the opposite direction of its natural growth. This will help create the slicked back look. Lastly, make sure you’re using a fine-tooth comb to evenly distribute the product and create a smooth finish.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that we’ve covered the basics, let’s go over the step-by-step instructions on how to get your hair to stay slicked back:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will help the product adhere better and last longer.

Step 2: Apply Product

Take a small amount of pomade or gel and rub it between your hands to warm it up. Apply it evenly throughout your hair, making sure to get the roots and ends.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Using a fine-tooth comb, comb your hair in the opposite direction of its natural growth. This will create the slicked back look.

Step 4: Smooth and Shape

Once your hair is combed back, use your hands to smooth and shape it into place. You can also use the comb to refine the shape and get rid of any flyaways.

Step 5: Add More Product (Optional)

If you need more hold or want a more polished look, you can add more product to your hair. Just be careful not to overdo it, as too much product can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help get your hair to stay slicked back:

  • Use a strong-hold product, but don’t overdo it. A little goes a long way.
  • Comb your hair in the opposite direction of its natural growth to create the slicked back look.
  • Use a fine-tooth comb to evenly distribute the product and create a smooth finish.
  • Smooth and shape your hair with your hands after combing it back.
  • Don’t touch your hair too much throughout the day, as this can cause it to lose its shape.

FAQ

How do I know if I’m using too much product?

If your hair looks greasy or feels heavy, you’re probably using too much product. Start with a small amount and work your way up if you need more hold.

Can I slick back curly or wavy hair?

Yes! While it may be more difficult to achieve the slicked back look with curly or wavy hair, it’s definitely possible. Just make sure you’re using a strong-hold product and combing your hair in the opposite direction of its natural growth.

How often should I wash my hair if I’m styling it in a slicked back look?

It’s best to wash your hair every other day if you’re using a lot of product. This will help prevent buildup and keep your hair looking fresh.

Hair Care for Slicked Back Hair

When styling your hair in a slicked back look, it’s important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and maintain its health. Here are some tips:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove any buildup from product.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain its shape.

With these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to get your hair to stay slicked back and maintain the look throughout the day. Remember to use a strong-hold product, comb your hair in the opposite direction of its natural growth, and smooth and shape your hair with your hands for a polished finish.
