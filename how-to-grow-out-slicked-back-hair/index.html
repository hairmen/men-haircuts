How To Grow Out Slicked Back Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Frank Oct 05, 2023 · 6 min read

If you've been sporting the slicked back hairstyle for a while and are now looking to switch things up, you might be wondering how to grow out slicked back hair. While it can be a bit tricky, it's definitely doable with the right approach. In this guide, we'll show you how to grow out slicked back hair step by step and provide you with some tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to help make the process as smooth as possible.

  • Step by Step Guide

How to Grow Out Slicked Back Hair

Growing out slicked back hair requires patience and a bit of effort, but it's definitely worth it if you're looking to switch up your hairstyle. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Start by letting your hair grow out

The first step to growing out slicked back hair is to simply let it grow. This means avoiding any haircuts or trims for a while, which can be tough if you're used to getting regular haircuts. However, it's important to resist the urge to trim your hair too early on, as this can slow down the growing process and make it harder to achieve the style you want.

Step 2: Experiment with different hairstyles

While you're waiting for your hair to grow out, you can experiment with different hairstyles to keep things fresh. You can try wearing your hair down, parting it differently, or even trying out a new hair accessory. This can help you get used to the idea of having longer hair and make the transition easier.

Step 3: Use hair products to manage your hair

As your hair grows out, you'll need to use hair products to manage it and keep it looking good. This might include using a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy, as well as using styling products like a pomade or hair wax to help shape your hair into the style you want.

Step 4: Visit your hairstylist for a trim

Once your hair has grown out a bit, it's a good idea to visit your hairstylist for a trim. This can help remove any split ends or damaged hair and give your hair a healthy start as you continue to grow it out. Be sure to communicate with your stylist about your goals for growing out your hair, as they can offer advice and guidance on how to achieve the look you want.

Step 5: Be patient

Growing out slicked back hair takes time, so it's important to be patient and stay committed to the process. You may experience some awkward phases where your hair looks a bit messy or unkempt, but don't let this discourage you. With the right hair care and styling products, you'll be able to achieve the look you want in no time.

Step 4: Visit your hairstylist for a trim

Step 5: Be patient

Tips for Growing Out Slicked Back Hair

Here are some tips to help make the process of growing out slicked back hair a bit easier:

  • Invest in quality hair care products to keep your hair healthy
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, which can damage your hair
  • Consider getting a shorter haircut to make the transition easier
  • Be patient and don't give up on the process

FAQs

Can I still style my hair while I'm growing it out?

Absolutely! While you're growing out slicked back hair, you can still style it using hair products like a pomade or hair wax. Just be sure to use these products sparingly and avoid using too much heat on your hair.

How long does it take to grow out slicked back hair?

The length of time it takes to grow out slicked back hair can vary depending on how quickly your hair grows and how long you want your hair to be. However, on average, it can take anywhere from six months to a year to grow out slicked back hair.

Hair Care Tips for Growing Out Slicked Back Hair

Here are some hair care tips to help keep your hair healthy and looking good as you grow it out:

  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage your hair and slow down the growing process
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair to avoid breakage and damage

Conclusion

Growing out slicked back hair can be a bit of a challenge, but with the right approach, it's definitely doable. By following the steps and tips outlined in this guide, you'll be able to achieve the look you want and enjoy healthy, beautiful hair. So don't be afraid to make the switch – your new hairstyle is just a few months away!
