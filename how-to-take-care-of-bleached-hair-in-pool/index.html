Description

Bleached hair looks gorgeous, but it needs special care to maintain its health and shine. If you're a pool lover, you must know that chlorine in pool water can damage your bleached hair. Chlorine removes natural oils from the hair, making it dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. In this post, we'll discuss how to take care of bleached hair in the pool and maintain its health.

How to Take Care of Bleached Hair in Pool

If you're planning to swim in the pool, you can take the following steps to protect your bleached hair:

  • Wet your hair before you enter the pool. Dry hair absorbs more chlorine, so wetting your hair before entering the pool reduces chlorine absorption.
  • Wear a swimming cap. A swimming cap can protect your hair from chlorine and other pool chemicals.
  • Rinse your hair after swimming. Rinse your hair with fresh water immediately after swimming to remove chlorine from your hair.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo. A clarifying shampoo can remove chlorine buildup from your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner. A leave-in conditioner can moisturize your hair and protect it from further damage.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to take care of your bleached hair in the pool:

  1. Before entering the pool, wet your hair with fresh water. This will reduce the amount of chlorine your hair absorbs.
  2. Wear a swimming cap to protect your hair from chlorine and other pool chemicals.
  3. After swimming, rinse your hair with fresh water to remove chlorine and other pool chemicals.
  4. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove chlorine buildup from your hair.
  5. Apply a leave-in conditioner to moisturize your hair and protect it from further damage.

Tips for Taking Care of Bleached Hair in Pool

Here are some tips to take care of your bleached hair in the pool:

  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your bleached hair.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair. Hot water can make your hair dry and frizzy.
  • Avoid using heated styling tools on your hair. Heated styling tools can damage your bleached hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun. Wear a hat or use a hair serum with SPF to protect your hair from the sun's harmful rays.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about taking care of bleached hair in the pool:

  • Can chlorine damage bleached hair? Yes, chlorine can damage bleached hair by making it dry, brittle, and prone to breakage.
  • Can I swim with bleached hair? Yes, you can swim with bleached hair, but you need to take special care to protect it from chlorine and other pool chemicals.
  • How often should I use a clarifying shampoo on my bleached hair? You should use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove chlorine buildup from your hair.

Hair Care for Bleached Hair in Pool

To maintain the health and shine of your bleached hair, you should follow a proper hair care routine. Here are some tips for hair care for bleached hair:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping natural oils from your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heated styling tools on your hair. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes or relaxers.

By following these tips, you can take care of your bleached hair in the pool and maintain its health and shine. Remember to protect your hair from chlorine and other pool chemicals, and follow a proper hair care routine to keep your hair healthy and beautiful.
