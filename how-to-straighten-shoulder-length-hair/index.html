How To Straighten Shoulder Length Hair

Written by Aprilia Aug 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your frizzy and curly hair? Do you want to achieve a sleek and straight look? If you have shoulder length hair, you're in luck because it's easier to straighten compared to longer hair. In this article, we'll discuss how to straighten shoulder length hair and provide you with tips and tricks to achieve your desired look.

How to Straighten Shoulder Length Hair

Straightening your shoulder length hair can be done in different ways. You can use a flat iron, a hair straightening brush, or blow dry your hair with a round brush. Whichever method you choose, it's important to prepare your hair properly to avoid damage.

1. Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help hydrate your hair and make it easier to straighten. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel dry it. Avoid rubbing your hair with the towel as this can cause frizz.

2. Apply a Heat Protectant

Before using any heat styling tools, it's important to apply a heat protectant. This will help prevent damage to your hair caused by heat. Spray the heat protectant evenly on your hair, focusing on the ends where there's more damage.

3. Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a round brush, blow dry your hair until it's 80% dry. This will make it easier to straighten with a flat iron or hair straightening brush. Use a nozzle attachment to focus the airflow on your hair and avoid frizz.

4. Straighten Your Hair

Section your hair and start straightening it with a flat iron or hair straightening brush. Take small sections of hair and run the flat iron or brush through it slowly. Don't hold the heat styling tool in one place for too long as this can cause damage. Repeat this process until you've straightened all sections of your hair.

Step by Step Guide

If you want a more detailed guide on how to straighten shoulder length hair, here's a step by step process:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel dry it.

Step 2: Apply a Heat Protectant

Spray a heat protectant evenly on your hair.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a round brush, blow dry your hair until it's 80% dry.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Section your hair using hair clips or ties. Start with the bottom section and work your way up.

Step 5: Straighten Your Hair

Take small sections of hair and run the flat iron or hair straightening brush through it slowly. Repeat this process until you've straightened all sections of your hair.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you achieve a straight and sleek look:

  • Use a high-quality flat iron or hair straightening brush.
  • Don't hold the heat styling tool in one place for too long.
  • Use a heat protectant to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Blow dry your hair with a round brush to make it easier to straighten.
  • Section your hair to make the straightening process easier.

FAQs

Can I straighten my hair without a heat protectant?

No, it's not recommended to straighten your hair without a heat protectant. Heat styling tools can cause damage to your hair, and a heat protectant can help prevent this.

How often can I straighten my hair?

It's not recommended to straighten your hair every day as this can cause damage. Try to limit heat styling to once a week or less.

What should I do if I accidentally burn my hair?

If you accidentally burn your hair, stop using the heat styling tool immediately. Apply a hair mask or treatment to help repair the damage. Avoid heat styling your hair until it has fully recovered.

Hair Care Tips

Straightening your hair can cause damage, so it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often.
  • Apply a hair mask or treatment once a week.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve a straight and sleek look for your shoulder length hair. Remember to always use a heat protectant and take care of your hair to prevent damage.
