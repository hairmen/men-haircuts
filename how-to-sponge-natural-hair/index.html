How To Sponge Natural Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 18, 2023 · 3 min read

If you're looking for an easy and low-maintenance hairstyle, sponging your natural hair may be a great option for you. Sponging is a technique that involves using a sponge with small holes to create defined curls and coils in your hair. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to sponge your natural hair and provide tips for maintaining your style.

Table of Contents

  • How to Sponge Natural Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Style
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to Sponge Natural Hair

Sponging is a simple process that can be done with just a few tools:

  • A sponge with small holes
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A leave-in conditioner or styling product

Before you start sponging, make sure your hair is clean and free of tangles. You may also want to detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or brush before starting.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to sponge your natural hair:

  1. Moisten your hair with the spray bottle filled with water. Make sure your hair is damp but not soaked.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to your hair. This will help define your curls and coils and keep them in place.
  3. Take the sponge and press it against your hair. Twist the sponge in a circular motion while moving it across your hair.
  4. Continue sponging your hair until you achieve the desired curl pattern. You can sponge your entire head or just certain sections of your hair.
  5. Once you've finished sponging, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair.

That's it! Sponging is an easy and quick way to achieve defined curls and coils in your natural hair.

Tips for Maintaining Your Style

Here are some tips to help you maintain your sponged natural hair:

  • Use a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase to protect your hair at night.
  • Avoid over-manipulating your hair to prevent frizz and maintain your curl pattern.
  • Refresh your curls with a spray bottle filled with water and a little leave-in conditioner.
  • Use a lightweight oil or serum to add shine and moisture to your hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about sponging natural hair:

Can I sponge my hair if it's not natural?

Sponging is typically done on natural hair, but you can try it on relaxed or chemically-treated hair as well. Keep in mind that the results may be different and you may need to use different products to achieve your desired style.

How long does sponging last?

The duration of your sponged style will depend on your hair type and how well you maintain your hair. Typically, sponged styles can last anywhere from 2-5 days.

Can I sponge my hair every day?

You can sponge your hair as often as you'd like, but keep in mind that over-manipulating your hair can cause damage and breakage. It's best to limit sponging to once or twice a week.

Hair Care

When sponging your natural hair, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips for healthy hair care:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or moisturizing product.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant before using heat styling tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or styling products that can damage your hair.

By following these tips and incorporating sponging into your hair routine, you can achieve defined curls and coils in your natural hair with ease!
