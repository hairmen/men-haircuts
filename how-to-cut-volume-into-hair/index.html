How To Cut Volume Into Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Sep 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to add some volume to your hair, cutting it is a great way to achieve that. In this article, we'll show you how to cut volume into hair in a relaxed and easy-to-understand way.

Description

Adding volume to your hair can help make it look fuller and thicker. While there are many ways to achieve this, cutting your hair is an effective and popular method. By strategically cutting your hair, you can create layers and texture that add volume and dimension.

How to

Cutting volume into your hair is a process that involves a few key steps. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to cut and style. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to help add even more volume to your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair ties or clips to keep each section separate.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Start with the back sections. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting at the bottom and working your way up. This will create layers and add volume. Repeat this process for each section in the back.

Step 4: Cut the Front

Move on to the front sections. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Make sure to blend the front sections with the back sections.

Step 5: Add Texture

Once you've finished cutting your hair, you can add even more volume by creating texture. Use a razor or texturizing shears to create choppy layers throughout your hair. This will help create movement and volume.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting volume into your hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry to ensure an accurate cut.
  • Always cut less than you think you need. You can always go back and cut more if needed.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can result in a botched haircut.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about cutting volume into hair:

Q: Will cutting my hair make it look thinner?

A: No, cutting your hair can actually make it look thicker and fuller. By creating layers and texture, you can add volume and dimension to your hair.

Q: Can I cut volume into my hair if it's short?

A: Yes, you can still cut volume into short hair. Just be sure to use small sections and cut at an angle to create layers and texture.

Q: Do I need to use any products to add volume to my hair after cutting it?

A: No, you don't necessarily need to use any products to add volume to your hair after cutting it. However, using a volumizing mousse or spray can help enhance the effect.

Hair Care

To maintain your newly cut hair and its added volume, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as brushes can cause breakage.

Now that you know how to cut volume into your hair, you can experiment with different styles and looks. Whether you're going for a sleek and polished look or a more tousled and textured style, cutting volume into your hair can help you achieve the perfect look.
