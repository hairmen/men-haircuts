How to Style Short Bits at Front of Hair: Tips, FAQ, and Hair Care

If you're someone who has short bits of hair at the front, it can be challenging to style them. However, you don't have to worry about it anymore because we've got you covered. In this article, we'll guide you on how to style short bits at the front of your hair. From step-by-step instructions to tips, and hair care, we've included everything you need to know. So, let's get started.

How to Style Short Bits at Front of Hair

Styling short bits at the front of your hair can be a bit tricky, but with a few simple steps, you can achieve the look you desire. Here's how:

1. Blow-dry your hair

Start by blow-drying your hair to create volume and lift at the roots. Use a round brush to lift the hair at the front and blow-dry it upwards to create a voluminous look.

2. Section off the front bits of hair

Next, section off the front bits of hair that you want to style. You can use a hair clip to keep the rest of your hair out of the way.

3. Apply a styling product

Apply a small amount of styling product to the front bits of hair to add texture and hold. Use a product that is suitable for your hair type and desired style.

4. Style your hair

Use a styling tool, such as a curling iron or straightener, to style your hair as desired. You can create waves, curls, or a sleek and straight look depending on your preference.

5. Finish with hairspray

Finish off your look by spraying hairspray on the front bits of hair to keep them in place. Use a light hold hairspray to avoid a stiff, unnatural look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to style short bits at the front of your hair:

  1. Blow-dry your hair to create volume and lift at the roots.
  2. Section off the front bits of hair that you want to style.
  3. Apply a small amount of styling product to the front bits of hair.
  4. Style your hair with a curling iron or straightener.
  5. Finish off with a light hold hairspray.

Tips for Styling Short Bits at Front of Hair

Here are some tips to help you style short bits at the front of your hair:

  • Use a small amount of styling product to avoid a greasy look.
  • Choose a styling tool that is suitable for your hair type and desired style.
  • Blow-dry your hair upwards to create a voluminous look.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what suits you best.
  • Don't overuse hairspray as it can make your hair look unnatural.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q. How do I style short bits at the front of my hair without heat?

A. You can style short bits at the front of your hair without heat by applying a styling product and using your fingers to create texture and volume. You can also try braiding or twisting the hair to add interest.

Q. How often should I wash my hair if I have short bits at the front?

A. It's recommended to wash your hair every 2-3 days if you have short bits at the front. Over-washing can strip the hair of its natural oils, making it dry and brittle.

Q. Can I style short bits at the front of my hair if I have curly hair?

A. Yes, you can style short bits at the front of your hair if you have curly hair. However, you may need to use more styling product to hold the hair in place.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

If you have short hair, it's essential to take care of it to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some hair care tips for short hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate the hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage to the hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your haircut.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a hair protectant spray.

Styling short bits at the front of your hair may seem challenging, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a variety of looks. Experiment with different styles and don't be afraid to try something new. Remember to take care of your hair by following a regular hair care routine, and your short hair will look healthy and shiny in no time.
