How to Angle Your Own Hair: Step-by-Step Guide | Hair Care Tips

Do you want to add some edge to your hair? Angled haircuts are a great way to add some style to your hair. However, going to the salon every time can be quite expensive. Luckily, with the right tools and techniques, you can angle your own hair at home. In this article, we will guide you through the process of how to angle your own hair.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How To Angle Your Own Hair

Angled haircuts are a great way to add some edge to your hair. However, before you start angling, you need to decide which type of angle you want. There are two types of angles you can go for:

  • Long angle: This type of angle starts from the front and gradually gets longer towards the back.
  • Short angle: This type of angle starts from the back and gradually gets shorter towards the front.

Once you have decided which type of angle you want, it's time to start angling your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to angle your own hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Part your hair in the middle.
  3. Brush your hair to make sure it's tangle-free.
  4. Take a small section of hair from one side and comb it straight.
  5. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle.
  6. Repeat on the other side.
  7. Check if both sides are even.
  8. If needed, make adjustments to even out the angles.
  9. Comb your hair and style it as desired.

Tips for a Perfect Angled Cut

Here are some tips to help you get a perfect angled cut:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid making big mistakes.
  • Take your time and be patient.
  • Use a mirror to check your angles.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about angling your own hair:

  • Can I angle my own hair if it's curly?
    Yes, you can angle your own hair even if it's curly. However, it might be a bit more challenging and require more time and patience.
  • How often should I angle my hair?
    It depends on how fast your hair grows and how often you want to change your look. On average, most people angle their hair every 4-6 weeks.
  • What if I make a mistake?
    Don't panic! Mistakes happen. If you make a mistake, you can always adjust it or even it out later. If the mistake is big, you can always go to a salon to fix it.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips to take care of your angled hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain your angled cut.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

Now that you know how to angle your own hair, you can add some edge to your hair without breaking the bank. Follow these steps and tips to get a perfect angled cut at home.
