How To Contour Each Face Shape A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Nov 28, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Contouring has been a popular makeup technique for years, but it can be intimidating to know where to start. The key to successful contouring is understanding your face shape and using the right techniques to enhance your natural features. In this guide, we will go over how to contour each face shape, including round, square, heart, diamond, and oval faces. Let's get started!

Contouring for Round Faces

If you have a round face, you want to create the illusion of a slimmer face by contouring along the cheekbones and jawline. Start by using a contour shade that is one to two shades darker than your skin tone. Apply the contour shade along your cheekbones, starting from your ears and blending towards the center of your cheeks. Then, apply contour along your jawline, starting at your ears and blending towards the center of your chin. Finally, blend the contour shade along your hairline to create a more defined shape.

For highlight, use a shade that is one to two shades lighter than your skin tone. Apply it to the center of your forehead, under your eyes, and on the bridge of your nose. This will help to bring those areas forward and create a more balanced look.

Contouring for Square Faces

If you have a square face, you want to soften your features by contouring along the jawline and forehead. Start by applying a contour shade along your jawline, starting at your ears and blending towards the center of your chin. Then, apply contour along your forehead, starting at your hairline and blending towards the center of your forehead. Finally, blend the contour shade along the sides of your nose to create a more defined shape.

For highlight, use a shade that is one to two shades lighter than your skin tone. Apply it to the center of your forehead, under your eyes, and on the bridge of your nose. This will help to bring those areas forward and create a more balanced look.

Contouring for Heart Faces

If you have a heart-shaped face, you want to create balance by contouring along the temples and jawline. Start by applying a contour shade along your temples, starting at your hairline and blending towards your cheeks. Then, apply contour along your jawline, starting at your ears and blending towards the center of your chin. Finally, blend the contour shade along the sides of your nose to create a more defined shape.

For highlight, use a shade that is one to two shades lighter than your skin tone. Apply it to the center of your forehead, under your eyes, and on the bridge of your nose. This will help to bring those areas forward and create a more balanced look.

Contouring for Diamond Faces

If you have a diamond-shaped face, you want to create balance by contouring along the forehead and chin. Start by applying a contour shade along your forehead, starting at your hairline and blending towards the center of your forehead. Then, apply contour along your chin, starting at the center and blending towards your jawline. Finally, blend the contour shade along your cheekbones to create a more defined shape.

For highlight, use a shade that is one to two shades lighter than your skin tone. Apply it to the center of your forehead, under your eyes, and on the bridge of your nose. This will help to bring those areas forward and create a more balanced look.

Contouring for Oval Faces

If you have an oval face, you want to enhance your natural features by contouring along the cheekbones and jawline. Start by applying a contour shade along your cheekbones, starting from your ears and blending towards the center of your cheeks. Then, apply contour along your jawline, starting at your ears and blending towards the center of your chin. Finally, blend the contour shade along your hairline to create a more defined shape.

For highlight, use a shade that is one to two shades lighter than your skin tone. Apply it to the center of your forehead, under your eyes, and on the bridge of your nose. This will help to bring those areas forward and create a more balanced look.

Contouring Tips

- Always blend your contour and highlight shades well to avoid harsh lines.

- Use a light hand when applying product and build up gradually.

- Use a matte contour shade to avoid any shimmer or shine.

- If you have fair skin, use a cool-toned contour shade. If you have darker skin, use a warm-toned contour shade.

- Don't forget to set your makeup with powder to make it last longer.

Contouring FAQ

Q: Do I need to contour every day?

A: No, contouring is not necessary for everyday makeup looks. It can be a fun technique to try for special occasions or when you want to enhance your features.

Q: Can contouring make my face look too harsh?

A: Yes, if not done correctly, contouring can make your face look too harsh. It's important to use a light hand and blend well to avoid any harsh lines.

Q: Can I contour with cream products?

A: Yes, cream products can work well for contouring. Just make sure to set the cream products with powder to make them last longer.

Hair Care for Contoured Faces

Once you've contoured your face, you want to make sure your hair complements your new look. Here are some tips for hair care:

- If you have a heart-shaped face, try a side-swept hairstyle to balance your features.

- If you have a square-shaped face, try a layered haircut to soften your features.

- If you have a round-shaped face, try a high ponytail or updo to elongate your face.

- If you have a diamond-shaped face, try a chin-length bob to accentuate your jawline.

- If you have an oval-shaped face, you can rock any hairstyle!

Now that you know how to contour each face shape, you can enhance your natural features and feel confident in your makeup skills. Remember to use a light hand and blend well for a flawless finish. Happy contouring!
