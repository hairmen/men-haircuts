Why Has My Hair Stopped Holding Curls

Written by Daniel Nov 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending hours curling your hair only to have it fall flat within minutes? It can be frustrating, but don't worry, you're not alone. Many people experience hair that doesn't hold curls, and there are several reasons why. In this article, we'll explore the possible causes and provide tips on how to make your curls last longer.

  1. Cause #1: Hair Type
  2. Cause #2: Over-Styling

Cause #1: Hair Type

One of the most common reasons why hair doesn't hold curls is due to hair type. If you have fine, straight hair, it may be more difficult to achieve long-lasting curls. This is because fine hair lacks the texture and volume that helps hold curls in place. On the other hand, if you have thick, coarse hair, it may hold curls better due to its natural texture and volume.

If you have fine hair and want to create curls that last, try using a smaller barrel curling iron and using a volumizing mousse before styling.

Cause #2: Over-Styling

Over-styling your hair can also cause your curls to fall flat. If you're using heat tools like curling irons or straighteners too often, it can damage your hair and make it harder to hold a curl. Additionally, using too many styling products, such as hairspray or mousse, can weigh down your hair and make it difficult for curls to stay in place.

To avoid over-styling, try to limit your use of heat tools and styling products. Instead, opt for heatless styling methods like braids or twist-outs.

Cause #3: Product Build-Up

Product build-up can also make it harder for your curls to hold. If you're using too much product or not washing your hair frequently enough, it can weigh down your hair and make it difficult for curls to stay in place. Additionally, using the wrong type of products for your hair type can also contribute to product build-up.

To avoid product build-up, make sure to wash your hair regularly and use products specifically designed for your hair type.

Cause #4: Heat Damage

Heat damage can also make it difficult for your hair to hold curls. If you're using heat tools like curling irons or straighteners at high temperatures, it can damage your hair and weaken its ability to hold a curl. Additionally, using heat tools on wet hair can also cause damage and make it harder for curls to stay in place.

To avoid heat damage, try to limit your use of heat tools and always use a heat protectant before styling. Additionally, make sure your hair is completely dry before using heat tools.

Tips for Making Your Curls Last Longer

Now that we've explored the possible causes of hair that doesn't hold curls, here are some tips for making your curls last longer:

  • Use a smaller barrel curling iron for tighter curls
  • Apply a volumizing mousse before styling to add texture and hold
  • Limit your use of heat tools and always use a heat protectant
  • Use a hairspray to set your curls in place

FAQs

Q: Can I make my curls last longer without using heat tools?

A: Yes, there are several heatless styling methods you can use to create long-lasting curls. Try braiding your hair before bed or using twist-outs to create defined curls.

Q: How often should I wash my hair to avoid product build-up?

A: It depends on your hair type and how much product you use. As a general rule, aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid product build-up.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair

Finally, to maintain healthy hair that is more likely to hold curls, here are some hair care tips to follow:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as it can cause damage
  • Limit your use of heat tools to avoid heat damage

By following these tips and avoiding common causes of hair that doesn't hold curls, you can achieve long-lasting, bouncy curls that will stay in place all day.
