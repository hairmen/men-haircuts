What Shampoo Do Dermatologists Recommend For Oily Hair In 2023

Written by William Aug 25, 2023 · 5 min read

Welcome to our hair care blog, where we share the latest tips and tricks to keep your locks looking healthy and beautiful. In this post, we will discuss the best shampoos recommended by dermatologists for oily hair. If you have been struggling with greasy hair, you are not alone. Oily hair can be frustrating and challenging to manage. But don't worry, we've got you covered with some expert advice on what shampoo to use.

What is Oily Hair?

Oily hair is caused by overactive sebaceous glands that produce an excess of sebum, a natural oil that keeps your hair and scalp moisturized. When there is too much sebum, it can make your hair look greasy, flat, and lifeless. Oily hair is more common in people with fine hair, but it can affect anyone, regardless of hair type.

How to Choose the Right Shampoo for Oily Hair?

Choosing the right shampoo for oily hair can be a daunting task, as there are so many options available in the market. However, dermatologists recommend looking for shampoos that are designed to remove excess oil and build-up without stripping your hair of its natural oils. Here are some tips to help you choose the right shampoo:

Look for a Clarifying Shampoo

Clarifying shampoos are designed to remove build-up from your scalp and hair, making them an excellent option for people with oily hair. Look for shampoos that contain ingredients like salicylic acid, tea tree oil, or charcoal, as they are known for their deep-cleansing properties.

Avoid Shampoos with Sulfates

Sulfates are harsh detergents that can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it dry and brittle. Look for shampoos that are sulfate-free or contain mild surfactants like cocamidopropyl betaine.

Choose a pH-Balanced Shampoo

Using a shampoo with a pH level that is too high or too low can disrupt the natural pH balance of your scalp, leading to dryness, irritation, and overproduction of oil. Choose a shampoo with a pH level between 4.5 and 5.5, which is the optimal range for healthy hair and scalp.

What Shampoo Do Dermatologists Recommend for Oily Hair?

Now that you know what to look for in a shampoo, let's talk about some of the best options recommended by dermatologists for oily hair:

1. Neutrogena Anti-Residue Shampoo

This clarifying shampoo is formulated to remove build-up from your scalp and hair, leaving it clean and refreshed. It contains no sulfates, parabens, or dyes, making it gentle enough for daily use.

2. Klorane Nettle Shampoo

This shampoo is enriched with nettle extract, which is known for its astringent and oil-regulating properties. It helps to control oil production, leaving your hair looking fresh and voluminous.

3. Paul Mitchell Tea Tree Special Shampoo

This shampoo contains tea tree oil, which is known for its antifungal and antibacterial properties. It helps to remove impurities from your scalp and hair, leaving it clean and healthy.

4. L'Oreal Paris Elvive Extraordinary Clay Shampoo

This shampoo is formulated with three types of clay, which work together to absorb excess oil and impurities from your scalp and hair. It also contains salicylic acid, which helps to exfoliate your scalp and prevent dandruff.

Tips for Managing Oily Hair

Using the right shampoo is essential for managing oily hair, but there are also some other things you can do to keep your hair looking fresh and clean:

Avoid Overwashing

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, leading to overproduction of oil. Try to limit washing your hair to every other day or every two days.

Avoid Touching Your Hair Too Much

Touching your hair too much can transfer oil from your hands to your hair, making it look greasy. Try to avoid touching your hair throughout the day.

Use Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo is an excellent option for absorbing excess oil between washes. Spray it on your roots and brush it through your hair to refresh your locks.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a conditioner if I have oily hair?

A: Yes, you can use a conditioner, but make sure to apply it only to the ends of your hair, not the roots. Look for lightweight conditioners that won't weigh down your hair or make it look greasy.

Q: Will washing my hair with hot water make it oilier?

A: Yes, washing your hair with hot water can stimulate the sebaceous glands, leading to overproduction of oil. Wash your hair with lukewarm water instead.

Q: Can I use a hair mask if I have oily hair?

A: Yes, but make sure to choose a hair mask that is specifically designed for oily hair. Look for masks that contain ingredients like clay or charcoal, which are known for their oil-absorbing properties.

Final Thoughts

Oily hair can be challenging to manage, but with the right shampoo and hair care routine, you can keep your locks looking healthy and beautiful. Choosing a shampoo that is designed to remove excess oil and build-up without stripping your hair of its natural oils is key. Remember to avoid overwashing, touching your hair too much, and using hot water, and use dry shampoo to absorb excess oil between washes. We hope these tips and recommendations help you achieve the healthy, shiny hair you deserve.
