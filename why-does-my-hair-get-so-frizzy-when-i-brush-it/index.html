Why Does My Hair Get So Frizzy When I Brush It

Written by Luffy Jun 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you ever brush your hair and end up with a frizzy mess? Don't worry, you're not alone! Many people experience frizz when they brush their hair, and it can be frustrating to deal with. In this article, we will explore the reasons why your hair gets frizzy when you brush it, and provide tips on how to prevent it from happening.

Why Does Frizz Happen?

Frizz happens when the hair cuticle is raised, allowing moisture to enter the hair shaft. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including humidity, dryness, and damage to the hair. When you brush your hair, you are essentially disrupting the hair cuticle, which can cause it to become frizzy.

Hair Type and Frizz

The type of hair you have can also contribute to frizz. People with curly or wavy hair are more prone to frizz because their hair cuticle is naturally more raised and porous than those with straight hair. Additionally, people with thin or fine hair may experience more frizz because their hair is more easily affected by environmental factors like humidity.

Brush Type and Frizz

The type of brush you use can also contribute to frizz. Brushes with natural bristles, like boar hair, can actually help to distribute the natural oils in your hair and reduce frizz. However, brushes with plastic bristles can create friction and cause the hair cuticle to become raised, resulting in frizz.

Brushing Wet Hair

Brushing wet hair can also contribute to frizz. When your hair is wet, it is more vulnerable and prone to damage. Brushing wet hair can cause the hair cuticle to become raised, resulting in frizz. It's best to wait until your hair is at least partially dry before brushing it.

Product Buildup and Frizz

Product buildup can also contribute to frizz. When you use too much product or don't wash your hair properly, it can cause a buildup of product on your hair. This buildup can cause the hair cuticle to become raised, resulting in frizz. It's important to use the correct amount of product for your hair type and to wash your hair regularly to prevent buildup.

Heat Styling and Frizz

Heat styling can also contribute to frizz. When you use heat on your hair, it can cause the hair cuticle to become raised, resulting in frizz. Additionally, using heat on already damaged hair can exacerbate the problem. It's important to use heat styling tools sparingly and to use a heat protectant product to minimize damage.

How to Prevent Frizz

Preventing frizz starts with proper hair care. Here are some tips for preventing frizz:

  • Use the correct products for your hair type
  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent product buildup
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or brush with natural bristles
  • Avoid brushing wet hair
  • Use a heat protectant product when heat styling
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week

FAQs

Q: Can brushing my hair too much cause frizz?

A: Yes, brushing your hair too much can cause the hair cuticle to become raised, resulting in frizz. It's best to brush your hair only when necessary and to use a wide-tooth comb or brush with natural bristles.

Q: Does humidity cause frizz?

A: Yes, humidity can cause frizz because it can cause the hair cuticle to become raised, allowing moisture to enter the hair shaft.

Q: Can using too much product cause frizz?

A: Yes, using too much product can cause a buildup on the hair, which can cause the hair cuticle to become raised, resulting in frizz.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional tips for keeping your hair healthy and reducing frizz:

  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair
  • Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with a towel
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles that can damage your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet with plenty of protein and vitamins
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

Conclusion

Frizz can be a frustrating problem, but it's not impossible to manage. By using the right products, tools, and techniques, you can keep your hair looking smooth and healthy. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid over-brushing, and use a wide-tooth comb or brush with natural bristles. With a little bit of care and attention, you can keep your hair looking its best and avoid frizz.
