How To Do Your Hair Like The 60S

Written by Eveline Aug 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to add some vintage flair to your hairstyle? Look no further than the swinging 60s! This era was full of iconic hairstyles that are still revered today. In this article, we will show you how to do your hair like the 60s with step-by-step instructions, tips, and FAQs. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step-by-Step
  • Tips
  • Hair Care
  • FAQ

How to

Before we dive into the specific hairstyles, let's talk about the basics of achieving a 60s look. First, you'll need to invest in some styling tools and products. A good hairspray, teasing comb, and hot rollers or a curling iron will be your best friends. You'll also want to consider using a volumizing mousse or powder to give your hair some extra lift.

Once you have your tools and products, it's time to start experimenting with different styles. The 60s were all about big, bold hair, so don't be afraid to go a little over-the-top. Teasing and backcombing your hair will help create that iconic bouffant look. You can also experiment with different types of curls and waves, like a classic beehive or loose, beachy waves.

Step-by-Step

Now, let's break down a few specific 60s hairstyles and how to achieve them:

The Bouffant

To create a bouffant, start by sectioning off the top portion of your hair. Use a teasing comb to backcomb the hair at the crown of your head, then smooth it out with a brush. Next, take the top section of hair and gently tease it, backcombing from the roots to the tips. Smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section, then use bobby pins to secure it in place. You can leave the rest of your hair down or pull it back into a ponytail or bun.

The Beehive

The beehive is a classic 60s hairstyle that's perfect for special occasions. To achieve this look, start by sectioning off the top portion of your hair. Use a teasing comb to backcomb the hair at the crown of your head, then smooth it out with a brush. Next, gather the back section of your hair into a ponytail or bun. Take the top section of hair and gently tease it, backcombing from the roots to the tips. Smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section, then use bobby pins to secure it in place. Finish the look by adding a hair accessory, like a headband or barrette.

Tips

  • Experiment with different types of curls and waves to find the perfect 60s look for you.
  • Don't be afraid to go a little over-the-top with your hair. The 60s were all about big, bold styles.
  • Invest in quality styling tools and products to make achieving your desired look easier.
  • Use a volumizing mousse or powder to give your hair extra lift and hold.
  • Consider getting a 60s-inspired haircut, like a shag or pixie cut, to make styling your hair easier.

Hair Care

To keep your 60s-inspired hair looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage. If you do use hot tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage. Finally, get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQ

What kind of hair works best for 60s hairstyles?

While 60s hairstyles can work for a variety of hair types, they tend to work best for medium to thick hair with some natural texture. If your hair is on the finer side, you may need to use more products and teasing to achieve the desired look.

How long does it take to do a 60s hairstyle?

The amount of time it takes to do a 60s hairstyle will depend on the specific style and your level of experience. Some styles, like a simple bouffant, can be achieved in just a few minutes. More complex styles, like a beehive or pin curls, may take closer to an hour.

Can I wear a 60s hairstyle every day?

While 60s hairstyles can be a fun way to mix up your look, they may not be practical for everyday wear. Some styles, like a beehive, can be quite elaborate and require a lot of time and effort to achieve. However, simpler styles like loose waves or a half-up bouffant can be more versatile and easier to wear on a daily basis.

There you have it - our guide to achieving that iconic 60s look! Whether you're going for a retro vibe or just want to mix up your hairstyle, these tips and techniques will help you achieve the perfect 60s look. Happy styling!
