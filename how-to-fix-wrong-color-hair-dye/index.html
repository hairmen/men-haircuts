How To Fix Wrong Color Hair Dye A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you struggling with a hair disaster after attempting to dye your hair at home? Don't worry, it's a common problem that many people face. The good news is that it's fixable. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to fix wrong color hair dye.

Description

Wrong color hair dye can be frustrating, especially when it doesn't turn out the way you expected. It could be too light, too dark, or the wrong tone altogether. However, there's no need to panic. You can easily fix the problem at home with a few simple steps.

How to Fix Wrong Color Hair Dye

Here are the steps you need to follow to fix wrong color hair dye:

Step 1: Assess the Damage

First, you need to assess the damage. Determine what color your hair is, what color you wanted, and what color you ended up with. This will help you decide on the appropriate solution.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

The next step is to wash your hair thoroughly with a clarifying shampoo. This will help remove excess hair dye and prepare your hair for the next step.

Step 3: Use a Color Remover

If your hair is too dark or has unwanted tones, you can use a color remover. Follow the instructions carefully and use a product that's designed for your hair type and the color you want to remove.

Step 4: Tone Your Hair

If your hair has turned out too light or has unwanted tones, you can use a toner. A toner will help correct the color and give your hair a more natural look. Again, make sure to follow the instructions and use the right product for your hair type and the color you want to achieve.

Step 5: Apply a Hair Dye

If you still don't have the desired color after using a color remover or toner, you can apply a hair dye. Choose a color that's close to the one you wanted and follow the instructions carefully.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when fixing wrong color hair dye:

  • Always do a patch test before using any hair dye or color remover
  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove excess hair dye
  • Follow the instructions carefully when using a color remover, toner, or hair dye
  • If you're not sure what to do, seek advice from a professional hairdresser

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing wrong color hair dye:

Q: Can I fix wrong color hair dye at home?

A: Yes, you can fix wrong color hair dye at home using a color remover, toner, or hair dye.

Q: How long should I wait before using a hair dye?

A: You should wait at least one week before using a hair dye after using a color remover or toner.

Q: Can I use a hair dye to lighten my hair?

A: Yes, you can use a hair dye to lighten your hair, but make sure to follow the instructions carefully and choose a product that's safe for your hair type.

Hair Care

Finally, it's important to take care of your hair after fixing wrong color hair dye. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner that's designed for color-treated hair. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and use a heat protectant spray when you do. Also, consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Conclusion

Fixing wrong color hair dye is possible with the right tools and techniques. Remember to assess the damage, use a color remover or toner if necessary, and apply a hair dye if needed. Follow the instructions carefully and take care of your hair afterwards. If you're not sure what to do, seek advice from a professional hairdresser. With a little patience and effort, you can achieve the hair color you want.
