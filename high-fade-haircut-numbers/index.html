High Fade Haircut Numbers A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Jun 29, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and modern haircut, a high fade might be just what you need. The high fade is a popular haircut that features a gradual taper on the sides and back of the head, with longer hair on top. However, there are different high fade haircut numbers that you can choose from, depending on the length of the hair you want to keep. In this article, we'll discuss everything you need to know about high fade haircut numbers.

What is a High Fade Haircut?

A high fade haircut is a type of men's haircut that features a gradual taper from short hair at the sides and back of the head to longer hair on top. This style is versatile and can be customized to suit different face shapes and hair types. The high fade is a popular choice for men who want a trendy and low-maintenance haircut.

How to Choose the Right High Fade Haircut Number

Choosing the right high fade haircut number depends on how short you want your hair to be. The high fade comes in different numbers, ranging from 0 to 8. The higher the number, the longer the hair on top will be. Here's a breakdown of the different high fade haircut numbers:

Number 0 High Fade

The number 0 high fade features shaved sides and back with no hair left. This is the shortest possible length for a high fade and is perfect for men who want a clean and edgy look.

Number 1 High Fade

The number 1 high fade features hair that's cut to 1/8 of an inch on the sides and back. This is a slightly longer length than the number 0 high fade, but still gives a close-cut appearance.

Number 2 High Fade

The number 2 high fade features hair that's cut to 1/4 of an inch on the sides and back. This is a popular length for men who want a short and neat look.

Number 3 High Fade

The number 3 high fade features hair that's cut to 3/8 of an inch on the sides and back. This length is longer than the previous numbers and is suitable for men who want a bit more hair on the sides and back.

Number 4 High Fade

The number 4 high fade features hair that's cut to 1/2 of an inch on the sides and back. This is a medium length and is ideal for men who want a classic and timeless look.

Number 5 High Fade

The number 5 high fade features hair that's cut to 5/8 of an inch on the sides and back. This is a longer length and is suitable for men who want a more casual and relaxed look.

Number 6 High Fade

The number 6 high fade features hair that's cut to 3/4 of an inch on the sides and back. This is a longer length and is perfect for men who want a more textured and tousled look.

Number 7 High Fade

The number 7 high fade features hair that's cut to 7/8 of an inch on the sides and back. This is a very long length and is ideal for men who want a lot of hair on the sides and back.

Number 8 High Fade

The number 8 high fade features hair that's cut to 1 inch on the sides and back. This is the longest possible length for a high fade and is perfect for men who want a lot of hair on the sides and back.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting a High Fade Haircut

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting a high fade haircut:

  1. Decide on the high fade haircut number you want
  2. Wash your hair and towel dry it
  3. Section your hair into the top section and the sides and back section
  4. Use clippers to cut the sides and back section to the desired length
  5. Blend the sides and back section into the top section using scissors or clippers
  6. Style the hair on top using a comb or brush
  7. Apply hair product to keep the style in place

Tips for Maintaining a High Fade Haircut

Here are some tips for maintaining a high fade haircut:

  • Get regular haircuts to keep the high fade looking fresh
  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy
  • Use hair product sparingly to avoid buildup
  • Experiment with different styles to keep the high fade looking new and exciting

FAQs About High Fade Haircut Numbers

What does a high fade haircut look like?

A high fade haircut features a gradual taper on the sides and back of the head, with longer hair on top.

What is the difference between a high fade and a low fade?

The main difference between a high fade and a low fade is where the taper starts. A high fade starts near the top of the head, while a low fade starts near the ears.

Which high fade haircut number is the best?

The best high fade haircut number depends on your personal preference and the look you want to achieve.

Hair Care for High Fade Haircuts

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining a high fade haircut. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can damage it
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Eat a healthy diet with plenty of vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth

Now that you know everything there is to know about high fade haircut numbers, you can choose the perfect length for your next haircut. Whether you want a short and edgy look or a longer and more relaxed style, the high fade is a versatile and trendy choice for men's haircuts.
