Why Is My Hair Getting Wavy As I Age

Written by Luffy Oct 07, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you noticed that your hair has started to get wavy as you age? This is a common phenomenon and is caused by a variety of factors, including hormonal changes, genetics, and styling habits. In this blog post, we’ll explore the reasons behind this change and provide tips on how to care for your newly-wavy locks.

What Causes Hair to Get Wavy?

There are several reasons why your hair may start to get wavy as you age. One of the most common causes is hormonal changes. As we age, our hormone levels start to fluctuate, which can affect the texture of our hair. This is especially true for women, whose hormone levels can change dramatically during menopause.

Another factor that can contribute to wavy hair is genetics. If your parents or grandparents had wavy or curly hair, there’s a good chance that you’ll develop a similar texture as you age.

Finally, the way you style your hair can also impact its texture over time. If you’ve been straightening or blow-drying your hair for years, this can damage the hair shaft and cause it to become more prone to waves and curls.

How to Care for Wavy Hair

If you’ve noticed that your hair is getting wavy, there are several things you can do to care for it and keep it looking its best:

1. Use the Right Products

Wavy hair tends to be more prone to frizz and dryness, so it’s important to use products that are designed to hydrate and tame your locks. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are designed for wavy or curly hair, and consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your strands moisturized.

2. Embrace Your Natural Texture

If you’re used to straightening or blow-drying your hair, it can be tempting to try to tame your waves. However, embracing your natural texture can actually be better for your hair in the long run. Try using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your waves, or use a curl-enhancing product to help define your curls.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

As mentioned earlier, heat styling can damage your hair and make it more prone to waves and curls. If you can, try to avoid using hot tools on your hair and let it air dry instead. If you must use heat styling, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

4. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can make your waves look frizzy and unkempt. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks to keep your hair looking its best.

FAQ

Why is my hair getting wavy as I age?

Hormonal changes, genetics, and styling habits can all contribute to hair becoming more wavy as we age.

Can I make my hair less wavy?

While you can use styling tools to straighten your hair, embracing your natural texture is often the healthiest option for your hair.

What products should I use on wavy hair?

Look for shampoos and conditioners that are designed for wavy or curly hair, and consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your strands moisturized.

Hair Care Tips for Wavy Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you care for your wavy hair:

1. Don’t Overwash Your Hair

Wavy hair tends to be more prone to dryness, so try to avoid washing your hair every day. Instead, aim to wash it every two to three days to help keep it moisturized.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Wavy hair can be more prone to tangles, so use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your locks. Avoid using a brush, as this can cause breakage and damage.

3. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Silk pillowcases are gentler on your hair than cotton ones, and can help prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.

4. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun’s UV rays can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz and dryness. Consider wearing a hat or using a hair product with UV protection when you’re spending time outdoors.

By following these tips and embracing your natural texture, you can keep your wavy hair looking healthy and beautiful as you age.
