Why Does My Hair Fall Out After I Dyed It

Written by Aprilia Jul 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you experiencing hair fall after dyeing your hair? This can be a frustrating experience, especially when you've put in the effort to create a new look. The good news is that this is a common problem, and there are ways to prevent it. In this article, we'll explore why hair fall happens after dyeing, how to prevent it, and how to take care of your hair post-dye.

Table of Contents

  • Why Hair Falls Out After Dyeing
  • Preventing Hair Fall After Dyeing
  • Post-Dye Hair Care
  • FAQ

Why Hair Falls Out After Dyeing

When you dye your hair, the chemicals in the dye can weaken your hair strands. The strength of your hair depends on the number of disulfide bonds it has. These bonds are responsible for keeping your hair strong and healthy. When you dye your hair, the chemicals break down these bonds, which can cause hair fall.

Additionally, hair dye can cause inflammation and irritation on your scalp. This can lead to hair fall as well. Finally, if you don't follow the instructions on the hair dye box and leave the dye on for too long, you can damage your hair and cause hair fall.

Preventing Hair Fall After Dyeing

The first step in preventing hair fall after dyeing is to choose a high-quality hair dye that doesn't contain harsh chemicals. Look for dyes that are free of ammonia, peroxide, and other chemicals that can damage your hair. Additionally, follow the instructions on the box carefully and don't leave the dye on for longer than recommended.

You can also prevent hair fall by taking care of your hair post-dye. Use a deep conditioner to help repair any damage and keep your hair hydrated. Avoid using heat styling tools for a few days after dyeing, and use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush to avoid pulling on your hair.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Choose a high-quality hair dye that doesn't contain harsh chemicals
  2. Follow the instructions on the box carefully and don't leave the dye on for longer than recommended
  3. Use a deep conditioner to help repair any damage and keep your hair hydrated
  4. Avoid using heat styling tools for a few days after dyeing
  5. Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush to avoid pulling on your hair

Tips:

  • Do a patch test before dyeing your hair to ensure you're not allergic to the dye
  • Consult with a hairstylist if you're unsure about which hair dye to use
  • Avoid dyeing your hair too frequently

Post-Dye Hair Care

After dyeing your hair, it's important to take care of it to prevent hair fall. Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Avoid using heat styling tools for a few days after dyeing, and use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush to avoid pulling on your hair. Additionally, avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy
  2. Avoid using heat styling tools for a few days after dyeing
  3. Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush to avoid pulling on your hair
  4. Avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils

Tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to keep your hair healthy
  • Avoid exposing your hair to too much sunlight, as this can cause damage
  • Wear a hat or use a scarf to protect your hair from the sun

FAQ

Q: How long should I wait to wash my hair after dyeing?

A: It's best to wait at least 48 hours before washing your hair after dyeing. This will give the dye time to set and prevent it from fading too quickly.

Q: Can I dye my hair if it's falling out?

A: No, it's not recommended to dye your hair if it's falling out. Dyeing your hair can further damage it and cause more hair fall. It's best to wait until your hair is healthy before dyeing it.

Q: How often should I dye my hair?

A: It's best to wait at least 6-8 weeks between dyeing your hair. Dyeing your hair too frequently can damage it and cause hair fall.

Q: What should I do if I experience hair fall after dyeing my hair?

A: If you experience hair fall after dyeing your hair, try to avoid dyeing it again until your hair is healthy. Use a deep conditioner and avoid using heat styling tools. If your hair fall persists, consult with a dermatologist or a trichologist for further advice.

In conclusion, hair fall after dyeing your hair is a common problem, but it can be prevented. By choosing a high-quality hair dye, following the instructions carefully, and taking care of your hair post-dye, you can prevent hair fall and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
