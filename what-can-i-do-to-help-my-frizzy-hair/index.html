What Can I Do To Help My Frizzy Hair

Written by Oliver Jun 26, 2023 · 5 min read

Frizzy hair can be a nightmare to deal with, especially if you have no idea how to tame it. But don’t worry, there are many things you can do to help your frizzy hair! In this article, we will discuss some of the most effective ways to help your hair stay smooth and manageable.

Table of Contents

  • How To Help Frizzy Hair
  • Step By Step Guide
  • Tips To Help Frizzy Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Products to Help Frizzy Hair

How To Help Frizzy Hair

The first step to helping your frizzy hair is to determine the root cause of the problem. Frizz can be caused by a variety of factors, including humidity, dryness, damage, and genetics. Once you have identified the cause, you can take steps to address it.

1. Use a Hydrating Shampoo and Conditioner

One of the best ways to combat frizz is to keep your hair moisturized. Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for frizzy hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, shea butter, and glycerin, which can help to hydrate and nourish your hair.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner or Hair Oil

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help lock in moisture. This can help to prevent your hair from becoming dry and brittle, which can lead to more frizz. Look for products that contain ingredients like jojoba oil, almond oil, and avocado oil, which can help to smooth your hair and add shine.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb or Detangling Brush

When your hair is wet, use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to gently remove any tangles. Avoid using a regular brush, as this can cause breakage and make your hair even frizzier. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, using gentle, downward strokes.

4. Dry Your Hair with a Microfiber Towel

When drying your hair, avoid rubbing it vigorously with a regular towel. This can cause frizz and damage to your hair. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess water. This can help to reduce frizz and prevent breakage.

Step By Step Guide

If you’re still struggling with frizzy hair, here is a step-by-step guide to help you tame those unruly locks:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a hydrating shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to focus on the ends of your hair, which tend to be the driest and most prone to frizz.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner or Hair Oil

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help lock in moisture. This can help to prevent your hair from becoming dry and brittle, which can lead to more frizz.

Step 3: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to gently remove any tangles. Avoid using a regular brush, as this can cause breakage and make your hair even frizzier.

Step 4: Dry Your Hair with a Microfiber Towel

When drying your hair, avoid rubbing it vigorously with a regular towel. This can cause frizz and damage to your hair. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess water.

Step 5: Apply a Smoothing Product

Apply a smoothing product, such as a hair serum, to help tame any remaining frizz. Use a small amount and work it through your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair as desired. Avoid using heat-styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can cause damage and lead to more frizz. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer.

Tips To Help Frizzy Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you keep your frizzy hair under control:

1. Avoid Over-Washing Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and frizz. Try to wash your hair no more than every other day.

2. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a cotton pillowcase can cause friction, which can lead to frizz and breakage. Switch to a satin pillowcase, which is gentler on your hair and can help to prevent frizz.

3. Avoid Touching Your Hair Too Much

Touching your hair too much can cause frizz and breakage. Try to avoid playing with your hair throughout the day.

4. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help to prevent split ends, which can make your hair look frizzy and damaged.

Frequently Asked Questions

What Causes Frizzy Hair?

Frizzy hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including humidity, dryness, damage, and genetics.

Can You Fix Frizzy Hair?

Yes, there are many things you can do to help fix frizzy hair. Using a hydrating shampoo and conditioner, applying a leave-in conditioner or hair oil, and using a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush can all help to tame frizz.

How Can I Prevent Frizzy Hair?

To prevent frizzy hair, try to avoid over-washing your hair, use a satin pillowcase, avoid touching your hair too much, and get regular trims.
