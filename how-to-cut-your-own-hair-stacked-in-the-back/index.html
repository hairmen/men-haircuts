How To Cut Your Own Hair Stacked In The Back A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 06, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of spending money and time at the salon to get a stacked haircut in the back? With just a few tools and some practice, you can learn how to cut your own hair stacked in the back at home. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide to help you achieve a beautiful stacked haircut.

Description

A stacked haircut is a popular hairstyle that features shorter layers at the back of the head, which gradually blend into longer layers towards the front. This hairstyle is perfect for adding volume and movement to your hair. With the right technique, you can create a stacked haircut that looks professionally done.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Stacked in the Back

Step 1: Gather the Tools

The first step is to gather all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a handheld mirror. Make sure that your scissors are sharp and clean.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure that your hair is clean and dry. This will help you to see the natural fall of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Next, use the comb to section your hair. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then combing it straight down. Clip the top section of your hair away from the bottom section using hair clips.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Section

Start by cutting the bottom section of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the center of the nape of your neck and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut it straight across using your scissors. Repeat this process, working your way towards the sides of your head.

Step 5: Cut the Top Section

After you have finished cutting the bottom section, unclip the top section of your hair. Comb it straight down and use the bottom section as a guide to cut the top section.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you have cut both sections, it’s time to blend the layers. Take a small section of hair from the back and comb it straight up. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, following the shape of your head. Repeat this process, working your way towards the sides of your head.

Step 7: Check Your Work

After you have finished cutting your hair, use the handheld mirror to check the back of your head. If you notice any uneven sections, use your scissors to trim them.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you are happy with your haircut, style your hair as desired. You can use a curling iron or straightener to add some volume and texture to your hair.

Tips

  • Start by cutting a small amount of hair and gradually work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and damage to your hair.
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as it will appear longer than it actually is.
  • Take breaks if you feel tired or frustrated. Cutting your own hair can be challenging, so it’s important to be patient and take your time.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair stacked in the back if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can still cut your own hair stacked in the back if you have curly hair. However, it’s important to remember that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you may want to cut your hair a little longer than you would if you had straight hair.

Do I need to use any products after cutting my hair?

It’s a good idea to use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil after cutting your hair to keep it healthy and moisturized.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips to keep your hair looking healthy:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage and split ends.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and strength.

By following these tips, you can maintain a beautiful stacked haircut that looks professional and stylish.
