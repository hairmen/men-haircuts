How To Cut Your Own Side Bangs For Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 12, 2023 · 4 min read
Are you tired of paying a fortune to get your side bangs cut at the salon every few weeks? Well, we have good news for you! With the right tools and technique, you can easily cut your own side bangs at home, without any professional help. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of cutting your own side bangs for long hair. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Description

  • Step-by-Step Guide

Description

Side bangs are a great way to add some style and personality to your long hair. They can frame your face, highlight your cheekbones, and give you a new and fresh look. However, getting them cut at a salon can be expensive, and sometimes, you might not get the desired results. That's why we have created this step-by-step guide to help you cut your own side bangs at home, without any hassle.

How to Cut Your Own Side Bangs for Long Hair

Cutting your own side bangs might seem intimidating, but with the right tools and preparation, it can be a fun and easy process. Here's what you will need:
  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • A hair tie or clip

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have all the necessary tools, here's how to cut your own side bangs for long hair:
  1. Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

  2. Part your hair in the middle or on the side, depending on where you want your bangs to fall.

  3. Using the comb, section off a triangle-shaped portion of hair. The base of the triangle should start from the middle or side part and end at the outer corner of your eyebrow.

  4. Twist the section of hair and clip it away from the rest of your hair.

  5. Using the comb, comb the section of hair downwards towards your forehead. This will ensure that your bangs are even and straight.

  6. Using the sharp scissors, cut the hair at an angle, starting from the middle of the section and moving downwards towards the outer corner of your eyebrow. Make sure to cut small sections at a time, and don't cut too much hair at once.

  7. Unclip the twisted section of hair and comb it down to blend it with the rest of your hair.

  8. Repeat the process on the other side of your face, making sure that the length and angle of your bangs match.

  9. Trim any stray hairs or uneven ends to ensure that your bangs are even and symmetrical.

  10. Style your bangs as desired, and voila! You have perfectly cut side bangs.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Side Bangs

Cutting your own side bangs can be a little tricky, but with these tips, you can make the process easier and more effective:
  • Start by cutting less hair than you think you need. You can always trim more hair later, but you can't add hair back once it's been cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean and precise cut.
  • Don't cut your bangs too short. It's better to have longer bangs that you can trim later than to have bangs that are too short to fix.
  • Take breaks in between cutting to check your progress and make sure your bangs are even.

FAQs

Q: How often should I trim my side bangs?
A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want your bangs to be. Generally, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their length and shape. Q: Can I cut my side bangs if I have curly hair?
A: Yes, you can cut your side bangs if you have curly hair. However, it's important to cut them when your hair is dry and in its natural state to ensure that the length and shape are accurate.

Hair Care Tips for Side Bangs

Now that you have cut your own side bangs, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking fresh and stylish. Here are some hair care tips for side bangs:
  • Wash your bangs regularly to prevent them from becoming oily or greasy.
  • Blow dry your bangs using a round brush to give them volume and shape.
  • Use a small amount of hair serum or oil to tame any frizz or flyaways.
  • Trim your bangs regularly to maintain their length and shape.
We hope this step-by-step guide on how to cut your own side bangs for long hair has been helpful. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you will have beautiful and stylish side bangs in no time!
