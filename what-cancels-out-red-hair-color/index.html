What Cancels Out Red Hair Color In 2023 A Comprehensive Guide

Red hair color can be a bold and beautiful statement, but sometimes it's just not the look you're going for. Whether you're trying to tone down your natural red hair or cancel out a dye job that didn't turn out as expected, there are ways to achieve the desired result. In this article, we'll explore what cancels out red hair color and various methods to help you get the hair color you want.

What Causes Red Hair Color?

Red hair color is caused by the presence of a pigment called pheomelanin. This pigment is responsible for the reddish-yellow color of hair. The amount of pheomelanin in the hair determines the shade of red. For example, people with light hair have less pheomelanin than those with dark hair.

What Cancels Out Red Hair Color?

If you're looking to cancel out red hair color, there are a few options available:

Bleach

Bleaching your hair is one way to cancel out red hair color. Bleach removes the pigment from your hair, leaving it lighter. However, bleaching can be damaging to your hair, so it's important to use a good quality bleach and follow the instructions carefully.

Color Correction

If you're not comfortable using bleach, you can try color correction. Color correction involves using a color opposite to red on the color wheel to cancel it out. In this case, green is the opposite of red, so using a green-toned hair dye can help cancel out red hair color. Be sure to choose the right shade of green for your hair color.

Color Remover

If you're not ready to bleach or dye your hair, you can try using a color remover. Color removers work by shrinking the dye molecules in your hair, making them easier to wash out. However, color removers can be drying to your hair, so be sure to follow up with a deep conditioning treatment.

How to Cancel Out Red Hair Color: Step by Step Guide

If you're ready to cancel out red hair color, here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Choose Your Method

Decide which method you want to use to cancel out your red hair color. Bleaching, color correction, and color remover are all effective options.

Step 2: Gather Your Supplies

Make sure you have all the supplies you need for your chosen method. This may include bleach, green-toned hair dye, color remover, gloves, and a deep conditioning treatment.

Step 3: Do a Strand Test

Before applying any product to your entire head, do a strand test to make sure you're happy with the result. Follow the instructions carefully and observe the results.

Step 4: Apply the Product

Apply the product to your hair according to the instructions. Be sure to protect your skin and clothes from any potential staining.

Step 5: Rinse and Condition

Rinse the product out of your hair thoroughly and follow up with a deep conditioning treatment to prevent damage.

Tips to Prevent Hair Damage

These methods can be damaging to your hair, so it's important to take steps to prevent damage:

  • Use a good quality product
  • Follow the instructions carefully
  • Do a strand test first
  • Deep condition your hair after using any of these methods
  • Limit the frequency of these treatments to avoid over-processing your hair

FAQs

Can I cancel out red hair color without damaging my hair?

These methods can be damaging to your hair, but you can minimize damage by using a good quality product and following the instructions carefully. Be sure to deep condition your hair after using any of these methods.

How long does it take to cancel out red hair color?

The time it takes to cancel out red hair color depends on the method you use and the shade of red. Bleaching can take several hours, while color correction and color removers can take up to an hour.

Will my hair go back to its natural color after canceling out red hair color?

It depends on the method you use and the condition of your hair. If you use bleach, your hair will be lighter, but it may still have a warm tone. If you use color correction or color remover, your hair may return to its natural color.

Hair Care After Cancelling Out Red Hair Color

After canceling out red hair color, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and maintain the desired result. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent fading
  • Deep condition your hair regularly
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

Cancelling out red hair color can be a daunting task, but with the right methods and care, you can achieve the desired result without damaging your hair. Be sure to follow the instructions carefully and take steps to prevent damage. If you're unsure about any of these methods, consult a professional hair stylist.
