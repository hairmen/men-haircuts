What Glasses Would Suit A Round Face The Ultimate Guide

Are you tired of trying on glasses that just don't seem to fit your round face? Do you feel like you're constantly searching for the perfect pair that will flatter your features? Look no further! In this guide, we'll be discussing what glasses would suit a round face and how to choose the perfect pair. From aviators to cat-eye frames, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • What to Look for in Glasses for a Round Face
  • Frame Styles That Compliment a Round Face
  • Colors and Patterns That Work Best

What to Look for in Glasses for a Round Face

When choosing glasses for a round face, it's important to look for frames that add angles and definition to your face. Rounded frames will only accentuate the roundness of your face, so it's best to avoid those. Instead, opt for frames with straight lines and sharp angles. Square, rectangular, and angular frames will create balance and add structure to your face. Additionally, frames that are wider than your face can help elongate your features.

Frame Styles That Compliment a Round Face

Now that you know what to look for in glasses for a round face, let's talk about some frame styles that will flatter your features. Here are some options to consider:

  • Rectangular Frames: These frames are perfect for adding structure and definition to a round face. Look for frames that are wider than your face to create balance.
  • Square Frames: Similar to rectangular frames, square frames add angles and structure to a round face. These frames are perfect for those who want to make a bold statement.
  • Wayfarer Frames: These frames have a classic and timeless look that work well on most face shapes, including a round face. The angular shape of the frames will create balance and add definition to your face.
  • Geometric Frames: Frames with unique geometric shapes, such as hexagons or octagons, can add interest and dimension to a round face.
  • Cat-Eye Frames: These frames have an upswept shape that can lift and elongate the face. Look for frames with angular edges to create balance and structure.

Colors and Patterns That Work Best

When it comes to colors and patterns for glasses, there are a few things to keep in mind. Bold, bright colors can be a great way to add some personality to your frames, but they may not always be the best option for a round face. Stick with neutral colors, such as black, brown, or tortoise shell, to create a classic look that will never go out of style. Additionally, patterns such as stripes or tortoise shell can add interest without overwhelming your features.

Hair and Makeup Tips to Compliment Your Glasses

Once you've found the perfect pair of glasses, it's important to consider your hair and makeup. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Hairstyles: Choose hairstyles that don't cover your face, as this can make your features appear smaller. Opt for hairstyles that add height or volume to the top of your head to balance out a round face.
  • Makeup: Keep your makeup simple and natural to avoid overwhelming your features. Focus on your eyes, as glasses will draw attention to this area. Use neutral eyeshadows and a bold eyeliner to create a dramatic look.

FAQs About Glasses for a Round Face

Here are some frequently asked questions about glasses for a round face:

  • Can I wear round glasses if I have a round face? It's best to avoid round glasses if you have a round face, as they can accentuate the roundness of your features.
  • What frame styles should I avoid? Rounded frames, such as aviators or circular frames, should be avoided if you have a round face.
  • Do I need to consider my face shape when choosing glasses? Yes, it's important to consider your face shape when choosing glasses. Different frames will flatter different face shapes, so it's important to choose a pair that works well with your features.

In conclusion, when it comes to choosing glasses for a round face, it's all about creating balance and definition. Look for frames with sharp angles and straight lines, and avoid rounded frames. Stick with neutral colors and simple makeup to complement your new frames. By following these tips, you'll be sure to find the perfect pair of glasses that flatter your features and make you feel confident.
