How To Dutch Braid Someone Else s Hair

Written by Daniel Aug 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to create a stunning hairstyle for your friend or loved one? Dutch braids are a great option, and they're easier to do than you might think. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to dutch braid someone else's hair. We'll also share some tips, answer common questions, and offer hair care advice to ensure that your friend's hair looks amazing.

Step-by-Step Guide

Before we dive into the steps, it's important to note that dutch braids are essentially inside-out french braids. Instead of crossing the sections of hair over each other, you'll be crossing them under. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by brushing your friend's hair to remove any knots and tangles.
  2. Section off a small piece of hair at the top of their head, near their forehead. Divide it into three equal sections.
  3. Begin braiding normally, crossing the right section under the middle section, then the left section under the middle section.
  4. As you continue braiding, add more hair to each section. To do this, pick up a small section of hair from each side of the braid and add it to the corresponding section before crossing it under the middle section.
  5. As you add more hair, the braid will start to form a pattern that sits on top of the head.
  6. Continue braiding until you reach the nape of your friend's neck.
  7. Finish the braid by braiding the remaining hair normally, without adding any extra sections.
  8. Secure the end of the braid with a hair elastic.
  9. Repeat on the other side of your friend's head to create a matching braid.
  10. If desired, you can pancake the braids by gently pulling on the sections to make them look fuller and more voluminous.

Tips for Dutch Braiding

Now that you know how to dutch braid someone else's hair, here are some tips to keep in mind to ensure that the braids look their best:

  • Practice makes perfect. If you're new to dutch braiding, it may take a few tries to get the hang of it. Don't be discouraged if your first attempt doesn't turn out perfectly.
  • Make sure the sections are even. Uneven sections can result in a messy braid.
  • Don't braid too tightly. Tight braids can cause discomfort and even headaches. Aim for a comfortable, medium tension.
  • Use a hair elastic that matches your friend's hair color. This will make the elastic less noticeable and give the braids a more polished look.
  • Consider adding some hair accessories, such as a headband or hair clips, to dress up the braids.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people have about dutch braiding:

How long does it take to dutch braid someone else's hair?

The time it takes to dutch braid someone else's hair will depend on the length and thickness of their hair, as well as your own level of experience. On average, it takes about 10-20 minutes per braid.

What if my friend's hair is too short or too thin for dutch braids?

Dutch braids work best on hair that is at least shoulder-length and medium to thick in texture. If your friend's hair is too short or thin, you may need to consider a different hairstyle.

Can I add extensions to make the braids look fuller?

Yes, you can add extensions to your friend's hair to make the braids look fuller. Just make sure to use extensions that match their hair color and texture.

Hair Care Tips

After you've finished braiding your friend's hair, it's important to take care of it to ensure that it stays healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid pulling on the braids too much, as this can cause breakage and damage to the hair.
  • Sleep with a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and tangling.
  • Use a lightweight hair oil or serum to add shine and moisture to the braids.
  • Wash the braids every 1-2 weeks to keep the scalp clean and healthy. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on the braids, as this can cause damage and frizz.

With these tips and techniques, you're now ready to create beautiful dutch braids on someone else's hair. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun with it!
