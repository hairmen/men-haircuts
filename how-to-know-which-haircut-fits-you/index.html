How To Know Which Haircut Fits You A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Jul 30, 2023 · 5 min read

Figuring out which haircut suits your face shape, hair type, and personal style can be a daunting task. With so many options available, it’s easy to feel overwhelmed and unsure of where to start. In this article, we’ll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to choose the perfect haircut for you.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape
  • Step 2: Consider Your Hair Type
  • Step 3: Think About Your Lifestyle
  • Step 4: Determine Your Personal Style
  • Tips for Choosing the Right Haircut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in choosing the perfect haircut is to determine your face shape. There are six common face shapes: round, square, oval, heart, diamond, and oblong. Knowing your face shape will help you choose a haircut that flatters your features and balances your proportions.

To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back. Use a measuring tape to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline.

If your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly the same width, you have a square face shape. If your forehead is wider than your cheekbones and jawline, you have a heart-shaped face. If your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are all about the same width, you have a round face.

If your forehead and jawline are roughly the same width, and your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, you have an oval face shape. If your face is longer than it is wide, you have an oblong face shape. If your face is widest at the cheekbones and narrows at the forehead and jawline, you have a diamond face shape.

Step 2: Consider Your Hair Type

Once you’ve determined your face shape, the next step is to consider your hair type. Different hair types require different haircuts to look their best. If you have thick, curly hair, for example, you’ll want a haircut that helps to control frizz and enhance your natural curls. If you have fine, straight hair, you’ll want a haircut that adds volume and texture.

It’s also important to consider your hair’s texture and density. If you have fine hair, for example, you’ll want to avoid haircuts that are too heavy or layered, as this can make your hair look limp and flat. If you have thick hair, on the other hand, you’ll want to avoid haircuts that are too blunt or one-length, as this can make your hair look bulky and heavy.

Step 3: Think About Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle should also play a role in choosing the perfect haircut. If you lead an active lifestyle and don’t have a lot of time to style your hair, you’ll want a low-maintenance haircut that can be easily styled on the go. If you work in a professional setting, you’ll want a haircut that looks polished and put together.

It’s also important to consider how often you’re willing to visit the salon for maintenance. If you’re someone who likes to keep their hair looking fresh and trimmed, you’ll want a haircut that requires frequent salon visits. If you’re someone who prefers a more low-maintenance approach, you’ll want a haircut that can be easily grown out and styled at home.

Step 4: Determine Your Personal Style

Finally, it’s important to consider your personal style when choosing a haircut. Your haircut should reflect your personality and make you feel confident and comfortable. If you’re someone who likes to make a statement with their hair, you may want to choose a bold, edgy haircut. If you prefer a more classic look, you may want to choose a timeless, elegant haircut.

Tips for Choosing the Right Haircut

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing the perfect haircut:

  • Bring pictures to your hairstylist to show them what you’re looking for
  • Consider trying a new haircut gradually with shorter layers
  • Don’t be afraid to experiment with different haircuts until you find the perfect one
  • Consider your skin tone when choosing a hair color to complement your new haircut
  • Keep in mind that hair grows back, so don’t be too afraid to take risks

FAQs

What haircut is best for a round face?

A long, layered haircut that falls below the chin is best for a round face.

What haircut is best for a square face?

A soft, layered haircut with face-framing layers is best for a square face.

What haircut is best for an oval face?

Almost any haircut flatters an oval face, but a long, layered haircut with face-framing layers is particularly flattering.

Hair Care Tips

Once you’ve chosen the perfect haircut, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner that’s right for your hair type
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair too often
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using heat tools
  • Get regular trims to keep your haircut looking fresh
  • Eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth and health

By following these tips and choosing the perfect haircut for you, you’ll be sure to turn heads and feel confident and comfortable in your own skin.
