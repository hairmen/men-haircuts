Why Is My Hair Spiky When Short

Written by Robby Nov 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever experienced having short hair that seems to defy gravity and spikes up no matter how much you try to tame it? You're not alone! Many people wonder why their hair becomes spiky when it's cut short. In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind this phenomenon and provide tips on how to manage it.

Description

When hair is cut short, the natural weight of the hair is removed, and it becomes easier for the hair to stand up. This is because there is less gravity pulling the hair down. Additionally, short hair tends to be more porous, meaning it absorbs moisture more easily. When the hair absorbs moisture, it swells, causing the hair to stand up even more.

How to

If you're wondering how to manage your spiky short hair, there are a few things you can try:

1. Use the right products

Using the right products can help control your hair's spiky tendencies. Look for products that provide hold and control, such as pomades or waxes. Apply a small amount to your hair and style as desired.

2. Blow-dry your hair

Blow-drying your hair can help tame spiky hair by directing the hair in the direction you want it to go. Use a round brush to smooth the hair down while blow-drying.

3. Style with your fingers

Using your fingers to style your hair can help create a more natural, textured look. Apply product to your fingertips and work it through your hair, shaping it as desired.

Step by Step

Follow these steps to manage your spiky short hair:

1. Wash and condition your hair

Clean hair is easier to style, so start by washing and conditioning your hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are formulated for your hair type.

2. Apply product

Apply a small amount of product to your hair. Start with a pea-sized amount and add more if needed. Rub the product between your palms to warm it up, then work it through your hair.

3. Style with your fingers

Use your fingers to style your hair as desired. Work the product through your hair, shaping it into the desired style.

4. Blow-dry your hair

Use a blow dryer to dry your hair while shaping it with a round brush. This will help smooth down any spiky bits and create a more polished look.

Tips

Here are some tips to help manage your spiky short hair:

1. Don't overuse product

Using too much product can make your hair look greasy and weighed down. Start with a small amount and add more if needed.

2. Use a light touch

When styling your hair, use a light touch. Over-manipulating your hair can make it spikier.

3. Experiment with different styles

Short hair is versatile, so don't be afraid to experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

FAQ

Why does my hair stand up when it's short?

When hair is cut short, the natural weight of the hair is removed, making it easier for the hair to stand up. Additionally, short hair tends to be more porous, meaning it absorbs moisture more easily. When the hair absorbs moisture, it swells, causing it to stand up even more.

Can I prevent my short hair from spiking up?

You can't prevent your hair from spiking up entirely, but you can manage it by using the right products and styling techniques.

What products should I use on my short hair?

Look for products that provide hold and control, such as pomades or waxes. Apply a small amount to your hair and style as desired.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for managing spiky short hair. Here are some tips:

1. Wash your hair regularly

Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are formulated for your hair type.

2. Protect your hair from heat

Heat can damage your hair, so use a heat protectant spray before blow-drying or using hot tools.

3. Get regular haircuts

Getting regular haircuts can help manage spiky short hair by removing split ends and keeping your hair healthy.

Short hair can be a challenge to style, but with the right products and techniques, you can manage your spiky hair and create a look that you love.
