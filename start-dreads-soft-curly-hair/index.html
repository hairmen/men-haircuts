Start Dreads Soft Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Are you tired of constantly styling your soft curly hair? Do you want to try something new and edgy? Starting dreads may be the perfect solution for you. In this guide, we will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to start dreads on soft curly hair, along with tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

  2. Step-by-Step Instructions

How to Start Dreads on Soft Curly Hair

Starting dreads on soft curly hair is slightly different than on other hair types. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Soft curly hair tends to be more delicate and prone to breakage, so it's essential to be gentle during the process.
  • It's recommended to start dreads on hair that is at least 3-4 inches long to ensure that they will hold and not unravel.
  • It's crucial to choose the right method for your hair type. The most common methods are the twist and rip, backcombing, and the crochet method.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you know what to keep in mind let's get started on the step-by-step instructions for starting dreads on soft curly hair:

  1. Wash your hair thoroughly with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or oils.
  2. Section your hair into small, equal-sized parts using a rat-tail comb. The size of the sections will depend on how thick you want your dreads to be.
  3. Apply a locking gel or wax to each section of hair to help them hold their shape. Be sure to use a product that is specifically designed for starting dreads.
  4. Using the twist and rip method, twist each section of hair until it starts to form a dread. Repeat this process until all of your hair is dreaded.
  5. At this point, you can choose to backcomb or use the crochet method to further tighten and shape your dreads. Be sure to research these methods thoroughly before attempting them.
  6. Allow your dreads to dry completely before styling or sleeping on them.

Tips for Maintaining Soft Curly Hair Dreads

Maintaining soft curly hair dreads can be a bit challenging, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep them looking healthy and neat. Here are some tips:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid buildup and keep your dreads clean.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your dreads and cause buildup.
  • Wrap your hair at night to prevent frizz and preserve the shape of your dreads.
  • Get regular maintenance done by a professional to keep your dreads looking neat and tidy.

FAQs

Here are some commonly asked questions about starting dreads on soft curly hair:

Can I start dreads on short soft curly hair?

It's recommended to start dreads on hair that is at least 3-4 inches long to ensure that they will hold and not unravel.

How often should I wash my soft curly hair dreads?

It's recommended to wash your soft curly hair dreads every 2-3 weeks to avoid buildup and maintain their shape.

Can I color my soft curly hair dreads?

Yes, you can color your soft curly hair dreads, but it's recommended to do so before starting the dread process to avoid damage or breakage.

Hair Care Advice for Soft Curly Hair Dreads

Starting dreads on soft curly hair requires proper care and maintenance to keep them healthy and looking great. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair before starting the dread process.
  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools or harsh chemicals on your hair, as they can damage your dreads and cause breakage.
  • Wear a hat or headscarf when in the sun to protect your dreads from UV damage.

Starting dreads on soft curly hair can be a fun and exciting way to switch up your look. With the right products, techniques, and maintenance, you can enjoy healthy, beautiful dreads for years to come.
