How To Do A Self Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Sep 22, 2023 Â· 5 min read

In this article, we will be discussing how to do a self fade, a popular haircut style for men. A fade is a haircut that gradually tapers from the top of the head down to the sides and back. This style requires precision and patience, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking fade right at home. So, let's get started!

Description

A fade is a hairstyle that involves cutting the hair short on the sides and back of the head and gradually blending it into longer hair on the top. This style has become increasingly popular in recent years, and it is a versatile look that can be worn in various ways. There are different types of fades, including low fades, mid fades, high fades, and skin fades. A self fade is when you cut your own hair and blend it into a fade haircut.

How to Do a Self Fade

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a pair of clippers, different clipper guards, a comb, and scissors. It is also essential to have a mirror or two, so you can see the back of your head while you are cutting your hair.

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it is clean and dry. Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup. Dry your hair with a towel or blow dryer.

Step 2: Decide on the Length and Style

Decide on the length and style of your fade. Do you want a low fade, mid fade, high fade, or skin fade? Do you want a tapered or blended look? Use a picture or a reference to help you visualize the style you want.

Step 3: Start with the Highest Guard

Start with the highest guard on your clippers and work your way down. Use a comb to lift the hair and clip the hair above the temple area. Move the clippers around the head in an upward motion, following the natural hairline.

Step 4: Switch to the Next Guard

Switch to the next guard and blend the hair from the previous cut. Use an upward motion and blend the hair into the next length, creating a smooth transition.

Step 5: Repeat with Lower Guards

Repeat steps three and four with lower guards until you reach the desired length. Use smaller guards as you work your way down towards the neckline.

Step 6: Fade the Sides and Back

Use the same technique to fade the sides and back of your head. Use shorter guards near the neckline and ears to create a clean and tapered look.

Step 7: Cut the Top

Cut the hair on the top of your head to the desired length. Use scissors to blend the top with the sides and back, creating a smooth and even transition.

Step 8: Clean Up the Edges

Use your clippers and scissors to clean up any stray hairs and create clean edges around the ears and neckline.

Step 9: Check Your Work

Use a mirror or two to check your work and make any final adjustments. Look for any uneven areas or spots that need more blending.

Step 10: Style Your Hair

Finish off by styling your hair as desired. Use a pomade, gel, or wax to create the look you want.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve a great self fade:

  • Start with a higher guard and work your way down gradually
  • Use a comb to lift the hair and create a smoother cut
  • Take your time and be patient
  • Use multiple mirrors to check your work from different angles
  • Practice makes perfect, so don't be afraid to try again if you don't get it right the first time

FAQ

Can I do a self fade if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can do a self fade if you have curly hair. However, it may be more challenging to achieve a smooth and even blend. Use a smaller guard and take your time to blend the curls into the shorter hair.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always start over or go to a professional barber for help. It's better to take your time and get it right than rush and make a mistake.

How often should I cut my hair?

The frequency of your haircuts depends on your hair type and style. If you have a fade, you may need to touch up the sides and back every two to three weeks. If you have longer hair on top, you may need to trim the ends every four to six weeks.

Hair Care

After you have finished cutting your hair, it's essential to take care of it. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suited for your hair type and style. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, and use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep it moisturized. Get regular haircuts to maintain your style and keep your hair healthy.

That's it! You now know how to do a self fade. With practice and patience, you can achieve a professional-looking fade right at home. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and have fun with it!
