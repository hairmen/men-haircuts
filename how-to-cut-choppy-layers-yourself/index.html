How To Cut Choppy Layers Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jun 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Choppy layers are a fun and edgy way to add some texture and dimension to your hair. They can be especially flattering for those with fine or thin hair, as they can create the illusion of more volume. But if you're not ready to shell out the cash for a professional haircut, you may be wondering how to cut choppy layers yourself. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you achieve the look you want.

Description

Cutting choppy layers yourself is a great way to save money and get a unique, personalized look. It can be nerve-wracking to take scissors to your own hair, but with a little practice and patience, you can achieve great results. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting choppy layers, from selecting the right tools to finishing touches.

How to Cut Choppy Layers Yourself

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting, you'll need to gather a few supplies. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors (preferably hair cutting scissors), a comb, and hair clips. It's also a good idea to have a handheld mirror or a mirror propped up on a surface so you can see the back of your head.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's best to start with clean, dry hair so you can see how it falls and moves naturally. Once your hair is clean and dry, comb it through so it's tangle-free.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using your comb and hair clips. You can start by parting your hair down the middle and then dividing each half into two or three sections. Clip each section out of the way so you can work on one section at a time.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting with the bottom section, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut into the hair at an angle, moving the scissors up and down as you go to create a choppy effect. Repeat this process on each section, working your way up to the top.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you've cut all the sections, check your work in the mirror to make sure everything looks even. If you notice any sections that are longer or shorter than the others, go back and trim them as needed.

Step 6: Finish with Texturizing

To really enhance the choppy, textured look, you can use texturizing scissors or a razor to thin out some sections. This will help create more movement and dimension.

Tips for Cutting Choppy Layers Yourself

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve great results when cutting choppy layers yourself:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up gradually
  • Use a comb to guide your scissors and create a more precise cut
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different angles and lengths to create the look you want
  • If you're unsure about cutting too much, start with a little and work your way up
  • Remember that hair grows back, so don't stress too much if you make a mistake

FAQ

Can I cut choppy layers with regular scissors?

While you can technically use regular scissors to cut your hair, it's best to use scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. Hair cutting scissors are sharper and more precise, which will help you achieve better results.

How do I know how short to cut my layers?

The length of your choppy layers will depend on the overall look you're going for. If you want a more dramatic choppy look, you can cut your layers shorter. If you want a more subtle look, you can leave your layers longer. It's best to start with a little and work your way up, as you can always cut more if needed.

Hair Care After Cutting Choppy Layers Yourself

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep your choppy layers looking fresh

Now that you know how to cut choppy layers yourself, you can experiment with different looks and styles. Just remember to take it slow and be patient, and you'll have a great new look in no time!
