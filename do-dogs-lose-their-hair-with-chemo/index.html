Do Dogs Lose Their Hair With Chemo

Written by Aprilia Jun 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Chemotherapy is a cancer treatment that uses drugs to destroy cancer cells. Chemotherapy is often used to treat dogs with cancer, just like in humans. However, one of the side effects of chemotherapy is hair loss. In this blog post, we will discuss whether dogs lose their hair with chemo, how to deal with it, and hair care tips for dogs undergoing chemotherapy.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Chemotherapy drugs attack rapidly dividing cancer cells, but they can also damage healthy cells, including hair follicles. This is why hair loss is a common side effect of chemotherapy in both humans and dogs. Not all dogs undergoing chemotherapy will lose their hair, but it is a possibility. The amount of hair loss can vary depending on the drug, dose, and duration of treatment.

How to

If you are concerned about your dog losing their hair during chemotherapy, you should talk to your veterinarian. They can provide information about the specific drugs being used and what side effects to expect. You should also ask about hair care for your dog during chemotherapy.

Step by step

If your dog does experience hair loss during chemotherapy, here are some tips to help them cope:

  1. Keep your dog warm. Without their fur, dogs can get cold easily. Provide them with warm blankets and clothing.
  2. Protect their skin. Without their fur, dogs are more susceptible to sunburn and other skin irritations. Use pet-safe sunblock and avoid exposing them to direct sunlight.
  3. Keep them clean. Use a mild pet shampoo to keep your dog's skin clean and free from irritation.
  4. Provide a comfortable bed. Dogs undergoing chemotherapy may be more tired than usual, so make sure they have a comfortable place to rest.
  5. Give them extra attention. Dogs undergoing chemotherapy may feel more vulnerable and anxious. Spend extra time with them and give them plenty of love and attention.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for your dog's hair during chemotherapy:

  • Use a soft brush. If your dog's hair is still intact, use a soft brush to avoid pulling out any loose hairs.
  • Trim their hair. If your dog's hair is falling out in clumps, consider trimming it short. This can make it easier to manage and reduce the amount of hair that falls out.
  • Avoid grooming tools. Avoid using grooming tools like clippers or scissors, which can irritate your dog's skin.
  • Avoid hair products. Avoid using any hair products on your dog, as they can irritate their skin and cause further hair loss.
  • Be patient. Hair loss during chemotherapy is temporary, and your dog's fur will eventually grow back. Be patient and give them time to recover.

FAQ

Do all dogs lose their hair with chemo?

No, not all dogs undergoing chemotherapy will lose their hair. The amount of hair loss can vary depending on the drug, dose, and duration of treatment. Talk to your veterinarian about what side effects to expect.

Is hair loss during chemotherapy permanent?

No, hair loss during chemotherapy is temporary. Your dog's fur will eventually grow back once treatment is finished.

Can I use hair products on my dog during chemotherapy?

No, it is best to avoid using any hair products on your dog during chemotherapy, as they can irritate their skin and cause further hair loss.

Hair Care

If your dog's hair has fallen out during chemotherapy, it is important to take care of their skin. Use a mild pet shampoo to keep their skin clean and free from irritation. You can also use pet-safe sunblock to protect their skin from the sun. Be patient and give your dog time to recover. Their fur will eventually grow back, and they will be back to their happy, healthy selves in no time.
