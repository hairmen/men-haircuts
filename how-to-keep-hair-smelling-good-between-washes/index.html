How To Keep Hair Smelling Good Between Washes Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Aug 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you struggle to keep your hair smelling fresh and clean between washes? You're not alone. Many people find that their hair starts to smell less-than-fresh after a day or two without a wash. But there are plenty of things you can do to keep your hair smelling great between washes. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks to help you keep your hair smelling fresh and clean, no matter how long it's been since your last shampoo.

How to Keep Hair Smelling Good Between Washes

There are several things you can do to keep your hair smelling fresh between washes:

  • Use dry shampoo
  • Apply essential oils
  • Use a leave-in conditioner
  • Avoid certain foods
  • Wash your pillowcase regularly

Let's take a closer look at each of these tips.

Use Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo is a lifesaver for those days when you don't have time to wash your hair. It absorbs oil and sweat, leaving your hair looking and smelling fresh. Simply spray it onto your roots, massage it in, and brush it out. You can also use dry shampoo before bed to prevent your hair from getting greasy overnight.

Apply Essential Oils

Essential oils are a natural way to keep your hair smelling great. You can add a few drops of your favorite oil to your shampoo or conditioner, or mix it with water in a spray bottle and spritz it onto your hair. Lavender, peppermint, and rosemary are all great options.

Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Leave-in conditioner not only helps to detangle your hair, but it also adds moisture and a pleasant scent. Apply it to the ends of your hair and work your way up, being careful not to apply too much to your roots.

Avoid Certain Foods

Believe it or not, the foods you eat can affect the way your hair smells. Foods like garlic, onions, and spicy foods can make your hair smell less-than-fresh. Try to avoid these foods, or at least limit your intake, if you want to keep your hair smelling great.

Wash Your Pillowcase Regularly

Your pillowcase can harbor sweat, oil, and bacteria, all of which can contribute to a less-than-pleasant smell. Be sure to wash your pillowcase regularly to keep it clean and fresh.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to keeping your hair smelling great between washes:

  1. Spray dry shampoo onto your roots and massage it in.
  2. Add a few drops of essential oil to your shampoo or conditioner, or mix it with water in a spray bottle and spritz it onto your hair.
  3. Apply leave-in conditioner to the ends of your hair.
  4. Avoid foods that can make your hair smell less-than-fresh.
  5. Wash your pillowcase regularly.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you keep your hair smelling great between washes:

  • Avoid using too much product, as it can build up and make your hair smell bad.
  • Wrap your hair in a scarf or hat to prevent sweat and oil from accumulating on your pillowcase.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove any buildup.

FAQ

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. Some people can go several days without washing their hair, while others need to wash it every day. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Can I use perfume in my hair?

You can, but be careful not to use too much, as it can be overpowering. Spray a small amount onto your hairbrush and run it through your hair.

What should I do if my hair smells really bad?

Try using a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup, and follow the tips in this article to keep your hair smelling fresh between washes.

Hair Care

Keeping your hair smelling fresh between washes is just one aspect of good hair care. Be sure to also:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat-styling tools too often.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your hair smelling great between washes and maintain healthy, beautiful hair.
