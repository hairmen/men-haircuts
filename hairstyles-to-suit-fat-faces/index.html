Hairstyles to Suit Fat Faces

Are you struggling to find the perfect hairstyle to flatter your face shape? Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll share some of the best hairstyles to suit fat faces. Whether you have short, medium, or long hair, there's a perfect style out there for you. Keep reading to find out more!

How To Determine Your Face Shape

Before we dive into hairstyles, it's important to determine your face shape. This will help you choose a style that flatters your features and enhances your natural beauty. Here are the most common face shapes:

  • Round: Full cheeks, rounded chin, and wide forehead.
  • Square: Strong jawline, square forehead, and angular features.
  • Oval: Balanced features, with a slightly rounded jawline and forehead.
  • Heart: Wide forehead, narrow chin, and high cheekbones.
  • Diamond: Narrow forehead and chin, with wide cheekbones.

Now that you've determined your face shape, let's take a look at some hairstyles that will flatter your features.

Hairstyles To Suit Fat Faces

When choosing a hairstyle for a fat face, the goal is to create the illusion of a more defined jawline and cheekbones. Here are some styles that will help you achieve this:

Short Haircuts

If you have short hair, consider a pixie cut or a bob. These styles add volume to the crown of the head, creating the illusion of a longer face. Avoid blunt cuts that end at the chin, as they can make the face appear wider.

Medium Haircuts

For medium hair, try a layered bob or a shoulder-length cut with soft waves. Layers add movement and texture, while waves soften the face and create a more feminine look. Avoid center parts, as they can make the face appear wider.

Long Haircuts

If you have long hair, try a layered cut with face-framing layers. Layers add volume and movement, while face-framing layers draw attention to the cheekbones and jawline. Avoid blunt cuts that end at the chin, as they can make the face appear wider.

Tips For Styling Your Hair

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add volume to your hair.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to add volume to the crown of the head.
  • Avoid center parts, as they can make the face appear wider.
  • Use a round brush to add volume and movement to your hair.
  • Try a side-swept bang to draw attention to the eyes and cheekbones.

FAQ

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a fat face?

Avoid blunt cuts that end at the chin, center parts, and straight hairstyles that add width to the face.

Can I wear a ponytail if I have a fat face?

Absolutely! A high ponytail can elongate the face and draw attention to the cheekbones.

Hair Care Tips

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as it can strip the natural oils.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.

Now that you know the best hairstyles to suit fat faces, it's time to book your next hair appointment. Remember, the key is to create the illusion of a more defined jawline and cheekbones. With the right cut and styling techniques, you can accentuate your best features and feel confident in your own skin.
