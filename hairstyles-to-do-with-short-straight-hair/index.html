Hairstyles To Do With Short Straight Hair

Are you tired of the same old boring hairstyle? Do you want to switch things up but you're not sure what to do with your short straight hair? Look no further! In this article, we'll explore some trendy and easy-to-do hairstyles that are perfect for short straight hair.

Pixie Cut

A pixie cut is a short hairstyle that is cut close to the scalp, but still leaves enough hair to style. This is a great hairstyle for those who want a low-maintenance and edgy look. This hairstyle is also perfect for those with fine hair since it creates more volume and texture.

How to:

To achieve a pixie cut, you'll need to ask your stylist to cut your hair short, leaving some length on top for styling. Once you have your pixie cut, you can style it with a little bit of product, such as a texturizing spray or pomade, to create a messy or sleek look.

Tips:

  • A pixie cut looks great with bold makeup, such as a bright lipstick or smoky eye.
  • If you have a round face, consider adding some height to your pixie cut to elongate your face.

FAQ:

  • Will a pixie cut work with my face shape?
  • Can I still style my pixie cut if I have fine hair?

Bob Cut

A bob cut is a classic hairstyle that never goes out of style. This hairstyle is perfect for those who want a short hairstyle that is still feminine and chic. A bob cut can be styled in many different ways, from sleek and straight to wavy and textured.

How to:

To achieve a bob cut, you'll need to ask your stylist to cut your hair to chin-length or shorter. You can also add layers or bangs to your bob cut to add more texture and volume. Once you have your bob cut, you can style it with a flat iron for a sleek look or with a curling iron for more texture and volume.

Tips:

  • A bob cut looks great with a side part and side-swept bangs.
  • If you have a square face, consider adding some layers to soften your features.

FAQ:

  • Can I still wear my bob cut in a ponytail?
  • What products should I use to style my bob cut?

Layers

Adding layers to your short straight hair can add more volume and texture to your hairstyle. Layers can also help frame your face and highlight your best features.

How to:

To add layers to your short straight hair, you'll need to ask your stylist to cut your hair with different lengths. The shortest layers should be at the top of your head, and the longest layers should be at the bottom. Once you have your layered cut, you can style it with a little bit of product, such as a volumizing mousse or hairspray, to add more texture and volume.

Tips:

  • Layers look great with a middle part and face-framing layers.
  • If you have a heart-shaped face, consider adding layers around your chin to balance your features.

FAQ:

  • How often do I need to get my layers trimmed?
  • Can I still wear my hair straight with layers?

Bangs

Bangs are a great way to add more dimension to your short straight hair. Bangs can help frame your face and add more texture and volume to your hairstyle.

How to:

To add bangs to your short straight hair, you'll need to ask your stylist to cut your hair shorter in the front. You can choose from different types of bangs, such as side-swept, blunt, or baby bangs. Once you have your bangs, you can style them with a little bit of product, such as a texturizing spray or pomade, to create a messy or sleek look.

Tips:

  • Side-swept bangs look great with a middle part.
  • Blunt bangs look great with a side part.

FAQ:

  • What type of bangs should I choose for my face shape?
  • How do I style my bangs if they start to grow out?

Updo

Just because you have short straight hair doesn't mean you can't wear an updo. There are many updo hairstyles that are perfect for short hair, such as a messy bun, a braided updo, or a twist.

How to:

To create an updo with your short straight hair, you'll need to start by pulling your hair up into a ponytail or a bun. You can then add braids, twists, or accessories to your updo to create a more intricate look. Once you have your updo, you can secure it with bobby pins or hair ties.

Tips:

  • A messy bun looks great with face-framing layers.
  • A braided updo looks great with side-swept bangs.

FAQ:

  • What accessories should I use to dress up my updo?
  • Can I wear an updo if I have very short hair?

Braids

Braids are a great way to add more texture and volume to your short straight hair. Braids can also help keep your hair out of your face and create a more elegant or bohemian look.

How to:

To create braids with your short straight hair, you can start by parting your hair to the side or in the middle. You can then create single braids or multiple braids, such as a fishtail braid or a Dutch braid. Once you have your braids, you can secure them with bobby pins or elastic bands.

Tips:

  • A side braid looks great with a messy bun or a twist.
  • A fishtail braid looks great with face-framing layers.

FAQ:

  • What products should I use to create braids?
  • Can I still wear my hair straight after braiding it?

Curls

Curls are a great way to add more texture and volume to your short straight hair. Curls can also help create a more romantic or playful look.

How to:

To create curls with your short straight hair, you'll need to use a curling iron or a flat iron. You can then curl your hair in different directions to create more texture and volume. Once you have your curls, you can style them with a little bit of product, such as a texturizing spray or hairspray, to keep them in place.

Tips:

  • Curls look great with a side part and face-framing layers.
  • For a more natural look, curl your hair away from your face.

FAQ:

  • What size curling iron should I use for my short hair?
  • Can I still wear my hair straight after curling it?

Accessories

Accessories are a great way to dress up your short straight hair. Accessories can include headbands, hair clips, bows, and hats.
