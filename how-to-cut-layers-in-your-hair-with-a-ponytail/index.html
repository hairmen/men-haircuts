How To Cut Layers In Your Hair With A Ponytail A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of the same old hairstyle? Do you want to add some movement and dimension to your hair without spending a fortune at the salon? Cutting layers into your hair can give you a fresh, new look and make your hair appear fuller and more voluminous. And the best part? You can do it yourself at home with just a ponytail and a pair of scissors. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut layers in your hair with a ponytail, step-by-step.

How to Cut Layers in Your Hair with a Ponytail

Before we dive into the steps, let's talk about the tools you'll need. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a hair tie, a comb, and some clips to section your hair. It's also a good idea to have a mirror in front of you and behind you, so you can see the back of your head.

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair so you can see the natural texture and movement of your locks. Wash and dry your hair as you normally would, and comb out any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to section your hair into two parts: the top and the bottom. Clip the top section out of the way for now, and tie the bottom section into a ponytail at the nape of your neck. The ponytail should be tight and secure, but not so tight that it pulls on your scalp.

Step 3: Determine the Length of Your Layers

Decide how long you want your layers to be. The shorter you cut, the more dramatic the layers will be. If you're not sure, start with a longer length and work your way up. Use your fingers to measure the length of the hair that will be cut. For example, if you want your layers to start at your chin, measure from your chin to the base of the ponytail.

Step 4: Cut the Ponytail

Hold the ponytail straight up, and use your scissors to cut the hair at the length you determined in step 3. Make sure your scissors are sharp and that you cut straight across. If you want more layers, you can cut the ponytail again, but make sure to cut at a slightly shorter length.

Step 5: Release the Top Section

Release the top section of your hair and comb it out. You should now have a ponytail at the nape of your neck, and a top section of hair that is longer and uncut.

Step 6: Create the Guide Layer

Take a small section of hair from the top of your head, near your forehead. This will be your guide layer. Hold the section straight up, and use your scissors to cut the hair at the length of your ponytail. This will create a guide for the rest of your layers.

Step 7: Cut the Layers

Starting with the hair closest to your face, take a small section of hair and hold it straight up. Use the guide layer as a reference, and cut the hair at the same length as the guide layer. Continue to take small sections of hair and cut them at the same length as the guide layer, working your way toward the back of your head. Make sure to keep your scissors at an angle, and not cut straight across, to create soft, feathered layers.

Step 8: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, use your scissors to blend them together. Take small sections of hair and cut into them at an angle, blending the layers together. This will create a more natural, seamless look.

Step 9: Check Your Work

Use your mirrors to check the back of your head and make sure the layers are even. If you notice any unevenness, use your scissors to even out the layers.

Step 10: Style Your Hair

Now that you've cut your layers, style your hair as you normally would. You may notice that your hair has more movement and volume than before. Enjoy your new, layered look!

Tips for Cutting Layers in Your Hair with a Ponytail

Here are some tips to keep in mind as you cut your layers:

  • Start with a longer length and work your way up, as it's easier to cut more hair than it is to add it back.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Hold your scissors at an angle to create soft, feathered layers.
  • Don't pull your hair too tight, as this can lead to uneven layers.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting layers can be tricky, especially if you're doing it for the first time.

FAQs

Can I cut layers into curly hair?

Yes, you can cut layers into curly hair. Just make sure not to cut your hair too short, as curly hair tends to shrink when it dries.

How often should I cut my layers?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how quickly your layers grow out. Generally, it's a good idea to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your layers looking fresh.

Hair Care

After you've cut your layers, make sure to take good care of your hair. Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your locks healthy and moisturized. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage your strands and make your layers look frizzy. And don't forget to get regular trims to maintain your layered look!
