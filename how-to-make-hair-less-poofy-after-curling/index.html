How To Make Hair Less Poofy After Curling Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Jul 01, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Do you love curling your hair, but hate the poofy, frizzy mess that comes with it? Don't worry, you're not alone! Many people struggle with keeping their curls looking smooth and defined, especially if they have naturally thick or curly hair. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks to help you make your hair less poofy after curling, so you can enjoy beautiful, bouncy curls without the frizz.

How to Make Hair Less Poofy After Curling

If you're tired of dealing with poofy curls, here are some things you can try:

1. Use the Right Products

One of the most important things you can do to prevent poofy curls is to use the right products. Start with a good shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type. If you have naturally curly or frizzy hair, look for products that are specifically formulated to tame frizz and define curls. After washing your hair, apply a styling product like mousse, gel, or curl cream to help hold your curls in place and prevent frizz. Be sure to choose a product that's lightweight and won't weigh your hair down.

2. Don't Overdo It

If you're prone to poofy curls, it's important not to overdo it with the heat styling. Curling your hair too often or using too high of a heat setting can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz. Instead, try to limit your heat styling to once or twice a week, and use a lower heat setting to avoid damaging your hair.

3. Use a Diffuser

If you're using a hair dryer to dry your curls, be sure to use a diffuser attachment. This will help distribute the heat more evenly and prevent your curls from getting too poofy. To use a diffuser, simply attach it to your hair dryer and use it to dry your hair in sections, scrunching your curls as you go.

4. Try the Smoothing Technique

If you're still struggling with poofy curls, try the smoothing technique. This involves using a smoothing serum or oil to help smooth down the frizz and flyaways. To do this, apply a small amount of serum or oil to your fingertips and gently smooth it over your curls, focusing on the areas that are the most frizzy or poofy.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Make Hair Less Poofy After Curling

Ready to try some of these tips for yourself? Here's a step-by-step guide to help you make your hair less poofy after curling:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are designed for your hair type. If you have naturally curly or frizzy hair, look for products that are specifically formulated to tame frizz and define curls.

Step 2: Apply a Styling Product

After washing your hair, apply a styling product like mousse, gel, or curl cream to help hold your curls in place and prevent frizz. Be sure to choose a product that's lightweight and won't weigh your hair down.

Step 3: Dry Your Hair

If you're using a hair dryer to dry your curls, be sure to use a diffuser attachment. This will help distribute the heat more evenly and prevent your curls from getting too poofy. To use a diffuser, simply attach it to your hair dryer and use it to dry your hair in sections, scrunching your curls as you go.

Step 4: Smooth Down Flyaways

If you're still dealing with poofy curls, try using a smoothing serum or oil to help smooth down the frizz and flyaways. Apply a small amount of serum or oil to your fingertips and gently smooth it over your curls, focusing on the areas that are the most frizzy or poofy.

Tips for Maintaining Your Curls

Now that you know how to make your hair less poofy after curling, here are some tips to help you maintain your curls:

1. Sleep with a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent your curls from getting flattened or frizzy overnight. Silk is a smooth, gentle fabric that won't rough up your hair like cotton or polyester.

2. Refresh Your Curls with Water

If your curls are starting to look a little droopy or frizzy, try refreshing them with a spray bottle filled with water. Simply mist your curls lightly with water and scrunch them up with your hands to help revive them.

3. Avoid Touching Your Hair Too Much

The more you touch your hair, the more likely it is to get frizzy and poofy. Try to avoid touching your curls too much throughout the day, and resist the urge to run your fingers through your hair.

FAQs about Making Hair Less Poofy After Curling

Q: Can I use a straightener to smooth down poofy curls?

A: Yes, you can use a straightener to help smooth down poofy curls. However, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and a lower heat setting to avoid damaging your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my hair if I have poofy curls?

A: It's generally a good idea to wash your hair every 2-3 days if you have poofy curls. Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it more prone to frizz.

Hair Care for Poofy Curls

If you're struggling with poofy curls, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips for keeping your hair in great condition:

1. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Using a deep conditioning treatment once a week can help nourish your hair and prevent it from getting too dry or damaged. Look for a treatment that's designed for your hair type, and leave it on for at least 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out.

2. Trim Your Ends Regularly

Trimming your ends regularly can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can make your hair look frizzy and poofy. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair looking healthy and strong.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Using heat styling tools like curling irons, straighteners, and hair dryers can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray before styling your hair, and try to limit your use of heat styling tools to once or twice a week.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can enjoy beautiful, bouncy curls without the frizz and poofiness.
