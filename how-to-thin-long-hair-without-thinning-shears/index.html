How To Thin Long Hair Without Thinning Shears

Written by Daniel Jun 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Thinning shears can be a great tool for creating a more manageable and voluminous hairstyle. However, not everyone has access to this tool or may not want to use it on their hair. The good news is that there are other ways to thin long hair without thinning shears. In this article, we will explore how to thin long hair without thinning shears, step by step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

How to Thin Long Hair Without Thinning Shears

There are several ways to thin long hair without using thinning shears. These methods include:

  • Razor Cutting
  • Point Cutting
  • Twist Cutting
  • Thinning Scissors

Each of these methods has its own unique advantages and disadvantages. It is important to choose the method that works best for your hair type and desired style.

Razor Cutting

Razor cutting is a technique used by professional hairstylists to thin hair. It involves using a razor tool to create thinning and texture in the hair. This method is best suited for those with thick and coarse hair. Razor cutting can help to create a softer and more manageable hairstyle.

Point Cutting

Point cutting is a technique that involves cutting the hair at an angle to create softer edges and texture. This method is best suited for those with fine and thin hair. Point cutting can help to create a more voluminous and textured hairstyle.

Twist Cutting

Twist cutting is a technique that involves twisting sections of hair and cutting them at an angle to create texture and movement. This method is best suited for those with curly or wavy hair. Twist cutting can help to create a more defined and textured hairstyle.

Thinning Scissors

Thinning scissors are a type of scissors that have teeth on one of the blades. They are used to remove bulk from the hair without creating a blunt line. This method is best suited for those who want to remove some volume from their hair without changing the overall shape of their style.

Step by Step Instructions

Before attempting any of these methods, it is important to have the right tools on hand. You will need a comb, scissors, and a mirror. Here are the step-by-step instructions for each method:

Razor Cutting

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections.
  3. Hold a section of hair at a 45-degree angle.
  4. Use a razor tool to cut the hair at the ends.
  5. Repeat on all sections of hair.
  6. Style as desired.

Point Cutting

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections.
  3. Hold a section of hair at a 90-degree angle.
  4. Use scissors to cut the hair at an angle.
  5. Repeat on all sections of hair.
  6. Style as desired.

Twist Cutting

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections.
  3. Twist a section of hair.
  4. Hold the twisted hair at a 45-degree angle.
  5. Use scissors to cut the hair at an angle.
  6. Repeat on all sections of hair.
  7. Style as desired.

Thinning Scissors

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections.
  3. Hold a section of hair at a 90-degree angle.
  4. Use thinning scissors to remove bulk from the hair.
  5. Repeat on all sections of hair.
  6. Style as desired.

Tips for Thinning Long Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when thinning long hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging the hair.
  • Be patient and take your time to ensure an even cut.
  • Consult with a hairstylist if you are unsure about how to thin your hair.

FAQs

Can I thin my hair at home?

Yes, you can thin your hair at home using the methods outlined in this article. However, it is important to be cautious and take your time to avoid damaging your hair.

Can thinning my hair make it look thinner?

No, thinning your hair can actually make it look more voluminous and manageable. It can help to remove excess bulk and create a more textured and layered style.

How often should I thin my hair?

The frequency of hair thinning depends on your hair type and desired style. It is generally recommended to thin hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and volume.

Hair Care Advice

After thinning your hair, it is important to take good care of it to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Thinning long hair without thinning shears is possible with the right tools and techniques. By following the step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care advice outlined in this article, you can achieve a more manageable and voluminous hairstyle that suits your needs and preferences. Remember to be patient and take your time to ensure an even and safe cut.
