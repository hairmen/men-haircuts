Good Fringes For Round Faces The Ultimate Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 29, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a new hairstyle that will flatter your round face shape? Look no further than a fringe! A well-chosen fringe can help to balance out your face and draw attention to your best features. In this article, we'll show you some of the best fringes for round faces, along with tips on how to style and care for your new look.

How to Choose the Best Fringe for Your Round Face

When choosing a fringe for a round face, the goal is to create the illusion of length and balance out the width of the face. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid blunt, straight-across fringes that cut the face off at the widest point
  • Look for fringes that are longer on the sides and shorter in the middle
  • Consider a side-swept fringe to create diagonal lines that elongate the face
  • Wispy or layered fringes can also help to soften the face and draw attention to the eyes

Side-Swept Fringe

A side-swept fringe is a classic choice for round faces. This style creates a diagonal line across the face, which helps to elongate the face and make it appear slimmer. To style a side-swept fringe, blow-dry it with a round brush and use a bit of hairspray to hold it in place.

Curtain Bangs

Curtain bangs are another great option for round faces. This style features long, face-framing bangs that are parted down the middle, creating a "curtain" effect. The length of the bangs helps to elongate the face, while the side part adds some asymmetry to balance out the roundness. To style curtain bangs, blow-dry them with a round brush and use a bit of texturizing spray to add volume.

Wispy Fringe

If you prefer a softer, more natural look, a wispy fringe might be the right choice for you. This style features shorter, textured bangs that are slightly longer on the sides. The layers and texture help to create movement and add dimension to the face. To style a wispy fringe, blow-dry it with a diffuser attachment and use a bit of styling cream to define the layers.

Blunt Bangs

While blunt bangs are generally not recommended for round faces, they can work if they're styled correctly. Opt for longer, tapered bangs that are slightly shorter in the middle and longer on the sides. This will help to create a more balanced look and draw attention to your eyes. To style blunt bangs, blow-dry them with a paddle brush and use a bit of smoothing serum to keep them looking sleek.

Layered Fringe

A layered fringe is a versatile option that can work for many face shapes, including round faces. This style features longer, face-framing layers that blend seamlessly into shorter, choppy layers. The layers help to create movement and add texture to the hair, while the longer pieces help to elongate the face. To style a layered fringe, blow-dry it with a round brush and use a bit of texturizing spray to add volume.

How to Style Your Fringe

Once you've chosen the perfect fringe for your round face, it's important to know how to style it to achieve the best results. Here are some tips:

  • Blow-dry your fringe with a round brush to add volume and shape
  • Use a bit of hairspray or texturizing spray to hold your fringe in place
  • If you're using a flat iron to style your fringe, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage
  • Experiment with different partings and angles to find the most flattering look for your face shape

Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe

To keep your fringe looking fresh and stylish, follow these tips:

  • Get regular trims every 4-6 weeks to keep your fringe the right length
  • Use a dry shampoo to keep your fringe from getting oily between washes
  • Avoid over-styling your fringe with heat tools, as this can cause damage and breakage
  • If you're experiencing breakouts along your hairline, try using a gentle, non-comedogenic shampoo

FAQs

Can I wear a fringe if I have a round face?

Absolutely! There are many fringes that can work well with a round face shape, as long as you choose the right style for your features.

How do I know which fringe is right for me?

Consider your face shape, hair type, and personal style when choosing a fringe. If you're not sure which style is best for you, consult with a hairstylist for advice.

What if I don't like my fringe?

If you're not happy with your fringe, don't worry - hair grows back! Talk to your stylist about adjusting the length or style to better suit your preferences.

Hair Care Tips for Fringed Hair

When you have a fringe, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and stylish. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or scarf

By following these tips and choosing the right fringe for your face shape, you can create a stylish and flattering new look that will have you feeling confident and beautiful!
