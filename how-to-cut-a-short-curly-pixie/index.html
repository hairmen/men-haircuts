How To Cut A Short Curly Pixie A Step By Step Guide

If you're considering a short curly pixie haircut, you've come to the right place! This trendy, low-maintenance style can be flattering on many face shapes and hair types. However, cutting short curly hair can be tricky, so it's important to approach it with care. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut a short curly pixie step by step, as well as provide tips and hair care advice to help you achieve the best results.

How to Cut a Short Curly Pixie

Cutting a short curly pixie can be intimidating, but with the right technique and tools, it's achievable for anyone. Here are the basic steps to follow:

1. Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a sharp pair of hairdressing scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair.

2. Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and dry. This will give you a more accurate idea of how much length you're taking off and how the curls will fall.

3. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into four sections: top, back, and two sides. Use hair clips to keep each section separate.

4. Cut the Back

Starting with the back section, comb your hair straight down and cut it to your desired length. Remember to cut less than you think you need, as curly hair tends to shrink when it dries.

5. Cut the Sides

Next, move on to the side sections. Comb your hair straight down and cut it to match the length of the back. Make sure you're cutting both sides evenly.

6. Cut the Top

Finally, work on the top section. Comb your hair forward, and again, cut it to match the length of the back and sides. You can also add layers to the top section for more volume and texture.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're still unsure about how to cut a short curly pixie, here's a more detailed guide to follow:

1. Wash and Dry Your Hair

As mentioned before, it's important to start with clean, dry hair. This will help you see how your curls naturally fall and prevent any surprises when your hair dries.

2. Section Your Hair

Use a comb to part your hair into four sections: top, back, and two sides. Use hair clips to keep each section separate.

3. Cut the Back

Starting with the back section, comb your hair straight down and cut it to your desired length. Use the point-cutting technique to create a softer, more natural look. This involves holding the scissors vertically and cutting into the hair at an angle.

4. Cut the Sides

Move on to the side sections, combing your hair straight down and cutting it to match the length of the back. Use the same point-cutting technique as before to blend the layers.

5. Cut the Top

Finally, work on the top section. Comb your hair forward, and again, cut it to match the length of the back and sides. You can add layers to the top section for more texture and volume, but be careful not to cut too much.

Tips for Cutting Short Curly Hair

Cutting short curly hair can be challenging, but these tips can help you achieve the best results:
  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. It's easier to take off more hair than to add it back.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry, as curly hair tends to shrink when it dries.
  • Use the point-cutting technique to create a softer, more natural look.
  • Don't cut too much from the top section, as this can make your hair look flat.
  • Consider adding layers to create more volume and movement.

FAQs

How often should I cut my short curly pixie?

It's recommended to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to maintain your style and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my short curly pixie at home?

Yes, you can cut your short curly pixie at home, but it's important to approach it with care and follow the proper steps.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

Don't panic! If you make a mistake, you can always visit a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care for Short Curly Pixie Haircuts

Maintaining your short curly pixie haircut is easy with these hair care tips:
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and disrupt your curls.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to enhance your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent tangles and preserve your curls.

Conclusion

Cutting a short curly pixie can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it's important to approach it with care and follow the proper steps. By using the right tools, techniques, and hair care tips, you can achieve a stylish and low-maintenance haircut that suits your face shape and hair type.
