How To Start Freeform Dreads With Curly Hair

Written by Bardi Nov 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly hair and want to start freeform dreads, you might be wondering where to begin. Freeform dreads are a natural and low-maintenance way of locking your hair without using any products or tools. In this article, we will guide you through the process of starting freeform dreads with curly hair. We will cover everything from how to section your hair to how to maintain your dreads. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Hair Preparation
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Twisting Your Hair
  • Maintenance Tips
  • FAQs

Hair Preparation

Before you start your freeform dread journey, it is essential to prepare your hair. You want to make sure your hair is clean and free of any product buildup. We recommend using a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup from your hair. You also want to make sure your hair is fully dry before starting the process.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is an essential step in the freeform dread process. You want to section your hair evenly to create uniform-sized dreads. We recommend using clips or rubber bands to hold the sections in place as you work on each one. You can use a comb to section your hair, but we suggest using your fingers to create a more natural look.

When sectioning your hair, keep in mind that the smaller the section, the smaller the dread will be. If you have thick hair, you can create larger sections to avoid having too many dreads. On the other hand, if you have thin hair, smaller sections will create the illusion of thicker dreads.

Twisting Your Hair

Once you have your hair sectioned, you can start twisting each section to create your dreads. To do this, take one section at a time and twist it in a clockwise motion until it starts to lock. You can also use a back and forth motion to create more texture. Repeat the process for each section until you have twisted all your hair.

It is essential to note that freeform dreads are meant to be organic, so don't worry about making them perfect. Embrace the natural shape and texture of your hair, and let the dreads form on their own.

Maintenance Tips

Freeform dreads require minimal maintenance, but there are a few things you can do to keep them looking their best. One of the most important things is to keep your scalp clean and moisturized. You can use a natural oil like coconut or jojoba oil to moisturize your scalp and prevent it from drying out.

You should also avoid using any products on your hair, including conditioner. This will prevent any buildup and allow your dreads to form naturally. You can also use a crochet hook to tidy up any loose hairs, but be careful not to overdo it.

FAQs

Can I start freeform dreads with short hair?

Yes! You can start freeform dreads with any hair length. Just keep in mind that the shorter your hair, the smaller your dreads will be.

Do I need to shave my head to start freeform dreads?

No. You can start freeform dreads with any hair length. You can also start with an undercut or fade and let the rest of your hair grow into dreads.

How long does it take for freeform dreads to form?

It can take anywhere from six months to a year for freeform dreads to form fully. Patience is key!

Hair Care

Once you have started your freeform dread journey, it is essential to take care of your hair. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your scalp clean and prevent any buildup.
  • Moisturize your scalp with natural oils like coconut or jojoba oil.
  • Avoid using any products on your hair, including conditioner.
  • Be patient and let your dreads form naturally.

Starting freeform dreads with curly hair is a natural and low-maintenance way of locking your hair. Follow these steps, and you will be on your way to a head full of beautiful, organic dreads. Remember to be patient and embrace the natural texture of your hair. Happy dreading!
