Why Is My Short Haired Dog Losing So Much Hair

Written by Daniel Jun 29, 2023 · 5 min read

If you’ve noticed that your short-haired pooch is shedding more than usual, it can be concerning. Dogs naturally shed their fur, but if you’ve noticed clumps of hair coming out, it could be a sign of an underlying issue. In this article, we’ll explore the reasons why your short haired dog is losing so much hair and what you can do about it.

Health Issues

One of the most common reasons for excessive shedding in dogs is health issues. If your pup is experiencing any medical problems, it can cause hair loss. Some common health issues that can contribute to excessive shedding include:

  • Hormonal imbalances
  • Thyroid problems
  • Fungal or bacterial skin infections
  • Cushings disease
  • Autoimmune diseases
  • Cancer

If you suspect that your dog’s shedding is due to a health issue, it’s important to take them to the vet for a thorough checkup. Your vet will be able to diagnose the issue and recommend a course of treatment.

Poor Nutrition

Diet plays a significant role in the health of your dog’s skin and coat. If your pup is not getting the right nutrients and vitamins, it can cause their hair to become brittle and fall out. Make sure your dog is getting a balanced diet that is high in protein and includes essential fatty acids. If you’re unsure about your dog’s diet, speak to your vet about the best food options for your pup.

Stress

Just like humans, dogs can become stressed, and it can manifest in different ways. If your dog is experiencing stress, it can cause them to lose more hair than usual. Some common causes of stress in dogs include:

  • Change in routine
  • Moving to a new home
  • Separation anxiety
  • Loud noises

If your dog is showing signs of stress, such as excessive barking, loss of appetite or lethargy, it’s important to identify the cause and take steps to reduce their stress levels.

Allergies

Allergies are another common reason for hair loss in dogs. Just like humans, dogs can be allergic to certain foods, environmental factors or even flea bites. Some common signs of allergies in dogs include:

  • Itchy or irritated skin
  • Red, inflamed skin
  • Bumps or hives
  • Excessive licking or scratching
  • Hair loss

If you suspect that your dog has allergies, take them to the vet for an allergy test. Your vet can recommend a course of treatment, such as antihistamines or allergy shots.

Parasites

Parasites such as fleas, ticks and mites can cause hair loss in dogs. These pests can irritate your dog’s skin, causing them to scratch and bite, which can lead to hair loss. Make sure your dog is up-to-date on their flea and tick prevention medications and regularly inspect their skin for any signs of parasites.

How to Stop Shedding

While shedding is a natural process for dogs, there are some steps you can take to reduce excessive shedding:

  • Brush your dog regularly to remove loose hair
  • Bathe your dog with a gentle shampoo to remove dirt and debris
  • Feed your dog a balanced diet that is high in protein and essential fatty acids
  • Make sure your dog is up-to-date on their flea and tick prevention medications
  • Reduce your dog’s stress levels by providing them with a calm and safe environment

Step-by-Step Guide to Managing Shedding

If you’re struggling to manage your dog’s shedding, here’s a step-by-step guide:

  1. Brush your dog daily with a slicker brush to remove loose hair
  2. Vacuum your home regularly to remove any loose hair
  3. Bathe your dog once a month with a gentle shampoo
  4. Feed your dog a balanced diet that is high in protein and essential fatty acids
  5. Make sure your dog is up-to-date on their flea and tick prevention medications
  6. Reduce your dog’s stress levels by providing them with a calm and safe environment

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to keep your dog’s coat healthy:

  • Brush your dog regularly to remove loose hair and tangles
  • Bathe your dog once a month with a gentle shampoo
  • Trim your dog’s hair regularly to prevent matting
  • Check your dog’s skin regularly for any signs of irritation or parasites

FAQs

Q: Is shedding normal for dogs?

A: Yes, shedding is a natural process for dogs. However, excessive shedding can be a sign of an underlying issue.

Q: How often should I brush my dog?

A: It’s recommended to brush your dog at least once a week, but more frequent brushing may be necessary for dogs with longer hair or thicker coats.

Q: Can stress cause hair loss in dogs?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss in dogs. If your dog is experiencing stress, it’s important to identify the cause and take steps to reduce their stress levels.

Conclusion

If you’ve noticed that your short haired dog is losing more hair than usual, it’s important to identify the underlying issue. Whether it’s due to health issues, poor nutrition, stress, allergies or parasites, there are steps you can take to manage your dog’s shedding. By providing your dog with a balanced diet, regular grooming and reducing their stress levels, you can help keep their coat healthy and shiny.
