When Does Hair Loss Start After Giving Birth?

Postpartum hair loss typically begins around three to four months after giving birth. This is because during pregnancy, the increased levels of estrogen in your body cause your hair to remain in the growing phase for longer than usual, resulting in thicker and fuller hair. However, after giving birth, the estrogen levels drop, causing the hair to enter the resting phase and eventually shed.

Itâs important to note that not all women will experience postpartum hair loss, and the severity can vary. Some women may only experience mild shedding, while others may lose a significant amount of hair, leading to thinning hair and bald patches.

How Long Does Postpartum Hair Loss Last?

The good news is that postpartum hair loss is temporary and should resolve on its own within six to twelve months after giving birth. However, the rate at which your hair grows back may vary depending on your hair type and genetics.

If you are concerned about the amount of hair loss you are experiencing or if it continues beyond a year, itâs essential to speak with your doctor or a dermatologist to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Tips for Managing Postpartum Hair Loss

While postpartum hair loss is a natural process, there are some things you can do to manage it and promote healthy hair growth:

  • Be gentle with your hair when brushing or styling to avoid further breakage
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools that can damage your hair
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote healthy hair growth
  • Consider taking a hair supplement that contains biotin, iron, and other nutrients that support healthy hair growth
  • Try to reduce stress in your life as stress can contribute to hair loss
