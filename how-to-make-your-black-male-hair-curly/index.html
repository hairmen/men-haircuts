How To Make Your Black Male Hair Curly

Written by Bardi Sep 26, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your straight hair and want to add some curls to your locks? Whether you want tight coils or loose waves, there are several methods you can use to make your black male hair curly. In this article, we’ll take you through the steps to achieve that curly look you’ve been dreaming of.

Description

Curly hair is a great way to switch up your look and add some dimension to your hair. However, not everyone is blessed with natural curls, especially black men. The good news is that you can make your hair curly with the right techniques and products.

How to

Method 1: Twist Out

Twist outs are a popular method for achieving curls in black hair. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Wash your hair and apply a leave-in conditioner
  2. Section your hair into small sections
  3. Apply a styling product such as a curl cream or gel to each section
  4. Twist each section tightly and secure the end with a perm rod or hair clip
  5. Let your hair dry completely or sit under a hooded dryer
  6. Remove the perm rods or clips and gently separate the twists to create curls

Method 2: Flexi Rods

Flexi rods are another popular tool for creating curls. Here’s how to use them:

  1. Wash your hair and apply a styling product such as a curl cream or gel
  2. Section your hair into small sections
  3. Wrap each section around a flexi rod, starting at the end and rolling up towards the scalp
  4. Secure the flexi rod in place and let your hair dry completely or sit under a hooded dryer
  5. Remove the flexi rods and gently separate the curls with your fingers

Method 3: Bantu Knots

Bantu knots are a traditional African hairstyle that can create beautiful curls. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Wash your hair and apply a leave-in conditioner
  2. Section your hair into small sections
  3. Apply a styling product such as a curl cream or gel to each section
  4. Twist each section tightly and wrap it around itself to create a small knot
  5. Secure the knot with a hair tie or bobby pin
  6. Let your hair dry completely or sit under a hooded dryer
  7. Remove the hair ties or bobby pins and gently separate the knots to create curls

Step by Step

Step 1: Wash your hair and apply a leave-in conditioner. This will help to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.

Step 2: Section your hair into small sections. This will make it easier to apply styling products and create curls.

Step 3: Apply a styling product such as a curl cream or gel to each section. This will help to define your curls and keep them in place.

Step 4: Use the method of your choice (twist outs, flexi rods, or bantu knots) to create curls in each section of your hair.

Step 5: Let your hair dry completely or sit under a hooded dryer. This will help to set the curls and create a longer-lasting style.

Step 6: Remove the perm rods, flexi rods, or bobby pins and gently separate the curls with your fingers. This will create a fuller, more natural look.

Tips

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair before styling
  • Don’t use too much product, as this can weigh down your curls
  • Wrap your hair in a satin scarf at night to prevent frizz and breakage
  • Be patient – creating curls can take some time, but the results are worth it!

FAQ

Q: How long will my curls last?

A: This depends on the method you used to create your curls and how well you maintain them. Twist outs and bantu knots can last up to a week, while flexi rods can last up to two weeks.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: This depends on your hair type and how active you are. Generally, it’s recommended to wash your hair every 7-10 days to prevent product buildup and keep your scalp healthy.

Hair Care

Creating curls can be a fun way to switch up your look, but it’s important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips for healthy hair:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil
  • Avoid using heat tools such as flat irons and blow dryers, as these can cause damage
  • Eat a balanced diet and drink plenty of water to promote hair health
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions with a hat or scarf

With these tips and techniques, you can make your black male hair curly and achieve the look you’ve been dreaming of. Remember to be patient and take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
