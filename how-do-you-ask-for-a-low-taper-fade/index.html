How Do You Ask For A Low Taper Fade

Written by Luffy Jun 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and trendy haircut, a low taper fade might be just what you need. This haircut is perfect for men who want a clean and sharp look without sacrificing their hair length. But how do you ask for a low taper fade in a relaxed English language? In this article, we'll guide you through the process of getting the perfect low taper fade haircut.

Description

A low taper fade is a haircut that starts with longer hair on the top and gradually fades down to shorter hair on the sides and back of the head. The fade is usually done with clippers and can be customized to your desired length. This haircut is versatile and can be styled in many different ways, making it a popular choice among men of all ages.

How to Ask for a Low Taper Fade

If you're not sure how to ask for a low taper fade, don't worry. Here are some tips to help you communicate with your barber or stylist:

1. Bring a Picture

One of the easiest ways to communicate what you want is to bring a picture of the haircut you like. You can find pictures online or in magazines, and show it to your barber or stylist. This will give them a clear idea of what you're looking for, and they can customize it to your head shape and hair texture.

2. Use the Right Terminology

When communicating with your barber or stylist, it's important to use the right terminology. Here are some terms you should know:

  • Fade: Gradually blending the hair from longer on the top to shorter on the sides and back.
  • Taper: Gradually decreasing the length of the hair from the top to the neckline.
  • Low Fade: Fading the hair down to the neckline.

3. Be Specific

Don't be afraid to be specific about what you want. If you want a low taper fade, tell your barber or stylist exactly how short you want the sides and back to be. You can also ask for a specific length on the top, or for a specific style such as a side part or a quiff.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide on how to ask for a low taper fade:

Step 1: Find a Picture

Find a picture of the low taper fade you like. This will help you communicate with your barber or stylist.

Step 2: Use the Right Terminology

Use the right terminology when communicating with your barber or stylist. Ask for a low taper fade and specify how short you want the sides and back to be.

Step 3: Be Specific

Be specific about what you want. Ask for a specific length on the top, or for a specific style such as a side part or a quiff.

Step 4: Trust Your Barber or Stylist

Trust your barber or stylist to give you the perfect low taper fade. They are professionals and know how to customize the haircut to your head shape and hair texture.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the best low taper fade:

  • Choose a reputable barber or stylist who has experience with low taper fades.
  • Bring a picture of the haircut you like to help you communicate with your barber or stylist.
  • Be specific about what you want, but also be open to suggestions from your barber or stylist.
  • Maintain your haircut with regular trims to keep it looking sharp.

FAQ

Q: How long does a low taper fade last?

A: A low taper fade can last anywhere from two to four weeks depending on how fast your hair grows.

Q: Can I style my low taper fade in different ways?

A: Yes, a low taper fade is versatile and can be styled in many different ways. You can try a side part, a quiff, or even a messy look.

Q: What is the difference between a low taper fade and a high taper fade?

A: The main difference between a low taper fade and a high taper fade is the length of the fade. A low taper fade fades down to the neckline, while a high taper fade fades up to the temples.

Hair Care

To keep your low taper fade looking sharp, you should maintain it with regular trims. You can also use a hair styling product to keep your hair in place and add texture. Be sure to wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and shiny.

Now that you know how to ask for a low taper fade, you can confidently go to your barber or stylist and get the perfect haircut. Remember to communicate clearly, be specific, and trust your barber or stylist to give you the look you want.
