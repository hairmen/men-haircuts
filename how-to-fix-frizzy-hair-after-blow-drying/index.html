How To Fix Frizzy Hair After Blow Drying

Written by Bardi Nov 12, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have frizzy hair, you know how frustrating it can be to try and tame it after blow drying. But fear not, there are ways to fix frizzy hair after blow drying. In this article, we’ll go over some tips and tricks to help you achieve smooth, frizz-free locks.

Table of Contents

  • How to Fix Frizzy Hair After Blow Drying
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Preventing Frizzy Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Frizzy Hair

How to Fix Frizzy Hair After Blow Drying

The first step in fixing frizzy hair after blow drying is to identify the cause of the frizz. There are several factors that can contribute to frizz, including humidity, dryness, and damage. Once you know the cause of your frizz, you can take steps to address it.

One of the most effective ways to fix frizzy hair after blow drying is to use a hair serum or oil. These products help to smooth the hair cuticle and add moisture, which can help to reduce frizz. Simply apply a small amount of serum or oil to your hands, and then run your hands through your hair, focusing on the ends.

Choose the Right Products

When it comes to fixing frizzy hair after blow drying, using the right products is key. Look for products that are specifically designed to combat frizz, such as anti-frizz shampoos and conditioners. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or a detangling spray to help smooth your hair and prevent frizz.

Avoid Over-Drying Your Hair

One of the biggest causes of frizz is over-drying your hair. When you blow dry your hair, you’re essentially removing moisture from your hair. If you over-dry your hair, your hair can become dry and brittle, which can lead to frizz. To avoid over-drying your hair, use a low heat setting on your blow dryer, and don’t hold the dryer too close to your hair.

Avoid Brushing Your Hair When It’s Wet

Another common mistake that can lead to frizzy hair is brushing your hair when it’s wet. Wet hair is more fragile than dry hair, and brushing it can cause damage and breakage, which can lead to frizz. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb to gently detangle your hair when it’s wet.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know some of the tips and tricks for fixing frizzy hair after blow drying, here’s a step-by-step guide to help you achieve smooth, frizz-free locks:

  1. Start by washing your hair with an anti-frizz shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel-dry your hair gently, being careful not to rub your hair too vigorously.
  3. Apply a hair serum or oil to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  4. Blow dry your hair using a low heat setting and a diffuser attachment.
  5. Once your hair is dry, use a wide-toothed comb to gently detangle your hair.
  6. Finish by applying a small amount of hair serum or oil to your hair, focusing on the ends.

Tips for Preventing Frizzy Hair

While there are ways to fix frizzy hair after blow drying, it’s always better to prevent frizz in the first place. Here are some tips for preventing frizzy hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, such as curling irons and straighteners.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes and bleach.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to extreme temperatures, such as hot sun or cold wind.

FAQ

Q: Can I use a hair mask to fix frizzy hair?

A: Yes, using a hair mask can help to hydrate and nourish your hair, which can help to reduce frizz. Look for a hair mask that is specifically designed to combat frizz, and use it once a week.

Q: Can I use a hair straightener to fix frizzy hair?

A: While a hair straightener can help to smooth your hair and reduce frizz, it’s not the best option for fixing frizzy hair after blow drying. Using a hair straightener can further damage your hair, so it’s best to use a hair serum or oil instead.

Q: How can I prevent frizz while I sleep?

A: One way to prevent frizz while you sleep is to wrap your hair in a silk scarf or use a silk pillowcase. Silk is gentle on your hair and helps to prevent friction, which can lead to frizz.

Hair Care Tips for Frizzy Hair

If you have frizzy hair, it’s important to take care of your hair to prevent further damage and frizz. Here are some hair care tips for frizzy hair:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a month to hydrate and nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, or use them sparingly.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes and bleach.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to extreme temperatures, such as hot sun or cold wind.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends, which can lead to frizz.

With these tips and tricks, you can fix frizzy hair after blow drying and achieve smooth, shiny locks. Remember to take care of your hair to prevent further damage and frizz, and use the right products to keep your hair looking its best.
