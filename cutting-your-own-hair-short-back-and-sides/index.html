Cutting Your Own Hair Short Back And Sides A Beginner s Guide

Written by Christine Nov 03, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking for a low-maintenance hairstyle that is both stylish and practical, then cutting your own hair short back and sides might be the perfect option for you. Not only is this style easy to achieve at home, but it's also a versatile look that can be adapted to suit a range of hair types and face shapes. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps required to cut your own hair short back and sides, as well as offering tips on how to maintain your new look and care for your hair.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Short Back and Sides

The first step in cutting your own hair short back and sides is to gather the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hairdressing scissors, a comb, and a handheld mirror. It's also a good idea to have a towel or apron to protect your clothes from any loose hair.

Step One: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you begin cutting your hair, it's important to start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see the shape of your head and ensure that your hair is even.

Step Two: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, divide your hair into three sections: the top, the back, and the sides. Pin the top section up and out of the way, leaving the back and sides loose.

Step Three: Start with the Sides

Starting with one side of your head, comb your hair straight down and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut your hair in a straight line, moving from the bottom up. Take care not to cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven results.

Step Four: Move to the Back

Once you've finished cutting one side, move on to the back of your head. Again, comb your hair straight down and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut your hair in a straight line, moving from the bottom up.

Step Five: Finish with the Other Side

After you've finished cutting the back, move on to the other side of your head. Repeat the same process, combing your hair straight down and holding it between your fingers before making the cut.

Step Six: Check for Evenness

Once you've finished cutting all the sections of your hair, use your handheld mirror to check the back and sides for evenness. Make any necessary adjustments until you're happy with the result.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Short Back and Sides

While cutting your own hair short back and sides can be a simple process, there are a few tips that can help you achieve the best possible results. Some of these tips include:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up gradually
  • Use a comb to help guide your scissors and create a straight line
  • Take regular breaks to stretch your hands and avoid cramps
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it

FAQ - Cutting Your Own Hair Short Back and Sides

Can I cut my own hair short back and sides if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can still cut your own hair short back and sides if you have curly hair. However, it's important to be aware that curly hair can shrink up when cut, so you may want to leave a little extra length to compensate for this.

How often should I cut my hair short back and sides?

This will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how short you want to keep it. As a general rule, you should aim to cut your hair short back and sides every four to six weeks to maintain the style.

Do I need to use any special products to care for my short back and sides haircut?

While you don't necessarily need to use any special products, it's a good idea to invest in a quality shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type. You may also want to use a styling product to help keep your hair in place.

Hair Care for Short Back and Sides Haircuts

Caring for your short back and sides haircut is relatively simple, but there are a few things that you can do to ensure that your hair stays healthy and looks great. Some tips include:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heated styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can enjoy a stylish and low-maintenance short back and sides haircut that looks great and feels great.
