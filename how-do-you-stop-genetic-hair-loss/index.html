How Do You Stop Genetic Hair Loss

Written by Bardi Sep 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss due to genetics? If so, you are not alone. According to the American Academy of Dermatology, hereditary hair loss affects approximately 80 million people in the United States. However, there are steps you can take to slow down or even prevent further hair loss. In this article, we will discuss How to stop genetic hair loss and related hair care tips.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Genetic Hair Loss
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Preventing Hair Loss
  • FAQs on Genetic Hair Loss

How to Stop Genetic Hair Loss

The first step in stopping genetic hair loss is to identify the cause. Male pattern baldness and female pattern baldness are the most common forms of hereditary hair loss. These conditions are caused by an inherited sensitivity to dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a hormone that affects hair growth. To stop genetic hair loss, you need to reduce the production of DHT in your body.

Medical Treatments

There are several medical treatments available to stop genetic hair loss. These include:

  • Finasteride: This medication blocks the production of DHT in the body. It is only available with a prescription from your doctor.
  • Minoxidil: This over-the-counter medication promotes hair growth by increasing blood flow to the scalp.
  • Hair transplant: This surgical procedure involves transplanting hair follicles from one part of the scalp to another.

Lifestyle Changes

In addition to medical treatments, there are several lifestyle changes you can make to stop genetic hair loss. These include:

  • Eating a healthy diet: A diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals can help promote hair growth.
  • Reducing stress: Stress can cause hair loss, so it is important to find ways to manage stress, such as meditation or exercise.
  • Avoiding tight hairstyles: Tight hairstyles, such as braids or ponytails, can cause hair to break and lead to hair loss.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to stop genetic hair loss:

  1. Identify the cause of your hair loss.
  2. Consult with your doctor to determine the best treatment plan.
  3. Follow a healthy diet and exercise regularly to reduce stress.
  4. Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause hair breakage.
  5. Consider using over-the-counter hair growth products, such as minoxidil.
  6. If necessary, consider a hair transplant procedure.

Tips for Preventing Hair Loss

Here are some tips for preventing hair loss:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes or perms.
  • Avoid pulling or twisting your hair, as this can cause hair breakage.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is free of sulfates and parabens.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as curling irons or flat irons, too often.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

FAQs on Genetic Hair Loss

Q: Is genetic hair loss reversible?

A: While genetic hair loss is not reversible, it can be slowed down or even stopped with the right treatment plan.

Q: Can stress cause genetic hair loss?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss, including genetic hair loss. It is important to find ways to manage stress, such as meditation or exercise.

Q: Is hair loss more common in men or women?

A: Hair loss affects both men and women, but it is more common in men.

Hair Care for Healthy Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet, as wet hair is more prone to breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

In conclusion, genetic hair loss can be a frustrating and challenging issue to deal with, but there are steps you can take to slow down or even stop further hair loss. By following a healthy diet, reducing stress, and using medical treatments or lifestyle changes, you can help promote hair growth and maintain healthy hair.
