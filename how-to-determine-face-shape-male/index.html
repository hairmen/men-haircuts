How To Determine Your Face Shape A Guide For Men

Written by Robby Aug 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Understanding your face shape is essential when it comes to choosing the right haircut, facial hair style, and even eyewear. While it may seem complicated, determining your face shape male is actually quite simple. In this guide, we’ll show you how to determine your face shape step by step, as well as provide tips and hair care advice for each face shape.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Measure Your Face
  • Step 2: Determine Your Face Shape
  • Tips for Each Face Shape
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Advice for Your Face Shape

Step 1: Measure Your Face

The first step in determining your face shape is to measure your face. Grab a tape measure and measure the following:

  • Forehead: Measure the widest part of your forehead, typically just above your eyebrows.
  • Cheekbones: Measure across your cheekbones, starting at the pointiest part below the outer corner of each eye.
  • Jawline: Measure from the tip of your chin to the point below your ear where your jaw angles upward.
  • Face length: Measure from the center of your hairline to the tip of your chin.

Write down each measurement for reference.

Step 2: Determine Your Face Shape

Once you’ve taken your measurements, it’s time to determine your face shape. Most men fall into one of six face shape categories:

  • Oval: Forehead and jawline are roughly equal in width, with a face length that is greater than the width of the cheekbones.
  • Round: Forehead and jawline are roughly equal in width, with a face length that is similar to the width of the cheekbones.
  • Square: Forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are all roughly equal in width.
  • Rectangle: Face length is the greatest measurement, with forehead, cheekbones, and jawline all similar in width.
  • Triangle: Jawline is the widest measurement, with forehead and cheekbones narrowing toward the chin.
  • Diamond: Forehead and jawline are narrower than the cheekbones, with a pointed chin.

Compare your measurements to the descriptions above to determine your face shape.

Tips for Each Face Shape

Now that you’ve determined your face shape, it’s time to learn how to flatter it with the right haircut, facial hair style, and eyewear. Here are some tips for each face shape:

Oval

An oval face shape is considered the most versatile, as it can pull off most haircuts and facial hair styles. However, avoid haircuts that are too short on the sides and too long on top, as this can elongate the face.

Round

To elongate a round face shape, opt for haircuts with volume on top and shorter sides. Avoid buzz cuts and slicked-back styles, as they can make the face appear wider.

Square

Avoid haircuts that are too short on the sides, as this can accentuate the width of the jawline. Instead, opt for longer styles with texture on top. A light beard can also soften the jawline.

Rectangle

Avoid haircuts that are too long, as this can elongate the face even further. Instead, opt for a style that adds volume on the sides and top. A light beard can also add width to the face.

Triangle

Balance out a wider jawline with haircuts that add volume to the top of the head. Avoid haircuts that are too short on the sides or too long on the chin, as this can accentuate the jawline.

Diamond

Opt for haircuts that add volume to the top of the head and avoid styles that are too short on the sides. Avoid facial hair that elongates the chin, and instead opt for a light beard or stubble.

FAQs

What if my face doesn’t fit into one of these categories?

While these are the most common face shapes, not everyone fits neatly into one category. If your face doesn’t fit into one of these categories, focus on the areas that you want to enhance or downplay when choosing a haircut or facial hair style.

Do face shape and hair texture matter?

Yes, hair texture and thickness can affect the way a haircut looks on different face shapes. Be sure to consult with your stylist about what styles will work best for your hair type and face shape.

What if I’m bald?

Even if you’re bald, it’s still important to consider your face shape when choosing eyewear and facial hair styles.

Hair Care Advice for Your Face Shape

Regardless of your face shape, there are a few general hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Don’t overwash your hair, as this can strip it of natural oils and make it dry and brittle.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Visit a stylist regularly to maintain your haircut and keep it looking fresh.

By following these tips and determining your face shape, you can find a haircut and facial hair style that flatters your features and enhances your overall look.
