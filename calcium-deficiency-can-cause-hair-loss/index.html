Calcium Deficiency Can Cause Hair Loss Understanding The Link

Written by Eveline Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Calcium is one of the most essential minerals for our body, playing a vital role in bone health, muscle function, nerve signalling, and blood clotting. However, many people overlook the importance of calcium for hair health. Calcium deficiency can lead to a range of hair problems, including hair loss, thinning, and dullness. In this article, we will explore the link between calcium deficiency and hair loss, and provide tips on how to prevent and treat this condition.

How Does Calcium Deficiency Cause Hair Loss?

Calcium is an important mineral for hair growth and maintenance, as it helps to strengthen the hair follicles and promote healthy hair growth. When the body lacks calcium, it can lead to a range of hair problems, including hair loss. Here's how:

  • Reduced blood circulation: Calcium is essential for maintaining healthy blood vessels and promoting blood circulation. When the body lacks calcium, it can lead to reduced blood flow to the scalp, which can weaken the hair follicles and cause hair loss.
  • Imbalanced hormone levels: Calcium plays a role in regulating hormone levels in the body, including those that affect hair growth. When the body lacks calcium, it can lead to imbalanced hormone levels, which can lead to hair loss.
  • Increased stress: Calcium is known to have a calming effect on the body, helping to reduce stress and anxiety. When the body lacks calcium, it can lead to increased stress levels, which can trigger hair loss.

How to Prevent Calcium Deficiency and Hair Loss?

Preventing calcium deficiency is crucial for maintaining healthy hair and preventing hair loss. Here are some tips to help you maintain adequate calcium levels:

  • Eat calcium-rich foods: Include calcium-rich foods in your diet, such as dairy products, leafy greens, nuts, and seeds. You can also take calcium supplements if you are unable to get enough calcium from your diet.
  • Get enough vitamin D: Vitamin D is essential for calcium absorption in the body. Make sure you get enough vitamin D from sunlight exposure or supplements.
  • Reduce stress: Try to incorporate stress-reducing activities in your daily routine, such as meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises.

How to Treat Hair Loss Caused by Calcium Deficiency?

If you are experiencing hair loss due to calcium deficiency, there are several treatments that can help. Here are some options:

  • Calcium supplements: Taking calcium supplements can help to restore calcium levels in the body and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Hair growth supplements: There are several hair growth supplements available that contain calcium and other vitamins and minerals that are essential for hair health.
  • Topical treatments: Topical treatments, such as minoxidil, can help to stimulate hair growth and prevent further hair loss.

FAQs

Can calcium deficiency cause hair loss in women?

Yes, calcium deficiency can cause hair loss in both men and women. However, women are more likely to experience hair loss due to calcium deficiency, especially during pregnancy and menopause.

How much calcium do I need for healthy hair?

The recommended daily intake of calcium for adults is 1000-1200mg. However, the exact amount may vary depending on your age, gender, and other factors. Consult with your healthcare provider to determine the right amount of calcium for your needs.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair

Aside from maintaining adequate calcium levels, there are several other hair care tips you can follow to promote healthy hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo: Use a mild, sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Condition regularly: Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid heat styling: Limit your use of heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling wands, to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet: Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and maintaining adequate calcium levels, you can prevent hair loss and promote healthy, beautiful hair.
