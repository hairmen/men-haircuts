How To Do 60S Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 03, 2023 Â· 3 min read

The 60s were a time of bold fashion statements, and hairstyles were no exception. From bouffants to beehives, 60s hair was all about volume and drama. If you're looking for a retro-inspired hairstyle that's easy to recreate, look no further. In this article, we'll walk you through how to do 60s hair in just a few simple steps.

How to Do 60s Hair: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start styling, it's important to prep your hair. Begin by washing your hair and applying a volumizing mousse or spray. Blow dry your hair upside down to add even more volume.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into three parts: the crown, the sides, and the back. Use clips to secure each section in place.

Step 3: Tease Your Crown

Take the crown section of your hair and tease it with a fine-tooth comb. Tease the hair at the roots, working your way down to the ends. This will create the iconic 60s bouffant.

Step 4: Smooth the Teased Hair

Once you've teased your crown, use a bristle brush to smooth the hair on top. This will give your bouffant a polished finish.

Step 5: Pin the Bouffant

Use bobby pins to pin the bouffant in place. Make sure to hide the pins underneath the teased hair.

Step 6: Style the Sides

Take down the side sections of your hair and smooth them with a bristle brush. Pull the sides back and secure them with bobby pins.

Step 7: Style the Back

Finally, take down the back section of your hair and style it however you like. You can leave it down for a more casual look, or pull it back into a ponytail or bun for a more formal style.

Tips for Doing 60s Hair

If you're new to styling 60s hair, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a fine-tooth comb for teasing your hair
  • Apply hairspray to help your style hold
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles
  • Practice makes perfect!

FAQ About Doing 60s Hair

Q: Can I do 60s hair with short hair?

A: Absolutely! While some 60s styles are better suited for longer hair, there are plenty of options for shorter hair as well. Try a sleek bob or a tousled pixie cut for a retro look.

Q: What products do I need to do 60s hair?

A: For most 60s styles, you'll need a volumizing mousse or spray, a fine-tooth comb, bobby pins, and hairspray.

Hair Care Tips for Styling 60s Hair

While 60s hair may be fun to style, it's important to take care of your hair to avoid damage. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a heat protectant spray when blow drying or using hot tools
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to easily recreate iconic 60s hairstyles. Whether you're going for a retro look or just want to add some volume to your hair, 60s hair is a fun and easy way to switch up your look.
