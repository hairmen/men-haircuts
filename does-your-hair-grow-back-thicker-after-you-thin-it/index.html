Does Your Hair Grow Back Thicker After You Thin It

Written by Oliver Jul 13, 2023 Â· 3 min read

In today's world, hair is a statement of style and beauty. Whether you have long locks or a short bob, everyone wants their hair to look healthy and voluminous. One common hair myth is that when you thin your hair, it grows back thicker. But is that really true? Let's dive in and find out!

Description

Thinning hair is a technique used to reduce the volume of your hair by removing some of the hair strands. It is a common technique used by people with thick hair to make it more manageable. The process involves using thinning shears or scissors to remove some of the hair strands, making the hair appear less thick.

Many people believe that when you thin your hair, it grows back thicker. However, this is a myth. Thinning your hair does not change the thickness of the hair follicles, which determine the thickness and texture of your hair. Hair thinning only affects the hair strands that have been cut, making them appear thinner.

How to Thin Your Hair

Thinning your hair at home is possible, but it's best to leave it to a professional hairstylist. They have the necessary tools and expertise to thin your hair without damaging it. However, if you want to try it at home, you'll need thinning shears, a comb, and a pair of sharp scissors.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to thin your hair at home:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Separate your hair into sections using a comb.
  3. Starting at the back of your head, take a small section of hair and comb it straight.
  4. Hold the thinning shears vertically and cut into the hair at a 45-degree angle.
  5. Continue to cut into the hair, moving the shears up and down the section of hair.
  6. Repeat this process on all sections of your hair.
  7. Finish by trimming any uneven ends with sharp scissors.

Tips for Thinning Your Hair

Thinning your hair can be tricky, so here are a few tips to make the process easier:

  • Start by thinning a small section of hair and gradually work your way up to larger sections.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. You can always go back and thin more if needed.
  • Use sharp scissors and thinning shears to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Consult with a hairstylist before thinning your hair at home.

FAQs

Does hair grow back thicker after you thin it?

No, hair does not grow back thicker after you thin it. Thinning your hair only affects the hair strands that have been cut, making them appear thinner.

Can thinning your hair cause damage?

Yes, thinning your hair can cause damage if not done properly. Using dull scissors or thinning shears can cause split ends and breakage. It's best to leave hair thinning to a professional hairstylist.

Hair Care Tips

After thinning your hair, it's important to take care of it to prevent damage and breakage. Here are a few hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suited for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep split ends at bay.

In conclusion, thinning your hair does not make it grow back thicker. It only affects the hair strands that have been cut, making them appear thinner. If you're unsure about thinning your hair at home, it's best to consult with a professional hairstylist.
