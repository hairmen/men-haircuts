How To Cut Your Own Hair With Thinning Shears

Written by Daniel Jun 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to add some texture and volume to your hair, using thinning shears is a great option. These shears are designed to remove bulk from your hair without sacrificing length. Cutting your own hair with thinning shears may seem daunting, but with the right techniques and tools, it can be done easily at home. Here's how to cut your own hair with thinning shears in a few simple steps.

How to Cut Your Own Hair with Thinning Shears

Step 1: Gather the Necessary Tools

Before you start, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of thinning shears, a comb, and hair clips. You may also want to have a mirror and a spray bottle of water handy.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's best to work with clean, dry hair. So, wash your hair and let it air dry or blow dry it before you start cutting. This will help you see the natural texture of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Working with one section at a time, take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the thinning shears parallel to the hair and start cutting about an inch from the roots. Make sure to close the shears gently, so you don't cut too much hair at once. Repeat this process on all sections of your hair, cutting as much or as little as you want.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After cutting each section, use a mirror to check your progress. Make sure the length and texture are consistent throughout your hair. If you notice any unevenness, go back and trim as necessary.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with your new haircut, style your hair as desired. You may want to use some hair products to enhance the texture and volume.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair with Thinning Shears

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Hold the thinning shears parallel to the hair to create a natural texture.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress and make sure the length and texture are consistent.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once to avoid any mistakes.
  • Be gentle when closing the shears to avoid any damage to your hair.

FAQ

Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

Thinning shears can be used on all hair types, but they work best on thick and curly hair. If you have fine or straight hair, be careful not to thin out too much, as it can make your hair look sparse.

How often should I cut my hair with thinning shears?

It depends on your hair growth and length. Generally, it's recommended to cut your hair with thinning shears every three to four months to maintain the texture and volume.

Do I need professional training to use thinning shears?

No, you don't need professional training, but it's always good to practice on a small section of hair before cutting your entire head. This will help you get a feel for the shears and avoid any mistakes.

Hair Care Tips

  • Use a heat protectant before using any hot tools on your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to avoid split ends.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair to prevent damage.
  • Invest in good quality hair products that suit your hair type.
  • Eat a balanced diet and stay hydrated for healthy hair growth.

With these simple steps and tips, you can easily cut your own hair with thinning shears at home. Just remember to be patient, start with small sections, and check your progress frequently. Happy cutting!
