How To Cut My Own Hair Short Layers Diy Guide

Written by Robby Aug 15, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle but don't want to spend a fortune at the salon, cutting your own hair into short layers is a great option. Not only is it cost-effective, but it also gives you more control over the finished product. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve short layers at home.

Description

Short layers are a versatile haircut that can add volume and dimension to your hair. This style involves cutting different lengths of hair at the crown and around the face, creating a textured and layered look. It's a popular choice for both long and short hair and can be customized to suit your face shape and hair type.

How to Cut My Own Hair Short Layers

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. It's also a good idea to have a mirror and a spray bottle filled with water to help you see and control your hair better.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see your natural hair texture and make it easier to cut. Once your hair is dry, brush it out and detangle any knots.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use the comb to section your hair into four parts: the top, sides, and back. Clip each section up and out of the way, leaving only the bottom section down.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer, comb your hair straight down and hold it between your fingers at the desired length. Use the scissors to cut across your hair in a straight line. Repeat this process for the entire bottom layer.

Step 5: Move to the Middle Layer

Release the middle layer and comb it straight down. Hold your hair between your fingers at the desired length, making sure to align it with the bottom layer. Cut across your hair in a straight line, following the same length as the bottom layer.

Step 6: Cut the Top Layer

Release the top layer and comb it straight down. Hold your hair between your fingers at the desired length, making sure to align it with the bottom and middle layers. Cut across your hair in a straight line, following the same length as the bottom and middle layers.

Step 7: Add Texture

To add texture and movement to your layers, use the point-cutting technique. This involves cutting into the ends of your hair at an angle, creating a feathered effect. To do this, hold your scissors vertically and make small, diagonal cuts into the ends of your hair. This will help blend the layers and create a softer look.

Step 8: Trim the Bangs (Optional)

If you have bangs, you can trim them to the desired length using the same technique as above. Comb your bangs straight down and hold them between your fingers. Cut into the ends at an angle, creating a soft, feathered effect.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to your desired length. You can always cut more if needed.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it will be longer than when it's dry.
  • Use a comb to create a straight line when cutting your hair.
  • Take breaks and step back to assess your progress.
  • Be patient and don't rush the process.

FAQ

1. Can I cut my hair into layers if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair into layers. However, it's important to cut your hair when it's dry and in its natural state. This will help you see how your curls fall and ensure that you don't cut too much. You can also use the point-cutting technique to create a softer, more natural look.

2. Is it easy to cut short layers?

Cutting short layers can be challenging, especially if you're doing it for the first time. However, with practice and patience, you can achieve great results. Start with small cuts and work your way up to your desired length, and take breaks to assess your progress.

3. How often should I cut my short layers?

Short layers require regular maintenance to keep their shape and texture. It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your layers looking fresh.

Hair Care

To keep your short layers looking their best, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to nourish your hair and prevent breakage. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and cause split ends. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser on a low heat setting. You can also use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

Cutting your own hair into short layers can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
