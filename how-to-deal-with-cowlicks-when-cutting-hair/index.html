How To Deal With Cowlicks When Cutting Hair

Written by Luffy Jun 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you having trouble dealing with stubborn cowlicks when cutting hair? Don't worry, you're not alone! Cowlicks are a common hair issue that can make it difficult to achieve a smooth and even haircut. In this article, we'll provide you with some tips and tricks on how to deal with cowlicks when cutting hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Deal with Cowlicks When Cutting Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Dealing with Cowlicks

Description

A cowlick is a section of hair that grows in a different direction from the rest of the hair. It is usually found at the hairline or crown of the head and can make it difficult to achieve a smooth and even haircut. Cowlicks can be caused by genetics or the way your hair is styled and can be difficult to manage.

How to Deal with Cowlicks When Cutting Hair

Dealing with cowlicks when cutting hair can be a challenge, but there are some things you can do to make the process easier. Here are some tips to help you deal with cowlicks:

1. Understand the Direction of the Cowlick

The first step in dealing with a cowlick is to understand its direction. Cowlicks can grow in any direction, so it's important to determine which way it's growing before you start cutting. This will help you to plan your haircut and avoid cutting against the direction of the cowlick.

2. Use the Right Tools

Using the right tools is essential when dealing with cowlicks. A good pair of scissors and a comb are essential for achieving a smooth and even cut. Make sure to use sharp scissors to avoid jagged edges and to use a comb to keep the hair in place as you cut.

3. Cut in Small Sections

Cutting in small sections can help you to achieve a more even and precise cut. This is especially important when dealing with cowlicks, as they can be difficult to manage. Make sure to cut slowly and carefully, taking your time to ensure that you're cutting the hair evenly.

4. Don't Overcut

Overcutting can cause the hair to look uneven and choppy. This is especially true when dealing with cowlicks, as they can cause the hair to stick up or lay flat in certain areas. Make sure to cut the hair just enough to achieve the desired look, but not so much that it looks uneven or choppy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you deal with cowlicks when cutting hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair. This will help you to see the natural direction of the cowlick and to determine where to cut.

Step 2: Comb the Hair

Using a comb, carefully comb the hair in the direction of the cowlick. This will help you to determine how much hair needs to be cut and in which direction.

Step 3: Cut in Small Sections

Using sharp scissors, cut the hair in small sections. Take your time and be careful not to cut too much hair at once.

Step 4: Check for Evenness

After cutting each section, check for evenness. Use a comb to make sure the hair is laying flat and in the right direction.

Step 5: Repeat as Needed

Repeat these steps as needed until you achieve the desired look.

Tips for Dealing with Cowlicks

Here are some additional tips to help you deal with cowlicks when cutting hair:

1. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Using a leave-in conditioner can help to soften the hair and make it easier to manage. This can be especially helpful when dealing with stubborn cowlicks.

2. Blow Dry the Hair in the Right Direction

Blow drying the hair in the right direction can help to smooth out cowlicks and make them less noticeable. Use a round brush to pull the hair in the direction you want it to go and blow dry on a low heat setting.

3. Use a Texturizing Product

Using a texturizing product can help to add volume and texture to the hair, making it easier to manage cowlicks. Apply the product to the roots of the hair and work it through with your fingers.

FAQs

1. Can cowlicks be permanently removed?

No, cowlicks cannot be permanently removed. They are a natural part of the hair growth pattern and can only be managed through proper styling and cutting techniques.

2. How can I prevent cowlicks from forming?

You cannot prevent cowlicks from forming, as they are determined by genetics and the natural growth pattern of the hair. However, you can manage them through proper styling and cutting techniques.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to help you manage cowlicks:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Using a gentle shampoo can help to keep the hair healthy and prevent damage that can make cowlicks more difficult to manage.

2. Avoid Over-Styling

Avoid over-styling the hair, as this can cause damage and make cowlicks more noticeable. Use heat styling tools sparingly and always use a heat protectant.

3. Get Regular Haircuts

Getting regular haircuts can help to keep the hair healthy and prevent cowlicks from becoming too unruly. Aim to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks.

Dealing with cowlicks when cutting hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and tools, it is possible to achieve a smooth and even cut. Remember to take your time, cut in small sections, and always use sharp scissors and a comb. With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to manage even the most stubborn cowlicks!
