When it comes to getting a haircut, it's important to know what you want and how to communicate it to your hairdresser. One of the most common ways to communicate the desired length of your hair is by using numbers. But what do the numbers mean when cutting hair? In this guide, we'll break it down for you step by step.

How to Understand the Numbers

The numbers refer to the length of the guard on the clippers that the hairdresser will use to cut your hair. The guard is the plastic attachment that fits over the clippers and determines how close to the scalp the hair will be cut. The higher the number, the longer the hair will be.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide on how to communicate the desired length of your hair to your hairdresser:

  1. Decide on the length you want
  2. Find out what number corresponds to that length
  3. Tell your hairdresser the number you want
  4. Confirm the length with your hairdresser before they start cutting
  5. Relax and let your hairdresser work their magic

It's that simple!

Tips for Communicating with Your Hairdresser

Here are some tips to help you communicate effectively with your hairdresser:

  • Bring a picture of the style you want
  • Be specific about the length you want
  • Use the correct terminology (e.g. "fade" or "taper")
  • Ask for recommendations based on your face shape and hair type
  • Don't be afraid to speak up if you're not happy with the cut

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I don't know what length I want?

If you're not sure what length you want, ask your hairdresser for recommendations based on your face shape and hair type. They can also show you pictures of different styles to help you decide.

What if the hairdresser uses a different numbering system?

If the hairdresser uses a different numbering system, ask them to explain it to you. It's important to confirm the length before they start cutting.

Can I switch numbers during the haircut?

Yes, you can switch numbers during the haircut if you change your mind about the length. Just let your hairdresser know before they start cutting.

Hair Care Tips After Your Haircut

After your haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the style and health of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized
  • Visit your hairdresser regularly for trims to maintain the shape of your haircut

Knowing what the numbers mean when cutting hair is essential to getting the perfect haircut. By following this guide and communicating effectively with your hairdresser, you'll be sure to get the style you want.
