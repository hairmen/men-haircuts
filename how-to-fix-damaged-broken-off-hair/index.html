How To Fix Damaged Broken Off Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 26, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to fix damaged broken off hair! If you've recently experienced hair breakage or damage, don't worry – you're not alone. Hair breakage is a common problem that can be caused by a variety of factors, including heat styling, chemical treatments, and even genetics. But the good news is that there are steps you can take to repair and strengthen your hair. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of fixing damaged broken off hair, from identifying the cause of the damage to implementing a hair care routine that will help restore your locks to their former glory.

Description

Before we dive into the details, let's talk about what we mean by "damaged broken off hair." This term refers to hair that has become weak and brittle, causing it to break off easily. This can manifest as split ends, frizz, or even patches of missing hair. There are a number of factors that can contribute to this type of damage, including:

  • Excessive heat styling
  • Chemical treatments like coloring or relaxing
  • Improper hair care, including harsh brushing and washing
  • Environmental factors like sun exposure and pollution

If you're experiencing any of these symptoms, it's important to take action to repair your hair and prevent further damage.

How to Fix Damaged Broken Off Hair

The process of repairing damaged broken off hair can be broken down into several key steps:

Step 1: Identify the Cause of the Damage

The first step in fixing your damaged broken off hair is to identify the cause of the damage. This will help you determine the best course of action for repairing your locks. For example, if your hair was damaged by excessive heat styling, you may need to cut back on the use of hot tools and implement a heat protectant product into your routine. On the other hand, if your hair was damaged by chemical treatments, you may need to consider more intensive repair treatments like protein masks or a keratin treatment.

Step 2: Cut Away Damaged Hair

If your hair is severely damaged, it may be necessary to cut away the damaged portions. While this can be a difficult decision, it's important to remember that by removing damaged hair, you're giving your healthy hair a chance to grow and thrive. Work with a stylist to determine the best course of action for your hair.

Step 3: Implement a Hair Care Routine

Once you've identified the cause of the damage and cut away any severely damaged hair, it's time to implement a hair care routine that will help repair and strengthen your locks. This may include:

  • Using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Incorporating a deep conditioning treatment into your routine once or twice a week
  • Using a leave-in conditioner or detangler to prevent breakage
  • Applying a heat protectant product before using hot tools
  • Avoiding harsh brushing or combing, and instead using a wide-tooth comb to detangle

Remember, consistency is key when it comes to repairing damaged hair. Stick with your new routine for several weeks to see the best results.

Tips for Fixing Damaged Broken Off Hair

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind as you work to repair your damaged broken off hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools as much as possible
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause breakage
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair health
  • Consider taking a hair growth supplement

FAQ

Q: Can damaged broken off hair be repaired?

A: Yes! With the right care and attention, damaged broken off hair can be repaired and strengthened.

Q: How long will it take to repair my damaged hair?

A: The amount of time it takes to repair damaged hair will vary depending on the severity of the damage and how well you stick to your new hair care routine. In general, you should start to see some improvement in the health and strength of your hair after a few weeks of consistent care.

Q: Can I color my hair if it's damaged?

A: It's generally best to avoid coloring damaged hair until it's had a chance to recover. If you must color your hair, work with a professional stylist who can recommend the best products and techniques for your hair type and level of damage.

Hair Care for Healthy Hair

Remember, the key to healthy hair is consistent care and attention. By implementing a hair care routine that's tailored to your hair's unique needs, you can repair and strengthen damaged broken off hair and enjoy healthy, shiny locks for years to come.
