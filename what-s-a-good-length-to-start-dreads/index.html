What s A Good Length To Start Dreads

Written by Frank Oct 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you thinking about starting dreadlocks but not sure if your hair is long enough? You're not alone! Many people wonder what the ideal length is for starting dreads. In this article, we'll discuss what length is good for starting dreads, how to start them, tips for maintenance, and hair care.

Table of Contents

  1. What's the Ideal Length to Start Dreads?
  2. How to Start Dreads
  3. Tips for Maintaining Dreads
  4. FAQs About Starting Dreads
  5. Hair Care for Dreads

What's the Ideal Length to Start Dreads?

The ideal length to start dreads varies depending on the method you choose. Generally, your hair should be at least 2-3 inches long. If your hair is shorter than that, it can be difficult to maintain the dreads and they may not form properly.

It's important to note that everyone's hair is different, and some people's hair may dread faster or slower than others. It's also important to consider your hair texture and thickness. If you have fine hair, you may need longer hair to form dreads, while thicker hair may be able to form dreads with shorter hair.

How to Start Dreads

There are several methods for starting dreadlocks, and each method has its pros and cons. Here are some popular methods:

Backcombing Method

The backcombing method involves sectioning your hair and backcombing each section to create knots. This method is time-consuming and can be painful, but it can produce neat and tidy dreads.

Crochet Method

The crochet method involves using a crochet hook to pull your hair into knots. This method is faster than the backcombing method, but it can cause damage to your hair if not done properly.

Twist and Rip Method

The twist and rip method involves sectioning your hair and twisting each section before ripping it apart to create knots. This method can produce messy-looking dreads, but it's a gentle method that doesn't cause as much damage as other methods.

Tips for Maintaining Dreads

Maintaining dreadlocks requires some effort and dedication. Here are some tips to help you keep your dreads looking healthy:

Wash Your Hair Regularly

Contrary to popular belief, you should wash your hair regularly when you have dreadlocks. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using conditioner or other hair products that can cause buildup.

Retwist Your Dreads

Retwisting your dreads regularly can help them maintain their shape and prevent them from unraveling. Use a locking gel or wax to help hold the twists in place.

Protect Your Dreads While Sleeping

Wear a satin or silk scarf or bonnet to protect your dreads while you sleep. This can help prevent them from getting tangled or flattened.

FAQs About Starting Dreads

Can I Start Dreads with Short Hair?

If your hair is shorter than 2-3 inches, it may be difficult to start dreads. It's best to wait until your hair is longer to start the process.

How Long Does It Take for Dreads to Form?

It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months for dreads to form, depending on your hair type and the method you use.

Can I Brush Out My Dreads?

Yes, you can brush out your dreads, but it can be a long and tedious process. It's best to consult with a professional stylist if you're considering brushing out your dreads.

Hair Care for Dreads

Dreadlocks require a different hair care routine than other hairstyles. Here are some tips for keeping your dreads healthy:

Avoid Using Heavy Hair Products

Heavy hair products can cause buildup and make it difficult to maintain your dreads. Use lightweight products or avoid using products altogether.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles like ponytails or buns, as they can pull on your dreads and cause damage.

Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help keep your dreads looking neat and healthy. It can also prevent split ends and breakage.

Starting dreadlocks is a big commitment, but it can be a rewarding and unique hairstyle. By following these tips and taking care of your dreads, you can enjoy your new hairstyle for years to come.
