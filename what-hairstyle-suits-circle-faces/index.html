What Hairstyle Suits Circle Faces A Complete Guide

Written by William Nov 17, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have a circle face shape, you may be wondering what hairstyle will flatter your features the most. In this guide, we’ll go over the best haircuts, styles, and tips for circle faces. Whether you have long hair, short hair or anything in between, we’ve got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Determine Your Face Shape
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing the Right Hairstyle
  • Tips for Styling Circle Faces

Description

A circle face shape is characterized by having a wide forehead, rounded cheeks, and a soft jawline. If you have a circle face, you want to choose a hairstyle that will elongate your face and make it appear more oval-shaped. The right hairstyle can also highlight your best features and draw attention away from areas you may not feel as confident about.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in finding the perfect hairstyle for your circle face is to determine your face shape. To do this, pull your hair back from your face and take a look at the shape of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. If your forehead and jawline are approximately the same width, and your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, you likely have a circle face shape.

Other common face shapes include:

  • Oval
  • Square
  • Heart
  • Diamond

Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing the Right Hairstyle

Now that you’ve determined your face shape, it’s time to find the perfect hairstyle. Here are some tips to help you choose:

1. Look for hairstyles that add height to your face.

Hairstyles that add height to the top of your head can help elongate your face and make it appear less round. Try a layered bob or a pixie cut with volume on top.

2. Avoid hairstyles that add width to your face.

Avoid styles that are too voluminous on the sides or add width to the face, such as blunt bangs or a chin-length bob. These styles can make your face appear even rounder.

3. Consider hairstyles with side-swept bangs.

Side-swept bangs can help elongate your face and draw attention to your eyes. Try a long, side-swept fringe or a shorter, choppy bang.

4. Experiment with different lengths.

While shorter haircuts can be flattering on circle faces, longer hair can also work if it’s cut in layers and styled with volume on top. Experiment with different lengths to see what works best for you.

5. Emphasize your best features.

If you have a great jawline or high cheekbones, choose a hairstyle that will highlight these features. For example, a sleek ponytail or top knot can show off your cheekbones, while a bob that ends just below the jawline can emphasize a chiseled jaw.

Tips for Styling Circle Faces

Once you’ve found the perfect hairstyle, here are some tips to help you style it:

1. Add volume at the crown.

Adding volume at the crown can help elongate your face and make it appear less round. Try backcombing the roots of your hair or using a volumizing spray.

2. Use a side part.

A side part can help create the illusion of a longer face. Experiment with different parting positions to find what works best for you.

3. Avoid flat hairstyles.

Hairstyles that are too flat to the head can emphasize a round face shape. Instead, try adding volume or curls to your hair.

4. Consider highlights or lowlights.

Adding highlights or lowlights to your hair can create dimension and draw attention away from a round face shape. Talk to your stylist about what shades would work best for you.

FAQ

What haircuts should I avoid if I have a circle face?

Avoid haircuts that add width to your face, such as blunt bangs or a chin-length bob. These styles can make your face appear even rounder.

Can I wear my hair in a middle part if I have a circle face?

A middle part can emphasize the roundness of a circle face. Instead, try a side part to create the illusion of a longer face.

What hairstyles are best for long hair?

If you have long hair and a circle face, try a layered cut or a voluminous ponytail or top knot. These styles can help elongate your face.

Hair Care for Circle Faces

Lastly, it’s important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for hair care:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo.

Sulfates can strip hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Look for a sulfate-free shampoo to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

2. Avoid heat styling.

Heat styling can damage hair and cause split ends. Try air-drying your hair or using a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.

3. Get regular trims.

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy and vibrant.

In conclusion, there are many hairstyles that can flatter a circle face shape. By following these tips and experimenting with different styles, you can find the perfect haircut for your features. Remember to take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
