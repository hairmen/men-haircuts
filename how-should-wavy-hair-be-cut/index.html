How Should Wavy Hair Be Cut A Guide To Flawless Waves

Written by Aprilia Aug 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have wavy hair, you know how difficult it can be to find the right cut that accentuates your waves, without making your hair look too bulky or frizzy. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this guide, we'll be discussing the best ways to cut wavy hair to bring out the best in your natural waves. Read on to learn more!

How to Cut Wavy Hair

When it comes to cutting wavy hair, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, it's important to choose a cut that complements your face shape and hair texture. For example, if you have a round face, a layered cut that frames your face can be flattering. On the other hand, if you have a square face, a long bob with soft layers can help soften your angles.

Another important factor to consider is the length of your hair. For wavy hair, it's often best to avoid very short cuts, as they can make your hair appear too voluminous. Instead, opt for a medium to long length cut, and incorporate layers to add movement and shape.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Wavy Hair

Now that you know the basics of cutting wavy hair, let's dive into the step-by-step process:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair, and let it air dry until it's about 80% dry.
  2. Separate your hair into sections, using clips or hair ties to keep each section separate.
  3. Starting at the back of your head, use a pair of sharp scissors to cut your hair, following the natural shape of your waves.
  4. Continue cutting each section of your hair, working your way up to the front.
  5. Once you've finished cutting, use a hairdryer with a diffuser attachment to dry your hair. This will help enhance your waves and add volume.
  6. Finish off with a bit of hair oil or serum to add shine and tame any frizz.

Tips for Cutting Wavy Hair

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when cutting wavy hair:

  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as this can result in uneven cuts.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you cut, to ensure that you're cutting evenly.
  • Consider getting a professional cut, especially if you're looking for a more complicated cut or have very thick hair.
  • Be patient! Cutting wavy hair can take a bit of time and practice to get the hang of.

FAQ: Cutting Wavy Hair

Here are a few frequently asked questions about cutting wavy hair:

Q: Should I cut my hair when it's wet or dry?

A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can result in uneven cuts.

Q: How often should I cut my wavy hair?

A: It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut my own wavy hair?

A: Yes, you can cut your own wavy hair, but it's important to be patient and follow the proper steps to avoid uneven cuts or damage to your hair.

Hair Care for Wavy Hair

Now that you know how to cut wavy hair, let's talk about how to care for it. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to enhance your waves and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners or curling irons too often, as this can damage your hair and make your waves look limp.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock flawless waves all year long!
