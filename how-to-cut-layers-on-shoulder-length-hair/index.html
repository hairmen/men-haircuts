How to Cut Layers on Shoulder Length Hair - Step by Step Guide

Are you looking to add some volume and texture to your shoulder length hair? Cutting layers is the perfect solution! In this step by step guide, we will show you how to cut layers on shoulder length hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Layers on Shoulder Length Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layers on Shoulder Length Hair
  • FAQs

Description

Layered hair is a great way to add volume, texture, and dimension to shoulder length hair. It can also help frame your face and create movement in your hair. While it may seem daunting to cut your own hair, with the right tools and techniques, it can be done at home.

How to Cut Layers on Shoulder Length Hair

Before you begin, it's important to have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair.

Step by Step Guide

1. Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you begin cutting.


2. Section your hair into four parts: top, left, right, and back. Clip each section out of the way except for the top section.


3. Take a small section of hair at the crown of your head and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut straight across using your scissors. This will be your guide for the rest of your layers.


4. Let down the left section of your hair and take a 1-2 inch section from the bottom. Comb the hair straight down and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Cut the hair at a slight angle, starting at the guide you just created.


5. Continue taking small sections of hair from the left, right, and back sections, using the guide to cut each section at a slight angle.


6. Once you have finished cutting all of the sections, release the top section of your hair and comb it straight down. Cut any long pieces that do not blend in with the rest of your layers.


7. Check your layers by pulling your hair straight up and looking in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments by cutting small pieces at a time.


8. Style your hair as desired and enjoy your new layered look!

Tips for Cutting Layers on Shoulder Length Hair

- Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.

- Cut at a slight angle to create a natural look.

- Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.

- Comb your hair straight down before cutting each section.

- Check your layers frequently to ensure they are even.

- When in doubt, always cut less rather than more.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut layers on my own hair?

A: Yes, with the right tools and techniques, you can cut layers on your own hair.

Q: How often should I cut my layers?

A: It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. On average, cutting layers every 6-8 weeks can help maintain the shape and volume of your hair.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After cutting layers on your shoulder length hair, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shape. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims can also help maintain the shape and health of your layers.

By following this step by step guide, you can successfully cut layers on your shoulder length hair and achieve a voluminous, textured look. Remember to take your time, start with small sections, and check your layers frequently to ensure you achieve the desired look.
