How To Cut Long Thick Wavy Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jun 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of shelling out hundreds of dollars for a haircut every few months? Do you have long, thick, wavy hair that seems impossible to manage? If so, you're not alone. Many people with this hair type struggle to find the right cut and style that works for them. But with a few simple tips and tricks, you can learn how to cut long thick wavy hair at home and save yourself time and money in the long run.

Description

Long, thick, wavy hair can be both a blessing and a curse. On the one hand, it's beautiful and voluminous, and can be styled in many different ways. On the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style, especially if you don't have the right cut. That's why learning how to cut your own hair at home can be a game-changer. Not only will you save money on salon visits, but you'll also have more control over your own hair and be able to experiment with different styles and cuts.

How to

If you're ready to take the plunge and cut your own long, thick, wavy hair at home, here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Once you have all your supplies, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. This will give you a better idea of how your hair naturally falls and where any problem areas might be.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to keep each section separate.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Starting with the back sections, take small sections of hair and trim the ends with your scissors. Be sure to cut straight across, and avoid cutting too much hair at once. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working your way from the back to the front.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

Once you've finished cutting, take a look in the mirror and make sure everything looks even. If you notice any uneven spots, go back and trim a little more until you're happy with the results.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair as desired. You can use a curling iron, straightener, or simply let your hair air dry for a natural look.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your own long, thick, wavy hair at home:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. It's better to take off a little bit at a time than to cut too much at once.
  • Be patient. Cutting your own hair can take longer than going to a salon, but the results can be just as good.
  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it more difficult to cut.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're not confident in your own cutting skills, ask a friend or family member to assist you.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting long, thick, wavy hair at home:

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly or straight?

Yes! While the process may be slightly different depending on your hair type, the basic principles remain the same. Start with small cuts and work your way up, and be sure to check your progress as you go.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how often you want to maintain your style. Most people with long, thick, wavy hair can go 3-6 months between haircuts, but this can vary.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

No! Regular scissors can damage your hair and make it more difficult to cut. Invest in a good pair of hair-cutting scissors instead.

Hair Care

Finally, it's important to take good care of your long, thick, wavy hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair over time.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.

By following these tips and learning how to cut your own long, thick, wavy hair at home, you can achieve the perfect cut and style without breaking the bank. Good luck!
