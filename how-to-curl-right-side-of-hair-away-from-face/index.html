How to Curl Right Side of Hair Away from Face | Step by Step Guide

Are you struggling to curl the right side of your hair away from your face? This can be a difficult task, but with the right technique and tools, it can be easily achieved. In this step-by-step guide, we will show you how to curl the right side of your hair away from your face in a relaxed and easy to understand language. So, let's get started!

How to Curl Right Side of Hair Away from Face

Before we start, let's gather the necessary tools:

  • Curling iron or wand
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Sectioning clips
  • Hairspray

Step 1: Preparing Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Apply a heat protectant spray to prevent any heat damage. Once your hair is dry, brush it to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Sectioning Your Hair

Use sectioning clips to divide your hair into two parts. Take the top section and clip it away.

Step 3: Curling the Right Side of Your Hair Away from Your Face

Take a small section of hair from the right side of your head. Clamp the curling iron or wand on the section of hair, making sure that the barrel is facing downwards.

Wrap the hair around the barrel and hold it for 5-10 seconds. Release the hair from the curling iron or wand and gently pull the curl downwards to loosen it.

Repeat this process with small sections of hair, curling away from your face. Alternate the direction of the curls to create a natural look.

Step 4: Finishing Touches

Once you have curled the right side of your hair away from your face, apply some hairspray to hold the curls in place. Let your hair cool and set for a few minutes before removing the sectioning clips and styling the rest of your hair.

Tips for Curling Your Hair

  • Always use a heat protectant spray to prevent heat damage to your hair.
  • Don't curl too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven and messy curls.
  • Alternate the direction of the curls to create a natural and effortless look.
  • Don't touch or brush your hair immediately after curling, as this can cause the curls to fall out.
  • Apply hairspray to hold the curls in place, but don't overdo it as this can make your hair look stiff and unnatural.

FAQs

How do I know if I am using the right curling iron or wand?

The size of the curling iron or wand depends on the type of curl you want to achieve. For tighter curls, use a smaller barrel size, and for looser curls, use a larger barrel size.

How often should I curl my hair?

Curling your hair too often can cause damage and breakage. It's best to limit curling to once or twice a week and to use a heat protectant spray before styling.

How can I make my curls last longer?

Apply hairspray to hold the curls in place and avoid touching or brushing your hair too much. You can also sleep with your hair in a loose bun or braids to help maintain the curls overnight.

Hair Care

Curling your hair can be damaging, so it's important to take care of your hair to prevent breakage and split ends. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week, and avoid using hot tools too often.

Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends, and use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily curl the right side of your hair away from your face and achieve a natural and effortless look. Remember to take care of your hair and use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage. Happy styling!
