What To Do When Bridesmaid Dress Is Too Small

Written by Christine Sep 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

As a bridesmaid, the last thing you want to worry about is a dress that doesn't fit. Unfortunately, it happens more often than we'd like. Whether it's a sizing issue or a weight change, a too-small bridesmaid dress can be stressful. But don't panic just yet â there are several things you can do to fix the situation. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you when your bridesmaid dress is too small.

Description

It's the day before the wedding, and you try on your bridesmaid dress. It's too small! What do you do? First, don't panic. There are several options available to you, depending on how much time you have before the wedding. You may need to get creative, but you can make it work.

How To

If your bridesmaid dress is too small, the first thing you should do is contact the bride or wedding planner. They may be able to provide a solution, such as ordering a new dress or altering the current one. If that's not an option, here's what you can do:

1. Try to Stretch It Out

If your dress is only slightly too small, you may be able to stretch it out. Hang it up and use a steamer or iron on a low setting to relax the fabric. You can also try wearing the dress around the house for a few hours to help stretch it out.

2. Let Out the Seams

If your dress is too small in specific areas, such as the bust or hips, a seamstress may be able to let out the seams. This will give you some extra room and make the dress more comfortable.

3. Add Panels

If your dress is too small all over, adding panels may be an option. A seamstress can add fabric to the sides or back of the dress to give you more room. This is a great option if you love the dress and don't want to order a new one.

4. Wear Shapewear

If all else fails, wearing shapewear can help smooth out any bumps and make the dress more comfortable. Look for shapewear that's designed to fit seamlessly under dresses, so it won't be visible.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you when your bridesmaid dress is too small:

Step 1: Assess the Situation

Take a deep breath and assess the situation. How much too small is the dress? Can it be fixed? Do you have enough time before the wedding?

Step 2: Contact the Bride or Wedding Planner

If you have enough time, contact the bride or wedding planner. They may be able to provide a solution, such as ordering a new dress or altering the current one.

Step 6: Wear Shapewear

If all else fails, wear shapewear to smooth out any bumps and make the dress more comfortable.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when dealing with a too-small bridesmaid dress:

1. Don't Panic

Take a deep breath and stay calm. There are several solutions available to you.

2. Contact the Bride or Wedding Planner

If you have enough time, contact the bride or wedding planner. They may be able to provide a solution.

3. Be Creative

If you're on a tight timeline, get creative. Can you wear a cardigan or jacket over the dress? Can you add a belt to cinch the waist?

4. Plan Ahead

If you're ordering a dress online, make sure to order it well in advance of the wedding. This will give you enough time to try it on and make any necessary alterations.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about bridesmaid dresses that are too small:

Q: What if my dress is too small and there's no time to fix it?

A: If there's no time to fix the dress, consider finding a similar dress in your size to wear instead. You can also wear a dress you already own that matches the wedding colors.

Q: Can I alter the dress myself?

A: It's not recommended to alter the dress yourself, as you may damage the fabric or alter the fit in a way that can't be fixed.

Q: Can I exchange the dress for a larger size?

A: It depends on the store's return policy. Check the policy before purchasing the dress, and make sure to order it well in advance of the wedding.

Hair Care

When dealing with a too-small bridesmaid dress, don't forget about your hair. Choose a hairstyle that complements the dress and makes you feel confident. Consider an updo or a sleek ponytail to draw attention to your face and neckline.

In conclusion, a too-small bridesmaid dress can be stressful, but there are several solutions available to you. Take a deep breath, assess the situation, and get creative. With a little bit of effort, you can make the dress work and feel confident on the big day.
