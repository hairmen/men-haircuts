How To Fix A Lopsided Head Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Nov 24, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you have noticed that your head is lopsided or asymmetrical, don't worry! There are many ways to fix it. In this article, we will discuss how to fix a lopsided head, the causes of head asymmetry, and how to prevent it in the future.

What Causes Lopsided Head?

Head asymmetry can be caused by a variety of factors, including:

  • Positioning during sleep or breastfeeding
  • Flat head syndrome
  • Injury or trauma to the head
  • Scoliosis
  • Torticollis (a condition where the neck muscles are tight on one side)

How to Fix a Lopsided Head?

Here are some tips and tricks to fix a lopsided head:

1. Visit a Physical Therapist

If you suspect that your lopsided head is due to a medical condition such as torticollis or scoliosis, it is important to visit a physical therapist. They can assess your condition and recommend exercises to help straighten your head and neck.

2. Change Your Sleeping Position

If your lopsided head is due to your sleeping position, try sleeping on your back with a pillow under your neck. Avoid sleeping on the same side every night as it can cause asymmetry. You can also try using a special pillow designed to prevent head asymmetry.

3. Wear a Helmet

If you have flat head syndrome, wearing a helmet can help reshape your head. The helmet applies gentle pressure to the flat areas, encouraging growth in the rounder areas.

4. Exercise Your Neck Muscles

Exercises such as neck stretches, neck rotations, and chin tucks can help strengthen your neck muscles and improve your posture. This can also help reduce head asymmetry.

5. Massage Your Head

Massaging your head can help improve blood flow and promote healing. Use your fingertips to gently massage the affected area in circular motions for a few minutes every day.

FAQ

Can head asymmetry be fixed in adults?

Yes, head asymmetry can be fixed in adults. However, it may take longer as the bones in the head have already fused.

Is head asymmetry a serious condition?

Head asymmetry is usually not a serious condition. However, if it is due to a medical condition, it is important to seek medical attention.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Head Asymmetry

Here are some hair care tips to prevent head asymmetry:

1. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids, ponytails, and buns. These styles can cause tension on the scalp and lead to head asymmetry.

2. Change Your Parting

If you always part your hair on the same side, try changing it up. Parting your hair on the opposite side can help prevent head asymmetry.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush. A brush can cause tension on the scalp and lead to head asymmetry.

4. Be Gentle

Be gentle when brushing or styling your hair. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair as it can cause tension on the scalp.

Conclusion

Head asymmetry can be caused by a variety of factors, but it can be fixed with the right treatment. By following these tips and tricks, you can straighten your head and prevent head asymmetry in the future. Remember to consult a physical therapist if you suspect that your head asymmetry is due to a medical condition.
