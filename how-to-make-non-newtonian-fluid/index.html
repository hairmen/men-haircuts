How To Make Non Newtonian Fluid A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Nov 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how to make non newtonian fluid? Non newtonian fluid is a substance that behaves like both a liquid and a solid, depending on the force applied to it. It's a fascinating substance that can be used in a variety of ways, from science experiments to practical applications. In this article, we'll show you how to make non newtonian fluid in a few easy steps.

Description

Non newtonian fluid is a substance that doesn't follow the laws of traditional physics. Unlike a liquid, it can resist deformation when force is applied to it. This makes it an interesting substance to work with, as it can be used in a variety of ways. Non newtonian fluid can be made using a few simple ingredients, and it's easy to experiment with different recipes to create different textures and consistencies.

How to Make Non Newtonian Fluid

Making non newtonian fluid is easy, and you only need a few ingredients. Here's what you'll need:

  • Cornstarch
  • Water
  • Food coloring (optional)

Here's what you need to do:

  1. Mix one part water with two parts cornstarch in a bowl. You can adjust the amounts depending on how much non newtonian fluid you want to make.
  2. Add a few drops of food coloring if you want to give your non newtonian fluid some color.
  3. Stir the mixture until it's smooth and free of lumps.
  4. Your non newtonian fluid is ready to use!

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're still not sure how to make non newtonian fluid, here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. In a bowl, mix one part water with two parts cornstarch.
  2. Add a few drops of food coloring if you want to give your non newtonian fluid some color.
  3. Stir the mixture until it's smooth and free of lumps.
  4. Your non newtonian fluid is ready to use!

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when making non newtonian fluid:

  • Use warm water to help the cornstarch dissolve more easily.
  • If your non newtonian fluid is too thick, add more water. If it's too thin, add more cornstarch.
  • Experiment with different ratios of water to cornstarch to create different textures and consistencies.
  • Non newtonian fluid can be messy, so be sure to work on a surface that's easy to clean.
