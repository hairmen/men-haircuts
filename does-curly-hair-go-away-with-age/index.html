Does Curly Hair Go Away With Age The Truth Behind The Myth

Written by Eveline Sep 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever heard the myth that curly hair goes away with age? This is a common misconception that many people believe to be true. The truth is, curly hair doesn't go away with age. However, there are some factors that can affect the texture and curl pattern of your hair over time. In this article, we'll discuss the truth behind the myth and provide tips on how to care for your curly hair as you age.

What Causes Curly Hair?

Curly hair is caused by the shape of the hair follicle. Follicles that are oval or asymmetrical produce curly hair, while round follicles produce straight hair. The amount of curl in your hair is determined by the number of disulfide bonds between hair proteins. These bonds are responsible for the strength and elasticity of the hair, which affects its curl pattern.

Does Curly Hair Go Away with Age?

The simple answer is no, curly hair does not go away with age. However, there are some factors that can affect the texture and curl pattern of your hair over time. One of the most common factors is hormonal changes. As we age, our hormone levels fluctuate, which can affect the texture of our hair. This can cause curly hair to become less curly or even straighten out completely.

Another factor that can affect the curl pattern of your hair is damage. Over time, heat styling, chemical treatments, and environmental factors can damage the hair shaft and cause it to lose its natural curl pattern. This can result in frizz, breakage, and limp, lifeless hair.

How to Care for Curly Hair as You Age

If you have curly hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and texture as you age. Here are some tips to help you care for your curly hair:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it's important to use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for a shampoo that is specifically designed for curly hair, and avoid shampoos that contain harsh detergents or sulfates.

2. Condition, Condition, Condition

Conditioner is your best friend when it comes to curly hair. Use a rich, moisturizing conditioner that will help to hydrate and nourish your curls. Leave the conditioner on for a few minutes before rinsing it out to allow it to penetrate the hair shaft.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage curly hair and cause it to lose its natural curl pattern. Instead of using a curling iron or straightener, try air-drying your hair or using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer. If you must use heat, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

4. Embrace Your Curls

Don't be afraid to embrace your natural curl pattern. Curly hair is beautiful and unique, and it's important to love and care for your hair regardless of its texture or curl pattern.

FAQ

Q: Can I Make My Hair Curlier?

A: If you have naturally curly hair, there are a few things you can do to enhance your curl pattern. Try using a curl-enhancing product, such as a curl defining cream or gel, to help define your curls and reduce frizz. You can also try diffusing your hair with a blow dryer to help enhance your natural curl pattern.

Q: How Can I Prevent Frizz?

A: Frizz is a common problem for curly hair, but there are a few things you can do to minimize it. Use a moisturizing conditioner, avoid heat styling, and don't brush your hair when it's dry. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.

Q: Can I Straighten My Curly Hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your curly hair, but it's important to use caution and minimize damage. Use a heat protectant spray, and avoid using heat styling tools on a regular basis. You can also try using a keratin treatment or other smoothing treatment to help reduce frizz and make your hair more manageable.

Conclusion

So, does curly hair go away with age? The answer is no, but there are some factors that can affect the texture and curl pattern of your hair over time. By taking proper care of your curly hair and embracing your natural texture, you can maintain healthy, beautiful curls at any age.
