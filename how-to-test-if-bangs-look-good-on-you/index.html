How To Test If Bangs Look Good On You

Written by Luffy Nov 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're considering getting bangs, you may be wondering if they'll look good on you. Bangs can be a great way to update your hairstyle, but they're not for everyone. In this article, we'll go over how to test if bangs look good on you and give you some tips on how to care for your new hairstyle.

How to Test if Bangs Look Good on You

The first step in testing if bangs look good on you is to consider your face shape. Bangs can look great on a variety of face shapes, but some styles may be more flattering than others. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Round face: Side-swept bangs, long and wispy bangs, or curtain bangs can be flattering.
  • Square face: Soft, wispy bangs or side-swept bangs can soften a strong jawline.
  • Heart-shaped face: Side-swept bangs or a wispy fringe can balance out a wider forehead.
  • Oval face: Almost any style of bangs can work with an oval face shape.

Once you've considered your face shape, you can try a few different techniques to test if bangs look good on you. Here are some options:

Option 1: Use a Virtual Makeover Tool

One of the easiest ways to test if bangs look good on you is to use a virtual makeover tool. Many beauty websites and mobile apps offer this feature, which allows you to upload a photo of yourself and try on different hairstyles.

Simply select a few different bang styles to see how they look on your face. You can also adjust the length and thickness of the bangs to customize the look.

Option 2: Create Fake Bangs

If you don't want to commit to cutting your hair, you can create fake bangs to see how they look. To do this, gather the front section of your hair and twist it back. Secure the twist with bobby pins, then let the remaining hair fall forward to create the illusion of bangs.

You can also try using clip-in bangs, which are available at many beauty supply stores and online retailers. This can be a great way to test out different styles without committing to a permanent change.

Option 3: Consult with a Hairstylist

If you're still not sure if bangs are right for you, consider consulting with a hairstylist. A professional can help you choose a style that flatters your face shape and works with your hair texture and lifestyle.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you've decided to take the plunge and get bangs, here's a step-by-step guide to help you prepare:

  1. Choose a style that flatters your face shape and works with your hair texture.
  2. Bring photos of the style you want to show your hairstylist.
  3. Discuss any concerns you have with your stylist, such as how much maintenance the style will require.
  4. Get your bangs cut by a professional. Don't try to do it yourself!
  5. Style your bangs daily using a blow dryer and a round brush.
  6. Consider using dry shampoo to extend the life of your bangs between washes.

Tips for Choosing Bangs

When choosing bangs, keep these tips in mind:

  • Consider your face shape and hair texture when choosing a style.
  • Start with a longer length and gradually shorten if you want a more dramatic look.
  • Choose a style that works with your lifestyle. If you don't have time to style your hair every day, opt for a low-maintenance style.
  • Remember that bangs can be versatile. You can wear them straight across, side-swept, or parted in the middle.

FAQs

How often should I trim my bangs?

This depends on the style of your bangs and how quickly your hair grows. As a general rule, plan to visit the salon every 4-6 weeks for a trim.

Can I wear bangs with curly hair?

Absolutely! Just keep in mind that the length and thickness of your bangs may need to be adjusted to work with your curls.

What if I don't like my bangs?

If you're not happy with your bangs, don't panic. You can always grow them out or have them trimmed into a different style.

Hair Care Tips for Bangs

To keep your bangs looking great, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your bangs daily or every other day to keep them clean and free of oil.
  • Blow dry your bangs with a round brush to create volume and shape.
  • Use dry shampoo to extend the life of your bangs between washes.
  • Avoid touching your bangs throughout the day to prevent them from becoming oily.
  • Consider using a heat protectant spray before blow drying or using hot styling tools.

By following these tips and testing out different styles, you can find the perfect bangs to flatter your face and enhance your hair style. Good luck!
