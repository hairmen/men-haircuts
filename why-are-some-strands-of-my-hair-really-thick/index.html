Why Are Some Strands Of My Hair Really Thick

Written by Eveline Sep 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered why some strands of your hair are thicker than others? It can be frustrating when you're trying to style your hair and it just won't cooperate. In this article, we'll explore why some strands of hair are thicker than others and what you can do to manage them.

Description

Thick hair strands can be a result of genetics or a variety of other factors. Some people simply have naturally thicker hair strands than others, while others may experience this due to certain health conditions or environmental factors. Regardless of the cause, it's important to understand the reasons behind thick hair strands so that you can properly care for your hair.

How to Identify Thick Hair Strands

Thick hair strands are easy to identify. They are wider in diameter than other hair strands and tend to be more resistant to styling. If you have thick hair strands, you may find that they are more difficult to manage and tend to be more prone to tangling.

Step by Step Guide to Managing Thick Hair Strands

If you're struggling to manage your thick hair strands, don't worry! There are several steps you can take to make styling your hair easier:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When combing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to prevent tangles and breakage. A wide-tooth comb will glide smoothly through your hair, minimizing the risk of damage.

2. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help to nourish and moisturize your hair, making it more manageable. Try to deep condition your hair at least once a week to keep it healthy and strong.

3. Use the Right Products

Choosing the right hair products can make a big difference in how your hair looks and feels. Look for products that are designed specifically for thick hair, such as volumizing shampoos and conditioners.

4. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks, or as needed.

5. Embrace Your Natural Texture

Instead of fighting against your hair's natural texture, embrace it! Try out different hairstyles that work with your hair's natural thickness and texture, rather than trying to force it to do something it doesn't want to do.

Tips for Managing Thick Hair Strands

Here are a few additional tips to help you manage your thick hair strands:

1. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools can be damaging to your hair, especially if you have thick hair strands. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, or use them sparingly.

2. Be Gentle When Brushing

When brushing your hair, be gentle to minimize breakage and damage. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots, using a gentle, detangling brush.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Exposure to the sun can be damaging to your hair, especially if you have thick hair strands. Wear a hat or use a hair product that contains SPF to protect your hair from UV rays.

FAQ

Q. Can I Thin Out My Thick Hair Strands?

A. Yes, you can thin out your hair strands by getting a haircut that removes some of the bulk. However, be careful not to overdo it, as thinning out your hair too much can lead to breakage and damage.

Q. Will Cutting My Hair Make It Thinner?

A. No, cutting your hair will not make it thinner. However, getting a haircut that removes some of the bulk can make your hair appear thinner and more manageable.

Q. Can I Straighten My Thick Hair Strands?

A. Yes, you can straighten your thick hair strands, but be sure to use a heat protectant and avoid heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage.

Hair Care for Thick Hair Strands

Caring for your hair is essential if you want to keep it healthy and strong. Here are a few tips for caring for thick hair strands:

1. Wash Your Hair Regularly

Washing your hair regularly can help to keep it clean and healthy. Aim to wash your hair every two to three days, or as needed.

2. Use a Lightweight Conditioner

When conditioning your hair, use a lightweight conditioner that won't weigh it down. Look for products that are designed specifically for thick hair.

3. Protect Your Hair While Sleeping

Protect your hair while you sleep by using a silk or satin pillowcase, which will minimize friction and prevent tangles.

4. Avoid Overstyling

Overstyling your hair can be damaging, especially if you have thick hair strands. Try to limit your use of heat styling tools and avoid using too many hair products.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can keep your thick hair strands looking healthy and beautiful!
