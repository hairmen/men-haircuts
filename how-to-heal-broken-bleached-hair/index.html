How To Heal Broken Bleached Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're someone who loves experimenting with hair colors, you might have experienced the damage that comes with it. Bleaching is one of the most damaging processes for hair that can leave your locks feeling brittle, dry, and lifeless. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this blog post, we'll share some effective tips and tricks on how to heal broken bleached hair and restore it to its former glory.

Description

Bleaching involves stripping the hair of its natural pigments, and while it can look great, it can also damage the hair's structure. Bleached hair is more prone to breakage, split ends, and dryness. It's essential to take care of your bleached hair to avoid further damage and breakage.

How to Heal Broken Bleached Hair

Here are some effective ways to heal broken bleached hair:

1. Deep Conditioning

Deep conditioning is an excellent way to restore moisture to your hair. Use a deep conditioner that contains protein to strengthen your hair and repair the damage caused by bleaching. Leave the conditioner in your hair for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for maximum effect.

2. Use a Hair Mask

Using a hair mask once or twice a week can also help heal broken bleached hair. Look for a hair mask that contains natural ingredients like coconut oil, shea butter, or avocado. These ingredients can help nourish and moisturize your hair, leaving it soft and shiny.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers can further damage your hair. Give your hair a break from these tools and let it air dry naturally. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help get rid of split ends and prevent further breakage. Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it healthy and prevent breakage.

5. Limit Sun Exposure

Exposure to the sun can damage your hair, especially if it's already bleached. Wear a hat or a scarf to protect your hair from the sun's harmful rays.

Step by Step

Here are the steps you can follow to heal your broken bleached hair:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo.
  2. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair, and leave it on for at least 30 minutes or overnight.
  3. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  4. Apply a hair mask to your hair once or twice a week.
  5. Avoid heat styling tools and let your hair air dry naturally.
  6. Trim your hair every six to eight weeks.
  7. Wear a hat or a scarf to protect your hair from the sun's harmful rays.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help heal your broken bleached hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair after washing.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals and hair dyes on your bleached hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet with plenty of vitamins and minerals to support healthy hair growth.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated from the inside out.

FAQ

Q. Can broken bleached hair be fixed?

A. Yes, broken bleached hair can be fixed with the right care and treatment.

Q. How long does it take for broken bleached hair to heal?

A. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to a few months for broken bleached hair to heal, depending on the severity of the damage.

Q. Should I cut off my broken bleached hair?

A. It's not always necessary to cut off broken bleached hair. Regular trims can help get rid of split ends and prevent further breakage.

Hair Care

Here are some general hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water, which can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair after washing.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals and hair dyes on your hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet with plenty of vitamins and minerals to support healthy hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you can heal your broken bleached hair and restore it to its former glory. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and you'll see results in no time.
