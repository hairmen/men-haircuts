Is Perming Hair Bad For Health

Written by Bardi Nov 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Perming hair has been a popular trend for decades, but there has been a growing concern about its impact on our health. In this article, we will explore the question "Is perming hair bad for health?" and provide you with tips and advice on how to care for your hair if you decide to perm it.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Perm Hair
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Perming Hair
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care after Perming

Description

Perming hair involves using a chemical process to change the structure of your hair. The chemicals used in the process can break down the protein bonds in your hair, which can weaken your hair and make it more prone to breakage. This can lead to hair loss, thinning hair, and damage to your scalp.

Perming hair is also associated with an increased risk of scalp burns, scalp irritation, and hair damage. The chemicals used in the process can cause allergic reactions, which can lead to redness, itching, and swelling of the scalp.

How to Perm Hair

If you are considering perming your hair, it is important to understand the process and how it works. There are two types of perms: alkaline perms and acid perms. Alkaline perms use a chemical called ammonium thioglycolate to break down the protein bonds in your hair, while acid perms use a chemical called glyceryl monothioglycolate.

The process of perming hair involves applying the chemical to your hair, wrapping it around perm rods, and then applying a neutralizer to set the curls. It is important to follow the instructions carefully and to avoid over-processing your hair, as this can lead to damage and breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to perm your hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  2. Divide your hair into sections and wrap each section around a perm rod.
  3. Apply the perm solution to your hair, making sure to saturate each section.
  4. Cover your hair with a plastic cap and let it process for the recommended time.
  5. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  6. Apply the neutralizer to your hair and let it sit for the recommended time.
  7. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  8. Remove the perm rods and style your hair as desired.

Tips for Perming Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when perming your hair:

  • Choose a reputable salon or stylist who has experience with perming hair.
  • Do a patch test to check for allergies or sensitivities to the chemicals.
  • Wait at least 48 hours after perming before washing or styling your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish your hair.
  • Avoid heat styling your hair, as this can cause further damage.

FAQs

Q: Is perming hair bad for your health?

A: Perming hair can weaken your hair and make it more prone to breakage, which can lead to hair loss and damage to your scalp.

Q: What are the risks of perming hair?

A: The risks of perming hair include scalp burns, scalp irritation, hair loss, and damage to your hair and scalp.

Q: How often can you perm your hair?

A: It is recommended to wait at least 6-8 weeks between perming your hair to avoid over-processing and damage.

Hair Care after Perming

After perming your hair, it is important to take care of it properly to avoid further damage. Here are some tips for caring for permed hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair for at least 48 hours after perming.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish your hair.
  • Avoid heat styling your hair, as this can cause further damage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to remove split ends and prevent breakage.

In conclusion, perming hair can be damaging to your hair and scalp, but if done properly and with care, it can also be a fun and stylish way to change up your look. Always choose a reputable salon or stylist, follow the instructions carefully, and take care of your hair after perming to avoid further damage.
