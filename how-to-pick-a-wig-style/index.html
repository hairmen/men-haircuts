How To Pick A Wig Style A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jun 14, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you looking to change up your hairstyle without committing to a drastic cut or color? Wigs are a great way to experiment with different looks and styles. However, with so many options available, it can be overwhelming to choose the perfect wig. In this guide, we’ll walk you through how to pick a wig style that suits your face shape, skin tone, and personal taste.

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in picking the right wig style is to determine your face shape. The most common face shapes are round, square, oval, heart, and diamond. Different wig styles will flatter different face shapes, so it’s important to know which category you fall into.

If you’re not sure what your face shape is, stand in front of a mirror and use a measuring tape to take the following measurements:

  • Forehead: Measure from the widest point of your forehead (usually the hairline) to the other side.
  • Cheekbones: Measure the widest part of your cheekbones.
  • Jawline: Measure from the tip of your chin to the area below your ear where your jawline starts.
  • Face length: Measure from the center of your hairline to the tip of your chin.

Once you have these measurements, you can use them to determine your face shape:

  • Round: Your face is as wide as it is long, with full cheeks and a rounded jawline.
  • Square: Your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly equal in width, with a strong, angular jawline.
  • Oval: Your face is longer than it is wide, with a gently rounded hairline and a slightly narrow chin.
  • Heart: Your forehead is wider than your jawline, with a pointed chin.
  • Diamond: Your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, with a narrow forehead and jawline.

Knowing your face shape will help you choose a wig style that complements your features and balances out your proportions.

Step 2: Consider Your Skin Tone

Another factor to consider when choosing a wig style is your skin tone. Just like with clothing, certain wig colors will look better on you depending on whether you have warm or cool undertones.

If you have warm undertones (yellow, peachy, or golden), you’ll look best in wigs with colors like honey blonde, copper, or auburn. If you have cool undertones (pink, red, or blue), go for colors like platinum blonde, ash brown, or black.

You can also consider your eye color when choosing a wig color. If you have blue or green eyes, warm blonde shades will make your eyes pop. If you have brown eyes, rich browns and reds will bring out the warmth in your eyes.

Step 3: Choose the Right Wig Cap

Before you start browsing wig styles, you’ll need to choose the right wig cap. There are several types of wig caps available, including:

  • Traditional: This is the most common type of wig cap, with a cap that sits snugly on your head and hair that is sewn onto the cap.
  • Lace front: This type of wig cap has a sheer lace panel at the front of the wig, which gives the illusion of a natural hairline.
  • Monofilament: This type of wig cap has a sheer mesh panel at the crown of the wig, which allows for a more natural-looking part.

Consider your comfort level and the look you’re going for when choosing a wig cap. Traditional caps are the most affordable and widely available, but lace front and monofilament caps offer a more natural look.

Step 4: Select a Style

Now that you’ve determined your face shape, skin tone, and wig cap preference, it’s time to select a style. The best wig styles will balance out your proportions and draw attention to your best features.

Here are some general guidelines for selecting a wig style based on your face shape:

  • Round: Look for wigs with height at the crown and volume on top to elongate your face.
  • Square: Soften your angles with wigs that have wispy bangs or long layers.
  • Oval: Lucky you! Most wig styles will look great on an oval face shape.
  • Heart: Draw attention to your eyes and cheekbones with wigs that have side-swept bangs or volume at the crown.
  • Diamond: Balance out your cheekbones with wigs that have volume at the chin or jawline.

Of course, these are just general guidelines. Feel free to experiment with different styles and find what works best for you.

Step 5: Try on Wigs

Once you’ve narrowed down your wig style options, it’s time to try them on! Most wig retailers will have a selection of wigs you can try on in-store. If you’re shopping online, look for sites that offer virtual wig try-on tools, or consider ordering a few different styles to try at home.

When you try on a wig, make sure it fits snugly but comfortably on your head. The wig should sit just above your natural hairline, with the ear tabs sitting right above your ears.

Tips for Choosing a Wig Style

  • Bring a trusted friend or family member with you when trying on wigs for a second opinion.
  • Consider the occasion or purpose for the wig – do you need a casual, everyday wig or a more formal one for special events?
  • Don’t be afraid to try something new – wigs are a great way to experiment with different looks!

FAQs

What wig material is best?

The best wig material depends on your needs and budget. Synthetic wigs are affordable and low-maintenance, but can’t be heat-styled. Human hair wigs are more expensive, but can be styled just like your natural hair.

How do I care for my wig?

Wash your wig every 6-8 wears with a wig-specific shampoo and conditioner. Store your wig on a wig stand or in a hairnet to prevent tangles. Avoid exposing your wig to heat, such as from blow dryers or curling irons.

Hair Care for Wigs

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your wig looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Invest in a wig brush or wide-tooth comb to prevent tangles.
  • Avoid using regular hair products on your wig, as they can weigh it down or cause damage.
  • Store your wig in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.

With these tips and guidelines, you’re ready to pick the perfect wig style for you. Have fun experimenting with different looks and enjoy your new hairstyle!
