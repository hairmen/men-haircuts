How To Cut Kid Boy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jul 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of taking your kid to the salon every time he needs a haircut? Or maybe you just want to save some money and do it yourself? Whatever the reason, cutting kid boy hair can be a daunting task. But fear not, with the right tools and techniques, you can give your little one a stylish and professional-looking haircut right at home.

  2. Preparation
  3. The Cut

Tools You'll Need

Before you start, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Scissors (preferably hairdressing scissors)
  • Clippers (with different guard sizes)
  • Comb
  • Hair spray or water spray
  • Cape or towel to cover the child's clothes

Preparation

Start by preparing your child for the haircut. Explain to him what you're going to do and make sure he's comfortable and relaxed. It might be helpful to distract him with a favorite toy or TV show.

Cover your child's clothes with a cape or towel to protect them from hair clippings. Then, wet your child's hair with a spray bottle or water. This will make the hair easier to cut and help you see how it falls naturally.

The Cut

Start by using the clippers to trim the hair on the sides and back of the head. Use the longest guard size first and work your way down to a shorter size until you reach the desired length. Make sure to blend the hair at the top with the hair on the sides and back to avoid any harsh lines.

Next, use the scissors to cut the hair on top of the head. Start by combing the hair straight up and trimming any excess length. Use the scissors to create layers and texture if desired.

For the bangs, comb the hair forward and trim the ends straight across. Make sure not to cut them too short as they will appear shorter once the hair dries.

Finishing Touches

Once you're done with the haircut, use the clippers to clean up any stray hairs around the ears and neck. Then, brush off any clippings from your child's clothes and apply some hair spray if necessary to keep the style in place.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to a shorter size to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use the comb to lift the hair as you cut to avoid cutting too much off.
  • Take breaks if your child becomes restless or uncomfortable.
  • Don't rush the haircut. Take your time and make sure you're happy with the results before finishing.

FAQs

How often should I cut my child's hair?

It depends on how fast your child's hair grows and the style you're going for. Generally, every 4-6 weeks is a good rule of thumb.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't worry, hair grows back! If you make a mistake, stop and assess the situation. You can always trim a little more off, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.

What if my child won't sit still?

Try distracting him with a toy or TV show. You can also take breaks if needed or ask for help from another adult.

Hair Care After the Haircut

After the haircut, make sure to rinse your child's hair thoroughly to remove any loose hairs. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools on young children's hair as it can damage the hair and scalp.

With these tips and techniques, you can give your little one a stylish and professional-looking haircut right at home. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
