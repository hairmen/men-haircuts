How To Know If You re Shedding Too Much Hair

Written by Petter Nov 19, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you noticing more hair than usual in your hairbrush or on your pillow? Hair shedding is a natural process, but excessive hair loss can be a sign of an underlying issue. In this article, we'll discuss how to know if you're shedding too much hair and what you can do about it.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

The first step in determining if you're shedding too much hair is to understand what's normal for you. On average, people lose between 50-100 hairs per day. If you have long hair, it may seem like you're shedding more because the hairs are longer and more noticeable. However, if you're losing clumps of hair or noticing bald patches, it's time to take action.

There are a few ways you can assess your hair shedding:

  1. Check your hairbrush: After brushing your hair, count the number of hairs that are left in the brush. If it's more than usual, it may be a sign of excessive shedding.
  2. Look at your pillow: If you notice a lot of hair on your pillow in the morning, it could be a sign that you're shedding too much hair.
  3. Examine your hair: Take a close look at your hairline, part, and crown. If you notice thinning or bald patches, it's time to take action.

Step by step

If you're shedding too much hair, there are a few steps you can take to address the issue:

  1. Identify the cause: Excessive hair shedding can be caused by a variety of factors, including stress, hormonal changes, genetics, and certain medications. Consult with a healthcare provider to determine the underlying cause.
  2. Improve your diet: Eating a balanced diet that includes protein, iron, and vitamins can help promote healthy hair growth.
  3. Reduce stress: Stress can contribute to hair shedding. Try incorporating stress-reducing activities into your daily routine, such as meditation or yoga.
  4. Use gentle hair care products: Harsh chemicals and heat styling tools can damage hair and contribute to shedding. Use gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners and avoid excessive heat styling.
  5. Consider hair loss treatments: There are a variety of hair loss treatments available, including medications and hair transplant surgery. Consult with a healthcare provider to determine the best option for you.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help prevent excessive hair shedding:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair, such as braids or ponytails.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling.
  • Avoid harsh hair care products, such as those containing sulfates.
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes protein, iron, and vitamins.
  • Reduce stress through activities such as meditation or yoga.

FAQ

Q: Is it normal to shed hair every day?

A: Yes, it's normal to shed between 50-100 hairs per day.

Q: Can stress cause hair shedding?

A: Yes, stress can contribute to hair shedding.

Q: Are there any vitamins or supplements that can help prevent hair shedding?

A: Biotin, vitamin D, and iron are all important for healthy hair growth. Consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplements.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important for preventing excessive shedding. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes protein, iron, and vitamins.
  • Consult with a healthcare provider if you're experiencing excessive hair shedding.

By following these tips, you can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent excessive shedding. Remember, shedding some hair is normal, but if you're noticing excessive shedding, it's important to take action.
