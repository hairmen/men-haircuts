How To Cut The Back Of Your Hair Bob A Guide For Beginners

Written by Frank Jun 25, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking for a fresh and stylish haircut, a bob is a great option. But what do you do when it's time to trim the back of your hair? Cutting the back of your hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're new to it. But don't worry, we've got you covered with this step-by-step guide on how to cut the back of your hair bob.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut the Back of Your Hair Bob
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for a Great Cut
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care After the Cut

How to Cut the Back of Your Hair Bob

Cutting the back of your hair bob can be tricky, but it's not impossible. Here are some things you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • Patience

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. It's also a good idea to wear a shirt that you don't mind getting hair on.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut the back of your hair bob:

  1. Part your hair down the middle and comb it straight down your back.
  2. Take a small section of hair from the center of your head and pull it straight up. This will be your guide for the length.
  3. Using your guide section, cut across the hair in a straight line. Be careful not to cut too much at once.
  4. Continue cutting small sections of hair across your head, using your guide section as a reference for length.
  5. When you reach the sides of your head, pull the hair straight back and cut across in a straight line.
  6. Check your work in the mirror to make sure the length is even.
  7. Trim any areas that need it, but be careful not to overdo it.

Remember to take breaks and step back to assess your work. It's better to take your time than to rush and make mistakes.

Tips for a Great Cut

Here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to take small sections and work slowly.
  • Step back and assess your work frequently to make sure the length is even.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're not comfortable cutting the back of your hair on your own.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about cutting the back of your hair bob:

Can I cut the back of my hair bob by myself?

Yes, but it's important to be patient and take your time. It's also a good idea to have a mirror behind you to help you see what you're doing.

How do I know if I'm cutting my hair evenly?

Step back from the mirror frequently to assess your work. You can also use a ruler or measuring tape to ensure the length is even.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! Mistakes happen. If you cut too much, you can always trim it down gradually. If you're really unhappy with the result, you can always go to a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care After the Cut

After you've cut the back of your hair bob, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Blow dry your hair on a low heat setting.
  • Use a heat protectant spray if you're using hot tools like a straightener or curling iron.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the length.

By following these tips, your hair will look great and stay healthy after your DIY haircut.

Remember, cutting the back of your hair bob can be intimidating at first, but with practice and patience, you can get great results. Happy cutting!
