How To Fix Dreads That Are Falling Out A Complete Guide

Written by Petter Nov 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have dreadlocks, you know how much time and effort goes into maintaining them. But what happens when your dreadlocks start falling out? Don't worry, it's a common problem, and there are ways to fix it. In this article, we'll go over how to fix dreads that are falling out and give you some tips on how to prevent it from happening in the future.

Description

Dreadlocks are a hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are created by twisting and knotting hair together to form a rope-like structure. Dreadlocks are popular among people of all races and ages, and they are a great way to express yourself. However, one of the biggest challenges of having dreadlocks is keeping them intact. Dreadlocks can easily become loose and fall out, especially if they are not properly maintained.

How to Fix Dreads That Are Falling Out

If your dreadlocks are falling out, don't panic. There are several ways to fix them, depending on the severity of the problem. Here are some of the things you can do:

1. Re-twist Your Dreads

If your dreads are just starting to come loose, you can try re-twisting them. To do this, you'll need to separate a section of your hair and twist it in the opposite direction of the way it's already twisted. This will help tighten the lock and keep it from falling out. You can use a locking gel or wax to help hold the twist in place.

2. Use a Crochet Hook

If your dreads are more than just a little loose, you may need to use a crochet hook to fix them. A crochet hook is a small tool that is used to pull loose hair back into the dread. To use a crochet hook, simply insert it into the dread and pull the loose hair back in. Be gentle, so you don't damage your hair or your scalp.

3. Add Extensions

If your dreads are falling out because they are too short, you can add extensions to them. Extensions are a great way to add length and volume to your dreads. You can use human hair or synthetic hair to create the extensions. Make sure to choose hair that is similar in texture and color to your natural hair.

Step by Step Guide

Now that you know some of the things you can do to fix your falling out dreads, let's go over the step-by-step process of each option:

Re-twist Your Dreads

Step 1: Separate a section of your hair that needs re-twisting.
Step 2: Twist the hair in the opposite direction of the way it's already twisted.
Step 3: Use a locking gel or wax to help hold the twist in place.

Use a Crochet Hook

Step 1: Insert the crochet hook into the dread where the hair is coming loose.
Step 2: Pull the loose hair back into the dread using the crochet hook.
Step 3: Be gentle, so you don't damage your hair or your scalp.

Add Extensions

Step 1: Choose human hair or synthetic hair that is similar in texture and color to your natural hair.
Step 2: Braid the extension hair into your natural hair.
Step 3: Use a crochet hook to pull the extension hair into the dread.
Step 4: Be gentle, so you don't damage your hair or your scalp.

Tips

Here are some tips to help prevent your dreadlocks from falling out:

  • Keep your hair clean and well-maintained.
  • Avoid using too much product on your hair.
  • Be gentle when washing and drying your hair.
  • Avoid twisting your hair too tightly, as this can cause breakage.
  • Get regular maintenance from a professional stylist.

FAQ

Can I fix my falling out dreads on my own?

Yes, you can fix your falling out dreads on your own, but be careful not to damage your hair or your scalp.

Can I still keep my dreadlocks if they keep falling out?

Yes, you can still keep your dreadlocks if they keep falling out. Just make sure to take good care of them and get regular maintenance from a professional stylist.

How long does it take to fix falling out dreads?

The time it takes to fix falling out dreads depends on the severity of the problem. It can take anywhere from a few minutes to a few hours.

Hair Care

To keep your dreadlocks healthy and strong, make sure to follow a good hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioner or hot oil treatment once a month to keep your hair healthy.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

By following these tips, you can keep your dreadlocks looking great and prevent them from falling out.
