Description

Thinning scissors are a great tool to use when cutting short hair. They can help create texture, add volume, and blend layers seamlessly. However, using them can be intimidating, especially if you're new to hair cutting. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about how to cut short hair with thinning scissors.

How to Cut Short Hair with Thinning Scissors

Before we get into the step-by-step guide, let's go over some basics. Thinning scissors have teeth on one blade that remove hair as they cut. They are used to thin out thick hair or create texture in short hair. When using them, it's important to remember that you're not cutting the hair, but rather removing some of it. This is what gives short hair its volume and texture.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now, let's get into the actual steps for using thinning scissors on short hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see the hair's natural texture and where you need to thin it out.
  2. Section off the hair you want to thin out. This can be the top layer or the sides, depending on the look you're going for.
  3. Hold the hair between your index and middle finger, about an inch away from the scalp.
  4. Using the thinning scissors, make small cuts into the hair, working your way down to the ends. Be sure to keep the scissors at an angle, so you're not cutting straight across.
  5. Repeat this process until you've thinned out the desired amount of hair.
  6. Move on to the next section, repeating the same process until you've thinned out all the hair you want to.
  7. Blend the layers together by combing through the hair with a regular comb. This will help create a seamless, natural look.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when using thinning scissors:

  • Start with small cuts, and work your way up to bigger ones. This will help you control the amount of hair you're removing.
  • Don't use thinning scissors on wet hair. This can cause the hair to become too thin, and you may end up removing too much.
  • Be gentle when using thinning scissors. They are sharp, and can easily damage hair if not used correctly.
  • Practice, practice, practice! Thinning scissors take some getting used to, so don't be discouraged if you don't get it right the first time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people have about using thinning scissors on short hair:

  • Can I use thinning scissors on curly hair? Yes, you can! Just be sure to use them sparingly, as curly hair tends to be more fragile than straight hair.
  • Can I use thinning scissors on long hair? Thinning scissors are best used on short hair, as they can create too much volume and texture in long hair. If you want to thin out long hair, it's best to use regular scissors.
  • Do thinning scissors damage hair? If used incorrectly, yes, they can. That's why it's important to be gentle and start with small cuts.

Hair Care After Thinning Scissors Cut

After using thinning scissors on your short hair, it's important to take care of your hair properly to maintain its health and texture. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair and make it look frizzy.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and texture of your short hair.

Now that you know how to cut short hair with thinning scissors, it's time to give it a try! Remember to take your time, start with small cuts, and be gentle. With practice, you'll be a pro in no time!
