Why Does My Hair Look Shorter When Dry

Written by Bardi Jul 07, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you wondering why your hair seems to shrink when it dries? You're not alone. Many people have experienced the phenomenon of their hair appearing shorter when it's dry. In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind this and provide tips on how to deal with it.

Description

When you get out of the shower, your hair is wet and stretched out. As it dries, it contracts, causing it to appear shorter. This happens because hair is made up of a protein called keratin, which is affected by moisture.

The amount of shrinkage you experience can vary depending on your hair type. People with curly or coily hair tend to experience more shrinkage than those with straight hair due to the nature of their hair's structure.

How to Deal With Shrinkage

If you're not a fan of the shorter look, there are ways to combat shrinkage. Here are a few tips:

Stretching Methods

There are several stretching methods you can use to elongate your hair, such as braiding or twisting it while it's wet. Once it's dry, you can undo the braids or twists, revealing longer hair.

Blow Drying

Blow drying your hair with a comb attachment can help stretch it out and reduce shrinkage. Be careful not to use too much heat, as this can damage your hair.

Low Manipulation Styles

Choosing low manipulation styles such as buns or twists can help reduce shrinkage. These styles don't require a lot of manipulation, which means less opportunity for your hair to contract.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to elongate your hair and reduce shrinkage:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help keep your hair hydrated and reduce the amount of shrinkage you experience.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that all areas of your hair are stretched out.

Step 3: Stretch Your Hair

Choose a stretching method such as braiding or twisting your hair while it's wet. Once it's dry, undo the braids or twists to reveal longer hair. You can also blow dry your hair with a comb attachment to stretch it out.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Choose a low manipulation style such as a bun or twist to help maintain the elongated look of your hair.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help reduce shrinkage:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using excessive heat
  • Choose low manipulation styles
  • Protect your hair at night with a satin or silk scarf

FAQs

Here are some commonly asked questions about shrinkage:

Q: Is shrinkage bad for my hair?

A: Shrinkage is a natural part of having curly or coily hair and isn't necessarily bad for your hair. However, it can be frustrating for those who are looking to maintain a certain length or style.

Q: Can I prevent shrinkage?

A: While you can't completely prevent shrinkage, there are ways to reduce it. Stretching methods and low manipulation styles can help elongate your hair and reduce the amount of shrinkage you experience.

Q: Is shrinkage permanent?

A: No, shrinkage is not permanent. Once your hair is stretched out, it will appear longer until it contracts again when it comes in contact with moisture.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, hydrated hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using excessive heat on your hair
  • Protect your hair from the elements with a hat or scarf
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

In conclusion, shrinkage is a natural part of having curly or coily hair. While you can't completely prevent it, there are ways to reduce it and elongate your hair. By following the tips and techniques outlined in this article, you can achieve the length and style you desire.
