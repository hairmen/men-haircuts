How To Cut Your Own Hair Short Layered Bob

Written by Aprilia Jun 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a fresh new look, why not try a short layered bob? This stylish cut is popular among women of all ages and can be easily achieved at home. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your own hair short layered bob in relaxed English language. Here's what you need to know:

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Short Layered Bob
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

A short layered bob is a chic and stylish haircut that can be customized to suit your face shape and hair type. This cut typically features shorter layers in the back and longer layers in the front, creating a flattering and face-framing shape. It's a great choice for women who want a low-maintenance yet fashionable look.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Short Layered Bob

Before you begin, gather the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips
  • Mirror

It's important to use sharp scissors to ensure a clean and precise cut. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.

Step by Step

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before cutting. This will make it easier to see the shape of your hair and ensure an even cut.

Step 2: Section your hair

Use clips to divide your hair into four sections: top, sides, and back. This will make it easier to cut each section separately.

Step 3: Cut the back section

Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the middle of your head and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. Repeat this process for the rest of the back section, working your way from the middle to the sides.

Step 4: Cut the side sections

Next, move on to the side sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the front of your ear and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at a slight angle, following the shape of your jawline. Repeat this process for the rest of the side sections, working your way towards the back.

Step 5: Cut the top section

Finally, move on to the top section of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the crown of your head and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle, creating longer layers in the front. Repeat this process for the rest of the top section, working your way towards the sides.

Tips

  • Start with small sections of hair, as it's easier to cut more hair than to fix a mistake.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your hair as you cut.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can result in an uneven cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to, as cutting your own hair can be tiring.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair, consider consulting a professional stylist.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

You can cut your hair wet or dry, but it's generally easier to see the shape of your hair when it's dry.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go to a professional stylist to fix the issue or try to blend the mistake into the rest of your hair.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and shape. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair. Avoid using heat tools like straighteners and curling irons, and try to air dry your hair whenever possible. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain its shape.

Congratulations, you now know how to cut your own hair short layered bob in relaxed English language! With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and fashionable look from the comfort of your own home.
