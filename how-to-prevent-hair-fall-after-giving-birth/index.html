How To Prevent Hair Fall After Giving Birth

Written by Eveline Aug 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Becoming a mother is a wonderful experience, but it comes with its own set of challenges. One of the most common issues that new mothers face is hair fall after giving birth. This can be a result of hormonal changes, stress, lack of sleep, or poor nutrition. However, there are ways to prevent hair fall and promote healthy hair growth. In this article, we will discuss some effective tips on how to prevent hair fall after giving birth.

How to Prevent Hair Fall

Preventing hair fall after giving birth requires a holistic approach. You need to take care of your body, mind, and hair to promote healthy hair growth. Here are some ways to prevent hair fall:

Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet is crucial for healthy hair growth. Make sure you consume enough protein, vitamins, and minerals. Foods that are rich in protein include eggs, meat, poultry, fish, beans, and lentils. Vitamins and minerals that are essential for healthy hair include vitamin A, B, C, D, E, iron, zinc, and selenium. You can get these nutrients from fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds, and whole grains.

Manage Stress

Stress can trigger hair fall. You need to find ways to manage stress, such as meditation, yoga, deep breathing, or spending time in nature. You can also talk to a therapist or counselor if you are feeling overwhelmed.

Get Enough Sleep

Sleep is essential for your overall health, including your hair. Lack of sleep can lead to hair fall. Try to get at least 7-8 hours of sleep every night.

Take Supplements

Taking supplements can help promote healthy hair growth. You can take supplements that contain biotin, collagen, or omega-3 fatty acids. Consult with your doctor before taking any supplements.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to prevent hair fall after giving birth:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair with a Mild Shampoo

Use a mild shampoo to wash your hair. Avoid using hot water, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils. Use lukewarm water instead.

Step 2: Condition Your Hair

Use a conditioner after shampooing your hair. Apply the conditioner from the mid-length of your hair to the tips. Avoid applying it to your scalp.

Step 3: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair. Start from the tips and work your way up to the roots. Avoid using a brush, as it can cause breakage.

Step 4: Dry Your Hair Gently

Use a soft towel to gently dry your hair. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously, as it can cause breakage.

Tips to Prevent Hair Fall

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair fall:

Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons, curling irons, or hair dryers. Heat can damage your hair and cause breakage.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles, such as braids, ponytails, or buns. These hairstyles can pull on your hair and cause breakage.

Massage Your Scalp

Massage your scalp regularly to promote blood circulation. You can use coconut oil or almond oil to massage your scalp.

Trim Your Hair

Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends. Split ends can cause breakage and make your hair look frizzy and dull.

FAQs

Q: How long does hair fall last after giving birth?

A: Hair fall after giving birth is temporary and usually lasts for a few months. However, if you are experiencing excessive hair fall, you should consult with your doctor.

Q: Can breastfeeding cause hair fall?

A: Yes, breastfeeding can cause hair fall due to hormonal changes. However, this is temporary and usually resolves on its own.

Q: Can I color my hair after giving birth?

A: It is best to wait until your hair fall has stopped before coloring your hair. Coloring your hair can cause further damage and breakage.

Hair Care Routine

Here is a hair care routine to prevent hair fall:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair 2-3 times a week with a mild shampoo.

Step 2: Condition Your Hair

Use a conditioner after shampooing your hair.

Step 3: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair.

Step 4: Massage Your Scalp

Massage your scalp regularly to promote blood circulation.

Step 5: Trim Your Hair

Trim your hair every 3-4 months to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can prevent hair fall after giving birth and promote healthy hair growth.
