How To Thin Your Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Nov 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Thinning your hair can be a great way to achieve a lighter, more manageable style. Although it is typically done at a salon, you can also do it yourself at home. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to thin your hair yourself.

How to Thin Your Hair Yourself

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary supplies. You will need a pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, and a mirror. You may also want to have a spray bottle filled with water to dampen your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see where you need to thin your hair. You can also use a detangling spray or conditioner to make your hair more manageable.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, section your hair into four parts: the top, the sides, and the back. Secure each section with a clip.

Step 4: Thin the Top Section

Start with the top section. Using your scissors, take small sections of hair and snip vertically into the hair. Be sure to only cut a little bit at a time – you can always go back and thin more later.

Step 5: Thin the Side Sections

Once the top section is thinned to your liking, move on to the side sections. Repeat the same technique, taking small sections of hair and snipping vertically into the hair.

Step 6: Thin the Back Section

Finally, move on to the back section. This can be a bit more difficult to do yourself, so you may want to ask a friend or family member to help you. Using the same technique, take small sections of hair and snip vertically into the hair.

Step 7: Blend Your Hair

Once you have thinned your hair, use your comb to blend the sections together. This will ensure that your hair looks natural and not choppy.

Step 8: Check Your Work

Using a mirror, check your work from all angles. If you notice any spots that need more thinning, go back and snip a little more hair.

Step 9: Style Your Hair

Now that you have thinned your hair, you can style it as desired. Whether you want to wear it down or in an updo, your hair should be lighter and easier to manage.

Tips for Thinning Your Hair Yourself

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when thinning your hair yourself:

  • Always use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take your time – don’t rush the process.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up.
  • Don’t thin your hair too much – it’s better to take it slow and steady.
  • Remember that it’s always better to have someone help you with the back section.

FAQs

How often should I thin my hair?

You should only thin your hair every 6-8 weeks to avoid over-thinning and damaging your hair.

Can I thin my hair with regular scissors?

No, you should always use hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean cut and avoid damaging your hair.

Will thinning my hair make it grow back thicker?

No, thinning your hair will not make it grow back thicker. It will simply make it lighter and more manageable.

Hair Care After Thinning Your Hair

After thinning your hair, it’s important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and avoid pulling or breaking it.

Thinning your hair yourself can be a great way to achieve a lighter, more manageable style. Just be sure to take your time and follow these steps to ensure a clean, even cut.
