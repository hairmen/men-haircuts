Do Side Bangs Suit Round Faces Everything You Need To Know

Written by Frank Oct 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Side bangs have been a popular hairstyle for years, and they're not going away anytime soon. But with so many different face shapes out there, it can be hard to know if side bangs will suit you. In this article, we'll be answering the question, "Do side bangs suit round faces?" and providing you with all the information you need to make an informed decision about your next hairstyle.

Description

Round faces are characterized by their soft, curved jawline and equal width and length. For those with round faces, finding the right haircut can be tricky. You want a style that will accentuate your features without making your face look even rounder. That's where side bangs come in. By framing your face and drawing attention to your eyes, side bangs can help elongate your face and create the illusion of more defined cheekbones. But not all side bangs are created equal, so it's important to choose the right style for your face shape.

How To

The key to finding the right side bangs for your round face is to choose a style that will balance out your features. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Step 1: Consider Your Hair Type

Before you can choose the right style of side bangs, you need to consider your hair type. If you have thick, coarse hair, you'll need a heavier, more blunt cut to avoid looking too fluffy. If you have fine hair, opt for softer, wispy bangs that will blend seamlessly into your hair.

Step 2: Choose the Right Length

The length of your side bangs is crucial when it comes to balancing out your face shape. If your face is very round, choose longer side bangs that hit just below your cheekbones. This will help create the illusion of a more oval-shaped face. If your face is only slightly round, you can go for shorter bangs that hit just above your eyebrows.

Step 3: Consider the Shape of Your Bangs

The shape of your side bangs is also important. If your face is very round, choose bangs that have a slight angle to them. This will help elongate your face and make it look less round. If your face is only slightly round, you can opt for straight-across bangs or a softer, more rounded shape.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing side bangs for your round face:

  • Consider your lifestyle - if you don't have a lot of time to style your hair, opt for a low-maintenance style with longer bangs
  • Choose a style that complements your personal style - if you're more of a bohemian type, opt for wispy, boho-inspired bangs
  • Consult with a hairstylist before making the cut - they can help you choose the right style for your face shape and hair type

FAQ

Will side bangs make my face look even rounder?

If you choose the right style of side bangs, they can actually help elongate your face and make it look less round. Just make sure to choose longer bangs that hit just below your cheekbones if your face is very round.

Can I still wear a ponytail with side bangs?

Absolutely! Side bangs look great with ponytails, just make sure to leave them out of the ponytail so they can frame your face.

How often will I need to trim my side bangs?

Side bangs do require more upkeep than other hairstyles, as they tend to grow out more quickly. You'll need to trim them every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

Hair Care

Once you've chosen the right style of side bangs for your round face, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to avoid greasy bangs
  • Blow-dry your bangs with a round brush to create volume and shape
  • Use a small amount of styling product to keep your bangs in place
  • Avoid touching your bangs throughout the day to prevent them from getting greasy

Now that you know everything there is to know about whether side bangs suit round faces, you're ready to make the cut! Just remember to choose the right style for your face shape, consult with a hairstylist, and take care of your bangs to keep them looking great.
