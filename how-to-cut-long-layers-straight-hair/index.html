How To Cut Long Layers Straight Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Sep 12, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking to add some movement and volume to your straight hair, long layers might just be the way to go. But before you schedule an appointment with your hairstylist, why not try cutting your own long layers at home? Not only will it save you money, but you'll also have full control over the final result. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps on how to cut long layers straight hair, along with some tips and FAQs.

How to Cut Long Layers Straight Hair

Cutting long layers in straight hair might seem intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, it's actually quite simple. Here are the basic steps:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

To cut long layers straight hair at home, you'll need a few basic tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Water spray bottle

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can cause split ends and damage your hair. You can use regular scissors or hair-cutting shears, whichever you feel most comfortable with.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. This will help you see the natural fall and movement of your hair, making it easier to cut the layers evenly.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is crucial to ensure even layers. Start by parting your hair down the middle, then divide it into two even sections. Clip or tie one section away, and work on the other section first.

Step 4: Determine the Length of Your Layers

Decide how long you want your layers to be. For longer layers, start cutting from the middle of your hair length. For shorter layers, start closer to the ends. Remember, it's always better to start with longer layers and work your way up gradually.

Step 5: Begin Cutting Your Layers

Take a small section of hair from the middle of your hair length, and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers, and cut it at a slight angle, starting from the length you want your layers to end. Repeat this process with small sections of hair, working your way around your head. Make sure to cut each section evenly, and avoid cutting too much at once.

Step 6: Move to the Next Section

Once you're done cutting the first section, unclip or untie the second section of hair, and repeat the same steps. Make sure to match the length and angle of the layers with the first section.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

After you've cut all the layers, check for evenness by running your fingers through your hair. If you notice any uneven sections, trim them with your scissors. Don't worry about making it perfect, as a slightly imperfect cut can add texture and movement to your hair.

Tips for Cutting Long Layers Straight Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting long layers straight hair:

  • Start with longer layers and work your way up gradually.
  • Don't cut too much at once, as it can lead to uneven layers.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and damage.
  • Section your hair evenly to ensure even layers.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer and heavier.
  • Don't worry about making it perfect, as a slightly imperfect cut can add texture and movement to your hair.

FAQs

Can I cut long layers straight hair with regular scissors?

Yes, you can use regular scissors to cut long layers straight hair, but make sure they're sharp to avoid split ends and damage.

How often should I cut my long layers straight hair?

It's recommended to cut your long layers straight hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Should I cut my hair when it's wet or dry?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer and heavier, resulting in uneven layers.

Hair Care After Cutting Long Layers Straight Hair

After cutting your long layers straight hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and shine. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.
