How To Keep Hair From Blowing In Wind Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Oct 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Do you ever wonder how to keep your hair from blowing in the wind? It can be frustrating to have your hair constantly in your face, especially when you're trying to enjoy the outdoors or take a nice photo. Fortunately, there are several ways to keep your hair in place, even on windy days. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks to help you keep your hair from blowing in the wind.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Keep Hair from Blowing in Wind
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Keeping Hair in Place
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Windy Days

How to Keep Hair from Blowing in Wind

Here are some ways to keep your hair from blowing in the wind:

1. Wear a Hat or Headband

One of the easiest ways to keep your hair in place is by wearing a hat or headband. Choose a style that fits snugly on your head to prevent it from flying off in the wind. You can also try a headband to keep your hair out of your face.

2. Tie Your Hair Back

Another way to keep your hair from blowing in the wind is by tying it back. Try a ponytail, bun, or braid to keep your hair secure. Use a hair tie that won't slip out easily, such as a scrunchie or elastic band.

3. Use Hair Clips or Bobby Pins

If you have shorter hair, you can use hair clips or bobby pins to keep it in place. Pin your hair back on both sides of your head, or use a few clips to secure it in a particular style.

4. Apply Hair Spray or Gel

Using hair spray or gel can help keep your hair in place. Apply a small amount to your hair before heading outside to help hold it in place. Be sure to choose a product that won't make your hair look greasy or weigh it down.

5. Use a Scarf or Bandana

A scarf or bandana can be a stylish way to keep your hair from blowing in the wind. Tie it around your head, making sure it's snug enough to keep your hair in place. You can also use a scarf to tie your hair back in a ponytail or bun.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to keeping your hair from blowing in the wind:

Step 1: Choose a Style

Decide on a hairstyle that will work well in windy conditions. Consider tying your hair back or wearing a hat or headband.

Step 2: Apply Products

If you're using hair spray or gel, apply it to your hair before heading outside. Be sure to use a small amount to avoid weighing your hair down.

Step 3: Secure Your Hair

Once you're outside, secure your hair in place using a hat, headband, scarf, or hair clips. Make sure it's snug enough to keep your hair from flying around.

Tips for Keeping Hair in Place

Here are some additional tips for keeping your hair in place on windy days:

1. Choose the Right Products

Use hair products that are designed to hold your hair in place without weighing it down. Look for products that are labeled "firm hold" or "extra hold."

2. Don't Overdo It

Using too much product can make your hair look greasy and weigh it down. Use a small amount of product and add more as needed.

3. Avoid Loose Styles

Loose hairstyles, such as loose braids or messy buns, are more likely to come undone in the wind. Choose styles that are tighter and more secure.

4. Choose the Right Accessories

Make sure your hat, headband, or scarf fits snugly on your head to prevent it from flying off in the wind. Choose accessories that are made from lightweight materials that won't weigh your hair down.

FAQs

Q: Can I use hairspray to keep my hair from blowing in the wind?

A: Yes, hairspray can be a useful tool for keeping your hair in place on windy days. Be sure to choose a product that won't weigh your hair down or make it look greasy.

Q: What hairstyle works best for windy conditions?

A: Tying your hair back in a ponytail, bun, or braid is often the best way to keep it in place in windy conditions. You can also try wearing a hat, headband, or scarf.

Q: Can I wear a wig on windy days?

A: Yes, you can wear a wig on windy days, but you'll want to make sure it's secured properly. Use hair clips or bobby pins to keep the wig in place, and consider wearing a hat or scarf over it to help hold it down.

Hair Care for Windy Days

Windy conditions can be tough on your hair, so it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for hair care on windy days:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Wind can dry out your hair, so it's important to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that contain natural oils or other moisturizing ingredients.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair, making it more prone to breakage and frizz. Avoid using hot tools on windy days, and let your hair air dry instead.

3. Brush Your Hair Gently

Brushing your hair too aggressively can cause it to break and become tangled. Use a wide-tooth comb or brush and work through tangles gently.

4. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Windy days often come with plenty of sunshine, which can also damage your hair. Wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the sun's rays.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your hair from blowing in the wind and enjoy your time outdoors without any frustration.
