How To Cut Side Swept Bangs Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 19, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcome to our blog post on how to cut side swept bangs yourself! If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle or just want to save some money on a salon visit, cutting your own bangs can be a great option. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting your own side swept bangs, step-by-step. We'll also provide some tips and FAQ to help you achieve the perfect look.

  • How to Cut Side Swept Bangs Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Side Swept Bangs

How to Cut Side Swept Bangs Yourself

Cutting side swept bangs yourself can seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and technique, it can be a breeze. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Water spray bottle

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning off the hair you want to cut. For side swept bangs, this typically means sectioning off a triangular piece of hair at the front of your head. Use a comb to create a triangle shape and clip the rest of your hair away.

Step 2: Wet Your Hair

Spray the hair you'll be cutting with a water bottle. This will make it easier to cut and ensure a more even result.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair

Using your sharp scissors, start cutting your hair at the desired length. It's always better to start longer and cut more off gradually, so be conservative at first. Cut at a slight angle, following the line of your eyebrows. Keep checking in the mirror to make sure you're happy with the length and shape.

Step 4: Blend Your Bangs

Once you've cut the length of your bangs, you'll want to blend them so they look more natural. Take small sections of hair and cut into them at a slight angle, so they blend in with the rest of your hair. Be careful not to cut too much off, as this can create a choppy look.

Step 5: Style Your Bangs

Now that you've cut your bangs, it's time to style them. Use a hairdryer and round brush to blow dry them, sweeping them to the side. You can also use a straightener or curling iron to add some texture.

Tips for Cutting Side Swept Bangs

  • Always cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink and give you an uneven result.
  • Start with small cuts and work your way up.
  • Don't cut straight across, as this can create a harsh look.
  • Use hair clips to keep the hair you're not cutting out of the way.
  • Don't be afraid to go to a professional if you're unsure or unhappy with the result.

FAQ

Can I cut my own bangs?

Yes, you can! Cutting your own bangs can save you money and give you more control over your hairstyle.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic! Hair grows back, and you can always go to a professional to fix it.

How often should I cut my bangs?

This depends on how fast your hair grows, but generally every 4-6 weeks is a good rule of thumb.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you've cut your side swept bangs, it's important to take care of them. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy and looking great:

  • Use a heat protectant spray when styling your hair with hot tools.
  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.

Cutting your own side swept bangs can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy look right at home. We hope this guide has been helpful, and don't forget to share your new hairstyle with us!
