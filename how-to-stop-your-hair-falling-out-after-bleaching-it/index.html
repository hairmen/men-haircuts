How To Stop Your Hair Falling Out After Bleaching It

Written by Bardi Jun 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've recently bleached your hair, you may have noticed that it's starting to fall out. This is a common problem, but there are steps you can take to stop your hair from falling out. In this blog post, we'll go over how to stop your hair falling out after bleaching it, including tips and tricks for preventing hair damage.

Description

Bleaching your hair is a fun way to switch up your look, but it can also be damaging to your hair. Bleaching strips your hair of its natural oils and proteins, leaving it dry and brittle. This can lead to hair breakage and hair loss. If you're experiencing hair loss after bleaching your hair, don't worry. There are things you can do to stop your hair from falling out and promote healthy hair growth.

How to

1. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

After bleaching your hair, it's important to use a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture to your hair. Look for a treatment that contains ingredients such as keratin and amino acids, which can help rebuild damaged hair.

2. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools such as curling irons and straighteners can further damage your already fragile hair. Try to avoid using them as much as possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

3. Don't Wash Your Hair Every Day

Washing your hair every day can strip it of its natural oils, making it more susceptible to damage. Try to wash your hair every other day or every few days, and use a gentle shampoo that won't further dry out your hair.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help prevent split ends, which can lead to hair breakage. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks, or as needed.

5. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can promote healthy hair growth. Make sure you're eating a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals.

Step by Step

Here are the steps you can take to stop your hair falling out after bleaching it:

  1. Use a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture to your hair.
  2. Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible.
  3. Don't wash your hair every day.
  4. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  5. Eat a healthy diet that promotes healthy hair growth.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for preventing hair damage:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause further damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes and perms.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.

FAQ

Q: Can hair loss from bleaching be reversed?

A: Yes, hair loss from bleaching can be reversed with proper hair care. Follow the steps outlined in this blog post to promote healthy hair growth.

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after falling out from bleaching?

A: It can take several months for hair to grow back after falling out from bleaching. Be patient and continue to take care of your hair to promote healthy hair growth.

Q: Can I still bleach my hair if I've experienced hair loss from bleaching in the past?

A: It's best to avoid bleaching your hair if you've experienced hair loss from bleaching in the past. Bleaching can further damage your already fragile hair.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important for preventing hair damage and promoting healthy hair growth. Follow the steps outlined in this blog post to stop your hair falling out after bleaching it, and remember to be gentle with your hair.
