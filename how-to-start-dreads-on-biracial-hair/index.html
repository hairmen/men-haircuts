How To Start Dreads On Biracial Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 03, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're a biracial individual looking to start dreadlocks, it can be challenging to find the right information and resources. With so many different hair textures and types, it's important to know how to start dreads on biracial hair specifically. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to starting your very own dreads, as well as provide tips for hair care and maintenance.

Table of Contents

  • How to Start Dreads on Biracial Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Hair Care and Maintenance

How to Start Dreads on Biracial Hair

Before starting your dreads, there are a few things you should consider. First and foremost, it's important to have healthy hair. This means keeping your hair clean, moisturized, and free from damage. Secondly, it's important to choose a method that works best for your hair type.

There are several methods for starting dreads, including the twist and rip method, the backcombing method, and the freeform method. Each method has its own benefits and drawbacks, so it's important to do your research and choose the one that works best for you.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to start your own dreads:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Choose a method for starting your dreads.
  3. Section your hair into small, even sections.
  4. Apply a locking gel or wax to each section.
  5. Twist each section tightly, using a comb or your fingers.
  6. Secure the end of each section with a rubber band or hair tie.
  7. Repeat this process for each section of hair.
  8. Leave the rubber bands or hair ties in place for at least a week.
  9. After a week, remove the rubber bands or hair ties and continue to twist your hair as needed.
  10. Be patient! It can take several months to fully lock your hair.

Tips for Hair Care and Maintenance

Once you've started your dreads, it's important to take good care of them to keep them healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Keep your hair clean and moisturized. Use a natural shampoo and conditioner, and moisturize your hair regularly with oil.
  • Avoid using products with harsh chemicals or alcohol, as these can dry out your hair and cause damage.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and slow down the locking process.
  • Be gentle when washing and styling your hair, as rough handling can cause damage and breakage.
  • Consider using a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz.

FAQs

Here are some commonly asked questions about starting dreads on biracial hair:

Q: Can I start dreads on relaxed hair?

A: Yes, you can start dreads on relaxed hair. However, it's important to make sure your hair is healthy and strong before starting the process.

Q: How often should I wash my dreads?

A: You should wash your dreads every 1-2 weeks, depending on your hair type and lifestyle. Use a natural shampoo and make sure to thoroughly rinse out all product.

Q: Can I color my hair with dreads?

A: Yes, you can color your hair with dreads. However, it's best to use a natural, gentle dye and to avoid excessive heat or chemicals.

Starting dreads on biracial hair can be a fun and rewarding process, but it's important to take the time to do it right. With the right care and maintenance, your dreads can look great for years to come.
