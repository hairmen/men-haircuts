What Can I Use To Make My Dreads Thicker

Description

Dreadlocks, also known as dreads, are a popular hairstyle that involves hair being twisted and matted into rope-like strands. While some people have naturally thick dreads, others may find that their dreads are thinner than they would like. If you're wondering what you can use to make your dreads thicker, there are a few options available. In this blog post, we'll explore some of the most effective ways to thicken your dreads.

How to Make Your Dreads Thicker

There are several methods you can use to make your dreads thicker:

  • Interlocking
  • Backcombing
  • Crocheting
  • Using dread wax or a locking accelerator

Each method has its own pros and cons, and some may work better for different hair types. It's important to experiment and find the method that works best for you.

Interlocking

Interlocking involves using a special tool to weave the hair together, creating a tighter and thicker dread. This method is especially effective for those with fine or straight hair. However, interlocking can be time-consuming and may cause some discomfort.

Backcombing

Backcombing is a popular method for creating thick dreads. It involves teasing the hair backwards, creating a tangled mass that can be locked in place with wax or a locking accelerator. While backcombing can create thick, voluminous dreads, it can also cause damage to the hair and scalp if not done correctly.

Crocheting

Crocheting involves using a small hook to pull the hair through itself, creating a tighter and thicker dread. This method is less damaging than backcombing, but may take longer to achieve the desired thickness.

Dread Wax or Locking Accelerator

Dread wax or a locking accelerator can be used in conjunction with any of the above methods to help lock the hair in place and create thicker, more voluminous dreads. However, it's important to use these products sparingly, as they can cause buildup and prevent the hair from locking properly.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to making your dreads thicker:

  1. Choose the method that works best for your hair type and texture.
  2. Prepare your hair by washing and conditioning it thoroughly.
  3. Section your hair into even, manageable sections.
  4. Begin the chosen method, being careful not to pull too tightly or cause discomfort.
  5. Use dread wax or a locking accelerator sparingly, if desired.
  6. Allow the dreads to dry completely.
  7. Retwist or maintain your dreads as needed.

Tips for Thicker Dreads

Here are some additional tips for making your dreads thicker:

  • Avoid using too much product, as this can cause buildup and prevent the hair from locking properly.
  • Be patient; thickening your dreads can take time and may require multiple sessions.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent breakage and preserve your dreads while you sleep.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can cause damage to the hair and scalp.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about making your dreads thicker:

Q: Can I make my dreads thicker without using any products?

A: Yes, you can use any of the above methods without using dread wax or a locking accelerator. However, these products can help to speed up the process and create a more polished look.

Q: How often should I maintain my dreads?

A: It's important to maintain your dreads regularly, at least once every 4-6 weeks. This can help to prevent buildup and keep your dreads looking neat and tidy.

Hair Care for Thicker Dreads

Once you've achieved the desired thickness for your dreads, it's important to take good care of them to ensure they stay healthy and strong. Here are some tips for maintaining your thick dreads:

  • Wash your dreads regularly, using a residue-free shampoo and warm water.
  • Avoid using heavy conditioners or oils, as these can cause buildup and prevent the hair from locking properly.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a month to keep your dreads moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes, as these can weaken the hair and cause breakage.

With these tips and methods, you can achieve the thick, voluminous dreads you've always wanted. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair, and your dreads will continue to look great for years to come.
