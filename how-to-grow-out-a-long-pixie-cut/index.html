How To Grow Out A Long Pixie Cut

Written by Petter Nov 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your short pixie cut and ready to grow it out? Growing out a long pixie cut can be a challenging process, but with the right care and patience, you can achieve your desired length. In this article, we'll give you tips and tricks to help you grow out your long pixie cut while maintaining healthy hair.

How to Grow Out a Long Pixie Cut

Growing out a long pixie cut requires patience and perseverance. The process can take anywhere from six months to a year, depending on how fast your hair grows. Here are some steps you can take to help you grow out your long pixie cut:

Step 1: Get Regular Trims

Even though you're growing out your hair, getting regular trims is essential for maintaining healthy hair. Trimming your hair every six to eight weeks will help prevent split ends and breakage.

Step 2: Experiment with Different Styles

As your hair grows, experiment with different styles to find what works best for you. Try adding layers or bangs to change up your look and make the growing-out process more manageable.

Step 3: Be Patient

Patience is key when growing out a long pixie cut. Remember that hair grows at a rate of about half an inch per month, so it may take several months before you notice a significant difference in length.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you grow out your long pixie cut:

Step 1: Set Realistic Goals

Before you start growing out your hair, set realistic goals for yourself. Decide on a length that you want to achieve and keep that in mind throughout the process.

Step 2: Choose the Right Products

Choosing the right products is essential for maintaining healthy hair while you grow it out. Look for products that are specifically designed for your hair type and avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.

Step 3: Get Regular Trims

As mentioned earlier, regular trims are crucial for maintaining healthy hair. Schedule an appointment with your stylist every six to eight weeks to get rid of split ends and prevent breakage.

Step 4: Experiment with Different Styles

As your hair grows, experiment with different styles to find what works best for you. Try adding layers, bangs, or even braids to change up your look and make the growing-out process more manageable.

Step 5: Be Patient

Remember that growing out a long pixie cut takes time and patience. Be kind to your hair and give it time to grow. Before you know it, you'll have the length you desire.

Tips for Growing Out a Long Pixie Cut

Here are some additional tips to help you grow out your long pixie cut:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth.
  • Consider taking hair growth supplements.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent breakage.

FAQs

Q: How long does it take to grow out a long pixie cut?

A: It can take anywhere from six months to a year, depending on how fast your hair grows.

Q: Can I still style my hair while growing it out?

A: Yes, you can still style your hair while growing it out. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

Q: Is it okay to use hair extensions while growing out a long pixie cut?

A: Yes, you can use hair extensions to help you achieve the length you desire. Just make sure to choose high-quality extensions that won't damage your hair.

Hair Care for Growing Out a Long Pixie Cut

Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind while growing out your long pixie cut:

  • Wash your hair every two to three days to prevent it from drying out.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair as much as possible.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when you do use hot tools.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause breakage.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can successfully grow out your long pixie cut while maintaining healthy hair. Remember to be patient and enjoy the journey!
