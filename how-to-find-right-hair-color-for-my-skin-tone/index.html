How To Find The Right Hair Color For Your Skin Tone

Written by Luffy Sep 30, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of your current hair color and looking to switch things up? Finding the right hair color for your skin tone can be a daunting task, but with the right tips and tricks, it can be a breeze. In this article, we’ll dive into how to find the perfect hair color for your skin tone and answer some frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine Your Skin Tone
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Finding the Right Hair Color
  • Tips for Choosing the Right Hair Color
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Colored Hair

How to Determine Your Skin Tone

The first step in finding the right hair color for your skin tone is determining what your skin tone actually is. There are three main categories: warm, cool, and neutral.

One easy way to determine your skin tone is to look at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If they appear blue, you likely have a cool undertone. If they appear green, you likely have a warm undertone. If you can’t tell if they’re blue or green, you may have a neutral undertone.

Warm Undertones

Those with warm undertones have skin that has a yellow or golden hue. Some people with warm undertones may also have peachy or apricot tones. If you have warm undertones, you’ll want to stick to hair colors with a warm base, such as golden blondes, caramel browns, and copper reds.

Cool Undertones

Cool undertones have skin that has a pink or blue hue. Those with cool undertones should stick to hair colors with a cool base, such as ashy blondes, cool browns, and burgundy reds.

Neutral Undertones

If you have a neutral undertone, you can pretty much pull off any hair color. Lucky you!

Step-by-Step Guide to Finding the Right Hair Color

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone

Refer to the previous section to determine your skin tone.

Step 2: Consider Your Natural Hair Color

Your natural hair color can also play a role in determining what hair color will look best on you. If you have dark hair, going too light can wash you out. If you have light hair, going too dark can be too harsh. Consider what colors will complement your natural hair color.

Step 3: Take Inspiration from Celebrities

Looking at celebrities with a similar skin tone to yours can give you an idea of what hair colors will look best on you. Take note of what colors they’ve tried and what looks good on them.

Step 4: Consult with a Professional

If you’re still unsure, consult with a professional hairstylist. They can give you recommendations based on your skin tone, natural hair color, and personal preferences.

Tips for Choosing the Right Hair Color

Tip 1: Don’t Go Too Far from Your Natural Color

If you’re not looking for a drastic change, stick to a hair color that’s within a few shades of your natural color. This will ensure that the color looks natural and complements your skin tone.

Tip 2: Consider Your Eye Color

Your eye color can also play a role in what hair color will look best on you. For example, those with blue eyes may want to stick to cooler hair colors, while those with brown eyes may want to try warmer hair colors.

Tip 3: Take Maintenance into Consideration

Some hair colors require more maintenance than others. If you’re not willing to spend a lot of time and money on upkeep, consider a lower maintenance hair color.

FAQs

What if I have a lot of redness in my skin?

If you have a lot of redness in your skin, you may want to stick to cooler hair colors to help balance out the redness.

Can I still dye my hair if I have sensitive skin?

Yes, you can still dye your hair if you have sensitive skin. Look for hair dyes that are labeled as gentle or formulated for sensitive skin.

What if I want to try a hair color that’s drastically different from my natural color?

If you want to try a hair color that’s drastically different from your natural color, be prepared for a bit of a shock. It may take some getting used to, but as long as you’ve chosen a color that complements your skin tone, you should be good to go!

Hair Care Tips for Colored Hair

Tip 1: Use Color-Safe Shampoo and Conditioner

To keep your new hair color looking fresh, use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner. These products are formulated to help prevent fading.

Tip 2: Avoid Hot Tools

Hot tools can cause damage to colored hair. If you must use hot tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray.

Tip 3: Get Regular Trims

Regular trims will help keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks.

By following these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to find the perfect hair color for your skin tone. Remember, it’s all about finding a color that complements your natural beauty and makes you feel confident.
