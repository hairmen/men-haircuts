How To Keep Straightened Hair From Getting Frizzy In Humidity

Written by Luffy Jul 14, 2023 · 4 min read

Humidity can be a nightmare for people with straightened hair. It can cause your hair to frizz up and ruin your perfectly styled hairdo. But don't worry, we have some tips and tricks that will help you keep your hair straight and frizz-free even in the most humid conditions.

Description

Humidity is the amount of moisture present in the air. When there is a high level of humidity, it can cause your hair to absorb moisture and become frizzy. This is especially true if you have naturally curly or wavy hair that has been straightened. The heat from the straightening process can cause your hair to become porous, which makes it more prone to frizzing.

How to Keep Straightened Hair from Getting Frizzy in Humidity

Step by Step

Step 1: Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Start by using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner that will help keep your hair hydrated. This will reduce the chances of your hair absorbing moisture from the air and becoming frizzy. Look for products that contain ingredients like glycerin or panthenol, as they help to retain moisture in the hair.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing and conditioning. This will help to lock in moisture and prevent your hair from becoming frizzy. Make sure to apply it evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 3: Use a Heat Protectant

If you're going to be using heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray or cream. This will help to protect your hair from the heat and prevent it from becoming damaged and frizzy.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush. This will help to smooth out your hair and prevent it from becoming frizzy. Make sure to dry your hair completely before moving on to the next step.

Step 5: Use a Flat Iron

Use a flat iron to straighten your hair. Make sure to use a high-quality flat iron that has ceramic plates, as this will help to distribute heat evenly and prevent hot spots. Work in small sections, and make sure to run the flat iron over each section several times to ensure that it's straightened properly.

Step 6: Apply a Hair Serum

Apply a hair serum to your hair after straightening. This will help to add shine and prevent frizz. Look for serums that contain ingredients like argan oil or jojoba oil, as they help to nourish the hair and prevent frizz.

Tips

- Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause it to become frizzy.

- Don't use too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

- Use a silk or satin pillowcase, as this will help to reduce friction and prevent your hair from becoming frizzy overnight.

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how oily your scalp is. If you have dry hair, you can wash it once or twice a week. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it every other day.

Q: Can I straighten my hair without using a flat iron?

A: Yes, you can use a hair straightening brush or a blow dryer with a comb attachment to straighten your hair.

Hair Care

It's important to take good care of your hair to prevent it from becoming frizzy. This includes washing and conditioning it regularly, using heat protectant products when styling, and avoiding harsh chemicals and treatments that can damage your hair. By following these tips, you can keep your straightened hair looking smooth and frizz-free even in humid conditions.
