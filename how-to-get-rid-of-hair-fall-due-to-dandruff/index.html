How To Get Rid Of Hair Fall Due To Dandruff A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Dandruff is a common scalp condition that affects millions of people around the world. It's characterized by the presence of white or yellow flakes on the scalp, which can be itchy, flaky, and embarrassing. While dandruff itself is not harmful, it can lead to hair fall if left untreated. In this article, we'll discuss how to get rid of hair fall due to dandruff, including tips, tricks, and home remedies.

Description

Dandruff is a skin condition that affects the scalp, causing it to become dry, itchy, and flaky. The flakes that are visible on the scalp are actually dead skin cells that have shed from the scalp. While dandruff is not harmful, it can lead to hair fall if left untreated. The itching and scratching caused by dandruff can damage hair follicles, leading to hair fall. In addition, the flakes on the scalp can clog hair follicles, preventing new hair growth.

How to Get Rid of Hair Fall Due to Dandruff

The key to getting rid of hair fall due to dandruff is to treat the underlying scalp condition. Here are some tips to help you get started:

1. Use a Dandruff Shampoo

The first step in treating dandruff is to use a medicated shampoo that contains ingredients like ketoconazole, selenium sulfide, or zinc pyrithione. These ingredients help to reduce inflammation and control the growth of yeast on the scalp, which can cause dandruff. Use the shampoo according to the instructions on the bottle, and be sure to rinse your hair thoroughly.

2. Avoid Using Harsh Chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, as they can further irritate the scalp and contribute to hair fall. This includes hair dyes, perms, and chemical straighteners. Instead, opt for natural hair care products that are gentle on your scalp.

3. Keep Your Scalp Clean

Keeping your scalp clean is essential for preventing dandruff and hair fall. Wash your hair regularly, and be sure to rinse your hair thoroughly to remove any shampoo or conditioner residue. Avoid using hot water, as it can dry out your scalp and make dandruff worse.

4. Manage Stress

Stress can contribute to dandruff and hair fall, so it's important to find ways to manage stress in your life. This could include meditation, yoga, or other relaxation techniques.

5. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy, balanced diet is essential for maintaining healthy hair and scalp. Be sure to include plenty of fruits, vegetables, and whole grains in your diet, as well as lean protein sources like fish and chicken. Avoid processed foods and sugary snacks, as they can contribute to inflammation and dandruff.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you get rid of hair fall due to dandruff:

Step 1: Use a Dandruff Shampoo

Start by using a medicated shampoo that contains ingredients like ketoconazole, selenium sulfide, or zinc pyrithione. Use the shampoo according to the instructions on the bottle, and be sure to rinse your hair thoroughly.

Step 2: Massage Your Scalp

After shampooing, massage your scalp gently with your fingertips. This will help to improve blood circulation to the scalp and promote healthy hair growth.

Step 3: Rinse Your Hair Thoroughly

Be sure to rinse your hair thoroughly to remove any shampoo or conditioner residue. Use cool water to rinse your hair, as hot water can dry out your scalp and make dandruff worse.

Step 4: Condition Your Hair

Use a conditioner to moisturize your hair and scalp. Be sure to choose a conditioner that is formulated for dandruff-prone hair, and apply it from the mid-lengths to the ends of your hair.

Step 5: Rinse Your Hair Again

Rinse your hair thoroughly to remove any conditioner residue. Use cool water, and be sure to rinse your hair well.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you get rid of hair fall due to dandruff:

  • Avoid scratching your scalp, as this can damage hair follicles and lead to hair fall.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools like straighteners and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and make dandruff worse.
  • If you have severe dandruff, see a dermatologist for a prescription-strength treatment.
  • Avoid wearing hats or other head coverings for long periods of time, as this can contribute to dandruff and hair fall.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and use a wide-tooth comb to avoid pulling on your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can dandruff cause hair fall?

A: Yes, dandruff can lead to hair fall if left untreated. The itching and scratching caused by dandruff can damage hair follicles, leading to hair fall. In addition, the flakes on the scalp can clog hair follicles, preventing new hair growth.

Q: What is the best shampoo for dandruff?

A: The best shampoo for dandruff is one that contains ingredients like ketoconazole, selenium sulfide, or zinc pyrithione. These ingredients help to reduce inflammation and control the growth of yeast on the scalp, which can cause dandruff.

Q: How often should I wash my hair if I have dandruff?

A: You should wash your hair at least twice a week if you have dandruff. Use a medicated shampoo that is formulated for dandruff-prone hair, and be sure to rinse your hair thoroughly to remove any shampoo or conditioner residue.

Hair Care for Dandruff

For optimal hair care when dealing with dandruff, it's important to use gentle hair care products that are formulated for dandruff-prone hair. Be sure to wash your hair regularly, and rinse your hair thoroughly to remove any shampoo or conditioner residue. In addition, avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, and be gentle when brushing or combing your hair. If you have severe dandruff, see a dermatologist for a prescription-strength treatment.
