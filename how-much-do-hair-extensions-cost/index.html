Types of Hair Extensions

There are many different types of hair extensions, each with their own pros and cons. Here are the most popular types:

Clip-In Hair Extensions

Clip-in hair extensions are a temporary option that you can easily take in and out. They come in a variety of widths and lengths, and can be made from synthetic or human hair.

Tape-In Hair Extensions

Tape-in hair extensions are a semi-permanent option that last for 6-8 weeks. They are applied using a special tape that is placed on the roots of your hair.

Sew-In Hair Extensions

Sew-in hair extensions are a popular option for those with thicker hair. They are applied by braiding your natural hair and then sewing the extensions onto the braids.

Micro-Bead Hair Extensions

Micro-bead hair extensions are a semi-permanent option that last for 3-4 months. They are applied using small beads that are clamped onto your natural hair.

Cost of Hair Extensions

The cost of hair extensions varies depending on the type of extension and the quality of the hair. Here is a breakdown of the average cost:

Clip-In Hair Extensions

Clip-in hair extensions can range from $50 to $200, depending on the quality of the hair.

Tape-In Hair Extensions

Tape-in hair extensions can cost between $200 and $600, depending on the length and quality of the hair.

Sew-In Hair Extensions

Sew-in hair extensions can cost between $300 and $1,500, depending on the length and quality of the hair. The cost may also include the price of the braiding and sewing services.

Micro-Bead Hair Extensions

Micro-bead hair extensions can cost between $500 and $1,500, depending on the length and quality of the hair. The cost may also include the price of the clamping services.

Hair Extension Care Tips

Proper hair extension care is crucial to keeping your extensions looking good for as long as possible. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner when washing your hair
  • Avoid using hot tools on your extensions
  • Brush your extensions gently and avoid tugging on them
  • Store your extensions in a cool, dry place when not in use
