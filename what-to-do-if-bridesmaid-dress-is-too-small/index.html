What To Do If Bridesmaid Dress Is Too Small

Written by Aprilia Oct 07, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Being a bridesmaid is an honor and a responsibility that comes with a lot of planning and preparation. One of the most important things to consider is the bridesmaid dress. However, what if you receive your dress and it's too small? Don't panic, there are steps you can take to fix this issue. In this article, we'll guide you through how to handle the situation and ensure that you feel comfortable and confident on the big day.

Description

It's not uncommon to receive a bridesmaid dress that doesn't quite fit. This can be due to a variety of reasons, such as inaccurate measurements or weight gain/loss. Whatever the reason, it's important to address the issue as soon as possible to ensure that you have enough time to make adjustments or find a replacement dress.

How to

Step 1: Assess the Situation

The first step is to try on the dress and see how it fits. Don't force yourself into the dress if it's too tight or uncomfortable. This can cause damage to the dress or even injury to yourself. Take note of where the dress is tight and where it fits well. This will help you determine what needs to be altered.

Step 2: Contact the Bridal Party

Let the bride or the bridal party know that the dress is too small. They may be able to help you find a solution or provide guidance on what to do next. It's important to keep them informed so that they can make any necessary arrangements.

Step 3: Find a Seamstress

If the dress is only slightly too small, you may be able to have it altered by a seamstress. Look for a reputable seamstress in your area and schedule an appointment as soon as possible. Be sure to bring the dress and any necessary accessories with you to the appointment.

Step 4: Consider a Replacement Dress

If the dress is too small to be altered or you don't have enough time for alterations, consider finding a replacement dress. Check with the bridal party to see if there are any extra dresses available or if they have any recommendations for where to find a replacement. Keep in mind the color and style of the original dress to ensure that it matches the rest of the bridal party.

Tips

  • Order your dress early to allow time for alterations or replacements
  • Be honest about your measurements when ordering the dress
  • Try on the dress as soon as you receive it to ensure that it fits
  • Consider buying a larger size and having it altered to fit

FAQ

What if I can't find a replacement dress?

If you can't find a replacement dress, consider renting a dress or borrowing one from a friend. You can also try searching online for a similar dress in your size.

What if the dress is too big?

If the dress is too big, you can have it altered by a seamstress. Be sure to try on the dress with your shoes and any necessary accessories to ensure that the alterations are accurate.

Hair Care

On the big day, it's important to have your hair looking its best. Consider getting a professional hair stylist to help you achieve the perfect look. Be sure to book your appointment in advance to ensure availability.

Don't let a too-small bridesmaid dress ruin your day. Follow these steps to find a solution and ensure that you feel comfortable and confident on the big day.
