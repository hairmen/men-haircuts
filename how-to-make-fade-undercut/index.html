How To Make Fade Undercut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a new haircut that is stylish and easy to maintain? Look no further than the fade undercut. This popular hairstyle combines a short, tapered cut on the sides and back with longer hair on top. The result is a modern and edgy look that works for all hair types and face shapes. In this guide, we'll show you how to make fade undercut at home, including tips and hair care advice.

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Perfect Fade Undercut

How to Make Fade Undercut

The first step in making a fade undercut is to gather the necessary tools. You will need:

  • Clippers with guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair product (optional)

Next, decide on the length of the hair on top. The longer the hair, the more dramatic the undercut will be. Once you have determined the length, section off the hair on top and clip it out of the way.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Determine the Length

Use the clippers with a guard to start the undercut. Begin at the bottom of the head and work your way up, using longer guards for the lower section and shorter guards for the upper section. Use the scissors to blend the hair as you go.

Step 2: Define the Line

Once you have reached the desired length, define the line between the short hair and the longer hair on top. Use the clippers without a guard to create a clean line. You can also use scissors to texturize the hair.

Step 3: Cut the Hair on Top

Unclip the hair on top and cut it to the desired length. You can use scissors or clippers with guards, depending on the style you want. Blend the hair with the shorter hair on the sides and back.

Step 4: Style the Hair

Apply hair product to the hair on top and style as desired. You can use a pomade, wax, or gel to achieve the look you want.

Tips for a Perfect Fade Undercut

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to ensure that you do not cut too much hair.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you go.
  • Blend the hair as you go to avoid any harsh lines.
  • Experiment with different hair products to find the one that works best for your hair type.

FAQ

How often do I need to get a fade undercut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain the style. Generally, you should get a touch-up every 4-6 weeks to keep the fade looking sharp.

Can I make a fade undercut with curly hair?

Absolutely! The fade undercut works well with curly hair, as it enhances the natural texture of the hair. Just be sure to find a stylist who is experienced with curly hair and can create a cut that works for you.

What if I mess up the cut?

If you are not confident in your cutting skills, it is best to go to a professional stylist. However, if you do make a mistake, you can always go shorter or try a different style until the hair grows back.

Hair Care for Fade Undercut

To keep your fade undercut looking fresh, it is important to take care of your hair. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair clean and healthy. Avoid using too much heat or styling products, as this can damage the hair. If you have longer hair on top, be sure to trim it regularly to avoid split ends.

With these tips and advice, you can easily make a fade undercut at home and enjoy a stylish and low-maintenance haircut. Have fun experimenting with different lengths and styles, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
