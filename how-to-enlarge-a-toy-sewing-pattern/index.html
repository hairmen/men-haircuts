How To Enlarge A Toy Sewing Pattern

Written by Daniel Aug 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're an avid sewer, you know how important it is to have the right pattern. But what do you do when you find the perfect pattern for a toy, but it's not the right size? Don't fret! Enlarging a toy sewing pattern is easier than you might think. In this article, we'll go over the steps to make your toy pattern bigger and tips to ensure your finished product turns out just how you want it.

How to Enlarge a Toy Sewing Pattern

Before you start, you'll need to gather some supplies. You'll need your toy pattern, tracing paper or a lightbox, a pencil, a ruler, and some extra paper. Once you have everything you need, follow these steps:

Step 1: Measure your original pattern

Measure the dimensions of your original pattern, including the height, width, and any other measurements that are important to the design. Record these measurements, as you'll need them later.

Step 2: Determine the size you want to make the toy

Decide how much bigger you want to make the toy. For instance, if your original pattern is 10 inches tall, and you want to make it 20 inches tall, you'll need to double the height measurement. Keep in mind that the proportion of the toy may change as you enlarge it, so consider if you want to adjust any other measurements as well.

Step 3: Create a grid

Using your ruler and pencil, create a grid on your tracing paper or extra paper. Each square on the grid should be the same size as the unit of measurement you used for your original pattern. For example, if your original pattern is in inches, the squares on your grid should be an inch in size.

Step 4: Trace your pattern onto the grid

Using your original pattern, trace each section onto the corresponding square on the grid. Be sure to mark any important measurements or markings accurately.

Step 5: Extend the lines

Using your ruler, extend the lines of each section to the corresponding squares on the grid. This will make the pattern larger.

Step 6: Connect the lines

Using your ruler, connect the lines to create the new, enlarged pattern.

Step 7: Transfer the pattern to your fabric

Once you have your enlarged pattern, transfer it to your fabric. You can use pins or a fabric marker to trace the pattern onto your material. Don't forget to cut out any necessary pieces and sew them together according to the instructions.

Tips for Enlarging a Toy Sewing Pattern

Here are some additional tips to help ensure your toy sewing pattern turns out perfectly:

  • Use a lightbox to make tracing your original pattern easier.
  • Double-check your measurements before transferring the pattern to your fabric.
  • Consider adjusting any additional measurements to maintain the proportion of the toy.
  • Test your pattern on scrap fabric before using your final fabric.

FAQ

Can I enlarge any sewing pattern?

Yes, you can enlarge any sewing pattern using the steps outlined above. Keep in mind that the proportion of the pattern may change as you enlarge it, so consider adjusting any additional measurements to maintain the design's overall look.

What if my pattern is too big?

If your pattern is too big, you can use the same steps to make it smaller. Instead of extending the lines, you'll need to shorten them to create a smaller pattern.

Conclusion

Enlarging a toy sewing pattern can seem daunting, but it's a manageable task with the right tools and steps. Now that you know how to make your pattern bigger, you can create the perfect toy for your needs. Just remember to double-check your measurements, adjust any additional measurements, and test your pattern on scrap fabric before using your final material. Happy sewing!
