How To Do Updos For Long Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Nov 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Updos are a great way to keep your long hair off your face and neck, and they can be a stylish and elegant addition to any outfit. However, many women are intimidated by the thought of creating an updo themselves. But fear not â with a little practice and patience, anyone can do updos for long hair themselves. In this article, we'll go through the steps to create a beautiful updo, along with some tips and tricks to make the process easier.

Description

Updos are hairstyles that involve pulling the hair back and securing it in place, often with pins, clips, or elastics. They can be simple or elaborate, depending on the occasion and the desired look. Updos are perfect for formal events like weddings or proms, or just for a night out on the town. They can also be a practical choice for hot summer days, when you want to keep your hair off your neck and out of your face.

How to

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start creating your updo, it's important to prep your hair. Wash and dry your hair thoroughly, and apply a styling product like mousse or hairspray to help hold the style. If your hair tends to be frizzy, you may want to use a smoothing serum or oil to tame any flyaways.

Step 2: Choose Your Updo

There are many different types of updos for long hair, from simple buns and ponytails to intricate braids and twists. Choose a style that works for your hair type and the occasion. If you're not sure where to start, try searching online for updo tutorials or inspiration.

Step 3: Gather Your Tools

To create an updo, you'll need a few basic tools, including bobby pins, elastics, and a comb or brush. You may also want to use a curling iron or flat iron to add some texture to your hair.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. Divide your hair into two or three sections, depending on the style you're creating. Use clips or elastics to keep the sections separate.

Step 5: Create Your Updo

Start by creating the base of your updo. This could be a ponytail, braid, or twist, depending on the style you've chosen. Secure the base with an elastic or pins.

Next, start adding sections of hair to the base, using pins or elastics to hold them in place. Work your way around your head, adding more sections of hair as you go.

Step 6: Finish Your Updo

Once you've added all the sections of hair, it's time to finish your updo. Use bobby pins to secure any loose strands or flyaways, and add hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips

  • Practice makes perfect â don't be afraid to try different updo styles and techniques until you find one that works for you.
  • Use high-quality hair products to help hold your updo in place.
  • If you have trouble getting your updo to stay put, try using more pins or elastics.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help â sometimes it's easier to create an updo with someone else's assistance.

FAQ

Q: How long does an updo last?

A: An updo can last anywhere from a few hours to a full day, depending on the style and the quality of the products you use. If you're planning to wear your updo for a long period of time, use extra pins and hairspray to help hold it in place.

Q: Can I create an updo with short hair?

A: While updos are typically associated with long hair, there are many styles that can be adapted for shorter hair. Try a half-updo or a braided crown for a stylish look.

Hair Care

Creating updos can be a great way to give your hair a break from daily styling, but it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims can also help keep your hair in good condition.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to create beautiful updos for long hair yourself. Whether you're getting ready for a special occasion or just want to switch up your everyday look, updos are a versatile and stylish choice that anyone can master.
