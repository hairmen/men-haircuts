How To Wear Thick Headbands With Hair Up A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Thick headbands are a versatile accessory that can add style and personality to any outfit. They are perfect for keeping your hair off your face and adding a pop of color or texture to your look. However, figuring out how to wear thick headbands with hair up can be a bit tricky. In this guide, we’ll provide you with step-by-step instructions and tips on how to wear thick headbands with hair up.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Wearing Thick Headbands with Hair Up
  4. FAQs

How to Wear Thick Headbands with Hair Up

Wearing thick headbands with hair up is a great way to add a touch of style to your hairdo. Here are some simple steps to follow:

Step 1: Choose the Right Thick Headband

The first step is to choose the right thick headband. You want to make sure that the headband is the right size and fits comfortably on your head. It should also complement your hair and outfit.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Before putting on the headband, you need to prep your hair. Make sure your hair is clean and dry. If you have curly hair, you may want to add some leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls. If you have straight hair, you may want to add some texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add some volume.

Step 3: Put on the Headband

Once your hair is prepped, it’s time to put on the headband. Place the headband on your head and position it where you want it. You can wear the headband at the top of your forehead or further back on your head.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Now that the headband is in place, you can style your hair. You can wear your hair up in a bun, ponytail, or braid. You can also leave some hair down and style it in loose waves or curls.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to wear thick headbands with hair up:

Step 1: Choose the Right Thick Headband

Choose a headband that complements your hair and outfit. Make sure it’s the right size and fits comfortably on your head.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair. Apply any hair products you need to achieve your desired style.

Step 3: Place the Headband on Your Head

Place the headband on your head and position it where you want it. You can wear the headband at the top of your forehead or further back on your head.

Step 4: Gather Your Hair

Gather your hair into a ponytail, bun, or braid. You can also leave some hair down and style it in loose waves or curls.

Step 5: Secure Your Hair with Bobby Pins

Use bobby pins to secure your hair in place. Tuck any loose strands of hair under the headband or pin them in place.

Step 6: Adjust the Headband

Adjust the headband as needed. Make sure it’s comfortable and secure.

Tips for Wearing Thick Headbands with Hair Up

Here are some tips to keep in mind when wearing thick headbands with hair up:

  • Choose a headband that complements your hair and outfit
  • Prep your hair with the right products before putting on the headband
  • Adjust the headband as needed to ensure it’s comfortable and secure
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find the one that works best for you

FAQs

What hairstyles work best with thick headbands?

Thick headbands work well with hairstyles that involve pulling your hair back or up, such as ponytails, buns, or braids. You can also leave some hair down and style it in loose waves or curls.

How do I choose the right size headband?

When choosing a headband, measure the circumference of your head and choose a headband that is slightly smaller than that measurement. You want the headband to fit snugly but not be too tight.

Hair Care for Wearing Thick Headbands with Hair Up

Wearing thick headbands with hair up can put stress on your hair, so it’s important to take care of your hair to prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles for extended periods of time
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent hair breakage

By following these tips, you can ensure that your hair stays healthy and strong while wearing thick headbands with hair up.
