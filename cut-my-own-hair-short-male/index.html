Cut My Own Hair Short Male A How To Guide

Written by Petter Sep 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a guy who prefers a neat and tidy look, you might be considering cutting your own hair short. It can save you money and time, and it's a skill that's worth learning. In this article, we'll guide you through the steps of cutting your own hair short, along with some tips and FAQs to help you get the best results.

How to Cut My Own Hair Short Male

Before you start cutting your own hair, it's important to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of haircutting scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle of water handy to keep your hair damp while you cut.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it until it's damp. Comb it in the direction you want it to fall, and then let it air dry or use a hairdryer on low heat.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, part your hair where you want to begin cutting. Clip the rest of your hair out of the way using hair clips.

Step 3: Trim the Sides and Back

Starting at the nape of your neck, use your scissors to trim the hair in a straight line. Work your way up the back of your head, using the comb to hold the hair in place as you cut. Repeat this process on the sides of your head.

Step 4: Blend the Top

Using your comb and scissors, blend the hair on top of your head with the sides and back. Use your fingers to lift the hair up and cut it in a straight line.

Step 5: Clean Up the Neckline and Sideburns

Using your scissors, trim the hair at the back of your neck and around your sideburns. Use the comb to hold the hair in place as you cut.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Short

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when cutting your own hair short:

  • Start with a longer length and work your way shorter as you gain confidence.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from all angles.
  • Take breaks if you need to, and don't rush the process.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Be patient and don't worry if it's not perfect the first time.

FAQs about Cutting Your Own Hair Short

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair short:

Q: Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?
A: No, it's best to use haircutting scissors as they are sharper and designed for cutting hair.

Q: How often should I cut my own hair?
A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you like to keep it. On average, most men cut their hair every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Should I cut my hair wet or dry?
A: It's easier to cut your hair when it's damp as it's more manageable. However, if you prefer to cut it dry, make sure to comb it thoroughly first.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair Short

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, such as hairdryers and straighteners.
  • Apply a hair oil or serum to keep your hair shiny and healthy.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

Learning how to cut your own hair short can be a challenging task, but with practice and patience, you can achieve great results. Follow these steps, tips, and FAQs to get started, and remember to take care of your hair after cutting it.
