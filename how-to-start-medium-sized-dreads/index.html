How to Start Medium Sized Dreads

  • Dreadlock shampoo
  • A fine-toothed comb
  • Locking gel or wax
  • Elastics or hair ties

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to start your medium sized dreads:

  1. Wash your hair with dreadlock shampoo to remove any product buildup and ensure that your hair is clean and free of oils.
  2. Part your hair into sections using a fine-toothed comb. The size of the sections will determine the size of your dreads. For medium sized dreads, aim for sections that are about 1 inch in diameter.
  3. Apply a small amount of locking gel or wax to each section, working it into your hair from the roots to the tips.
  4. Twist each section of hair tightly, using your fingers to create a spiral pattern. Secure the end of each section with an elastic or hair tie.
  5. Repeat this process for all sections of hair, making sure that each dread is uniform in size and shape.
  6. Leave your dreads in for at least 24 hours to allow the gel or wax to set and your hair to begin locking together.
  7. Remove the elastics or hair ties from the ends of your dreads, and use a fine-toothed comb to gently tease out any knots or tangles.
  8. Wash your hair regularly with dreadlock shampoo, and use a residue-free conditioner to keep your dreads soft and manageable.
  9. Avoid using any products that contain alcohol or sulfates, as these can dry out your hair and cause damage to your dreads.
  10. Be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and your dreads will slowly but surely mature over time.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Here are some additional tips to help you maintain your medium sized dreads:

  • Avoid using excessive heat or styling tools on your dreads, as this can cause damage and weaken your hair.
  • Wear a scarf or hat to protect your dreads from harsh weather conditions and UV rays.
  • Use a crochet hook to tighten loose hairs and create a neater appearance.
  • Regularly palm roll your dreads to encourage locking and prevent them from becoming flat or lumpy.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting medium sized dreads:

  • Q: How long does it take for dreads to fully mature?
  • A: It can take anywhere from 6 months to a year or more for your dreads to fully mature, depending on your hair type and how well you care for them.
  • Q: Can I start dreads with short hair?
  • A: Yes, you can start dreads with hair as short as 2-3 inches in length. However, the process may take longer and require more maintenance than starting with longer hair.

Hair Care for Your Dreads

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy and beautiful dreads. Here are some tips for caring for your dreads:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using any products that contain alcohol or sulfates, as these can dry out your hair and cause damage to your dreads.
  • Use a residue-free conditioner to keep your dreads soft and manageable.
  • Avoid using excessive heat or styling tools on your dreads, as this can cause damage and weaken your hair.
  • Wear a scarf or hat to protect your dreads from harsh weather conditions and UV rays.

Starting medium sized dreads is a process that requires patience, dedication, and proper care.
