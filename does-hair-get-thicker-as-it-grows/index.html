Does Hair Get Thicker As It Grows

Written by Frank Nov 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're someone who's always been curious about whether hair gets thicker as it grows, you're not alone. Many people have this question on their minds, and for good reason. After all, having thick, luscious locks is something that many people desire. In this blog post, we'll explore whether hair gets thicker as it grows and what you can do to promote thicker hair growth.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Thicker Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Thicker Hair

How to Get Thicker Hair

The short answer to whether hair gets thicker as it grows is no, it doesn't. Hair thickness is determined by the number of hair follicles on your scalp, which is something that you're born with. However, there are things that you can do to promote thicker hair growth.

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

Your hair needs certain nutrients to grow strong and healthy. Make sure that you're eating a well-balanced diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals. Some of the best foods for hair growth include leafy greens, eggs, nuts, and fish.

2. Take Supplements

In addition to eating a healthy diet, you can also take supplements to promote thicker hair growth. Biotin and vitamin D are two of the most popular supplements for hair growth.

3. Drink Plenty of Water

Drinking plenty of water is important for overall health, but it's also important for hair growth. When you're dehydrated, your hair can become dry and brittle, which can lead to breakage. Make sure that you're drinking at least eight glasses of water per day.

Step by Step Guide

While you can't make your hair thicker as it grows, you can take steps to promote thicker hair growth. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Eat a Healthy Diet

Make sure that you're eating a well-balanced diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals.

Step 2: Take Supplements

Consider taking supplements like biotin and vitamin D to promote hair growth.

Step 3: Drink Plenty of Water

Make sure that you're drinking at least eight glasses of water per day to keep your hair hydrated.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and lead to breakage. Try to avoid heat styling as much as possible.

Step 5: Get Regular Trims

While getting regular trims won't actually make your hair thicker, it will help to prevent split ends and breakage, which can make your hair appear thinner.

Tips for Thicker Hair

Here are some additional tips for promoting thicker hair growth:

1. Use a Thickening Shampoo

Using a thickening shampoo can help to add volume to your hair, making it appear thicker.

2. Try a Scalp Massage

Massaging your scalp can help to increase blood flow to your hair follicles, which can promote hair growth. Try massaging your scalp for a few minutes each day.

3. Don't Brush Your Hair When It's Wet

Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so make sure that you're not brushing your hair when it's wet. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair.

FAQs about Thicker Hair

Q: Can I make my hair thicker?

A: While you can't make your hair thicker as it grows, you can take steps to promote thicker hair growth. Eating a healthy diet, taking supplements, and drinking plenty of water can all help to promote hair growth.

Q: Does cutting your hair make it thicker?

A: No, cutting your hair doesn't actually make it thicker. However, getting regular trims can help to prevent split ends and breakage, which can make your hair appear thinner.

Hair Care for Thicker Hair

Here are some additional tips for caring for thicker hair:

1. Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

Using a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair can help to prevent breakage.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoiding tight hairstyles can help to prevent breakage and damage to your hair.

3. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to prevent hair breakage and reduce frizz.

In conclusion, while hair doesn't actually get thicker as it grows, there are things that you can do to promote thicker hair growth. Eating a healthy diet, taking supplements, and drinking plenty of water can all help to promote hair growth, while getting regular trims and avoiding heat styling can help to prevent breakage and damage. By following these tips, you can help to promote thicker, healthier hair.
