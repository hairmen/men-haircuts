How To Cut Your Hair To Make It Look Thicker

Written by Christine Oct 30, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're struggling with thin hair, you may be wondering how to cut your hair to make it look thicker. While there is no one-size-fits-all answer, there are certain techniques and styles that can add volume and fullness to your hair. In this post, we'll cover the steps you can take to achieve a thicker, fuller head of hair.

Description

Thinning hair can be a source of frustration for many people, but there are ways to combat it. Cutting your hair in the right way can make it look thicker and more voluminous. This post will provide tips and techniques for achieving a fuller head of hair through cutting and styling.

How to Cut Your Hair to Make It Look Thicker

Here are the steps you can take to cut your hair to make it look thicker:

1. Go for a blunt cut

Blunt cuts can make your hair appear thicker by creating the illusion of more volume. Ask your stylist for a cut that is straight across the bottom, with no layers. This will make your hair look fuller and more voluminous.

2. Add layers strategically

While blunt cuts can add volume, layers can create movement and texture in your hair. However, too many layers can make your hair look thin and stringy. Ask your stylist to add layers only in strategic places, such as around your face or at the crown of your head.

3. Opt for a shorter length

Shorter hair can make your hair look thicker by reducing the weight of your hair. This can create more volume and lift at the roots. Consider a bob or a pixie cut if you're looking for a shorter style.

4. Try bangs

Bangs can create the illusion of thicker hair by adding volume around your face. Consider getting blunt, thick bangs that fall just above your eyebrows. This will make your hair look fuller and more voluminous.

5. Embrace your natural texture

If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, embrace it! Curly hair can look thicker than straight hair, so work with your natural texture to create volume and fullness. Avoid using heat tools that can damage your hair and make it look thinner.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for cutting your hair to make it look thicker:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to create more volume and fullness.

Step 2: Section your hair

Section your hair into four sections: the top, left side, right side, and back. Use hair clips to keep each section in place.

Step 3: Cut the back section

Start with the back section of your hair. Use a comb to create a straight line across the bottom of your hair. Cut your hair in a straight line, removing any split ends or damaged hair.

Step 4: Cut the side sections

Move on to the side sections of your hair. Use the back section as a guide, and cut your hair in a straight line. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once.

Step 5: Cut the top section

Finally, cut the top section of your hair. Use the side sections as a guide, and cut your hair in a straight line. Be sure to cut your hair evenly, removing any split ends or damaged hair.

Step 6: Style your hair

Once you've cut your hair, style it to create volume and fullness. Use a volumizing mousse or spray to add texture and lift to your hair.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair to Make It Look Thicker

Here are some additional tips for cutting your hair to make it look thicker:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and damage to your hair
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can create an uneven cut
  • Consider getting a professional haircut if you're unsure about cutting your hair yourself
  • Avoid using heat tools that can damage your hair and make it look thinner

FAQ

Can I make my hair look thicker without cutting it?

Yes, there are other ways to make your hair look thicker without cutting it. You can use volumizing products, blow dry your hair upside down, and avoid using heat tools that can damage your hair.

How often should I cut my hair to make it look thicker?

You should cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and damage. This can help your hair look healthier and thicker in the long run.

Hair Care for Thicker Hair

Here are some tips for caring for thicker hair:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat tools that can damage your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet with plenty of protein and vitamins
  • Don't wash your hair too often, as this can strip it of natural oils
  • Consider using hair masks and treatments to nourish your hair

By following these tips and techniques, you can cut your hair to make it look thicker and fuller. Whether you go for a blunt cut, add layers, or opt for bangs, there are plenty of ways to achieve a voluminous head of hair.
