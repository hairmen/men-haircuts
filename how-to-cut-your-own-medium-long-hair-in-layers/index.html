How To Cut Your Own Medium Long Hair In Layers

Written by Christine Aug 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon every time you need a trim? Do you want to learn how to cut your own medium long hair in layers? With a few basic tools and some patience, you can achieve a salon-worthy layered haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this blog post, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting your own medium long hair in layers, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

How to Cut Your Own Medium Long Hair in Layers

Before we dive into the step-by-step instructions, let's go over the tools you'll need:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Now that you have your tools ready, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing your hair and drying it completely. This will give you a better idea of your natural hair texture and length.

Step 2: Part your hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to part your hair down the middle or to one side, depending on your preference. Secure each section with hair clips.

Step 3: Begin cutting

Starting with the bottom layer of hair, use your comb to separate a small section of hair (about 1-2 inches wide) and hold it straight up with your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, creating a slight V shape. Continue to work your way up, cutting each section of hair at an angle until you reach the top layer.

Step 4: Blend the layers

Once you've completed the layering, blend the layers together by taking small sections of hair and cutting into them at a diagonal angle. This will help to create a seamless transition between the layers.

Step 5: Trim the ends

After you've finished layering and blending, it's time to trim the ends. Hold a small section of hair between your fingers and cut the ends at a straight angle. Repeat this process until you've trimmed all of the ends.

Step 6: Check for symmetry

Use your comb to check for symmetry and make any necessary adjustments. If you notice any unevenness, simply trim the longer sections until they match the shorter sections.

Step 7: Style your new haircut

Once you're happy with your new haircut, style it as desired. You can use a blow dryer, curling iron, or straightener to achieve your desired look.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Medium Long Hair in Layers

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with these tips, you'll be a pro in no time:

  • Start with small sections of hair, especially if you're new to cutting your own hair.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Take breaks as needed to avoid getting fatigued or making mistakes.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head and ensure symmetry.
  • Experiment with different angles and techniques to find what works best for your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own medium long hair in layers:

Can I cut my own hair if I have curly or wavy hair?

Absolutely! In fact, cutting curly or wavy hair is often easier than cutting straight hair because the curls can help hide any mistakes.

Should I cut my hair wet or dry?

You can cut your hair wet or dry, but cutting it wet can help you achieve a more precise cut.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy ends and prevent split ends.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you've learned how to cut your own medium long hair in layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and beauty. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and mastering the art of cutting your own hair, you can save time and money while still looking your best. Happy cutting!
