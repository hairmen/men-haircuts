How To Cut Your Own Hair In Layers Curly

Written by Eveline Jun 05, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have curly hair and want to add some layers to your look, you may be wondering how to cut your own hair. Cutting curly hair in layers can be tricky, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful and natural-looking style. In this article, we'll provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to cut your own hair in layers curly, as well as some tips, FAQ, and hair care advice.

  1. How to Cut Your Own Hair in Layers Curly
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  4. Frequently Asked Questions

How to Cut Your Own Hair in Layers Curly

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips or elastics, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp during the cutting process.

Once you have your tools ready, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair as you normally would. This will help to remove any product buildup and make your hair easier to cut. After washing, gently towel-dry your hair until it's damp.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Using your comb, divide your hair into several sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle, then divide each side into two or three sections. Use hair clips or elastics to secure each section.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the back of your head and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and use your scissors to cut off the desired length. Repeat this process for the rest of the bottom layer, working your way from the back to the front of your head.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Once you've finished cutting the bottom layer, release the clips or elastics from the middle layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the back of your head and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length, making sure to cut at an angle to create layers. Repeat this process for the rest of the middle layer, working your way from the back to the front of your head.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, release the clips or elastics from the top layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the back of your head and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length, again making sure to cut at an angle to create layers. Repeat this process for the rest of the top layer, working your way from the back to the front of your head.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a quick summary of the steps you'll need to follow to cut your own hair in layers curly:

  1. Wash and condition your hair
  2. Divide your hair into sections
  3. Cut the bottom layer
  4. Cut the middle layer
  5. Cut the top layer

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Cutting curly hair can be challenging, but these tips can help you achieve a beautiful and natural-looking style:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair
  • Work in small sections to ensure even cuts
  • Cut at an angle to create layers
  • Start with a longer length and trim as needed
  • Use a mirror to check your progress

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own hair in layers curly?

Yes, you can cut your own hair in layers curly. However, it can be challenging, especially if you're not experienced with cutting hair. If you're unsure about cutting your hair, consider seeing a professional stylist.

What type of scissors should I use to cut curly hair?

Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair. You can use regular hair-cutting scissors or specialty scissors designed for cutting curly hair.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, it's a good idea to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain your style.

Hair Care Advice

To keep your curly hair healthy and looking great, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo
  • Condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry to prevent frizz
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often

With these tips and techniques, you can cut your own hair in layers curly and achieve a beautiful and natural-looking style. Remember to take your time, work in small sections, and use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair. And if you're unsure about cutting your hair, consider seeing a professional stylist.
