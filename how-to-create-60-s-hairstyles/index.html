How To Create 60 S Hairstyles A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

The 60's were a time of change, rebellion, and style. From the beehive to the bouffant, the 60's hairstyles were iconic and still inspire modern hairdos today. Whether you're getting ready for a themed party, or just want to add some vintage flair to your look, here's how to create 60's hairstyles in a few easy steps.

Description

The 60's were all about big hair, bold colors, and statement styles. Women wore their hair high and voluminous, with beehives, bouffants, and flips. These hairstyles were often achieved with a lot of teasing, hairspray, and patience. The 60's also saw the rise of the pixie cut, made famous by Twiggy, and the long, straight hair popularized by the hippie movement.

How to Create 60's Hairstyles

Creating 60's hairstyles requires some basic tools and a lot of hairspray. Here's what you'll need:

  • Hairspray
  • Teasing comb
  • Bobby pins
  • Hot rollers or curling iron

Now, let's get started!

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. If you want to create a beehive or bouffant, you'll want to blow-dry your hair upside down to add volume. If you're going for a straight, sleek look, you can use a flat iron to smooth out any kinks.

Step 2: Tease your hair

Teasing your hair is key to achieving 60's hairstyles. Take small sections of hair and backcomb them towards the roots using a teasing comb. Spray each section with hairspray to hold the tease in place.

Step 3: Style your hair

Now it's time to create your 60's hairstyle! Here are a few classic styles to try:

Beehive

To create a beehive, gather the top section of your hair and tease it towards the crown of your head. Smooth the top layer of hair over the tease and secure it with bobby pins. Use more bobby pins to shape the beehive and hold it in place. Finish with a generous spray of hairspray.

Bouffant

To create a bouffant, tease the top section of your hair and smooth it over the crown of your head. Pin it in place with bobby pins. Then, take the sides of your hair and tease them towards the back of your head. Smooth the top layer of hair over the tease and pin it in place. Finish with hairspray.

Flip

To create a 60's flip, curl the ends of your hair under using hot rollers or a curling iron. Brush out the curls to create a smooth, rounded shape. Spray with hairspray to hold the flip in place.

Step 4: Add accessories

To complete your 60's look, add some accessories. Headbands, scarves, and barrettes were popular in the 60's and can add a pop of color or pattern to your hairdo.

Tips

  • Use a light hand when teasing your hair to avoid damaging it.
  • Use bobby pins that match your hair color to hide them in your hairstyle.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and accessories to make the look your own.

FAQ

What kind of hairspray should I use?

Look for a strong hold hairspray that will keep your style in place all day. You may also want to use a flexible hold hairspray to keep your hair from looking too stiff.

Can I create 60's hairstyles on short hair?

Yes! While some 60's hairstyles require longer hair, you can still achieve a 60's look with a pixie cut or bob. Try adding some volume with a teasing comb and hairspray.

How can I make my 60's hairstyle last all day?

Use plenty of hairspray to hold your style in place. You can also try using hot rollers or a curling iron to set your hair before styling it.

Hair Care

Teasing your hair can cause damage and breakage, so be sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy. Use a heat protectant spray before using hot rollers or a curling iron to minimize damage from heat styling.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to create your own 60's hairstyles and channel your inner retro diva.
