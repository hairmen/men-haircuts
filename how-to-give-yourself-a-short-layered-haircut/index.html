How To Give Yourself A Short Layered Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle without breaking the bank, giving yourself a short layered haircut might be just the thing you need. Not only is it a fun DIY project, but it also allows you to customize your hair to your liking. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about giving yourself a short layered haircut, including how to do it, tips, FAQs, and hair care.

How to Give Yourself a Short Layered Haircut

Before you start cutting, it's important to gather all the materials you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair ties
  • Water spray bottle

Once you have everything ready, it's time to get started.

Step 1: Wet Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is damp. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you don't accidentally cut off too much. Use a water spray bottle to dampen your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, section your hair into two parts: the top and the bottom. Tie the top portion of your hair in a bun or ponytail to keep it out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer of your hair, separate a small section of hair with your fingers or comb. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut straight across with your scissors. Repeat this process, working your way around the back of your head.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layer

Once you've finished cutting the bottom layer, untie the top portion of your hair and let it down. Again, separate a small section of hair and cut it straight across. The key to layering your hair is to cut each section slightly shorter than the previous one. This will create a gradual, layered effect.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

After you've finished cutting both layers, it's time to blend them together. Take a small section of hair from the top layer and hold it up at a 45-degree angle. Use your scissors to cut into the ends of the hair, creating a textured, blended effect. Repeat this process for the rest of your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you give yourself a short layered haircut:

  1. Gather all the materials you'll need: sharp hair cutting scissors, comb, hair ties, and water spray bottle.
  2. Wet your hair using a water spray bottle.
  3. Section your hair into two parts: the top and the bottom. Tie the top portion of your hair in a bun or ponytail to keep it out of the way.
  4. Cut the bottom layer of your hair, working your way around the back of your head.
  5. Untie the top portion of your hair and let it down.
  6. Cut the top layer of your hair, making sure to cut each section slightly shorter than the previous one.
  7. Blend the layers together by cutting into the ends of your hair at a 45-degree angle.
  8. Trim any uneven pieces or flyaways.
  9. Style your hair as desired.
  10. Clean up any hair clippings and sanitize your scissors.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

Here are some tips to keep in mind when giving yourself a short layered haircut:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting off too much at once.
  • Cut your hair in a well-lit area to ensure that you can see what you're doing.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's dry, as this can lead to uneven cutting and breakage.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and time-consuming.

FAQs

Can I give myself a short layered haircut if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you may want to cut your hair slightly longer than you want it to be.

How often should I give myself a haircut?

This depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it becomes damaged. As a general rule, you should aim to give yourself a haircut every 6-8 weeks.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

No, you should always use sharp hair cutting scissors. Regular scissors can damage your hair and lead to split ends.

Hair Care After Your Haircut

After you've given yourself a short layered haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking good. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can lead to breakage and damage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it looking neat and healthy.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or scarf.

By following these tips and techniques, you'll be able to give yourself a short layered haircut that looks great and feels great. Whether you're looking to switch up your style or save some money on haircuts, this DIY project is a fun and rewarding way to achieve your hair goals.
