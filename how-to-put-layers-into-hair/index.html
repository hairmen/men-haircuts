How To Put Layers Into Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Nov 18, 2023 · 4 min read

If you want to add some dimension and movement to your hair, adding layers is a great option. Not only does it create volume, but it also adds texture and interest to your look. In this guide, we’ll show you how to put layers into hair, step by step.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Put Layers into Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Layering Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care for Layered Hair

How to Put Layers into Hair

Layering hair involves cutting different lengths of hair throughout the head to create depth and movement. The idea is to remove some weight from the bottom of the hair while keeping the length intact. This creates texture and volume, making the hair look fuller and bouncier.

Who Can Get Layers?

Layering works well on most hair types, including straight, wavy, curly, and thick hair. It’s a great option if you want to add volume and texture to your hair without sacrificing length. However, if you have very fine or thin hair, layering may not be the best option, as it can make the hair appear even thinner.

When to Get Layers?

You can get layers at any time, but it’s best to get them when your hair is healthy and in good condition. If your hair is damaged or has split ends, layering can make it look even more frayed and unhealthy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Layering hair is best done by a professional hair stylist, but if you’re feeling adventurous, you can do it at home. Here’s how:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Start by separating the top section of your hair from the bottom section.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer, comb your hair straight and cut it straight across. This will be your base layer.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the next section of hair and comb it straight. Cut it at an angle, starting from the bottom of the hair and working your way up. This will create a soft, blended look.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Release the top layer of hair and comb it straight. Cut it at a steeper angle than the middle layer, starting from the bottom of the hair and working your way up. This will create more movement and volume.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Use thinning shears or scissors to blend the layers together. This will create a seamless transition from one layer to the next.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

Make sure the layers are even by checking them from different angles. If you notice any unevenness, trim the ends until they’re even.

Tips for Layering Hair

Here are some tips to create the best layered look:

  • Choose the right length for your face shape.
  • Avoid over-layering, as it can create a choppy look.
  • Ask your stylist for a razor cut if you want a more textured look.
  • Add highlights or lowlights to create even more dimension.
  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to enhance the layers.

FAQs

Can I Layer My Hair if I Have Bangs?

Absolutely! Bangs can be incorporated into layered haircuts, and they can actually enhance the overall look.

How Often Should I Get My Layers Trimmed?

It’s recommended to get your layers trimmed every 6-8 weeks to maintain their shape and prevent split ends.

Can I Layer My Hair if I Have Curly Hair?

Yes, layering can work well on curly hair, but it’s important to find a stylist who is experienced in cutting curly hair. Layers can help enhance the natural bounce and movement of curly hair.

Hair Care for Layered Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your layered hair:

  • Use a heat protectant when using styling tools.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet, as it can cause breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to the ends of your hair to prevent split ends.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.

Now that you know how to put layers into hair, it’s time to book an appointment with your stylist or grab a pair of scissors and get started at home. With these tips and techniques, you’ll be able to create a layered look that’s perfect for you.
