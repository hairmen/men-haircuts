How To Get Rid Of Red Tones In Blonde Hair

Written by Eveline Nov 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Blonde hair is a classic, timeless look. However, sometimes blonde hair can take on unwanted red tones, making it look brassy or orange. If you're struggling with red tones in your blonde hair, don't worry - there are plenty of ways to fix it. In this article, we'll go over how to get rid of red tones in blonde hair, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • Step by Step Guide

How to Get Rid of Red Tones in Blonde Hair

There are several methods to remove red tones from blonde hair:

Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a great way to neutralize red tones in blonde hair. This type of shampoo contains purple pigments that counteract yellow or orange hues in your hair, leaving you with a cool-toned blonde. To use purple shampoo, wet your hair in the shower and lather it up like you would with regular shampoo. Leave the shampoo on for 5-10 minutes before rinsing it out.

Try a Toning Treatment

Another option is to use a toning treatment. These treatments are designed to neutralize unwanted tones in your hair and can be found at most beauty supply stores. To use a toning treatment, apply it to your damp hair after shampooing and leave it on for the recommended amount of time before rinsing it out.

Bleach Your Hair

If all else fails, you can bleach your hair. This is the most extreme option and should only be done by a professional stylist. Bleaching your hair will remove any unwanted red or orange tones, but it can also damage your hair if not done correctly.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide on how to get rid of red tones in blonde hair:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup. This will help the toner or purple shampoo work more effectively.
  2. Apply the toner or purple shampoo to your hair, making sure to cover all areas evenly.
  3. Leave the toner or purple shampoo on for the recommended amount of time (usually 5-10 minutes).
  4. Rinse out the toner or purple shampoo thoroughly.
  5. Condition your hair to add moisture back into your strands.
  6. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Maintaining Blonde Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining blonde hair and preventing red tones from appearing:

  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage and make your blonde hair look brassy.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools to minimize damage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as chlorine from swimming pools.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use regular shampoo instead of purple shampoo?

Regular shampoo won't have the same effect as purple shampoo when it comes to neutralizing red tones in blonde hair. Purple shampoo is specifically designed to counteract unwanted hues in blonde hair, so it's best to use that instead.

How often should I use purple shampoo?

It depends on your hair and how often you wash it. Some people use purple shampoo every time they wash their hair, while others use it once or twice a week. Start by using it once a week and see how your hair reacts.

Hair Care for Blonde Hair

Maintaining blonde hair can be tricky, but with the right hair care routine, you can keep your hair healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips for caring for blonde hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can cause damage and dryness.
  • Rinse your hair with cool water to help seal the cuticle and reduce frizz.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to add moisture to your strands.

With these tips and tricks, you can get rid of unwanted red tones in your blonde hair and keep it looking vibrant and healthy.
