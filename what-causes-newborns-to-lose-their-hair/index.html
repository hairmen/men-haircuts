What Causes Newborns To Lose Their Hair

Written by Daniel Sep 28, 2023 · 3 min read

If you’re a new parent, you may notice your newborn losing hair. It’s important to understand that this is a normal occurrence and happens to many newborns. In this article, we’ll discuss what causes newborns to lose their hair and provide tips on how to care for their hair.

Table of Content

  • Causes of Newborn Hair Loss
  • How to Care for Your Newborn’s Hair
  • Tips for Dealing with Newborn Hair Loss

Causes of Newborn Hair Loss

Newborn hair loss is common and usually occurs within the first few months of life. There are a few reasons why your baby may be losing hair:

Hormones

One of the most common causes of hair loss in newborns is due to hormones. During pregnancy, the mother’s hormones can affect the baby’s hair growth. After birth, the baby’s hormones adjust and can cause hair to fall out. This is known as telogen effluvium and is a temporary condition.

Friction

Newborns often rub their head against their bedding or car seat, which can cause hair loss. This is usually more noticeable on the back of the head where the hair rubs against the surface.

Genetics

Some babies are simply born with less hair than others. If one or both parents had little hair at birth, it’s possible that the baby will too.

How to Care for Your Newborn’s Hair

While newborn hair loss is normal, it’s important to care for your baby’s hair properly. Here are some tips:

Wash Hair Regularly

You should wash your baby’s hair once or twice a week with a gentle baby shampoo. Be sure to rinse thoroughly to avoid any soap residue.

Be Gentle

When washing your baby’s hair, be gentle. Use a soft washcloth and avoid rubbing the scalp too hard. Also, avoid using hair accessories that can pull on the hair.

Brush Hair Gently

Use a soft-bristled brush to gently brush your baby’s hair. This will help to distribute natural oils and prevent tangles.

Tips for Dealing with Newborn Hair Loss

Here are some tips for dealing with newborn hair loss:

Don’t Worry!

Newborn hair loss is normal and usually temporary. It’s important to remember that your baby’s hair will grow back.

Try Different Hairstyles

If your baby’s hair loss is noticeable, you can try different hairstyles to disguise it. For example, you can use a headband or hat to cover up any bald spots.

FAQs About Newborn Hair Loss

When will my baby’s hair grow back?

Most babies’ hair begins to grow back around 3-6 months of age.

Is newborn hair loss a sign of a health problem?

No, newborn hair loss is usually normal and not a sign of a health problem. However, if you’re concerned, it’s always best to speak with your pediatrician.

Hair Care Products for Newborns

When it comes to hair care products for newborns, it’s important to choose products that are gentle and free of harsh chemicals. Here are some products to consider:

Baby Shampoo

Choose a gentle baby shampoo that is free of sulfates and parabens. Look for products that are specifically designed for newborns.

Baby Brush

Use a soft-bristled baby brush to gently brush your baby’s hair.

Baby Hat

A baby hat can help to protect your baby’s head from the sun and also cover up any bald spots.

In conclusion, newborn hair loss is normal and nothing to worry about. With proper care, your baby’s hair will grow back in no time!
