How To Cut Layers On The Side Of Your Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a way to switch up your hairstyle, adding layers to the side of your hair can give you a fresh new look. Cutting layers on the side of your hair can add volume, texture, and movement to your hair, making it look more dynamic and interesting. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps to cut layers on the side of your hair.

Description

Cutting layers on the side of your hair can be a bit tricky, especially if you're doing it for the first time. However, with some practice and patience, you can achieve a gorgeous layered look that complements your face shape and hair type. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut layers on the side of your hair in a relaxed, easy-to-understand language.

How to Cut Layers on the Side of Your Hair

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Comb your hair thoroughly to remove any tangles or knots. If you have curly hair, you may want to straighten it before cutting to get a more accurate cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections, starting from the crown of your head down to your ears. Use hair clips to secure the top sections of your hair, leaving the bottom section loose.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer, comb your hair straight down and cut it to the desired length. Use scissors to cut the hair in a straight line, making sure to cut at an angle to create a layered effect.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layer

Next, release the top layer of your hair and comb it straight down. Cut it to the same length as the bottom layer, making sure to cut at an angle. This will create a layered effect that blends seamlessly with the bottom layer.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Using scissors, blend the layers together by cutting into the hair at an angle. This will create a more natural and textured look.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to the desired length to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean and precise cut.
  • If you're not confident in cutting your own hair, ask a friend or a professional stylist for help.

FAQ

How do I know what length to cut my hair?

It's important to have a clear idea of the length you want before you start cutting. You can use a ruler or measuring tape to get an accurate measurement. You can also use a photo for reference or consult with a stylist.

Can I cut layers on the side of my hair if I have short hair?

Yes, you can still cut layers on the side of your hair if you have short hair. However, it may be more challenging to achieve the desired layered effect, so it's best to consult with a stylist.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6 to 8 weeks to maintain the health and shape of your hair.

Hair Care

Cutting your hair can cause some damage, so it's important to take care of your hair after cutting. Use a hair mask or deep conditioner to nourish your hair and protect it from further damage. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and use a heat protectant spray when you do. Trim your hair regularly to maintain its shape and health.

Cutting layers on the side of your hair can give you a fresh new look that adds volume, texture, and movement to your hair. With this step-by-step guide, you can achieve the perfect layered look that complements your face shape and hair type. Remember to take care of your hair after cutting to maintain its health and beauty.
