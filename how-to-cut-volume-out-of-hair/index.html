How To Cut Volume Out Of Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Aug 12, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have naturally voluminous hair, you may find that it can be challenging to manage, style, and maintain. While many people love the look of big, bouncy hair, it can sometimes be overwhelming or difficult to tame. Fortunately, there are several ways to cut volume out of hair, whether you want a sleeker, more polished look or simply want to make your hair more manageable.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

Description

Cutting volume out of hair can be done in a variety of ways, depending on your hair type, length, and personal preferences. Some options include:

  • Layering: Cutting hair into layers can help to remove some of the bulk and weight of voluminous hair, making it more manageable and easier to style.
  • Thinning: Thinning hair involves using special shears to remove some of the hair's thickness, without necessarily changing the overall shape or style of the hair.
  • Graduated cuts: Graduated cuts involve cutting shorter layers into the hair, which taper down to longer layers at the bottom. This can create a more structured, polished look while still retaining some volume.

How to Cut Volume Out of Hair

If you're looking to cut volume out of your hair, there are a few steps you can follow to achieve the desired result:

Step 1: Decide on the right cut

The first step is to decide on the right type of cut for your hair. Consider factors like your face shape, hair type, and personal style preferences when choosing a cut.

Step 2: Find a skilled stylist

Once you've decided on a cut, it's essential to find a stylist who is skilled in cutting voluminous hair. Look for someone who has experience working with your hair type and can offer advice and guidance on how to achieve the best results.

Step 3: Communicate your goals

When you meet with your stylist, be sure to communicate your goals clearly. Let them know that you want to cut volume out of your hair, but also be specific about how much volume you want to remove and what type of style you're looking for.

Step 4: Trust the process

Finally, trust the process and allow your stylist to work their magic. Cutting volume out of hair can be a delicate process that requires skill and expertise, so be patient and let them do their job.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide for cutting volume out of hair:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. This will help your stylist get a clear sense of your hair's texture and volume.

Step 2: Consult with your stylist

Consult with your stylist to discuss the type of cut you want and how much volume you want to remove. They may also ask you questions about your hair care routine and styling preferences.

Step 3: Section your hair

Your stylist will likely section your hair into smaller sections before cutting. This will help them to create a more precise cut and ensure that they're cutting each section evenly.

Step 4: Cut the hair

Your stylist will then start cutting the hair, using the chosen technique (such as layering, thinning, or graduated cuts) to remove volume from the hair. They may also use different tools, such as razor blades or scissors, to achieve the desired result.

Step 5: Style the hair

Once the cutting is complete, your stylist will likely style your hair to help you see the final result. They may also offer tips and advice on how to style and maintain your hair at home.

Tips for Cutting Volume Out of Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting volume out of hair:

  • Choose a cut that complements your face shape and personal style.
  • Be specific about the amount of volume you want to remove.
  • Find a skilled stylist who has experience working with voluminous hair.
  • Consider using styling products, such as hair serums or mousses, to help manage your hair after cutting.

FAQs

Can I cut volume out of my hair at home?

While it's possible to trim your hair at home, cutting volume out of hair is best left to a professional stylist. They have the experience and expertise to achieve the best results and can help you avoid any mistakes or mishaps that could damage your hair.

Will cutting volume out of my hair damage it?

No, cutting volume out of your hair shouldn't damage it as long as you use a skilled stylist and take care of your hair afterward. In fact, removing some of the bulk and weight of voluminous hair can actually help to make it healthier and more manageable.

Hair Care for Voluminous Hair

To keep your hair healthy and manageable after cutting volume out of it, here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's formulated for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as they can damage your hair and cause it to become frizzy or brittle.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to help nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage and damage.

Cutting volume out of hair can be a great way to achieve a more manageable, polished look. By following these tips and working with a skilled stylist, you can create a hairstyle that looks and feels great.
