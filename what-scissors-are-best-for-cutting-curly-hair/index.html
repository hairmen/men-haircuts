What Scissors Are Best For Cutting Curly Hair

Written by Frank Oct 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be challenging to cut. It requires a specific set of skills and tools to achieve the desired result. One of the essential tools in a hairstylist's arsenal is the scissors. However, not all scissors are created equal, and not all of them are suitable for cutting curly hair. In this article, we will discuss what scissors are best for cutting curly hair and how to use them properly.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Curly hair requires a different approach than straight hair. It's important to use scissors that are specifically designed for curly hair. These scissors have a unique blade that can handle the thickness and texture of curly hair. The blade is also curved, which allows for a more precise cut without damaging the hair.

The type of scissors you use will depend on the type of cut you want to achieve. For example, if you want to create layers, you'll need scissors with smaller blades. If you want to create a blunt cut, you'll need scissors with larger blades.

How to

When cutting curly hair, it's important to start with dry hair. Wet hair can stretch, which can result in an uneven cut. Before you start cutting, comb through the hair to remove any tangles or knots. Then, section the hair into smaller sections using clips.

When using scissors, hold them at a slight angle and start cutting from the bottom of the hair. Cut small sections at a time, focusing on the ends of the hair. This will help to prevent split ends and breakage. If you're creating layers, start by cutting the hair at a 45-degree angle.

Step by step

1. Start with dry hair.

2. Comb through the hair to remove any tangles or knots.

3. Section the hair into smaller sections using clips.

4. Hold the scissors at a slight angle and start cutting from the bottom of the hair.

5. Cut small sections at a time, focusing on the ends of the hair.

6. If you're creating layers, start by cutting the hair at a 45-degree angle.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for cutting curly hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent damage to the hair.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to take small sections and cut them slowly.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of the hair as you cut.
  • Don't cut the hair too short, as it may spring up and look shorter than intended.
  • Don't cut the hair when it's wet, as it can stretch and result in an uneven cut.

FAQ

What type of scissors are best for cutting curly hair?

Scissors with a curved blade and smaller blades are best for cutting curly hair.

Can I cut curly hair with regular scissors?

You can cut curly hair with regular scissors, but it's not recommended. Regular scissors can damage the hair and result in split ends and breakage.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended to cut curly hair every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

Hair Care

After cutting curly hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness, and avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair, and apply a leave-in conditioner to keep it moisturized. Finally, trim the ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Cutting curly hair requires a specific set of skills and tools. By using the right scissors and following the proper techniques, you can achieve a beautiful and healthy-looking cut. Remember to take care of your hair properly after cutting it to keep it looking its best.
