How To Keep Thick Hair Curly Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Oct 22, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're blessed with thick, curly hair, you know that it can be both a blessing and a curse. While your hair may be enviable for some, it can also be a challenge to style and maintain. If you're struggling to keep your curls looking their best, don't worry – we're here to help. In this post, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to keep thick hair curly, so you can enjoy your natural texture without the frizz and fuss.

How to Keep Thick Hair Curly

Before we dive into the specifics, it's important to understand the basics of how to keep thick hair curly. Essentially, you need to focus on two things: moisture and definition. Curly hair tends to be drier than other hair types, so it's crucial to keep it hydrated. At the same time, you want to define your curls so that they don't become a frizzy mess.

Moisture

The first step to keeping thick hair curly is to moisturize it. This means using products that are designed to hydrate your hair and keep it soft and supple. Look for shampoos, conditioners, and styling products that are specifically formulated for curly hair. These products are designed to penetrate your hair shaft and provide deep moisture, which helps to prevent frizz and breakage.

Definition

Once you've got your hair moisturized, the next step is to define your curls. This means using products that will enhance your natural curl pattern and help your curls hold their shape. Look for styling products like curl creams, gels, and mousses that are designed to provide hold without weighing down your hair. These products will help your curls stay defined and bouncy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you understand the basic principles of how to keep thick hair curly, let's go through a step-by-step guide.

Step 1: Start with clean hair

The first step is to wash your hair with a hydrating shampoo and conditioner. This will help to remove any dirt and buildup that can weigh down your curls.

Step 2: Apply a leave-in conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help keep your hair moisturized. This will also help to detangle your hair, making it easier to style.

Step 3: Apply a curl-defining product

Next, apply a curl-defining product like a curl cream or gel. Be sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends of your hair where your curls tend to be the driest.

Step 4: Use a diffuser

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair. The diffuser will help to distribute the heat evenly, which will help to prevent frizz. Be sure to use a low heat setting, as high heat can damage your hair.

Step 5: Scrunch your curls

Once your hair is dry, use your hands to scrunch your curls. This will help to define your curls and give them bounce.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you keep your thick hair curly:

  • Don't brush your hair when it's dry – this can break up your curls and cause frizz.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and help your curls retain their shape.
  • Avoid using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective spray.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQs

How often should I wash my curly hair?

It depends on your hair type and how much oil your scalp produces. Some people with curly hair can go several days without washing, while others need to wash their hair every other day. Experiment to find a routine that works for you.

What's the best way to dry curly hair?

The best way to dry curly hair is to let it air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer. Avoid using a regular hair dryer without a diffuser, as the direct heat can cause frizz.

Can I straighten my curly hair?

Yes, you can straighten your curly hair, but it's important to use heat protectant products and be gentle with your hair. Straightening your hair too often can damage your hair and cause it to lose its natural curl pattern.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Keeping thick hair curly is all about taking good care of your hair. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy, hydrated curls:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, rather than a brush.
  • Avoid using hot water when you wash your hair, as this can dry out your hair and lead to frizz.
  • Protect your hair from chlorine by wearing a swim cap or using a leave-in conditioner before swimming.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your thick hair curly and healthy. Remember to be patient and experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair type. With a little bit of effort, you can have beautiful, bouncy curls that are the envy of everyone around you.
