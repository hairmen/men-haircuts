How To Use Thinning Shears On Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of your thick, heavy bangs? Do you want to add some texture and movement to your hairstyle? Thinning shears might be just what you need! Thinning shears are a special type of scissors that can remove bulk and weight from your hair without affecting the length. In this article, we will show you how to thin your bangs with thinning shears in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

What are Thinning Shears?

Thinning shears, also known as texturizing shears, are scissors that have teeth on one or both blades. These teeth can vary in size and spacing, allowing you to remove different amounts of hair at once. Thinning shears are commonly used by hairstylists to create texture, blend layers, and remove bulk from thick or curly hair.

How to Thin Your Bangs with Thinning Shears

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start using thinning shears on your bangs, you should wash and dry your hair as usual. Make sure your bangs are completely dry, as wet hair can stretch and cause uneven cuts.

Step 2: Comb Your Bangs

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your bangs and make sure they are straight. You can also use clips to section off the rest of your hair and focus on your bangs only.

Step 3: Hold the Thinning Shears Properly

Hold the thinning shears like you would hold any other scissors, with your thumb on the lower handle and your index and middle fingers on the upper handle. Make sure the teeth are pointing towards the ends of your hair, not towards your scalp.

Step 4: Start Thinning Your Bangs

Take a small section of your bangs between the thinning shears and close the blades gently. Slide the shears down the section of hair, opening and closing the blades as you go. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as thinning shears can be quite powerful.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After thinning a section of your bangs, comb through it with your fingers or a comb to see how it looks. If you want to remove more bulk, repeat the process on another section. If you are happy with the result, move on to the next step.

Step 6: Blend Your Bangs

If you have long or side-swept bangs, you might want to blend them into the rest of your hair for a more natural look. To do this, take a small section of your bangs and the surrounding hair and twist them together. Use the thinning shears to remove some of the hair from the twisted section, creating a seamless transition.

Step 7: Style Your Bangs

Once you are done thinning and blending your bangs, you can style them as usual. Use a round brush and a blow dryer to create volume and movement, or use a flat iron to straighten them out.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Bangs

Thinning shears can be a great tool for achieving a more textured and natural-looking hairstyle. However, there are some tips you should keep in mind to avoid common mistakes and achieve the best results.

  • Start with a small amount of hair and gradually thin more if needed.
  • Use the thinning shears only on the ends of your hair, not near your scalp.
  • Always comb through your hair after thinning to check your progress.
  • Use a light hand and avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • If you have very thick or curly hair, consider getting a professional haircut to avoid uneven results.

FAQ About Thinning Shears and Bangs

Can I use regular scissors instead of thinning shears?

Regular scissors can also be used to trim or cut your bangs, but they are not as effective as thinning shears for removing bulk and adding texture.

Can thinning shears damage my hair?

Thinning shears are not likely to damage your hair if used properly. However, if you cut too much hair at once or use them too frequently, you might end up with split ends or frizz.

Can I thin my own bangs with thinning shears?

Thinning your own bangs with thinning shears can be challenging, especially if you are not familiar with the technique. It is recommended to seek professional help or practice on a small section of hair first.

Hair Care After Thinning Your Bangs

After thinning your bangs, you might need to adjust your hair care routine to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips to follow:

  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools to prevent damage.
  • Trim your bangs regularly to maintain their shape and avoid split ends.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid brushing your hair too often, as it can cause breakage and frizz.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful and natural-looking hairstyle with thinned-out bangs. Thinning shears can be a great tool for adding texture and movement to your hair, as long as you use them correctly and with care.
