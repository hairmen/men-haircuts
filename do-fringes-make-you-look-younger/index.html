Do Fringes Make You Look Younger Here s The Truth

Written by Aprilia Sep 05, 2023 · 4 min read

As we age, we all want to look younger. But is it possible to achieve this with something as simple as a fringe? In this blog post, we’ll explore the question, “do fringes make you look younger?” and provide you with tips and tricks on how to achieve a youthful look with a fringe.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get a Fringe
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips

Description

First things first, let’s define what a fringe is. A fringe is a hairstyle where the hair is cut to hang over the forehead. It’s also known as bangs in some parts of the world. A fringe can be cut in different styles, such as blunt, side-swept, or layered.

Now that we know what a fringe is, let’s answer the question, “do fringes make you look younger?” The answer is yes! A fringe can instantly give you a more youthful appearance. It can cover forehead wrinkles, soften facial features, and make you look more playful and fun.

How to Get a Fringe

If you’re convinced that a fringe is the way to go, here’s how you can get one:

1. Choose the Right Type of Fringe

There are many types of fringes to choose from, so make sure you choose one that complements your face shape and hair type. For example, if you have a round face shape, a side-swept fringe can help elongate your face. If you have thick hair, a blunt fringe can help control the volume.

2. Decide on the Length

Once you’ve chosen the type of fringe, you need to decide on the length. A shorter fringe can make you look younger, but it requires more maintenance. A longer fringe is easier to manage, but it can cover your eyes and make you look sleepy.

3. Consult with Your Hairstylist

Finally, consult with your hairstylist. They can help you choose the right type and length of fringe for you. They can also give you tips on how to style and maintain your fringe.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own fringe:

1. Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your hair to remove any tangles.

2. Section Your Hair

Section your hair by creating a triangle shape at the top of your head. The base of the triangle should be at your hairline, and the point should be at the top of your head.

3. Cut Your Fringe

Cut your fringe in a straight line using scissors. Start at the center of the triangle and work your way outwards. Cut your fringe a little longer than you want it to be, as it will shrink when it dries.

4. Style Your Fringe

Use a blow dryer and a round brush to style your fringe. Blow dry your fringe in the direction you want it to go, and use the round brush to create a curve.

Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe

Here are some tips on how to maintain your fringe:

  • Trim your fringe every two to three weeks to keep it looking fresh.
  • Use dry shampoo to keep your fringe from looking oily.
  • Use a small amount of hair wax or gel to style your fringe.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will a fringe suit me?

A: It depends on your face shape and hair type. Consult with your hairstylist to find out what type of fringe will suit you.

Q: How often do I need to trim my fringe?

A: You should trim your fringe every two to three weeks to keep it looking fresh.

Q: Can I style my fringe if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, you can style your fringe if you have curly hair. Use a small amount of hair wax or gel to tame your curls.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to keep your fringe looking great:

  • Wash your hair every two to three days to prevent it from getting greasy.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your fringe, as it can damage the hair.

In conclusion, fringes can make you look younger. By choosing the right type and length of fringe, consulting with your hairstylist, and maintaining your fringe, you can achieve a youthful and playful look. Follow our tips and tricks, and you’ll be sure to turn heads with your new fringe!
