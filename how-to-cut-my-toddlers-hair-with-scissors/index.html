How To Cut My Toddler s Hair With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 08, 2023 · 4 min read

As a parent, you may find yourself wanting to save a little money and cut your toddler's hair at home. While it can be intimidating at first, cutting your toddler's hair with scissors is actually quite manageable with the right tools and techniques. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about how to cut your toddler's hair with scissors.

Description

Cutting your toddler's hair with scissors can seem daunting, but it's actually quite simple with the right tools and techniques. With a little practice and patience, you can save money and give your child a great haircut.

How to Cut My Toddler's Hair with Scissors

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step to cutting your toddler's hair with scissors is to gather all the tools you'll need. You'll need a good pair of scissors, a comb or brush, and a spray bottle filled with water. You may also want to have some hair clips on hand to section the hair.

Step 2: Choose the Right Time

Choose a time when your toddler is well-rested and in a good mood. You want to avoid cutting their hair when they're tired, hungry, or cranky, as this can make the process more difficult.

Step 3: Wet the Hair

Spray your toddler's hair with water to dampen it. This will make it easier to cut and prevent split ends.

Step 4: Section the Hair

Using your comb or brush, section your toddler's hair into manageable sections. Use hair clips to keep the sections separated.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Begin cutting the hair, starting with the longest sections first. Use your comb to hold the hair straight and cut it straight across. Work your way around the head, section by section, until all the hair is cut to the desired length.

Step 6: Trim the Bangs

If your toddler has bangs, you'll want to trim them separately. Comb the bangs straight down and cut them straight across. Be sure to leave them long enough to tuck behind the ears.

Step 7: Clean Up

Once you're finished cutting your toddler's hair, use a brush or comb to remove any loose hair. You may also want to use a damp cloth to wipe away any stray hairs.

Tips for Cutting My Toddler's Hair with Scissors

  • Use sharp, high-quality scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Be patient and take breaks if your child gets restless.
  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to the desired length.
  • Don't cut too much at once, as you can always go back and trim more later.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp and easier to cut.
  • Offer your child a toy or book to keep them occupied during the process.

FAQs

Can I cut my toddler's hair with regular scissors?

While you can technically cut your toddler's hair with regular scissors, it's best to use a pair of scissors designed specifically for cutting hair. Hair cutting scissors are sharper and more precise, which makes the process easier and safer.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my toddler's hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your toddler's hair, don't panic. It's best to stop cutting and assess the situation before making any further cuts. You can always take your child to a professional hairdresser to fix any mistakes.

How often should I cut my toddler's hair?

The frequency of haircuts for toddlers depends on their hair type and growth rate. In general, most toddlers need a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep their hair looking neat and healthy.

Hair Care for My Toddler

In addition to cutting your toddler's hair with scissors, it's important to take good care of their hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner designed for kids.
  • Brush your toddler's hair gently to avoid tangles.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your toddler's hair.
  • Protect your toddler's hair from the sun with a hat or scarf.
  • Trim split ends regularly to prevent damage.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your toddler's hair with scissors and keep their locks looking neat and healthy. Remember to be patient, take breaks when needed, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
