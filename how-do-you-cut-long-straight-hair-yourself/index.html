How To Cut Long Straight Hair Yourself

Written by Frank Jul 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have long straight hair. But with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we’ll go through step-by-step instructions on how to cut long straight hair yourself, along with tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • Tools
  • How to Cut Long Straight Hair Yourself
  • Tips

Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clip or hair tie
  • Comb or brush
  • Handheld mirror

It’s also a good idea to have a towel or cape to catch any hair that falls during the haircut.

How to Cut Long Straight Hair Yourself

Follow these steps to cut your long straight hair:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. This will help you see the natural fall of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Divide your hair

Divide your hair into sections using a comb or clip. Start by creating a center part and then divide your hair into two equal sections. Clip one section out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the first section

Start with the section of hair that is closest to your face. Comb through the hair and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Use your scissors to cut the ends, starting with a small amount and trimming more if needed.

Step 4: Match the second section

Once you’ve cut the first section, use it as a guide to match the length of the second section. Comb through the hair and hold it between your fingers, making sure it matches the length of the first section. Trim the ends as needed.

Step 5: Check the back

Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your hair. If you notice any unevenness, trim the ends as needed.

Step 6: Blend the layers

If you have layers in your hair, use your scissors to blend them together. Comb through the hair and hold it between your fingers, snipping off any stray hairs that stick out.

Step 7: Finish with styling

Once you’ve finished cutting your hair, style it as desired. Use a hair straightener or curling iron to add waves or curls, or leave it straight for a sleek look.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and trim more as needed
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to avoid split ends
  • Make sure your hair is dry before cutting to avoid any shrinkage
  • Take breaks if you feel tired or frustrated

FAQs

Can you cut long straight hair with regular scissors?

While it’s possible to cut your hair with regular scissors, it’s best to use sharp hair cutting scissors to achieve a professional-looking haircut.

How often should you cut long straight hair?

It’s recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and free of split ends. However, if you’re cutting your own hair, you can do it more or less frequently as needed.

Is it easy to cut long straight hair yourself?

Cutting your own hair can be challenging, especially if you’ve never done it before. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut from the comfort of your own home.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it to maintain its health and shine. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot tools on a daily basis to prevent damage
  • Apply a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to nourish your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

By following these tips, you can keep your long straight hair looking healthy and vibrant.
