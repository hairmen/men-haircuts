Will Perms Ever Come Back In Style

Description

Perms have been around for decades, and while they were all the rage in the 80s, they fell out of popularity in the 90s and early 2000s. However, as we move further into the 2020s, there has been a resurgence of interest in perms. So, will perms ever come back in style? The answer is yes.

While perms may not be as popular as they once were, there is a growing trend towards natural-looking, effortless styles, and perms fit the bill perfectly. With advancements in hair technology and techniques, perms are no longer limited to tight, spiral curls. Instead, modern perms can create anything from loose waves to beachy textures, making them more versatile than ever before.

How to Perm Your Hair

If you're considering getting a perm, it's important to know what to expect. First, you'll need to find a reputable stylist who has experience with perms. Next, you'll need to decide on the type of perm you want. There are several different types of perms, including:

  • Spiral perm
  • Body perm
  • Stack perm
  • Spot perm

Once you've chosen the type of perm you want, your stylist will apply a solution to your hair that breaks down the bonds that hold your hair's natural shape. After rinsing out the solution, your stylist will wrap your hair around perm rods and apply a neutralizer to set the new shape. The process can take a few hours, depending on the length and thickness of your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for getting a perm:

  1. Find a reputable stylist who has experience with perms.
  2. Decide on the type of perm you want.
  3. Wash your hair and do not apply any products.
  4. Apply protective cream around your hairline and ears.
  5. Apply the perm solution to your hair.
  6. Wrap your hair around perm rods.
  7. Apply the neutralizer to set the new shape.
  8. Rinse your hair thoroughly.
  9. Dry your hair and style as desired.
  10. Maintain your perm with regular trims and deep conditioning treatments.

Tips for Maintaining Your Perm

Once you have a perm, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the shape and prevent damage. Here are some tips for maintaining your perm:

  • Avoid washing your hair for at least 24 hours after getting a perm.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and change the shape of your perm.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and moisturized.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about perms:

Can I get a perm if I have color-treated hair?

Yes, but it's important to tell your stylist about any color treatments you've had before getting a perm. They may need to adjust the strength of the perm solution to avoid damaging your hair.

How long will my perm last?

Perms typically last 2-6 months, depending on how fast your hair grows and how well you take care of it.

Can I still straighten my hair after getting a perm?

Yes, but it's best to wait at least a week after getting a perm before heat styling your hair. Be sure to use a heat protectant to prevent damage.

Hair Care for Permed Hair

Permed hair requires special care to keep it healthy and maintain the shape. Here are some tips for caring for permed hair:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid chlorine and saltwater, as they can dry out your hair and cause damage.

In conclusion, perms are making a comeback and are more versatile than ever before. With the right care and maintenance, you can enjoy beautiful, natural-looking curls and waves for months to come.
