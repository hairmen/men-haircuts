What Foods Should You Eat If Your Hair Is Falling Out

Written by Bardi Jul 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you are experiencing hair loss, you are not alone. Hair loss affects millions of people worldwide, and it can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, stress, hormonal changes, and poor nutrition. While there is no magic food that can cure hair loss, there are certain foods that can promote healthy hair growth and prevent further hair loss.

Protein

Protein is an essential nutrient for hair growth as it helps to build and repair tissues, including hair follicles. Lack of protein in the diet can lead to hair thinning and hair loss. Foods rich in protein include lean meats, fish, poultry, beans, and nuts. It is recommended to consume at least 46 grams of protein per day for women and 56 grams for men.

Iron

Iron is another important nutrient for healthy hair growth. Iron helps to carry oxygen to the hair follicles, which is necessary for hair growth. Iron deficiency can lead to hair loss, so it is important to consume iron-rich foods such as red meat, poultry, fish, beans, and leafy green vegetables. Women are at higher risk of iron deficiency due to menstrual blood loss, so it is recommended to consume 18 mg of iron per day.

Vitamin C

Vitamin C is an antioxidant that helps to protect the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals. It also helps the body to absorb iron, which is necessary for healthy hair growth. Foods rich in vitamin C include citrus fruits, strawberries, kiwi, bell peppers, broccoli, and spinach. It is recommended to consume at least 75 mg of vitamin C per day for women and 90 mg for men.

Biotin

Biotin is a B-complex vitamin that is essential for healthy hair growth. Biotin helps to strengthen the hair follicles and prevent hair breakage. Foods rich in biotin include eggs, nuts, whole grains, and leafy green vegetables. It is recommended to consume at least 30 mcg of biotin per day.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Omega-3 fatty acids are essential fats that cannot be produced by the body and must be obtained through the diet. Omega-3s help to keep the scalp hydrated and promote healthy hair growth. Foods rich in omega-3s include fatty fish, flaxseeds, chia seeds, and walnuts. It is recommended to consume at least 250-500 mg of omega-3 fatty acids per day.

Zinc

Zinc is a mineral that is important for healthy hair growth as it helps to repair damaged hair follicles. Zinc also helps to regulate the oil glands in the scalp, which can prevent dandruff and hair loss. Foods rich in zinc include oysters, beef, poultry, beans, and nuts. It is recommended to consume at least 8 mg of zinc per day for women and 11 mg for men.

FAQ

How long will it take for my hair to grow back?

The rate of hair growth varies from person to person, but on average, hair grows about half an inch per month. It may take several months to a year for noticeable hair growth to occur.

Can supplements help with hair loss?

While supplements can help to promote healthy hair growth, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before taking any supplements, as some may interact with medications or have side effects.

Hair Care Tips

In addition to consuming a healthy diet, there are several other tips to promote healthy hair growth and prevent hair loss:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair, such as braids and ponytails.
  • Avoid heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage the hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or brush to detangle hair.
  • Avoid over-washing hair, as it can strip the hair of natural oils.
  • Protect hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

By following these tips and consuming a healthy diet rich in protein, iron, vitamin C, biotin, omega-3 fatty acids, and zinc, you can promote healthy hair growth and prevent hair loss.
