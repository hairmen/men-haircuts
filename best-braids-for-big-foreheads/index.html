Types of Braids for Big Foreheads

When it comes to braids, there are many different styles to choose from. However, not all braids work well for those with big foreheads. Here are some of the best braids for big foreheads:

Dutch Braids

Dutch braids are a great option for those with big foreheads. These braids start at the top of the head and are braided in a way that creates a raised, three-dimensional effect. This style draws attention away from the forehead and towards the braids themselves.

Fishtail Braids

Fishtail braids are another great option for those with big foreheads. These braids start at the nape of the neck and are braided in a way that creates a slimming effect. This style draws attention away from the forehead and towards the slim, elegant braid.

Crown Braids

Crown braids are a classic style that works well for those with big foreheads. These braids start at the front of the head and are braided in a circular pattern, creating a crown-like effect. This style draws attention away from the forehead and towards the beautiful braid.

How to Style Braids for Big Foreheads

Now that you know which braids work best for big foreheads, itâs time to learn how to style them. Hereâs a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start braiding, make sure your hair is clean, dry, and detangled. You can also add some hair oil or serum to add shine and moisture to your locks.

Step 2: Choose Your Braid

Choose the braid style that works best for your forehead shape and personal style. You can also mix and match different braid styles for a unique look.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small, manageable sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to braid and ensure a neat, uniform look.

Step 4: Start Braiding

Begin braiding your hair, starting at the top of your head or the nape of your neck, depending on the braid style. Make sure to braid tightly and evenly, and use a comb or your fingers to smooth out any bumps or flyaways.

Step 5: Secure Your Braid

Secure the end of your braid with a hair tie or bobby pins. You can also use hairspray or hair wax to keep your braid in place and prevent frizz.

Tips and Tricks for Maintaining Braids

Braids are a great protective style that can last for weeks with the right care. Here are some tips and tricks for maintaining your braids:

Tip 1: Sleep with a Satin Scarf or Pillowcase

Sleeping with a satin scarf or pillowcase can help prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep. It also helps to keep your braids in place and prevent them from unraveling.

FAQs

Q: How long do braids last?

A: Braids can last anywhere from 2-8 weeks, depending on the style and how well you care for them.

Q: Can I wash my braids?

A: Yes, you can wash your scalp and braids. Use a gentle shampoo and warm water, and be sure to dry your hair thoroughly to prevent mildew and odor.

Q: Can I wear my braids in different styles?

A: Yes, you can wear your braids in different styles, such as a ponytail or bun. Just be sure to use gentle hair ties and avoid pulling your braids too tightly.
