How To Cut Hair With Thinning Scissors Video A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to cut hair with thinning scissors video! If you're new to the world of hairdressing or just looking for a new technique to try out, using thinning scissors can be a great way to achieve a unique, textured look. In this article, we'll be breaking down the process of using thinning scissors, step-by-step, so you can master this technique and create beautiful, on-trend hairstyles for your clients.

Description

Thinning scissors, also known as texturizing scissors, are a type of haircutting tool that are designed to remove bulk and create texture in hair. They work by cutting out small sections of hair at a time, rather than slicing through the entire length of the hair like regular scissors. This creates a softer, more natural look that can be especially effective for those with thick or curly hair.

How to Cut Hair with Thinning Scissors Video

If you're looking for a visual guide to cutting hair with thinning scissors, there are plenty of helpful videos available online. Here's a step-by-step breakdown of the process:

Step 1: Preparation

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all your tools and products ready to go. You'll need a pair of thinning scissors, a regular pair of scissors, a comb, and some styling products like hairspray or pomade. It's also a good idea to have a towel or cape handy to protect your client's clothing.

Step 2: Sectioning

Next, use your comb to section off the hair you'll be cutting. This will depend on the style you're going for, but generally, you'll want to start at the back of the head and work your way forward, sectioning off one-inch sections of hair at a time.

Step 3: Cutting with Thinning Scissors

Once you have your section of hair ready, take your thinning scissors and hold them vertically, with the teeth facing the ceiling. Starting at the ends of the hair, make small, diagonal cuts through the hair, working your way up towards the roots. Be sure to cut at an angle and avoid cutting straight across the hair, as this can create uneven layers.

Step 4: Blending with Regular Scissors

After you've cut with the thinning scissors, switch to your regular scissors and use them to blend and smooth out the cut. This will help to create a more natural, textured look and prevent any unevenness or choppy layers.

Step 5: Repeat

Repeat this process on each section of hair until you've achieved the desired look. Remember to take your time and work carefully, as it can be easy to overdo it with thinning scissors and create a choppy, uneven look.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when using thinning scissors:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up, rather than trying to cut too much at once.
  • Be sure to keep the thinning scissors vertical and cut at an angle to avoid creating blunt, uneven layers.
  • Use your regular scissors to blend and smooth out the cut for a more natural look.
  • Practice on mannequin heads or with friends and family members before trying this technique on clients.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about cutting hair with thinning scissors:

Can you use thinning scissors on any hair type?

Thinning scissors are especially effective on thick or curly hair, but can be used on any hair type to create texture and remove bulk.

How often should you use thinning scissors?

This will depend on the individual client and their hair type. It's generally a good idea to use thinning scissors sparingly, as overuse can create a choppy, uneven look. Talk to your client and assess their hair to determine the best approach.

Do thinning scissors damage hair?

When used correctly, thinning scissors should not damage hair. However, it's important to be careful and work slowly, as cutting too aggressively or using the wrong technique can lead to breakage or split ends.

Hair Care

After cutting hair with thinning scissors, it's important to advise your client on how to care for their hair to maintain the look. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using excessive heat styling tools, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner or styling product to help define and enhance the texture.

With these tips and techniques, you should be well on your way to mastering the art of cutting hair with thinning scissors. Remember, practice makes perfect, so don't be afraid to experiment and try new things!
