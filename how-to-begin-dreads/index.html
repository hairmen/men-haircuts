How To Begin Dreads A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 05, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're looking to start your dreadlock journey, you've come to the right place. Whether you're doing it for spiritual or cultural reasons, or simply because you love the look, starting dreadlocks can feel overwhelming. But fear not, this step-by-step guide will help you begin your dreadlock journey with ease.

How to

First things first, decide what method you want to use to start your dreads. There are several methods, including the twist and rip method, backcombing, and freeform. Research each method and determine which one is best for you.

Once you've decided on a method, it's time to prepare your hair. This means washing it thoroughly with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any build-up and oils that could inhibit the locking process.

After washing your hair, it's time to section it. Use a comb to section your hair into the size of dreadlocks you want. It's important to note that the smaller the section, the thinner the dreadlock will be.

Next, it's time to begin the actual process of starting your dreads. This will vary depending on the method you've chosen, but generally involves backcombing, twisting, and palm rolling.

Once you've started your dreads, it's important to maintain them. This means washing them regularly, using a residue-free shampoo, and palm rolling them to keep them tight.

Step-by-Step

Step 1: Choose Your Method

As mentioned earlier, there are several methods for starting dreadlocks. Research each method and determine which one is best for you.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any build-up and oils that could inhibit the locking process.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair into the size of dreadlocks you want.

Step 4: Begin the Locking Process

This will vary depending on the method you've chosen. Backcombing, twisting, and palm rolling are common methods.

Step 5: Maintain Your Dreads

Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo, and palm roll them to keep them tight.

Tips

- Be patient. Starting dreadlocks takes time and dedication.

- Don't over-wash your dreads. This can cause them to become dry and brittle.

- Try not to use too much product. This can cause build-up and inhibit the locking process.

FAQ

How long does it take to start dreadlocks?

The time it takes to start dreadlocks varies depending on the method used and the texture of your hair. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months.

Can I start dreadlocks with short hair?

Yes, you can start dreadlocks with short hair. However, it may take longer for them to mature.

Can I wash my dreads?

Yes, you can and should wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important when starting dreadlocks. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove build-up and oils, and a residue-free shampoo to wash your dreads. Palm roll your dreads regularly to keep them tight and well-maintained.

Starting dreadlocks can be an exciting journey. Follow this step-by-step guide and take care of your hair, and you'll be well on your way to beautiful, healthy dreadlocks.
