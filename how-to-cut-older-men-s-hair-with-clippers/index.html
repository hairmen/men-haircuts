How To Cut Older Men s Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Nov 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you are looking to learn how to cut older men's hair with clippers, you have come to the right place. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to achieve a neat and professional looking haircut for older men using clippers. We will also provide you with tips and tricks to make the process easier and more efficient. Let's get started!

How to:

Step 1: Prepare Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you need to make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a good quality pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, and a hair cape or towel to protect the clothes. It is also important to have a spray bottle filled with water to dampen the hair, making it easier to cut.

Related Keywords: Older Men's Haircut, Clippers, Scissors, Comb, Hair Cape, Spray Bottle

Step 2: Set Up Your Work Area

Find a comfortable and well-lit area to work in. Make sure the person you are cutting sits on a chair with a backrest and armrests. Cover the floor with a sheet or newspaper to make cleaning up easier.

Related Keywords: Work Area, Chair, Backrest, Armrests, Floor, Sheet, Newspaper

Step 3: Decide on the Haircut Style

Before you start cutting, decide on the style of the haircut. Older men usually prefer a classic and simple haircut. You can discuss with the person to find out what they like and what fits their face shape and hair type.

Related Keywords: Haircut Style, Classic, Simple, Face Shape, Hair Type

Step 4: Wash and Dampen the Hair

Wash the hair with shampoo and conditioner, and then dampen it with the spray bottle. Damp hair is easier to cut and will give you a more accurate result.

Related Keywords: Wash Hair, Shampoo, Conditioner, Dampen, Spray Bottle

Step 5: Use the Clippers to Cut the Hair

Start cutting the hair with the clippers. Use a longer guard attachment for the top and sides of the head and a shorter one for the back and sides. Use the comb to guide the clippers and make sure you cut the hair evenly.

Related Keywords: Clippers, Guard Attachment, Top, Sides, Back, Comb, Evenly

Step 6: Blend the Hair

Blend the hair by using a higher guard attachment and cutting the hair in an upward motion to create a natural-looking fade. Use the scissors to trim any longer hair and create a more polished look.

Related Keywords: Blend, Higher Guard Attachment, Upward Motion, Natural-looking Fade, Scissors, Polished Look

Step 7: Clean Up the Neckline and Sideburns

Use the clippers to clean up the neckline and sideburns. Make sure the lines are straight and even. You can also use the scissors to trim any stray hairs.

Related Keywords: Clean Up, Neckline, Sideburns, Straight, Even, Scissors, Stray Hairs

Step 8: Style the Hair

Once you have finished cutting the hair, style it using a small amount of hair gel or pomade. Use your fingers or a comb to create the desired look.

Related Keywords: Style, Hair Gel, Pomade, Fingers, Comb, Desired Look

Tips:

  • Start with a longer guard attachment and work your way down to a shorter one to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Take breaks if the person you are cutting is feeling uncomfortable or needs to stretch.
  • Trim the hair around the ears and the back of the neck carefully to avoid any accidents.

FAQ:

Q: Can I cut older men's hair with scissors instead of clippers?

A: Yes, you can cut older men's hair with scissors, but clippers are faster and more efficient.

Q: How often should older men get a haircut?

A: It depends on the person's hair type and the desired length. Generally, older men should get a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care:

After the haircut, remind the person to apply a moisturizing conditioner and avoid using too much heat on their hair. Encourage them to use a wide-tooth comb to avoid pulling and breaking the hair.

Conclusion:

Cutting older men's hair with clippers is not difficult, but it requires some practice and patience. By following the steps and tips we have provided, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut that will make the person feel confident and refreshed. Remember to take your time, communicate with the person, and enjoy the process!
