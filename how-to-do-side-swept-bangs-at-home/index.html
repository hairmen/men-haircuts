How To Do Side Swept Bangs At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 01, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your plain old hairstyle and looking for a simple way to spice things up? Side swept bangs might be just what you need! With this step-by-step guide, you'll learn how to do side swept bangs at home in no time.

How to Do Side Swept Bangs at Home

Side swept bangs are a great way to add some character to your hairstyle. They can be a little tricky to do at home, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy look in no time.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to do side swept bangs at home:

  1. Wash and blow dry your hair as you normally would.
  2. Section off the front portion of your hair that you want to cut into bangs. Use a comb to create a triangle shape that starts at the top of your head and ends at your temples.
  3. Clip the rest of your hair back so it's out of the way.
  4. Take a small section of hair from the triangle and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and use your other hand to hold the scissors.
  5. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the outer corner and working your way in. This will create a gradual slope that blends into the rest of your hair.
  6. Repeat this process with the rest of the sections in the triangle. Make sure to cut each section at the same angle for a cohesive look.
  7. Once you've finished cutting, use a comb to brush your bangs to the side. You can use a flat iron to straighten them if necessary.
  8. Trim any stray hairs or uneven pieces until you're happy with the length and shape of your bangs.

Tips for Successful Bangs

Here are some tips to keep in mind as you're cutting your side swept bangs:

  • Start with longer bangs and work your way shorter if necessary. It's always easier to cut more hair off than to try and fix a mistake.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut your bangs straight across. This can create an unflattering blunt look. Instead, angle the scissors to create a gradual slope.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting your own hair can be nerve-wracking, but with a steady hand and a clear plan, you can do it!

FAQs About Side Swept Bangs

Here are some common questions people have about side swept bangs:

How long does it take to grow out side swept bangs?

It can take anywhere from three to six months for side swept bangs to grow out. In the meantime, you can use bobby pins or headbands to keep them out of your face.

Should I cut my bangs when my hair is wet or dry?

You should always cut bangs when your hair is dry. Wet hair can be deceptive and make your bangs look shorter than they actually are.

Can I cut my bangs if I have curly hair?

Cutting bangs on curly hair can be more challenging, but it's definitely doable! Just make sure to cut them a little longer than you want them to be, since curly hair tends to shrink when it dries.

Hair Care Tips for Bangs

Once you've cut your side swept bangs, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are some tips:

  • Blow dry your bangs as soon as you get out of the shower to make sure they dry smoothly.
  • Use a flat iron to straighten your bangs if they're looking a little unruly.
  • Wash your bangs separately from the rest of your hair to prevent them from getting greasy too quickly.
  • When sleeping, use a loose hair tie to keep your bangs out of your face. This will prevent them from getting tangled or mashed down.

Now that you know how to do side swept bangs at home, it's time to give it a try! With a little bit of practice and patience, you'll be rocking a fresh new hairstyle in no time.
