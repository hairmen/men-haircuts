How To Take Care Of Guys Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Nov 30, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're a guy with curly hair, you know how hard it can be to maintain a healthy and stylish look. Curly hair tends to be frizzy, dry, and prone to breakage, which can make it difficult to manage. However, with the right care and styling routine, you can keep your curls looking their best. In this article, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about how to take care of guys curly hair.

How to Take Care of Guys Curly Hair

The first step in taking care of your curly hair is to understand your hair type. Curly hair can range from loose waves to tight coils, and each type requires different care. Generally, curly hair tends to be dry and brittle, so you need to focus on moisturizing and nourishing your hair. Here are some tips on how to take care of guys curly hair:

1. Wash your hair less frequently

Curly hair is prone to dryness, so you don't need to wash it as often as straight hair. Shampooing your hair too often can strip away natural oils, leaving your hair dry and frizzy. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, or even less frequently if possible.

2. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates are harsh detergents that can strip away natural oils from your hair. Look for a sulfate-free shampoo that's specially formulated for curly hair. These shampoos are designed to cleanse your hair without stripping away moisture.

3. Condition your hair regularly

Conditioning is essential for curly hair. It helps to moisturize and detangle your curls, making them easier to manage. Use a conditioner that's specially formulated for curly hair, and leave it on for at least 5 minutes to allow it to penetrate your hair.

4. Use a leave-in conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to keep your curls moisturized throughout the day. Look for a lightweight formula that won't weigh down your curls. Apply the conditioner to damp hair, focusing on the ends.

5. Don't towel dry your hair

Towel drying your hair can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze the excess water out of your hair.

6. Use a wide-tooth comb

Curly hair tends to tangle easily, so it's important to use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up, being careful not to pull or tug at your hair.

7. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to take care of guys curly hair:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Wet your hair with warm water and apply a sulfate-free shampoo. Massage your scalp gently with your fingertips, then rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Condition your hair

Apply a conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave the conditioner on for at least 5 minutes, then rinse thoroughly.

Step 3: Apply a leave-in conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to damp hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 4: Detangle your hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.

Step 5: Dry your hair

Gently squeeze the excess water out of your hair using a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt. Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer.

Tips for Keeping Your Curls Healthy

Here are some additional tips for keeping your curls healthy:

1. Protect your hair from the sun

The sun can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. Wear a hat or use a hair product that contains SPF to protect your hair from the sun.

2. Stay hydrated

Drinking plenty of water can help to keep your hair hydrated from the inside out. Aim to drink at least 8 glasses of water per day.

3. Avoid harsh chemicals

Harsh chemicals, such as chlorine and bleach, can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. Avoid swimming in chlorinated pools or use a swimming cap to protect your hair.

FAQs

Q: How often should I trim my hair?

A: It's recommended that you trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I use regular shampoo on my curly hair?

A: Regular shampoo can be too harsh for curly hair, as it can strip away natural oils. Use a sulfate-free shampoo that's specially formulated for curly hair.

Q: Should I brush my curly hair?

A: Brushing curly hair can cause breakage and frizz. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead.
