How To Keep Layered Hair From Flipping Out

Written by Robby Aug 25, 2023 · 4 min read

Layered hair is a popular hairstyle that adds volume and texture to your hair. However, it can be frustrating when your layered hair flips out and ruins your perfect style. In this article, we will discuss how to keep layered hair from flipping out and provide you with helpful tips and tricks to maintain your hairstyle.

Description

Layered hair is a versatile hairstyle that can be styled in many different ways. However, the layers can sometimes flip out and ruin your look. This can be caused by several factors, including the length of your hair, the type of hair products you use, and the way you style your hair. In this article, we will discuss how to keep layered hair from flipping out and provide you with tips to maintain your hairstyle.

How to Keep Layered Hair from Flipping Out

Step 1: Use the Right Hair Products

The type of hair products you use can have a significant impact on your hairstyle. If you have layered hair, it's essential to use products that will help keep your layers in place. Look for products that provide a firm hold, such as hairspray, mousse, or gel. These products can help keep your hair from flipping out and maintain your hairstyle throughout the day.

Step 2: Blow Dry Your Hair Correctly

Blow drying your hair correctly can also help keep your layered hair from flipping out. Start by blow drying your hair in the direction you want it to go. Use a round brush to add volume and texture to your hair. Once your hair is dry, use a cool setting on your blow dryer to set your style.

Step 3: Avoid Using Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools, such as curling irons and straighteners, can damage your hair and cause your layers to flip out. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from damage.

Step 4: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular haircuts are essential for maintaining healthy hair and preventing your layers from flipping out. Visit your hairstylist every six to eight weeks to keep your hair in good condition. A trim can also help remove any split ends and keep your layers looking fresh and healthy.

Tips

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and avoid pulling on your layers.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and cause your layers to flip out.
  • Try using a hair serum to add shine and smoothness to your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

FAQ

Why does my layered hair flip out?

Layered hair can flip out for several reasons, including the length of your hair, the type of hair products you use, and the way you style your hair. Using the right hair products and blow drying your hair correctly can help keep your layers in place.

Can I still use heat styling tools on my layered hair?

While heat styling tools can damage your hair and cause your layers to flip out, you can still use them if you use a heat protectant spray and avoid using them too often.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy hair and preventing your layers from flipping out. Be sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type. Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils. Instead, use lukewarm water to wash and rinse your hair.

Additionally, avoid using too many hair products, as this can weigh down your hair and cause your layers to flip out. Instead, use a small amount of product and distribute it evenly throughout your hair. Finally, be sure to trim your hair regularly to keep your layers looking fresh and healthy.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your layered hair from flipping out and maintain a beautiful hairstyle that you'll love.
