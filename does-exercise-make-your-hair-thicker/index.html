Does Exercise Make Your Hair Thicker

Written by Bardi Sep 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who's trying to achieve thicker and healthier hair? You've probably heard that exercise can help with that. But is there any truth to this claim? In this article, we'll explore whether or not exercise can actually make your hair thicker and what you can do to maximize the benefits of physical activity on your hair health.

How to Exercise for Thicker Hair

The science behind exercise and hair thickness is still unclear, but there are a few ways that physical activity may help promote healthier, thicker hair:

  • Improved blood flow: Exercise increases blood flow and circulation throughout the body, including the scalp. This can help deliver more nutrients and oxygen to hair follicles, which may promote hair growth.
  • Reduced stress: High levels of stress can contribute to hair loss and thinning. Exercise is a great way to reduce stress levels and improve overall mental health, which can indirectly benefit hair health.
  • Better overall health: Exercise is associated with a range of health benefits, from improved cardiovascular health to reduced inflammation. When your body is healthier overall, it may be better equipped to support healthy hair growth.

Step-by-Step Guide to Exercise for Thicker Hair

If you're interested in using exercise to promote thicker hair, here are some tips to get started:

  1. Choose an exercise routine that you enjoy, whether it's running, weightlifting, yoga, or something else entirely. Consistency is key, so choose an activity that you'll stick with over the long term.
  2. Try to exercise for at least 30 minutes a day, 5 days a week. Aim for moderate-intensity exercise, such as brisk walking or cycling, that gets your heart rate up.
  3. Consider incorporating scalp massage into your exercise routine. Use your fingertips to gently massage your scalp in circular motions for a few minutes before or after your workout. This can help stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth.
  4. Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water before, during, and after your workouts. Dehydration can contribute to hair dryness and breakage.

Tips for Exercising for Thicker Hair

Here are a few additional tips to help you maximize the benefits of exercise for hair thickness:

  • Don't overdo it. While exercise can be beneficial for hair health, too much physical activity can actually have the opposite effect. Overtraining can lead to stress, inflammation, and other factors that can contribute to hair loss and thinning.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet. Your hair needs a range of nutrients to stay healthy, including protein, vitamins, and minerals. Make sure you're fueling your body with plenty of nutrient-dense foods to support healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles. Tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails can pull on the hair follicles and contribute to hair loss and thinning. Opt for looser hairstyles or let your hair down while you exercise.

FAQs About Exercise and Hair Thickness

Can exercise make your hair grow faster?

While exercise can improve blood flow and circulation to the scalp, there's no evidence to suggest that it can actually speed up hair growth. Hair growth is largely determined by genetics and hormonal factors, so exercise alone may not be enough to significantly impact hair growth rate.

Can exercise prevent hair loss?

Exercise may help reduce stress levels, which can indirectly benefit hair health and reduce the risk of hair loss. However, if you're experiencing significant hair loss, it's important to speak with your healthcare provider to rule out any underlying medical issues.

What types of exercise are best for hair thickness?

Any type of physical activity that gets your heart rate up and increases blood flow throughout the body can potentially benefit hair thickness. However, there's no one-size-fits-all approach, so choose an exercise routine that you enjoy and can stick with over the long term.

Hair Care Tips for Thicker Hair

In addition to exercise, there are a few other things you can do to promote thicker, healthier hair:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and flat irons too frequently, as they can damage the hair and contribute to breakage.
  • Get regular haircuts to remove split ends and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid harsh chemical treatments like coloring and perming, which can damage the hair and contribute to hair loss and thinning.

While exercise alone may not be enough to significantly impact hair thickness, it can be a helpful component of an overall healthy lifestyle that supports hair health. Incorporate regular physical activity, a healthy diet, and good hair care habits into your routine to promote thicker, healthier hair.
