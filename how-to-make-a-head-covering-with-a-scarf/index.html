How To Make A Head Covering With A Scarf

Written by Christine Aug 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a stylish and practical way to cover your head? One easy solution is to use a scarf! Scarves come in many colors, patterns, and fabrics, so you can choose one that matches your style and needs. In this article, we will teach you how to make a head covering with a scarf in a few simple steps.

Description

A head covering is a piece of fabric that covers your head and hair. It can be worn for religious, cultural, or practical reasons. Scarves are a popular choice for head coverings because they are versatile, affordable, and easy to find. You can wear a scarf as a headband, turban, or hijab, depending on your preference and comfort level.

How to

Follow these steps to make a head covering with a scarf:

Step 1: Choose a scarf

Look for a scarf that is long enough to wrap around your head and tie securely. A rectangular scarf is ideal, but you can also use a square or triangular scarf. Choose a fabric that is lightweight, breathable, and comfortable.

Step 2: Fold the scarf

Fold the scarf in half lengthwise to create a long strip. If the scarf is too wide, you can fold it in half again to make it narrower. Adjust the length and width of the scarf to fit your head size and shape.

Step 3: Place the scarf on your head

Place the scarf on your head, with the folded edge at the back of your neck. The two ends of the scarf should be hanging down on either side of your face. You can adjust the scarf to cover your hairline and ears, depending on your preference and style.

Step 4: Tie the scarf

Tie the two ends of the scarf at the top of your head, above your forehead. You can tie a knot or a bow, depending on the look you want to achieve. Make sure the scarf is snug but not too tight, to avoid discomfort or headache.

Step 5: Tuck the ends

Tuck the loose ends of the scarf under the knot or bow, to create a neat and tidy finish. You can also leave the ends hanging down, for a more casual and relaxed look.

Tips

Here are some tips to make your head covering with a scarf look and feel great:

  • Experiment with different scarf styles, colors, and patterns to find your favorite look.
  • Use bobby pins or hair clips to secure the scarf in place, especially if you have short hair or layers.
  • Wear a cap or a bandana underneath the scarf, for extra coverage and comfort.
  • Accessorize your head covering with earrings, bracelets, or necklaces, to add some sparkle and flair.
  • Practice tying the scarf in different ways, to master the technique and save time in the morning.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about making a head covering with a scarf:

What type of scarf is best for a head covering?

A lightweight, breathable, and stretchy fabric like cotton, silk, or jersey is ideal for a head covering. Avoid fabrics that are too thick, heavy, or slippery, as they may slide off or cause discomfort.

How do I tie a scarf as a turban?

To tie a scarf as a turban, follow these steps:

  1. Place the scarf on your head, with the middle of the scarf at the back of your neck.
  2. Take one end of the scarf and wrap it around your head, crossing it over the other end at the top of your head.
  3. Take the other end of the scarf and wrap it around your head in the opposite direction, crossing it over the first end at the top of your head.
  4. Tuck the ends of the scarf under the loops, or tie them at the back of your head.

Can I wear a scarf as a hijab?

Yes, you can wear a scarf as a hijab, which is a head covering worn by Muslim women for religious reasons. To wear a hijab, you need to cover your hair, neck, and chest, while leaving your face visible. You can use a rectangular scarf or a shawl, and wrap it around your head and shoulders in a way that is modest and comfortable.

Hair Care

Wearing a head covering with a scarf can protect your hair from sun, wind, and pollution, but it can also cause some hair care issues. Here are some tips to take care of your hair while wearing a scarf:

  • Choose a scarf that is clean and free of lint, to avoid transferring dirt and bacteria to your hair.
  • Wash your hair regularly, to prevent sweat, oil, and product buildup from clogging your scalp and hair follicles.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil, to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles or braids, which can pull on your hair and cause breakage or traction alopecia.
  • Massage your scalp regularly, to improve blood circulation and promote hair growth.

By following these tips, you can enjoy wearing a head covering with a scarf while keeping your hair healthy and beautiful.
