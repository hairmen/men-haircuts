How To Blunt Cut My Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 14, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to give your hair a fresh new look without breaking the bank, you might be wondering how to blunt cut your hair at home. Don't worry, it's easier than you think! Blunt cutting is a simple technique that involves cutting your hair straight across, creating a clean and polished look. In this guide, we'll show you how to blunt cut your hair at home, step-by-step.

Description

Blunt cutting is a popular technique for achieving a sleek and modern hairstyle. This technique involves cutting the hair straight across, without any layers or angles. Blunt cutting can be done on any hair length or texture, and is a great way to achieve a clean and polished look. Blunt cutting can also help to give the appearance of thicker hair, as the cut creates a uniform shape.

How to Blunt Cut Your Hair at Home

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start blunt cutting your hair at home, you'll need to gather a few tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips or ties to section your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. It's important to start with clean, dry hair so that you can see the natural shape and texture of your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to section your hair into four equal parts: top, left, right, and back. Clip or tie each section to keep it separate.

Step 4: Cut the Back Section

Starting with the back section, comb your hair straight down and cut it straight across with your scissors. Make sure to cut small sections at a time to ensure an even cut.

Step 5: Cut the Side Sections

Next, move on to the side sections. Comb your hair straight down and cut it straight across, following the line of the back section. Again, make sure to cut small sections at a time.

Step 6: Cut the Top Section

Finally, move on to the top section. Comb your hair straight down and cut it straight across, following the line of the side sections. Once again, make sure to cut small sections at a time.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

Once you've finished cutting your hair, check for evenness. Use your comb to comb through each section, making sure that the hair is cut evenly. If you notice any unevenness, go back and make small adjustments.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with your blunt cut, style your hair as desired. You can keep your hair straight and sleek, or add some texture with waves or curls.

Tips for Blunt Cutting Your Hair at Home

- Always use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean cut
- Cut small sections at a time to ensure an even cut
- Take breaks to rest your hands and prevent fatigue
- Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it can shrink when it dries
- Start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed

FAQs About Blunt Cutting Your Hair at Home

Can I blunt cut my hair if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can blunt cut your hair if you have curly hair. However, it's important to remember that curly hair can shrink when it dries, so be cautious when cutting and start with a longer length.

How often should I blunt cut my hair?

It's recommended to blunt cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Can I blunt cut my own hair if I have long hair?

Yes, you can blunt cut your own hair if you have long hair. However, it may be easier to enlist the help of a friend or family member to ensure an even cut.

Hair Care After Blunt Cutting Your Hair at Home

After blunt cutting your hair at home, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain a healthy and shiny appearance. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage and breakage, and use a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to nourish your hair. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a heat protectant spray when you do. Finally, get regular trims every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.
