How To Keep Men s Long Hair In Place

Written by Oliver Sep 18, 2023 · 6 min read

Men's long hair is becoming increasingly popular, but it can be challenging to keep it in place. Whether you're rocking a man bun, a long ponytail, or simply letting your locks flow freely, keeping your hair under control is essential. In this article, we'll show you how to keep men's long hair in place, with tips and tricks for maintaining your style throughout the day.

Description

Men's long hair requires a specific level of care and attention to keep it looking its best. It needs to be washed, conditioned, and styled regularly to maintain its shape and texture. However, even with the right products and styling techniques, it can be challenging to keep your hair in place. Wind, humidity, and other environmental factors can wreak havoc on your style, leaving you with a frizzy mess instead of the sleek, sophisticated look you were going for.

How to Keep Men's Long Hair in Place

Here are some tips for keeping your men's long hair in place:

1. Use the Right Products

The right hair products can make all the difference when it comes to keeping your hair in place. Look for products that are designed for your hair type and style, such as pomades, waxes, or gels. These products can help hold your hair in place, add shine, and reduce frizz. Be careful not to overdo it, though, as too much product can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.

2. Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow-drying your hair can help set your style and keep it in place. Use a blow dryer on a low or medium heat setting, and direct the air down the shaft of your hair to smooth it and reduce frizz. If you're aiming for a specific style, use a round brush to create volume and shape.

3. Use a Hairspray

Hairspray is a classic styling product that can help keep your hair in place. Look for a hairspray that has a firm hold but doesn't leave your hair feeling crunchy or sticky. Hold the can about 6-8 inches away from your head and spray in short bursts. Don't overdo it, though, as too much hairspray can make your hair look dull and lifeless.

4. Tie Your Hair Up

If you're struggling to keep your hair in place, tying it up may be the solution. A man bun, ponytail, or braids can keep your hair off your face and prevent it from getting tangled or messy. Use a hair tie or scrunchie to secure your hair, and consider adding a few bobby pins for extra security.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to keeping men's long hair in place:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are designed for your hair type and style. Rinse thoroughly and gently towel dry your hair.

Step 2: Apply Styling Products

Apply styling products, such as pomades, waxes, or gels, to your hair. Use a small amount and work it through your hair, focusing on the ends and any areas that tend to get frizzy.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Use a blow dryer on a low or medium heat setting to dry your hair. Direct the air down the shaft of your hair to smooth it and reduce frizz. Use a round brush to create volume and shape if desired.

Step 4: Apply Hairspray

Spray a firm-hold hairspray onto your hair, holding the can about 6-8 inches away from your head. Spray in short bursts, focusing on any areas that tend to get frizzy or flyaway.

Step 5: Tie Your Hair Up

If desired, tie your hair up into a man bun, ponytail, or braids to keep it in place throughout the day. Use a hair tie or scrunchie to secure your hair, and add a few bobby pins for extra security.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for keeping men's long hair in place:

1. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and leave it dry and brittle. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, and use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for your hair type.

2. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Regular use of heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can damage your hair and make it more difficult to keep in place. Use these tools sparingly, and always use a heat protectant spray or serum to minimize damage.

3. Get Regular Trims

Regular haircuts can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can make it more difficult to style. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks, or as needed to maintain your desired length and shape.

FAQ

Q: How do I keep my man bun from falling out?

A: To keep your man bun from falling out, use a hair tie or scrunchie to secure your hair tightly. Twist the hair tie around your hair several times, and tuck any loose ends under the bun. Use bobby pins to secure any loose pieces, and add a spritz of hairspray to hold it in place.

Q: What's the best way to prevent frizz in men's long hair?

A: To prevent frizz in men's long hair, use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for your hair type. Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel after washing, as this can create friction and lead to frizz. Instead, gently pat your hair dry with a soft towel or t-shirt. Use a leave-in conditioner or anti-frizz serum to add moisture and control frizz throughout the day.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for keeping men's long hair in place. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots, being careful not to pull or yank on your hair.

2. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Exposure to the sun's UV rays can damage your hair and make it more difficult to style. Wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner with SPF to protect your hair from the sun.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet is essential for healthy hair. Eat plenty of protein, fruits, and vegetables to provide your hair with the nutrients it needs to grow and stay strong.

4. Get Enough Sleep

Getting enough sleep is essential for healthy hair. Aim for 7-8 hours of sleep per night to give your body the rest it needs to repair and maintain your hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your men's long hair in place and looking its best. With the right products, styling techniques, and hair care practices, you can achieve the sleek, sophisticated look you've been dreaming of.
