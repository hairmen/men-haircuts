Is It Haram To Be A Hairdresser

Written by William Jul 02, 2023 · 4 min read

If you are a Muslim and considering a career as a hairdresser, you may be wondering if it is halal or haram. In Islam, halal refers to actions that are permissible and acceptable, while haram refers to actions that are forbidden and sinful. In this article, we will explore whether being a hairdresser is considered haram in Islam and provide some tips for Muslim hairdressers.

Description

Being a hairdresser involves cutting, styling, and coloring hair, as well as providing other beauty treatments such as manicures, pedicures, and facials. It is a popular career choice for many people, including Muslims, who may enjoy helping others look and feel their best. However, some Muslims may have concerns about whether this profession is in line with Islamic teachings.

How to

There is no clear-cut answer to whether being a hairdresser is haram in Islam. Some scholars argue that it is permissible as long as it does not involve any haram activities such as cutting hair in a style that imitates non-Muslims or using haram products such as alcohol-based hair sprays. Others argue that it is haram because it involves altering one's natural appearance or imitating the practices of non-Muslims.

Step by Step

If you are a Muslim considering a career as a hairdresser, here are some steps you can take to ensure that your work is in line with Islamic teachings:

  • Research the opinions of Islamic scholars on the permissibility of being a hairdresser.
  • Avoid using haram products such as alcohol-based hair sprays or dyes made from animal products.
  • Avoid cutting hair in styles that imitate non-Muslims or are considered immodest.
  • Be honest and transparent with your clients about what you can and cannot do based on your religious beliefs.
  • Consider specializing in modest haircuts or beauty treatments that are in line with Islamic teachings.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for Muslim hairdressers:

  • Pray regularly and seek guidance from Allah in your career decisions.
  • Surround yourself with supportive friends and family who respect your religious beliefs.
  • Consider joining a Muslim professional organization or networking group to connect with other Muslim hairdressers and share tips and advice.
  • Stay up to date on the latest trends and techniques in hairdressing to provide the best possible service to your clients.
  • Remember that being a hairdresser can be a rewarding and fulfilling career that allows you to help others look and feel their best.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about being a hairdresser in Islam:

Is it haram to cut your hair?

No, it is not haram to cut your hair. In fact, many Muslims believe that it is recommended to trim one's hair regularly as a form of hygiene and self-care.

Is it haram to dye your hair?

It depends on the type of dye used. If the dye is made from haram ingredients such as animal products or contains alcohol, then it would be considered haram. However, if the dye is made from halal ingredients and does not cause harm to the body, then it would be permissible.

Is it haram to wear makeup?

No, it is not haram to wear makeup as long as it is done in moderation and does not cause harm to the body. However, Muslim women are encouraged to wear modest clothing and avoid excessive adornment in public.

Hair Care

Whether you are a hairdresser or simply someone who cares about their hair, here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy and beautiful:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools such as curling irons or straighteners too often, as they can damage the hair.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair health.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Consider using natural remedies such as coconut oil or aloe vera to nourish and moisturize your hair.

In conclusion, the question of whether being a hairdresser is haram in Islam is a complex one with no clear answer. However, by following Islamic teachings and taking steps to ensure that your work is in line with your religious beliefs, you can pursue this career path with confidence and integrity.
