Why Would A Squirrel Have Patches Of Fur Missing

Written by Petter Jun 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've ever seen a squirrel with patches of fur missing, it can be quite concerning. What could be causing this issue? In this article, we'll explore the reasons why a squirrel may have patches of fur missing and what you can do to help.

Description: What Do Patches of Missing Fur Look Like?

Patches of missing fur in squirrels can vary in size and location. They may be small, circular areas or larger, irregular shapes. The fur may be completely gone, leaving the skin exposed, or it may be thin and patchy. In some cases, the missing fur may be accompanied by other symptoms, such as redness, swelling, or scabbing.

How to: Identify the Cause of Missing Fur

Identifying the cause of missing fur in squirrels can be tricky, as there are several potential culprits. Here are some common reasons why a squirrel may have patches of fur missing:

Predator Attack

Squirrels are a common prey item for many predators, including birds of prey, cats, and dogs. If a squirrel manages to escape an attack, it may have patches of fur missing where it was grasped by the predator.

Parasites

Squirrels can be infested with various parasites, such as fleas, mites, and lice. These parasites can cause itching, irritation, and hair loss in the affected areas.

Fungal or Bacterial Infection

Squirrels can contract fungal or bacterial infections that affect their skin and fur. These infections can cause patchy hair loss, as well as other symptoms like redness, swelling, and scabbing.

Allergic Reaction

Like humans, squirrels can have allergic reactions to certain substances, such as pollen or certain foods. These reactions can cause skin irritation and hair loss in the affected areas.

Step-by-Step: What to Do if You See a Squirrel with Missing Fur

If you see a squirrel with patches of missing fur, there are a few things you can do:

Observe from a Distance

It's important to give the squirrel space and observe it from a safe distance. This will reduce stress for the squirrel and minimize the risk of injury to yourself.

Take Note of Other Symptoms

Take note of any other symptoms the squirrel may be exhibiting, such as lethargy, difficulty breathing, or abnormal behavior. These symptoms can help you determine the cause of the missing fur.

Contact a Wildlife Rehabilitator

If you're concerned about the squirrel's health, contact a wildlife rehabilitator in your area. They can provide advice on what to do next and may be able to take in the squirrel for treatment.

Tips: How to Prevent Missing Fur in Squirrels

Here are some tips for preventing missing fur in squirrels:

Keep Your Yard Clean

Keeping your yard free of debris and trash can reduce the likelihood of attracting predators and parasites that can harm squirrels.

Provide a Safe Habitat

Providing a safe habitat for squirrels, such as a nest box or tree cavity, can help protect them from predators and other threats.

Avoid Feeding Squirrels Human Food

Feeding squirrels human food can disrupt their natural diet and may cause allergic reactions or other health issues.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Can missing fur in squirrels grow back?

Yes, missing fur in squirrels can grow back over time, depending on the cause of the hair loss.

Do squirrels shed their fur?

Yes, squirrels shed their fur twice a year, in the spring and fall.

Hair Care: How to Help Squirrels with Missing Fur

If you're caring for a squirrel with missing fur, there are a few things you can do to help:

Provide a Nutritious Diet

A well-balanced diet can help promote healthy skin and fur in squirrels.

Keep the Habitat Clean

Keeping the squirrel's habitat clean can help prevent infection and promote healing.

Consult a Veterinarian

If the squirrel's hair loss is due to a medical condition, a veterinarian may be able to provide treatment to help the fur grow back.
