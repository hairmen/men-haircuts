How To Make Curly Hair Straight Permanently At Home

Description

Curly hair can be beautiful, but sometimes you just want to switch it up and have straight hair. While there are many temporary ways to straighten hair, such as flat ironing or blow drying, these methods only last until the next wash. If you're looking for a more permanent solution, you can make curly hair straight permanently at home. This article will guide you through the process, from start to finish.

How to

The process of making curly hair straight permanently at home involves using chemicals to break the bonds in your hair that cause it to curl. This is a process called chemical straightening, which is also known as a relaxer. It's important to note that this process can be damaging to your hair if not done correctly, so it's important to follow the instructions carefully and take the necessary precautions.

Step by step

Before starting the process, make sure you have all the necessary materials, including a relaxer kit, gloves, petroleum jelly, a wide-tooth comb, and a neutralizing shampoo.

  1. Apply petroleum jelly around your hairline to protect your skin.
  2. Divide your hair into four sections using the wide-tooth comb.
  3. Apply the relaxer to each section, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Make sure to follow the instructions carefully, as the timing and application process can vary depending on the kit.
  4. Rinse out the relaxer thoroughly with warm water.
  5. Apply the neutralizing shampoo to your hair, working it in gently from the roots to the ends. Rinse out the shampoo thoroughly with warm water.
  6. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and let it sit for at least 10 minutes. Rinse out the conditioner with cool water.
  7. Style your hair as desired. It's important to note that your hair may be more fragile and prone to breakage after the chemical straightening process, so be gentle when styling.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you make curly hair straight permanently at home:

  • Do a patch test before applying the relaxer to your entire head to avoid an allergic reaction.
  • Follow the instructions carefully and don't leave the relaxer on for longer than recommended.
  • Use a neutralizing shampoo to stop the chemical process and prevent damage to your hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair for at least 48 hours after the process to give your hair time to adjust.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about making curly hair straight permanently at home:

  • Can I do this process on colored hair? It's best to avoid chemical straightening on colored hair, as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • How long will the straightening last? The results can last up to six months, depending on how well you care for your hair.
  • Can I still style my hair after the process? Yes, you can still style your hair as desired, but be gentle and avoid using heat styling tools too often.

Hair Care

After the chemical straightening process, it's important to take extra care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or hair dyes on your hair.

With these tips and steps, you can make curly hair straight permanently at home. Just remember to follow the instructions carefully and take extra care of your hair after the process to keep it healthy and beautiful.
