How Should Thick Wavy Hair Be Cut

Written by Frank Jun 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have thick wavy hair, you know how difficult it can be to find the right haircut. You want a style that enhances your natural waves, but won't leave you with a frizzy mess. In this article, we'll go over how should thick wavy hair be cut, along with some tips and tricks for keeping your locks looking their best.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Thick Wavy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Styling Thick Wavy Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Thick Wavy Hair

How to Cut Thick Wavy Hair

The first step in cutting thick wavy hair is to find a stylist who is experienced in working with this hair type. Look for someone who has a portfolio of clients with similar hair to yours, and who can offer recommendations for styles that will work well with your waves.

When it comes to the actual cut, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Layering is key: Layers can help to reduce bulk and add movement to your hair. Ask your stylist to create layers that start at your chin or below, as this will help to avoid the dreaded "triangle" shape.
  • Keep it long: If you have thick wavy hair, a long cut will help to distribute the weight of your hair evenly, which can make styling easier. Aim for a length that falls between your collarbone and mid-back.
  • Avoid blunt cuts: Blunt cuts can make thick wavy hair look heavy and shapeless. Instead, opt for a cut with textured ends, which will enhance your waves and add dimension to your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting thick wavy hair:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair, and let it air dry until it's about 80% dry.
  2. Using a wide-tooth comb, separate your hair into sections, starting at the nape of your neck and working your way up.
  3. Starting at the back of your head, take a small section of hair and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Point-cut the ends of the hair, using scissors to cut into the hair vertically rather than straight across.
  4. Continue working your way up your head, taking small sections of hair and point-cutting the ends. Be sure to keep the sections even in size to ensure a consistent cut.
  5. Once you've finished cutting, style your hair as desired. You can let it air dry for a more natural look, or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your waves while still keeping frizz at bay.

Tips for Styling Thick Wavy Hair

Now that you've got your cut, here are some tips for styling your thick wavy hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb: A wide-tooth comb will help to detangle your hair without causing breakage, and will also help to distribute product evenly.
  • Invest in a good diffuser: A diffuser attachment for your blow dryer can help to enhance your waves while reducing frizz. Be sure to use it on a low heat setting to avoid damage.
  • Opt for lightweight products: Thick wavy hair doesn't need heavy, greasy products to look great. Look for lightweight styling products that won't weigh your hair down.
  • Embrace your natural texture: Don't fight your natural waves! Instead, embrace them and work with them to create a style that's uniquely you.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting and styling thick wavy hair:

Q: Should I get a straight across cut if I have thick wavy hair?

A: No, a straight across cut can make thick wavy hair look heavy and shapeless. Opt for a cut with textured ends instead.

Q: How often should I get my thick wavy hair cut?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much maintenance you're willing to do. Generally, a trim every 6-8 weeks will help to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I still wear my hair straight if I have thick wavy hair?

A: Absolutely! Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray and a straightening iron with adjustable heat settings to avoid damage.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Wavy Hair

Finally, here are some tips for keeping your thick wavy hair healthy and looking its best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner: Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and frizzy.
  • Avoid over-washing: Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, so aim to wash every 2-3 days.
  • Deep condition regularly: Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools can be damaging to your hair, so try to limit your use of them as much as possible.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be well on your way to rocking the perfect cut for your thick wavy hair!
