Does Trimming Damaged Hair Help It Grow

Written by Robby Jul 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're dealing with damaged hair, you may be wondering if trimming it will help it grow. While it may seem counterintuitive to cut your hair when you're trying to grow it out, there are a few reasons why trimming damaged hair can actually be beneficial. In this article, we'll explore the science behind hair growth, why trimming can help, and some tips for maintaining healthy hair.

  • Why Trimming Can Help
  • How to Properly Trim Damaged Hair

The Science Behind Hair Growth

Hair growth is a complex process that involves a variety of factors, including genetics, hormones, and overall health. Hair follicles go through three main phases: the anagen phase (growth phase), the catagen phase (transition phase), and the telogen phase (resting phase). During the anagen phase, hair grows at a rate of about 1/2 inch per month. This phase can last anywhere from 2 to 7 years, depending on genetics and other factors. After the anagen phase, hair enters the catagen phase, where it transitions to a resting state. Finally, during the telogen phase, the hair falls out and the cycle begins again.

Why Trimming Can Help

While it may seem counterintuitive to cut your hair when you're trying to grow it out, trimming damaged hair can actually help it grow faster and healthier. This is because damaged hair is more prone to breakage and split ends, which can slow down the growth process. By trimming off the damaged ends, you can prevent further breakage and make your hair stronger overall. Additionally, trimming your hair regularly can help to remove any buildup or debris that may be weighing down your hair and preventing it from growing.

How to Properly Trim Damaged Hair

If you're going to trim your damaged hair, it's important to do it properly to avoid causing further damage. Here are some tips:

1. Use the Right Tools

Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. Avoid using regular scissors or kitchen scissors, as these can cause split ends and further damage.

2. Trim Small Sections at a Time

Start by trimming small sections of hair, about 1/4 inch at a time. This will help you to avoid cutting off too much hair at once.

3. Cut at an Angle

Cut your hair at a slight angle, rather than straight across. This will help to prevent split ends and make your hair look more natural.

4. Don't Overdo It

Don't trim your hair too often, as this can cause it to become too short and prevent it from growing. Aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Trimming your hair is just one part of maintaining healthy hair. Here are some other tips:

1. Use the Right Products

Choose hair care products that are designed for your hair type and avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates.

2. Protect Your Hair

Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair. When you do use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

3. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals can help to promote healthy hair growth. Focus on eating plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins.

FAQs

Q: Will trimming my hair make it grow faster?

A: While trimming your hair can help to prevent breakage and split ends, it won't necessarily make your hair grow faster. Hair growth is largely determined by genetics and overall health.

Q: How often should I trim my hair?

A: Aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

Q: Can I trim my hair myself?

A: While it's possible to trim your hair yourself, it's recommended that you visit a professional stylist to ensure that your hair is trimmed properly and evenly.

Conclusion

Trimming damaged hair can be an effective way to promote healthy hair growth. By removing split ends and preventing breakage, you can make your hair stronger and more resilient. Remember to use the right tools and techniques, and to maintain a healthy hair care routine to keep your hair looking its best.
