How Do You Layer Your Own Shoulder Length Hair

Written by Luffy Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Layering your shoulder length hair can give it volume and movement, making it look more lively and interesting. If you don't want to spend money on a professional hair stylist, you can layer your own hair at home. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of layering your own shoulder length hair.

Description

Layering is a technique that involves cutting different lengths of hair to create volume and movement. This technique can be used on any hair length, but it is particularly effective on shoulder length hair. Layered hair can make your hair look thicker, fuller, and more textured. It is also a great way to add shape and dimension to your hair.

How To

Before you start layering your hair, you need to make sure that your hair is clean and dry. You should also have all the necessary tools, including a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. Here's how to layer your own shoulder length hair:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. Use a comb to create a center part, and then divide your hair into two sections. Clip one section out of the way while you work on the other section.

Step 2: Determine The Length Of Your Layers

Determine the length of your layers. If you want shorter layers, start at the top of your head. If you want longer layers, start below your chin. Use your comb to create a section of hair at the length you want your layers to be.

Step 3: Cut Your Layers

Use your scissors to cut the section of hair at the length you want your layers to be. Cut the hair at an angle, so that the shortest layer is at the top and the longest layer is at the bottom. You can create as many layers as you want, but make sure that they blend well with the rest of your hair.

Step 4: Repeat On The Other Side

Repeat the process on the other side of your head. Make sure that the layers on both sides are even, and that they blend well with each other.

Step 5: Blend The Layers

Use your scissors to blend the layers together. This will help to create a more natural look, and will prevent your hair from looking choppy. You can also use your comb to tease your hair, which will add volume and texture.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it look frizzy.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to cut small sections of hair at a time, so that you can control the length and the shape of your layers.
  • Take your time. Layering your hair can be a time-consuming process, but it's worth it if you want to achieve a professional-looking result.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about layering shoulder length hair:

Can I layer my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can layer curly hair. However, you need to be careful not to cut your curls too short, as this can make your hair look frizzy.

How often should I layer my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. As a general rule, you should layer your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and volume.

Can I layer my hair if it's thin?

Yes, layering can actually make thin hair look thicker and more voluminous. However, you should avoid cutting your hair too short, as this can make your hair look even thinner.

Hair Care

Layering your hair can make it look more interesting and lively, but it can also make it more vulnerable to damage. Here are some tips to help you take care of your layered hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and make it look frizzy.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and keep it healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Layering your own shoulder length hair can be a great way to achieve a professional-looking result without spending money on a hair stylist. Just make sure that you have the right tools and take your time, and you'll be able to create beautiful layers that add volume, movement, and texture to your hair.
