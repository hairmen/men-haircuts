How To Cut Your Own Hair Short In Back Long In Front A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle without breaking the bank, cutting your hair short in the back and long in the front might be just the thing. This trendy look is easy to achieve at home, and we've got a step-by-step guide to help you do it yourself. Keep reading for tips, tricks, and everything you need to know to get the perfect cut.

Description

Short in the back, long in the front is a popular hairstyle that's been around for a while. It's also known as an angled bob or a graduated bob, and it's a flattering look that works for a variety of hair types and face shapes. This style is perfect for those who want a low-maintenance, edgy look that's easy to style.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Short in Back Long in Front

Before we get started, it's important to note that cutting your own hair can be tricky, especially if you're not used to it. However, with the right tools and a little patience, you can achieve the perfect cut. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you've got everything you need, it's time to get started. Follow these steps to cut your own hair short in back long in front:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb it out and make sure it's tangle-free.
  2. Section your hair. Use hair ties or clips to separate the front from the back.
  3. Start with the back. Use your clippers or scissors to cut your hair to the desired length. Start at the nape of your neck and work your way up.
  4. Blend the back with the sides. Use your comb and scissors to blend the back with the sides. Make sure there are no harsh lines or uneven spots.
  5. Move on to the front. Unclip the front section and comb it forward. Decide how long you want the front to be and cut it at an angle.
  6. Blend the front with the sides. Use your comb and scissors to blend the front with the sides. Be careful not to cut too much off.
  7. Check for symmetry. Use your mirror to make sure both sides are even.
  8. Style as desired. Use your favorite styling products to create the perfect look.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you get the perfect cut:

  • Start with small cuts. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Be patient. Cutting your own hair can be time-consuming, but it's worth it in the end.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair short in back long in front:

Can I cut my hair short in back long in front if I have curly hair?

Absolutely! This style works for all hair types, including curly hair. Just be sure to cut it when it's dry so you can see how it will look.

Do I need to use clippers?

No, clippers are optional. You can achieve this look with just scissors, but clippers can make it easier to get a straight line in the back.

How do I maintain this style?

Trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the length and shape. You can also use a texturizing spray or mousse to add volume and definition.

Hair Care

Now that you've got your new cut, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause damage.
  • Get a regular trim to keep your hair looking its best.

Cutting your own hair short in back long in front is a fun and easy way to switch up your look. With the right tools and a little patience, you can achieve the perfect cut at home. Follow our step-by-step guide, and don't forget to take care of your hair afterwards. Happy cutting!
