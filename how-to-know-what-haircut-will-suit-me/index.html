How To Know What Haircut Will Suit Me A Guide To Finding Your Perfect Hairstyle

Are you tired of getting haircuts that don't suit you? Do you want to find a hairstyle that flatters your face shape and enhances your features? If so, you've come to the right place. In this article, we will guide you through the process of figuring out what haircut will suit you best. From determining your face shape to understanding your hair texture, we've got you covered.

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step to finding your perfect haircut is determining your face shape. Your face shape plays a significant role in determining which hairstyles will flatter you and which ones won't. There are six basic face shapes:

  • Round
  • Oval
  • Square
  • Heart
  • Diamond
  • Triangle

To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and take a photo of your face head-on. Then, use a ruler to measure the following:

  • Forehead: Measure the widest point of your forehead.
  • Cheekbones: Measure the widest point of your cheekbones.
  • Jawline: Measure the distance from the tip of your chin to the point below your earlobe where your jawbone angles upward.
  • Face length: Measure the distance from the center of your hairline to the tip of your chin.

Once you have these measurements, compare them to the following guidelines:

  • Round: Your face is roughly as wide as it is long, with soft, rounded features.
  • Oval: Your face is longer than it is wide, with a gently rounded hairline and jawline.
  • Square: Your face is roughly as wide as it is long, with a strong, angular jawline.
  • Heart: Your face is wider at the temples and narrows at the jawline, with a pointed chin.
  • Diamond: Your face is widest at the cheekbones and narrows at both the forehead and jawline.
  • Triangle: Your face is wider at the jawline and narrows at the forehead, with a pointed chin.

Knowing your face shape will help you choose a haircut that flatters your features and enhances your natural beauty.

Understanding Your Hair Texture

Another important factor to consider when choosing a haircut is your hair texture. Your hair texture refers to the thickness, coarseness, and curl pattern of your hair. There are three main hair textures:

  • Straight
  • Wavy
  • Curly

Each hair texture requires different cutting techniques and styling products. For example, if you have curly hair, you may want to avoid getting a blunt, straight-across cut, as this can cause your curls to look frizzy and uneven. Instead, opt for layers and texturizing to enhance your natural curl pattern.

Considering Your Lifestyle

When choosing a haircut, it's also important to consider your lifestyle. Do you have a busy schedule and need a low-maintenance style? Or do you have more time to dedicate to styling your hair each day?

If you lead an active lifestyle and don't have a lot of time to spend on your hair, you may want to opt for a short, easy-to-maintain style. On the other hand, if you enjoy experimenting with different hairstyles and have more time to spend on your hair, you may want to consider a longer, more versatile cut.

Identifying Your Hair Type

In addition to hair texture, it's important to identify your hair type when choosing a haircut. Your hair type refers to the amount of oil your scalp produces and the thickness of your individual strands.

There are four main hair types:

  • Straight
  • Wavy
  • Curly
  • Kinky

Kinky hair is the most tightly coiled, while straight hair is the most fine and delicate. Knowing your hair type will help you choose a haircut that works with your hair's natural tendencies.

Choosing Your Hair Length

When it comes to hair length, there are a wide variety of options to choose from. Some people prefer short, cropped styles, while others like to keep their hair long and flowing. There are also medium-length styles that fall somewhere in between.

When choosing your hair length, consider factors such as your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle. For example, if you have a round face shape, you may want to avoid very short haircuts, which can make your face appear even rounder. On the other hand, if you have fine hair, a shorter cut may help your hair look fuller and thicker.

Getting Haircut Ideas

If you're not sure what kind of haircut you want, there are plenty of resources available to help you get inspired. You can browse magazines, websites, and social media to find pictures of hairstyles that you like. You can also ask your hairstylist for recommendations based on your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle.

When looking for haircut ideas, keep in mind that not all hairstyles will work for everyone. It's important to choose a haircut that flatters your unique features and fits your personal style.

Getting a Professional Consultation

When in doubt, it's always a good idea to schedule a consultation with a professional hairstylist. A hairstylist can help you determine your face shape, hair texture, and hair type, and recommend a haircut that will suit you best.

During your consultation, be sure to communicate your preferences and concerns to your stylist. Let them know if you have any specific styling needs, such as a low-maintenance style or a cut that can be worn both curly and straight.

Maintaining Your Haircut

Once you've chosen your perfect haircut, it's important to maintain it properly. This may involve regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and free of split ends. It may also involve using styling products and tools that are appropriate for your hair texture and type.

Be sure to ask your hairstylist for tips on how to maintain your new haircut. They may recommend specific products or styling techniques that will help you keep your hair looking its best.

Tips for Finding Your Perfect Haircut

  • Do your research: Browse magazines, websites, and social media to find haircut ideas that you like.
  • Communicate with your stylist: Let your hairstylist know your preferences and concerns, and ask for their recommendations.
  • Consider your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle: Choose a haircut that flatters your features and fits your personal style.
  • Maintain your haircut: Regular trims and proper styling can help you keep your hair looking healthy and stylish.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I have multiple face shapes?

It's not uncommon to have a combination of face shapes. In this case, focus on the most prominent features of your face to determine your overall face shape.

Can I pull off a certain haircut if I have thin hair?

Yes, there are haircuts that can help make thin hair look thicker and more voluminous. Talk to your hairstylist about options such as layers, texturizing, and shorter cuts.

How often should I get my hair trimmed?

It's generally recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair looking healthy and free of split ends.
