How To Shape Short Natural Hair A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of your short natural hair looking shapeless and unmanageable? Don't worry, shaping your hair is easier than you might think! Here's a step-by-step guide on how to shape short natural hair to achieve the look you desire.

How to Shape Short Natural Hair

Shaping short natural hair involves creating a style that complements your face shape and personal style. The key to shaping natural hair is to start with a good haircut that works with your hair type and texture. Once you have a good foundation, you can use styling techniques to create volume, define curls, and add movement to your hair. Here are some tips to help you shape your short natural hair:

1. Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in shaping your natural hair is to determine your face shape. This will help you choose a haircut that flatters your features and complements your natural texture. There are four basic face shapes: oval, round, square, and heart-shaped. Once you know your face shape, you can work with your stylist to choose a haircut that enhances your best features.

2. Choose the Right Haircut

Choosing the right haircut is essential for shaping short natural hair. A good haircut will work with your hair texture and type to create a style that is easy to maintain and looks great. If you have curly hair, consider a layered cut that will add volume and movement. If you have straight hair, a blunt cut can give you a sleek and polished look. Your stylist can recommend a cut that works best for your hair type and texture.

3. Use Styling Products

Styling products can help you shape your short natural hair by adding volume, defining curls, and smoothing frizz. Look for lightweight products that won't weigh down your hair or leave a greasy residue. A good styling cream or mousse can help you create a natural-looking style that lasts all day. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your curls and add volume.

4. Experiment with Different Styles

The great thing about short natural hair is that it is versatile and easy to style. Experiment with different styles to find the one that works best for you. Try adding braids or twists for a more intricate look. Use a headband or scarf to add a pop of color or pattern to your style. Don't be afraid to try something new!

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to shape short natural hair:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Use a towel to blot your hair dry, but avoid rubbing your hair vigorously as this can cause breakage and frizz.

Step 2: Apply Styling Product

Apply a lightweight styling product to your hair, focusing on the roots and ends. Use a comb or your fingers to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your curls and add volume. Blow dry your hair on a low heat setting, using your fingers to scrunch your hair as you go.

Step 4: Shape Your Hair

Use a comb or your fingers to shape your hair into the desired style. If you want more volume, use a pick to lift your roots and create height. If you want more definition, use a curling iron or flat iron to create curls or waves.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

Finish your style with a light mist of hairspray to hold your hair in place. Use your fingers to fluff your hair and create a natural-looking finish.

Tips for Shaping Short Natural Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you shape your short natural hair:

  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find the one that works best for you.
  • Use styling products that are designed for your hair type and texture.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair or leave a greasy residue.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your curls and add volume.
  • Don't be afraid to try something new!

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my short natural hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and texture. Some people can go a week or more between washes, while others need to wash their hair every other day. Experiment with different washing schedules to find the one that works best for you.

Q: Can I use heat styling tools on my short natural hair?

A: Yes, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage. Use the lowest heat setting possible and avoid using heat styling tools every day.

Q: How can I add volume to my short natural hair?

A: Use a pick to lift your roots and create height. You can also try using a volumizing mousse or styling cream to add body and fullness to your hair.

Hair Care for Short Natural Hair

Here are some tips for caring for short natural hair:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water, which can dry out your hair and scalp.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or relaxers on your hair, which can cause damage and breakage.
  • Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends and maintain a healthy shape.

Shaping short natural hair is all about finding the right haircut, styling products, and techniques that work best for you. With a little practice and experimentation, you can create a style that is uniquely yours and looks great every day.
