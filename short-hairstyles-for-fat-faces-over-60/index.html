Short Hairstyles For Fat Faces Over 60 Tips And Ideas

If you have a round or chubby face, finding the right hairstyle can be challenging. But don't worry, you're not alone! Many women over 60 struggle to find a flattering hairstyle that complements their features. In this blog post, we'll share some tips and ideas for short hairstyles for fat faces over 60.

How to Choose the Right Short Hairstyle for Your Face Shape

The first step in finding the right short hairstyle for your face shape is to determine your face shape. If you have a round face, you should avoid hairstyles that add volume to the sides of your face, as this can make your face appear even rounder. Instead, opt for hairstyles that add height and length to your face.

Some of the best short hairstyles for fat faces over 60 include:

Short Bob

The short bob is a classic hairstyle that looks great on women of all ages. It's also a great option for women with round faces, as it adds length to your face and draws attention to your features. To make the most of this hairstyle, consider adding layers to create volume and movement.

Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a bold and daring hairstyle that can be incredibly flattering for women over 60. It's also a great option for women with round faces, as it creates height and adds definition to your features. To make the most of this hairstyle, consider adding bangs to frame your face.

Layered Crop

The layered crop is a versatile hairstyle that can be customized to suit your face shape and personal style. It's also a great option for women with round faces, as it adds volume and movement to your hair. To make the most of this hairstyle, consider adding layers and texture to create a messy, tousled look.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Short Hair

Styling short hair can be quick and easy, especially if you follow these simple steps:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel dry your hair to remove excess moisture.
  3. Apply a styling product, such as mousse or gel, to your hair.
  4. Use a round brush to blow dry your hair, adding volume and movement.
  5. Finish with a hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips for Maintaining Your Short Hairstyle

Short hair requires regular maintenance to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips to help you maintain your short hairstyle:

  • Visit your stylist every six to eight weeks for regular trims.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools, such as a curling iron or flat iron.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Consider using a dry shampoo to refresh your hair between washes.

FAQs About Short Hairstyles for Fat Faces Over 60

Q: Can I still wear long hair if I have a round face?

A: Yes, you can still wear long hair if you have a round face. However, it's important to choose a hairstyle that adds length to your face, such as a layered cut with side-swept bangs.

Q: What's the best way to style a pixie cut?

A: The best way to style a pixie cut is to use a styling product, such as a wax or pomade, to create texture and movement. You can also use a flat iron to add definition to your hair.

Q: Are there any hairstyles I should avoid if I have a round face?

A: Yes, you should avoid hairstyles that add volume to the sides of your face, such as a blunt bob or a full fringe. These styles can make your face appear even rounder.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Short hair requires different care than long hair. Here are some hair care tips to help you keep your short hair looking its best:

  • Wash your hair every two to three days to avoid stripping away natural oils.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as a blow dryer or flat iron, every day.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

With these tips and ideas, you can find a short hairstyle that flatters your face shape and complements your features. Remember to experiment with different styles and have fun with your hair!
