Do Shaved Hair Really Grow Back Thicker

Written by Daniel Aug 25, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you have ever shaved your hair, you might be familiar with the myth that your hair grows back thicker and darker. But is there any truth to this myth? Let's find out in this blog post.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Shave Hair
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Shaving Hair
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care After Shaving

Description

First of all, let's clarify that shaving your hair does not make it grow back thicker. This is just a myth that has been around for ages, but it's not true. Your hair grows from the hair follicles in your scalp, and shaving only removes the hair that is above the skin's surface. It does not affect the hair follicles or the rate at which your hair grows.

So, if you shave your hair, it will grow back at the same rate and thickness as it did before. However, the hair might feel coarser or stubbly when it first grows back because the hair shafts have been cut at an angle, making them blunt. But this is just a temporary effect and will go away as the hair grows back to its normal length.

How to Shave Hair

Now that we have established that shaving does not affect the thickness of your hair, let's talk about how to shave hair properly. Here is a step-by-step guide:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Trim your hair with scissors or clippers before shaving to make it easier to shave.
  2. Wet your hair with warm water to soften it.
  3. Apply shaving cream or gel to your hair.
  4. Use a razor with a sharp blade and shave in the direction of hair growth.
  5. Rinse your skin with cool water and pat it dry with a towel.
  6. Apply a moisturizer or aftershave lotion to soothe your skin.

Tips for Shaving Hair

Here are some tips to make your shaving experience smoother:

  • Use a shaving cream or gel that is designed for your hair type.
  • Use a razor with a sharp blade to avoid cuts and nicks.
  • Shave in the direction of hair growth to avoid ingrown hairs.
  • Apply a moisturizer or aftershave lotion to soothe your skin.
  • Don't shave too often as it can irritate your skin and cause razor burn.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about shaving:

1. Does shaving make hair grow back thicker?

No, shaving does not affect the thickness of your hair. It will grow back at the same rate and thickness as it did before.

2. How often should I shave?

It depends on your hair growth and personal preference. Some people prefer to shave every day, while others shave once a week or less.

3. Can shaving cause ingrown hairs?

Yes, shaving can cause ingrown hairs if you shave against the direction of hair growth or if you shave too closely.

Hair Care After Shaving

After shaving, it's important to take care of your hair and skin to avoid irritation and ingrown hairs. Here are some tips:

  • Apply a moisturizer or aftershave lotion to soothe your skin.
  • Avoid touching or rubbing your skin too much.
  • Don't wear tight clothes that can irritate your skin.
  • Use a gentle exfoliator to prevent ingrown hairs.
  • Avoid sun exposure and use a sunscreen to protect your skin.

In conclusion, shaving your hair does not make it grow back thicker. It's just a myth that has been around for ages. Shaving only removes the hair that is above the skin's surface and does not affect the hair follicles or the rate at which your hair grows. So, feel free to shave your hair without worrying about it growing back thicker.
