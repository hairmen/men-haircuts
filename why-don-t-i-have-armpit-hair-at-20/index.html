Why Don t I Have Armpit Hair At 20

As a 20-year-old, you may be wondering why you don't have armpit hair yet. It's a common question that many people have asked themselves, and it's nothing to be concerned about. Description

Armpit hair is a natural part of puberty, and it usually starts growing between the ages of 9 and 14. How to

If you're wondering why you don't have armpit hair yet, there are a few things you can do to help determine the cause. First, consider your family history. If your parents or siblings also developed armpit hair later in life, it's possible that genetics are playing a role.

You can also talk to your doctor about your hormone levels. Hormonal imbalances can affect hair growth, and your doctor can perform tests to see if this is the case for you.

Step by Step

If you're concerned about your lack of armpit hair, here are some steps you can take:

  1. Talk to your doctor about your hormone levels.
  2. Consider your family history.
  3. Be patient. Armpit hair can take time to develop.

FAQ

Q: Is it normal to not have armpit hair at 20?

A: Yes, it's normal. Everyone develops at their own pace, and some people may not develop armpit hair until later in life.

Q: Can hormonal imbalances cause a lack of armpit hair?

A: Yes, hormonal imbalances can affect hair growth, including armpit hair.

Q: Should I be concerned if I don't have armpit hair?

A: If you're otherwise healthy, there's no need to be concerned. However, if you're experiencing other symptoms, such as a lack of pubic hair or irregular periods, it's a good idea to talk to your doctor.

  • Trim your armpit hair if it becomes too long.
