How To Fix Face Framing Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have face framing layers that just won't behave? Are they too long, too short, or just not quite right? Don't worry, you're not alone! Face framing layers can be tricky to get just right, but with a little know-how, you can fix them yourself at home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to fix your face framing layers and get the perfect look you're after.

Description

Face framing layers are an essential element of many hairstyles. They're designed to frame the face, highlight your best features, and add volume and movement to your hair. However, if they're not cut or styled correctly, they can be a real headache. Whether your face framing layers are too short, too long, or just not quite right, it's important to address the issue so that you can feel confident and look your best.

How to Fix Face Framing Layers

Step 1: Identify the Problem

The first step in fixing your face framing layers is to identify what's wrong. Are they too short? Too long? Not framing your face the way you want them to? Once you know what the problem is, you can start working on a solution.

Step 2: Decide on the Look You Want

Before you start cutting or styling your hair, it's important to have a clear idea of the look you're after. Do you want your face framing layers to be shorter or longer? Do you want them to frame your face differently? Take some time to look at pictures of hairstyles you like and decide on the look you want to achieve.

Step 3: Trim Your Face Framing Layers

If your face framing layers are too long, you'll need to trim them to the desired length. Start by combing your hair straight down and using sharp scissors to cut the layers to the desired length. It's important to cut small sections at a time, so you don't accidentally cut off too much hair.

Step 4: Add Layers

If your face framing layers are too short or not framing your face the way you want them to, adding layers can help. Use sharp scissors to cut additional layers around your face, being sure to blend them in with the rest of your hair.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once your face framing layers are the right length and shape, it's time to style your hair. You can use a hair straightener or curling iron to add volume and movement, or you can simply blow-dry your hair straight for a sleek, polished look. Experiment with different styles until you find the one that works best for you.

Tips

  • Consider consulting with a professional stylist if you're unsure about how to fix your face framing layers.
  • Invest in quality hair care products that will help keep your hair healthy and looking great.
  • Be patient and take your time when cutting or styling your hair.

FAQ

Can I fix my face framing layers at home?

Yes, you can fix your face framing layers at home with the right tools and techniques. Just be sure to take your time and follow the steps carefully.

How often should I trim my face framing layers?

This will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how quickly your face framing layers start to lose their shape. As a general rule, it's a good idea to trim your face framing layers every 6-8 weeks to keep them looking their best.

What products should I use to keep my hair healthy?

Look for hair care products that are designed to nourish and protect your hair, such as shampoos and conditioners that are free from harsh chemicals, and hair oils or serums that can help to hydrate and smooth your hair.

Hair Care

It's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. This means using quality hair care products, avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling tools, and protecting your hair from the sun and other environmental factors. Be sure to wash and condition your hair regularly, and consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair looking and feeling its best.
