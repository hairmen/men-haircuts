How To Cut Shoulder Length Wavy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to switch up your hairstyle? If you have shoulder length wavy hair, cutting your hair can be a great way to refresh your look. However, cutting your own hair can be intimidating. Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll provide you with a step by step guide on how to cut shoulder length wavy hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Shoulder Length Wavy Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Shoulder Length Wavy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Shoulder Length Wavy Hair

Description

Shoulder length wavy hair can be a great hairstyle for those who want to keep their hair at a manageable length while still having some texture and movement. However, as your hair grows, it can start to lose its shape and become unruly. That's where cutting your hair comes in. Cutting your hair can help to remove split ends and give your hair a fresh, new look.

How to Cut Shoulder Length Wavy Hair

If you're cutting your hair at home, it's important to have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. You may also want to have a spray bottle of water on hand to help dampen your hair.

Step by Step Guide

1. Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help to remove any product buildup and make it easier to work with.

2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles.

3. Separate your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back.

4. Take one section of your hair and comb it straight down. Hold the section between your fingers and cut the ends off with your scissors. Make sure to cut the ends straight across.

5. Repeat this process for each section of your hair.

6. Once you've cut all of your hair, check to make sure that the ends are even. If they're not, trim any uneven pieces until they're even.

7. Use your comb to check for any stray hairs that you may have missed.

8. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Shoulder Length Wavy Hair

- If you're not sure how much hair to cut, start by cutting a small amount and then gradually work your way up.

- Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

- If you have particularly thick hair, you may want to thin it out by using thinning shears.

- If you're not comfortable cutting your hair yourself, consider going to a professional stylist.

FAQs

Q. How often should I cut my hair?

A. It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Q. Should I cut my hair straight across or at an angle?

A. For shoulder length wavy hair, it's best to cut your hair straight across to maintain the length and shape of your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Shoulder Length Wavy Hair

- Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.

- Avoid using heat styling tools, such as straighteners and curling irons, too often as they can damage your hair.

- Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and prevent breakage.

- Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help tame frizz and add shine to your hair.

- Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with these tips and a little bit of practice, you can achieve a fresh new look for your shoulder length wavy hair.
