How To Take Care Of A Synthetic Lace Front Wig Tips And Tricks

Written by Eveline Oct 18, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Wigs have become a popular accessory for many women in recent years, especially for those who want to switch up their look without damaging their natural hair. Synthetic lace front wigs are a great option for those who want a natural-looking wig that is affordable and easy to maintain. However, proper care is essential to ensure that your wig lasts as long as possible. In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks on how to take care of a synthetic lace front wig.

How to Take Care of a Synthetic Lace Front Wig

Synthetic wigs are made of synthetic fibers that require different care than human hair wigs. Here are some tips for taking care of your synthetic lace front wig:

1. Brush your wig regularly

Brushing your wig regularly helps to prevent tangling and prolong its lifespan. Use a wide-tooth comb or a wig brush to gently brush your wig, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Avoid using a regular hairbrush, as it can damage the fibers.

2. Wash your wig after 6-8 wears

Washing your wig too often can damage the fibers, so it is recommended to wash it after 6-8 wears. To wash your wig, fill a sink with cool water and add a small amount of synthetic wig shampoo. Gently swirl the wig in the water for a few minutes, then rinse it thoroughly with cool water.

3. Air dry your wig

After washing, gently blot your wig with a towel to remove excess water, then lay it flat on a towel to air dry. Do not wring or twist the wig, as this can damage the fibers. Avoid using a hairdryer or other heat sources to dry your wig, as this can cause the fibers to melt.

4. Store your wig properly

When you are not wearing your wig, store it on a wig stand or mannequin head to help maintain its shape. Avoid storing your wig in direct sunlight or in humid areas, as this can cause the fibers to deteriorate.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to take care of a synthetic lace front wig:

Step 1: Brush your wig

Use a wide-tooth comb or a wig brush to gently brush your wig, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Step 2: Fill a sink with cool water

Fill a sink with cool water and add a small amount of synthetic wig shampoo.

Step 3: Swirl the wig in the water

Gently swirl the wig in the water for a few minutes, then rinse it thoroughly with cool water.

Step 4: Blot the wig with a towel

After washing, gently blot your wig with a towel to remove excess water.

Step 5: Air dry the wig

Lay the wig flat on a towel to air dry. Avoid using a hairdryer or other heat sources to dry your wig.

Step 6: Store the wig properly

When you are not wearing your wig, store it on a wig stand or mannequin head to help maintain its shape.

Tips for Maintaining Your Wig

Here are some additional tips for maintaining your synthetic lace front wig:

1. Avoid heat sources

Avoid exposing your wig to heat sources, such as hot water, hair dryers, or curling irons. The fibers can melt and become irreparably damaged.

2. Use a wig cap

Wearing a wig cap can help protect your natural hair and prevent tangling.

3. Don't sleep in your wig

Avoid sleeping in your wig, as this can cause tangling and damage to the fibers.

4. Use a detangling spray

If your wig becomes tangled, use a detangling spray to help loosen the knots.

FAQs

Q: Can I use regular shampoo on my synthetic wig?

A: No, regular shampoo can damage the synthetic fibers. Use a synthetic wig shampoo instead.

Q: How often should I wash my synthetic wig?

A: Wash your wig after 6-8 wears, or as needed if it becomes dirty or tangled.

Q: Can I dye my synthetic wig?

A: No, synthetic wigs cannot be dyed. If you want a different color, consider purchasing a new wig.

Hair Care for Synthetic Wigs

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your hair while wearing a synthetic wig:

1. Keep your hair clean

Wash and condition your natural hair regularly to keep it healthy and prevent damage.

2. Moisturize your hair

Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your natural hair moisturized and prevent dryness.

3. Protect your hair

Wear a wig cap to protect your natural hair from tangles and damage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can ensure that your synthetic lace front wig looks great and lasts as long as possible.
