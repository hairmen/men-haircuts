How To Put Layers In Your Own Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 25, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of having the same hairstyle for years? Adding layers is an easy way to switch up your look and add dimension to your short hair. But going to the salon can be expensive, and not everyone has the time to schedule an appointment. Fortunately, putting layers in your own short hair is easier than you might think. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a layered look at home.

Table of Contents

  • Materials Needed
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Blending Your Layers
  • Finishing Touches

Materials Needed

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools on hand:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle of water

These materials are essential to help you achieve a precise cut and prevent split ends.

Prepping Your Hair

Before cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. If your hair is wet, it can be difficult to achieve an accurate cut. Use a comb to detangle any knots or tangles, and then section your hair into four parts. Comb each section thoroughly and make sure they are even in length.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is crucial to ensure that each layer is cut to the same length. Start by dividing your hair down the middle, from your forehead to your nape. Then, make a part from ear to ear, creating four sections. Use hair clips to secure each section and keep them out of the way.

Cutting Your Hair

Now it's time to start cutting your hair. Begin with the front section, taking a small section from the middle and combing it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. Repeat this process for each section, working towards the back of your head.

Remember to cut slowly and take small sections at a time. You can always take more hair off, but it's difficult to add it back once it's been cut.

Blending Your Layers

After you've cut your hair, it's time to blend your layers. Comb each section of hair straight up and cut into the ends to create a softer, more blended look. Take care not to cut too much or you may end up with uneven layers.

Finishing Touches

Once you've finished cutting and blending your layers, it's time to add some finishing touches. Use your scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven ends. You can also use a razor to create a more textured look.

Tips for Success

  • Take your time and cut slowly
  • Work with small sections of hair at a time
  • Always use sharp scissors to prevent split ends
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as it can be difficult to fix mistakes

FAQs

Can I put layers in my own short hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can still add layers to curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when dry, so cut less than you think you need.

How often should I cut my layered short hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, make sure to take good care of it to prevent damage and maintain healthy hair. Use a nourishing hair mask once a week to keep your hair soft and shiny. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and always use a heat protectant spray when styling.

Now that you know how to put layers in your own short hair, you can switch up your look whenever you want. Just remember to take your time and follow these steps carefully to achieve the best results.
