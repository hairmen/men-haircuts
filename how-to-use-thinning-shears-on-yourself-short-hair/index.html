How To Use Thinning Shears On Yourself Short Hair

Written by Aprilia Jul 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning shears are an essential tool for people with thick hair or those who want to add texture and volume to their hair. If you have short hair and want to use thinning shears on yourself, it can be a bit tricky, but with the right technique and tools, it is doable. In this article, we will show you step by step how to use thinning shears on yourself short hair.

Description

Thinning shears are scissors with teeth that are used to remove bulk from hair without changing the length. They work by cutting some hair strands while leaving others uncut, resulting in a softer, more natural look. They are commonly used on thick, curly, or coarse hair to reduce weight and add texture.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Yourself Short Hair

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair and Tools

Before using thinning shears, you need to prepare your hair and tools. Shampoo and condition your hair as usual, and let it dry completely. Comb your hair to remove any tangles and knots. You will also need a pair of thinning shears, a regular pair of scissors, and hair clips to section your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. You can start with the bottom layer and work your way up. Leave a small section of hair at the bottom to use as a guide.

Step 3: Use Regular Scissors to Cut the Guide

Use regular scissors to cut the guide section to the desired length. This will help you determine how much hair you want to thin out.

Step 4: Hold the Thinning Shears Properly

Hold the thinning shears with the teeth pointing down and the blades pointing up. Use your dominant hand to hold the shears and your non-dominant hand to hold the hair.

Step 5: Use Thinning Shears to Cut the Hair

Starting at the ends of the hair, use the thinning shears to cut in a diagonal motion. Make sure to cut only a small section of hair at a time and avoid cutting too close to the scalp. Repeat this process on each section of hair until you achieve the desired look.

Step 6: Blend the Hair

After thinning out your hair, use the regular scissors to blend the hair and create a seamless look. This will help to avoid any unevenness or choppy layers.

Tips

  • Use a mirror to see the back of your head while cutting.
  • Start with small sections of hair and gradually increase the amount of hair you thin out.
  • Don't thin out your hair too much, as it can result in wispy ends.
  • Practice on a small section of hair before moving on to the rest of your hair.

FAQ

  • Can I use thinning shears on wet hair?

    • It is not recommended to use thinning shears on wet hair as it can result in uneven cutting and damage to the hair.

  • Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

    • Thinning shears are best used on thick, curly, or coarse hair to reduce weight and add texture.

  • How often should I use thinning shears on my hair?

    • It depends on your hair type and the desired look. It is recommended to use thinning shears every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care

After using thinning shears on your hair, it is important to take good care of it. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and use a heat protectant when necessary. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain the shape.

Using thinning shears on yourself short hair can be a bit challenging, but with patience and practice, you can achieve a gorgeous, textured look. Follow these steps and tips to get the best results and take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and beautiful.
