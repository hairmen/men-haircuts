How To Wear Headscarves With Short Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Daniel Nov 13, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of wearing the same old hairstyles with your short hair? Do you want to add some variety to your look? If so, headscarves are a great accessory to try out! In this article, we will show you how to wear headscarves with short hair in different styles and offer some tips for caring for your hair while wearing them. Let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  1. How to Wear Headscarves with Short Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Wearing Headscarves with Short Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Tips

How to Wear Headscarves with Short Hair

Headscarves can be worn in many different ways, depending on the style you want to achieve. Here are some popular styles:

1. The Basic Wrap

This is the simplest way to wear a headscarf. Simply fold the scarf into a triangle, place the longest edge at the nape of your neck, and tie the two ends at the top of your head. You can leave the knot on top of your head or tuck it under the scarf for a cleaner look.

2. The Headband

If you want to keep your hair out of your face, the headband style is perfect for you. Fold the scarf into a thin strip, place it at the top of your forehead, and tie a knot at the nape of your neck. You can leave the ends of the scarf hanging or tuck them under the knot.

3. The Turban

The turban style is perfect for a bohemian or vintage look. Fold the scarf into a triangle, place the longest edge at the nape of your neck, and bring the two ends to the top of your head. Tie a knot in the center of your forehead and tuck the ends of the scarf under the knot. You can also leave the ends hanging for a more casual look.

4. The Top Knot

If you want to add some volume to your hair, the top knot style is perfect for you. Tie your hair into a high ponytail, fold the scarf into a thin strip, and tie it around the base of the ponytail. Then, wrap the scarf around the ponytail and tie a knot at the top. You can leave the knot on top of your head or tuck it under the scarf.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for the basic wrap style:

  1. Fold the scarf into a triangle.
  2. Place the longest edge at the nape of your neck.
  3. Tie the two ends at the top of your head.
  4. Adjust the scarf to your desired tightness and style.

For the headband style:

  1. Fold the scarf into a thin strip.
  2. Place it at the top of your forehead.
  3. Tie a knot at the nape of your neck.
  4. Adjust the scarf to your desired tightness and style.

For the turban style:

  1. Fold the scarf into a triangle.
  2. Place the longest edge at the nape of your neck.
  3. Bring the two ends to the top of your head.
  4. Tie a knot in the center of your forehead.
  5. Tuck the ends of the scarf under the knot.
  6. Adjust the scarf to your desired tightness and style.

For the top knot style:

  1. Tie your hair into a high ponytail.
  2. Fold the scarf into a thin strip.
  3. Tie the scarf around the base of the ponytail.
  4. Wrap the scarf around the ponytail.
  5. Tie a knot at the top.
  6. Adjust the scarf to your desired tightness and style.

Tips for Wearing Headscarves with Short Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when wearing headscarves with short hair:

  • Choose a scarf made of soft material to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.
  • Match the color of the scarf to your outfit or accessories.
  • Use bobby pins to secure the scarf in place.
  • Wash your scarf regularly to keep it clean and fresh.

FAQs

Can I wear a headscarf with very short hair?

Yes, you can! The headband style works well with very short hair, as it keeps the hair out of your face.

Will wearing a headscarf damage my hair?

No, as long as you choose a scarf made of soft material and do not tie it too tightly, wearing a headscarf should not damage your hair.

Can I wear a headscarf to work?

It depends on your workplace dress code. If headscarves are not allowed, you may want to save them for weekends or casual outings.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips for caring for your hair while wearing headscarves:

  • Avoid tying the scarf too tightly, as this can cause hair breakage.
  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

With these tips and styles in mind, you can confidently wear headscarves with short hair and add some variety to your look. Have fun experimenting!
