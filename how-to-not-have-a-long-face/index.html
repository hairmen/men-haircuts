How To Not Have A Long Face Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Sep 23, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have a long face, you may feel self-conscious about your appearance. However, there are several ways to enhance your features and make your face appear more balanced. In this blog post, we'll explore some tips and tricks for how to not have a long face.

Table of Contents

  • How to Not Have a Long Face
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Long Faces

How to Not Have a Long Face

Before we dive into the specifics, it's important to understand what causes a long face. A long face is typically characterized by a high forehead, narrow chin, and elongated features. While there's no way to change the shape of your face, there are several things you can do to enhance your features and create a more balanced look.

1. Contouring

Contouring is a makeup technique that involves using darker shades to create the illusion of shadows and depth. By contouring your cheekbones, jawline, and temples, you can create a more defined, sculpted look that can help balance out a long face.

2. Hairstyles

Your hairstyle can have a big impact on the appearance of your face. If you have a long face, consider opting for a hairstyle with layers or volume around the chin and cheekbones. Avoid hairstyles that are too flat or sleek, as these can make your face appear even longer.

3. Eyebrows

Your eyebrows can also play a role in the appearance of your face. If you have a long face, consider shaping your eyebrows to create a more horizontal shape. This can help balance out your features and make your face appear less elongated.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for how to not have a long face:

1. Start with a clean, moisturized face.

Before applying makeup, make sure your face is clean and moisturized. This will help your makeup go on smoothly and last longer.

2. Apply foundation.

Apply foundation to even out your skin tone and create a smooth base for your makeup.

3. Contour your cheekbones, jawline, and temples.

Using a contour brush and a matte bronzer, contour your cheekbones, jawline, and temples to create shadows and depth. Blend well to create a natural look.

4. Fill in your eyebrows.

Using an eyebrow pencil or powder, fill in your eyebrows to create a more horizontal shape. This can help balance out a long face.

5. Apply blush.

Apply blush to the apples of your cheeks to add a pop of color and create a more youthful, radiant look.

6. Apply mascara and eyeliner.

Apply mascara to your lashes to define and lengthen them. You can also apply eyeliner to your upper lash line to create a more dramatic look.

7. Style your hair.

Opt for hairstyles with layers or volume around the chin and cheekbones to help balance out a long face. Avoid hairstyles that are too flat or sleek.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for how to not have a long face:

1. Wear statement earrings.

Statement earrings can draw attention away from a long face and add a pop of color and sparkle to your look.

2. Experiment with different lip colors.

Try different lip colors to find shades that complement your skin tone and enhance your features.

3. Practice good posture.

Standing up straight can help elongate your neck and create a more balanced look.

FAQs

Q: Can I change the shape of my face?

A: No, you can't change the underlying bone structure of your face. However, you can enhance your features and create the illusion of a more balanced look.

Q: What kind of hairstyles should I avoid if I have a long face?

A: Avoid hairstyles that are too flat or sleek, as these can make your face appear even longer. Instead, opt for hairstyles with layers or volume around the chin and cheekbones.

Hair Care for Long Faces

If you have a long face, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips for hair care:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo.

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Opt for a sulfate-free shampoo to keep your hair looking healthy and shiny.

2. Condition regularly.

Conditioning can help keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage. Use a conditioner that's designed for your hair type and apply it from mid-length to the ends.

3. Protect your hair from heat damage.

Heat styling tools can damage your hair and make it look dry and frizzy. Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools, and avoid using them too frequently.

By following these tips and tricks, you can enhance your features and create a more balanced look. Remember, confidence is key – embrace your unique features and rock them with pride!
