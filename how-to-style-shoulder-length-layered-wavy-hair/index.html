How to Style Shoulder Length Layered Wavy Hair

Shoulder length layered wavy hair is one of the most versatile hair types out there. It can be styled in countless ways, from sleek and straight to voluminous and bouncy. Here are some of the most popular ways to style your hair:

1. Beachy Waves

Beachy waves are a classic style that works well on shoulder length layered wavy hair. To achieve this look, you can use a curling iron or a flat iron to create loose waves. Alternatively, you can braid your hair and leave it overnight for a more natural-looking wave.

2. Sleek and Straight

If you want a more polished look, you can straighten your hair using a flat iron. This style works well for both casual and formal occasions.

3. Half Up Half Down

A half up half down hairstyle is a great way to show off your wavy locks while keeping your hair out of your face. You can add some braids or twists to make the style more interesting.

4. Messy Bun

A messy bun is a quick and easy hairstyle that you can wear on a lazy day. To create this look, gather your hair into a high ponytail and twist it into a bun. Leave some strands loose to create a messy look.

5. Side Braid

A side braid is a cute and romantic hairstyle that works well for both casual and formal events. You can create a fishtail braid or a French braid depending on your preference.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to create some of the hairstyles we mentioned above:

Beachy Waves

  1. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair.
  2. Section your hair and curl it using a curling iron or a flat iron.
  3. Apply a texturizing spray to add volume and hold.

Sleek and Straight

  1. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair.
  2. Section your hair and straighten it using a flat iron.
  3. Apply a shine serum to add some extra shine.

Half Up Half Down

  1. Take the top section of your hair and tie it into a ponytail.
  2. Twist the ponytail into a bun and secure it with bobby pins.
  3. Leave some strands loose to frame your face.

Messy Bun

  1. Gather your hair into a high ponytail.
  2. Twist the ponytail into a bun and secure it with bobby pins.
  3. Pull out some strands to create a messy look.

Side Braid

  1. Part your hair on the side.
  2. Take a section of hair from the front and divide it into three strands.
  3. Create a French braid or a fishtail braid.
  4. Secure the end of the braid with a hair tie.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you style your shoulder length layered wavy hair:

  • Always use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Apply a texturizing spray to add volume and hold.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find what works best for you.
  • Don't overwash your hair as it can strip away natural oils.

FAQs

How often should I wash my shoulder length layered wavy hair?

You should aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days. However, this can vary depending on your hair type and lifestyle.

What is the best way to detangle my hair?

Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair from the ends to the roots.

Can I use hot tools on my hair?

Yes, you can use hot tools on your hair, but make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

Hair Care Routine

Here is a simple hair care routine to keep your shoulder length layered wavy hair healthy and beautiful:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you can easily style your shoulder length layered wavy hair and keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
