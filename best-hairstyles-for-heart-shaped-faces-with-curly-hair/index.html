Best Hairstyles For Heart Shaped Faces With Curly Hair

Written by Oliver Jul 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have a heart shaped face and curly hair, you may be wondering what the best hairstyles are for your unique features. Luckily, there are plenty of options that can flatter your face shape and showcase your gorgeous curls. In this article, we'll go over some of the best hairstyles for heart shaped faces with curly hair, as well as some tips for caring for your curls.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Heart shaped faces are characterized by a wider forehead and a narrower chin. If you have curly hair, you'll want to choose a hairstyle that balances out the proportions of your face and complements your curls. Some of the best hairstyles for heart shaped faces with curly hair include:

Long Layers

Long layers can help balance out a heart shaped face by adding volume to the bottom half of your hair. This can help draw attention away from your forehead and towards your curls.

Side Swept Bangs

Side swept bangs can also be a great option for heart shaped faces with curly hair. They can help soften the forehead and create a more balanced look.

Bob

A layered bob can be a great choice for heart shaped faces with curly hair. It can help add volume to the bottom half of your hair while also framing your face in a flattering way.

Half Up Half Down

A half up half down hairstyle can be a great choice if you want to show off your curls while also keeping your hair out of your face. It can also help balance out your face shape by adding volume to the top half of your hair.

How To

To achieve these hairstyles, you'll want to start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are designed for curly hair. You may also want to use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls and reduce frizz.

From there, you can follow these steps for each hairstyle:

Long Layers

  1. Start by blow-drying your hair with a diffuser attachment to enhance your curls.
  2. Use a curling iron to touch up any areas that need more definition.
  3. Ask your stylist to add long layers to your hair, focusing on the bottom half of your hair.

Side Swept Bangs

  1. Blow-dry your hair with a diffuser attachment.
  2. Use a curling iron to touch up any areas that need more definition.
  3. Ask your stylist to cut side swept bangs that start at the top of your forehead and angle down towards the side of your face.

Bob

  1. Blow-dry your hair with a diffuser attachment.
  2. Use a curling iron to touch up any areas that need more definition.
  3. Ask your stylist to cut a layered bob that hits at the collarbone.

Half Up Half Down

  1. Start by blow-drying your hair with a diffuser attachment.
  2. Use a curling iron to touch up any areas that need more definition.
  3. Section off the top half of your hair and secure it with a hair tie or clip.
  4. Leave the bottom half of your hair down to showcase your curls.

Step By Step

Here's a step-by-step guide for achieving a long layered look:

  1. Wash and condition your hair with products designed for curly hair.
  2. Blow-dry your hair with a diffuser attachment.
  3. Use a curling iron to touch up any areas that need more definition.
  4. Ask your stylist to add long layers to the bottom half of your hair.
  5. Use a styling cream or mousse to enhance your curls and reduce frizz.

Tips

Here are some tips for caring for your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls when they're wet.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce frizz and protect your curls while you sleep.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hairstyles for heart shaped faces with curly hair:

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a heart shaped face with curly hair?

You should generally avoid hairstyles that add volume to the top half of your hair, as this can make your forehead appear even wider. Additionally, you may want to avoid hairstyles that are too short, as they can make your face look longer.

Can I wear my hair straight if I have a heart shaped face with curly hair?

Of course! While curly hair can be a great way to balance out a heart shaped face, you can also wear your hair straight if you prefer. Just be sure to use a heat protectant and avoid heat styling tools too often to prevent damage.

Hair Care

Caring for curly hair can be a bit tricky, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your curls looking healthy and gorgeous. Here are some tips for caring for your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls when they're wet.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce frizz and protect your curls while you sleep.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.

With these tips and hairstyles, you can show off your heart shaped face and gorgeous curls with confidence.
