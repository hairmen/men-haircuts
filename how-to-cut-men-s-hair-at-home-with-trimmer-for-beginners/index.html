How To Cut Men s Hair At Home With Trimmer For Beginners

Are you tired of spending money at the barbershop every time you need a haircut? Learning how to cut men's hair at home with a trimmer can save you time and money. Plus, it's a useful skill to have in case of emergency situations like a pandemic. This guide will teach you how to cut men's hair at home with a trimmer for beginners.

How to Cut Men's Hair at Home with Trimmer for Beginners

Before we begin, you'll need a few things:

  • Trimmer with various length guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror
  • Towel or cape to cover the shoulders

Step 1: Wash and Dry Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair. Wet hair is harder to cut and can stick to the skin, making it difficult to trim evenly. Use a towel or cape to cover the shoulders to prevent hair from getting on clothes.

Step 2: Choose the Right Guard

Choose the right length guard for the desired haircut. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard if necessary. It's always better to start longer and trim more if needed than to cut too much at once.

Step 3: Trim the Sides and Back First

Start trimming the sides and back of the hair first. Use the longest guard and run it against the hair growth direction. Move the trimmer in an upward motion, slowly trimming the hair. Repeat until the desired length is achieved.

Step 4: Trim the Top

Trim the top of the hair with scissors or the trimmer, depending on the desired style. Use a comb to lift the hair and trim the excess hair with scissors or trimmer without a guard.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Blend the hair by adjusting the guard length and trimming the hair around the sides and back of the head. Work in sections and use the shortest guard to trim around the neck and ears.

Step 6: Clean Up the Neckline

Use the trimmer without a guard to clean up the neckline. Start at the center and move outward to create a straight line. Use a mirror to check the back of the head.

Tips for Cutting Men's Hair at Home with Trimmer

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard if necessary.
  • Trim hair when it's dry, as it's easier to see the length and texture.
  • Use hair clips to separate hair into sections for easier trimming.
  • Take breaks and step back to evaluate the progress.
  • Be patient and go slow to avoid mistakes.

FAQs

Can I Cut My Own Hair with a Trimmer?

Yes, you can cut your own hair with a trimmer. Follow the steps in this guide and use the right guard for the desired length.

What's the Best Trimmer for Cutting Men's Hair at Home?

The best trimmer for cutting men's hair at home is one with various length guards and sharp blades. Look for a trimmer with good reviews and a warranty.

Hair Care after Cutting Men's Hair at Home with Trimmer

After cutting men's hair at home with a trimmer, it's important to wash the hair to remove any loose hairs. Apply a conditioner to make the hair soft and shiny. Trim the hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the desired length.

Cutting men's hair at home with a trimmer can save you time and money. Follow the steps in this guide and use the right tools to achieve a professional-looking haircut. Remember to be patient and go slow to avoid mistakes. Happy trimming!
