How Can I Cut My Long Hair By Myself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Oct 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune on haircuts? Or maybe you're just feeling adventurous and want to change up your look. Whatever the reason may be, cutting your own hair can be a daunting task. But fear not, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve salon-worthy results from the comfort of your own home. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to cut your long hair by yourself.

Tools Needed

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather the necessary tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Towel or cape to protect your clothes

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can result in uneven cuts and split ends.

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Comb it out to remove any tangles or knots. It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is.

Next, decide on the length you want to cut your hair. It's a good idea to start with a conservative length, as you can always go shorter later. Use your fingers to measure the length you want to cut and mark it with a clip or elastic band.

Sectioning Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections to make it easier to cut. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then from ear to ear. Secure each section with a clip or elastic band.

Working with one section at a time, comb through your hair and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Use your scissors to cut straight across, following the guide you set earlier. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as it's easier to take off more hair later than to add it back.

Cutting Your Hair

Continue cutting each section of your hair, working from the back to the front. Use a mirror to check the length and make sure the cuts are even on both sides. It's better to take your time and make small cuts than to rush and end up with an uneven or undesired result.

Layering Your Hair

If you want to add layers to your hair, start by taking a small section from the top of your head. Hold it straight up and cut into it at a 45-degree angle. Continue cutting small sections at an angle until you reach the desired amount of layers.

Trimming Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's time to trim any uneven ends. Take small sections of hair and twist them together. Use your scissors to trim any split ends that stick out.

Cleaning Up

After you've finished cutting and trimming your hair, it's important to clean up any loose hair. Use a vacuum or broom to sweep up any hair on the floor, or do the cutting outside to make cleanup easier.

Styling Your Hair

Once you've cut your hair, you may want to style it. Use a blow dryer and round brush to add volume and shape to your hair. You can also use a straightener or curling iron to create different styles.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up gradually
  • Use a mirror to check your progress and make sure your cuts are even
  • Don't cut too much at once, as it's easier to take off more hair than to add it back
  • Take breaks if you need to, as cutting your own hair can be tiring
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it

FAQs

Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

While you can technically cut your hair with regular scissors, it's best to use hair cutting scissors. Hair cutting scissors are designed to be sharper and more precise, resulting in a cleaner cut.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth. However, the frequency of haircuts may vary depending on the individual's hair type and desired style.

Is it easy to cut my own hair?

Cutting your own hair can be challenging, especially if you're inexperienced. It's important to take your time and be patient, as rushing can result in uneven cuts.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and appearance. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat when styling. Regular trims can also help prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

Cutting your own hair can be a rewarding experience, but it's important to take your time and be patient. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve salon-worthy results from the comfort of your own home. Happy cutting!
