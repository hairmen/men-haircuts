How To Properly Pick Your Hair A Step By Step Guide

As someone with relaxed hair, picking your hair is an essential part of your hair care routine. It helps to detangle your hair, prevent breakage, and promote healthy hair growth. However, picking your hair incorrectly can lead to damage and breakage. In this article, we'll guide you through how to properly pick your hair, including tips, frequently asked questions, and hair care advice.

How to Properly Pick Your Hair

Picking your hair involves using a comb or pick to detangle your hair and remove any knots or tangles. It's important to do this correctly to avoid damaging your hair. Here's how to properly pick your hair:

1. Start with clean, conditioned hair

It's best to pick your hair when it's clean and conditioned. This will make it easier to detangle and prevent breakage. Apply a conditioner or detangling spray to your hair and let it sit for a few minutes before picking.

2. Choose the right tool

Choose a comb or pick with wide teeth to avoid pulling or snagging your hair. Avoid using brushes or combs with small teeth, as they can be too harsh on your hair.

3. Divide your hair into sections

Divide your hair into small sections using hair clips or bands. This will make it easier to pick through your hair and ensure that you don't miss any tangles.

4. Start at the ends

Start picking your hair at the ends and work your way up to the roots. This will help to prevent breakage and make it easier to detangle any knots or tangles.

5. Be gentle

Be gentle when picking your hair. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause breakage. Instead, use a gentle motion to detangle your hair.

Tips for Picking Your Hair

Here are some tips to help you pick your hair correctly:

1. Use a leave-in conditioner.

Using a leave-in conditioner can help to detangle your hair and make it easier to pick.

2. Don't pick your hair when it's wet.

Wet hair is more vulnerable to breakage, so it's best to wait until your hair is dry before picking.

3. Use a detangling spray.

A detangling spray can help to make it easier to pick through your hair.

4. Use a wide-tooth comb.

Using a wide-tooth comb can help to prevent breakage and make it easier to detangle your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How often should I pick my hair?

You should pick your hair at least once a week, but it's best to do it every other day to prevent tangles and knots.

2. Can I pick my hair when it's wet?

No, it's best to wait until your hair is dry before picking. Wet hair is more vulnerable to breakage.

3. Can I use a brush to pick my hair?

No, it's best to use a wide-tooth comb or pick to prevent breakage.

Hair Care Advice

Picking your hair is just one part of your hair care routine. Here are some other tips to help you maintain healthy hair:

1. Get regular trims.

Getting regular trims can help to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

2. Use a deep conditioner.

Using a deep conditioner once a week can help to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

3. Protect your hair at night.

Wear a satin or silk bonnet or scarf at night to protect your hair from breakage and damage.

4. Avoid using heat on your hair.

Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as they can cause damage and breakage.

By following these tips and properly picking your hair, you can maintain healthy, beautiful, and strong hair.
