What Happens When You Cut Damaged Hair

Written by Aprilia Jul 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you struggling with damaged hair and wondering if cutting it is the right solution? Cutting your hair can be a scary decision, but it can also be the best thing you can do for your hair's health. In this blog post, we will explore what happens when you cut damaged hair and provide you with some tips on how to care for your hair after the cut.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Damaged Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Damaged Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Damaged Hair

Description

Damaged hair can be caused by various factors such as heat styling, chemical treatments, and environmental factors like pollution. Damaged hair can become brittle, dry, and frizzy, making it difficult to manage. Cutting damaged hair can help to remove split ends and breakage, resulting in healthier, more manageable hair.

How to Cut Damaged Hair

Cutting damaged hair is not rocket science, but it is essential to do it correctly to avoid making the problem worse. Here are some things to keep in mind when cutting damaged hair:

1. Get the Right Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. Using dull scissors can cause more damage to your hair, so invest in a good pair of shears.

2. Wash Your Hair Before Cutting

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo before cutting it. This will help to remove any dirt and oil buildup, making it easier to cut your hair evenly.

3. Cut Small Sections at a Time

When cutting damaged hair, it is best to cut small sections at a time. This will help you to focus on each section, ensuring that you cut off all the damaged ends.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to cut damaged hair:

1. Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts, using hair clips to hold them in place. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

2. Start at the Ends

Starting at the ends, take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off only the damaged ends.

3. Move Up the Hair Shaft

Continue cutting small sections of hair, moving up the hair shaft until you have removed all the damaged ends.

4. Check for Evenness

After cutting your hair, check for evenness. If there are still uneven sections, trim them until you achieve your desired length.

Tips for Cutting Damaged Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting damaged hair:

1. Cut Your Hair When Dry

Cutting your hair when it is wet can result in uneven cuts. It is best to cut your hair when it is dry, so you can see exactly how much you are cutting.

2. Don't Cut Too Much at Once

It is best to cut small sections of hair at a time to avoid cutting off too much. Cutting too much can result in an uneven haircut.

3. Get a Professional Haircut

If you are not confident in cutting your hair, it is best to get a professional haircut. A hairstylist can assess the amount of damage and recommend the best haircut to suit your needs.

FAQs

Q: Will cutting my damaged hair make it grow faster?

A: No, cutting your hair will not make it grow faster. However, cutting off damaged ends can prevent further breakage, resulting in healthier hair.

Q: How often should I cut my damaged hair?

A: It is best to cut your hair every six to eight weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Cutting Damaged Hair

After cutting your damaged hair, it is essential to take care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo to wash your hair, avoiding harsh chemicals that can further damage your hair.

2. Apply a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair once a week to nourish and moisturize your hair.

3. Limit Heat Styling

Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons, which can cause further damage to your hair. If you must use heat styling, use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from heat damage.

4. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eat a diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth. Foods like salmon, nuts, and leafy greens are excellent for your hair.

In conclusion, cutting damaged hair can be a scary decision, but it can also be the best thing you can do for your hair's health. Follow the tips and guidelines outlined in this blog post to ensure that you cut your hair correctly and take care of it after the cut.
