How To Thicken Thin Oily Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have thin and oily hair, you know how frustrating it can be to style it. Your hair may look limp and lifeless, and it can be difficult to add volume or texture. Fortunately, there are several ways to thicken thin oily hair and give it the fullness and body you desire. In this article, we will discuss the best methods to achieve thicker and healthier hair.

How to Thicken Thin Oily Hair

The first step to thickening thin oily hair is to understand the cause of the problem. Oily hair is often a result of overactive sebaceous glands, which produce excess sebum. This can make your hair appear greasy and limp. On the other hand, thin hair is often hereditary, but it can also be caused by several factors such as stress, diet, and hormonal changes.

One of the best ways to thicken thin oily hair is to adopt a healthy hair care routine. This may include using the right shampoo and conditioner, avoiding harsh chemicals, and eating a balanced diet. You can also try some natural remedies such as massaging your scalp with essential oils, using egg white masks, and applying aloe vera gel.

Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

Choosing the right shampoo and conditioner is key to thickening thin oily hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for oily hair and that contain ingredients such as tea tree oil or salicylic acid, which can help control excess oil. Avoid shampoos that contain sulfates or silicones, as these can weigh down your hair and make it look even thinner.

Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Harsh chemicals such as hair dyes, relaxers, and perms can damage your hair and make it look thin and lifeless. If you must use these products, try to limit their use and use them sparingly. You can also opt for natural hair dyes or henna, which are gentler on your hair.

Eat a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent hair loss. Make sure to include foods that are rich in protein, iron, and vitamins such as A, C, and E. Some good examples include spinach, eggs, nuts, and fish.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to thicken thin oily hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a shampoo that is specifically designed for oily hair. Massage your scalp gently with your fingertips, and rinse thoroughly with warm water. Avoid using hot water, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Step 2: Condition Your Hair

Apply a conditioner to the ends of your hair, avoiding the roots. Leave it on for a few minutes and then rinse thoroughly with cool water. Cool water can help seal the cuticles and prevent frizz.

Step 3: Towel Dry Your Hair

Gently towel dry your hair to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously, as this can cause damage and breakage.

Step 4: Apply a Hair Thickening Product

Apply a hair thickening product such as a volumizing mousse or spray to your hair. Focus on the roots and apply it evenly throughout your hair.

Step 5: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush to add volume and lift to your hair. Use the cool setting instead of the hot setting, as this can help set your hair and prevent frizz. You can also use a diffuser attachment to add more volume and texture.

Tips to Maintain Thick and Healthy Hair

Here are some tips to maintain thick and healthy hair:

  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles such as ponytails and braids, as these can cause tension and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or scarf.

FAQs

Q: Can oily hair be genetic?

A: Yes, oily hair can be genetic. If your parents or grandparents had oily hair, you may be more likely to have it as well.

Q: Can stress cause thinning hair?

A: Yes, stress can contribute to thinning hair. When you are stressed, your body produces cortisol, which can disrupt the hair growth cycle and lead to hair loss.

Q: Can diet affect hair growth?

A: Yes, a balanced diet can help promote healthy hair growth. Make sure to include foods that are rich in protein, iron, and vitamins such as A, C, and E.
