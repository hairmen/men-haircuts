How To Start Dreadlocks With Relaxed Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you're someone who has relaxed hair and wants to start dreadlocks, you may be wondering where to start. Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that can be achieved with any hair type, including relaxed hair. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to start dreadlocks with relaxed hair, as well as tips and FAQ to help you along the way.

How to Start Dreadlocks with Relaxed Hair

Starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair can be done in a few different ways. One common method is to use the twist and rip method, which involves twisting sections of hair and then ripping the hair apart to create dreadlocks. Another method is backcombing, which involves teasing the hair backward to create knots that will eventually turn into dreadlocks.

Regardless of the method you choose, there are a few things you'll need to do to prepare your hair for dreadlocks. First, you'll need to stop using any relaxer or other chemical treatments on your hair. These products can weaken your hair and make it more difficult for your dreadlocks to form properly. You'll also want to make sure your hair is clean and free of any product buildup.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to start dreadlocks with relaxed hair:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into small, even sections. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the dreadlocks you want to create. Use hair clips or bands to keep the sections separate.

Step 2: Use the Twist and Rip Method

Take one section of hair and twist it tightly from the roots to the ends. Once the hair is twisted, use your fingers to rip the hair apart in the opposite direction of the twist. Repeat this process on each section of hair, making sure to twist and rip in the same direction for each section.

Step 3: Backcomb the Hair

If you prefer the backcombing method, use a comb to tease small sections of hair backward. This will create small knots that will eventually turn into dreadlocks. Repeat this process on each section of hair, making sure to tease in the same direction for each section.

Step 4: Maintain Your Dreadlocks

Once you've created your dreadlocks, you'll need to maintain them to keep them looking their best. This involves regular washing and conditioning, as well as palm rolling and retwisting to keep your dreadlocks tight and neat.

Tips for Starting Dreadlocks with Relaxed Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair:

  • Be patient. It can take several months for your dreadlocks to fully form, so don't get discouraged if they don't look perfect right away.
  • Avoid using any products that contain alcohol or other harsh chemicals, as these can dry out your hair and make it more difficult for your dreadlocks to form.
  • Consider getting your hair professionally done. A stylist who specializes in dreadlocks can help you achieve the look you want and provide you with tips on how to maintain your hair.

FAQs

Here are a few frequently asked questions about starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair:

Can you start dreadlocks with relaxed hair?

Yes, you can start dreadlocks with relaxed hair. However, you'll need to stop using any chemical treatments on your hair and prepare your hair properly to ensure your dreadlocks form correctly.

How long does it take for dreadlocks to form?

It can take several months for dreadlocks to fully form, as the hair needs time to knot and mat together.

What's the best way to maintain dreadlocks?

You'll need to regularly wash and condition your dreadlocks, as well as palm roll and retwist them to keep them tight and neat.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Here are a few tips for caring for your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo to prevent buildup.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

Starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair is possible, but it requires patience and proper preparation. Whether you choose the twist and rip or backcombing method, make sure to take good care of your hair to ensure your dreadlocks form properly and look their best.
