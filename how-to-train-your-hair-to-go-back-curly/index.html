How To Train Your Hair To Go Back Curly

Do you miss your natural curls after getting your hair relaxed? It can be frustrating to feel like you've lost your natural texture. But, don't worry, you can train your hair to go back curly! In this article, we'll be sharing our tips on how to train your hair to go back curly, step by step. We'll also answer some frequently asked questions and provide some essential hair care tips.

How to Train Your Hair to Go Back Curly

Training your hair to go back curly may take some time, but it's worth the effort. Here are some tips on how to train your hair to go back to its natural texture:

1. Stop relaxing your hair

The first step to train your hair to go back curly is to stop relaxing it. If you've been relaxing your hair for a while, it may take some time for your natural texture to come back. But, with patience and consistency, you can do it!

2. Start using a sulfate-free shampoo

Using a sulfate-free shampoo can help keep your hair hydrated and reduce breakage. Look for a shampoo that is specifically formulated for curly hair.

3. Deep condition your hair regularly

Deep conditioning your hair can help restore hydration and elasticity. Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your curls healthy and bouncy.

4. Use a leave-in conditioner

Using a leave-in conditioner can help keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage. Apply a leave-in conditioner after washing your hair and before styling.

5. Don't use heat styling tools

Heat styling tools can damage your hair and make it harder to train your hair to go back curly. Avoid using flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to train your hair to go back curly:

Step 2: Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Step 6: Use a diffuser

Use a diffuser attachment when blow-drying your hair to help enhance your natural curls.

Step 7: Finger comb your hair

Use your fingers to gently comb your hair when styling. This will help preserve your natural curl pattern.

Step 8: Use a satin pillowcase

Switch to a satin pillowcase to help prevent breakage and preserve your curls while sleeping.

Tips for Training Your Hair

Here are some additional tips for training your hair to go back curly:

  • Be patient. It may take some time for your natural texture to come back.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol or sulfates.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and promote healthy growth.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause breakage.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.

FAQs

Can you train your hair to go back curly?

Yes, you can train your hair to go back curly by following the steps outlined in this article.

How long does it take to train your hair to go back curly?

It may take several months for your natural texture to come back, but it's worth the effort.

What products should I use to train my hair to go back curly?

Use sulfate-free shampoo, deep conditioner, leave-in conditioner, and a diffuser attachment for blow-drying.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some essential hair care tips to keep your curls healthy and bouncy:

  • Wash your hair less frequently to prevent dryness.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can dry it out.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product with SPF.

Training your hair to go back curly may take some time, but it's worth it. By following the tips outlined in this article, you can restore your natural texture and enjoy bouncy, healthy curls. Remember to be patient and consistent, and don't forget to take good care of your hair!
