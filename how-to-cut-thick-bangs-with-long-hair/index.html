How To Cut Thick Bangs With Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of your long hair getting in your face all the time? Cutting bangs can be a great solution, but if you have thick hair, it can be tricky to get them just right. In this article, we'll show you how to cut thick bangs with long hair, step by step. By the end, you'll have a fresh new look that frames your face perfectly!

How to Cut Thick Bangs with Long Hair

Cutting bangs with long hair requires a bit of extra attention, especially if you have thick hair. Here are the steps you should follow:

Step 1: Choose the Right Length

Before you start cutting, decide how long you want your bangs to be. If you're not sure, go with a longer length at first, as you can always trim them shorter later.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair

Divide your hair into three sections - left, right, and middle. Use a comb to create a straight line down the middle of your head, from the front to the back. This will separate the left and right sides of your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Bangs

Use the comb to create a triangle shape at the front of your head, with the point of the triangle at the center of your forehead. The base of the triangle should be the length of your desired bangs. This will be the section of hair that you will cut into bangs.

Step 4: Wet Your Bangs

Before you start cutting, wet your bangs with water. This will make them easier to manage and ensure a more even cut.

Step 5: Cut Your Bangs

Using hair cutting scissors, start at the center of your forehead and cut straight across the hair you sectioned off. Be sure to cut small sections at a time to ensure an even cut.

Step 6: Trim Your Bangs

Once you've cut your bangs, use the scissors to trim any uneven sections. Be careful not to cut too much, as you can always trim more later.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basic steps, here's a more detailed guide:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is wet, it will be harder to manage and cut evenly.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Use a comb to get rid of any tangles or knots in your hair. This will make it easier to section and cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into three sections - left, right, and middle. Use a comb to create a straight line down the middle of your head, from the front to the back. This will separate the left and right sides of your hair.

Step 4: Section Your Bangs

Use the comb to create a triangle shape at the front of your head, with the point of the triangle at the center of your forehead. The base of the triangle should be the length of your desired bangs. This will be the section of hair that you will cut into bangs.

Step 5: Wet Your Bangs

Before you start cutting, wet your bangs with water. This will make them easier to manage and ensure a more even cut.

Step 6: Cut Your Bangs

Using hair cutting scissors, start at the center of your forehead and cut straight across the hair you sectioned off. Be sure to cut small sections at a time to ensure an even cut.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

After you've cut your bangs, check in the mirror to make sure they're even. If they're not, use the scissors to trim any uneven sections. Be careful not to cut too much, as you can always trim more later.

Tips for Cutting Thick Bangs

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting thick bangs:

  • Use hair cutting scissors, not regular scissors. Hair cutting scissors are sharper and will give you a cleaner cut.
  • Cut small sections at a time to ensure an even cut.
  • Don't cut your bangs too short at first. You can always trim them shorter later.
  • If you're not sure how to cut your bangs, start by watching tutorials online or getting advice from a stylist.

FAQs

How often should I trim my bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to look uneven. As a general rule, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

What if I mess up my bangs?

If you accidentally cut your bangs too short or unevenly, don't panic. You can always pin them back or wear a headband until they grow out. You can also visit a stylist to have them fixed.

Hair Care After Cutting Bangs

After you've cut your bangs, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but avoid using too much shampoo on your bangs.
  • Blow dry your bangs with a round brush to give them volume and shape.
  • Use a hair serum or oil to keep your bangs looking smooth and shiny.
  • Avoid using heat tools like straighteners or curling irons on your bangs too often, as this can damage them.

Congratulations! You now know how to cut thick bangs with long hair. Follow these steps and tips to get the perfect look for you. Remember, if you're not sure how to cut your bangs, it's always a good idea to visit a stylist for advice.
