How To Cut Thick Wavy Hair Into A Bob

Written by William Nov 06, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of dealing with the weight and bulk of your thick, wavy hair? A bob haircut could be the solution you’ve been looking for! Not only is a bob trendy and stylish, but it’s also a great way to lighten up your hair and make it more manageable. In this article, we’ll show you how to cut your thick, wavy hair into a bob with step-by-step instructions, helpful tips, and answers to common questions.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Thick Wavy Hair into a Bob
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Thick Wavy Hair into a Bob
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care After Cutting a Bob

How to Cut Thick Wavy Hair into a Bob

The first step in cutting your hair into a bob is to decide on the length you want. Bobs can range from chin-length to shoulder-length, so consider what will work best for your face shape and personal style. Once you’ve decided on the length, you’ll need to gather a few tools:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your thick wavy hair into a bob:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb through your hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Section off the top half of your hair and clip it out of the way.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the bottom back of your head and comb it straight down. Cut it to the desired length, using the first cut as a guide for the rest of your hair.
  4. Continue taking small sections of hair from the back of your head, working your way up to the crown, and cutting them to the desired length.
  5. Unclip the top half of your hair and repeat the same process, cutting the hair to the desired length.
  6. Once all of your hair is cut to the same length, it’s time to add some texture. Take small sections of hair and point-cut into them, creating shorter pieces that will add movement and volume to your hair.
  7. Continue point-cutting throughout your hair until you’ve achieved the desired texture.
  8. Style your hair as desired, using a flat iron or curling iron to add waves or straightening it for a sleek look.

Tips for Cutting Thick Wavy Hair into a Bob

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your hair into a bob:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can’t put it back once it’s cut.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can cause split ends and damage your hair.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help. If you’re not confident in your cutting skills, consider going to a professional stylist.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting your hair can be time-consuming, but the results are worth it.

FAQs

Here are some common questions and answers about cutting thick wavy hair into a bob:

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It’s recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut my own hair?

A: Yes, but it’s important to be careful and take your time. If you’re not confident in your cutting skills, consider going to a professional stylist.

Q: Will a bob look good on me?

A: Bobs can be flattering on many face shapes and hair types, but it’s important to consult with a stylist to find the best length and style for you.

Hair Care After Cutting a Bob

Now that you’ve got your new bob haircut, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair every day, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Cutting your thick wavy hair into a bob can be a great way to refresh your look and make your hair more manageable. With the right tools, techniques, and care, you can achieve a stylish and trendy bob that flatters your face shape and personal style.
