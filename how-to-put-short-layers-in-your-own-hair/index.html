How To Put Short Layers In Your Own Hair

Written by Eveline Aug 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to add some volume and texture to your hair, short layers are a great option. Not only do they give your hair some body and movement, but they can also help to frame your face and highlight your best features. In this article, we'll go over how to put short layers in your own hair, step by step.

Description

Short layers are a popular haircut for those looking to add some dimension to their hair. They can be cut at different lengths, depending on your preference, and are typically styled with some volume to create a more dynamic look. With the right tools and technique, you can easily put short layers in your own hair at home.

How to Put Short Layers in Your Own Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to section your hair. It's also a good idea to have a mirror set up so you can see the back of your head.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into manageable sections. Use the comb to create a part down the middle of your head, then section off the hair at the back of your head with hair clips. Next, section off the hair on each side of your head, leaving only the top layer of hair loose.

Step 3: Cut the First Layer

Take a small section of hair from the top layer and comb it straight up. Use the scissors to cut the hair at an angle, creating a short layer. Repeat this process for the rest of the top layer, working from front to back.

Step 4: Move to the Sides

Once you've finished the top layer, move on to the hair on the sides of your head. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle, following the shape of your head. Make sure the layers are blended well with the top layer.

Step 5: Cut the Back

Finally, move on to the hair at the back of your head. This can be a bit trickier, as you'll need to use the mirror to see what you're doing. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle, following the shape of your head. Make sure the layers are blended well with the sides and top layer.

Tips

- Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.

- Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can result in uneven layers.

- Make sure to blend the layers well for a natural look.

FAQ

Q: Can I put short layers in my own hair if it's curly?

A: Yes, you can still put short layers in your own hair if it's curly. Just make sure to cut the hair at an angle to avoid creating too much volume.

Q: How often should I cut my short layers?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the layers lose their shape. As a general rule, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of your layers.

Hair Care

After cutting your short layers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Make sure to use a shampoo and conditioner that's suited for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat when styling your hair. You can also use a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and shiny.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily put short layers in your own hair and enjoy a fresh, new look. Remember to take your time and be patient, as cutting your own hair can be a bit challenging at first. With practice, you'll soon be a pro at creating your own stylish haircuts!
