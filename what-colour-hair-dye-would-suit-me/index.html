What Colour Hair Dye Would Suit Me A Guide To Finding Your Perfect Shade

Written by Christine Jun 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you itching to change up your hair colour but not sure where to start? Choosing the right hair dye can be overwhelming, especially if you've never dyed your hair before. In this guide, we'll help you determine what colour hair dye would suit you best based on your skin tone, eye colour, and personal style.

How to Determine Your Skin Tone

Before we dive into hair colours, it's important to determine your skin tone. Your skin tone will help guide you in choosing the right hair colour that will complement you. Here are a few ways to determine your skin tone:

  • Undertones: Look at the veins on your wrist. If they appear blue, you have cool undertones. If they appear green, you have warm undertones. If you can't quite tell, you may have neutral undertones.
  • Jewelry: Do you look better in silver or gold jewelry? If silver, you likely have cool undertones. If gold, you likely have warm undertones.
  • Sunburn: How does your skin react to the sun? If you burn easily and never tan, you likely have cool undertones. If you tan easily and rarely burn, you likely have warm undertones.

Now that you have a better idea of your skin tone, let's move onto choosing the right hair colour.

Choosing the Right Hair Colour for Your Skin Tone

Once you've determined your skin tone, you can use it to guide you in choosing the right hair colour. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Cool Undertones: If you have cool undertones, you'll look great in cool-toned hair colours like ash blonde, platinum blonde, and cool brown shades. Avoid warm-toned colours like gold and copper.
  • Warm Undertones: If you have warm undertones, you'll look great in warm-toned hair colours like honey blonde, caramel, and warm brown shades. Avoid cool-toned colours like ash and platinum blonde.
  • Neutral Undertones: Lucky you, you can pull off both warm and cool-toned hair colours! Try experimenting with different shades to find what you like best.

Personal Style

Aside from your skin tone, your personal style should also be taken into consideration when choosing a hair colour. Do you prefer a bold look or something more natural? Here are a few suggestions:

  • Bold: If you're feeling daring, try a bright and bold hair colour like red or purple. These colours will make a statement and show off your personality.
  • Natural: If you prefer a more natural look, try a hair colour that's close to your natural shade. Balayage or highlights can add dimension and depth to your hair without being too drastic.
  • In Between: If you want something in between bold and natural, try a trendy hair colour like rose gold or silver. These colours are eye-catching but still have a natural feel to them.

FAQs

Here are a few frequently asked questions about choosing the right hair colour:

Q: Can I dye my hair a different colour if I have previously dyed it?

A: Yes, but it's important to take into consideration your current hair colour and the colour you want to dye it. If you want to go from dark to light, it may take multiple sessions to achieve the desired result.

Q: Will dying my hair damage it?

A: Dying your hair can cause damage, especially if you're going from a darker to lighter shade. It's important to use a deep conditioning treatment regularly to keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care

Once you've dyed your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain the colour and keep your hair healthy. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically for coloured hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently.
  • Use a heat protectant if you frequently use hot styling tools.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid chlorine and saltwater, as they can fade your hair colour.

Now that you have a better idea of what colour hair dye would suit you, it's time to book that salon appointment or grab a box of hair dye and get to work! Remember to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great.
