How To Not Lose Hair Postpartum A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 25, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you a new mom struggling with hair loss after giving birth? You’re not alone. Postpartum hair loss affects up to 50% of women, but there are ways to prevent or minimize it. In this article, we’ll explore how to not lose hair postpartum and offer tips and advice for maintaining healthy hair during this challenging period.

Table of Contents

  • How to Not Lose Hair Postpartum
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Preventing Postpartum Hair Loss
  • FAQ

How to Not Lose Hair Postpartum

Postpartum hair loss is caused by hormonal changes in the body after pregnancy. During pregnancy, higher levels of estrogen prevent hair from falling out at its normal rate, resulting in thicker, fuller hair. But after giving birth, estrogen levels drop, causing hair to shed more than usual.

To prevent or minimize postpartum hair loss, try the following:

Eat a Nutritious Diet

A balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals such as iron, biotin, and zinc can promote healthy hair growth. Incorporate foods such as leafy greens, eggs, nuts, and fish into your diet to ensure you’re getting the nutrients your hair needs.

Stay Hydrated

Drinking enough water is essential for maintaining healthy hair. Aim for at least eight glasses of water per day to keep your hair and scalp hydrated.

Manage Stress

Stress can contribute to hair loss, so finding ways to manage stress is important. Try meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises to reduce stress levels.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to not lose hair postpartum:

Step 1: Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid harsh treatments such as perms, straightening, or coloring during this period. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle hair and avoid pulling or tugging.

Step 2: Use Gentle Hair Products

Choose hair products that are gentle and nourishing. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are sulfate-free and contain natural ingredients such as aloe vera or coconut oil.

Step 3: Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can lead to further hair loss.

Step 4: Take Supplements

Supplements such as biotin, iron, and zinc can help promote healthy hair growth. Consult with your doctor before taking any supplements.

Tips for Preventing Postpartum Hair Loss

Here are some additional tips for preventing postpartum hair loss:

1. Be Patient

Postpartum hair loss is temporary and typically resolves on its own within a few months. Be patient and don’t stress too much about it.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as tight braids or ponytails. These styles can cause further hair loss.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Avoid using heat styling tools such as hair dryers or flat irons, which can damage hair and lead to further hair loss.

4. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can improve blood flow and promote healthy hair growth. Use your fingertips to gently massage your scalp for a few minutes each day.

FAQ

When does postpartum hair loss occur?

Postpartum hair loss typically occurs between three and six months after giving birth.

Is postpartum hair loss permanent?

No, postpartum hair loss is temporary and typically resolves on its own within a few months.

What can I do if I’m experiencing excessive hair loss postpartum?

If you’re experiencing excessive hair loss, consult with your doctor or a dermatologist to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Hair Care Tips for New Moms

Here are some additional hair care tips for new moms:

1. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Using a silk pillowcase can help prevent tangles and breakage while you sleep.

2. Don’t Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of natural oils and lead to dryness and breakage. Aim to wash your hair every two to three days.

3. Use a Hair Mask

Using a hair mask once a week can help nourish and strengthen hair. Look for a mask that contains ingredients such as avocado or honey.

4. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Excessive sun exposure can damage hair and lead to dryness and breakage. Wear a hat or use a hair product with SPF to protect your hair from the sun.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can prevent or minimize postpartum hair loss and maintain healthy, beautiful hair during this challenging period.
