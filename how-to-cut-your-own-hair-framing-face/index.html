How To Cut Your Own Hair Framing Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune on haircuts? Or maybe you just want to try something new? Whatever the reason, cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting your own hair framing face, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Framing Face
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair at Home
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially when it comes to framing your face. But with the right tools and techniques, it's easier than you might think. Framing your face is all about creating layers that accentuate your features and flatter your face shape.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Framing Face

Before you get started, you'll need a few tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Mirror

Once you have your tools, it's time to get started.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own hair framing face:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would.
  2. Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back.
  3. Starting with the back section, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut the ends with your scissors.
  4. Continue taking small sections of hair and cutting them to your desired length.
  5. Repeat this process for the side sections, combing the hair forward and cutting at an angle to create layers that frame your face.
  6. For the top section, comb the hair forward and hold it between your fingers. Cut the ends at an angle to create layers that blend with the side sections.
  7. Once you've finished cutting, use your comb to check for any uneven sections and trim as needed.
  8. Style your hair as desired, using clips or hair ties to create volume and texture.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair at Home

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair framing face:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut a little at a time. You can always go back and trim more later.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Make sure you're cutting evenly on both sides.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so don't rush it.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about cutting your own hair framing face:

  • Can I cut my own hair if it's curly? Yes, but keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so cut less than you think you need to.
  • What if I mess up? Don't worry! Hair grows back, and there are plenty of hair salons that can fix any mistakes.
  • How often should I cut my hair? It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows, but most people cut their hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care

After cutting your own hair framing face, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner that's right for your hair type.
  • Avoid heat styling too often, as it can damage your hair.
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote hair growth.

Now that you know how to cut your own hair framing face, you can save money and have fun experimenting with new styles. Just remember to take it slow, use sharp scissors, and take breaks when you need to. Happy cutting!
