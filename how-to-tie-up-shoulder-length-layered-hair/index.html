How to Tie Up Shoulder Length Layered Hair

Follow these steps to tie up your shoulder length layered hair:

Step 1: Brush your hair

Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles or knots. Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to gently brush your hair from the roots to the ends.

Step 2: Decide on your hairstyle

Decide on the type of hairstyle you want to create. You can choose from a high ponytail, low ponytail, messy bun, or sleek bun. The type of hairstyle you choose will depend on the occasion and your personal preference.

Step 3: Gather your hair

Gather your hair with your hands and bring it to the back of your head. Use a hair tie to secure your hair in place. Make sure the hair tie is tight enough to hold your hair in place but not too tight that it causes discomfort.

Step 4: Style your hair

Once your hair is tied up, you can style it to your liking. You can create a messy bun by pulling out a few strands of hair and loosening the hair tie. You can also create a sleek bun by using a hair gel or hairspray to smooth down any flyaway hairs.

Step 5: Finish with hairspray

Finish your hairstyle with a light mist of hairspray to hold your hair in place. This will help your hairstyle last longer and prevent any flyaway hairs.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when tying up your shoulder length layered hair:

  • Use a hair tie that matches your hair color to create a seamless look.
  • For a more secure hold, use bobby pins to pin any loose strands of hair.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find the one that suits you best.

Yes, you can still tie up your hair even if you have bangs. Simply leave your bangs out or sweep them to the side and secure them with bobby pins.

Use a hair tie that is gentle on your hair, such as a scrunchie or a fabric hair tie. Avoid using elastic hair ties as they can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
