How to Cut Layers on Your Own Short Hair

Cutting layers on short hair can be a bit tricky, but with the right technique, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home. Here are the key steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair and blow dry it until itâs completely dry. Make sure your hair is free of any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, divide your hair into three sections: the top, the sides, and the back. Clip the top section away, leaving the sides and back loose.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Starting at the nape of your neck, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Using hair-cutting scissors, cut the hair straight across at the desired length. Repeat this process, working your way up to the crown of your head.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

For the sides, use the previous section as a guide and cut the hair at the same length. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. Repeat this process on the other side.

Step 5: Cut the Top

For the top section, take a horizontal section of hair and hold it between your fingers at a 45-degree angle. Use the scissors to cut the hair at a slight angle, creating a layered effect. Repeat this process, working your way from the front to the back of your head.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

To blend the layers, take small sections of hair from the top and bottom layers and cut them at a slight angle. This will create a seamless transition between the layers.
