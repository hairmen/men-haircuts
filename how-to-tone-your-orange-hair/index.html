How To Tone Your Orange Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 25, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to tone your orange hair! If you’ve recently bleached your hair, you might find that it has turned an unwanted shade of orange. Don’t worry, this is a common problem and can be easily fixed with the right toner. In this article, we will show you how to tone your orange hair and achieve the perfect blonde shade.

Table of Contents

  • Why Does Hair Turn Orange?
  • How to Tone Your Orange Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Toning Your Orange Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Why Does Hair Turn Orange?

When you bleach your hair, the process removes the natural pigment from your hair strands. If your hair has a lot of red pigment, it can turn orange when the bleach removes the red but not all of the yellow pigment. This is why it’s important to use a toner to neutralize the orange and achieve the perfect blonde shade.

How to Tone Your Orange Hair

The first step in toning your orange hair is to choose the right toner. Look for a toner with a blue or purple base, as these colors will neutralize the orange tones in your hair. You can find toners at most beauty supply stores or online.

Choosing the Right Toner

When choosing a toner, consider the level of blonde you want to achieve. If you want a cooler, ashy blonde, look for a toner with a blue base. If you want a warmer, golden blonde, look for a toner with a purple base. Make sure to read the instructions on the toner carefully and follow them closely.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s how to tone your orange hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any build-up or residue. This will help the toner to absorb better.

Step 2: Mix the Toner

Follow the instructions on your toner to mix it properly. Most toners require mixing with a developer, which is usually sold separately. Mix the toner and developer together in a plastic bowl.

Step 3: Apply the Toner

Apply the toner to your hair using a tint brush. Make sure to saturate your hair evenly with the toner. Leave the toner on for the recommended amount of time, usually 20-30 minutes.

Step 4: Rinse and Condition

Rinse the toner out of your hair with cool water until the water runs clear. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and leave it on for 5-10 minutes before rinsing it out.

Tips for Toning Your Orange Hair

  • Don’t leave the toner on for too long, as it can turn your hair a different color.
  • Use a toner with a blue or purple base to neutralize the orange tones in your hair.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any build-up or residue before toning your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioner after toning to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Don’t tone your hair too often, as it can cause damage and breakage.

FAQs

How long does toner last?

Toner can last anywhere from 2-6 weeks, depending on how often you wash your hair and how quickly your hair grows. You may need to touch up your toner every few weeks to maintain your desired shade.

Can toner damage my hair?

Toner is generally safe for your hair, but it can cause damage if used too often or left on for too long. Make sure to follow the instructions on the toner carefully and not to overuse it.

Hair Care Tips

  • Avoid using heat-styling tools on your hair too often, as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat-styling tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or peroxide, too often.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

That’s it for our guide on how to tone your orange hair! We hope you found this article helpful and that you’re able to achieve the perfect blonde shade. Remember to always take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
