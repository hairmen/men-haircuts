How To Cut Long Hair Guys A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jun 26, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to cut long hair guys! If you're tired of long hair and want to try something new, or if you're just looking to save some money on haircuts, this guide is for you. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about cutting long hair for men, from the tools you'll need to the best techniques to use. So, let's get started!

Description

Cutting long hair guys can be a bit tricky, especially if you've never done it before. However, with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. In this guide, we'll cover both the basics and more advanced techniques, so you'll be able to give yourself or someone else a great haircut.

How to Cut Long Hair Guys

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first thing you'll need to do is gather all the necessary tools. You'll need:

  • A pair of sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • Hair clippers (optional)
  • A mirror (or two)
  • A towel or cape to catch the hair

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can cause split ends and make the haircut look uneven.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. If you have curly hair, you may want to straighten it first so you can see the length more clearly. Comb your hair out so it's smooth and free of tangles.

Step 3: Decide on the Length

Decide how much you want to cut off. If you're not sure, start with a small amount and work your way up. Remember, you can always cut more later, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Begin cutting at the back of your head, using the comb to separate a small section of hair. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across, using the comb as a guide. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working your way up to the top of your head.

For a more layered look, use a technique called point cutting. Hold the hair between your fingers, and instead of cutting straight across, cut into the hair at an angle. This will create a softer, more textured look.

Step 5: Trim the Sides and Bangs (Optional)

If you want to trim the sides or bangs, use the same technique as above, but be careful not to cut too much off. It's better to start with a small amount and work your way up.

Step 6: Clean Up the Edges

Use the scissors or clippers to clean up the edges around your ears and the back of your neck. You can also use the clippers to create a fade if you want.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up
  • Use the comb as a guide to ensure an even cut
  • Be careful not to cut too much off at once
  • For a more layered look, use the point cutting technique
  • Clean up the edges with scissors or clippers

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair. However, it can be a bit tricky, especially if you've never done it before. Take your time, start with small cuts, and be patient.

Do I need hair clippers?

No, hair clippers are optional. If you prefer a shorter cut, or if you want to create a fade, you'll need clippers. Otherwise, you can just use scissors.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how short you want to keep it. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair, make sure you take good care of it. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat when styling. If you're not sure what products to use, ask your stylist for recommendations.

Cutting long hair guys can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. Take your time, start with small cuts, and be patient. And remember, if you're not happy with the results, you can always go to a professional stylist.
