How To Wear Long Hair In Summer Tips Styles And Hair Care

Summer is a great season to enjoy the outdoors, but it can be challenging for those with long hair. Sweaty and sticky hair is not a good look. Fortunately, there are plenty of ways to wear long hair in summer that are stylish, comfortable, and low-maintenance.

  • How to Wear Long Hair in Summer

How to Wear Long Hair in Summer

The first step to wearing long hair in summer is to keep it off your face and neck. This will keep you cool and prevent your hair from getting sticky and sweaty. Here are some ways to do it:

  • Wear a high ponytail
  • Tie your hair in a messy bun
  • Try a top knot
  • French braid your hair
  • Wear a headband or scarf

These hairstyles are easy to do and can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion. They are also perfect for keeping your hair out of the way when you're at the beach, pool, or park.

Step by Step: Hairstyles for Summer

Here are some step-by-step instructions on how to do some of the most popular hairstyles for summer:

High Ponytail

  1. Brush your hair to remove any tangles
  2. Gather your hair at the crown of your head and secure it with an elastic band
  3. Use a comb or your fingers to smooth out any bumps
  4. Wrap a small section of hair around the elastic band and secure it with a bobby pin

Messy Bun

  1. Gather your hair into a high ponytail and secure it with an elastic band
  2. Twist your hair around the elastic band and secure it with bobby pins
  3. Pull out a few strands of hair to create a messy look

Top Knot

  1. Gather your hair into a high ponytail and secure it with an elastic band
  2. Twist your hair around the elastic band to create a bun
  3. Secure the bun with bobby pins

French Braid

  1. Brush your hair to remove any tangles
  2. Divide your hair into three sections at the crown of your head
  3. Cross the right section over the middle section
  4. Cross the left section over the middle section
  5. Add a small section of hair from the right side to the right section
  6. Cross the right section over the middle section
  7. Add a small section of hair from the left side to the left section
  8. Cross the left section over the middle section
  9. Repeat steps 5-8 until you reach the end of your hair
  10. Secure the braid with an elastic band

Tips for Maintaining Long Hair in Summer

Here are some tips for keeping your long hair healthy and beautiful during the summer months:

  • Wash your hair regularly to remove sweat and dirt
  • Avoid using hot styling tools, which can damage your hair
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and protected from the sun
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective spray
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

FAQs about Wearing Long Hair in Summer

Q: Is it better to wear my hair up or down in the summer?

A: It's better to wear your hair up in the summer to keep it off your face and neck, which can make you feel hot and sweaty. Updos also protect your hair from the sun and prevent tangles and breakage.

Q: Will wearing my hair up all the time damage it?

A: Wearing your hair up all the time can cause breakage and tension on your scalp, especially if you use tight hairstyles like braids and buns. It's important to switch up your hairstyles and give your hair a break from time to time.

Q: How can I prevent my hair from getting frizzy in the summer?

A: Frizz is a common problem in the summer, especially if you have curly or wavy hair. To prevent frizz, avoid using hot styling tools and use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner. You can also use a frizz-fighting serum or cream to tame flyaways.

Hair Care Tips for Summer

Here are some additional hair care tips for summer:

  • Avoid washing your hair with hot water, which can strip it of its natural oils
  • Rinse your hair with cool water after swimming to remove chlorine and salt
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush, which can cause breakage
  • Avoid wearing tight hats or headbands, which can cause scalp irritation and breakage
  • Protect your hair from the wind by wearing a scarf or wrap

With these tips and hairstyles, you can enjoy the summer sun without sacrificing your long hair. Whether you're at the beach, park, or pool, you can look and feel your best with these easy and low-maintenance hairstyles.
