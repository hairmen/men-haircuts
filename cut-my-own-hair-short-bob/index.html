Cut My Own Hair Short Bob A Diy Guide

Written by Christine Sep 10, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the salon every few weeks just to maintain your short bob haircut? Well, why not try cutting your own hair at home? With a little bit of practice and patience, you can achieve a professional-looking short bob haircut without leaving the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your own hair short bob, step-by-step. Read on to learn more!

Description

A short bob haircut is a classic and timeless hairstyle that never goes out of fashion. It's versatile, easy to maintain, and suits almost every face shape. However, getting a short bob haircut at the salon can be expensive, and it's not always easy to find a stylist who can perfectly execute the look you want. That's why many people choose to cut their own hair at home, especially during these times of pandemic when visiting a salon is not always possible.

How to Cut My Own Hair Short Bob

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting your own hair short bob is to gather all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, a handheld mirror, and a large mirror. Make sure your scissors are specifically designed for hair cutting and are sharp enough to avoid split ends.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help you see the natural fall of your hair and ensure that you don't cut too much off. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid any damage to your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into manageable parts. Use hair clips to clip the top section of your hair away from the bottom section. Then, divide the bottom section into two equal parts, one on each side of your head.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting with one section, comb your hair straight downwards, and hold it between your fingers at the length you want to cut. Use your other hand to hold the scissors and cut the hair in a straight line. Be sure to cut small sections at a time and avoid cutting too much off at once. Repeat this step on the other side of your head.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut both sides, it's time to blend the layers. Comb your hair downwards and check for any uneven spots. Use the scissors to trim any areas that need it, taking care to blend the layers seamlessly. Remember that the goal is to achieve a natural and balanced look.

Step 6: Check the Back

Using the handheld mirror and the large mirror, check the back of your head. If there are any uneven spots, use the scissors to trim them until you're satisfied with the overall look. Take your time and be patient, as it can be challenging to see the back of your head.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting, style your hair as desired. You can use a blow dryer and a round brush to add volume and shape, or you can let your hair air dry for a more natural look.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Short Bob

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting your own hair short bob:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut a little at a time
  • Use sharp scissors designed for hair cutting
  • Take breaks if your arms get tired
  • Don't cut too much off at once
  • Be patient and take your time
  • Watch tutorials online to get a better idea of the technique

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair short bob without any experience?

Yes, you can cut your own hair short bob even if you have no prior experience. However, it's essential to be patient and take your time. Start with small sections of hair and cut a little at a time. Watch tutorials online to get a better idea of the technique.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. Hair grows back, and you can always visit a salon to fix any major errors. However, it's best to avoid making any significant changes to your hair if you're not confident in your skills.

How often should I cut my hair short bob?

It's recommended to cut your hair short bob every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and style.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair Short Bob

After cutting your own hair short bob, it's essential to take care of your hair to avoid any damage or breakage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated
  • Trim your hair regularly to avoid split ends
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair

Conclusion

Cutting your own hair short bob can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and a little bit of patience, you can achieve a professional-looking hairstyle without breaking the bank. Remember to take your time, start with small sections of hair, and watch tutorials online to get a better idea of the technique. Happy cutting!
