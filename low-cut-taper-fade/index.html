Low Cut Taper Fade The Ultimate Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 31, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Welcome to our ultimate guide on the low cut taper fade, the hairstyle that has been taking the world by storm in recent years. In this article, we will cover everything you need to know about this popular hairstyle, including how to get it, step by step instructions, tips, frequently asked questions, and hair care advice. So, let's get started!

What is a Low Cut Taper Fade?

A low cut taper fade is a type of fade haircut that starts with a short cut on the sides and back of the head, gradually getting longer towards the top of the head. The hair is tapered from the shortest length at the neckline to a longer length at the top of the head, creating a seamless transition between the two. This hairstyle is perfect for men who want a low maintenance, yet stylish look.

How to Get a Low Cut Taper Fade

Getting a low cut taper fade is a fairly simple process, but it does require some skill and knowledge. Here are the steps you need to follow:

Step 1: Decide on the Length

The first step in getting a low cut taper fade is to decide on the length you want for the top of your head. This will determine how much hair needs to be cut from the sides and back. It's important to communicate your desired length with your barber, so they can give you the right cut.

Step 2: Start with a High Guard

To begin the cut, your barber will use a high guard on their clippers to remove any excess hair from the sides and back of your head. This will create the initial fade and help blend the hair into the longer length at the top of your head.

Step 3: Gradually Decrease the Guard Size

As your barber works their way up your head, they will gradually decrease the size of the guard on their clippers, creating a seamless transition from the shorter length to the longer length at the top. This is what creates the "taper" effect.

Step 4: Blend the Top

Once the sides and back are tapered, your barber will blend the top of your hair with scissors or clippers, depending on your desired length. This will create a seamless transition between the shorter and longer lengths of hair.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Finally, it's time to style your hair! Depending on your hair type and personal preference, you can style your low cut taper fade with a variety of products, including pomade, wax, or gel. Experiment with different products until you find the one that works best for you.

Tips for Maintaining Your Low Cut Taper Fade

Now that you have your low cut taper fade, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking fresh and stylish. Here are some tips to help you maintain your new hairstyle:

  • Visit your barber every 2-3 weeks for a touch-up
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy
  • Invest in a high-quality hair product that works for your hair type
  • Avoid over-styling your hair, as this can damage it

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a low cut taper fade and a high fade?

The main difference between a low cut taper fade and a high fade is where the fade starts. A low cut taper fade starts with a shorter length at the neckline and gradually gets longer towards the top of the head, while a high fade starts higher up on the head and creates a more dramatic contrast between the shorter and longer lengths of hair.

Can anyone get a low cut taper fade?

Yes, a low cut taper fade is a versatile hairstyle that can work for anyone, regardless of hair type or face shape. However, it's important to communicate your desired length and style with your barber to ensure you get the best cut for your hair.

Is a low cut taper fade easy to maintain?

Yes, a low cut taper fade is a low maintenance hairstyle that requires minimal styling and upkeep. Regular visits to your barber for touch-ups and proper hair care can help keep your hairstyle looking fresh and stylish.

Hair Care for Your Low Cut Taper Fade

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining a healthy, stylish low cut taper fade. Here are some tips to help you keep your hair looking and feeling its best:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils
  • Condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized and healthy
  • Avoid using hot tools like hair dryers, as these can damage your hair
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals like bleach or hair dye, as these can also damage your hair

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock your low cut taper fade with confidence and style. Remember to communicate your desired length and style with your barber, and take care of your hair with proper hair care and maintenance. Happy styling!
