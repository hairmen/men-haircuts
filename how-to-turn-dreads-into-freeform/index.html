How To Turn Dreads Into Freeform

Written by Christine Sep 19, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to turn dreads into freeform. In this article, we will show you step-by-step how to achieve the freeform look with your locks. If you're tired of the uniform look of your dreadlocks, freeform can be a liberating change. Here are some tips to help you make the transformation.

Description

Freeform locks are a natural progression from traditional dreadlocks. Rather than having a uniform shape, freeform locks are more organic and have a looser, less structured appearance. They are also known as "neglect locks" or "freeform dreads" because they are allowed to form without much intervention. Freeform locks are a good choice for those who want a more natural look and are willing to let their hair do its thing.

How to Turn Dreads into Freeform

To turn your dreads into freeform, follow these steps:

Step 1: Stop Maintaining Your Dreads

The first step to achieving freeform locks is to stop maintaining your dreads. This means you should stop palm rolling, twisting, and retwisting your locks. Let your hair grow naturally and allow the locks to form on their own.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

It's important to keep your hair clean during the freeform process. Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo to keep your locks healthy and free from buildup.

Step 3: Separate Your Locks

As your hair grows and locks form, you may need to separate them to prevent them from growing together. Use your fingers to gently pull apart any locks that are starting to merge. Be gentle to avoid causing damage to your hair.

Step 4: Be Patient

The process of turning your dreads into freeform can take time. Be patient and let your hair do its thing. The more you try to force the locks to form a certain way, the longer it will take to achieve the freeform look.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you turn your dreads into freeform:

  • Don't use any products in your hair.
  • Don't use a hairbrush or comb on your locks.
  • Be gentle when washing and drying your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools.
  • Allow your hair to dry naturally whenever possible.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about turning dreads into freeform:

Can I speed up the process of turning my dreads into freeform?

No, the process of turning your dreads into freeform takes time. Be patient and let your hair do its thing.

Do I need to cut off my existing dreads to achieve freeform locks?

No, you can achieve freeform locks without cutting off your existing dreads. Simply stop maintaining them and allow them to grow naturally.

Can I still maintain my dreads if I decide I don't like the freeform look?

Yes, you can always go back to maintaining your dreads if you decide you don't like the freeform look. However, it's important to give the freeform process a fair chance before making a decision.

Hair Care

Whether you have traditional dreads or freeform locks, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips for healthy dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Don't use any products in your hair.
  • Be gentle when washing and drying your hair.
  • Keep your hair moisturized with natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools.

Now that you know how to turn dreads into freeform, it's time to embrace the natural look of your locks. Remember to be patient and let your hair do its thing. With time and care, you'll achieve the freeform look you desire.
