Should You Layer Short Curly Hair

Written by Robby Jul 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Short curly hair is one of the most versatile hairstyles, and it's perfect for those who want to add volume and movement to their hair. However, when it comes to styling short curly hair, there are many questions that need to be answered, such as should you layer short curly hair? In this article, we will answer this question and provide you with some tips on how to style your short curly hair.

Description

Layering short curly hair can add body, texture, and definition to your hair. It can also help to control frizz and make your curls more defined. However, it's important to keep in mind that layering can also make your hair look shorter, especially if you have tight curls. So, before you decide to layer your hair, it's essential to consider your hair type, face shape, and personal style.

How To Layer Short Curly Hair

If you've decided to layer your short curly hair, there are several techniques that you can use. One of the most popular techniques is the twist and cut method. This involves twisting sections of your hair and cutting them at an angle to create layers. Another technique is the point cutting method, which involves cutting the ends of your hair at different lengths to create layers.

Twist and Cut Method

Here's how to do the twist and cut method:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair.
  2. Blow-dry your hair until it's about 80% dry.
  3. Divide your hair into four sections.
  4. Take a small section of hair and twist it tightly.
  5. Use a pair of sharp scissors to cut the twisted section at an angle.
  6. Repeat this process on all sections of your hair.
  7. Once you've finished cutting your hair, shake your hair to loosen the curls and create more volume.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to layer short curly hair using the twist and cut method:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual, and towel dry it gently.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair to help define your curls and prevent frizz.
  3. Use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to dry your hair until it's about 80% dry.
  4. Divide your hair into four sections, using hair clips or hair ties to keep them separate.
  5. Take a small section of hair from the back of your head and twist it tightly.
  6. Use a pair of sharp scissors to cut the twisted section at an angle, making sure to cut the hair at a length that suits your face shape and personal style.
  7. Repeat this process on all sections of your hair, making sure to cut each section at the same angle to create a uniform look.
  8. Once you've finished cutting your hair, shake your hair to loosen the curls and create more volume.

Tips for Styling Layered Short Curly Hair

Here are some tips for styling your layered short curly hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, and avoid using a brush, which can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Apply a hair oil or serum to your hair to add shine and prevent frizz.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to dry your hair, as this can help to define your curls and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage your hair and make it frizzy.
  • Experiment with different hair products, such as mousse, gel, or curl cream, to find the products that work best for your hair type.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about layering short curly hair:

  • Will layering short curly hair make my hair look shorter? - Yes, layering can make your hair look shorter, especially if you have tight curls. So, it's important to consider your face shape and personal style before deciding to layer your hair.
  • Can I layer my short curly hair myself? - While it's possible to layer your short curly hair yourself, it's best to visit a professional stylist who can help you achieve the look you want.
  • What kind of hair products should I use on my layered short curly hair? - You should use hair products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as mousse, gel, or curl cream. These products can help to define your curls and prevent frizz.

Hair Care for Short Curly Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your short curly hair:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid using hot water on your hair, as this can cause your hair to become dry and frizzy.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and moisturize your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as this can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

In conclusion, layering short curly hair can add volume, texture, and definition to your hair, but it's important to consider your hair type, face shape, and personal style before deciding to layer your hair. If you decide to layer your hair, make sure to visit a professional stylist who can help you achieve the look you want.
