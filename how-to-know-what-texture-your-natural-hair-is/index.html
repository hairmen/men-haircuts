How To Know What Texture Your Natural Hair Is

Written by Petter Oct 19, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of not knowing what texture your natural hair is? It can be frustrating trying to figure out what products to use and how to style your hair if you don't know your hair type. In this article, we will give you step-by-step instructions on how to determine your natural hair texture.

Table of Contents

  1. Definition of Hair Texture
  2. How to Determine Your Hair Texture
  3. Types of Hair Texture
  4. Tips for Caring for Your Natural Hair
  5. FAQs

Definition of Hair Texture

Hair texture refers to the thickness or diameter of individual strands of hair. Knowing your hair texture is important because it can help you choose the right products and styling techniques for your hair.

How to Determine Your Hair Texture

Determining your hair texture is easy. All you need to do is take a single strand of hair and examine it closely. Here are the steps:

  1. Wash your hair and let it air dry or blow dry it.
  2. Take a single strand of hair from the crown of your head.
  3. Hold the strand of hair between your index finger and thumb.
  4. Run your fingers down the strand of hair from the root to the tip.
  5. Determine the thickness of the strand of hair.

Once you have determined the thickness of the strand of hair, you can use that information to determine your hair texture.

Types of Hair Texture

There are four main types of hair texture: straight, wavy, curly, and kinky. Here is a brief overview of each:

Straight Hair

Straight hair is typically very smooth and shiny. It is the thinnest hair texture and reflects light the most. It is also the most prone to oiliness and can become greasy quickly.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair has a slight curl to it and is thicker than straight hair. It can be prone to frizziness and can be difficult to manage.

Curly Hair

Curly hair has a definite curl pattern to it and is the thickest hair texture. It can be prone to dryness and frizziness and requires a lot of moisture and care.

Kinky Hair

Kinky hair has a very tight curl pattern and is the most fragile hair texture. It requires a lot of moisture and care to maintain its health.

Tips for Caring for Your Natural Hair

Regardless of your hair texture, there are some tips that are universal when it comes to caring for natural hair:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals and heat on your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a silk or satin bonnet or using a silk or satin pillowcase
  • Be gentle when detangling your hair to prevent breakage

FAQs

Q: Can my hair texture change over time?

A: Yes, your hair texture can change due to a variety of factors, including hormonal changes, age, and certain health conditions.

Q: Can I have more than one hair texture?

A: Yes, it is possible to have multiple hair textures on your head. This is known as hair type or hair pattern.

Q: How often should I wash my natural hair?

A: It depends on your hair texture and personal preference. Some people may need to wash their hair once a week, while others may need to wash it every other day.

Knowing your hair texture is the first step in taking care of your natural hair. By following the tips outlined in this article, you can keep your hair healthy and beautiful.
