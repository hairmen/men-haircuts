How To Cut Men s Wavy Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending money on expensive haircuts for your wavy hair? With a little practice, you can learn how to cut your own hair at home. Not only will this save you money, but it will also give you more control over your style. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut men's wavy hair yourself.

Description

Wavy hair has a natural texture that requires a different approach than straight hair. It's important to understand the techniques that work best for wavy hair to achieve the desired result. With a few tools and some patience, you can give yourself a professional-looking haircut at home.

How to Cut Men's Wavy Hair Yourself

Before you begin, you'll need a few tools:

  • Scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties (optional)
  • Mirror

Once you have your tools ready, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will remove any product buildup and make it easier to cut. Towel-dry your hair, but don't use a hairdryer. Wavy hair is prone to frizz, and using a hairdryer can make it worse.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Comb your hair to remove any tangles. Make sure your hair is completely smooth before you start cutting. If you have longer hair, you may want to tie it up in sections to make it easier to work with.

Step 3: Trim the Sides and Back

Use your clippers or scissors to trim the sides and back of your hair. Start with a longer guard or length setting and work your way down if you want a shorter cut. Keep the clippers or scissors parallel to your head and use a comb to guide your cutting.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Next, it's time to cut the top of your hair. This is where you'll want to focus on the wavy texture of your hair. Use your scissors to cut the hair in small sections, starting at the crown and working your way forward. Cut each section at an angle to create layers.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut the top of your hair, use your scissors to blend the layers. Hold your hair up and cut into the ends to create a more natural-looking finish. Make sure to blend the layers evenly across your head.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Use your mirror to check your work from all angles. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure your hair is even and the desired length. If you're using clippers, switch to a shorter guard or length setting to clean up any stray hairs.

Tips

  • Start with a longer length setting and work your way down to avoid cutting too much off at once.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wavy hair can shrink when it dries, so it's best to cut it when it's dry.
  • Take your time and be patient. It's better to cut your hair in stages than to rush and make a mistake.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair with just scissors?

Yes, you can cut your hair with just scissors. However, clippers can make the process faster and easier, especially for trimming the sides and back.

What length should I cut my hair?

The length you cut your hair will depend on your personal preference. It's best to start with a longer length setting and work your way down until you find the right length.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your desired length and style.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain your style. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's designed for wavy hair to keep it healthy and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz.

Cutting men's wavy hair yourself can seem intimidating, but with a little practice, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home. Follow these steps and tips to get started on your DIY haircut journey.
