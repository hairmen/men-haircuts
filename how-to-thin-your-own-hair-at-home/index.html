How To Thin Your Own Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 17, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have thick hair, you know how frustrating it can be to manage. It takes forever to dry, and it can feel heavy and uncomfortable. One solution to this problem is to thin your hair. Thinning your hair means removing some of the bulk, making it lighter and easier to manage. In this article, we will show you how to thin your own hair at home.

How to Thin Your Own Hair at Home

Thinning your hair at home is not as difficult as it may seem. You just need the right tools and some patience. Here are the steps:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any build-up and make your hair easier to work with. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel dry it.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair into four parts: two on the top and two on the bottom. Clip each section in place.

Step 3: Choose Your Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are scissors that have teeth on one blade to help remove bulk from your hair. You can find them at most beauty supply stores. Choose a pair that has teeth that are close together for a more natural look.

Step 4: Start Thinning Your Hair

Start with the bottom section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and start cutting with the thinning shears about an inch from the roots. Cut about halfway down the hair shaft and then release. Repeat this process until you have thinned out the entire section.

Step 5: Move to the Top Sections

Repeat the same process on the top sections of your hair, working from the back to the front. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as it can be difficult to fix.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

Once you have finished thinning your hair, check your progress in a mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that your hair looks even and natural.

Tips for Thinning Your Hair

Thinning your hair can be a bit tricky, so here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Use sharp scissors or thinning shears to avoid split ends and hair damage.
  • Don't thin your hair too much at once. It's better to thin it gradually to avoid any mistakes.
  • Make sure you're holding your hair correctly. Hold it between your fingers and cut at an angle to avoid harsh lines.
  • If you're unsure about how much to thin your hair, start with a small section and work your way up.

FAQs

Can I thin my hair with regular scissors?

No, you should use thinning shears to thin your hair. Regular scissors can create blunt lines and damage your hair.

How often should I thin my hair?

You should thin your hair every 6-8 weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows.

Will thinning my hair make it grow back thicker?

No, thinning your hair will not affect its texture or thickness. It will just remove some of the bulk.

Hair Care After Thinning Your Hair

After thinning your hair, you should take extra care to keep it healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to avoid split ends.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.

Thinning your hair at home can be a great way to keep it manageable and healthy. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a natural, even look without having to go to a salon.
