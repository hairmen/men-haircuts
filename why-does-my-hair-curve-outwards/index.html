Why Does My Hair Curve Outwards

Written by Aprilia Sep 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of dealing with hair that seems to have a mind of its own? Do you find that no matter what you do, your hair always seems to curve outwards? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with hair that refuses to lay flat, and it can be frustrating trying to figure out why.

Description

Having hair that curves outwards can be caused by a number of factors, including genetics, hair care habits, and even the weather. It's important to understand what's causing your hair to behave this way so that you can take steps to manage it.

How to Determine Why Your Hair is Curving Outwards

The first step in figuring out why your hair is curving outwards is to look at your hair type. If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, it's likely that your hair will naturally curve outwards. However, if you have straight hair and it's still curving outwards, there may be other factors at play.

Another thing to consider is how you're styling your hair. Are you using too much heat on your hair? Are you using the wrong products for your hair type? These factors can contribute to hair that doesn't lay flat.

Step by Step Guide to Managing Hair That Curves Outwards

1. Determine your hair type - If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, you may need to accept that your hair will always curve outwards to some degree. However, if you have straight hair and it's still curving outwards, you may need to adjust your hair care routine.

2. Use the right products - Make sure you're using products that are specifically designed for your hair type. If you have straight hair, look for products that help to smooth and straighten your hair. If you have curly hair, look for products that enhance your curls without making them frizzy.

3. Avoid using too much heat - Heat can damage your hair and cause it to become frizzy and unmanageable. Try to avoid using heat tools like flat irons and blow dryers too often, and when you do use them, make sure to use a heat protectant spray.

4. Get regular haircuts - Regular haircuts can help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can contribute to hair that doesn't lay flat.

Tips for Managing Hair That Curves Outwards

- Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush, which can cause your hair to frizz.

- Consider getting a keratin treatment, which can help to smooth your hair and make it more manageable.

- Sleep on a silk pillowcase to help prevent your hair from becoming tangled and frizzy overnight.

FAQ About Hair That Curves Outwards

Q: Can genetics play a role in hair that curves outwards?

A: Yes, genetics can play a role in the texture and behavior of your hair. If your parents have hair that curves outwards, it's likely that you will too.

Q: Can using too much product cause hair that curves outwards?

A: Yes, using too much product can make your hair heavy and weighed down, which can cause it to curve outwards.

Q: Is it possible to permanently straighten hair that curves outwards?

A: Yes, there are a number of hair straightening treatments available, including keratin treatments and chemical straightening. However, these treatments can be damaging to your hair and should be done by a professional.

Hair Care Tips to Keep Your Hair Healthy

- Wash your hair regularly, but not too often.

- Use a conditioner after every shampoo to keep your hair moisturized.

- Limit your use of heat tools and always use a heat protectant spray.

- Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals to keep your hair healthy from the inside out.

Conclusion

Dealing with hair that curves outwards can be frustrating, but it's important to understand that there are a number of factors that can contribute to this issue. By following the tips and advice in this article, you can help to manage your hair and keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
