How To Make Thick Hair Less Puffy Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Oct 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thick hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it can make your hair look voluminous and healthy, it can also make it look puffy and frizzy. If you have thick hair and are tired of dealing with the puffiness, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll provide you with tips and tricks on how to make thick hair less puffy.

Table of Contents

  1. Hair Care
  2. How to Make Thick Hair Less Puffy
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips and Tricks
  5. FAQs

Hair Care

Before we dive into how to make thick hair less puffy, it's important to take care of your hair. Thick hair requires special attention and care to look its best. Here are some hair care tips for thick hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers and flat irons too often. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray first.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet. This will prevent breakage and damage.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often. This can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it look dry and frizzy.

How to Make Thick Hair Less Puffy

Now that we've covered hair care tips, let's dive into how to make thick hair less puffy. Here are some methods you can try:

  1. Use a Hair Serum: Hair serums can help to tame frizz and flyaways. Look for a serum that is specifically designed for thick hair.
  2. Avoid Touching Your Hair: The more you touch your hair, the more likely it is to become puffy. Try to avoid playing with your hair throughout the day.
  3. Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down: This will help to add volume to your roots without making your hair puffy.
  4. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment: Deep conditioning treatments can help to nourish and moisturize your hair, making it less prone to puffiness.
  5. Get Regular Haircuts: Regular haircuts can help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can lead to puffiness.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to make thick hair less puffy:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel dry your hair gently.
  3. Apply a hair serum to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  4. Blow dry your hair upside down, using a round brush to add volume to your roots.
  5. Finish with a light hairspray to keep your hair in place.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks on how to make thick hair less puffy:

  • Avoid brushing your hair too often. Brushing can stimulate the scalp and make your hair puffy.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage.
  • Avoid using too much product in your hair. This can weigh it down and make it look puffy.
  • Try using a dry shampoo to absorb excess oil and add volume to your roots.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a hair straightener to make my hair less puffy?

A: While a hair straightener can help to smooth out frizz, it can also make your hair look flat and lifeless. Instead, try using a hair serum or deep conditioning treatment to tame your hair.

Q: Can I use a hair mask to make my hair less puffy?

A: Yes, a hair mask can help to moisturize and nourish your hair, making it less prone to puffiness. Look for a mask that is specifically designed for thick hair.

Q: Can I use a volumizing shampoo to make my hair less puffy?

A: No, volumizing shampoos are designed to add volume to your hair, which can make it look puffy. Instead, opt for a sulfate-free shampoo that is gentle on your hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make your thick hair less puffy and more manageable. Remember to take care of your hair and avoid using too much heat or product. With a little bit of effort, you can have healthy, beautiful hair.
