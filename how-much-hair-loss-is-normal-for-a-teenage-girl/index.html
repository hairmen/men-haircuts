How Much Hair Loss Is Normal For A Teenage Girl

Written by William Oct 09, 2023 · 3 min read

Welcome to our blog post all about how much hair loss is normal for a teenage girl. We know that hair loss can be a worrying and distressing issue, particularly if you’re a teenager. But rest assured, it’s normal to lose some hair every day. In this post, we’ll explore how much hair loss is normal for a teenage girl, what causes hair loss, and some tips on how to care for your hair.

Table of Contents

  • How much hair loss is normal for a teenage girl?
  • What causes hair loss in teenage girls?
  • How to care for your hair
  • FAQs

How much hair loss is normal for a teenage girl?

On average, it’s normal to lose around 50-100 hairs per day. This may sound like a lot, but it’s important to remember that we all have around 100,000 hairs on our scalp. So, losing 50-100 hairs per day is just a small percentage of the total number of hairs we have.

If you’re noticing more hair loss than this, it’s worth keeping an eye on it. You could try counting the number of hairs you lose each day for a week or two to see if it’s within the normal range. If you’re losing significantly more than 100 hairs per day, it’s worth speaking to your doctor or a hair specialist to find out what’s causing it.

What causes hair loss in teenage girls?

There are many different reasons why a teenage girl might experience hair loss. Some of the most common causes include:

  • Hormonal changes: Hormonal changes during puberty can lead to hair loss. This is particularly common in teenage girls who have started taking birth control pills.
  • Nutritional deficiencies: A lack of certain vitamins and minerals, such as iron or vitamin D, can lead to hair loss.
  • Stress: Stressful events, such as exams or family problems, can cause hair loss.
  • Traction alopecia: This is hair loss caused by repeatedly pulling hair into tight hairstyles, such as braids or ponytails.
  • Alopecia areata: This is an autoimmune condition that causes hair loss in patches.

How to care for your hair

Looking after your hair is important, particularly if you’re experiencing hair loss. Here are some tips to help keep your hair healthy:

  • Eat a healthy diet: Make sure you’re getting enough vitamins and minerals in your diet, particularly iron and vitamin D.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles: Avoid pulling your hair into tight hairstyles that could cause traction alopecia.
  • Be gentle: Be gentle when brushing and styling your hair, and avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Wash your hair regularly: Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.

FAQs

Q: Is it normal to lose hair as a teenager?

A: Yes, it’s normal to lose some hair every day. On average, we lose around 50-100 hairs per day.

Q: How can I tell if I’m losing too much hair?

A: If you’re losing significantly more than 100 hairs per day, it’s worth speaking to your doctor or a hair specialist to find out what’s causing it.

Q: Will my hair grow back if I’m experiencing hair loss?

A: This depends on the cause of your hair loss. In some cases, hair will grow back once the underlying issue has been treated. In other cases, hair loss may be permanent.

We hope you’ve found this post helpful. Remember, if you’re concerned about hair loss, it’s always worth speaking to a doctor or hair specialist to find out what’s causing it.
