How To Contour And Oval Face In 2023 A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Nov 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've been following the beauty industry, you've probably heard of contouring. This makeup technique has been around for a while and has become a staple in many makeup routines. The idea behind contouring is to use light and dark shades to sculpt and define the face. In this article, we will explore how to contour and oval face, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Contour and Oval Face
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Contouring an Oval Face
  • FAQs

Description

Before we dive into the specifics of how to contour and oval face, let's talk about what an oval face shape looks like. An oval face is longer than it is wide and has a gently rounded hairline and jawline. This face shape is considered one of the most versatile and is often described as the ideal face shape. However, contouring can help enhance the natural features of an oval face and create a more defined look.

How to Contour and Oval Face

Contouring an oval face involves using a combination of light and dark shades to create depth and dimension. The goal is to enhance the natural structure of the face and create the illusion of more defined cheekbones, a slimmer nose, and a sharper jawline. Here are the steps to contour an oval face:

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Start with a clean, moisturized face. Apply your foundation as usual and blend it well to create a smooth base.

Step 2: Choose a contour shade that is one to two shades darker than your skin tone. Apply this shade below your cheekbones, along your temples, and under your jawline. Blend well to create a natural-looking shadow.

Step 3: Apply a highlight shade that is one to two shades lighter than your skin tone. Apply this shade on the high points of your face, such as your forehead, bridge of your nose, cheekbones, and cupid's bow. Blend well to create a subtle glow.

Step 4: Use a blush to add a pop of color to the apples of your cheeks. Smile and apply the blush to the roundest part of your cheek, blending it out towards your temples.

Step 5: Finish with a setting powder to lock everything in place.

Tips for Contouring an Oval Face

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your contouring routine:

  • Choose a contour shade that is cool-toned to create a more natural-looking shadow.
  • Blend, blend, and blend some more. The key to a natural-looking contour is to blend it well.
  • Use a light hand when applying your contour shade. You can always build up the intensity, but it's harder to remove too much product.
  • Make sure to apply your contour shade in natural light to ensure it looks seamless.

FAQs

Q: Can I contour if I have an oval face?

A: Yes, contouring can enhance the natural features of an oval face and create a more defined look.

Q: What products do I need to contour an oval face?

A: You'll need a contour shade, a highlight shade, a blush, and a setting powder.

Q: How do I choose the right contour shade?

A: Choose a shade that is one to two shades darker than your skin tone and cool-toned for a more natural-looking shadow.

Hair Care for Oval Faces

While contouring can help enhance the natural features of an oval face, your hairstyle can also make a big difference. Here are some tips for hair care for oval faces:

  • Choose a hairstyle that adds volume to the sides of your face to balance out your features.
  • Avoid hairstyles that are too long and straight, as they can elongate the face.
  • Consider adding layers to your hair to create movement and dimension.
  • Avoid center parts, as they can make your face appear longer.

Now that you know how to contour and oval face, you can enhance your natural features and create a more defined look. Remember to choose shades that are one to two shades darker and lighter than your skin tone, blend well, and use a light hand when applying your contour shade. With these tips, you'll be contouring like a pro in no time!
