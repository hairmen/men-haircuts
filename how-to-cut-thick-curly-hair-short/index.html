How To Cut Thick Curly Hair Short

Written by Luffy Jul 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have thick curly hair and you're looking to cut it short, you might be feeling a bit intimidated. Cutting curly hair requires a different approach than cutting straight hair, and thick hair can be particularly challenging to work with. However, with the right techniques and tools, you can achieve a stylish and flattering short cut that works for your hair type.

Description

In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut thick curly hair short. We will also share some tips and tricks to ensure that you get the best results possible. Additionally, we will answer some frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair and provide some tips for taking care of your new short cut.

How to Cut Thick Curly Hair Short: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to make sure that your hair is clean and dry. It's also a good idea to detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush. This will make it easier to section your hair and ensure that you cut it evenly.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Once your hair is detangled, you'll want to section it into manageable sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, create two more sections on each side of your head, using your ear as a guide. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Starting with one section, take a small section of hair and twist it tightly. Use your scissors to cut off the ends, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Repeat this process for each section, making sure to cut evenly and not take off too much at once.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all of your hair, you'll want to blend the layers to create a more cohesive look. Take small sections of hair and use your scissors to cut into the ends, creating soft layers that blend together.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with the length and shape of your new cut, it's time to style your hair. Use a curling iron or diffuser attachment to enhance your natural curls and add volume. You can also use styling products like mousse or gel to help define your curls and keep them in place.

Tips for Cutting Thick Curly Hair Short

Here are a few additional tips to help you get the best results when cutting thick curly hair short:

  • Don't cut too much at once - it's better to take off small sections of hair and work your way up gradually
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut
  • Consider getting your hair cut by a professional stylist who has experience working with curly hair
  • Be patient - cutting curly hair can take longer than cutting straight hair, but the results are worth it!

FAQ: Cutting Curly Hair

Q: Can I cut curly hair when it's wet or dry?

A: It's generally best to cut curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be misleading and may result in uneven cuts. However, some people prefer to cut their hair when it's wet, so it really depends on what works best for you.

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: It's recommended that you cut curly hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Q: What's the best way to take care of my new short cut?

A: To keep your new short cut looking its best, it's important to use hair products that are designed for curly hair. You should also avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and cause it to lose its natural curl pattern.

Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

Finally, it's important to take good care of your thick curly hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished
  • Avoid using shampoos that contain sulfates, as these can strip your hair of its natural oils
  • Use a diffuser attachment when blow-drying your hair to prevent frizz
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and flattering short cut that works for your thick curly hair. Whether you decide to cut your hair yourself or go to a professional stylist, be patient and take your time to ensure that you get the best results possible.
