How To Get Thick Hair To Lay Back A Step By Step Guide

Do you have thick hair that just won't lay back no matter what you do? It can be frustrating to spend hours trying to style your hair, only to have it fall flat within minutes. In this article, we'll show you how to get thick hair to lay back and stay in place all day long.

How to Get Thick Hair to Lay Back

The key to getting thick hair to lay back is to use the right styling products and techniques. Here are some tips to help you achieve the look you want:

1. Use a Strong Hold Pomade or Wax

One of the best ways to get thick hair to lay back is to use a strong hold pomade or wax. These products are designed to provide a firm hold that will keep your hair in place all day long. Look for products that are specifically designed for thick hair, as they will be more effective at controlling your hair's volume and texture.

2. Blow Dry Your Hair Backwards

Before applying any styling products, it's important to start with clean, dry hair. Use a blow dryer to dry your hair backwards, away from your face. This will help to create a smooth, sleek base for your styling products to work on.

3. Apply Product to Damp Hair

Once your hair is dry, apply a small amount of pomade or wax to your fingertips and rub it between your palms to warm it up. Then, apply the product to your hair, starting at the back and working your way forward. Be sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair to ensure that every strand is coated.

4. Comb Your Hair Backwards

Using a fine-toothed comb, comb your hair backwards to create the desired style. Be gentle and take your time, making sure to smooth out any bumps or tangles as you go. Once you've achieved the desired look, use a hair dryer on a low setting to help set the style in place.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting your thick hair to lay back:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Blow dry your hair backwards, away from your face.
  3. Apply a small amount of pomade or wax to your fingertips and rub it between your palms to warm it up.
  4. Apply the product to your hair, starting at the back and working your way forward.
  5. Use a fine-toothed comb to comb your hair backwards.
  6. Smooth out any bumps or tangles as you go.
  7. Use a hair dryer on a low setting to help set the style in place.
  8. Repeat steps 3-7 if necessary to achieve the desired look.
  9. Finish with a light hairspray to help hold the style in place.

Tips for Styling Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you style your thick hair:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for thick hair to help control volume and texture.
  • Don't over-wash your hair, as this can strip away natural oils and make your hair more difficult to style.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find the one that works best for your hair type.
  • Consider getting a haircut that is specifically designed for thick hair, as this can make it easier to style.

FAQs

Q: Can I still get my hair to lay back if it's really curly?

A: Yes, you can still get your hair to lay back even if it's curly. However, you may need to use a different styling product or technique to achieve the desired look. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair type.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It's generally recommended that you wash your hair every 2-3 days. However, this can vary depending on your hair type and lifestyle. If you exercise regularly or live in a humid climate, you may need to wash your hair more frequently.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your thick hair:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for thick hair.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip away natural oils and make your hair more difficult to style.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as this can damage your hair and make it more difficult to style.
  • Get regular haircuts to help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and techniques, you can get your thick hair to lay back and stay in place all day long. With a little bit of practice and experimentation, you'll be able to achieve the perfect style for any occasion.
