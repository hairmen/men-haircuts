How To Fade A Men s Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 31, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, a fade is a great option. Fades offer a clean and modern look that can be customized to your preferences. In this guide, we'll go over the steps to achieving a fade haircut, as well as some tips and FAQs.

Description

A fade haircut involves gradually transitioning from short hair at the bottom to longer hair at the top. The transition can be subtle or dramatic, depending on your preference. Fades can be done with any length of hair, from short buzz cuts to longer styles. They can also be combined with other haircuts, such as a pompadour or a crew cut.

How to Fade a Men's Haircut

Here are the steps to achieving a fade haircut:

Step 1: Choose the Right Clippers

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. Clippers are essential for achieving a fade. Look for clippers that come with guards of different lengths, as well as a taper lever. The taper lever allows you to adjust the length of the clippers' blades for more precise cutting.

Step 2: Start with the Bottom

Begin cutting the hair at the bottom of your head, using the shortest guard. Use the clippers to create a straight line around the back of your head, just above the hairline. This will be the shortest point of your fade.

Step 3: Move Up the Head

Gradually switch to longer guards as you move up the head. Use a comb as a guide to create a smooth transition between the shorter and longer lengths. You can also use the taper lever to blend the different lengths together.

Step 4: Define the Top

Once you've created the fade, use scissors to trim the hair on top of your head to your desired length. You can leave it longer for a more dramatic contrast, or keep it short for a subtle look. Use a comb to create a part or style the hair as desired.

Tips

Here are some tips for achieving the perfect fade:

Tip 1: Take Your Time

Fades require patience and precision. Take your time and don't rush the process. It's better to go slowly and get it right than to make a mistake and have to start over.

Tip 2: Practice Makes Perfect

If you're new to cutting your own hair, it may take some practice to achieve the perfect fade. Don't get discouraged if it doesn't turn out perfectly the first time. Keep practicing and you'll get better with each attempt.

Tip 3: Use a Mirror

Use a mirror to check your progress as you go. It can be helpful to have a second mirror behind you to see the back of your head.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about fading men's haircuts:

What is the difference between a fade and a taper?

A fade involves gradually transitioning from short hair at the bottom to longer hair at the top, while a taper involves gradually decreasing the length of hair from the bottom to the top. Fades tend to be more dramatic and noticeable than tapers.

Can you fade long hair?

Yes, you can fade long hair. The process is the same as fading short hair, but you'll need to use longer guards on your clippers. Keep in mind that the longer your hair, the more gradual the fade will be.

How often should you get a fade?

How often you get a fade depends on how quickly your hair grows and how long you want to maintain the style. Some men get a fade every 2-3 weeks, while others can go several months between fades.

Hair Care

To maintain your fade, you'll need to keep your hair clean and well-groomed. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type, and avoid over-washing, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils. Keep your hair trimmed regularly to prevent split ends and maintain the style.

With these tips and steps, you can achieve a fade haircut that suits your style and preferences. Remember to take your time and practice, and you'll soon be a pro at fading men's haircuts!
