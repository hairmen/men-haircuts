How To Cut 1 Year Old Boy Hair With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a parent or caregiver of a 1-year-old boy, you might be wondering how to cut his hair with scissors. While it can be challenging to cut a wiggly toddler's hair, it's not impossible with the right tools and techniques. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut 1 year old boy hair with scissors, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cut 1 Year Old Boy Hair with Scissors
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting 1 Year Old Boy Hair with Scissors
  • FAQ about Cutting 1 Year Old Boy Hair with Scissors
  • Hair Care for 1 Year Old Boys

How to Cut 1 Year Old Boy Hair with Scissors

The first step in cutting a 1-year-old boy's hair with scissors is to gather the necessary tools. You'll need:

  • Sharp scissors made for cutting hair
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A comb
  • A towel or cape to protect clothes

Once you have your tools, follow these steps:

Step by Step Guide:

Step 1: Wet your child's hair with the spray bottle. This will make it easier to cut and prevent flyaways.

Step 2: Comb through the hair to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 3: Section off the hair into manageable sections. You can use clips or elastics to keep the hair in place.

Step 4: Start with the back of the head and work your way forward. Use your fingers to hold the hair in place and cut in a straight line. It's best to start with small snips and work your way up to the desired length.

Step 5: Move to the sides of the head and cut in a similar fashion, using your fingers to hold the hair in place and cutting in a straight line.

Step 6: Finally, trim the hair on top of the head. You can use the comb to lift sections of hair and cut in a straight line.

Step 7: Check the hair for any uneven sections and trim as needed.

Tips for Cutting 1 Year Old Boy Hair with Scissors:

Here are some tips to make the process of cutting a 1-year-old boy's hair with scissors easier:

  • Choose a time when your child is well-rested and fed. A tired or hungry child is more likely to be fussy during the haircut.
  • Use distraction techniques, such as toys or videos, to keep your child entertained and still during the haircut.
  • Don't worry about getting a perfect haircut. It's more important to make sure your child is comfortable and safe during the process.
  • If your child is particularly fussy or wiggly, consider asking a friend or family member to help hold them still during the haircut.

FAQ about Cutting 1 Year Old Boy Hair with Scissors:

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting a 1-year-old boy's hair with scissors:

  • Is it safe to cut a 1-year-old boy's hair with scissors? Yes, as long as you use the proper tools and techniques and keep your child still and safe during the process.
  • Do I need to use special scissors to cut a 1-year-old boy's hair? Yes, it's best to use scissors made specifically for cutting hair to ensure a clean cut and prevent split ends.
  • How often should I cut a 1-year-old boy's hair? This can vary depending on how fast your child's hair grows. It's recommended to trim their hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for 1 Year Old Boys:

In addition to cutting your 1-year-old boy's hair, it's important to take care of their hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner made for babies or children.
  • Brush their hair regularly to remove any tangles or knots.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on their hair, such as blow dryers or straighteners.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep their hair moisturized.

By following these tips and techniques, you can successfully cut your 1-year-old boy's hair with scissors and keep their hair healthy and strong.
