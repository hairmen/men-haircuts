How Long Does It Take To Freeform Dreads

Written by Christine Sep 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Freeform dreads, also known as organic or natural locks, are a popular hairstyle that involves letting your hair grow and mat together without any manipulation or maintenance. This style is often associated with the Rastafarian culture, but it has gained popularity among people of all races and backgrounds.

In this article, we will discuss how long it takes to freeform dreads, how to start the process, and provide tips for hair care and maintenance.

Table of Contents

  • How to Start Freeform Dreads
  • Timeline for Freeform Dreads
  • Tips for Hair Care and Maintenance
  • FAQs

How to Start Freeform Dreads

The first step to starting freeform dreads is to stop using combs, brushes, and any other tools that can manipulate your hair. This will allow your hair to naturally mat together and form dreadlocks. It is important to note that this process can be messy and may take some time to achieve the desired look.

Here are some tips to help you start your freeform dread journey:

  • Stop brushing or combing your hair
  • Avoid using hair products that contain oils or moisturizers
  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo
  • Avoid using a towel to dry your hair, use a t-shirt or microfiber towel instead to reduce frizz
  • Avoid using heat tools or hair dryers as they can damage your hair and slow down the locking process

Timeline for Freeform Dreads

The timeline for freeform dreads varies depending on your hair type, length, and texture. Generally, it takes around 6 months to a year for the dreads to fully form and mature. However, it can take up to 2-3 years to achieve the desired length and thickness.

Here is a general timeline for freeform dreads:

  • Month 1-3: Your hair will begin to tangle and coil together, forming small knots
  • Month 4-6: The knots will start to tighten and form into small baby dreads
  • Month 7-12: The dreads will continue to mature and become more defined
  • Year 2-3: Your dreads will reach their desired length and thickness

Tips for Hair Care and Maintenance

Freeform dreads require minimal maintenance, but it is important to take care of your hair to prevent breakage and promote healthy growth. Here are some tips for hair care and maintenance:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo to prevent buildup
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to moisturize your hair and prevent dryness
  • Retwist your dreads every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape and prevent them from becoming too loose
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair as they can damage your locks
  • Avoid sleeping on your back to prevent flattening your dreads, use a satin or silk pillowcase to reduce friction

FAQs

How often should I wash my freeform dreads?

You should wash your freeform dreads every 1-2 weeks with a residue-free shampoo to prevent buildup and promote healthy hair growth.

Can I start freeform dreads with short hair?

Yes, you can start freeform dreads with short hair. The process may take longer, but with patience and proper hair care, your hair will eventually lock and form dreadlocks.

Can I wear hats or headscarves with freeform dreads?

Yes, you can wear hats or headscarves with freeform dreads. Just make sure to choose materials that are breathable and won't cause your hair to become too dry or tangled.

Do I need to trim my freeform dreads?

You do not need to trim your freeform dreads. However, if you notice any split ends or damaged hair, it is recommended to trim them to prevent further damage.


In conclusion, starting freeform dreads is a process that requires patience and dedication. The timeline for freeform dreads varies depending on your hair type and texture, but with proper hair care and maintenance, your hair will eventually lock and form into beautiful dreadlocks. Remember to be patient, embrace the journey, and enjoy your new hairstyle!
