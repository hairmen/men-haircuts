How To Ask A Barber For A Taper Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Oct 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a fresh and stylish haircut, a taper fade is a great option. This haircut is versatile and can be customized to suit your personal style. However, it's important to know how to ask your barber for a taper fade to get the best results. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to ask your barber for a taper fade, as well as tips and FAQs.

Table of Contents:

  1. How to Ask for a Taper Fade
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Getting a Great Taper Fade
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care for a Taper Fade

How to Ask for a Taper Fade

When you're at the barbershop, it's important to communicate clearly with your barber about the type of haircut you want. Here are some tips on how to ask for a taper fade:

  • Be specific: Use clear and concise language to describe the type of fade you want. For example, you can say "I want a low skin fade" or "I want a high taper fade."
  • Show pictures: Bring in pictures of the type of taper fade you want. This will help your barber visualize the look you're going for.
  • Communicate your preferences: Let your barber know how short you want the sides and back, as well as how much hair you want to leave on top. You can also discuss the length of the fade and any other styling preferences you have.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know how to ask your barber for a taper fade, here's a step-by-step guide on what to expect during the haircut:

  1. Wash your hair: Your barber will start by washing your hair to remove any dirt or product buildup.
  2. Section your hair: Your barber will section off your hair and clip it to keep it out of the way while they work on the fade.
  3. Start the fade: Your barber will start with a longer guard on their clippers and gradually work their way down to a shorter guard to create the fade.
  4. Blend the fade: Your barber will use a comb and scissors to blend the fade and make it look seamless.
  5. Trim the top: Your barber will use scissors to trim the hair on top to your desired length.
  6. Style your hair: Your barber will style your hair and apply any products you want, such as gel or pomade.

Tips for Getting a Great Taper Fade

Here are some tips to help you get the best possible taper fade:

  • Choose a reputable barber: Look for a barber with experience in cutting fades who can give you the look you want.
  • Be patient: A good taper fade takes time, so be prepared to spend at least 30 minutes in the chair.
  • Maintain your haircut: To keep your taper fade looking sharp, schedule regular appointments with your barber and follow a hair care routine at home.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about taper fades:

What is a taper fade?

A taper fade is a haircut where the hair gradually gets shorter from the top of the head down to the hairline. The hair is typically longer on top and shorter on the sides and back.

What's the difference between a taper fade and a regular fade?

A taper fade is a more gradual fade that blends the hair from longer to shorter. A regular fade is a more abrupt transition from longer to shorter hair.

How often should I get a taper fade?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain your haircut. As a general rule, most people should get a taper fade every 2-4 weeks.

Hair Care for a Taper Fade

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair after getting a taper fade:

  • Use a good shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Style your hair with a comb or brush, and use a hair dryer if necessary.
  • Maintain regular appointments with your barber to keep your taper fade looking sharp.

Now that you know how to ask your barber for a taper fade, you can confidently walk into any barbershop and get the fresh haircut you deserve. Remember to communicate clearly with your barber, be patient during the process, and maintain your haircut with regular appointments and proper hair care.
