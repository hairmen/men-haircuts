How To Cut Teenage Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a parent, you may be wondering how to cut teenage hair at home. With the right tools and techniques, it is possible to give your teenager a great haircut without having to go to a salon. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps of cutting your teenager's hair at home, as well as provide tips and advice for achieving the perfect look.

How to Cut Teenage Hair at Home

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting your teenager's hair at home is to gather all the necessary tools. These include:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Cape or old towel
  • Spray bottle with water

Make sure that your tools are clean and sharp before starting. This will make the haircut easier and more accurate.

Step 2: Choose the Right Style

Before you start cutting your teenager's hair, it's important to decide on the right style. Talk to your teenager about what they want and look for inspiration online. Make sure that the style you choose is achievable and fits your teenager's face shape and hair type.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Once you have chosen the style, it's time to section the hair. Use the comb to create a center part and then divide the hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Secure each section with a clip.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting with the back sections, use the scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. Use the comb to guide the scissors and make sure that the hair is even. Repeat this process with the front sections.

If you are using clippers, start with a longer guard and work your way down to the desired length. Use the comb to lift the hair and the clippers to trim it.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you have cut all the sections, it's time to blend the hair. Use the scissors or clippers to blend the top and sides of the hair, making sure that there are no visible lines or uneven sections.

Step 6: Check for Mistakes

Before you finish the haircut, check for any mistakes or uneven sections. Use the scissors or clippers to make any necessary adjustments.

Step 7: Clean Up

After you have finished the haircut, it's important to clean up. Remove the clips and shake out the hair. Use the comb and scissors to trim any stray hairs and clean up the neck and ear areas.

Tips for Cutting Teenage Hair at Home

  • Start with a longer length and work your way down. You can always cut more, but you can't add hair back once it's been cut.
  • Use the comb to guide the scissors and clippers. This will help you achieve a more even cut.
  • Don't cut too much at once. Take small sections and make sure that each one is even before moving on.
  • Watch tutorials online to learn new techniques and styles.

FAQs

How often should I cut my teenager's hair?

It depends on the style and how fast their hair grows. As a general rule, you should cut their hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and length.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting their hair?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. Use the scissors or clippers to make any necessary adjustments. If the mistake is severe, you may need to take them to a salon to fix it.

Hair Care

After you have cut your teenager's hair, it's important to take care of it. Make sure that they wash and condition their hair regularly and use a heat protectant if they use styling tools. Trim their hair regularly to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to cut teenage hair at home, you can save money and give your teenager a great haircut. With the right tools, techniques, and tips, you can achieve a professional-looking result without leaving your home.
