How To Cut Your Own Naturally Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 09, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune on salon visits just to trim your naturally curly hair? Or perhaps you're hesitant to trust a new stylist with your precious locks. Whatever your reason, cutting your own naturally curly hair is a great way to save money and have more control over your style. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a gorgeous haircut without leaving your home.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Naturally Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Successful Cut
  • FAQs

How to Cut Your Own Naturally Curly Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's go over some things you'll need to prepare:

  • Sharp hair cutting shears
  • Comb or detangling brush
  • Clips or hair ties to section your hair
  • A well-lit area with a mirror

Now that you have your tools ready, let's get started!

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with clean, detangled hair. It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can stretch and lead to uneven cuts. Use a comb or detangling brush to remove any knots or tangles.

Step 2: Section your hair. Use clips or hair ties to divide your hair into manageable sections. You can do this by creating a middle part and dividing your hair into left and right sections, then further dividing each side into top and bottom sections.

Step 3: Cut the first section. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your hair cutting shears to cut the ends at a slight angle, trimming off any split ends or damaged hair.

Step 4: Work your way through all the sections. Continue cutting small sections of hair and working your way through each section until you've trimmed all your hair.

Step 5: Check for balance. Once you've finished cutting, check to make sure both sides are balanced. If you notice any unevenness, make small adjustments until your hair looks even.

Tips for a Successful Cut

Tip 1: Start with small cuts. It's better to take small sections of hair and make several small cuts than to take a large section and cut too much at once.

Tip 2: Use a mirror. It can be helpful to have a mirror in front of you and behind you so you can see all angles of your hair.

Tip 3: Take breaks. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so take frequent breaks to rest your arms and hands.

FAQs

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep your ends healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut my hair when it's wet?

A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry to avoid uneven cuts due to stretching.

Q: How do I know which length to cut?

A: It's best to start with small cuts and gradually work your way to your desired length.

Hair Care Tips

Tip 1: Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your curly hair hydrated and healthy.

Tip 2: Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and reduce frizz.

Tip 3: Avoid using heat tools on your hair, as this can damage your curls.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to cut your own naturally curly hair with confidence and ease. Happy cutting!
