How To Add Volume To Flat Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia Jun 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Flat curly hair can be frustrating to deal with, especially if you're looking for volume and bounce. Fortunately, there are several ways to add volume to your hair without damaging it. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to add volume to flat curly hair.

Description

Flat curly hair can be caused by several factors, including genetics, lifestyle habits, and hair care routines. While some people are born with naturally flat hair, others may experience flatness due to over-styling, heat damage, or lack of proper hair care. Whatever the cause, adding volume to flat curly hair can help you achieve a fuller, more voluminous look.

How to Add Volume to Flat Curly Hair

1. Choose the Right Products

The right hair products can make a big difference in adding volume to flat curly hair. Look for volumizing shampoos and conditioners that are formulated to add body and fullness to your hair. Additionally, using a lightweight mousse or styling foam can help create volume and hold without weighing down your curls.

2. Use the Right Tools

Flat irons and straighteners can make your hair look sleek and smooth, but they can also flatten your curls and make your hair appear thinner. Instead, use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your curls and create volume. Additionally, using a round brush when blow-drying your hair can help add lift and body to your curls.

3. Try a Haircut

A fresh haircut can do wonders for adding volume to flat curly hair. Ask your stylist for layers and texture to create movement and bounce. Additionally, adding face-framing layers can help draw attention to your curls and create the illusion of more volume.

4. Experiment with Hairstyles

Experimenting with different hairstyles can help you find the right look to add volume to your flat curly hair. Try wearing your hair in a high ponytail or top knot to create height and volume on top. Alternatively, wearing your hair down with a deep side part can help create the illusion of more volume.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with volumizing products. Rinse thoroughly and gently squeeze out excess water.

Step 2: Apply Styling Products

Apply a lightweight mousse or styling foam to your damp hair, focusing on the roots and mid-lengths. Gently scrunch your hair to distribute the product.

Step 3: Diffuse Your Curls

Attach a diffuser to your hair dryer and use it to dry your hair, focusing on your roots. Use a low heat and low speed setting to prevent heat damage.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, use your fingers to gently tousle your curls and create volume. You can also use a round brush to lift your curls and create more body.

Tips for Adding Volume to Flat Curly Hair

- Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your curls
- Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and tangling
- Avoid using hot tools on high heat, as this can cause heat damage
- Consider using a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove buildup and refresh your curls
- Don't over-brush your hair, as this can flatten your curls and create frizz
- Experiment with different hairstyles to find the right look for your hair type and face shape

FAQ

Q: Can I add volume to my flat curly hair without using heat?

A: Yes, you can add volume to your hair by using a volumizing mousse or styling foam and letting your hair air dry. You can also try using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer on a low heat setting.

Q: Can I add volume to my flat curly hair if I have fine hair?

A: Yes, you can add volume to fine curly hair by using lightweight styling products and avoiding heavy conditioners and oils that can weigh down your hair. Additionally, getting a haircut with layers can help create movement and bounce.

Hair Care Tips for Flat Curly Hair

- Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent stripping your hair of natural oils
- Avoid using hot tools on high heat, as this can cause heat damage
- Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it healthy and hydrated
- Avoid using hair ties that can pull on your curls and create breakage
- Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the health of your curls
