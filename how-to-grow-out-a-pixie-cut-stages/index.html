How To Grow Out A Pixie Cut Stages A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jun 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've recently chopped off your hair into a pixie cut, you may be wondering how to grow it out. The process can be frustrating, but with patience and the right techniques, you can achieve your desired look. In this guide, we'll go through the different stages of growing out a pixie cut and provide tips and tricks to make the process easier.

Table of Contents

  1. Stage 1: The Awkward Stage
  2. Stage 2: The Shaggy Stage
  3. Stage 3: The Bob Stage
  4. Stage 4: The Shoulder-Length Stage
  5. Tips for Growing Out a Pixie Cut
  6. FAQs
  7. Hair Care Tips for Growing Out a Pixie Cut

Stage 1: The Awkward Stage

The first stage of growing out a pixie cut is the awkward stage. This is when your hair is too short to style, but too long to keep its shape. Your hair may appear uneven and messy, which can be frustrating. The best thing to do during this stage is to be patient and resist the urge to chop off more hair.

During the awkward stage, you can experiment with hair accessories and hats to add some flair to your hair. You can also use hair products to tame your hair, such as wax or pomade. This will help keep your hair in place and make it easier to style.

Stage 2: The Shaggy Stage

The second stage of growing out a pixie cut is the shaggy stage. This is when your hair is a few inches longer and can be styled in different ways. Your hair may appear messy and tousled, but this is the perfect time to experiment with different hairstyles.

During the shaggy stage, you can try different haircuts, such as a layered bob or a pixie with bangs. You can also experiment with different hair colors and highlights to add dimension to your hair.

Stage 3: The Bob Stage

The third stage of growing out a pixie cut is the bob stage. This is when your hair is long enough to be styled in a bob. Your hair may still appear uneven, but you can use hair products to add volume and texture.

During the bob stage, you can experiment with different lengths and styles, such as an asymmetrical bob or a blunt bob. You can also add layers to your hair to add texture and movement.

Stage 4: The Shoulder-Length Stage

The fourth and final stage of growing out a pixie cut is the shoulder-length stage. This is when your hair is long enough to be styled in different ways, such as braids and updos. Your hair may still appear uneven, but you can use hair products to add volume and texture.

During the shoulder-length stage, you can experiment with different haircuts, such as a lob or a long bob. You can also add layers to your hair to add volume and movement.

Tips for Growing Out a Pixie Cut

Here are some tips to make the process of growing out a pixie cut easier:

  • Be patient and resist the urge to chop off more hair
  • Experiment with different hairstyles and haircuts
  • Use hair accessories and hats to add flair to your hair
  • Use hair products to tame your hair and add volume and texture
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote hair growth

FAQs

How long does it take to grow out a pixie cut?

The amount of time it takes to grow out a pixie cut depends on your hair's growth rate. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month. It can take anywhere from 6 months to a year to grow out a pixie cut.

Should I trim my hair while growing it out?

It's important to get regular trims while growing out a pixie cut to maintain healthy hair. Trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks will help prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

How can I style my hair during the awkward stage?

You can use hair accessories and hats to add flair to your hair during the awkward stage. You can also use hair products, such as wax or pomade, to tame your hair and make it easier to style.

Hair Care Tips for Growing Out a Pixie Cut

Here are some hair care tips to promote healthy hair growth:

  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to nourish your hair from the inside out
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can cause damage to your hair
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth
  • Use hair masks and treatments to nourish and hydrate your hair

Growing out a pixie cut can be a challenging process, but with patience and the right techniques, you can achieve your desired look. By following these tips and tricks, you can make the process easier and promote healthy hair growth.
