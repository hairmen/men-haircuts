How To Fix Roots With Ombre Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 09, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're sporting ombre hair, you know how much of a statement it can make. However, after a few weeks or months, your roots will start to show and it can ruin the look you were going for. Don't worry though! We've got you covered with this step-by-step guide on how to fix roots with ombre hair.

Description

Ombre hair is a popular trend that has been around for a few years now. It's a hairstyle where the hair is darker at the roots and gradually gets lighter towards the ends. The transition between the colors is usually smooth and blended, giving a natural look. However, as the hair grows, the roots will start to show, and it can be hard to maintain the ombre effect. In this article, we'll show you how to fix roots with ombre hair, so you can keep your hair looking fabulous.

How to Fix Roots with Ombre Hair

Step 1: Choose the Right Color

The first step in fixing roots with ombre hair is to choose the right color. You want to pick a color that is close to the color of the ends of your hair. If you're unsure, it's always best to go a shade lighter, as you can always add more color later.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Before you start coloring your hair, you need to section it. Use clips to separate your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. This will make it easier to apply the color evenly.

Step 3: Apply the Color

Now it's time to apply the color to your roots. Start at the back of your head and work your way to the front. Use a brush to apply the color to your roots, making sure to cover all the gray hairs or new growth.

Step 4: Blend the Color

Once you've applied the color, you need to blend it into the rest of your hair. Use a comb or your fingers to gently blend the color into the ombre effect. Be careful not to brush too hard, or you'll end up with a harsh line between the colors.

Step 5: Rinse and Condition

After you've blended the color, it's time to rinse it out. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water until the water runs clear. Then, use a deep conditioner to nourish your hair and keep it healthy.

Tips

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to keep your color looking fresh
  • Avoid washing your hair every day to prevent the color from fading
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair to prevent further damage

FAQ

How often should I touch up my roots?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows. On average, most people need to touch up their roots every 4-6 weeks.

Can I fix my roots at home?

Yes, you can fix your roots at home. However, if you're not confident in your skills, it's always best to go to a professional.

What if I want to change the color of my ombre?

If you want to change the color of your ombre, it's best to go to a professional. They can help you achieve the color you want without damaging your hair.

Hair Care

To keep your ombre hair looking fabulous, it's important to take good care of it. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a deep conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and healthy. Avoid using hot tools on your hair, and try to wash it every other day to prevent the color from fading. With the right care, you can keep your ombre hair looking fabulous for weeks to come.
