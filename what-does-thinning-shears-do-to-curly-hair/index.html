What Do Thinning Shears Do To Curly Hair

Written by Frank Oct 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it can be voluminous and bouncy, it can also be challenging to manage, especially when it comes to thinning it out. This is where thinning shears come in. In this article, we'll be discussing what thinning shears do to curly hair and how to use them to achieve the perfect look.

Description

Thinning shears are scissors that have teeth on one blade and a sharp edge on the other. They are used to remove bulk from hair without changing the overall length. When used on curly hair, thinning shears can help to reduce bulk and create a more even texture. They are especially useful for people with thick curly hair who want to reduce the amount of hair they have while maintaining the length.

How To Use Thinning Shears On Curly Hair

Using thinning shears on curly hair is not difficult, but it does require some skill and knowledge. Here are some tips on how to use thinning shears on curly hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Make sure it is completely dry before you begin.

2. Section your hair into several parts, depending on how thick your hair is. Use hair clips to secure each section.

3. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and use the thinning shears to cut into the hair. Be sure to start at the bottom of the section and work your way up. Repeat this process on each section of hair.

4. Once you have thinned out all the sections of hair, style your hair as desired.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Curly Hair

- Use sharp thinning shears to avoid damaging your hair.

- Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up to larger sections.

- Be careful not to cut too much hair at once. It's always better to cut less than to cut too much.

- If you're not sure how to use thinning shears, it's best to go to a professional stylist.

FAQ

Q: Can thinning shears damage curly hair?

A: Thinning shears can damage curly hair if not used properly. It's essential to use sharp thinning shears and to cut small sections of hair at a time.

Q: How often should I use thinning shears on my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. It's best to consult with a professional stylist to determine how often you should use thinning shears.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

- Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.

- Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.

- Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized.

- Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to prevent frizz.

- Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

Thinning shears can be a useful tool for managing curly hair. They can help to reduce bulk and create a more even texture. However, it's essential to use them properly to avoid damaging your hair. If you're not sure how to use thinning shears, it's best to consult with a professional stylist. Remember to take care of your curly hair by using the right products and techniques, and your hair will look and feel great.
