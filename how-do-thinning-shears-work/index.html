How Do Thinning Shears Work

Written by Luffy Nov 07, 2023 · 4 min read

Thinning shears are a type of hair-cutting tool that is commonly used by hairstylists to create texture, add volume, and remove bulk from hair. But how do thinning shears actually work? In this article, we’ll take a closer look at the mechanics of thinning shears and how they can be used to achieve different haircuts and styles.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Use Thinning Shears
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Using Thinning Shears
  • FAQ

Description

Thinning shears, also known as texturizing shears or blending shears, are scissors that have teeth on one or both blades. These teeth are designed to cut some of the hair while leaving some strands untouched, which helps to create a softer, more natural look. The more teeth on the blade, the more hair is cut with each snip. Thinning shears can be used on any hair type, but they are particularly useful for thick, heavy hair that can be difficult to manage.

How to Use Thinning Shears

Using thinning shears is a technique that requires practice and skill. Before you start, it’s important to have a clear idea of the haircut you want to achieve and the areas of the hair that need to be thinned out. It’s also important to use the right type of thinning shears for your hair type and the desired effect.

Select the Right Shears

Thinning shears come in different sizes and with different numbers of teeth. The more teeth the shears have, the thinner the strands of hair that will be cut. For fine hair, choose a pair of shears with fewer teeth. For thick hair, choose a pair with more teeth. It’s also a good idea to choose a pair of shears with sharp blades that are well-maintained.

Section the Hair

Before you start cutting, it’s important to section the hair into manageable sections. Use clips or hair ties to separate the hair into sections that are easy to work on.

Start Cutting

Hold the shears at a 45-degree angle and cut into the hair, making sure to leave some strands untouched. Move the shears up and down the length of the hair, cutting some strands while leaving others. The more teeth on the blade, the more hair will be cut. Repeat the process on each section of hair until you achieve the desired effect.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to use thinning shears:

  1. Wash and dry the hair.
  2. Section the hair into manageable sections.
  3. Select the right pair of thinning shears.
  4. Hold the shears at a 45-degree angle.
  5. Cut into the hair, making sure to leave some strands untouched.
  6. Move the shears up and down the length of the hair, cutting some strands while leaving others.
  7. Repeat the process on each section of hair until you achieve the desired effect.
  8. Blend the hair using regular shears if needed.
  9. Style the hair as desired.
  10. Clean and maintain your thinning shears.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears:

  • Use sharp shears to avoid damaging the hair.
  • Start with fewer teeth and work your way up to more teeth if needed.
  • Don’t overdo it – it’s better to start with less hair and cut more if needed.
  • Comb the hair before and after cutting to check for any unevenness.
  • Use a light touch when cutting – don’t press too hard.

FAQ

Can thinning shears be used on any hair type?

Yes, thinning shears can be used on any hair type, but they are particularly useful for thick, heavy hair.

What’s the difference between thinning shears and regular scissors?

Thinning shears have teeth on one or both blades, while regular scissors have straight blades. Thinning shears are designed to cut some of the hair while leaving some strands untouched, which creates a softer, more natural look.

Can thinning shears be used to create layers?

Yes, thinning shears can be used to create layers by cutting some strands shorter than others. However, it’s important to use them carefully to avoid creating choppy or uneven layers.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and looking great:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners too often.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals.

Thinning shears are a valuable tool for hairstylists and anyone looking to add texture and volume to their hair. With the right technique and a little practice, you can achieve a variety of haircuts and styles using thinning shears.
