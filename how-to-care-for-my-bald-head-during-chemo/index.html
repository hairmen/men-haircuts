How To Care For My Bald Head During Chemo

Written by Daniel Oct 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Dealing with cancer and chemotherapy is never easy, and one of the most common side effects of the treatment is hair loss. This can be a challenging time for anyone, but it's important to know that there are ways to care for your bald head during chemo. In this article, we will discuss the steps you can take to keep your scalp healthy and comfortable during this time.

  1. How to Care for Your Bald Head During Chemo
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Caring for Your Bald Head

How to Care for Your Bald Head During Chemo

It's important to keep in mind that your scalp will likely be more sensitive during chemotherapy, so you will need to be gentle when caring for it. Here are some steps you can take to care for your bald head during chemo:

1. Keep Your Scalp Clean

It's important to keep your scalp clean to avoid any infections. Use a gentle, fragrance-free shampoo to wash your scalp every two to three days. Avoid using hot water, which can be harsh on your scalp. Instead, use lukewarm water to wash your head.

2. Protect Your Scalp from the Sun

Your scalp will be more sensitive to the sun during chemotherapy, so it's important to protect it by wearing a hat or scarf when you're outdoors. You can also use sunscreen on your scalp, but make sure it's a gentle, fragrance-free formula.

3. Moisturize Your Scalp

Chemotherapy can dry out your scalp, so it's important to keep it moisturized. Use a fragrance-free lotion or oil to moisturize your scalp. Avoid using products that contain alcohol or other harsh ingredients that can irritate your skin.

4. Avoid Irritants

Your scalp will be more sensitive during chemotherapy, so it's important to avoid any irritants that could cause discomfort or irritation. Avoid using harsh hair products, and avoid wearing hats or scarves that are too tight or rough.

5. Be Gentle When Brushing Your Hair

If you have any hair remaining, be gentle when brushing it. Use a soft-bristled brush or a wide-toothed comb to avoid pulling or tugging on your hair. If your scalp is sensitive, you may want to avoid brushing your hair altogether.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to caring for your bald head during chemo:

  1. Wash your scalp every two to three days with a gentle, fragrance-free shampoo.
  2. Avoid using hot water when washing your scalp.
  3. Protect your scalp from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf.
  4. Moisturize your scalp with a fragrance-free lotion or oil.
  5. Avoid using harsh hair products.
  6. Avoid wearing hats or scarves that are too tight or rough.
  7. Be gentle when brushing any remaining hair.

Tips for Caring for Your Bald Head

Here are some additional tips for caring for your bald head during chemo:

  • Use a satin pillowcase to avoid any friction on your scalp while you sleep.
  • Stay hydrated to keep your scalp and skin healthy.
  • Avoid using hair dryers or other heat styling tools on your scalp.
  • Wear a wig or hairpiece if you prefer.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help from friends and family.

FAQ

Q: Will my hair grow back after chemotherapy?

A: Yes, in most cases, your hair will grow back after chemotherapy. It may take several months or even a year for your hair to fully grow back.

Q: Can I dye my hair during chemotherapy?

A: It's generally not recommended to dye your hair during chemotherapy because your scalp will be more sensitive. If you want to dye your hair, wait until after your treatment is complete.

Q: Is it normal to have an itchy scalp during chemotherapy?

A: Yes, it's common to experience an itchy scalp during chemotherapy. You can use a fragrance-free lotion or oil to help relieve any itching.

Hair Care After Chemotherapy

After your chemotherapy treatment is complete and your hair starts to grow back, it's important to continue caring for your scalp and hair. Here are some tips for hair care after chemotherapy:

  • Use a gentle, fragrance-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair.
  • Be gentle when brushing or styling your hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or a soft-bristled brush.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.

Caring for your bald head during chemotherapy can be a challenge, but with these tips, you can keep your scalp healthy and comfortable. Remember to be gentle when caring for your scalp, and don't be afraid to ask for help from friends and family during this time.
