How To Prevent Frizzy Curly Hair

Written by Aprilia Aug 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair is beautiful, but it can also be a hassle to deal with. One of the biggest issues that people with curly hair face is frizz. Frizzy hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including humidity, heat damage, and dryness. If you're tired of dealing with frizzy hair, don't worry, there are steps you can take to prevent it. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks on how to prevent frizzy curly hair.

  • Step by Step

  • FAQ

How to Prevent Frizzy Curly Hair

The key to preventing frizz in curly hair is to keep it hydrated and healthy. Here are some steps you can take to prevent frizzy hair:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates are harsh detergents that can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to frizz. Switching to a sulfate-free shampoo can help keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

2. Condition your hair regularly

Conditioning your hair regularly will help keep it hydrated and prevent frizz. Look for a conditioner that is specifically designed for curly hair, as it will help define your curls and keep them moisturized.

3. Use a leave-in conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help keep your hair hydrated throughout the day. Apply it to damp hair after washing and conditioning, and don't rinse it out.

4. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it prone to frizz. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat low.

5. Don't towel dry your hair

Towel drying your hair can rough up the cuticles and cause frizz. Instead, gently squeeze out excess water with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to prevent frizzy curly hair:

1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo. Massage it into your scalp and hair, and rinse thoroughly.

2. Condition your hair

Apply conditioner to your hair, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends. Leave it in for a few minutes, then rinse thoroughly.

3. Apply a leave-in conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to damp hair, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends. Don't rinse it out.

4. Don't towel dry your hair

Gently squeeze out excess water with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt. Don't towel dry your hair, as this can cause frizz.

5. Style your hair

Style your hair as desired, using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer if you're using one. Avoid using heat styling tools if possible.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for preventing frizzy curly hair:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's damp.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair and make it prone to frizz.
  • Consider getting a haircut that is specifically designed for curly hair. A good haircut can help prevent frizz and define your curls.

FAQ

Q: Can I use a regular conditioner instead of one specifically designed for curly hair?

A: While you can use a regular conditioner, a conditioner specifically designed for curly hair will help define your curls and keep them moisturized.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type, but most people with curly hair don't need to wash their hair every day. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

Q: Can I still use heat styling tools?

A: While it's best to avoid heat styling tools if possible, you can still use them if you need to. Just make sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat low.

Hair Care

Preventing frizzy curly hair requires a good hair care routine. Here are some additional tips for taking care of your curly hair:

  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as it can dry out your hair and make it prone to frizz. Use lukewarm or cool water instead.
  • Avoid using too many styling products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it prone to frizz. Stick to a leave-in conditioner and a styling product like mousse or gel.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product with SPF.

By following these tips and taking care of your curly hair, you can prevent frizz and enjoy beautiful, healthy curls.
