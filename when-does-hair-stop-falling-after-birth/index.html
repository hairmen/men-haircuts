When Does Hair Stop Falling After Birth A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Sep 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a new mom, you might be experiencing hair loss after giving birth. It's a common occurrence, but it can be alarming at times. The good news is that it's temporary, and your hair will eventually stop falling out. In this post, we'll discuss everything you need to know about postpartum hair loss, including when it starts and when it stops.

When Does Hair Loss Start After Birth?

Postpartum hair loss usually starts around three months after giving birth. This is because during pregnancy, your body produces more estrogen, which helps keep your hair in the growing phase, preventing it from falling out. After giving birth, estrogen levels drop, and the excess hair that didn't fall out during pregnancy starts to shed.

The shedding usually lasts for a few months, and you might notice more hair coming out in the shower or when you brush your hair. It's important to note that not all women experience postpartum hair loss, and some might have more severe shedding than others.

How Long Does Postpartum Hair Loss Last?

Postpartum hair loss typically lasts for six to twelve months after giving birth. However, some women might experience shedding for longer, up to two years after giving birth. It's important to remember that this is a temporary condition, and your hair will eventually stop falling out.

What Causes Postpartum Hair Loss?

Postpartum hair loss is caused by hormonal changes in the body after giving birth. During pregnancy, your body produces more estrogen, which helps keep your hair in the growing phase, preventing it from falling out. After giving birth, estrogen levels drop, and the excess hair that didn't fall out during pregnancy starts to shed.

Other factors that can contribute to postpartum hair loss include stress, lack of sleep, and poor nutrition. It's important to take care of yourself after giving birth to help reduce the severity of shedding.

Can You Prevent Postpartum Hair Loss?

It's not possible to prevent postpartum hair loss completely. However, there are some things you can do to reduce the severity of shedding. These include:

  • Eating a healthy diet with plenty of vitamins and minerals
  • Getting enough sleep and rest
  • Reducing stress through relaxation techniques
  • Avoiding harsh chemical treatments on your hair

What Are Some Treatments for Postpartum Hair Loss?

There are several treatments available for postpartum hair loss, including:

  • Medications that block the hormone responsible for shedding
  • Hair growth supplements that contain vitamins and minerals
  • Topical treatments that stimulate hair growth

It's important to talk to your doctor before starting any treatments to ensure they're safe for you and your baby if you're breastfeeding.

How Can You Take Care of Your Hair During Postpartum Hair Loss?

It's important to take care of your hair during postpartum hair loss to prevent further damage. Some tips include:

  • Avoiding tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair
  • Using a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoiding excessive heat from styling tools
  • Using a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoiding harsh chemical treatments on your hair

Tips for Dealing With Postpartum Hair Loss

Dealing with postpartum hair loss can be stressful, but there are some tips that can help make it easier:

  • Remember that shedding is temporary and your hair will eventually stop falling out
  • Talk to your doctor if you're concerned about the severity of shedding
  • Try a new hairstyle or cut to make your hair look fuller
  • Take care of yourself by eating a healthy diet and getting enough rest
  • Consider talking to a therapist if you're feeling anxious or stressed about hair loss

FAQs About Postpartum Hair Loss

Q: Is postpartum hair loss normal?

A: Yes, postpartum hair loss is a normal occurrence for many women after giving birth.

Q: When does postpartum hair loss start?

A: Postpartum hair loss usually starts around three months after giving birth.

Q: How long does postpartum hair loss last?

A: Postpartum hair loss typically lasts for six to twelve months after giving birth.

Q: Can you prevent postpartum hair loss?

A: It's not possible to prevent postpartum hair loss completely, but you can reduce the severity of shedding by taking care of yourself.

Q: Are there treatments available for postpartum hair loss?

A: Yes, there are several treatments available for postpartum hair loss, including medications, supplements, and topical treatments.

Postpartum hair loss can be stressful, but it's important to remember that it's a temporary condition. Taking care of yourself and your hair can help reduce the severity of shedding and make the experience more manageable. If you have any concerns about the severity of shedding, it's important to talk to your doctor.
