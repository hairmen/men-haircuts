How To Thicken Baby Fine Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Jul 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have baby fine hair, you know how frustrating it can be to style it. It can be difficult to add volume and texture to your hair, and it can often feel limp and lifeless. However, there are a few tricks you can use to thicken your hair and give it more body. In this article, we'll go over some of the most effective tips and tricks for thickening baby fine hair.

How to Thicken Baby Fine Hair

1. Use volumizing products

One of the easiest ways to thicken baby fine hair is to use volumizing products. Look for shampoos, conditioners, and styling products that are designed to add volume and texture to your hair. These products contain ingredients that coat the hair shaft and create the illusion of thicker, fuller hair.

2. Blow dry your hair upside down

Another trick for adding volume to baby fine hair is to blow dry your hair upside down. This technique helps to lift the hair at the roots and create more volume. Use a round brush to lift and separate the hair as you blow dry, focusing on the roots.

3. Avoid heavy products

While volumizing products can be helpful, be careful not to use too many heavy products on your hair. Heavy products can weigh down your hair and make it look even thinner. Stick to lightweight products that won't weigh down your hair.

4. Use a thickening spray

A thickening spray can also be helpful for adding body and texture to baby fine hair. These sprays contain ingredients that help to thicken the hair shaft and create the illusion of thicker hair. Apply the spray to damp hair before blow drying for best results.

5. Try a new haircut

The right haircut can also help to thicken baby fine hair. Consider getting a layered haircut to add texture and movement to your hair. A shorter haircut can also help to create the illusion of thicker hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Thicken Baby Fine Hair

Follow these steps to thicken baby fine hair:

Step 1: Wash your hair with volumizing shampoo and conditioner

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. These products will help to add volume and texture to your hair.

Step 2: Apply a thickening spray

Apply a thickening spray to damp hair. This will help to thicken the hair shaft and create the illusion of thicker hair.

Step 3: Blow dry your hair upside down

Blow dry your hair upside down to add volume at the roots. Use a round brush to lift and separate the hair as you blow dry.

Step 4: Style your hair

Style your hair as desired, using lightweight products to avoid weighing down your hair.

Tips for Thicker Baby Fine Hair

Here are a few additional tips for thickening baby fine hair:

1. Use a clarifying shampoo once a week

A clarifying shampoo can help to remove buildup from your hair and scalp, which can weigh down your hair and make it look thinner.

2. Use a wide-tooth comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will help to avoid breakage and damage, which can make your hair look thinner.

3. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it look thinner. Avoid using hot tools as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you thicken baby fine hair?

Yes, there are several tricks you can use to thicken baby fine hair, such as using volumizing products, blow drying your hair upside down, and getting a layered haircut.

What is the best haircut for baby fine hair?

A layered haircut is often the best option for baby fine hair, as it can add texture and movement to your hair.

Can diet affect hair thickness?

Yes, diet can affect hair thickness. Eating a healthy diet that includes protein, vitamins, and minerals can help to promote healthy hair growth.

Hair Care for Thicker Baby Fine Hair

To keep your hair looking thick and healthy, it's important to take good care of it. Here are a few tips for hair care:

1. Avoid over-washing your hair

Washing your hair too frequently can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it look thinner. Try to wash your hair every other day, or even less frequently if possible.

2. Use a leave-in conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to nourish and protect your hair, keeping it looking healthy and thick.

3. Protect your hair from the sun

Exposure to the sun can damage your hair and make it look thinner. Wear a hat or use a hair protectant spray when spending time in the sun.

By following these tips and tricks, you can thicken your baby fine hair and enjoy fuller, more voluminous locks.
