What To Do If Your Wedding Dress Is Too Short

Written by Frank Jul 13, 2023 · 3 min read

Table of Content:

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

One of the worst things that could happen to a bride on her wedding day is realizing that her dress is too short. It can be a nightmare come true, but don't worry, there are solutions to fix this problem. In this article, we will give you some tips on what to do if your wedding dress is too short.

How to

The first thing to do when you realize that your wedding dress is too short is to remain calm. Panicking will only make the situation worse, and you might not be able to come up with a solution. Take a deep breath, and let's get started.

Step by step

Step 1: Check the length of your dress. Is it too short?

Step 2: Find a tailor. You can ask your wedding planner or go online to find a tailor near you.

Step 3: Schedule an appointment with the tailor. Make sure you bring your dress with you.

Step 4: Discuss your options with the tailor. They will suggest the best solution for your dress.

Step 5: Try on your dress after the alterations have been made. Make sure it fits perfectly.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when dealing with a too-short wedding dress:

  • Try to avoid buying a dress that is too short in the first place. Make sure you try on the dress before purchasing it.
  • If you can't find a tailor, try using double-sided tape or fashion tape to hold the dress in place.
  • If your dress is too short because of the heels you're wearing, consider changing to lower heels or flats.
  • If you're wearing a mermaid or trumpet style dress, make sure you can walk in it before buying it.

FAQ

Q: Can I alter my wedding dress myself?

A: We do not recommend altering your wedding dress yourself. It's a delicate and intricate process that should be done by a professional tailor.

Q: How much does it cost to alter a wedding dress?

A: The cost of altering a wedding dress varies depending on the alterations needed and the location of the tailor. It can range from $50 to $500.

Hair Care

Don't forget to take care of your hair on your wedding day. Here are some tips:

  • Get a trim a few weeks before the wedding to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage.
  • Consider a simple, elegant hairstyle that will complement your dress.

Don't let a too-short wedding dress ruin your special day. With these tips, you can fix the problem and look stunning on your wedding day.
