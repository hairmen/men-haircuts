How To Fix A Dress That s Too Short

Written by Eveline Aug 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

In this article, we will discuss how to fix a dress that's too short. Whether you accidentally bought a dress that's too short or your dress has shrunk in the wash, we've got you covered. We'll go through step-by-step instructions on how to fix your dress so that it fits perfectly. Let's get started!

Description

Having a dress that's too short can be frustrating, especially if you really love the dress. However, there are several ways to fix this issue. You don't have to give up on your dress just yet. By following these simple steps, you can wear your dress with confidence knowing that it fits perfectly.

How to

There are several ways to fix a dress that's too short. Here are some methods:

Method 1: Add Fabric

If your dress is just a few inches too short, you can add fabric to the hemline to make it longer.

Step 1: Measure the length that you need to add to the dress.

Step 2: Cut a piece of fabric that's the same width as the dress and the length that you need to add.

Step 3: Pin the fabric to the hemline of the dress.

Step 4: Sew the fabric to the dress using a sewing machine or by hand.

Step 5: Cut off any excess fabric.

Step 6: Iron the hemline to make it look neat.

Method 2: Add Lace

If you want to add some style to your dress, you can add lace to the hemline to make it longer.

Step 1: Measure the length that you need to add to the dress.

Step 2: Cut a piece of lace that's the same width as the dress and the length that you need to add.

Step 3: Pin the lace to the hemline of the dress.

Step 4: Sew the lace to the dress using a sewing machine or by hand.

Step 5: Cut off any excess lace.

Step 6: Iron the hemline to make it look neat.

Method 3: Wear Leggings or Tights

If you don't want to alter your dress, you can wear leggings or tights underneath to make it look longer.

Step 1: Choose a pair of leggings or tights that match your dress.

Step 2: Put on the leggings or tights.

Step 3: Put on your dress.

Step 4: Adjust the leggings or tights so that they're not visible.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to fix a dress that's too short:

  1. Identify the problem with your dress.
  2. Determine the method that you want to use to fix your dress.
  3. Gather the necessary materials.
  4. Measure the length that you need to add to the dress.
  5. Cut the fabric or lace to the appropriate length.
  6. Pin the fabric or lace to the hemline of the dress.
  7. Sew the fabric or lace to the dress using a sewing machine or by hand.
  8. Cut off any excess fabric or lace.
  9. Iron the hemline to make it look neat.
  10. Try on the dress to make sure that it fits correctly.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when fixing a dress that's too short:

  • Choose a method that fits your skill level.
  • Use a sewing machine if you're not confident in your hand-sewing skills.
  • Iron the hemline to make it look neat and professional.
  • Try on the dress before and after making alterations to ensure that it fits correctly.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're not sure what to do.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing a dress that's too short:

Can I use fabric glue instead of sewing?

Fabric glue can be used as an alternative to sewing, but it may not be as durable. It's better to use a sewing machine or hand-sew the fabric to ensure that it stays in place.

What if I don't have a sewing machine?

You can sew the fabric to the dress by hand. It may take longer, but it's still an effective method.

What if my dress is too short in the middle?

If your dress is too short in the middle, you can add a panel of fabric to the front and back of the dress to make it longer. Follow the same steps as adding fabric to the hemline.

Hair Care

While fixing your dress, don't forget to take care of your hair! Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers and straighteners, as this can damage your hair over time. Try using a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and reduce frizz.

Now that you know how to fix a dress that's too short, you can confidently wear your favorite dress without worrying about it being too short. Have fun experimenting with different fabrics and styles to create your perfect dress!
