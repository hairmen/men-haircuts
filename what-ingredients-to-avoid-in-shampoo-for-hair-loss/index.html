What Ingredients To Avoid In Shampoo For Hair Loss

Written by Robby Nov 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're experiencing hair loss, one of the first things you may consider is changing your shampoo. However, not all shampoos are created equal and some may even contain ingredients that can worsen your hair loss. In this article, we'll explore what ingredients to avoid in shampoo for hair loss, so you can make an informed decision when selecting a shampoo.

Sulfates

Sulfates are a common ingredient found in many shampoos. They are responsible for creating the lather that we associate with clean hair. However, sulfates can be too harsh for some scalps and can strip the hair of its natural oils. This can lead to dryness and breakage, which can worsen hair loss. Look for shampoos that are sulfate-free, or that contain gentler sulfates such as sodium lauryl sulfoacetate (SLSA) or sodium cocoyl isethionate (SCI).

Parabens

Parabens are a type of preservative that are commonly used in shampoos to prevent the growth of bacteria and fungi. However, some studies have linked parabens to hormone disruption and cancer. While the evidence is not conclusive, it's best to avoid shampoos that contain parabens if you're experiencing hair loss.

Alcohol

Alcohol is often used in shampoos to help evaporate excess water and to help other ingredients penetrate the hair shaft. However, alcohol can be drying to the scalp and hair, which can lead to breakage and thinning. Look for shampoos that are alcohol-free, or that contain fatty alcohols such as cetyl alcohol, which can actually help moisturize the hair.

Silicones

Silicones are a type of synthetic polymer that are often used in shampoos to help smooth and detangle the hair. While they can give the hair a shiny appearance, they can also build up on the scalp and hair, which can clog hair follicles and worsen hair loss. Look for shampoos that are silicone-free, or that contain water-soluble silicones such as dimethicone copolyol.

Fragrance

Fragrance is often added to shampoos to give them a pleasant scent. However, many fragrances are made up of dozens or even hundreds of different chemicals, some of which can be irritating to the scalp and hair. Look for shampoos that are fragrance-free, or that use natural fragrances such as essential oils.

Synthetic Colors

Synthetic colors are often added to shampoos to give them an appealing color. However, some synthetic colors can be derived from petroleum and can be harmful to the scalp and hair. Look for shampoos that are color-free, or that use natural colors such as henna.

Phthalates

Phthalates are a type of plasticizer that are often used in shampoos to help make them more flexible and pliable. However, some studies have linked phthalates to hormone disruption and developmental problems. Look for shampoos that are phthalate-free, or that use natural plasticizers such as glycerin.

Formaldehyde

Formaldehyde is a type of preservative that is often used in shampoos to prevent the growth of bacteria and fungi. However, formaldehyde can be irritating to the scalp and can cause allergic reactions. Look for shampoos that are formaldehyde-free, or that use natural preservatives such as vitamin E.

Triclosan

Triclosan is a type of antimicrobial agent that is often used in shampoos to kill bacteria and fungi. However, triclosan has been linked to hormone disruption and can contribute to the development of antibiotic-resistant bacteria. Look for shampoos that are triclosan-free, or that use natural antimicrobial agents such as tea tree oil.

FAQ

What should I look for in a shampoo for hair loss?

You should look for shampoos that are gentle, nourishing, and free from harsh chemicals such as sulfates, parabens, and alcohol.

Can shampoos cause hair loss?

Yes, shampoos that contain harsh chemicals or that are too drying can contribute to hair loss.

How often should I shampoo my hair if I'm experiencing hair loss?

You should shampoo your hair as often as necessary to keep it clean and healthy, but avoid over-shampooing, which can strip the hair of its natural oils.

Are natural shampoos better for hair loss?

Natural shampoos can be a good choice for hair loss, as they are often free from harsh chemicals and can provide nourishing benefits to the hair and scalp.

Hair Care Tips

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair, as this can contribute to hair loss.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet that includes plenty of vitamins and minerals that are essential for healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons, which can damage the hair and contribute to breakage.
  • Get regular trims to help prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid brushing wet hair, as this can cause breakage and damage to the hair.

By avoiding these harmful ingredients and following these tips, you can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent further hair loss.
