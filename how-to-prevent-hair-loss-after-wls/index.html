How To Prevent Hair Loss After Wls Tips And Tricks

If you're considering weight loss surgery (WLS), you may be wondering about the potential side effects. One of the most common concerns is hair loss. While it's true that some people experience hair loss after WLS, there are steps you can take to prevent it. In this article, we'll explain how to prevent hair loss after WLS, and give you some tips and tricks for maintaining healthy hair.

How to Prevent Hair Loss After WLS

Hair loss after WLS is a common side effect because the body goes through significant changes during the weight loss process. The surgery itself, as well as the rapid weight loss that follows, can cause stress on the body and lead to hair loss. However, there are steps you can take to minimize the effects of hair loss.

Steps to Take

1. Get enough protein: Hair is made up of protein, so it's important to get enough of it in your diet. After WLS, you may need to increase your protein intake to help your body recover.

2. Take your vitamins: After WLS, you'll need to take a variety of vitamins to ensure your body is getting the nutrients it needs. Biotin, in particular, is important for hair health.

3. Stay hydrated: Drinking enough water is important for overall health, including hair health. Dehydration can lead to dry, brittle hair that's more prone to breakage.

4. Avoid harsh chemicals: Hair treatments like coloring, perming, and straightening can damage hair and lead to breakage. It's best to avoid these treatments, especially in the months following WLS.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

1. Be gentle: After WLS, your hair may be more fragile than usual. Be gentle when brushing and combing, and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.

2. Use a gentle shampoo: Look for a shampoo that's specifically designed for thinning hair. These shampoos are formulated to be gentle and nourishing, and can help protect your hair from damage.

3. Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage hair and lead to breakage. It's best to avoid these tools, or use them sparingly.

FAQ

Q: How long does hair loss after WLS last?

A: Hair loss can last for several months after WLS, but it usually starts to improve after about six months.

Q: Will my hair grow back after WLS?

A: Yes, in most cases. Hair loss after WLS is usually temporary, and hair will grow back once your body has adjusted to the changes.

Hair Care

In addition to following the steps and tips outlined above, there are a few other things you can do to care for your hair after WLS.

1. Massage your scalp: Massaging your scalp can help increase blood flow to the hair follicles, which can promote hair growth.

2. Eat a healthy diet: A balanced diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals can help keep your hair healthy.

3. Get enough sleep: Getting enough sleep is important for overall health, including hair health. Lack of sleep can lead to stress on the body, which can contribute to hair loss.

By following these steps and tips, you can minimize the effects of hair loss after WLS and maintain healthy, beautiful hair.
