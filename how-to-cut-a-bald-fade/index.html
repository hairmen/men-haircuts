How To Cut A Bald Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Aug 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and low-maintenance haircut, then a bald fade might be just what you need. A bald fade is a type of haircut that involves cutting the hair extremely short on the sides and back of the head, while leaving the hair longer on top. The result is a gradual fade from hair to skin, which gives the haircut a clean and modern look.

How to Cut a Bald Fade

Cutting a bald fade can be tricky, especially if you're not used to cutting hair. However, with the right tools and some patience, you can achieve a great-looking bald fade at home. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Scissors
  • Clipper guards
  • Haircutting cape
  • Mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut a bald fade:

  1. Start by cutting the hair on the sides and back of the head using the clippers and a #2 guard. Make sure to go against the grain to get a closer cut.
  2. Next, switch to a #1 guard and cut the hair about an inch above the hairline. This will create a fade effect.
  3. Use the clippers without a guard to create a bald line around the head, just above the ears.
  4. Blend the bald line into the fade using the clippers with a #1 guard.
  5. Switch to a #3 guard and blend the fade into the longer hair on top of the head.
  6. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs on top of the head. Be sure to blend the top of the head into the fade for a natural look.
  7. Double-check the cut for any uneven spots, and make any necessary adjustments.
  8. Brush off any loose hair and remove the haircutting cape.
  9. Show your client the finished product in the mirror and make any final adjustments if necessary.

Tips for Cutting a Bald Fade

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect bald fade:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one to create a gradual fade.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting hair is a skill that requires practice.
  • Use a mirror to check your work from all angles.
  • Be conservative when cutting hair. You can always cut more later, but you can't put it back once it's gone.
  • Invest in high-quality tools, such as clippers and scissors. They will make the job easier and produce better results.

FAQs

Can I cut a bald fade on myself?

Cutting a bald fade on yourself can be difficult, especially if you're not experienced with cutting hair. It's best to have someone else do it for you, or to seek the help of a professional barber or hairstylist.

What type of hair is best for a bald fade?

A bald fade can work on any type of hair, but it looks especially good on thick, curly, or wavy hair. If you have thin or fine hair, a bald fade may not be the best option for you.

How often should I get a bald fade?

How often you get a bald fade depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you want to maintain the style. For most people, getting a bald fade every two to four weeks is sufficient.

Hair Care Tips for Bald Fades

Once you've got your bald fade, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking fresh. Here are some hair care tips for bald fades:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair and scalp healthy.
  • Don't wash your hair too often, as this can dry out your scalp and make your hair brittle.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and shiny.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair, as this can damage your hair and scalp.
  • Visit your barber or hairstylist regularly to maintain your bald fade.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to cut a bald fade like a pro. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Good luck!
