If You Cut Curly Hair Will It Grow Back Straight

Written by Christine Oct 11, 2023 ¬∑ 4 min read

Curly hair is beautiful and unique, but many people wonder what will happen if they cut it. One common concern is whether curly hair will grow back straight. In this article, we will explore this question and provide tips for caring for curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To Cut Curly Hair
  • Step By Step Guide
  • Tips for Caring for Curly Hair
  • FAQ

Description

If you cut curly hair, it will not grow back straight. The shape and texture of your hair are determined by the shape of your hair follicle. Curly hair has an oval or elliptical shape, while straight hair has a round shape. When you cut your hair, you only affect the length, not the shape of your hair follicle.

However, cutting your curly hair can change the appearance of your curls. If you cut your hair too short, your curls may appear tighter or frizzier. If you cut your hair in layers, your curls may have more bounce and movement.

How To Cut Curly Hair

Cutting curly hair requires special techniques to avoid damaging the curls. Here are some tips:

1. Cut Your Hair Dry

Cutting curly hair when it is wet can result in uneven cuts. When curly hair dries, it shrinks, and you may end up cutting more than you intended. Cutting your hair dry allows you to see the true length of your curls and ensure a more even cut.

2. Use Sharp Scissors

Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends. Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.

3. Cut in Sections

Divide your hair into sections to ensure an even cut. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up.

Step By Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for cutting your curly hair:

1. Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start with clean, conditioned hair. Remove any tangles with a wide-tooth comb.

2. Divide Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Start with the bottom layer.

3. Cut Your Hair

Using sharp scissors, cut your hair in small sections. Start with the tips and work your way up. Cut less than you think you need to, as curly hair shrinks as it dries.

4. Check for Evenness

After cutting each section, check for evenness by comparing it to the other sections. Make any necessary adjustments.

5. Style Your Hair

After cutting your hair, style it as desired. Avoid using heat tools, as they can damage your curls.

Tips for Caring for Curly Hair

Here are some tips for caring for your curly hair:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Curly hair is prone to dryness, so avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of natural oils. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are designed for curly hair.

2. Avoid Brushing Your Hair

Brushing curly hair can cause frizz and damage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help moisturize your hair and define your curls. Apply it to damp hair and scrunch your hair to encourage your curls.

4. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent frizz and breakage.

FAQ

Will Cutting My Hair Make It Grow Faster?

No, cutting your hair does not make it grow faster. Hair growth is determined by genetics and other factors.

Can I Straighten My Curly Hair?

Yes, you can straighten your curly hair using heat tools, but be aware that this can damage your hair over time. Use a heat protectant and avoid using heat tools too often.

Hair Care

Caring for curly hair can be challenging, but with the right techniques and products, you can keep your curls looking healthy and beautiful. Remember to cut your hair dry, use sharp scissors, and avoid using heat tools too often. With proper care, your curly hair will continue to grow and thrive.
