How To Take Care Of Your Hair While Wearing A Wig

Wearing a wig can be a great way to switch up your look, but it's important to take care of both your wig and your natural hair while wearing it. In this article, we'll go over how to take care of your hair while wearing a wig, including steps, tips, and FAQs.

How to Take Care of Your Hair While Wearing a Wig

When wearing a wig, it's important to take care of your natural hair in order to prevent damage and maintain healthy locks. Here are some steps to take:

1. Keep Your Hair Clean

Before putting on your wig, ensure that your hair is clean and free from oils and debris. This will help prevent any buildup from transferring to your wig and potentially causing damage.


2. Use a Wig Cap

Wearing a wig cap can help protect your hair from rubbing against the wig and causing friction. It can also help keep your natural hair in place and prevent any stray hairs from poking out.


3. Avoid Tight Braids or Ponytails

Tight braids or ponytails can cause tension on your scalp and potentially lead to hair breakage or loss. Instead, opt for looser hairstyles or simply let your hair hang loose.


4. Moisturize Your Hair

Wearing a wig can cause your hair to become dry or brittle, so it's important to moisturize regularly. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your locks hydrated.


5. Take Breaks

It's important to give your hair a break from wearing a wig. Try to take it off for a few hours each day, or alternate between wearing a wig and letting your natural hair breathe.


Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to taking care of your hair while wearing a wig:

Step 1: Cleanse Your Hair

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to remove any buildup or oils. Allow your hair to air dry or use a blow dryer on a low heat setting.

Step 2: Apply a Wig Cap

Put on a wig cap to protect your hair from rubbing against the wig. Make sure the cap is snug but not too tight.

Step 3: Put on Your Wig

Carefully place your wig over the wig cap, ensuring that it fits comfortably and securely. Adjust the wig as needed.

Step 4: Moisturize Your Hair

Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your natural hair to keep it hydrated and healthy.

Step 5: Take Breaks

Try to take off your wig for a few hours each day, or alternate between wearing a wig and letting your natural hair breathe.

Tips for Wig and Hair Care

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your wig and natural hair:

1. Use a Wig Stand

When not wearing your wig, store it on a wig stand to help maintain its shape and prevent tangling.

2. Don't Sleep in Your Wig

Avoid sleeping in your wig, as this can cause tangles and damage.

3. Brush Your Wig Regularly

Use a wig brush to gently detangle your wig and prevent any knots or matting.

4. Protect Your Wig from Heat

Avoid exposing your wig to high heat, such as from a blow dryer or flat iron. This can cause damage and shorten the lifespan of your wig.

FAQs

1. How often should I wash my wig?

It's recommended to wash your wig every 6-8 wears, or as needed if it becomes dirty or oily.

2. Can I wear a wig if I have a sensitive scalp?

Yes, there are wigs made specifically for sensitive scalps that are designed to be lightweight and gentle on the skin.

3. How do I choose the right wig for me?

Consider factors such as wig material, style, and color when choosing a wig. It's also helpful to try on different wigs and consult with a stylist or wig specialist.

By following these tips and steps, you can take care of both your wig and natural hair while enjoying a new look!
