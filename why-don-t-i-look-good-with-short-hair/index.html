Why Don t I Look Good With Short Hair

Written by Aprilia Oct 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Short hair is an easy and low-maintenance style that many women opt for. However, some women may feel like they don't look good with short hair. If you're one of those women, don't worry, you're not alone. In this article, we'll discuss the reasons why some women don't look good with short hair and what you can do about it.

Reasons Why Short Hair May Not Look Good on You

There are several reasons why you may not look good with short hair:

Face Shape

Short haircuts can highlight certain facial features, including the shape of your face. If you have a round face, a short haircut may make your face look even rounder. On the other hand, if you have a long face, a short haircut may make your face look longer.

Hair Texture

Short haircuts can also emphasize your hair texture. If you have thick, curly hair, a short haircut may make your hair look even bigger. If you have fine, straight hair, a short haircut may make your hair look flat and lifeless.

Personal Style

Short haircuts can also clash with your personal style. If you prefer a more feminine or glamorous look, a short haircut may not be the best choice for you.

How to Determine if Short Hair Will Look Good on You

Before you chop off your locks, there are a few things you can do to determine if short hair will look good on you:

Use a Virtual Hairstyle Tool

There are several virtual hairstyle tools available online that allow you to upload a photo of yourself and try on different hairstyles. This can give you an idea of how you will look with short hair.

Ask for Opinions

Ask your friends and family for their opinion on whether short hair will look good on you. They may have a different perspective and can provide valuable feedback.

Step-by-Step Guide to Finding the Right Short Haircut

If you've decided that short hair is the way to go, here's a step-by-step guide to finding the right haircut:

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

Knowing your face shape is important when choosing a short haircut. There are four basic face shapes: round, square, oval, and heart-shaped.

Step 2: Consider Your Hair Texture

Your hair texture will also play a role in determining the right short haircut for you. If you have thick, curly hair, a layered haircut may work best. If you have fine, straight hair, a bob may be a good option.

Step 3: Research Short Haircuts

Look online and in magazines for short haircuts that you like. Bring these pictures to your hairstylist to give them an idea of what you're looking for.

Tips for Making Short Hair Look Good

If you've already gotten a short haircut and are struggling to make it look good, here are a few tips:

Use the Right Products

Using the right products for your hair type can make a big difference. For example, if you have fine hair, use a volumizing mousse to add body. If you have curly hair, use a curl-enhancing product to define your curls.

Find the Right Styling Tools

Invest in high-quality styling tools, such as a blow dryer and flat iron, to help you achieve the look you want.

FAQs

1. Can I pull off a pixie cut?

Pixie cuts can be flattering on many face shapes, but they tend to work best on those with oval or heart-shaped faces.

2. How often do I need to get my short hair cut?

Short haircuts require more frequent trims to maintain their shape. You should plan on getting your hair cut every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Short hair requires different care than long hair. Here are a few hair care tips:

Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

Choose a shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for your hair type. If you have color-treated hair, use products specifically designed for colored hair.

Protect Your Hair from Heat

Use a heat protectant before using hot styling tools to prevent damage to your hair.

Get Regular Trims

As mentioned earlier, short hair requires more frequent trims to maintain its shape. Don't skip your appointments with your hairstylist.

In conclusion, if you feel like you don't look good with short hair, don't fret. There are many factors that can contribute to this, including face shape, hair texture, and personal style. Use the tips and tricks outlined in this article to determine if short hair is right for you and how to make it look its best.
