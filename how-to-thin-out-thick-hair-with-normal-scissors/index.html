How To Thin Out Thick Hair With Normal Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Nov 25, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it may look great when styled properly, it can also be a hassle to manage on a day-to-day basis. If you're looking to thin out your thick hair, you might be considering a trip to the salon. However, you can achieve the same results at home with just a pair of normal scissors. In this article, we'll show you how to thin out thick hair with normal scissors.

How to Thin Out Thick Hair with Normal Scissors

Thinning out thick hair with normal scissors is a simple process that can be done at home. Here are the steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you're cutting through clean hair.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Using a wide-tooth comb, comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to see which areas need to be thinned out.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using hair clips, section your hair into four equal parts (two on top and two on the bottom). This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you're cutting evenly.

Step 4: Start Thinning Out Your Hair

Using your normal scissors, start cutting into your hair in a diagonal motion. Make sure to start from the ends of your hair and work your way up. Take small sections at a time and cut into the hair at an angle. This will help to remove some of the bulk from your hair.

Step 5: Blend Your Hair

After you've thinned out your hair, blend it together by combing through it with your wide-tooth comb. This will help to ensure that your hair looks natural and not choppy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to thin out thick hair with normal scissors:

Step 1: Gather Your Materials

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary materials. You'll need:

  • Normal scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Hair clips

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you're cutting through clean hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are suitable for your hair type.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Using a wide-tooth comb, comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to see which areas need to be thinned out.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Using hair clips, section your hair into four equal parts (two on top and two on the bottom). This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you're cutting evenly.

Step 5: Determine Which Areas Need Thinning Out

Using your comb, determine which areas of your hair need to be thinned out. Look for areas that are particularly thick or bulky.

Step 6: Start Thinning Out Your Hair

Using your normal scissors, start cutting into your hair in a diagonal motion. Make sure to start from the ends of your hair and work your way up. Take small sections at a time and cut into the hair at an angle. This will help to remove some of the bulk from your hair.

Step 7: Blend Your Hair

After you've thinned out your hair, blend it together by combing through it with your wide-tooth comb. This will help to ensure that your hair looks natural and not choppy.

Tips for Thinning Out Thick Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out thick hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Take your time and work in small sections to avoid cutting off too much hair.
  • Start from the ends of your hair and work your way up to avoid making your hair look choppy.
  • Blend your hair together after thinning it out to make it look natural.

FAQs

Can I use any type of scissors to thin out my hair?

No, it's important to use sharp scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair. Using dull scissors or regular household scissors can damage your hair.

How often should I thin out my hair?

This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to thin out thick hair every 6-8 weeks.

Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner overall?

No, thinning out your hair will only remove some of the bulk. It won't make your hair look thinner overall.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of thick hair:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for thick hair.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.

Thinning out thick hair with normal scissors is a simple process that can be done at home. Follow these steps and tips to achieve the perfect look for your hair. Remember to take your time and work in small sections to avoid cutting off too much hair. With a little practice, you'll be able to achieve salon-worthy results from the comfort of your own home.
