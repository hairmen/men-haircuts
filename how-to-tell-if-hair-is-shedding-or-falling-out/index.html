How To Tell If Hair Is Shedding Or Falling Out

Written by Aprilia Aug 29, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you noticing more hair than usual coming out when you brush or wash your hair? It can be concerning to see excess hair falling out, but it's important to understand whether it's shedding or hair loss. Shedding is a natural process where old hair falls out to make room for new hair growth, while hair loss is a more serious condition that can lead to thinning or baldness. In this article, we'll discuss how to tell if hair is shedding or falling out and what you can do about it.

How to Tell if Hair is Shedding

Shedding is a normal process that occurs in all hair types, including curly, straight, thick, and thin hair. The average person sheds 50 to 100 hairs per day, but this can vary depending on factors such as age, genetics, and hormonal changes. Here are some signs that your hair is shedding:

1. Hair is falling out in small amounts

If you notice a few strands of hair on your brush or in the shower, it's likely shedding. Shedding usually occurs evenly throughout the scalp and doesn't result in noticeable patches of hair loss.

2. Hair is falling out from the root

When hair sheds, it falls out from the root, leaving a small white bulb at the end of the hair shaft. This is a sign that the hair has completed its natural growth cycle and is ready to be replaced by new hair.

3. Hair is growing back

Shedding is a normal part of the hair growth cycle, and new hair will grow back to replace the old hair. If you notice new hair growth on your scalp, it's a sign that your hair is shedding and regenerating as it should.

How to Tell if Hair is Falling Out

Hair loss, also known as alopecia, is a more serious condition that can lead to permanent hair loss or thinning. Here are some signs that your hair is falling out:

1. Hair is falling out in clumps

If you notice large amounts of hair coming out at once, it's likely hair loss. This can result in noticeable patches of baldness or thinning hair.

2. Hair is falling out unevenly

Hair loss can occur in specific areas of the scalp or in a pattern. This can result in uneven hair loss, such as a receding hairline or patchy bald spots.

3. Hair is not growing back

Unlike shedding, hair loss can result in permanent hair loss or thinning. If you notice that your hair is not growing back in areas where it has fallen out, it's a sign of hair loss.

What Causes Hair Shedding and Hair Loss?

There are many factors that can contribute to hair shedding and hair loss, including:

1. Hormonal changes

Hormonal changes, such as those that occur during pregnancy or menopause, can cause hair shedding or hair loss.

2. Genetics

Genetics can play a role in hair shedding and hair loss, as some people are more prone to these conditions than others.

3. Medical conditions

Medical conditions such as thyroid disorders, autoimmune diseases, and scalp infections can contribute to hair shedding and hair loss.

4. Stress

Stress can cause hair shedding or hair loss, as it can disrupt the hair growth cycle and lead to a condition known as telogen effluvium.

How to Care for Your Hair

Whether you're experiencing shedding or hair loss, it's important to take good care of your hair to promote healthy growth. Here are some tips:

1. Avoid harsh chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or relaxers, as these can damage the hair and lead to shedding or hair loss.

2. Eat a healthy diet

Eating a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can promote healthy hair growth.

3. Reduce stress

Reducing stress through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques can help prevent hair shedding or hair loss.

4. Use gentle hair care products

Use gentle hair care products that are free from sulfates and other harsh chemicals that can damage the hair.

FAQs

Q: Is it normal to lose hair every day?

A: Yes, shedding is a normal process where old hair falls out to make room for new hair growth. The average person sheds 50 to 100 hairs per day.

Q: Can hair loss be reversed?

A: It depends on the cause of the hair loss. In some cases, hair loss can be reversed with treatment. In other cases, it may be permanent.

Q: Can hair loss be prevented?

A: While some types of hair loss cannot be prevented, taking good care of your hair and reducing stress can help prevent shedding and hair loss.

Conclusion

Shedding is a normal process where old hair falls out to make room for new hair growth, while hair loss is a more serious condition that can lead to permanent hair loss or thinning. By understanding the signs and causes of shedding and hair loss, you can take steps to care for your hair and promote healthy growth. If you're experiencing excessive shedding or hair loss, consult with a healthcare professional to determine the underlying cause and appropriate treatment.
