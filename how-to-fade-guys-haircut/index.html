How To Fade Guys Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Oct 28, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to change up your hairstyle, getting a fade is a great option. Fades are a popular haircut style for men because they are versatile and can be tailored to suit different preferences. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps on how to fade guys haircut. Whether you're looking for a low, mid, or high fade, we've got you covered.

What is a Fade?

A fade is a haircut that gradually fades from short to long. It's a popular hairstyle among men because it's easy to maintain and can be customized to suit different hair types and face shapes. Fades can be low, mid, or high, depending on how high up the head the fade starts.

How to Fade Guys Haircut: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Decide on the Type of Fade

The first step in getting a fade is deciding on the type of fade you want. There are three main types of fades: low, mid, and high. A low fade starts just above the ear, a mid fade starts halfway up the head, and a high fade starts near the top of the head.

Step 2: Choose the Length of the Fade

Once you've decided on the type of fade, you'll need to choose the length of the fade. This will depend on your personal preference and the length of your hair. If you have longer hair, you may want a longer fade. If you have shorter hair, you may want a shorter fade.

Step 3: Prepare the Hair

Before you start cutting, you'll need to prepare the hair. This means washing and drying it thoroughly. You'll also want to comb it out so that it's tangle-free and easy to work with.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start cutting the hair with the longest guard on your clippers. This will create the base for your fade. Work your way up the head, gradually switching to shorter guards as you go. Make sure to blend the hair well by using a comb to create a seamless transition between the different lengths.

Step 5: Create the Fade

Once you've cut the majority of the hair, it's time to create the fade. Switch to a shorter guard and start cutting the hair around the edges of the fade. Use the clippers to create a smooth transition between the different lengths of hair. Take your time and make sure to blend the hair well.

Step 6: Clean Up the Edges

Once you've created the fade, it's time to clean up the edges. Use a trimmer to create sharp lines around the hairline and sideburns. You can also use scissors to trim any longer hairs that may have been missed.

Step 7: Style the Hair

Once you've finished cutting, it's time to style the hair. You can use a variety of products to achieve different looks, such as pomade, gel, or wax. Experiment with different products to find the one that works best for you.

Tips for Fading Guys Haircut

Here are some tips to keep in mind when fading guys haircut:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down gradually
  • Blend the hair well to create a seamless transition
  • Take your time and don't rush the process
  • Use a comb to help guide the clippers
  • Trim the hair around the ears and neckline to create a clean look

FAQs

Q: How often should I get a fade?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain your haircut. On average, most men get a fade every 2-4 weeks.

Q: Can I fade my own hair?

A: While it's possible to fade your own hair, it's not recommended unless you have experience cutting hair. It's best to go to a professional barber or stylist to ensure a clean and even haircut.

Hair Care for Faded Haircut

After getting a fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the look. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy
  • Visit your barber regularly for touch-ups

Now that you know how to fade guys haircut, you can experiment with different styles and find the one that suits you best. Remember to take your time and be patient, as fading takes practice to master. Good luck!
