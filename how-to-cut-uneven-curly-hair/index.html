How To Cut Uneven Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Curly hair can be beautiful, but it can also be challenging to cut. If you have uneven curly hair, you may be wondering how to get a haircut that looks great and is easy to manage. In this article, we’ll walk you through the process of cutting curly hair, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Uneven Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Cut Uneven Curly Hair

The first thing to remember when cutting curly hair is to cut it dry. When hair is wet, it stretches and can look longer than it actually is. This can lead to uneven cuts.

It’s also important to use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage the hair and create split ends.

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is detangled and free of any knots or tangles. You can use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently separate the curls.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow-drying it with a diffuser. You want to start with clean, dry hair so you can see the natural shape of your curls.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using clips, section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don’t miss any areas.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer, take a small section of hair and twist it. This will create a more defined curl and make it easier to see where to cut. Using your sharp scissors, cut the ends of the twisted section. Repeat this process for all of the sections in the bottom layer.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Once you’ve finished the bottom layer, move on to the middle layer. Take a small section of hair and twist it, then cut the ends with your scissors. Repeat this process for all of the sections in the middle layer.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, move on to the top layer. Take a small section of hair and twist it, then cut the ends with your scissors. Repeat this process for all of the sections in the top layer.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting curly hair:

  • Don’t cut too much at once. It’s better to take small sections and work slowly than to cut too much and regret it later.
  • Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.
  • If you’re not comfortable cutting your own hair, consider going to a professional stylist who specializes in curly hair.

FAQs

Can I cut my curly hair while it’s wet?

No, it’s best to cut curly hair when it’s dry. When hair is wet, it stretches and can look longer than it actually is. This can lead to uneven cuts.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It’s recommended to trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use your fingers to gently separate the curls.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

By following these tips and using the step-by-step guide above, you can achieve a great haircut for your uneven curly hair. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don’t hesitate to seek the help of a professional stylist if you need it.
