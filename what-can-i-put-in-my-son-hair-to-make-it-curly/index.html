What Can I Put In My Son s Hair To Make It Curly

Written by Petter Oct 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have a son with straight or wavy hair, you may be wondering how you can get those luscious curls that you see on other kids. While genetics may play a role in determining hair texture, there are a few things you can do to enhance your son's natural curls. In this article, we'll explore some of the best products and techniques to make your son's hair curly.

Table of Contents

  • How to Make Your Son's Hair Curly
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips for Curly Hair Care
  • FAQ

How to Make Your Son's Hair Curly

Before we dive into the products and techniques, it's important to note that not all hair types are the same. Some hair types are naturally more prone to curling, while others may require a bit more effort. Here are some of the best ways to make your son's hair curly:

1. Use a Curl-Enhancing Shampoo and Conditioner

One of the easiest ways to enhance your son's natural curls is to use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair. These products are formulated to moisturize and define curls, leaving them bouncy and shiny. Look for products that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and glycerin, which can help to lock in moisture and prevent frizz.

2. Apply Curl-Defining Cream or Gel

To further enhance your son's curls, consider using a curl-defining cream or gel. These products can be applied to damp hair and help to define and hold curls in place. Look for products that are lightweight and non-greasy, as heavy products can weigh down curls and make them look limp.

3. Use a Diffuser Attachment on Your Hair Dryer

Another way to enhance your son's natural curls is to use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer. A diffuser helps to distribute heat evenly, which can help to prevent frizz and enhance curl definition. To use a diffuser, simply attach it to your hair dryer and use a low heat setting to dry your son's hair. Be sure to scrunch hair gently with your hands as you dry to enhance curls.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Ready to make your son's hair curly? Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Hair

Begin by washing your son's hair with a curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to massage the products into the scalp and hair, and rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 2: Apply Curl-Defining Cream or Gel

Next, apply a small amount of curl-defining cream or gel to your son's damp hair. Be sure to distribute the product evenly, and avoid applying too much, as this can weigh down curls.

Step 3: Dry Hair with a Diffuser

Attach a diffuser to your hair dryer and use a low heat setting to dry your son's hair. Be sure to scrunch hair gently with your hands as you dry to enhance curls.

Step 4: Style Hair as Desired

Once hair is dry, style as desired. You can use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently separate curls and create volume.

Tips for Curly Hair Care

Here are some tips to help you care for your son's curly hair:

1. Avoid Brushing Hair When Dry

Brushing curly hair when it's dry can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently separate curls when hair is wet or damp.

2. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help to prevent friction and breakage, which can be especially beneficial for curly hair.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can cause damage to curly hair. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and use the lowest heat setting possible.

FAQ

Q: Can I make my son's hair curly if it's naturally straight?

A: While you may not be able to completely change your son's hair texture, you can enhance his natural curls with the right products and techniques.

Q: How often should I wash my son's curly hair?

A: It's best to wash curly hair no more than twice a week to avoid stripping natural oils from the hair.

Q: What should I do if my son's curls are frizzy?

A: To combat frizz, use a curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner, apply a curl-defining cream or gel, and use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer. You can also try using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help smooth frizz.

By following these tips and using the right products and techniques, you can help your son achieve the curly hair of his dreams. Remember to be patient and experiment with different products to find what works best for his hair type.
