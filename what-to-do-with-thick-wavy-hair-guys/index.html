What To Do With Thick Wavy Hair Guys

Written by Frank Sep 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thick wavy hair can be a blessing and a curse. On the one hand, it looks great when it's styled properly. On the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style. If you're a guy with thick wavy hair, you may be wondering what you can do to make the most of your locks. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about what to do with thick wavy hair guys.

Description

Thick wavy hair is characterized by its volume, thickness, and texture. It can be curly or straight, but it always has a lot of body. This type of hair can be difficult to manage because it tends to frizz and get tangled easily. However, if you learn to work with your hair's natural texture, you can create some amazing styles.

How to

The first step to styling thick wavy hair is to find the right haircut. You want a cut that will enhance your natural texture and work with the shape of your face. Some good options for guys with thick wavy hair include the undercut, the side part, and the messy fringe.

Once you have the right haircut, it's time to start styling. Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your thick wavy hair:

Step by step

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a styling product that's designed for your hair type. If you have thick wavy hair, you may want to use a styling cream or a light gel.
  3. Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to work the product through your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly.
  4. Use a hair dryer to dry your hair, using your fingers to scrunch your hair as you go. This will help to enhance your natural texture.
  5. If you want a more defined look, you can use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer. This will help to create more defined curls or waves.
  6. Once your hair is dry, you can use a small amount of styling wax or pomade to define your style and add some shine.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you get the most out of your thick wavy hair:

  • Don't wash your hair every day. This can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it harder to style.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons. These can damage your hair and make it frizzy.
  • Invest in a good quality hair brush or comb. This will help to detangle your hair and distribute product evenly.
  • If you have particularly unruly hair, you may want to consider getting a keratin treatment. This can help to smooth and tame your hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about what to do with thick wavy hair guys:

What is the best haircut for thick wavy hair?

The best haircut for thick wavy hair is one that enhances your natural texture and works with the shape of your face. Some good options include the undercut, the side part, and the messy fringe.

What styling products should I use for thick wavy hair?

If you have thick wavy hair, you may want to use a styling cream or a light gel. These products will help to define your curls or waves without weighing your hair down.

How often should I wash my thick wavy hair?

You should aim to wash your thick wavy hair every 2-3 days. Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and make it harder to style.

Hair Care

Taking care of your thick wavy hair is essential if you want to keep it looking great. Here are some tips to help you maintain healthy, shiny hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons.
  • Invest in a good quality hair brush or comb.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair when you brush or comb it.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.

By following these tips, you can keep your thick wavy hair looking great and feeling healthy.

Conclusion

Thick wavy hair can be a challenge to manage, but with the right haircut and styling products, you can create some amazing styles. Remember to take care of your hair by using gentle products and avoiding heat styling tools. With a little bit of effort, you can make the most of your natural texture and look great every day.
