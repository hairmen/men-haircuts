How To Cut Asian Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jun 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're an Asian with long, thick hair, you know the struggle of finding a good hair stylist who can give you the perfect haircut. Going to a salon can be expensive, time-consuming, and sometimes, disappointing. If you're tired of dealing with all these hassles, why not try cutting your hair at home? In this article, we'll guide you on how to cut Asian hair at home, step by step.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cut Asian Hair at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Asian Hair at Home
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting

How to Cut Asian Hair at Home

Cutting your own hair can seem intimidating, but with a little patience and practice, you can achieve great results. Here are some things you'll need to get started:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Two mirrors (one to see the back of your head)
  • Clips to hold your hair in place
  • A comb
  • A spray bottle with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut Asian hair at home:

  1. Start with dry, untangled hair. It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, so you can see how it falls naturally. Comb your hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. Use clips to divide your hair into four or more sections, depending on the thickness of your hair.
  3. Start with the back. Use a mirror to see the back of your head. Lift a section of hair and cut it straight across, using the bottom of the previous section as a guide.
  4. Move to the sides. Take a section of hair from the sides and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, using the previous section as a guide.
  5. Cut the bangs. If you have bangs, separate them from the rest of your hair. Cut them straight across or at an angle, depending on your preference.
  6. Check for symmetry. Use two mirrors to check if your hair is symmetrical. If not, trim the longer side to match the shorter side.
  7. Blend the layers. Use scissors to blend the layers together, so your hair looks natural and not choppy.
  8. Trim the ends. Check for split ends and trim them off. Cut the ends at an angle for a natural look.
  9. Style your hair. Wash and style your hair as usual. Your new haircut is ready!

Tips for Cutting Asian Hair at Home

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to cut a little bit at a time, so you can see the results and adjust accordingly.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and stressful, so take breaks if you feel overwhelmed.
  • Watch tutorials. There are many tutorials online that can teach you how to cut Asian hair at home. Watch them to get some inspiration and learn new techniques.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting Asian hair at home:

Q: Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?
A: Yes, you can. Follow the same steps, but keep in mind that curly hair tends to bounce up when it's dry, so cut it a little longer than you want it to be. Q: How often should I cut my hair?
A: It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, it's recommended to cut your hair every six to eight weeks. Q: What should I do if I make a mistake?
A: Don't panic. Mistakes happen, and hair grows back. If you make a mistake, try to fix it by trimming the other side to match. If it's a big mistake, you can always go to a salon to get it fixed.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy.

With these tips and steps, you can confidently cut your own Asian hair at home. Not only will you save time and money, but you'll also have the satisfaction of knowing that you did it yourself!
