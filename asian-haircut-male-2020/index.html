Asian Haircut Male 2020 A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Oct 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a male of Asian descent looking for a new haircut, you're in luck. The year 2020 saw a surge in trendy and stylish haircuts for Asian men, and we're here to help you find the perfect one for you. In this guide, we'll cover everything from the most popular styles to how to care for your new cut.

Description

The Asian male haircut scene has always been diverse, with a range of styles that cater to different face shapes, hair types, and personal preferences. In 2020, however, we saw a shift towards more modern and edgy styles, with a focus on texture, layers, and contrasts. From buzz cuts to long hairstyles, there's something for everyone.

How To

The first step in getting an Asian haircut is to find the right stylist. Look for someone who has experience working with Asian hair, as it tends to be thicker and coarser than other types of hair. You can also bring in pictures of hairstyles you like to show your stylist what you're looking for.

Once you've found your stylist, it's time to choose your haircut. Some of the most popular styles for Asian men in 2020 include:

The Buzz Cut

A classic and low-maintenance style, the buzz cut involves cutting the hair short all around. This style works well for those with round or square faces, as it helps to balance out the proportions.

The Undercut

The undercut involves shaving the sides and back of the head, while leaving the top longer. This creates a bold and edgy look that works well for those with oval or oblong faces. You can also experiment with different lengths and textures on top, such as a slicked-back style or a messy fringe.

The Side Part

A timeless and versatile style, the side part involves parting the hair on one side and combing it over to the other side. This can be done with short or medium-length hair, and works well for those with rectangular or diamond faces.

Step By Step

Once you've chosen your style, your stylist will begin cutting your hair. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get an Asian haircut:

  1. Wash your hair and towel-dry it.
  2. Sit in the chair and let your stylist assess your hair type and face shape.
  3. Discuss the style you want and any modifications you'd like to make.
  4. Let your stylist begin cutting your hair, starting with the sides and back and moving to the top.
  5. Once your hair is cut, your stylist may use scissors or a razor to add texture and layers.
  6. Style your hair using your preferred products, such as gel or pomade.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when getting an Asian haircut:

  • Choose a style that complements your face shape and hair type.
  • Bring in pictures of hairstyles you like to show your stylist.
  • Ask your stylist for product recommendations to help style and maintain your new cut.
  • Consider getting a touch-up every 4-6 weeks to keep your hair looking fresh.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about Asian haircuts:

What is the best haircut for Asian men?

The best haircut for Asian men depends on their face shape, hair type, and personal style. Some popular styles include the buzz cut, undercut, and side part.

How do I choose the right haircut?

To choose the right haircut, consider your face shape and hair type, as well as any personal preferences you may have. You can also bring in pictures of styles you like to show your stylist.

How do I care for my Asian haircut?

To care for your Asian haircut, use products that are designed for your hair type and style. This may include shampoo and conditioner, hair gel, and hair wax. You should also get regular touch-ups to keep your hair looking fresh.

Hair Care

To keep your Asian haircut looking its best, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as from blow dryers or straighteners.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock a stylish Asian haircut that looks great and is easy to maintain.
