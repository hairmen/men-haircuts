How To Cut Curly Hair Layers At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 28, 2023 · 4 min read

Curly hair can be tricky to handle, especially when it comes to cutting layers. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful layered haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we’ll show you how to cut curly hair layers at home, step by step.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Curly Hair Layers at Home
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Curly Hair Layers
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

How to Cut Curly Hair Layers at Home

The first step in cutting curly hair layers at home is to gather the right tools. You’ll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair.

Next, it’s important to have a clear idea of the type of layers you want. Do you want long layers, short layers, or something in between? Have a picture or a reference handy so you can visualize the end result.

Once you have everything you need, it’s time to start cutting. Follow these simple steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with your favorite shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair, then detangle it with a wide-tooth comb. Blow dry your hair with a diffuser attachment until it’s about 80% dry.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using hair clips, divide your hair into four or five sections: two at the front, two at the sides, and one at the back.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the back, and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, then cut the ends in a straight line.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the next section of hair, which should be about an inch above the bottom layer. Comb it straight down, then hold it between your fingers and cut the ends in a straight line.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Release the top layer of your hair, which should be about an inch above the middle layer. Comb it straight down, then hold it between your fingers and cut the ends in a straight line.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Using scissors or a razor, blend the layers together by cutting into the ends of the hair at a slight angle. This will create a seamless transition between the layers.

Step 7: Check for Balance

Once you’ve finished cutting, check for balance by comparing both sides of your hair in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that both sides are even.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired, using a curling iron or other styling tools.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair Layers

  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as curly hair tends to shrink when dry.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take your time and work slowly, cutting small sections of hair at a time.
  • Consult a professional hairstylist if you’re unsure about cutting your hair at home.

FAQs

Can I cut my curly hair layers with regular scissors?

No, it’s important to use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean cut. Regular scissors can damage the hair and create split ends.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It’s recommended to cut your curly hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

What’s the best way to style curly hair?

The best way to style curly hair is to use a curling iron or other styling tools to enhance your natural curls.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent frizz and dryness.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as this can damage your hair.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can cut curly hair layers at home like a pro. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and consult a professional hairstylist if you’re unsure. Happy cutting!
