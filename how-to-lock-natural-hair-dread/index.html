How To Lock Natural Hair Dread A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Nov 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of constantly having to style your natural hair every day? Dreadlocks may be the answer for you. Not only are they low-maintenance, but they also look great on all hair types. In this article, we’ll be discussing how to lock natural hair dread in a relaxed and easy-to-follow manner.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Lock Natural Hair Dread
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Locked Natural Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Locked Natural Hair

Description

Dreadlocks, also known as “locs,” are formed by matting and twisting sections of hair. This process can take anywhere from a few months to a few years, but once locked, they require minimal maintenance. Locking natural hair can be done in several ways, including the traditional method of backcombing, twist and rip, and interlocking. In this article, we’ll be focusing on the backcombing method.

How to Lock Natural Hair Dread

The backcombing method involves sectioning your hair and teasing it in the opposite direction of growth until it forms a tight knot. To lock natural hair, this process is repeated until each section is fully locked.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Section your hair into small, manageable pieces using a rat-tail comb.

Step 2: Take one section of hair and tease it using a comb, starting from the ends and working your way up towards the scalp.

Step 3: Once the section is teased, roll it between your palms to form a tight knot.

Step 4: Repeat steps 2 and 3 on each section of hair until your entire head is locked.

Step 5: Once your hair is fully locked, maintain it by washing it regularly with a residue-free shampoo and avoiding heavy oils and products that can weigh down your locks.

Tips for Maintaining Locked Natural Hair

Tip 1: Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can cause your locks to unravel. Stick to washing your hair once or twice a week.

Tip 2: Keep your scalp moisturized by using a light oil or moisturizer.

Tip 3: Protect your locks at night by wearing a satin or silk scarf or bonnet.

Tip 4: Avoid using heat on your locks, as this can cause damage and weaken the hair.

FAQs

Q: How long does it take to lock natural hair?

A: The time it takes to lock natural hair can vary depending on the method used and your hair texture. It can take anywhere from a few months to a few years.

Q: Can I color my locked hair?

A: Yes, you can color your locked hair, but be sure to use a gentle, ammonia-free hair dye and avoid excessive heat.

Q: Can I swim with locked hair?

A: Yes, you can swim with locked hair, but be sure to wash and condition your hair thoroughly afterwards to remove any chlorine or saltwater buildup.

Hair Care for Locked Natural Hair

Keeping your locked natural hair healthy and strong requires proper hair care. Here are a few tips:

Tip 1: Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid buildup and keep your locks clean.

Tip 2: Massage your scalp regularly to promote blood flow and healthy hair growth.

Tip 3: Avoid using heavy products or oils on your locks, as this can cause buildup and weigh down your hair.

Tip 4: Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf, as prolonged exposure to UV rays can cause damage and dryness.

Now that you know how to lock natural hair dread, it’s time to start your journey towards low-maintenance, beautiful locks. Remember to be patient, take care of your hair, and enjoy the journey!
