How To Go From Wavy To Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 14, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have wavy hair and want to achieve bouncy, defined curls, you're in the right place. In this guide, we'll show you how to go from wavy to curly hair in easy steps. From understanding your hair type to choosing the right products, we'll cover everything you need to know to achieve the curly hair of your dreams.

Table of Contents

  • Understanding Your Hair Type
  • Choosing the Right Products
  • Preparing Your Hair
  • Curling Techniques
  • Hair Care Tips

Understanding Your Hair Type

Before you start trying to make your hair curly, it's important to understand your hair type. Wavy hair falls somewhere between straight and curly hair, and can range from loose S-shaped waves to more defined curls. Knowing your hair type will help you choose the right products and techniques to achieve the look you want.

To determine your hair type, look at the shape of your hair strands. Straight hair is round, while curly hair is oval or flat. Wavy hair falls somewhere in between, with a slightly flattened shape.

Wavy hair can also be categorized by its curl pattern. Type 2A hair has loose, beachy waves, while Type 2B hair has more defined S-shaped waves. Type 2C hair has more pronounced waves that can start to look like loose curls.

Choosing the Right Products

To achieve curly hair, you'll need to use products that help enhance your natural wave pattern. Look for products specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl-enhancing shampoos and conditioners. These products contain ingredients that help define your curls and reduce frizz.

You may also want to try using a curl-defining cream or gel. These products help hold your curls in place and give them more definition. Be sure to choose a product that's appropriate for your hair type and curl pattern.

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start curling your hair, it's important to prepare it properly. Start by washing your hair with a curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair gently, being careful not to rub it too vigorously.

Next, apply a curl-defining product to your hair, working it through from root to tip. You can also use a leave-in conditioner to help moisturize your hair and reduce frizz.

Curling Techniques

There are a variety of techniques you can use to curl your hair, depending on your hair type and curl pattern. Here are a few to try:

  • Finger coiling: This technique involves twisting small sections of hair around your finger to create defined curls.
  • Plopping: Plopping involves wrapping your hair in a T-shirt or microfiber towel to help enhance your curls.
  • Diffusing: Using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer can help enhance your curls and reduce frizz.

Experiment with different techniques to find out what works best for your hair.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your curls looking their best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and reduce curl definition.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, being careful not to pull or tug too hard.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to help reduce friction and prevent frizz.

FAQ

Can I go from wavy to curly hair permanently?

While you can make your hair appear more curly using techniques and products, you can't permanently change your hair's natural texture.

Can I use the same products for wavy and curly hair?

While some products may work for both hair types, it's best to use products specifically designed for your hair type and curl pattern.

How often should I wash my curly hair?

It's best to wash curly hair every 2-3 days, as frequent washing can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause frizz.

How can I reduce frizz in my curly hair?

To reduce frizz, avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, detangle your hair gently with a wide-toothed comb or your fingers while it's wet.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve bouncy, defined curls that last. Experiment with different products and techniques to find out what works best for your hair type and curl pattern.
