How To Cut A Pixie Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Oct 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a bold and daring hairstyle, a pixie cut might be just what you need. Not only is it fashionable and low-maintenance, but it's also a great way to show off your facial features. However, getting a pixie cut at a salon can be pricey, which is why many people choose to do it themselves. In this article, we'll go over the steps you need to follow to cut a pixie yourself.

Description

A pixie cut is a short hairstyle that typically features cropped hair on the sides and back of the head, with longer hair on top. It's a versatile cut that can be styled in various ways, from sleek and sophisticated to edgy and playful. Pixie cuts are great for all hair types, but they work particularly well with thin, fine hair, as they can create the illusion of volume and thickness.

How to Cut a Pixie Yourself

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few essential tools. These include:

  • Scissors
  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Hair ties
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror

Make sure your scissors and clippers are sharp and clean, and that you have plenty of hair ties and clips on hand to keep your hair out of the way.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: the top, back, and two sides. Use hair ties or clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin by cutting the back section of your hair with clippers. Use a longer guard for the top of your head and a shorter guard for the bottom. Move the clippers up and down in even strokes until you've cut all the hair in the back section.

Next, move on to the sides. Use the same clippers and guard to cut the hair on the sides of your head, moving from the bottom up. Be careful not to cut too high, as you want to maintain some length on top.

Finally, it's time to cut the top section of your hair. Start by combing it straight up, and then use scissors to cut it to your desired length. You can also use the point-cutting technique to create texture and movement.

Step 4: Refine Your Cut

Once you've cut all the sections of your hair, it's time to refine your cut. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven sections, and use clippers to blend the sides and back into the top. Take your time and make sure everything looks even and balanced.

Tips

  • Start with clean, dry hair to make it easier to cut.
  • Use a spray bottle of water to dampen your hair if needed while cutting.
  • Be patient and take your time. It's better to cut a little bit at a time than to take off too much.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you go.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair yourself, consider asking a friend or family member for help.

FAQ

Can I cut a pixie myself?

Yes, you can cut a pixie yourself with the right tools and technique. However, if you're unsure about cutting your hair yourself, it's always best to consult a professional stylist.

What's the best way to style a pixie cut?

There are many ways to style a pixie cut, from sleek and polished to messy and textured. Experiment with different products, such as pomade, wax, or mousse, to find the look that works best for you. You can also use accessories like headbands, clips, or scarves to add some flair to your style.

How often do I need to trim my pixie cut?

It's recommended to trim your pixie cut every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and style. However, this can vary depending on your hair's growth rate and how quickly your style grows out.

Hair Care

To keep your pixie cut looking its best, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid over-styling or using too much heat. Regular trims can also help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Cutting a pixie yourself can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it's important to take your time and follow the right steps. With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to achieve a stylish and low-maintenance pixie cut that suits your personality and lifestyle.
