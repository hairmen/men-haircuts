How To Make Thinning Shears

Written by Petter Aug 24, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune on hair tools? Thinning shears can be quite expensive, but did you know that you can make them at home? In this article, we'll teach you how to make your own thinning shears using simple materials.

Description

Thinning shears are a type of scissors with teeth on one or both blades that are used to thin out hair. They are commonly used by hairstylists to create texture and volume in haircuts. Thinning shears come in different sizes and styles, and can be quite expensive. However, making your own thinning shears can save you a lot of money.

How to Make Thinning Shears

Here's what you'll need to make your own thinning shears:

  • A pair of regular scissors
  • A metal file
  • A piece of sandpaper
  • A ruler
  • A metal ruler or straight edge
  • A piece of aluminum or tin can
  • A pair of pliers

Follow these steps to make your own thinning shears:

Step 1: Cut the Piece of Metal

Using a pair of regular scissors, cut a piece of aluminum or tin can to the length of the blade of your regular scissors.

Step 2: File the Edges

Using a metal file, round the edges of the piece of metal you just cut. This will prevent it from cutting or scratching the hair.

Step 3: Sand the Surface

Using a piece of sandpaper, sand the surface of the metal piece until it is smooth.

Step 4: Measure and Mark

Using a ruler, measure and mark the center of the piece of metal. Then, using a metal ruler or straight edge, make a line down the center of the piece of metal.

Step 5: Cut the Teeth

Using a pair of pliers, cut teeth into the metal piece along the center line. The teeth should be evenly spaced and should be about 1/8 inch apart.

Step 6: Test the Shears

Test the shears by cutting a piece of hair. If the shears are too sharp, use the metal file to round the edges of the teeth. If the shears are not sharp enough, use the metal file to sharpen the teeth.

Tips

  • Be careful when handling the metal piece as it can be sharp.
  • You can use different materials for the metal piece, such as old credit cards or plastic rulers.
  • Practice on a piece of hair before using the shears on a person's hair.

FAQ

Q: Can I use any type of scissors to make thinning shears?

A: No, it's best to use a pair of regular scissors that have a sharp blade.

Q: How many teeth should I cut into the metal piece?

A: It depends on the texture and thickness of the hair. Start with fewer teeth and add more if needed.

Hair Care

Thinning shears can be a great tool to add texture and volume to haircuts, but they should be used with caution. Overuse of thinning shears can cause damage to the hair and lead to split ends. It's important to only use thinning shears on healthy hair and to maintain a regular hair care routine to keep the hair strong and healthy.

Now that you know how to make your own thinning shears, you can save money and create unique haircuts with added texture and volume. Happy crafting!
