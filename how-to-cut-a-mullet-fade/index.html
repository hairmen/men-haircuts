How To Cut A Mullet Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a new haircut that's both trendy and edgy, a mullet fade might be just what you need. This unique hairstyle combines the classic mullet with a modern fade, creating a look that's both retro and contemporary. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting a mullet fade, from choosing the right tools to finishing touches.

Description:

A mullet fade is a hairstyle that combines a classic mullet with a modern fade. The top of the hair is left long and often styled with layers, while the sides and back are cut short with a gradual fade towards the neckline. This creates a distinct contrast between the length of the hair on top and the shorter sides and back.

How to Cut a Mullet Fade: Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools on hand. You'll need a pair of scissors, clippers, a comb, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep the hair damp as you work.

Step 2: Start with the Top

Begin by cutting the top of the hair to the desired length. Use your scissors to create layers and texture, if desired. Keep in mind that the top of the hair should be significantly longer than the sides and back.

Step 3: Move to the Sides

Using your clippers, start cutting the sides of the hair. Begin with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one, creating a gradual fade towards the neckline. Use your comb to blend the hair as you go.

Step 4: Work on the Back

Next, move to the back of the head and repeat the same process as you did for the sides. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one, creating a gradual fade towards the neckline. Use your comb to blend the hair as you go.

Step 5: Refine the Cut

Once you've finished cutting, take a step back and evaluate the overall shape of the haircut. Use your scissors and clippers to refine any areas that need additional attention.

Step 6: Style the Hair

After you've finished cutting, it's time to style the hair. Apply a small amount of styling product, such as pomade or wax, to the hair on top and use your fingers to create your desired look.

Tips for Cutting a Mullet Fade

- Take your time and cut slowly. It's better to take off less hair than too much.
- Use a mirror to check your progress from all angles.
- Keep the hair damp as you work, using a spray bottle filled with water.
- Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're unsure about any part of the process.

FAQ

Q: How long should the top of the hair be for a mullet fade?
A: The length of the top of the hair can vary depending on personal preference, but it should be significantly longer than the sides and back.

Q: How often should I get a mullet fade?
A: It's recommended to get a trim every four to six weeks to maintain the shape of the haircut.

Hair Care Tips

- Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
- Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage.
- Trim the ends of your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
- Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant product.

With this step-by-step guide, you're ready to tackle the mullet fade haircut. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and ask for help if needed. With a little practice, you'll be able to master this trendy and edgy hairstyle.
