How To Cut The Back Of My Own Short Hair A Diy Guide

Written by Eveline Nov 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have short hair, you know that regular haircuts are essential to keep your locks looking fresh and healthy. However, going to the salon every few weeks can be expensive and time-consuming. That's why many people opt to cut their hair at home. But, cutting the back of your own short hair can be tricky, especially if you don't have experience. In this article, we'll show you how to cut the back of your own short hair like a pro.

Description

Cutting the back of your own short hair can seem daunting, but it's not as difficult as it may seem. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll provide you with step-by-step instructions, tips, and tricks to help you achieve the perfect cut every time.

How To

To cut the back of your own short hair, you'll need a few tools, including:

  • Sharp scissors or clippers
  • A handheld mirror
  • A larger mirror
  • Clips or elastics to section your hair
  • A comb or brush

Once you have your tools ready, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's important to start with clean and dry hair. This will help you see the natural shape of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using clips or elastics, section your hair into manageable parts. Start at the bottom and work your way up. This will help you see what you're doing and prevent you from cutting too much hair at once.

Step 3: Cut One Section at a Time

Start with the bottom section and work your way up. Use your handheld mirror to see the back of your head, and the larger mirror to see the front. Take small sections of hair and cut them at a slight angle. This will help create layers and a more natural look.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the sections, blend the layers together. Use your comb or brush to comb through your hair and make sure everything is even.

Step 5: Trim Any Stray Hairs

Finally, use your scissors or clippers to trim any stray hairs or uneven sections. Take your time and be very careful, as you don't want to accidentally cut off too much hair.

Tips

  • Invest in a good pair of scissors or clippers. This will make the process much easier and give you better results.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting your own hair can be time-consuming, but it's worth it to achieve the perfect cut.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're struggling to see the back of your head or need someone to double-check your work, ask a friend or family member to lend a hand.

FAQ

How often should I cut my short hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

Don't panic! If you accidentally cut off too much hair or make a mistake, there are a few things you can do. First, assess the damage and see if you can fix it yourself. If not, don't be afraid to go to a professional for help. They can fix any mistakes and help you get back on track.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims will also help keep your hair looking fresh and prevent split ends.

Cutting the back of your own short hair can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve great results. Follow these steps, take your time, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. With a little practice, you'll be a DIY hair-cutting pro in no time!
