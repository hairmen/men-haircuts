How To Layer Natural Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have natural curly hair, you know how important it is to keep your curls defined and healthy. One way to achieve this is by layering your hair. Layering can add volume, definition, and shape to your curls, making them look more polished and put-together. In this article, we'll go through a step-by-step guide on how to layer natural curly hair, including tips and hair care advice.

How to Layer Natural Curly Hair

Layering natural curly hair can be a bit tricky, but with the right technique, you can achieve a gorgeous, voluminous look. Here are the basic steps:

Step 1: Decide on the Length of Your Layers

The first step is to decide on the length of your layers. Generally, layers start at chin-level or lower, but you can choose to have shorter or longer layers depending on your preference.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Next, divide your hair into sections. You can do this by parting your hair down the middle and then from ear to ear. Clip each section with a hair clip to keep them separate.

Step 3: Cut the First Layer

Starting with the bottom section of your hair, cut the first layer. Use a pair of sharp hair scissors and make small, angled cuts. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven layers.

Step 4: Move on to the Next Section

Once you've finished cutting the first layer, move on to the next section and repeat the process. Make sure to cut each layer slightly shorter than the previous one to create a gradual, layered effect.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

After you've finished cutting all the layers, blend them together by using the scissors to make small, quick cuts in the hair. This will help to create a seamless, natural-looking effect.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to layer natural curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help detangle your curls and keep them smooth. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly.

Step 3: Blow-Dry Your Hair

Blow-dry your hair using a diffuser attachment on a low heat setting. This will help to enhance your curls and create volume.

Step 4: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections as described above.

Step 5: Cut the First Layer

Cut the first layer as described above.

Step 6: Move on to the Next Section

Repeat the process for each section, making sure to cut each layer slightly shorter than the previous one.

Step 7: Blend the Layers

Blend the layers together as described above.

Tips for Layering Natural Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when layering your natural curly hair:

  • Make sure to use sharp hair scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven layers.
  • Be patient and take your time. Layering can be a time-consuming process, but the results are worth it.
  • Experiment with different layer lengths to find the right look for you.
  • Consider getting a professional haircut if you're not comfortable cutting your own hair.

FAQs

How often should I layer my natural curly hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your layers lose their shape. Generally, you should aim to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain your layers.

Can I layer my natural curly hair at home?

Yes, you can layer your natural curly hair at home, but it's important to use the right technique and tools. If you're not comfortable cutting your own hair, consider getting a professional haircut.

Hair Care for Layered Natural Curly Hair

Once you've layered your natural curly hair, it's important to take care of it properly to keep your curls defined and healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls, and avoid using a brush.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to keep your curls smooth and defined.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

Layering natural curly hair can be a great way to enhance your curls and create a more polished look. By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, healthy curls that you'll love showing off.
