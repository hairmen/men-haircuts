How To Sleep With Long Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 29, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have long curly hair, you know that it can be a challenge to keep it looking its best. One of the biggest challenges is figuring out how to sleep with long curly hair without damaging it or waking up with an unruly mess. In this guide, we’ll provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to sleep with long curly hair and wake up with beautiful, healthy locks.

Description

Long curly hair is prone to tangling and frizzing, which can be exacerbated by sleeping on it. Traditional sleep movements can cause hair to rub against the pillowcase, causing it to become tangled and frizzy. In addition, sleeping with damp hair can lead to breakage and damage. Therefore, it is important to take steps to protect your long curly hair while you sleep.

How to Sleep with Long Curly Hair

Step 1: Detangle Your Hair

Before going to bed, make sure your hair is completely detangled. Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to gently brush your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. This will help prevent tangles and knots from forming while you sleep.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-in Conditioner

After detangling your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage. Apply the conditioner evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 3: Choose the Right Pillowcase

Choosing the right pillowcase can make a big difference in how your hair looks in the morning. Cotton pillowcases can cause friction and lead to tangles and frizz. Instead, choose a satin or silk pillowcase, which will allow your hair to glide over the surface without causing damage. These fabrics are also gentler on your skin, reducing the risk of wrinkles and creases.

Step 4: Tie Your Hair Up

Tying your hair up in a loose bun or braid can help prevent tangles and frizz while you sleep. Make sure the bun or braid is loose enough to prevent pulling or breakage, but secure enough to keep your hair in place.

Tips for Sleeping with Long Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for sleeping with long curly hair:

  • Use a silk or satin scarf to wrap your hair if you don’t want to tie it up
  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair, as this can lead to breakage and damage
  • Consider sleeping on your back to reduce friction and prevent tangles
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal clasps, as these can damage your hair
  • Apply a hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair to help prevent split ends and breakage

FAQs

Q: Will tying my hair up while I sleep damage it?

A: Tying your hair up in a loose bun or braid should not cause damage. However, it is important to make sure the bun or braid is loose enough to prevent pulling or breakage.

Q: How often should I wash my long curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how much oil your scalp produces. Some people with curly hair can go several days between washes, while others may need to wash their hair every other day. Experiment to find a washing schedule that works for you.

Q: Can I use a regular cotton pillowcase if I don’t have a silk or satin one?

A: While cotton pillowcases can cause friction and lead to tangles and frizz, they are better than sleeping with no pillowcase at all. If you don’t have a silk or satin pillowcase, consider wrapping your hair in a silk or satin scarf.

Hair Care for Long Curly Hair

To keep your long curly hair looking its best, it is important to follow a regular hair care routine. This should include washing and conditioning your hair with products designed for curly hair, using a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to gently detangle your hair, and using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized. It is also a good idea to get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these tips and incorporating them into your nightly routine, you can wake up with beautiful, healthy long curly hair every morning.
