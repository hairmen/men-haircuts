What To Do If You Have Sores On Your Scalp

Written by Frank Nov 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have sores on your scalp, it can be uncomfortable and worrisome. But don't worry, there are steps you can take to get relief and heal the sores. In this article, we'll cover some tips and tricks on how to deal with scalp sores.

Description

Scalp sores can appear as red bumps, scabs, or blisters on your scalp. They can be itchy, painful, and sometimes even lead to hair loss. There are a variety of reasons why you might get scalp sores, including infections, allergies, or skin conditions like psoriasis or eczema. If you're experiencing persistent scalp sores, it's important to see a dermatologist to determine the underlying cause and get proper treatment.

How To

If you have a mild case of scalp sores, there are some things you can do to get relief and help the sores heal. Here are some tips:

1. Keep your scalp clean

Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo to keep your scalp clean. Avoid using harsh shampoos that can irritate your scalp and make the sores worse.

2. Use a medicated shampoo

If you have an infection or skin condition causing the scalp sores, your dermatologist may recommend a medicated shampoo to help clear it up. Follow the instructions carefully and use it as directed.

3. Avoid scratching

It can be tempting to scratch the sores, but avoid doing so as it can make them worse and lead to infection. Try using a cool compress or applying a soothing cream to relieve the itchiness.

4. Take care of your overall health

Eating a healthy diet, getting enough rest, and managing stress can help improve your overall health and boost your immune system, which can help your body fight off infections and heal faster.

Step By Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on what to do if you have scalp sores:

Step 1: See a dermatologist

If you have persistent scalp sores, it's important to see a dermatologist to determine the underlying cause and get proper treatment.

Step 2: Keep your scalp clean

Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo to keep your scalp clean.

Step 3: Use a medicated shampoo

If your dermatologist recommends a medicated shampoo, use it as directed to help clear up the infection or skin condition causing the sores.

Step 4: Avoid scratching

Avoid scratching the sores to prevent infection and further irritation.

Step 5: Take care of your overall health

Eat a healthy diet, get enough rest, and manage stress to boost your immune system and help your body heal faster.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for dealing with scalp sores:

1. Avoid using hair products that contain harsh chemicals

Chemicals in hair products can irritate your scalp and make the sores worse. Look for gentle, natural products instead.

2. Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun

If you're going to be outside for a prolonged period, wearing a hat or scarf can help protect your scalp from the sun and prevent further irritation.

3. Be patient

Scalp sores can take time to heal, so be patient and follow your dermatologist's instructions for treatment.

FAQ

Q: Can scalp sores cause hair loss?

A: Yes, in some cases, scalp sores can lead to temporary hair loss. If you're experiencing hair loss, it's important to see a dermatologist to determine the underlying cause and get proper treatment.

Q: Can scalp sores be contagious?

A: It depends on the underlying cause. Some infections that cause scalp sores, like ringworm, can be contagious. It's important to see a dermatologist to determine the cause and take appropriate precautions to prevent spreading the infection.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important when dealing with scalp sores. Here are some tips:

1. Avoid tight hairstyles

Avoid hairstyles that pull on your scalp, as this can irritate the sores and make them worse.

2. Use a wide-tooth comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair and avoid pulling on your scalp.

3. Be gentle when styling

Be gentle when styling your hair and avoid using heat styling tools that can further damage your hair and scalp.

Dealing with scalp sores can be uncomfortable, but with the right treatment and care, you can get relief and heal the sores. Remember to see a dermatologist if you have persistent scalp sores, and be patient as they can take time to heal.
