How To Grow Out Short Hair Styles A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've recently chopped your hair into a short style, but now you're regretting it and want to grow it out, you're not alone. Many women have been there, including celebrities like Emma Watson and Jennifer Lawrence. Growing out short hair styles can be a frustrating process, but with a little patience and some hair care tips, you can achieve your desired length in no time. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to grow out short hair styles.

Description

Growing out short hair styles can be a lengthy and frustrating process, but it's possible to achieve your desired length with some patience and a few tips. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about growing out short hair styles, from how to care for your hair during the process to styling tips and tricks.

How to Grow Out Short Hair Styles

Step 1: Be Patient

The first step in growing out short hair styles is to be patient. Hair typically grows about half an inch per month, so it could take several months or even a year to grow out a short style, depending on how short your hair is and how long you want it to be. While waiting for your hair to grow, try to embrace the length and experiment with different styles that work with your current length.

Step 2: Keep Your Hair Healthy

Keeping your hair healthy is essential for growth. Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid over-styling with heat tools like straighteners and curling irons, which can damage your hair and slow down growth. Consider taking hair growth supplements or eating a healthy diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals.

Step 3: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly may seem counterintuitive when you're trying to grow it out, but it's essential for keeping your hair healthy and preventing split ends, which can lead to breakage and slow down growth. Aim to trim your hair every six to eight weeks, or whenever you notice split ends.

Step 4: Experiment with Styles

While you're growing out your hair, experiment with different styles that work with your current length. Try braids, twists, and updos to keep your hair off your face and help it appear longer. You can also use hair accessories like headbands and clips to add a little flair to your style.

Tips for Growing Out Short Hair Styles

Tip 1: Use Hair Extensions

If you're struggling to wait for your hair to grow, consider using hair extensions to add length and volume. There are many types of extensions available, including clip-ins, sew-ins, and tape-ins. Consult with a hair stylist to find the best option for your hair and budget.

Tip 2: Try Hair Growth Products

There are many hair growth products on the market that claim to stimulate hair growth and promote healthy hair. Look for products containing ingredients like biotin, keratin, and castor oil, which are known to promote hair growth and strength.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat Styling

As mentioned earlier, heat styling can damage your hair and slow down growth. If you must use heat tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low. Consider air-drying your hair or using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to minimize damage.

FAQs

How long does it take to grow out short hair styles?

It can take several months or even a year to grow out a short hair style, depending on how short your hair is and how long you want it to be.

Should I trim my hair while growing it out?

Yes, trimming your hair regularly is essential for keeping it healthy and preventing split ends, which can lead to breakage and slow down growth.

Can I use hair extensions while growing out my hair?

Yes, hair extensions can be a great option for adding length and volume while you wait for your hair to grow.

Hair Care Tips for Growing Out Short Hair Styles

Growing out short hair styles can be a frustrating process, but with a little patience and some hair care tips, you can achieve your desired length in no time. Remember to be patient, keep your hair healthy, trim it regularly, and experiment with different styles that work with your current length. Consider using hair extensions or hair growth products if you're struggling to wait for your hair to grow, and avoid heat styling as much as possible. With these tips, you'll have long, healthy locks before you know it!
