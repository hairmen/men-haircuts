How To Cut Hair Evenly Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Oct 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts or waiting in long lines at the salon? Learning how to cut hair evenly yourself can save you time and money. With the right tools and some practice, you can achieve professional-looking results. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut hair evenly yourself.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools Needed
  2. Preparation
  3. Sectioning
  4. Cutting
  5. Blending
  6. Tips for Success
  7. FAQs
  8. Haircare After Cutting

Tools Needed

Before getting started, it's important to have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips or hair ties
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Mirror

Preparation

Start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can shrink and dry unevenly, making it harder to cut evenly. If your hair is long, consider washing it the night before and letting it air-dry.

Find a well-lit area with a large mirror. It's important to be able to see what you're doing from all angles. Cover the floor with a towel or sheet to catch any stray hairs.

Sectioning

Sectioning your hair makes it easier to cut evenly. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then dividing it into four equal sections, two in the front and two in the back. Use sectioning clips or hair ties to keep each section separate.

Starting with one section, comb the hair straight and hold it between your fingers. Use your other hand to snip off any split ends or uneven pieces. Take your time and work your way around the section, checking your progress in the mirror as you go.

Cutting

Once you've trimmed any split ends and uneven pieces, it's time to start cutting. Start with one section and comb it straight down. Hold it between your fingers and cut across the ends in a straight line. Repeat this process with each section, working your way around your head.

If you want to add layers, start by cutting the top section slightly shorter than the bottom section. Use your comb to lift each section and cut at an angle, creating a gradual slope. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as it's easier to cut more later than to fix a mistake.

Blending

Once you've finished cutting, it's time to blend the sections together. Use your comb to lift each section and check for any uneven spots. Use your scissors to trim any longer pieces until the sections blend seamlessly together.

If you're using clippers, start with the longest guard and work your way down until you reach the desired length. Use the clippers to create a fade, gradually blending the longer hair on top with the shorter hair on the sides and back.

Tips for Success

  • Start with small snips and work your way up, as it's easier to cut more than to fix a mistake.
  • Don't cut too much at once, as it's harder to fix a mistake than to cut more later.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep your hair damp, as it's easier to cut when it's wet.
  • Take breaks and step back to look at your progress in the mirror often. It's easy to get caught up in cutting and lose sight of the big picture.
  • Practice makes perfect. Don't be discouraged if your first few haircuts don't turn out perfectly. Keep practicing and you'll get better.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair evenly with just scissors?

Yes, you can cut your hair evenly with just scissors. However, clippers can make the process faster and easier, especially if you're going for a shorter style or a fade.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if it's curly. However, it's important to cut it when it's dry and in its natural state to avoid any surprises. Cut small sections at a time and be patient.

Haircare After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take good care of it. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and hydrated. Avoid using heat-styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair soft and healthy.

Congratulations, you now know how to cut hair evenly yourself! With some practice and patience, you can achieve professional-looking results from the comfort of your own home.
