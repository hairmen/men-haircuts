Women s Short Haircuts For Thick Hair 2018

Written by Frank Aug 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of dealing with unruly, thick hair that never seems to stay in place? If so, you may want to consider a short haircut. Not only are short haircuts trendy and stylish, but they can also help tame your thick mane. In this article, we will discuss some of the best women's short haircuts for thick hair in 2018.

Description

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it's great to have a full head of hair, it can also be difficult to manage. Short haircuts are a great option for women with thick hair because they can help reduce the amount of maintenance required to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

How To

When it comes to choosing a short haircut, there are many options to consider. Some popular styles include pixie cuts, bobs, and shags. To determine which style is best for you, it's important to consider your face shape and personal style. It's also a good idea to consult with a hairstylist who can help you choose a flattering cut.

Pixie Cuts

Pixie cuts are a popular short haircut for women with thick hair. This style typically features short, choppy layers that frame the face. It's a great option for women who want a low-maintenance haircut that still looks stylish and trendy.

Bobs

Bobs are another popular short haircut for women with thick hair. This style typically features a blunt cut that falls just above the shoulders. It's a great option for women who want a classic, timeless look that's easy to maintain.

Shags

Shags are a great option for women who want a short haircut that's a little more edgy and modern. This style typically features choppy layers and a lot of texture, which can help to reduce the appearance of thick hair.

Step by Step

If you're considering a short haircut for your thick hair, here are the steps you should take:

  1. Research different short haircuts and determine which style is best for you.
  2. Consult with a hairstylist to determine if the style you want will work with your face shape and hair texture.
  3. When you're ready to get your haircut, bring pictures of the style you want to show your hairstylist.
  4. Take care of your new haircut by using quality hair products and getting regular trims to maintain the shape.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when choosing a short haircut for your thick hair:

  • Consider your face shape and personal style when choosing a haircut.
  • Consult with a hairstylist to ensure that the style you want will work with your hair texture.
  • Invest in quality hair products to keep your hair looking healthy and shiny.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your haircut.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about short haircuts for women with thick hair:

What is the best short haircut for thick hair?

The best short haircut for thick hair depends on your personal style and face shape. Pixie cuts, bobs, and shags are all popular options.

Will a short haircut make my hair look thinner?

A short haircut can help to reduce the appearance of thick hair, but it won't necessarily make your hair look thinner. The key is to choose a style that works with your hair texture and face shape.

How do I maintain a short haircut for thick hair?

To maintain a short haircut for thick hair, it's important to use quality hair products and get regular trims. This will help to keep your hair looking healthy and shiny.

Hair Care

If you have thick hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for thick hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your haircut.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair looking healthy and shiny.

By following these tips and choosing a flattering short haircut, you can tame your thick hair and look stylish and trendy in 2018.
