How To Cut Layers Into Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you have short hair, you might think that adding layers is impossible. But, with the right technique, it's actually quite easy! In this article, we'll show you how to cut layers into short hair in a relaxed, easy-to-understand way.

How to Cut Layers into Short Hair

Cutting layers into short hair can add volume and texture to your hair, giving it a more dynamic look. Here's what you need to do:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and dry, so wash and blow-dry your hair before you start.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use hair clips to section your hair into four parts: the top, the sides, and the back.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start at the back of your head, and use the comb to tease a small section of hair. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends at a slight angle. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working your way up to the top of your head.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the sections, use the scissors to blend the layers together. This will give your hair a more natural and seamless look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

As mentioned earlier, you'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use the comb to part your hair down the middle, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, section your hair into four parts: the top, the sides, and the back. Use hair clips to keep each section in place.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start at the back of your head, and choose a small section of hair to start with. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends at a slight angle. This will create a layer. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working your way up to the top of your head. Remember to cut each section to the same length.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the sections, use the scissors to blend the layers together. This will give your hair a more natural and seamless look. Take small sections of hair and snip into them at different angles to create a textured effect.

Tips for Cutting Layers into Short Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting layers into short hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, or you might end up with an uneven cut.
  • Take your time and work slowly.
  • If you're unsure, start with a small amount of hair and work your way up.
  • If you're not confident in your own ability to cut layers into your hair, consider going to a professional stylist.

FAQs

Can you cut layers into really short hair?

Yes, you can! Just be sure to work slowly and take your time.

How often should you cut layers into short hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the layers grow out. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every six to eight weeks.

Can you cut layers into curly hair?

Yes, you can! Just be sure to cut the hair when it's dry, as curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Short Hair

Here are some tips to keep your layered short hair looking healthy and beautiful:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heated styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to protect your hair from environmental damage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh.
  • Consider using a hair serum to add shine and control frizz.

Cutting layers into short hair can seem intimidating, but with a little practice and patience, it's a great way to add volume and texture to your hair. Follow these steps and tips for a beautiful and stylish layered look!
