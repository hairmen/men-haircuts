Does Oral Chemo Make You Lose Your Hair Here s What You Need To Know

Written by Aprilia Nov 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you or someone you know is going through chemotherapy, you may be wondering if oral chemo can cause hair loss. It's a common concern, as hair loss is a well-known side effect of traditional chemotherapy treatments. In this article, we'll explore whether oral chemo can lead to hair loss and provide tips on how to manage hair loss during treatment.

What is Oral Chemo?

Oral chemotherapy, also known as chemotherapy by mouth, is a type of chemotherapy that is taken in pill or liquid form rather than being administered through an IV. Oral chemo can be just as effective as traditional chemotherapy, but it is often used when a patient's cancer is in an early stage or when traditional chemo would not be effective.

Does Oral Chemo Make You Lose Your Hair?

The answer to this question is not as straightforward as you might think. While traditional chemotherapy treatments are known to cause hair loss, oral chemo may or may not lead to hair loss depending on the specific drugs being used.

Some oral chemotherapy drugs, such as capecitabine (Xeloda) and methotrexate, are less likely to cause hair loss than other chemotherapy drugs. However, other oral chemotherapy drugs, such as temozolomide (Temodar) and erlotinib (Tarceva), can cause hair loss in some patients.

If you are taking oral chemo and are concerned about hair loss, talk to your doctor. They can tell you whether the drugs you are taking are likely to cause hair loss and what you can do to manage it.

How to Manage Hair Loss During Oral Chemo

If you do experience hair loss during oral chemo, there are several things you can do to manage it:

Consider a new hairstyle or wig

If you are concerned about hair loss, consider getting a new hairstyle or wig before you start treatment. This will help you get used to your new look and make the transition easier.

Protect your scalp

During treatment, your scalp may become more sensitive. Protect it from the sun and the cold with a hat or scarf. Avoid using harsh chemicals on your scalp, such as hair dyes or perms.

Be gentle with your hair

Use a gentle shampoo and avoid using hot styling tools like curling irons or straighteners. Let your hair air dry whenever possible.

Step-by-Step Guide for Hair Care During Oral Chemo

If you are concerned about hair loss during oral chemo, here is a step-by-step guide for caring for your hair:

Step 1: Wash your hair less frequently

Washing your hair less frequently can help prevent hair loss. Try to wash your hair every other day instead of every day.

Step 2: Use a gentle shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that is free of harsh chemicals. Look for a shampoo that is designed for sensitive scalps.

Step 3: Avoid hot styling tools

Avoid using hot styling tools like curling irons or straighteners, as they can damage your hair and make it more likely to fall out.

Step 4: Let your hair air dry

Let your hair air dry whenever possible. Avoid using a hair dryer, as the heat can damage your hair and make it more likely to fall out.

Step 5: Protect your scalp

Protect your scalp from the sun and the cold with a hat or scarf. Avoid using harsh chemicals on your scalp, such as hair dyes or perms.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will I definitely lose my hair during oral chemo?

A: Not necessarily. Whether or not you will lose your hair depends on the specific drugs you are taking. Talk to your doctor to find out whether hair loss is a potential side effect of the drugs you are taking.

Q: Will my hair grow back after oral chemo?

A: In most cases, yes. However, it may take several months for your hair to grow back to its pre-treatment length and thickness.

Q: Can I color my hair during oral chemo?

A: It is generally not recommended to color your hair during chemo, as the chemicals in hair dye can be harsh on your scalp and may cause further hair loss. However, if you do choose to color your hair, talk to your doctor first.

In conclusion, if you are taking oral chemo and are concerned about hair loss, talk to your doctor. While hair loss is a potential side effect of some oral chemotherapy drugs, there are things you can do to manage it. Follow the tips in this article to care for your hair during treatment and remember that your hair will likely grow back once treatment is over.
