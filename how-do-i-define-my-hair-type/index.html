How Do I Define My Hair Type

Written by Christine Jun 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Understanding your hair type is the first step towards achieving healthy and beautiful hair. Knowing your hair type will help you choose the right products and hair care routine that suits your hair's specific needs. In this article, we will guide you step by step on how to define your hair type.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Know the Hair Type Categories
  • Step 2: Observe Your Hair's Texture
  • Step 3: Check Your Hair's Porosity
  • Step 4: Determine Your Hair's Elasticity
  • Tips for Defining Your Hair Type
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Different Hair Types

Step 1: Know the Hair Type Categories

There are four main categories of hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and coily/kinky. Straight hair is characterized by smooth and straight strands, while wavy hair has a slight wave pattern. Curly hair has a more defined curl pattern, and coily/kinky hair has a tight coil or zigzag pattern.

Subcategories of Hair Types

Within each hair type category, there are subcategories that further define the hair type. For example, straight hair can be fine, medium, or coarse. Wavy hair can be categorized as 2A, 2B, or 2C, while curly hair can be classified as 3A, 3B, or 3C. Coily/kinky hair can be categorized as 4A, 4B, or 4C.

Step 2: Observe Your Hair's Texture

Hair texture refers to the thickness or diameter of each hair strand. To determine your hair texture, take a single strand of hair and roll it between your fingers. If you can barely feel the strand, you have fine hair. If you can feel the strand but it's not thick or thin, you have medium hair. If the strand feels thick and sturdy, you have coarse hair.

Step 3: Check Your Hair's Porosity

Hair porosity refers to how well your hair can absorb and retain moisture. To determine your hair porosity, take a strand of hair and place it in a bowl of water. If the hair sinks to the bottom immediately, you have high porosity hair. If the hair floats on the surface, you have low porosity hair. If the hair sinks to the bottom but takes some time to do so, you have normal porosity hair.

Step 4: Determine Your Hair's Elasticity

Hair elasticity refers to how well your hair can stretch and return to its natural state without breaking. To determine your hair's elasticity, take a strand of hair and stretch it gently. If the hair stretches and returns to its original length without breaking, you have normal elasticity hair. If the hair stretches and breaks, you have low elasticity hair. If the hair stretches excessively and takes a long time to return to its natural state, you have high elasticity hair.

Tips for Defining Your Hair Type

  • Take a close-up picture of your hair to better observe the curl pattern and texture.
  • Consult with a hairstylist to help you determine your hair type.
  • Use a hair analysis kit to help you determine your hair type.
  • Be patient and take your time to observe and test your hair.

FAQs

Q: Can my hair type change?

A: Yes, your hair type can change due to factors such as hormones, age, and hair damage.

Q: Is it necessary to know my hair type?

A: Yes, knowing your hair type can help you choose the right products and hair care routine that suits your hair's specific needs.

Hair Care for Different Hair Types

Each hair type requires a different hair care routine and products to keep it healthy and beautiful.

Straight Hair

Straight hair tends to be oily, so it's important to use a gentle shampoo that won't strip away the natural oils. Use a lightweight conditioner to avoid weighing down the hair. Avoid using heavy styling products that can make the hair look greasy.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair needs extra moisture to maintain its curl pattern. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to avoid frizz. Use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to enhance the curl pattern.

Curly Hair

Curly hair needs extra moisture and hydration to prevent frizz and breakage. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week. Use a leave-in conditioner and curl defining cream to enhance the curl pattern.

Coily/Kinky Hair

Coily/kinky hair needs plenty of moisture and hydration to prevent breakage and promote hair growth. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week. Use a leave-in conditioner and hair oil to seal in moisture and prevent hair breakage.

Final Thoughts

Defining your hair type is an important step towards achieving healthy and beautiful hair. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can determine your hair type and choose the right products and hair care routine that suits your hair's specific needs.
