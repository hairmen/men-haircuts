What To Do If Your Hair Starts Falling Out After Bleaching

Written by Bardi Jun 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've recently bleached your hair and noticed that it's starting to fall out, don't panic. Hair loss after bleaching is a common occurrence and can be caused by a variety of factors, including over-processing, using too strong of a bleach, or not properly caring for your hair after the bleaching process. In this article, we'll go over what you can do to stop your hair from falling out and keep it healthy.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step by Step
  • Tips

Description

Hair loss after bleaching can be caused by a variety of factors, including using too strong of a bleach, over-processing the hair, or not properly caring for it after the bleaching process. If you're experiencing hair loss after bleaching, it's important to take action to stop it and prevent further damage to your hair.

How To

The first thing you should do if you're experiencing hair loss after bleaching is to stop any further processing of your hair. This means no more bleach, no more color, no more heat styling, and no more harsh chemicals. Your hair needs time to recover and repair, so give it a break.

Next, it's important to start caring for your hair properly. This means using a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for damaged hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, biotin, and vitamin E, which can help strengthen and nourish your hair.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you stop hair loss and care for your hair after bleaching:

  1. Stop any further processing of your hair.
  2. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for damaged hair.
  3. Avoid heat styling and harsh chemicals.
  4. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and strengthen your hair.
  5. Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.
  6. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead.
  7. Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair, like tight braids or ponytails.
  8. Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals to help nourish your hair from the inside out.
  9. Consider taking a hair supplement that's specifically formulated for hair growth and strength.
  10. Be patient. It can take time for your hair to recover and grow back.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you care for your hair after bleaching:

  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help protect your hair from heat styling and other environmental stressors.
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal parts, as these can snag and break your hair.
  • Wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the sun.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as these can dry out your hair and make it more prone to breakage.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair loss after bleaching:

Q: Can bleach cause permanent hair loss?

A: In some cases, bleach can cause permanent hair loss if it's used incorrectly or if the hair is already damaged. However, in most cases, hair loss after bleaching is temporary and can be reversed with proper care.

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after falling out from bleach?

A: It can take several weeks or even months for hair to grow back after falling out from bleach. The amount of time it takes depends on the severity of the damage and how well you care for your hair during the recovery process.

Q: Can I still dye my hair if it's falling out from bleach?

A: No, it's not recommended to dye your hair if it's falling out from bleach. Your hair needs time to recover and repair, and adding more chemicals to it will only make the problem worse.

Hair Care

Caring for your hair after bleaching is crucial to preventing further damage and promoting healthy hair growth. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can stop hair loss and help your hair recover.

Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and avoid any harsh chemicals or processing until it's fully recovered. With time and care, your hair can grow back healthy and strong.
