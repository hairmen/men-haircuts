How To Cut Thick Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you have thick curly hair, you know that finding the right haircut can be a challenge. It can be difficult to find a stylist who knows how to work with your hair type, and even when you do, it can be hard to communicate exactly what you want. That's why we've put together this step-by-step guide to help you achieve the perfect haircut for your thick curly hair.

How to Cut Thick Curly Hair

Cutting thick curly hair requires a different approach than cutting straight or fine hair. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with dry hair: Curly hair shrinks when it dries, so if you cut it wet, you may end up cutting off more than you intended.
  • Use the right tools: Make sure you have sharp scissors, a wide-tooth comb, and a spray bottle filled with water.
  • Work in small sections: Cutting thick curly hair in large sections can result in unevenness. Work in small sections to ensure a more even cut.
  • Don't be afraid to layer: Layering can help to remove some of the bulk from thick curly hair and make it more manageable.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your thick curly hair:

  1. Start by sectioning your hair: Part your hair down the middle and then divide it into four sections (two in the front and two in the back).
  2. Work on one section at a time: Starting with one of the back sections, comb through it to remove any tangles. Then, take a small section of hair (no more than an inch wide) and twist it tightly. Cut off the ends of the twist, being careful not to cut too much off.
  3. Continue working through the section: Take another small section of hair and twist it tightly. Cut off the ends of the twist, making sure to match the length of the first twist.
  4. Repeat on the other sections: Once you've finished one section, move on to the next until you've cut all of your hair.
  5. Check for any unevenness: Once you've finished cutting your hair, check for any areas that may need additional trimming.

Tips for Cutting Thick Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you get the perfect haircut:

  • Consider getting a dry cut: A dry cut allows you to see exactly how your curls will fall, making it easier to achieve the look you want.
  • Choose the right length: For thick curly hair, a shorter length can help to remove some of the bulk and make your hair more manageable.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment: Curly hair is versatile, so don't be afraid to try out different styles and lengths to find the one that works best for you.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about cutting thick curly hair:

Q: Should I cut my hair wet or dry?
A: It's best to cut thick curly hair when it's dry to avoid cutting off more than you intended. Q: How often should I trim my thick curly hair?
A: It depends on your hair's growth rate, but most people need a trim every 6-8 weeks. Q: Can I cut my own thick curly hair?
A: While it's possible to cut your own hair, it's best to go to a professional stylist who has experience working with curly hair.

Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

Caring for your thick curly hair is just as important as cutting it. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Avoid heat styling: Using heat styling tools can damage your hair and cause it to become frizzy.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment: Curly hair tends to be dry, so use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep it hydrated.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals: Chemical treatments like relaxers and perms can damage your hair, so avoid them if possible.

By following these tips and using the right techniques, you can achieve the perfect haircut for your thick curly hair. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
