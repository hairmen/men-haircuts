What Clothing Was Popular In The 60S

Written by William Jun 22, 2023 · 5 min read

Welcome to our guide on the popular clothing styles of the 60s. This era was defined by its political and social changes, which greatly influenced the fashion trends of the time. In this article, we will take you through the popular clothing items, styles, and accessories of the 60s. We will also provide some tips on how to incorporate these styles into your wardrobe today.

What Were the Popular Clothing Items in the 60s?

The 60s was a time of experimentation and self-expression, and this was reflected in the clothing styles of the time. Some of the popular clothing items included:

1. Mini Skirts

The mini skirt was one of the most iconic fashion items of the 60s. It was a symbol of the liberation and empowerment of women during this era. The mini skirt was usually paired with knee-high boots, and it was a staple item in every young woman's wardrobe. Today, you can still rock a mini skirt with leggings or tights to keep the look modern.

2. Bell Bottoms

Bell bottoms were another popular clothing item in the 60s. They were wide-legged pants that flared out from the knee. Bell bottoms were often worn with platform shoes and a colorful top. Today, you can still incorporate bell bottoms into your wardrobe by pairing them with a crop top and sandals.

3. Tie-Dye

Tie-dye was a popular clothing trend in the 60s, and it was often associated with the hippie culture of the time. Tie-dye was used to create colorful and bold patterns on t-shirts, dresses, and skirts. Today, you can still rock a tie-dye shirt or dress with denim shorts and sandals.

4. Shift Dresses

The shift dress was a simple and elegant dress that was popular in the 60s. It was a loose-fitting dress that was often made from a lightweight fabric. The shift dress was often paired with knee-high boots or ballet flats. Today, you can still wear a shift dress with a pair of ankle boots and a denim jacket for a modern look.

What Were the Popular Clothing Styles in the 60s?

The 60s was a time of freedom and experimentation, and this was reflected in the clothing styles of the time. Some of the popular clothing styles included:

1. Mod Style

The mod style was a fashion trend that originated in London in the early 60s. It was characterized by bold colors, geometric patterns, and simple silhouettes. The mod style was often associated with the music and youth culture of the time.

2. Hippie Style

The hippie style was a fashion trend that originated in San Francisco in the mid-60s. It was characterized by tie-dye, bell bottoms, and colorful patterns. The hippie style was often associated with the anti-war movement and the counterculture of the time.

3. Preppy Style

The preppy style was a fashion trend that originated in the Ivy League colleges of the 60s. It was characterized by classic and conservative clothing items such as blazers, cardigans, and polo shirts. The preppy style was often associated with the upper class and the traditional values of the time.

How to Incorporate 60s Fashion into Your Wardrobe Today

If you want to incorporate 60s fashion into your wardrobe today, here are some tips:

1. Choose Bold Patterns and Colors

The 60s was all about bold patterns and colors, so choose clothing items that have a retro feel. Look for items with geometric patterns, tie-dye, and bright colors.

2. Experiment with Different Silhouettes

The 60s was a time of experimentation, so don't be afraid to try different silhouettes. Try a mini skirt with knee-high boots or a shift dress with ankle boots.

3. Accessorize with Bold Jewelry

Jewelry was an important accessory in the 60s, so don't be afraid to add some bold jewelry to your outfit. Look for statement necklaces, large earrings, and chunky bracelets.

Hair Care

The 60s was a time of experimentation with hairstyles as well as fashion. Here are some popular hairstyles of the 60s:

1. The Beehive

The beehive was a popular hairstyle for women in the 60s. It was a high, voluminous hairstyle that was created by backcombing the hair and shaping it into a beehive shape.

2. The Bouffant

The bouffant was a popular hairstyle for women in the 60s. It was a high, voluminous hairstyle that was created by teasing the hair and shaping it into a rounded shape.

3. The Shag

The shag was a popular hairstyle for men and women in the 60s. It was a layered hairstyle that was often styled with bangs and lots of volume.

In conclusion, the 60s was a time of experimentation and self-expression, and this was reflected in the fashion trends of the time. Today, you can still incorporate 60s fashion into your wardrobe by choosing bold patterns and colors, experimenting with different silhouettes, and accessorizing with bold jewelry.
