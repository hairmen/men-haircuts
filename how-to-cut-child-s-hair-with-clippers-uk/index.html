How To Cut Child s Hair With Clippers Uk

Written by Frank Jun 05, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to cut your child's hair with clippers in the UK. As parents, we understand the importance of saving money and time by giving your child a haircut at home. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home. We have provided a step-by-step guide, tips, and FAQs to help you through the process.

Table of Contents:

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQs

How to Cut Your Child's Hair with Clippers UK

Before you start, it is essential to have the right tools. You will need:

  • Clippers with different guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Cape or towel to cover your child's clothes
  • Clip or hair tie to section off hair

Once you have all the tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Prepare Your Child

Make sure your child is comfortable and in a good mood. It is best to cut their hair after a bath or shower when their hair is clean and easy to comb. Cover them with a cape or towel to keep hair off their clothes. If they are old enough, distract them with a movie or toy.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Use a clip or hair tie to section off the hair you want to cut. Start with the sides and back, leaving the top for last. Use a comb to make sure the hair is even and straight.

Step 3: Choose the Right Guard

Choose the right guard for the length you want to cut. Start with a longer guard and work your way down if needed. It is better to start longer and go shorter than to cut too much off at once.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Turn the clippers on and start cutting from the bottom up. Move the clippers in an upward motion against the direction of hair growth. Use the comb to guide the clippers and make sure the hair is even.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you have finished cutting with the first guard, switch to a shorter guard and blend the hair by using an upward motion. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs and make sure the hair is even.

Step 6: Cut the Top

Use scissors to cut the top, starting from the front and working your way back. Use the comb to make sure the hair is even and straight. Cut off small amounts at a time until you achieve the desired length.

Step 7: Finish the Haircut

Use scissors to trim around the ears and neck. Use the clippers without a guard to trim the hairline. Remove the cape or towel and brush the hair to remove any loose hairs.

Tips for Cutting Your Child's Hair with Clippers UK

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down if needed
  • Cut small amounts at a time to avoid cutting too much off
  • Make sure the hair is dry and clean before cutting
  • Use scissors to trim any stray hairs
  • Have patience and take breaks if needed

FAQs

How often should I cut my child's hair?

It depends on the length and style you want. If you want a short haircut, you may need to cut it every 4-6 weeks. If you want a longer style, you may be able to wait 6-8 weeks.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go shorter or use scissors to blend the hair. If you are unsure, it is best to leave it and ask a professional for help.

Can I use the same clippers for my child and myself?

It is not recommended to use the same clippers for different people. It can spread germs and bacteria. It is best to have separate clippers for each person.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Child's Hair with Clippers UK

After cutting your child's hair, make sure to wash their hair to remove any loose hairs. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep their hair healthy. Use a comb to detangle their hair and style as desired.

We hope this guide has helped you learn how to cut your child's hair with clippers in the UK. With the right tools and techniques, you can save time and money by giving your child a professional-looking haircut at home. Remember to have patience and take breaks if needed. Happy cutting!
