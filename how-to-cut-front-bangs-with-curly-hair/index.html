How To Cut Front Bangs With Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jun 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how challenging it can be to style it. Curly hair often has a mind of its own, and it can be difficult to get it to cooperate. If you're thinking about cutting front bangs with curly hair, you might be feeling a bit intimidated. But don't worry! With the right technique and some patience, you can achieve the perfect front bangs for your curly hair.

How to Cut Front Bangs with Curly Hair

Cutting front bangs with curly hair is a bit different than cutting them with straight hair. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Make sure your hair is dry and styled the way you normally wear it. This will give you a better idea of how the bangs will look with your hair.
  • Consider the texture of your hair. Curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries, so your bangs may end up shorter than you intended if you cut them while your hair is wet.
  • Start with a small section of hair. You can always cut more if you need to, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Take your time. Cutting bangs can be nerve-wracking, but rushing can lead to mistakes.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut front bangs with curly hair:

  1. Part your hair in the middle or on the side, depending on how you normally wear it.
  2. Take a small section of hair from the front of your head. This section should be about as wide as your forehead.
  3. Hold the section of hair between your index and middle fingers. Make sure it's taut but not too tight.
  4. Using your other hand, hold the scissors vertically and snip into the hair. This will create a textured look.
  5. Continue snipping into the hair until you've reached your desired length. Remember, you can always cut more if you need to.
  6. Repeat on the other side.
  7. Check to make sure both sides are even. If they're not, adjust as needed.

Tips for Cutting Front Bangs with Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you get the perfect front bangs for your curly hair:

  • If you're nervous about cutting your own hair, consider going to a professional stylist. They can help you achieve the look you want without the stress of doing it yourself.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before cutting. This will make it easier to work with.
  • Don't cut too much off at once. It's better to take your time and cut a little bit at a time than to make a big mistake.
  • If you're not sure how short to cut your bangs, start by cutting them longer than you think you want. You can always go back and cut more later.

FAQs

Can I cut my bangs while my hair is wet?

You can, but keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries. If you cut your bangs while your hair is wet, they may end up shorter than you intended.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! If you make a mistake, you can always go back and fix it. If you're not comfortable fixing it yourself, consider going to a professional stylist.

How often do I need to trim my bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to look overgrown. As a general rule, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires a bit of extra care to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry. This can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Avoid using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons. They can damage your hair and make it look dry and brittle.
  • Consider using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to help define your curls.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve the perfect front bangs for your curly hair. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
