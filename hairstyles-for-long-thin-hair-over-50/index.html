Hairstyles For Long Thin Hair Over 50 Tips How Tos And Care

Written by Frank Jun 14, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re over 50 and have long thin hair, you may think that there aren’t many hairstyles that will suit you. But that’s not true! There are plenty of options for women with long thin hair over 50. In this article, we’ve put together a list of hairstyles, tips, how-tos, and hair care advice to help you feel confident and stylish.

Hairstyles for Long Thin Hair Over 50

Here are some of the best hairstyles for long thin hair over 50:

1. Layered Haircut

A layered haircut is a great option for long thin hair as it adds volume and texture. Layers can be cut at different lengths to create movement and body. You can also add some side-swept bangs to frame your face.

2. Bob Haircut

A bob haircut is another great option for long thin hair as it can make your hair look fuller and thicker. A chin-length bob with some layers can add volume and texture.

3. Beachy Waves

Beachy waves can give your long thin hair a fuller and more voluminous look. You can achieve this look by using a curling iron or hot rollers. Use a texturizing spray to add some volume and hold.

4. Ponytail

A high or low ponytail is a simple and easy hairstyle for long thin hair. You can add some volume to your ponytail by teasing the crown of your head. You can also wrap a small section of hair around the hair tie to hide it.

5. Half Up Half Down

A half up half down hairstyle is a great way to add some volume and texture to your long thin hair. You can tease the crown of your head and pull back the top section of your hair, leaving the rest down.

How to Style Long Thin Hair Over 50

Here are some tips for styling long thin hair over 50:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add volume and thickness to your hair.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to add volume at the roots.
  • Use a round brush when blow drying to add some curl and texture.
  • Use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add some volume and hold.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Long Thin Hair Over 50

Here’s a step-by-step guide to styling long thin hair over 50:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots.
  3. Blow dry your hair upside down using a round brush.
  4. Use a curling iron or hot rollers to add some texture and volume.
  5. Finish with a texturizing spray or dry shampoo.

Tips for Maintaining Long Thin Hair Over 50

Here are some tips for maintaining long thin hair over 50:

  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or hair dye.

FAQs About Long Thin Hair Over 50

What is the best haircut for long thin hair over 50?

The best haircut for long thin hair over 50 is a layered haircut or a bob haircut.

How can I add volume to my long thin hair?

You can add volume to your long thin hair by using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, blow drying your hair upside down, and using a texturizing spray.

Is it okay to use heat styling tools on long thin hair over 50?

It’s okay to use heat styling tools on long thin hair over 50, but it’s important to use them in moderation and use a heat protectant spray.

Hair Care for Long Thin Hair Over 50

Here are some tips for taking care of long thin hair over 50:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won’t weigh down your hair.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair as it can dry out your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Avoid pulling your hair back tightly as it can cause breakage.
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal parts to prevent damage.

With these hairstyles, tips, how-tos, and hair care advice, you can feel confident and stylish with long thin hair over 50. Try out different hairstyles and techniques to find what works best for you and your hair type. Don’t be afraid to experiment and have fun with your hair!
