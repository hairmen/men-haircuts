Starting Dreadlocks With Long Hair A Complete Guide

Written by William Jun 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering starting dreadlocks with your long hair but don't know where to begin? Look no further than this comprehensive guide on how to start your dreadlock journey with ease. From the initial steps to proper hair care, we've got you covered.

Description

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries, and its popularity continues to grow. As more people become interested in starting their dreadlock journey, it's important to understand the process, especially if you have long hair. This article will provide a step-by-step guide on how to start your dreadlocks with long hair, including tips and hair care advice to keep your locs healthy.

How to Start Your Dreadlocks

Starting your dreadlocks with long hair requires patience and dedication. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Determine Your Dreadlock Size

The first step in starting your dreadlocks is to determine the size you want. Small dreads will take longer to form but will result in a more defined and textured look. Large dreads, on the other hand, will form quicker but may result in a less structured appearance. Choose a size that is comfortable for you and your lifestyle.

Step 2: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before starting your dreadlocks, it's important to wash and condition your hair. This will remove any product buildup or dirt and make your hair easier to work with. Use a clarifying shampoo and a deep conditioner to ensure your hair is clean and moisturized.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is essential in starting your dreadlocks. Use a rattail comb to create even sections throughout your hair. The size of the sections will depend on the size of your desired dreadlocks. Use hair ties to secure each section and make the process easier.

Step 4: Twist and Backcomb Each Section

Once you have created sections, it's time to start forming your dreadlocks. Begin by twisting and backcombing each section. This will create the foundation for your dreadlocks. Repeat this step until all sections are twisted and backcombed.

Step 5: Maintain Your Dreadlocks

Maintaining your dreadlocks is essential in keeping them healthy and looking great. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using products that contain wax or oils. You can also palm roll your dreadlocks to keep them tight and smooth.

Tips for Starting Your Dreadlocks

Starting your dreadlocks with long hair can be challenging, but these tips will make the process easier:

  • Be patient. It takes time for dreadlocks to form, especially if you have long hair.
  • Avoid using products that contain wax or oils. These can cause buildup and damage your hair.
  • Use a residue-free shampoo to keep your dreadlocks clean and healthy.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can cause your dreadlocks to unravel.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent breakage and keep your hair healthy.

FAQs

Can I start my dreadlocks with long hair?

Yes, you can start your dreadlocks with long hair. It may take longer for your dreadlocks to form, but the end result will be worth it.

How often should I wash my dreadlocks?

You should wash your dreadlocks once a week or every two weeks. Washing too often can cause your dreadlocks to unravel.

Can I dye my dreadlocks?

Yes, you can dye your dreadlocks. However, it's important to use a dye that is safe for dreadlocks and avoid using bleach, which can damage your hair.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Proper hair care is essential in keeping your dreadlocks healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to keep your dreadlocks clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using products that contain wax or oils, as these can cause buildup and damage your hair.
  • Palm roll your dreadlocks to keep them tight and smooth.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and cause breakage.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent breakage and keep your hair healthy.

Starting your dreadlocks with long hair may seem daunting, but with patience, dedication, and proper hair care, your locs will look amazing. Follow these steps and tips, and you'll be well on your way to having the dreadlocks you've always wanted.
