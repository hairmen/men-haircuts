How To Change Men s Hairstyle A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Changing your hairstyle is a great way to switch up your look and boost your confidence. However, some men may feel intimidated by the idea of changing their hairstyle. Fear not, as this guide will take you through the process step by step. Whether you want to switch up your look entirely or just make a few subtle changes, this guide has got you covered.

How to Change Your Hairstyle

Changing your hairstyle can be a daunting task, especially if you have been sporting the same look for years. Here is a step-by-step guide to help you change your hairstyle.

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

Knowing your face shape is crucial when choosing a new hairstyle, as it can help you determine which styles will suit you best. Here are the different face shapes and the hairstyles that work well for each one.

  • Oval: Most hairstyles work well for an oval face shape, but a side-swept fringe or slicked-back look can accentuate your features.
  • Round: A longer hairstyle with layers can help elongate a round face shape.
  • Square: Shorter, textured hairstyles can complement a square face shape.
  • Heart: A layered, textured hairstyle can soften a heart-shaped face.
  • Diamond: A short, textured cut can accentuate the cheekbones of a diamond face shape.

Step 2: Gather Inspiration

Before heading to the salon, it's a good idea to gather some inspiration for your new hairstyle. Look online for pictures of hairstyles that you like and think would suit your face shape. Don't be afraid to save multiple pictures, as your hairstylist can help you determine which elements of each style will work best for you.

Step 3: Find a Hairstylist

When changing your hairstyle, it's important to find a hairstylist that you trust. Look for reviews online or ask friends and family for recommendations. Once you find a hairstylist, schedule a consultation to discuss your desired look and determine the best approach.

Step 4: Experiment with Products

After you've changed your hairstyle, it's important to experiment with different products to find what works best for you. Consider using pomade, wax, or gel to style your new do.

Tips for Changing Your Hairstyle

Here are some tips to keep in mind when changing your hairstyle.

  • Be open to suggestions from your hairstylist.
  • Start with a subtle change if you're feeling nervous.
  • Consider your lifestyle and maintenance preferences when choosing a new style.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for styling tips from your hairstylist.
  • Keep in mind that it may take some time to get used to your new hairstyle.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I change my hairstyle?

There's no set rule for how often you should change your hairstyle. It's entirely up to you and your personal preference. Some people prefer to switch up their look every few months, while others stick with the same style for years.

How can I maintain my new hairstyle?

To maintain your new hairstyle, it's important to follow a good hair care routine. Use products that are suited for your hair type and style, and make sure to wash and condition your hair regularly. Consider using a heat protectant when styling your hair with heat tools, and get regular trims to keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care

Changing your hairstyle can also mean changing your hair care routine. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair.

  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or relaxers.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner for added hydration.

Changing your hairstyle can be a fun and exciting experience. By following these steps and tips, you can ensure that your new look suits you and is easy to maintain. Remember to be open to suggestions from your hairstylist, and don't be afraid to experiment with different products and styles.
