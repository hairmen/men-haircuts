Description

The 1950s was a decade of glamour and sophistication, and the hairstyles of the era perfectly encapsulated this. From the iconic beehive to the classic bob, 50s hair was all about volume, curls, and elegant shapes. If you want to add a touch of vintage glamour to your look, learning how to 50s hair is a must.

How To

Getting the perfect 50s hairstyle requires some practice and the right tools. Here are the basic steps:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

Wash and condition your hair as you normally would, and let it air dry or use a blow dryer. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start styling.

2. Use some styling products

Apply a mousse or a volumizing spray to your roots to add some lift and hold. You can also use a heat protectant spray if you plan to use hot tools.

3. Choose your hairstyle

There are many 50s hairstyles to choose from, so pick one that suits your hair length and texture. Some popular options include the beehive, the bouffant, the pompadour, and the short bob.

4. Get the right tools

You'll need a few tools to create your 50s hairstyle, including a teasing comb, bobby pins, hair clips, a hair dryer, and hot rollers or a curling iron.

5. Start styling

Follow the step-by-step instructions below to create your chosen 50s hairstyle.

Step By Step

Beehive

The beehive is one of the most iconic 50s hairstyles, and it's surprisingly easy to create:

  1. Part your hair to one side, and backcomb the top section of your hair.
  2. Smooth the top section with a brush, and secure it with bobby pins at the back of your head.
  3. Tease the sides of your hair, and pin them back to create a rounded shape.
  4. Smooth the sides with a brush, and spray with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Bouffant

The bouffant is another classic 50s hairstyle that's perfect for special occasions:

  1. Part your hair to one side, and backcomb the crown section of your hair.
  2. Smooth the top section with a brush, and pin it back at the crown of your head.
  3. Tease the sides of your hair, and pin them back to create a rounded shape.
  4. Curl the ends of your hair under with hot rollers or a curling iron.
  5. Spray with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Pompadour

The pompadour is a bold and stylish 50s hairstyle that's perfect for short hair:

  1. Apply a styling product to your hair, and blow dry it back and to one side.
  2. Tease the top section of your hair, and pin it back at the crown of your head.
  3. Smooth the sides of your hair back, and pin them at the back of your head.
  4. Curl the ends of your hair under with a curling iron.
  5. Spray with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Short Bob

The short bob is a classic 50s hairstyle that's still popular today:

  1. Part your hair to one side, and blow dry it straight with a round brush.
  2. Curl the ends of your hair under with a curling iron.
  3. Pin back the shorter side of your hair with a hair clip or bobby pin.
  4. Spray with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your 50s hairstyle:

  • Use hot rollers or a curling iron to create soft, bouncy curls.
  • Tease your hair gently to avoid damaging it.
  • Use bobby pins and hair clips that match your hair color to keep your hairstyle in place.
  • Finish with a light mist of hairspray to hold the style in place.

FAQ

Can I create a 50s hairstyle with short hair?

Yes, you can! The pompadour and the short bob are both great options for short hair.

Do I need to use hot rollers to create a 50s hairstyle?

No, you don't. You can also use a curling iron or even foam rollers to create curls.

How long does a 50s hairstyle last?

With the right products and techniques, a 50s hairstyle can last all day or even longer.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and strong, make sure to:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using too many styling products that can weigh down your hair.

Now that you know how to 50s hair, you can add a touch of vintage glamour to your look any time you want.
