How To Cut Thin Curly Hair A Guide For Relaxed Hair

Written by William Nov 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

For those with thin curly hair, it can be a challenge to find the right haircut that flatters your face shape and enhances your natural curls. In this guide, we'll go over how to cut thin curly hair to achieve the perfect look. From how to section the hair to the best tools to use, we'll cover everything you need to know.

Description

Thin curly hair can be tricky to cut because the curls can easily lose their shape if not handled properly. It's important to approach cutting thin curly hair with care and attention to detail to ensure a great result. This guide will provide tips and tricks to help you achieve a beautiful haircut that enhances your natural curls.

How to Cut Thin Curly Hair

Step 1: Section the Hair

The first step in cutting thin curly hair is to section the hair. This will help you to see each section clearly and ensure that you cut each section evenly. Use hair clips to separate the hair into manageable sections.

Step 2: Choose the Right Tools

When cutting thin curly hair, it's important to use the right tools. Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair before cutting. You may also consider using thinning shears to remove some of the bulk and create a more even look.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Start by cutting the hair at the nape of the neck and work your way up. Use a comb to guide the hair as you cut and be sure to cut each section evenly. Take your time and work slowly to avoid making any mistakes.

Step 4: Check the Length

After you've finished cutting the hair, check the length to make sure it's even. You may need to make some minor adjustments to ensure that the hair is the desired length.

Tips for Cutting Thin Curly Hair

Cutting thin curly hair can be challenging, but these tips can help you achieve a great result:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends
  • Detangle the hair before cutting to ensure an even cut
  • Consider using thinning shears to remove some of the bulk
  • Take your time and work slowly to avoid making any mistakes

FAQs

Can I cut my own thin curly hair?

While it's possible to cut your own hair, it's best to leave it to a professional if you're not experienced in cutting hair. A professional stylist will have the tools and expertise to create a great haircut that flatters your face shape and enhances your natural curls.

How often should I cut my thin curly hair?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Thin Curly Hair

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, beautiful thin curly hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils
  • Use a deep conditioner to keep the hair hydrated and nourished
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage to the hair
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying to enhance your natural curls
  • Avoid brushing the hair when it's dry to prevent frizz

Cutting thin curly hair can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great result. Remember to take your time and work slowly to avoid making any mistakes. And don't forget to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and beautiful.
