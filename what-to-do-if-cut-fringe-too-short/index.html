What To Do If Cut Fringe Too Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Accidents happen, and one of the most common mishaps in hair care is cutting your fringe too short. It can be a frustrating experience, especially if you're not used to styling your hair or don't have the time or money to fix it at a salon. Fortunately, there are ways to salvage your hair and still look great. In this post, we'll walk you through what to do if you cut your fringe too short and provide some tips on how to avoid it in the future.

Description: Understanding Your Fringe

Before we dive into the steps, let's first understand what a fringe is. A fringe, also known as bangs, is a section of hair that falls over your forehead. It's a popular hairstyle that can frame your face, highlight your eyes, or give you a youthful appearance. However, unlike other parts of your hair, your fringe is more visible and can affect your overall look. That's why cutting your fringe too short can be nerve-racking.

How to: Fixing Your Fringe

If you've cut your fringe too short, don't panic. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Assess the Damage

Take a deep breath and look at your hair in the mirror. How short is it? Is it uneven? Is it too thin or sparse? Don't touch it yet or try to style it. Just observe and analyze your hair.

Step 2: Trim the Edges

If your fringe is uneven or has stray hairs, you can trim the edges with a pair of hair scissors. Make sure to use sharp and clean scissors, so you don't damage your hair or create more split ends. Start with small snips and work your way slowly. Don't cut too much at once, or you might end up with a shorter fringe. You can also use a razor comb to feather the ends or create a softer look.

Step 3: Create Volume

If your fringe is too thin or sparse, you can create volume by teasing it. Use a fine-tooth comb or a teasing brush to backcomb your hair gently. Start at the roots and work your way to the tips. Don't tease too much or too aggressively, or you might damage your hair or create knots. You can also use a volumizing powder or a dry shampoo to add texture and body to your hair.

Step 4: Style Your Fringe

Once you've trimmed and teased your hair, you can now style your fringe. You can part it in the middle, on the side, or sweep it to one side. You can also clip it with bobby pins, a hairband, or a hair accessory. Experiment with different styles and see what works best for you. Don't worry if your fringe is still short or imperfect. Own it and embrace your unique look.

Tips: Preventing Fringe Mishaps

Now that you've fixed your fringe, you might be wondering how to avoid cutting it too short in the future. Here are some tips:

  • Use sharp and clean scissors
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet or damp
  • Start with small snips and work your way slowly
  • Don't cut your hair in a rush or when you're emotional
  • Follow a tutorial or ask for help if you're unsure
  • Learn how to style your hair before cutting it

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does it take for a fringe to grow back?
A: It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month. So, if you've cut your fringe too short, it might take a few weeks or months to grow back to its original length.

Q: Can I wear a wig or a hairpiece if I don't like my fringe?
A: Yes, you can. Wigs and hairpieces are a great way to change your look without damaging your hair or waiting for it to grow back. Just make sure to choose a wig or a hairpiece that matches your hair color and texture and fits comfortably.

Hair Care: Taking Care of Your Hair

Finally, remember to take care of your hair. Cutting your fringe too short can be stressful, but it's not the end of the world. With some patience, creativity, and love, you can fix your hair and feel confident again. Also, make sure to nourish your hair with a healthy diet, regular washing, and conditioning, and protecting it from heat and chemicals. Your hair is your crown, so wear it proudly.
