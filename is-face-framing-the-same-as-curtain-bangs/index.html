Is Face Framing The Same As Curtain Bangs

Written by Oliver Jul 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

When it comes to haircuts, there are so many different styles to choose from. Face framing and curtain bangs are two popular styles that many people confuse with each other. While they may look similar at first glance, there are some key differences between the two. In this article, we'll explore the question, "Is face framing the same as curtain bangs?" and provide some tips on how to achieve each style.

Description

Face framing is a haircut technique where the stylist cuts layers around the face to create a framing effect. This can be done on any length of hair and can be customized to suit the individual's face shape and hair texture. The goal of face framing is to draw attention to the face and highlight its features.

Curtain bangs, on the other hand, are a specific type of bang that are parted down the middle and swept to each side, creating a "curtain" effect. Curtain bangs are typically longer than traditional bangs and blend seamlessly into the rest of the hair. They're a great option for those who want to add some framing around their face without committing to a full fringe.

How to Achieve Face Framing

If you're interested in getting a face framing haircut, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, it's important to choose a stylist who has experience with this technique. They will be able to tailor the cut to your specific face shape and hair texture.

During the haircut, the stylist will typically start by sectioning off the hair around the face. They'll then cut layers that frame the face and blend seamlessly into the rest of the hair. The result is a soft, flattering look that draws attention to your best features.

Tips:

  • Be sure to communicate with your stylist about the specific look you're going for
  • Consider bringing in pictures of hairstyles you like to give your stylist a better idea of what you want
  • Remember that face framing can work on any length of hair, from short pixie cuts to long, flowing locks

How to Achieve Curtain Bangs

If you're interested in trying out curtain bangs, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, it's important to choose a length that works for your face shape and hair type. Longer bangs can be easier to style and may be a better option for those with wavy or curly hair.

To achieve the curtain bang look, the stylist will typically start by sectioning off the hair at the front of the head. They'll then cut a long, wispy fringe that's parted down the middle. The bangs should be blended seamlessly into the rest of the hair for a natural, effortless look.

Tips:

  • Consider using a round brush and blow dryer to style your curtain bangs
  • Experiment with different parting styles to find the one that works best for you
  • Use a small amount of hair oil or serum to keep your bangs looking shiny and healthy

FAQ

Q: Are face framing and curtain bangs the same thing?

A: No, they're not the same thing. Face framing is a technique where layers are cut around the face to highlight its features, while curtain bangs are a specific type of bang that are parted down the middle and swept to each side.

Q: Can I have both face framing and curtain bangs?

A: Yes, you can definitely have both! Talk to your stylist about incorporating both techniques into your next haircut.

Q: Will face framing or curtain bangs work on my hair type?

A: Both techniques can work on any hair type, from straight to curly. It's important to choose a length and style that works best for your individual hair type and face shape.

Hair Care Tips

Whether you opt for face framing or curtain bangs, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils
  • Avoid using heat tools on a daily basis, and always use a heat protectant before styling
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair looking its best
  • Experiment with different hair products to find the ones that work best for your hair type

In conclusion, while face framing and curtain bangs may look similar at first glance, they're two distinct techniques that can be customized to suit your individual style. Whether you opt for face framing or curtain bangs, be sure to choose a stylist who has experience with these techniques and take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
