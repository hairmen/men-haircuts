How To Cut Men s Short Layered Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 03, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you looking to give your male clients a fresh new look? If so, then learning how to cut men's short layered hair is a great skill to have in your arsenal. Layered haircuts are versatile, easy to maintain, and can work for a variety of hair types and face shapes.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step-by-Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

A layered haircut involves cutting the hair at different lengths to create texture and volume. Men's short layered haircuts can range from subtle layers to more dramatic styles, depending on the client's preference. Layered haircuts can also work for a variety of hair types, including straight, wavy, or curly hair.

How To

To cut men's short layered hair, you'll need a few tools, including:

  • Scissors
  • Clipper
  • Comb
  • Haircut Cape
  • Spray Bottle

Before you begin cutting, make sure your client's hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can make it difficult to see the natural shape and texture of the hair.

Step-by-Step

Step 1: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair into three parts: the top, the sides, and the back. Use clips to keep the hair in place while you work on each section.

Step 2: Cut the Sides and Back

Using a clipper, start at the bottom of the sides and work your way up. Use a comb to lift the hair as you go and cut the hair at an angle to create a layered look. Repeat this process on the back of the head.

Step 3: Cut the Top

Starting at the front of the head, comb the hair straight up and cut the hair at an angle to create layers. Work your way back to the crown of the head, making sure to blend the layers with the sides and back of the head.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once all the sections have been cut, use the scissors to blend the layers together. This will create a seamless transition between the different lengths of hair.

Step 5: Finish the Cut

Use the clipper to trim the hairline around the ears and the back of the neck. Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs and create a clean, polished look.

Tips

  • Start with longer layers and work your way shorter to avoid cutting off too much hair.
  • Use a mirror to check the haircut from different angles.
  • Ask your client for feedback throughout the cutting process.
  • Use thinning shears to remove bulk and create a more natural look.

FAQ

How often should men get their hair cut?

Most men should get their hair cut every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and style of the haircut.

Can layered haircuts work for all hair types?

Yes, layered haircuts can work for all hair types, including straight, wavy, and curly hair. The key is to adjust the length and angle of the layers to suit the natural texture of the hair.

Hair Care

After the haircut, recommend that your client use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to maintain the health and shine of their hair. Regular trims will also help to keep the haircut looking fresh and polished.

Learning how to cut men's short layered hair can be a valuable skill for any hairstylist. With the right tools and techniques, you can create a variety of looks to suit your clients' individual style and preferences.
