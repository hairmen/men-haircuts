How To Cut Your Own Curly Hair In Short Layers

Written by Petter Aug 25, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the salon every time you need a haircut? Do you have curly hair and struggle to find a stylist who can give you the perfect cut? Look no further! In this article, we will give you step-by-step instructions on how to cut your own curly hair in short layers.

Description

Short layers are a great way to add volume and shape to curly hair. This cut is perfect for those who want to keep their curls looking bouncy and full, while also adding some definition. With a little practice and patience, you can easily achieve this look from the comfort of your own home.

How to

Before you begin cutting your hair, make sure that your hair is clean and dry. It’s also a good idea to have all the necessary tools on hand, such as sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips.

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 2: Cut the Bottom Layer

Take one of the back sections and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across. Repeat this step for the other back section.

Step 3: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the top two sections of your hair and comb them straight down. Pin the top layer out of the way with a hair clip. Take the middle layer and cut it straight across, using the bottom layer as a guide. Repeat this step for the other middle section.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layer

Release the top layer of your hair and comb it straight down. Use the middle layer as a guide and cut the top layer straight across. This will create the shortest layer of your cut.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Use your scissors to blend the layers together. Take small sections of hair and cut into them at a diagonal angle. This will create a seamless transition between the layers.

Tips

- Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always take more off, but you can’t put it back on.

- Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet. Curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so it’s best to cut it when it’s dry.

- Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, it’s recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Q: Can I cut my curly hair myself?

A: Yes, you can! Just make sure to follow the steps carefully and take your time.

Hair Care

After you’ve cut your hair, it’s important to take good care of it to maintain its shape and health. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and deep condition your hair once a week. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to avoid frizz.

Cutting your own curly hair in short layers may seem intimidating at first, but with these step-by-step instructions and tips, you’ll be able to achieve the perfect cut in no time. Just remember to take your time, start with small cuts, and practice, practice, practice!
