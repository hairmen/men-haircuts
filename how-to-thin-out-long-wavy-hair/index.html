How To Thin Out Long Wavy Hair Step By Step Guide

Description

Long wavy hair can be absolutely gorgeous, but it can also be a handful to manage. One of the biggest issues that people with this type of hair face is that it can get very thick and heavy, making it difficult to style or manage. This is where thinning comes in – by removing some of the bulk, you can make your hair more manageable and easier to style. In this article, we'll show you how to thin out long wavy hair in a relaxed, easy-to-understand way.

How to Thin Out Long Wavy Hair

Thinning out long wavy hair can be done in a few different ways. One of the most popular methods is by using thinning shears, which are specially designed scissors that can remove some of the bulk without completely cutting off the hair. Another method is by using a razor, which can create a more feathered look. Both methods can be effective, so it's really up to you to decide which one you prefer.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for thinning out long wavy hair:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a good quality shampoo and conditioner. This will help to detangle your hair and make it easier to work with.
  2. Towel dry your hair and comb it out to remove any tangles.
  3. Section off your hair into manageable sections using hair clips.
  4. Start with the bottom section of your hair and use your thinning shears or razor to remove some of the bulk. Make sure to only cut a little bit at a time, as you can always go back and thin more if needed.
  5. Work your way up to the top sections of your hair, thinning as you go.
  6. Once you've finished thinning your hair, wash it again to remove any loose hairs.
  7. If you want to add some more texture, you can use a texturizing spray or mousse to create a more tousled look.

Tips to Keep in Mind

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out long wavy hair:

  • Use sharp scissors or a razor to avoid damaging your hair
  • Don't cut too much at once, as it can be difficult to fix mistakes
  • Thinning your hair can make it more prone to breakage, so be sure to use a good quality conditioner to keep it healthy
  • If you're not confident in your ability to thin your own hair, it's always best to go to a professional stylist

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning out long wavy hair:

How often should I thin out my hair?

It really depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick it is. Some people may need to thin their hair out every few months, while others may only need to do it once or twice a year.

Will thinning my hair make it look thinner overall?

Thinning your hair can remove some of the bulk, but it won't necessarily make it look thinner overall. In fact, it can actually make your hair look more voluminous and textured.

Can I thin out my hair if it's already damaged?

If your hair is already damaged, it's best to wait until it's healthy again before thinning it out. Thinning can make damaged hair more prone to breakage, so it's important to keep it as healthy as possible.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips to keep your long wavy hair healthy and beautiful:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause damage to your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner
  • Get regular trims to keep split ends at bay

Thinning out long wavy hair can be a great way to make it more manageable and easy to style. Just remember to take it slow and be careful not to cut too much at once. With a little bit of practice, you'll be able to achieve the perfect balance of volume and texture.
