How To Cut Your Hair With Thinning Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 02, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're looking for a way to add volume and texture to your hair, using thinning scissors is a great option. Thinning scissors are designed to remove some of the hair's thickness without altering the overall length. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut your hair with thinning scissors.

How to Cut Your Hair with Thinning Scissors

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of thinning scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural texture of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use hair clips to section your hair into manageable pieces. Start by clipping the top section of your hair away from the bottom section.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Comb each section of your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will help you see the natural texture of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 4: Cut Your Hair

Hold the thinning scissors with the teeth pointing down and the regular scissors in the other hand. Use the regular scissors to trim any split ends or damaged hair. Then, use the thinning scissors to cut into the hair in a zigzag motion.

Start at the bottom of the hair section and work your way up. Take small sections of hair and cut into them in a zigzag motion. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once.

Step 5: Blend Your Hair

After you've cut all the sections of your hair, blend them together using your fingers. This will create a natural, textured look.

Step 6: Finish Your Hair

Style your hair as desired. You can blow-dry your hair for extra volume or use a texturizing spray to enhance the texture.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair with Thinning Scissors

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your hair with thinning scissors:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time
  • Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up
  • Blend your hair after cutting to create a natural look

FAQs about Cutting Your Hair with Thinning Scissors

Can you use thinning scissors on any hair type?

Yes, thinning scissors can be used on any hair type. However, it's important to use the right technique and avoid cutting too much hair at once to prevent damage.

How often should you cut your hair with thinning scissors?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair healthy, make sure to:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions
  • Eat a healthy diet to nourish your hair from the inside out

Cutting your hair with thinning scissors is a great way to add volume and texture to your hair. Follow these steps and tips to achieve a natural, textured look.
