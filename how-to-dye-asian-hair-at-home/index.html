How To Dye Asian Hair At Home

Written by Aprilia Oct 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hair color but don't want to spend a fortune at the salon, dyeing your hair at home is a great option. However, if you have Asian hair, you may have some unique challenges when it comes to dyeing your hair. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to dye Asian hair at home.

Table of Contents

  • How to Dye Asian Hair at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Dyeing Asian Hair at Home
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Dyeing

How to Dye Asian Hair at Home

Before you start dyeing your hair, it's important to choose the right color. If you have warm-toned skin, you should opt for warm colors like golden browns and reds. If you have cool-toned skin, you should go for cooler colors like ash browns and blues.

Next, you should choose the right type of hair dye. If you have virgin hair (hair that has never been dyed before), you can use any type of hair dye. However, if you have previously dyed hair, you should use a color remover to strip the color before dyeing again.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are the steps to dye Asian hair at home:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Put on gloves to protect your hands from the dye.
  3. Mix the hair dye according to the instructions on the package.
  4. Apply the dye to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  5. Leave the dye on for the recommended amount of time (usually around 30 minutes).
  6. Rinse out the dye with cool water until the water runs clear.
  7. Apply the conditioner that comes with the hair dye and leave it on for a few minutes.
  8. Rinse out the conditioner with cool water.
  9. Dry and style your hair as usual.

Tips for Dyeing Asian Hair at Home

Here are some tips to make the process of dyeing Asian hair at home easier:

  • Make sure to do a patch test before dyeing your hair to check for any allergic reactions.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment before and after dyeing your hair to minimize damage.
  • If you're dyeing your hair a lighter color, you may need to bleach your hair first.
  • If you're dyeing your hair a darker color, choose a shade that's no more than two shades darker than your natural hair color.
  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to help preserve the color.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about dyeing Asian hair at home:

Can I dye my hair if I have a perm?

Yes, you can dye your hair if you have a perm. However, you should wait at least a week after getting a perm before dyeing your hair to avoid damaging your hair.

Can I dye my hair if I have henna in it?

No, you should not dye your hair if you have henna in it. Henna can react with hair dye, leading to unpredictable results.

Hair Care After Dyeing

After dyeing your hair, it's important to take care of it to prevent damage and preserve the color. Here are some tips for hair care after dyeing:

  • Avoid washing your hair for the first 48 hours after dyeing to allow the color to set.
  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to help preserve the color.
  • Avoid using hot tools (like curling irons and straighteners) as much as possible to prevent damage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to too much sun or chlorine, as this can cause the color to fade.

Now that you know how to dye Asian hair at home, you can experiment with different colors and styles without breaking the bank. Just remember to choose the right color and type of hair dye, follow the instructions carefully, and take care of your hair after dyeing.
