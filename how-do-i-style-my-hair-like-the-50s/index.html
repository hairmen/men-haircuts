How Do I Style My Hair Like The 50S

Written by Daniel Jul 22, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to add some vintage flair to your hairstyle, the 50s are a great decade to draw inspiration from. With their voluminous curls and sleek updos, 50s hairstyles are both glamorous and timeless. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to style your hair like the 50s, as well as some tips and FAQs to help you perfect the look.

How to Style Your Hair Like the 50s

Before we dive into the specifics of each hairstyle, there are a few general tips to keep in mind when trying to achieve a 50s-inspired look:

  • Invest in some high-quality hair tools, like a curling iron or hot rollers.
  • Use hairspray and other styling products to hold your hairstyle in place.
  • Consider getting a hair cut or trim to help your hair hold its shape better.

Step-by-Step Guide: Victory Rolls

Victory rolls are perhaps the most iconic 50s hairstyle, characterized by their rolled-up curls that frame the face. Here's how to create the look:

  1. Part your hair to one side, using a comb or your fingers.
  2. Take a section of hair from the front of your head, near your part. This should be a triangular section that ends at your temple.
  3. Curl this section of hair towards your face, using a curling iron or hot rollers. Once the curl is set, roll it upward towards your scalp, securing it with bobby pins.
  4. Repeat this process on the other side of your head, creating a symmetrical look.
  5. Finish with hairspray to hold the rolls in place.

Step-by-Step Guide: Bouffant

The bouffant is a classic 50s updo that's perfect for formal occasions. Here's how to create the look:

  1. Part your hair to one side, using a comb or your fingers.
  2. Using a teasing comb, tease the hair at your crown to create volume.
  3. Gather the teased hair into a ponytail, leaving the hair around your face out.
  4. Roll the ponytail inward towards your scalp, securing it with bobby pins as you go.
  5. Take the hair around your face and smooth it upward, tucking it into the rolled ponytail and securing with bobby pins.
  6. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Styling Your Hair Like the 50s

Here are a few additional tips to help you perfect your 50s-inspired hairstyle:

  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different hair accessories, like headbands or scarves.
  • Consider adding some volume to your hair with a volumizing mousse or spray.
  • Practice your technique before an important event to make sure you get the look just right.

FAQs: Styling Your Hair Like the 50s

What kind of hair works best for 50s hairstyles?

While 50s hairstyles can work on a variety of hair types, it's generally easier to achieve the look if you have medium to long hair. If your hair is shorter, you may need to use extensions or hairpieces to create the desired effect.

How long does it take to style your hair like the 50s?

It depends on the style you're going for and your level of experience. Victory rolls, for example, can take anywhere from 10-30 minutes depending on how much hair you have and how intricate you want the rolls to be. A bouffant may take a bit longer, especially if you're new to the technique.

How do I maintain my 50s hairstyle throughout the day?

Using hairspray and other styling products can help your hairstyle stay in place throughout the day. You may also want to consider carrying some bobby pins or other hair accessories with you in case you need to make any touch-ups.

Hair Care Tips for 50s-Inspired Hairstyles

Styling your hair like the 50s can be a fun way to switch up your look, but it's important to take care of your hair in the process. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools like curling irons or straighteners.
  • Don't overdo it on the hairspray, as this can lead to buildup and damage over time.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

Conclusion

Styling your hair like the 50s can be a fun and glamorous way to switch up your look. Whether you're trying out victory rolls or a bouffant, with a little practice and some high-quality hair tools, you'll be rocking a vintage-inspired hairstyle in no time!
