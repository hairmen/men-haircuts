How To Thin Out Curly Hair With Thinning Shears A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 30, 2023 · 4 min read
If you have curly hair, you know that it can be a blessing and a curse. While curly hair is beautiful and unique, it can also be difficult to manage and style. One of the most common problems that people with curly hair face is thickness. If your hair is too thick, it can be hard to style and can feel heavy on your head. Luckily, there is a simple solution: thinning shears. In this article, we will explain how to thin out curly hair with thinning shears in a relaxed English language.

How to Thin Out Curly Hair with Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are a type of scissors that have teeth on one or both blades. These teeth help to remove some of the hair while leaving the rest intact. To thin out curly hair with thinning shears, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start thinning out your hair, you need to make sure it is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see how much hair you need to remove.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

To make the process easier, section your hair into four equal parts. Use clips to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Start Thinning Out Your Hair

Starting with one of the sections, take a small section of hair and hold it in between your fingers. Use the thinning shears to cut into the hair, starting at the mid-lengths and working your way down to the ends. Repeat this process until you have thinned out the entire section.

Step 4: Repeat on the Other Sections

Repeat the process on the other three sections of your hair, making sure that you are removing the same amount of hair from each section.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you have thinned out all four sections of your hair, use regular scissors to blend the layers together. This will help to create a more natural look.

Tips for Thinning Out Curly Hair

Thinning out curly hair can be a bit tricky, so here are some tips to help you get the best results: - Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections. - Only thin out your hair a little bit at a time. You can always go back and thin out more if you need to. - Be careful not to thin out your hair too much. You want to maintain the integrity of your curls. - Remember to blend the layers together once you have thinned out your hair.

FAQs

Is it okay to thin out my curly hair with thinning shears?

Yes, it is perfectly fine to thin out your curly hair with thinning shears. Just make sure to follow the steps outlined in this article and be careful not to thin out your hair too much.

Can I use regular scissors to thin out my curly hair?

While it is possible to use regular scissors to thin out your curly hair, thinning shears are specifically designed for this purpose and will give you better results.

Hair Care for Thinned Out Curly Hair

Once you have thinned out your curly hair, it is important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips: - Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated. - Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. If you do use them, make sure to use a heat protectant spray. - Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair. - Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your hair.

Conclusion

Thinning out curly hair with thinning shears is a great way to make your hair more manageable and give it a more natural look. By following the steps outlined in this article and taking proper care of your hair, you can keep your curls looking beautiful and healthy.
