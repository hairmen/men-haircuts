What Causes Frontal Hair Loss?

Frontal hair loss is often caused by a combination of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, stress, and poor hair care habits. For example, if your parents or grandparents had receding hairlines, you may be more likely to experience it too. Hormonal changes, such as those that occur during pregnancy, menopause, or androgenic alopecia, can also lead to hair loss in the front of the scalp.

Stress is another common cause of frontal hair loss. When youâre under a lot of stress, your body produces cortisol, a hormone that can damage hair follicles and cause hair loss. Finally, poor hair care habits, such as using harsh shampoos, over-styling your hair, or not protecting your hair from the sun, can also contribute to hair loss in the front of the scalp.

How to Stop Frontal Hair Loss

Stopping frontal hair loss requires a multifaceted approach. You need to address the underlying causes of your hair loss while also taking steps to promote healthy hair growth. Here are some things you can do to stop frontal hair loss:

  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet that includes plenty of protein and vitamins
  • Avoid over-styling your hair and using harsh chemicals
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as braids, cornrows, or ponytails
  • Get enough sleep and manage your stress levels
