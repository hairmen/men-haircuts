How To Cut Long Layers On Sides Of Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 13, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of your long hair looking flat and lifeless? Adding some layers to the sides of your hair can give it more dimension and movement. But how do you cut long layers on the sides of your hair without making a mess? In this article, we'll show you how to do it step-by-step, with tips and FAQs to help you along the way. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Long Layers on Sides of Hair

To cut long layers on the sides of your hair, you'll need a few basic tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or ties

It's also helpful to have a mirror and good lighting to see what you're doing. Now, let's get started with the step-by-step guide.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will help you see the natural shape and movement of your hair, which will make it easier to cut the layers. Comb your hair straight down, and divide it into two sections: left and right.

Step 2: Clip the Top Section of Your Hair

Clip the top section of your hair away from the sides, so you can focus on one side at a time. Use a hair clip or tie to keep the top section out of the way.

Step 3: Determine the Length of Your Layers

Decide how long you want your layers to be. For longer layers, start at the bottom of your hair. For shorter layers, start higher up. Use your comb to lift a small section of hair from the side of your head, and hold it between your fingers at the length you want your layers to be.

Step 4: Cut Your First Layer

Using your scissors, cut the section of hair at the length you're holding it. This will be your guide for the rest of the layers. Don't worry if it's not perfect – you can always even it out later.

Step 5: Continue Cutting Layers

Take another small section of hair next to the first one, and use the first layer as a guide to cut it at the same length. Continue cutting layers in this way, working your way up the side of your head.

Step 6: Repeat on the Other Side

Once you've finished one side, unclip the top section of your hair and repeat the process on the other side.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

Once you've cut all the layers, check for evenness by combing your hair straight down and looking in the mirror. If any layers are longer than others, trim them until they're even.

Tips for Cutting Long Layers on Sides of Hair

Here are some tips to make the process of cutting long layers on the sides of your hair easier:

  • Use sharp scissors to make clean cuts.
  • Start with longer layers and work your way up to shorter layers.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once – it's better to take smaller sections and work your way up.
  • Take breaks if you need to – cutting your own hair can be tiring!

FAQs

Can I Cut Layers on the Sides of My Hair if I Have Curly Hair?

Yes, you can cut layers on the sides of your hair if you have curly hair. However, be careful not to cut too much, as curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries.

Do I Need to Cut Layers on Both Sides?

It's up to you! Some people prefer to only cut layers on one side, while others like to cut layers on both sides for a more symmetrical look.

How Often Should I Cut My Layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how often you want to maintain your layers. Generally, it's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Cutting Long Layers on Sides of Hair

After cutting long layers on the sides of your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to cut long layers on the sides of your hair, it's time to try it out for yourself! With a little practice and patience, you'll be able to give your hair a fresh new look without spending a lot of money at the salon. Happy cutting!
