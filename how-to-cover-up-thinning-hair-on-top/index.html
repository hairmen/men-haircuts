How To Cover Up Thinning Hair On Top

Written by Aprilia Jun 29, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Thinning hair on top can be a challenging problem for both men and women. It can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, aging, and hormonal changes. If you are struggling with thinning hair on top, you may feel self-conscious and frustrated. Fortunately, there are several ways to cover up thinning hair and boost your confidence.

Description

Thinning hair on top is a common problem that affects millions of people around the world. It can cause a loss of self-confidence and lead to feelings of embarrassment and shame. Thinning hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, aging, and hormonal changes. Fortunately, there are several ways to cover up thinning hair and boost your confidence.

How to Cover Up Thinning Hair on Top

If you are struggling with thinning hair on top, there are several ways to cover it up. Here are some of the most effective methods:

1. Use Hair Fibers

Hair fibers are a great way to cover up thinning hair on top. They are made from natural keratin fibers that bond to your existing hair and give the appearance of thicker, fuller hair. To use hair fibers, simply sprinkle the fibers onto your thinning areas and pat them down gently. The fibers will stay in place until you shampoo your hair.

2. Try a Hairpiece

If you are looking for a more dramatic solution, you may want to consider a hairpiece. Hairpieces are available in a variety of styles and colors and can be customized to fit your head perfectly. They are a great way to cover up thinning hair and give you a full head of hair.

3. Use a Thickening Shampoo

Thickening shampoos can help to give the appearance of thicker, fuller hair. They work by coating your hair with a layer of protein that helps to add volume and thickness. To use a thickening shampoo, simply apply it to your hair and massage it into your scalp. Rinse it out thoroughly and style your hair as usual.

4. Try a Hair Transplant

If you are looking for a more permanent solution, you may want to consider a hair transplant. A hair transplant involves taking hair from a donor area and transplanting it to your thinning areas. This procedure can give you a full head of hair and restore your confidence.

Step by Step

Here are the step-by-step instructions for covering up thinning hair on top:

Step 1: Clean Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo. This will remove any dirt and oil that may be weighing your hair down.

Step 2: Apply Hair Fibers

If you are using hair fibers, sprinkle them onto your thinning areas and pat them down gently. The fibers will bond to your existing hair and give the appearance of thicker, fuller hair.

Step 3: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as usual, using a comb or brush to distribute the hair fibers evenly.

Tips

Here are some tips for covering up thinning hair on top:

1. Avoid Overstyling

Overstyling your hair can make it look thinner and more brittle. Try to stick to simple styles that don't require a lot of heat or styling products.

2. Use a Volumizing Mousse

Volumizing mousses can help to give your hair more body and volume. They work by coating your hair with a layer of protein that helps to add volume and thickness.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles can pull on your hair and cause it to break or fall out. Try to stick to looser styles that don't put too much strain on your hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about covering up thinning hair on top:

1. Can Hair Fibers Damage My Hair?

No, hair fibers are made from natural keratin fibers that are safe for your hair. They won't cause any damage or harm to your existing hair.

2. How Long Do Hair Fibers Last?

Hair fibers can last until you shampoo your hair. They will stay in place until they are washed out with shampoo.

3. Will a Hair Transplant Leave Scars?

A hair transplant may leave scars, but they are usually small and can be hidden by your hair. Talk to your doctor about the risks and benefits of a hair transplant.

Hair Care

Taking good care of your hair can help to prevent further thinning and damage. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Avoid harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils. Use a gentle shampoo that is designed for your hair type.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and cause it to become brittle and thin. Try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.

3. Eat a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet can help to keep your hair healthy and strong. Eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins to give your hair the nutrients it needs.

4. Get Regular Haircuts

Regular haircuts can help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends and breakage. Try to get a haircut every six to eight weeks.

In conclusion, covering up thinning hair on top can be a challenge, but there are several ways to boost your confidence and give you the appearance of thicker, fuller hair. Whether you choose hair fibers, a hairpiece, or a hair transplant, there is a solution that can work for you. By taking good care of your hair and following these tips, you can keep your hair healthy and strong.
