Small Low Maintenance Dog Breeds That Don t Shed

Written by Petter Nov 25, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking for a furry companion but don't have the time or energy to clean up after a shedding dog? Look no further than small low maintenance dog breeds that don't shed! These adorable pups are perfect for busy people who still want the joy of a four-legged friend without the hassle of constant cleaning.

Description

Small low maintenance dog breeds that don't shed require minimal grooming and are great for people with allergies. These breeds typically have hair rather than fur, which means they shed less and require less maintenance. They also tend to be smaller in size, making them ideal for apartment living or small homes.

How to Choose the Right Breed

Choosing the right breed is important when it comes to bringing a new dog into your home. Consider factors such as size, energy level, and grooming requirements. If you have allergies, make sure to choose a breed that is hypoallergenic. Some popular small low maintenance dog breeds that don't shed include:

  • Maltese
  • Poodle
  • Bichon Frise
  • Shih Tzu
  • Yorkshire Terrier

Maltese

The Maltese is a small breed that weighs between 4 and 7 pounds. They have long, silky hair that requires regular brushing to prevent matting. Maltese dogs are friendly and affectionate, making them great family pets.

Poodle

Poodles come in three sizes: toy, miniature, and standard. They are known for their curly, hypoallergenic coats that require regular grooming. Poodles are intelligent and easy to train, making them great for families with children.

Bichon Frise

The Bichon Frise is a small, fluffy breed that weighs between 10 and 18 pounds. They have curly white coats that require regular grooming to prevent matting. Bichon Frises are friendly and playful, making them great for families.

Shih Tzu

The Shih Tzu is a small breed that weighs between 9 and 16 pounds. They have long, silky hair that requires regular grooming to prevent matting. Shih Tzus are affectionate and loyal, making them great companion dogs.

Yorkshire Terrier

The Yorkshire Terrier, or Yorkie, is a small breed that weighs between 4 and 7 pounds. They have long, silky hair that requires regular grooming to prevent matting. Yorkies are energetic and playful, making them great for families with children.

Step by Step Tips for Grooming

Grooming is an important part of keeping your small low maintenance dog breed healthy and happy. Here are some step by step tips for grooming:

  1. Brush your dog's coat regularly to prevent matting.
  2. Trim your dog's nails regularly to prevent overgrowth.
  3. Clean your dog's ears regularly to prevent infections.
  4. Bathe your dog as needed, but not too frequently as it can dry out their skin.
  5. Brush your dog's teeth regularly to prevent dental problems.

FAQ

What is the best small dog breed that doesn't shed?

The best small low maintenance dog breed that doesn't shed depends on your lifestyle and preferences. Some popular breeds include the Maltese, Poodle, Bichon Frise, Shih Tzu, and Yorkshire Terrier.

Do small low maintenance dogs need a lot of exercise?

Small low maintenance dog breeds don't require a lot of exercise, but they still need to be walked and played with daily to stay healthy and happy.

Are small low maintenance dog breeds good with children?

Many small low maintenance dog breeds are great with children, but it's important to supervise interactions between dogs and children to prevent accidents. Make sure to socialize your dog with children from a young age.

Hair Care

Regular grooming is important to keep your small low maintenance dog breed's coat healthy and shiny. Brush your dog's hair regularly to prevent matting and tangles. You can also take your dog to a professional groomer for regular haircuts and maintenance.

Overall, small low maintenance dog breeds that don't shed are great for people who want a furry companion without the hassle of constant cleaning. Consider factors such as size, energy level, and grooming requirements when choosing the right breed for you.
