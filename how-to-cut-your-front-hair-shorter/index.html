How To Cut Your Front Hair Shorter A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Sep 25, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your long front hair getting in the way of your face? Do you want to switch up your look and try a shorter style? Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a fresh, new look in no time. In this article, we’ll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut your front hair shorter, as well as tips and hair care advice to ensure a successful haircut.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You’ll Need
  • How to Cut Your Front Hair Shorter
  • Tips for a Successful Haircut
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Your Haircut

Tools You’ll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you’ll need to gather a few tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Handheld mirror (optional)


Make sure your scissors are sharp to ensure a clean cut. You can purchase hair cutting scissors at most beauty supply stores or online.

How to Cut Your Front Hair Shorter

Follow these steps to cut your front hair shorter:

  1. Wash and dry your hair: Start with clean, dry hair. Comb through your hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Section your hair: Use the comb to section off your front hair from the rest of your hair. Clip the remaining hair back.
  3. Decide on your desired length: Determine how short you want your front hair to be. It’s best to start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed.
  4. Trim the ends: Using your scissors, make small cuts to trim the ends of your hair. Start at the middle of your hair and work your way down to the tips. Be sure to cut straight across.
  5. Texturize the ends: To create a more natural look, use the point of your scissors to make small, vertical cuts into the ends of your hair. This will create a textured, layered effect.
  6. Blend in the sides: Unclip the rest of your hair and blend the front section with the sides. Use the comb to blend the hair together and make sure there are no visible lines.
  7. Check your work: Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your hair to ensure an even cut. Make any additional cuts if necessary.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your front hair shorter:

  • Start with clean, dry hair: Cutting wet hair can make it difficult to see the true length and texture of your hair.
  • Take your time: Don’t rush through the haircut. Take small, precise cuts to ensure an even cut.
  • Use the right scissors: Using dull scissors or regular household scissors can result in a jagged, uneven cut.
  • Don’t cut too much: It’s better to start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed.
  • Ask for help: If you’re unsure about cutting your own hair, ask a friend or family member to assist you.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your front hair shorter:

Can I cut my hair shorter if it’s curly?

Yes, you can cut curly hair shorter. However, it’s important to cut curly hair when it’s dry to ensure an even cut.

How often should I cut my front hair?

It’s recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Can I cut my front hair shorter if it’s thin?

Yes, you can cut thin hair shorter. However, it’s important to avoid cutting too much to prevent further thinning.

Hair Care After Your Haircut

After cutting your front hair shorter, it’s important to take care of your hair to maintain a healthy, shiny look. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain a healthy length.
  • Eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth.


In conclusion, cutting your front hair shorter can be a simple and rewarding way to switch up your look. Follow these steps and tips to achieve a successful haircut and maintain healthy hair.
