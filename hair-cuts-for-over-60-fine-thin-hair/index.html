Hair Cuts For Over 60 Fine Thin Hair Tips How To And Faq

Written by Oliver Aug 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As we age, our hair tends to become finer and thinner. This can make it challenging to find the right hair cut that will flatter our face and make us feel confident. If you're over 60 and have fine thin hair, don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll give you tips on how to choose the right hair cut, step-by-step guide on how to maintain it, and answer some frequently asked questions.

How to Choose the Right Hair Cut

Choosing the right hair cut is crucial, especially if you have fine thin hair. Here are some tips to help you find the perfect hair cut:

1. Consider Your Face Shape

It's essential to choose a hair cut that flatters your face shape. If you have a round face, opt for a layered cut that adds height to your crown. If you have a long face, a blunt bob can make your face appear wider.

2. Go for Shorter Hair

Shorter haircuts can make fine thin hair appear thicker and fuller. Pixie cuts, bobs, and layered cuts are excellent options for women over 60 with fine thin hair.

3. Layers are Your Friend

Layered haircuts can add volume and texture to your fine thin hair. Opt for long layers that start at your chin or shorter layers that add volume to the crown.

4. Avoid Heavy Bangs

Heavy bangs can make your fine thin hair appear even thinner. Instead, opt for side-swept bangs that add volume to your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide on How to Maintain Your Hair Cut

Now that you've found the perfect hair cut, it's essential to maintain it properly. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to maintain your hair cut:

1. Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner that's specially formulated for fine thin hair. Avoid using heavy conditioners that can weigh down your hair.

2. Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow drying your hair upside down can add volume and lift to your roots. Use a round brush to add more volume and texture.

3. Use a Root Lifter

A root lifter can add volume and texture to your hair. Apply it to your roots before blow-drying your hair.

4. Avoid Using Heavy Products

Avoid using heavy products like hair wax and pomade that can weigh down your hair. Opt for lightweight products like mousse and hairspray that add volume and hold.

Tips for Hair Care

Here are some additional tips to help you care for your fine thin hair:

1. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair every day can strip it of its natural oils, making it look dry and brittle. Opt for washing your hair every two to three days.

2. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Heat styling tools like curling irons and straighteners can damage your hair. If you must use them, apply a heat protectant spray to your hair before styling.

3. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can prevent split ends and breakage, making your hair appear healthier and thicker.

FAQ

1. What Hair Cut Should I Get if I Have Fine Thin Hair?

Opt for shorter haircuts like pixie cuts, bobs, and layered cuts. Layers can add volume and texture to your hair.

2. How Often Should I Wash My Hair?

Wash your hair every two to three days to prevent stripping it of its natural oils.

3. Can I Use Heavy Products on My Fine Thin Hair?

Avoid using heavy products like hair wax and pomade that can weigh down your hair. Opt for lightweight products like mousse and hairspray that add volume and hold.

4. How Can I Add Volume to My Fine Thin Hair?

Blow drying your hair upside down, using a root lifter, and opting for layered haircuts can add volume and texture to your fine thin hair.

Conclusion

Choosing the right hair cut and maintaining it properly is essential if you have fine thin hair. Opt for shorter haircuts, add layers, and use lightweight products to add volume and texture to your hair. Washing your hair every two to three days, protecting it from heat, and getting regular trims can also help keep your hair looking healthy and fuller. With these tips, you'll be able to find the perfect hair cut and feel confident in your skin.
