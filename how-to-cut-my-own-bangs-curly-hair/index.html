How To Cut My Own Bangs Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Nov 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair is beautiful, but when it comes to cutting your own bangs, it can be a bit of a challenge. However, with the right technique and some patience, you can achieve the look you want. In this blog post, we'll show you how to cut your own bangs on curly hair, step-by-step, so you can achieve that perfect, face-framing look.

  • How to Cut My Own Bangs Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

Description

Cutting your own bangs can be a daunting task, but with curly hair, it can be even more challenging. Curly hair tends to bounce up and shrink when dry, so it's important to cut your bangs when they are wet and stretched out. Additionally, curly hair can be unpredictable, so it's important to take your time and be patient when cutting your own bangs.

How to Cut My Own Bangs Curly Hair

To cut your own bangs on curly hair, you'll need a few essential tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own bangs on curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would, and then gently towel-dry your hair to remove excess water.
  2. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and then separate your bangs from the rest of your hair using hair clips.
  3. Wet your bangs with a spray bottle filled with water, and then use your fingers to stretch them out as much as possible.
  4. Take your scissors and make a small, vertical cut in the middle of your bangs.
  5. Next, take small sections of your bangs and cut them a little at a time, following the initial cut as a guide.
  6. Continue cutting small sections of your bangs, checking in the mirror frequently to see how they look.
  7. When you're happy with the length of your bangs, use the scissors to add texture and layers if desired.
  8. Use a wide-tooth comb to fluff up your bangs, and then style them as desired.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own bangs on curly hair:

  • Always cut your bangs when they are wet and stretched out, as curly hair tends to bounce up and shrink when dry.
  • Take your time and cut small sections at a time, checking in the mirror frequently to see how they look.
  • If you're unsure about how much to cut, start with a little and then cut more as needed.
  • If your curls are particularly tight, consider cutting your bangs a little longer than you normally would, as they will bounce up when dry.

FAQ

Here are some commonly asked questions about cutting your own bangs on curly hair:

  • Can I cut my bangs dry? It's not recommended to cut curly hair dry, as it can be unpredictable and difficult to manage.
  • How often should I cut my bangs? It's recommended to cut your bangs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their length and shape.
  • What if I make a mistake? If you make a mistake while cutting your bangs, don't panic! You can always trim them a little shorter or ask a professional stylist for help.

Hair Care

Curly hair requires special care to stay healthy and frizz-free. Here are some tips to keep your curly hair looking its best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Don't wash your hair too often, as this can dry out your curls and lead to frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as brushes can break up your curls and cause frizz.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair, as this can damage your curls and lead to breakage.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to keep your curls moisturized and defined.

By following these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to cut your own bangs on curly hair with confidence. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll achieve the perfect look in no time!
