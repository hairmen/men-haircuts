Is Drop Fade and Low Fade the Same? | Haircut Guide

Are you looking for a new haircut but confused about the difference between drop fade and low fade? This guide will help you understand the difference and choose the right one for you.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Drop fade and low fade are two popular types of fades that are commonly used in men's haircuts. They both involve gradually cutting the hair shorter towards the bottom, but the difference lies in where the fade starts.

A low fade starts just above the ears and gradually tapers down towards the neck. On the other hand, a drop fade starts higher up on the head and curves down towards the nape of the neck. This creates a more dramatic and noticeable fade.

How to

If you're interested in getting a drop fade or low fade, it's important to find a skilled barber who can execute the style properly. Bring in a picture of the style you want to help communicate your desired look.

The barber will start by cutting the hair to the desired length on top, then use clippers to gradually fade the hair shorter towards the bottom. The difference between a low fade and drop fade is where the fade starts, so be sure to communicate your preference to the barber.

Step by Step

Here are the basic steps for getting a drop fade or low fade:

  1. Choose the type of fade you want (low fade or drop fade).
  2. Show your barber a picture of the style you want.
  3. The barber will cut the hair to the desired length on top.
  4. Using clippers, the barber will gradually fade the hair shorter towards the bottom.
  5. The barber will blend the fade into the rest of the hair to create a seamless look.
  6. Your haircut is complete!

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the best results with your drop fade or low fade:

  • Choose a skilled barber who has experience with fades.
  • Bring in a picture of the style you want to help communicate your desired look.
  • Communicate clearly with your barber to ensure you get the type of fade you want.
  • Use hair products like pomade or styling gel to help maintain the shape of your haircut.

FAQ

Here are some commonly asked questions about drop fade and low fade:

Q: Is a drop fade or low fade better for thick hair?

A: Both drop fade and low fade can work well for thick hair, but a low fade may be easier to maintain.

Q: How often do I need to get a drop fade or low fade?

A: This depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain your haircut. Generally, you should plan to get a touch-up every 2-4 weeks.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is important to maintain the look of your drop fade or low fade. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair healthy and soft.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers or straighteners too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use hair products like pomade or styling gel to help maintain the shape of your haircut.

Now that you know the difference between drop fade and low fade, you can choose the right one for your next haircut. Remember to find a skilled barber and communicate clearly to ensure you get the look you want. With proper hair care, your fade will look great for weeks to come.
