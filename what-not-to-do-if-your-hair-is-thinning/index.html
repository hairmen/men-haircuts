What Not To Do If Your Hair Is Thinning

Written by Oliver Jun 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you are experiencing hair thinning or hair loss, it can be a frustrating and difficult experience. While there are many products and treatments available to help combat hair thinning, it's important to remember that there are also things you should avoid doing that can make the problem worse. In this article, we'll go over what not to do if your hair is thinning.

Table of Contents

  • Over-washing your hair
  • Using harsh hair products
  • Heat styling your hair
  • Wearing tight hairstyles
  • Not eating a healthy diet
  • Not getting enough sleep
  • Being too stressed
  • Smoking
  • Not seeing a doctor
  • Ignoring the problem

Over-washing your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle, which can lead to breakage and hair loss. It's important to find a balance between keeping your scalp clean and not over-washing your hair. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it more often, but if you have dry hair, you may be able to wash it less frequently.

Using harsh hair products

Harsh hair products, such as those that contain sulfates or alcohol, can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage and hair loss. Look for gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners that are specifically formulated for thinning hair.

Heat styling your hair

Heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage and hair loss. If you must use heat styling tools, use them on the lowest heat setting possible and use a heat protectant spray or serum to minimize damage.

Wearing tight hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, such as braids, ponytails, and buns, can pull on your hair and cause it to break and fall out. If you must wear your hair in a tight style, try to loosen it up as much as possible and avoid pulling it too tightly.

Not eating a healthy diet

A healthy diet is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure you are eating a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals. Some foods that are particularly good for hair health include eggs, spinach, salmon, and avocados.

Not getting enough sleep

Getting enough sleep is important for overall health, including hair health. Lack of sleep can lead to stress, which can cause hair loss. Aim for at least seven to eight hours of sleep per night.

Being too stressed

Stress can cause hair loss by disrupting the normal hair growth cycle. Try to manage your stress levels by practicing relaxation techniques, such as deep breathing, yoga, or meditation.

Smoking

Smoking can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage and hair loss. If you smoke, consider quitting to improve your overall health and the health of your hair.

Not seeing a doctor

If you are experiencing hair thinning or hair loss, it's important to see a doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions that may be causing the problem. Your doctor may be able to recommend treatments or medications that can help.

Ignoring the problem

Ignoring hair thinning or hair loss will not make it go away. It's important to take action early on to prevent further damage and to promote healthy hair growth. If you are experiencing hair thinning, take steps to address the problem and seek help if necessary.

Tips for Healthy Hair

  • Use a wide-toothed comb or brush to minimize breakage
  • Avoid rubbing your hair dry with a towel, instead, gently pat it dry
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal parts, as they can damage your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective spray

FAQ

Can hair thinning be reversed?

In some cases, hair thinning can be reversed with the right treatment. However, it's important to address the problem early on for the best chance of success.

What causes hair thinning?

There are many factors that can contribute to hair thinning, including genetics, hormonal changes, medication, and medical conditions.

Can stress cause hair loss?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss by disrupting the normal hair growth cycle.

Hair Care

Remember, taking care of your hair is important for healthy hair growth. Be gentle with your hair, avoid harsh products and styling tools, and eat a healthy diet to promote healthy hair growth.
