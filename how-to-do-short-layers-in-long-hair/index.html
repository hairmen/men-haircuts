How To Do Short Layers In Long Hair

Written by Eveline Jun 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your long hair feeling heavy and lifeless? Adding short layers to your long hair can give it movement and texture, making it look more voluminous and dynamic. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of achieving short layers in long hair, from the tools and products you'll need to the step-by-step instructions. Let's get started!

Description

Short layers in long hair are a great way to freshen up your look without sacrificing length. The layers are cut shorter than the rest of the hair, creating movement and texture. This technique is suitable for all hair types, from straight to curly, and can be customized to suit your face shape and personal style.

How to

Tools Required

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Products Required

Here are some products that can help you achieve the perfect short layers:

  • Heat protectant spray
  • Hair serum or oil
  • Hairspray

Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these step-by-step instructions to achieve short layers in your long hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb through your hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Section off the top layer of your hair and clip it away.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, about an inch or two from the ends.
  4. Using your scissors, cut the hair at an angle, starting from the length you're comfortable with and working your way up towards the roots. Be sure to cut at an angle to create a layered effect.
  5. Release the section of hair and check the length of the layers. If you want shorter layers, repeat the process on another section of hair. Make sure to cut each section to the same length.
  6. Once you've finished cutting the bottom layer, unclip the top layer and repeat the process.
  7. When you've cut all the layers, comb through your hair to blend the layers together.
  8. Apply a heat protectant spray and use a curling iron or straightener to style your hair as desired.
  9. Finish off with a hair serum or oil to add shine and a light hairspray to hold the style.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up. You can always cut more if you want shorter layers.
  • When cutting, use small, sharp scissors, as they'll give you a cleaner cut.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair tends to look longer and can result in uneven layers.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, consider going to a professional stylist.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about short layers in long hair:

Is it possible to achieve short layers in curly hair?

Yes, it is possible to achieve short layers in curly hair. However, it's important to work with a stylist who is experienced in cutting curly hair, as curly hair tends to shrink when it dries.

Can I still tie my hair up after getting short layers?

Yes, you can still tie your hair up after getting short layers. The layers will add texture and movement to your hair, making it look more voluminous even when tied up.

How often should I get my hair trimmed after getting short layers?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of your layers and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

Here are some tips to take care of your hair after getting short layers:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair from drying out.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and keep it healthy.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your layers.

Now that you know how to do short layers in long hair, go ahead and give it a try. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a fresh, new look that suits your personal style. Happy cutting!
