Little Boy Haircuts 2020 The Ultimate Guide

If you're a parent, you know how important it is to keep your little boy looking sharp and stylish. In 2020, there are plenty of trendy haircuts for little boys to choose from. In this guide, we'll break down everything you need to know about little boy haircuts in 2020, from the most popular styles to how to care for your child's hair.

Table of Contents

  • Popular Styles
  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

Popular Styles

There are a variety of little boy haircuts that are popular in 2020. Here are some of the most trendy options:

Short Buzz Cut

The short buzz cut is a classic haircut that is perfect for little boys who want a low-maintenance style. This cut involves trimming the hair short all over the head, using clippers or scissors. It's a great option for active little boys who don't want to spend time styling their hair.

Long Shaggy Hair

The long shaggy hair look is popular for boys who want a more relaxed, surfer-inspired style. This cut involves leaving the hair longer on top, with layered, shaggy ends. This style works best on boys with thick, wavy hair.

Undercut

The undercut is a trendy look that involves shaving the hair short on the sides and leaving it longer on top. This style can be worn with a variety of lengths on top, from a short, choppy cut to a longer, slicked-back style.

How To

If you're interested in giving your little boy a trendy haircut, here are some general tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose a style that complements your child's face shape and hair type.
  • Invest in quality hair clippers or scissors to ensure a clean, even cut.
  • Practice patience and take your time when cutting your child's hair.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help from a professional stylist if you're unsure about cutting your child's hair.

Step By Step

Here is a basic step-by-step guide to cutting your little boy's hair:

  1. Start by washing and drying your child's hair.
  2. Choose the desired haircut and gather your tools.
  3. Use clippers or scissors to trim the hair to the desired length.
  4. Blend the hair using scissors or clippers, depending on the style.
  5. Clean up the neckline and sideburns with clippers or scissors.
  6. Style the hair as desired.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when giving your little boy a haircut:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut the hair shorter, rather than cutting too much off at once.
  • Use a comb to guide your scissors or clippers for a more even cut.
  • Take breaks if your child becomes fussy or restless.
  • Be patient and don't rush the process.

FAQ

How often should I get my child's hair cut?

The frequency of haircuts will depend on your child's hair type and desired style. As a general rule, it's a good idea to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep the hair looking neat and healthy.

What if my child has sensitive skin?

If your child has sensitive skin, it's important to use gentle, fragrance-free hair products and avoid harsh chemicals. You may also want to consider using clippers instead of scissors to reduce irritation on the scalp.

Hair Care

After your child's haircut, it's important to take proper care of their hair to keep it looking healthy and stylish. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for children.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to help detangle and protect the hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools (like blow dryers or flat irons) on your child's hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Encourage your child to wear a hat or use sun protection when playing outside to avoid damage from the sun.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to give your little boy a stylish and trendy haircut that he'll love. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
