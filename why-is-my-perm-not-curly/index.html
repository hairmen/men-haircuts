Why Is My Perm Not Curly Understanding The Issue

Written by Bardi Sep 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you one of those people who have recently got a perm, but unfortunately, it's not as curly as you expected? Well, you're not alone. Many people face the same issue after getting a perm, and it can be quite frustrating. However, before you get upset about it, you need to understand why your perm is not curly.

What is a Perm?

Perm is a chemical process that involves breaking down the natural bonds of your hair and reshaping them into curls. The process involves applying a perm solution to your hair, which makes your hair shaft swell and become more porous. After that, the hair is wrapped around perm rods and neutralized.

Why Is My Perm Not Curly?

There are several reasons why your perm is not curly:

Incorrect Processing Time

The processing time is crucial in getting the right curl pattern. If the perm solution is left on your hair for too long, it can over-process your hair, resulting in weak or no curls.

Incorrect Perm Rod Size

The size of the perm rod used also determines the curl pattern. If the perm rod is too big or too small, it can affect the curl pattern. For instance, a small perm rod will create tight curls while a big perm rod will give you loose curls.

Chemical Damage

If your hair is already chemically treated, such as dyed or highlighted, it can weaken your hair, making it difficult for the perm solution to take hold.

Heat Damage

If you use heat styling tools, such as straighteners or curling irons, it can damage your hair and affect the perm's curl pattern.

Incorrect Aftercare

The aftercare is as important as the perm process. If you don't follow the instructions given by your stylist, it can affect the perm's curl pattern. For instance, if you wash your hair too soon after getting a perm or don't use the right products, it can weaken the curl pattern.

How to Fix Your Perm?

Fixing your perm depends on the reason why it's not curly:

Incorrect Processing Time

If the processing time was too long, you can try using a protein treatment to strengthen your hair. However, if your hair is severely damaged, you may need to wait for your hair to grow out and get a new perm.

Incorrect Perm Rod Size

If the perm rod size was incorrect, you can get a new perm with the right perm rod size.

Chemical Damage

If your hair is chemically treated, you may need to wait for your hair to grow out and get a new perm.

Heat Damage

If your hair is heat damaged, you may need to wait for your hair to grow out and get a new perm. In the meantime, avoid using heat styling tools.

Incorrect Aftercare

If you didn't follow the aftercare instructions, you can try using products specifically designed for permed hair. Also, avoid washing your hair for at least 48 hours after getting a perm.

Tips for Maintaining Your Perm

Once you've got the perfect curls, you need to take care of them to maintain them. Here are some tips:

Use a Sulfate-Free Shampoo

Sulfate can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it dry and brittle. Use a sulfate-free shampoo to keep your curls healthy and bouncy.

Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools can damage your hair, so avoid using them as much as possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray.

Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls hydrated and frizz-free.

Avoid Touching Your Hair Too Much

Avoid touching your hair too much, as it can cause frizz and damage your curls.

FAQ

How long should I wait before washing my hair after getting a perm?

You should wait at least 48 hours before washing your hair after getting a perm.

Can I color my hair after getting a perm?

You should wait at least two weeks before coloring your hair after getting a perm.

Can I use a deep conditioner after getting a perm?

Yes, you can use a deep conditioner after getting a perm to keep your hair hydrated.

Hair Care

Getting a perm is a great way to switch up your hairstyle and add some curls to your hair. However, it's essential to understand why your perm is not curly and how to fix it. Make sure to follow the aftercare instructions given by your stylist to maintain your curls and keep them healthy.
