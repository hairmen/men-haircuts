What Can Cause Hair Loss In Teenage Girls

Written by Petter Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Hair loss is a common problem among both men and women. While it is often associated with aging, hair loss can also affect teenage girls. It can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, and certain medical conditions. In this article, we will discuss the common causes of hair loss in teenage girls and some tips for preventing and treating it.

Table of Contents

  1. Genetics
  2. Hormonal Changes
  3. Medical Conditions
  4. Stress and Anxiety
  5. Hair Care
  6. FAQs

Genetics

One of the most common causes of hair loss in teenage girls is genetics. If a girl's parents or grandparents experienced hair loss, she may be more likely to experience it as well. This type of hair loss is known as female pattern baldness and is characterized by a gradual thinning of the hair. It typically starts around the crown of the head and can spread outward. While there is no cure for female pattern baldness, there are treatments available that can slow down or even stop the hair loss.

Hormonal Changes

Another common cause of hair loss in teenage girls is hormonal changes. During puberty, girls experience a surge of hormones that can affect the growth and quality of their hair. This can result in thinning or shedding of the hair. Hormonal changes can also occur during pregnancy and menopause, which can further contribute to hair loss. In most cases, hair loss related to hormonal changes is temporary and will resolve on its own over time.

Medical Conditions

There are several medical conditions that can cause hair loss in teenage girls. These include:

  • Alopecia areata, an autoimmune disorder that causes hair loss in patches
  • Thyroid disorders, such as hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism
  • Anemia, which can cause hair loss due to a lack of iron in the body
  • Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), a hormonal disorder that can cause hair loss and other symptoms
  • Lupus, an autoimmune disorder that can cause hair loss and other symptoms

If a teenage girl is experiencing hair loss, it is important to see a doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Stress and Anxiety

Stress and anxiety can also contribute to hair loss in teenage girls. When the body is under stress, it can enter a state known as telogen effluvium, which causes the hair to enter a resting phase and eventually fall out. This type of hair loss is usually temporary and will resolve on its own once the stressor has been removed.

Hair Care

Poor hair care practices can also contribute to hair loss in teenage girls. Using harsh chemicals, such as hair dyes and relaxers, can damage the hair and cause it to break or fall out. Tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, can also cause hair loss if worn too frequently or too tightly. It is important to use gentle hair care products and avoid harsh treatments if a teenage girl is experiencing hair loss.

FAQs

Q: Is hair loss in teenage girls normal?

A: Hair loss is not uncommon in teenage girls, but it is not considered normal. If a teenage girl is experiencing hair loss, she should see a doctor to determine the underlying cause.

Q: Can hair loss in teenage girls be treated?

A: The treatment for hair loss in teenage girls depends on the underlying cause. If it is due to genetics or hormonal changes, there are treatments available that can slow down or stop the hair loss. If it is due to a medical condition, treating the condition may help to resolve the hair loss. In some cases, hair loss may resolve on its own without treatment.

Q: Can hair loss in teenage girls be prevented?

A: While some causes of hair loss in teenage girls, such as genetics and hormonal changes, cannot be prevented, there are steps that can be taken to promote healthy hair growth. These include eating a balanced diet, avoiding harsh hair care practices, and managing stress and anxiety.

Overall, hair loss in teenage girls can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, medical conditions, stress, and poor hair care practices. If a teenage girl is experiencing hair loss, she should see a doctor to determine the underlying cause and develop a treatment plan.
