How To Stop Hair Loss While Breastfeeding

Written by Eveline Aug 04, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're a new mom, you may have noticed that your hair is falling out more than usual. This is a common experience for many new mothers, but it can be frustrating and concerning. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks on how to stop hair loss while breastfeeding.

Description

Breastfeeding is a beautiful and natural process, but it can also take a toll on your body. One common side effect of breastfeeding is hair loss. This occurs because the hormones that stimulate hair growth during pregnancy are no longer present, and the body is producing more prolactin, which can cause hair loss. While this is normal, it can be concerning for new mothers who are already dealing with sleepless nights and other challenges of motherhood.

How to Stop Hair Loss While Breastfeeding

While you can't completely prevent hair loss while breastfeeding, there are some things you can do to minimize it. Here are some tips:

1. Eat a Balanced and Nutritious Diet

Eating a balanced and nutritious diet can help promote hair growth. Aim to eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein. Also, make sure to drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

2. Take a Prenatal Vitamin

Taking a prenatal vitamin can help ensure that you're getting all the nutrients you need for healthy hair growth. Look for a vitamin that contains biotin, iron, and vitamin D.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles like braids, ponytails, and buns, as these can pull on your hair and cause breakage. Instead, opt for looser styles like a messy bun or a low ponytail.

4. Be Gentle When Brushing and Styling Your Hair

Be gentle when brushing and styling your hair, as rough handling can cause breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to minimize pulling.

5. Use a Gentle Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's free of sulfates and harsh chemicals. These can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause breakage.

6. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help improve blood flow and stimulate hair growth. Use your fingers to gently massage your scalp for a few minutes each day.

7. Try Hair Growth Products

There are many hair growth products on the market that can help stimulate hair growth. Look for products that contain ingredients like biotin, keratin, and caffeine.

8. Get Plenty of Rest

Getting enough rest is important for overall health, but it's also important for healthy hair growth. Aim to get at least 7-8 hours of sleep each night.

Step by Step

Follow these steps to help minimize hair loss while breastfeeding:

  1. Eat a balanced and nutritious diet
  2. Take a prenatal vitamin
  3. Avoid tight hairstyles
  4. Be gentle when brushing and styling your hair
  5. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  6. Massage your scalp
  7. Try hair growth products
  8. Get plenty of rest

Tips

  • Use a silk pillowcase to minimize friction and breakage
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons
  • Consider getting a shorter haircut to minimize the appearance of hair loss
  • Don't stress too much about hair loss – it's a normal part of the postpartum experience

FAQ

Can breastfeeding cause hair loss?

Yes, breastfeeding can cause hair loss. This is because the hormones that stimulate hair growth during pregnancy are no longer present, and the body is producing more prolactin, which can cause hair loss.

How long does postpartum hair loss last?

Postpartum hair loss typically lasts for a few months to a year, but it varies from person to person.

What can I do to prevent hair loss while breastfeeding?

While you can't completely prevent hair loss while breastfeeding, you can minimize it by eating a balanced and nutritious diet, taking a prenatal vitamin, avoiding tight hairstyles, being gentle when brushing and styling your hair, using a gentle shampoo and conditioner, massaging your scalp, trying hair growth products, and getting plenty of rest.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair during and after breastfeeding is important for healthy hair growth. Follow these tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your scalp clean and healthy
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals like bleach or hair dye
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf
  • Avoid over-brushing or over-styling your hair

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can help minimize hair loss while breastfeeding and promote healthy hair growth.
