How To Cut A Mullet At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 08, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for a new hairstyle to try out at home, why not go for a classic mullet? This trendy cut has been making a comeback in recent years, and with the right tools and technique, you can easily achieve the look in the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of how to cut a mullet at home, as well as share some tips and tricks for maintaining your new 'do.

Description: What is a Mullet?

Before we dive into the how-to, let's first define what a mullet is. A mullet is a hairstyle that is short on the top and sides, with longer hair at the back. The length and shape of the mullet can vary, but typically the hair at the back is cut in a V-shape and can extend down to the shoulders or even longer. The mullet has a reputation for being a bit of a "party in the back" style, but it can also be worn in a more subtle, modern way.

How to Cut a Mullet at Home

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary supplies on hand. You'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • A mirror
  • A towel or cape to catch hair

Once you have everything you need, find a well-lit area where you can comfortably cut your hair.

Step 2: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

It's best to start with clean, dry hair so you can see the natural texture and shape of your hair. Wash and dry your hair as you normally would, and avoid using any styling products before you start cutting.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. Use your comb to make a part down the middle of your head, from your forehead down to the nape of your neck. Then, make another part from ear to ear, dividing your hair into four sections. Clip the top sections of hair up and out of the way, leaving the bottom sections loose.

Step 4: Cut the Sides and Top

Using your scissors, start cutting the sides and top of your hair. You can use either a point cutting or a blunt cutting technique, depending on the look you're going for. If you want a more modern, subtle mullet, keep the top and sides shorter and more layered. If you're going for a more traditional mullet, leave the top a bit longer and keep the layering to a minimum.

Step 5: Cut the Back

Once you've finished cutting the sides and top, it's time to move on to the back. Take down the top sections of hair and use them as a guide for cutting the back. You can use clippers to create a clean, blunt line at the bottom of the V-shape, or you can use your scissors to create a more textured, layered look.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting, take a step back and check your work. Make sure the length and shape of your mullet are even on both sides, and make any necessary adjustments. You can also use your scissors to add more texture and layering if desired.

Step 7: Style Your Mullet

Now that you've cut your mullet, it's time to style it. You can use a variety of styling products to achieve different looks, from a sleek, polished style to a messy, tousled look. Experiment with different products and techniques to find the style that works best for you.

Tips for Cutting a Mullet at Home

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up, rather than cutting off too much at once.
  • Use your fingers to guide your scissors, rather than cutting straight across.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting hair can be tricky, but with practice, you'll get the hang of it.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, consider asking a friend or family member to help.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions About Cutting a Mullet at Home

Q: Can I cut a mullet with curly hair?

A: Absolutely! In fact, curly hair can look great in a mullet, as the natural texture adds to the overall look. Just be sure to take your curl pattern into account when cutting, and consider leaving the back a bit longer to allow for shrinkage.

Q: Do I need to use clippers to cut a mullet?

A: No, clippers are optional. You can achieve a mullet with just scissors, but clippers can help create a clean, blunt line at the bottom of the V-shape.

Q: Can I fix a bad mullet cut?

A: Yes, but it may take some time and patience. If you're unhappy with your mullet cut, try adding more texture and layering to even out the length. You can also try styling your hair in a different way to disguise any unevenness.

Hair Care for Your Mullet

Now that you've cut your mullet, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for maintaining your new 'do:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use styling products that are appropriate for your hair type and desired look.
  • Get regular trims to keep your mullet looking fresh.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to cut a mullet at home like a pro. Whether you're going for a subtle modern look or a full-on party in the back, a mullet can be a fun and trendy style to try out. Happy cutting!
