How To Cut A Fuller Fringe Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 02, 2023 · 5 min read
How to Cut a Fuller Fringe - Step by Step Guide | Hair Care Tips

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, cutting a fuller fringe can be a great option. However, it's important to know the right techniques and steps to ensure that you end up with a flattering and stylish look. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting a fuller fringe, including tips and hair care advice to help you maintain your new look.

How to Cut a Fuller Fringe

Cutting a fuller fringe is all about creating layers and texture. This style works best on medium to long hair lengths, and can be customized to suit your face shape and personal style. Here are the basic steps for cutting a fuller fringe:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin cutting, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You'll need sharp hair-cutting scissors, a fine-toothed comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning off the hair that you want to cut into a fringe. Use the comb to create a triangle-shaped section at the front of your head, with the point of the triangle at the center of your forehead. Clip the rest of your hair back to keep it out of the way.

Step 3: Cut Your Fringe

Using your scissors, cut your fringe at an angle. Start at the center of your forehead and work your way outwards, cutting small sections at a time. Make sure to cut at an angle so that the hair is longer in the middle and shorter on the sides.

Step 4: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting, take a step back and check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to the length or shape of your fringe.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed guide to cutting a fuller fringe:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural texture and movement of your hair, which will make it easier to cut your fringe.

Step 2: Create a Triangle-Shaped Section

Using your comb, create a triangle-shaped section at the front of your head. The point of the triangle should be at the center of your forehead, and the base of the triangle should be at the top of your temples.

Step 3: Section Off Your Hair

Clip the rest of your hair back to keep it out of the way. Then, use your comb to create a vertical part down the middle of the triangle section. This will help you create an even fringe.

Step 4: Cut Your Fringe

Starting at the center of your forehead, use your scissors to cut small sections of hair at an angle. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at a slight angle towards the side of your head. Repeat this process on both sides of the center part.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Take a step back and check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to the length or shape of your fringe.

Tips for Cutting a Fuller Fringe

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect fuller fringe:

  • Start by cutting your fringe longer than you think you want it. You can always go back and trim it shorter if needed.
  • Use sharp hair-cutting scissors to ensure a clean and precise cut.
  • Cut your fringe when your hair is dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.
  • Take your time and cut small sections at a time to avoid any mistakes.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own fringe, consider going to a professional stylist for help.

FAQ

How often should I cut my fuller fringe?

It's best to trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and length.

Can I cut my fringe while it's wet?

We recommend cutting your fringe when your hair is dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my fringe?

If you make a mistake while cutting your fringe, don't panic! You can always go back and trim it shorter or adjust the shape as needed. If you're unsure about how to fix your mistake, consider going to a professional stylist for help.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Fuller Fringe

Once you've cut your fuller fringe, it's important to take care of it properly to ensure that it looks its best. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your fringe, as this can cause damage and split ends.
  • Use a high-quality hair oil or serum to keep your fringe smooth and shiny.
  • Consider using a dry shampoo between washes to keep your fringe looking fresh.
  • Trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and length.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and flattering fuller fringe in no time. Whether you're looking for a bold new look or just want to switch things up, cutting a fuller fringe is a great option for any hair type or face shape. So grab your scissors and get ready to transform your look!
