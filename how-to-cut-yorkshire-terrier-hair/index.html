How To Cut Yorkshire Terrier Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you own a Yorkshire Terrier, you know how important it is to keep their hair well-groomed. However, taking your pup to the groomer can be expensive and time-consuming. That's why learning how to cut your Yorkshire Terrier's hair at home can be a great way to save money and bond with your furry friend. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut Yorkshire Terrier hair at home.

How to Cut Yorkshire Terrier Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting your Yorkshire Terrier's hair, make sure you have all the necessary supplies. You'll need a pair of scissors, clippers, a comb, and a brush. It's also a good idea to have some treats handy to reward your pup for good behavior.

Step 2: Brush and Comb Your Dog's Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your dog's hair is free of tangles and mats. Use a brush and comb to gently work through any knots.

Step 3: Trim the Hair Around the Ears

Start by trimming the hair around your dog's ears. Use your scissors to carefully trim any long hairs that are sticking out. Make sure to be gentle and avoid cutting your dog's skin.

Step 4: Trim the Hair Around the Eyes

Next, use your scissors to trim the hair around your dog's eyes. Be careful not to get too close to the eyes, as this can be dangerous for your pup.

Step 5: Trim the Hair on the Body

Now it's time to trim the hair on your dog's body. Use your clippers to trim the hair on your dog's back, sides, and belly. Start with a longer blade and gradually switch to a shorter one to achieve the desired length.

Step 6: Trim the Hair on the Legs

Finally, use your scissors to trim the hair on your dog's legs. Be careful not to cut too much, as this can be painful for your pup. Use your comb to guide you as you trim.

Step 7: Reward Your Dog

Once you've finished cutting your dog's hair, make sure to reward them for their good behavior. Give them a treat and some extra love and attention to show them how much you appreciate them.

Tips for Cutting Yorkshire Terrier Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your Yorkshire Terrier's hair:

  • Start with small snips and gradually work your way up to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a high-quality pair of scissors and clippers to ensure a clean cut.
  • Be patient and take breaks as needed to avoid stressing out your pup.
  • Consider using a grooming table to make it easier to reach all areas of your dog's body.

FAQs About Cutting Yorkshire Terrier Hair

How often should I cut my Yorkshire Terrier's hair?

It's recommended to cut your Yorkshire Terrier's hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking healthy and well-groomed.

Can I cut my Yorkshire Terrier's hair if they have matted fur?

If your dog has matted fur, it's best to take them to a professional groomer to avoid causing any pain or discomfort.

What should I do if I accidentally cut my dog's skin?

If you accidentally cut your dog's skin, stop immediately and apply pressure to the area to stop the bleeding. If the cut is deep or won't stop bleeding, seek veterinary attention.

Yorkshire Terrier Hair Care

Keeping your Yorkshire Terrier's hair well-groomed is an important part of their overall health and well-being. In addition to regular haircuts, make sure to brush and comb your dog's hair regularly to avoid tangles and mats. You should also bathe your dog every 4-6 weeks to keep their coat clean and healthy.

By following these tips and guidelines, you can ensure that your Yorkshire Terrier looks and feels their best. Happy grooming!
