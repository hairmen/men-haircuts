What Hair Colour Suits Your Skin Tone A Guide To Choosing The Right Shade

Written by Luffy Jul 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Choosing the right hair colour can be a daunting task, especially if you're not sure what suits your skin tone. With so many shades and hues to choose from, it's easy to get overwhelmed. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of identifying your skin tone and choosing the perfect hair colour to complement it.

  • Hair Colours for Warm Skin Tones
  • Hair Colours for Cool Skin Tones
  • Hair Colours for Neutral Skin Tones

  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Identify Your Skin Tone

The first step in choosing the right hair colour is to identify your skin tone. There are three main skin tones: warm, cool, and neutral.

If your skin has a yellow or golden undertone, you have a warm skin tone. If your skin has a pink or blue undertone, you have a cool skin tone. If your skin has a mix of both, you have a neutral skin tone.

The easiest way to identify your skin tone is to look at the veins on your wrist. If they appear green, you have a warm skin tone. If they appear blue or purple, you have a cool skin tone. If they appear both green and blue, you have a neutral skin tone.

Hair Colours for Warm Skin Tones

If you have a warm skin tone, you should opt for hair colours that have golden, caramel, or honey undertones. These colours complement your skin tone by bringing out the warmth in it. Some hair colours that work well for warm skin tones include:

  • Golden blonde
  • Caramel brown
  • Honey blonde
  • Copper
  • Auburn

Hair Colours for Cool Skin Tones

If you have a cool skin tone, you should opt for hair colours that have ashy or cool undertones. These colours complement your skin tone by bringing out the coolness in it. Some hair colours that work well for cool skin tones include:

  • Platinum blonde
  • Ash brown
  • Silver
  • Jet black
  • Burgundy

Hair Colours for Neutral Skin Tones

If you have a neutral skin tone, you can pull off a wide range of hair colours. Lucky you! Some hair colours that work well for neutral skin tones include:

  • Chocolate brown
  • Blonde highlights
  • Warm red
  • Caramel balayage
  • Black with a hint of blue

Tips for Choosing the Right Hair Colour

When choosing the right hair colour, keep these tips in mind:

  • Consider your lifestyle. If you have a busy schedule, opt for a low-maintenance hair colour.
  • Think about your natural hair colour. If you're going for a drastic change, keep in mind that it may require more upkeep.
  • Consult with a professional. A hair stylist can help you choose the right hair colour based on your skin tone and lifestyle.
  • Choose a hair colour that complements your eye colour. For example, if you have blue eyes, consider a cool-toned hair colour.

FAQ

What if I have a warm skin tone but want to go blonde?

Opt for a golden blonde or honey blonde. Avoid platinum or cool-toned blonde shades.

Can I dye my hair a different colour if I have a warm skin tone?

Of course! You can dye your hair any colour you like. Just keep in mind that certain colours may complement your skin tone better than others.

What if I have a neutral skin tone?

Lucky you! You can pull off a wide range of hair colours. Just keep in mind the tips we mentioned above.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've chosen the perfect hair colour, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's formulated for coloured hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip the colour.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as this can damage your hair and fade the colour.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Now that you know what hair colour suits your skin tone, it's time to book that appointment with your hair stylist. Whether you're going for a subtle change or a bold transformation, choosing the right hair colour can make all the difference in how you look and feel.
