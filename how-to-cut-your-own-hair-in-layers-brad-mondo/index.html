How To Cut Your Own Hair In Layers Brad Mondo s Guide

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon just to get a simple layer cut? Well, you're in luck because you can easily cut your own hair in layers at home with the help of Brad Mondo's expert tips and tricks. Follow this step-by-step guide to achieve the perfect layered haircut without leaving your house.

Description

A layered haircut is a timeless and versatile hairstyle that can work for any hair type and length. It adds dimension, movement, and texture to your hair, making it look more voluminous and stylish. However, getting a layer cut at a salon can be expensive and time-consuming, especially if you have a busy schedule or a tight budget. That's why learning how to cut your own hair in layers is a useful skill that can save you money, time, and hassle.

How to Cut Your Own Hair in Layers: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first thing you need to do is gather all the necessary tools and materials for cutting your hair. You will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean to ensure a precise and even cut. You can also use thinning shears or razor scissors for a softer and more textured look.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner, and then towel dry it until it's slightly damp. Avoid cutting wet hair, as it can lead to uneven and unpredictable results.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections to make it easier to cut and control. Start by parting your hair in the middle or on the side, and then divide it into two equal sections. Clip the top section away from the bottom section, and then divide the bottom section into two more sections.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start cutting the bottom layer of your hair by taking a small section of hair from one side and combing it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and then cut it straight across at the desired length. Repeat this technique on the other side, and then check if both sides are even.

Step 5: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the top section of your hair and clip the bottom layer away. Take a small section of hair from the middle layer and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers, and then cut it at an angle towards the ends of the hair. This will create a soft and seamless transition between the bottom and top layers.

Step 6: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, release the bottom layer of your hair and clip the middle layer away. Take a small section of hair from the top layer and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers, and then cut it at an angle towards the ends of the hair. This will create the longest and most visible layer of your hair, giving it a natural and bouncy look.

Step 7: Blend and Texturize

After cutting all the layers, blend and texturize your hair by taking small sections of hair and cutting them diagonally or vertically. This will remove any harsh lines or unevenness in your hair, and create a more natural and effortless look.

Step 8: Check and Adjust

Check your hair in the mirror from all angles to make sure it's even and balanced. If you notice any unevenness or mistakes, you can always trim or adjust it accordingly. However, be careful not to overcut or ruin your haircut by trying to fix minor imperfections.

Step 9: Style Your Hair

Once you're satisfied with your layer cut, style your hair as desired. You can use a blow dryer, straightener, curling iron, or any other styling tool to create different looks and textures. Experiment with different hairstyles and products to find the best one for your hair type and personality.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair in Layers

  • Start with small and gradual cuts, and don't cut too much hair at once.
  • Use a comb and scissors instead of electric clippers or trimmers, as they are more precise and versatile.
  • Don't rush or force the cutting process, and take breaks if needed.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help you if you're not confident or experienced enough to cut your hair alone.
  • Watch tutorials and videos on YouTube or social media to learn from professional hairdressers and stylists.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair in layers if I have curly or wavy hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair in layers regardless of your hair texture or type. However, keep in mind that curly or wavy hair tends to shrink when dry, so you may need to cut it longer than desired to achieve the desired length and shape.

What if I make a mistake or cut my hair too short?

If you make a mistake or cut your hair too short, don't panic. You can always wait for it to grow back, or try to fix it by trimming the ends or adding more layers. However, it's always better to be cautious and start with small cuts, especially if you're new to cutting your own hair.

How often should I cut my hair in layers?

The frequency of cutting your hair in layers depends on your hair growth rate and personal preferences. Generally, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy and vibrant hair. However, you can also trim your hair at home between salon visits to keep it looking fresh and neat.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Haircuts

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and frizz.
  • Avoid using too much heat or styling products that can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask or leave-in conditioner once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Get regular trims and cuts to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and pollution by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective spray.

With the help of Brad Mondo's expert advice and these step-by-step instructions, you can achieve a flawless and fashionable layered haircut that suits your style and personality.
