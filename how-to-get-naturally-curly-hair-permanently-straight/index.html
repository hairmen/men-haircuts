How To Get Naturally Curly Hair Permanently Straight

Written by Robby Aug 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have naturally curly hair but would love to switch things up and have it straight all the time, there are a few options available to you. While there are many temporary methods of straightening your hair, such as using a flat iron or blow-drying it straight, these methods require daily effort and can be damaging to your hair in the long run. In this article, we will discuss how to get naturally curly hair permanently straight, so you can enjoy straight hair without the daily upkeep.

Description

Getting naturally curly hair permanently straight involves altering the chemical structure of your hair. This can be done through chemical treatments such as relaxing, keratin treatments, or Japanese hair straightening. Each method has its own pros and cons, and it's important to consider your hair type and condition before deciding which method is best for you.

How to Get Naturally Curly Hair Permanently Straight

1. Chemical Relaxer

A chemical relaxer is a common method used to permanently straighten curly hair. The process involves applying a chemical solution to your hair that breaks down the bonds that give your hair its shape. This allows your hair to be molded straight while the solution is on your hair, and then neutralized to lock in the straight shape. It's important to note that chemical relaxers can be damaging to your hair, so it's important to follow the instructions carefully and to have a professional perform the treatment.

2. Keratin Treatment

A keratin treatment involves applying a keratin protein solution to your hair that is then sealed in with heat. This treatment can help to smooth out curls and frizz, making your hair appear straighter. It's important to note that while keratin treatments are less damaging than chemical relaxers, they still involve the use of chemicals and heat, which can be damaging to your hair over time.

3. Japanese Hair Straightening

Japanese hair straightening involves using a chemical solution to break down the bonds in your hair, similar to a chemical relaxer. However, this method involves more steps and is often more expensive. The solution is applied to your hair, then neutralized, and then a flat iron is used to lock in the straight shape. Japanese hair straightening is often more effective than other methods, but it can also be more damaging to your hair.

Step by Step

While the exact steps for each method of getting naturally curly hair permanently straight will vary depending on the product and technique used, here is a general overview of what to expect:

1. Consultation

Before undergoing any chemical treatment, it's important to have a consultation with a professional stylist. They can assess the health of your hair and recommend the best treatment for your hair type and condition.

2. Preparation

Before the treatment, your hair will need to be washed and dried. This will ensure that the product can penetrate your hair properly.

3. Application

The product will be applied to your hair in sections, starting at the roots and working down to the ends. The stylist will ensure that the product is evenly distributed and that all areas are covered.

4. Processing

Depending on the product and technique used, the processing time can vary. Typically, you will need to leave the product on your hair for a set amount of time to allow it to do its job.

5. Neutralizing

After the processing time is complete, the product will need to be neutralized. This will help to lock in the straight shape and prevent any further damage to your hair.

6. Styling

Once the treatment is complete, your hair will be rinsed and styled. You may need to avoid washing your hair for a few days to allow the product to fully set in.

Tips

Here are a few tips for maintaining your newly straightened hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair for the first few days after the treatment.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent further damage to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQ

Is it safe to get my hair permanently straightened?

While the process of getting naturally curly hair permanently straight can be damaging to your hair, it is generally safe if done by a professional and if you take proper care of your hair afterwards.

How long will the results last?

The results of a chemical treatment can last anywhere from a few months to a year or more, depending on the product and technique used. Keratin treatments tend to last around 3-6 months, while Japanese hair straightening can last up to a year or more.

Can I color my hair after getting it permanently straightened?

It's best to wait at least a week after getting your hair straightened before coloring it. This will give your hair time to recover from the chemical treatment.

Hair Care

It's important to take good care of your hair after getting it permanently straightened to prevent further damage. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, and get regular trims to keep your hair healthy. With proper care, you can enjoy your straight hair for months to come.
