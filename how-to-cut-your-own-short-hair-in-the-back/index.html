How To Cut Your Own Short Hair In The Back

If you're looking to save some money and cut your own short hair in the back, you've come to the right place! In this article, we'll go over step-by-step instructions on how to cut your own short hair in the back, as well as tips, FAQ, and hair care. Let's get started!

How to Cut Your Own Short Hair in the Back

First, you'll need some tools to get started. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, a mirror, and hair clips. Once you've gathered your tools, it's time to start cutting!

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Start by washing your hair and letting it air-dry. This will give you a better idea of how your hair naturally falls.

Step 2: Comb your hair straight down the back and divide it into two sections. Use hair clips to secure each section in place.

Step 3: Take one section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the scissors at a slight angle and start cutting at the desired length. Remember to cut a little bit at a time to avoid mistakes.

Step 4: Repeat step 3 on the other section of hair.

Step 5: Look in the mirror and check for any uneven spots. Make any necessary adjustments by trimming small sections of hair at a time.

Step 6: Once you're happy with the length and shape, release the hair clips and comb your hair to make sure everything looks even.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Short Hair in the Back

  • Start with a longer length and work your way shorter. It's easier to make adjustments and cut more hair off than it is to add hair back.
  • Use hair clips to keep hair out of the way and avoid cutting any unwanted sections.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process. Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with patience and practice, you'll get the hang of it.
  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. Dull scissors can cause uneven cuts and damage to your hair.

FAQ

Can I cut my own short hair in the back with clippers?

Yes, you can use clippers to cut your own short hair in the back. However, clippers are better suited for shorter hairstyles like buzz cuts or fades.

How often should I cut my own short hair in the back?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and the desired length. On average, it's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my own short hair in the back?

Don't panic! Hair grows back, and mistakes can be fixed. If you're unsure, it's best to trim a little bit at a time and make adjustments as you go.

Hair Care Tips After Cutting Your Own Short Hair in the Back

  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons, which can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and length of your haircut.
  • Consider using hair masks or oils to nourish and protect your hair.

Now that you know how to cut your own short hair in the back, you can save money and have more control over your hairstyle. Remember to take your time, use proper tools, and practice patience. Happy cutting!
