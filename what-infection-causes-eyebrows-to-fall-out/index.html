What Infection Causes Eyebrows to Fall Out?

Are you experiencing hair loss in your eyebrows? It could be a result of an infection. In this article, we will discuss the different types of infections that can cause eyebrow hair loss, how to prevent it, and tips on how to take care of your eyebrows.

Table of Content:

  • Types of Infections
  • Prevention
  • Eyebrow Hair Care
  • FAQs

Types of Infections

There are different types of infections that can cause eyebrow hair loss. The most common ones are:

Bacterial Infection

A bacterial infection can occur when bacteria enter the hair follicles of the eyebrows. This can cause inflammation, leading to hair loss. Symptoms include redness, swelling, and pain around the eyebrows.

Fungal Infection

A fungal infection can also cause hair loss in the eyebrows. It can happen when there is an overgrowth of fungi on the skin. Symptoms include itching, flaking, and scaling of the skin around the eyebrows.

Viral Infection

A viral infection can cause hair loss in the eyebrows. This is usually a result of an autoimmune disorder, where the immune system attacks the hair follicles. Symptoms include the sudden loss of hair in the eyebrows.

Prevention

Preventing infections that cause eyebrow hair loss can be done by practicing good hygiene. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hands frequently.
  • Avoid touching your face with dirty hands.
  • Do not share personal items such as towels, combs, and brushes.
  • Clean your makeup brushes regularly.
  • Avoid using expired makeup products.

Eyebrow Hair Care

Taking care of your eyebrows is essential to prevent hair loss. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid over-plucking your eyebrows.
  • Do not use harsh chemicals on your eyebrows.
  • Use a gentle cleanser to wash your face.
  • Moisturize your eyebrows with a natural oil such as castor oil.
  • Avoid rubbing your eyebrows too hard when washing your face.

FAQs

Can stress cause eyebrow hair loss?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss in the eyebrows. It can lead to a condition called telogen effluvium, where hair falls out due to stress.

Will eyebrow hair grow back after an infection?

It depends on the severity of the infection. In mild cases, the hair will grow back after the infection is treated. However, in severe cases, the hair may not grow back.

Can antibiotics treat an infected eyebrow follicle?

Yes, antibiotics can treat bacterial infections that cause eyebrow hair loss. It is important to consult a doctor before taking antibiotics.
