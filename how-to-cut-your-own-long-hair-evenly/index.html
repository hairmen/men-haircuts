How To Cut Your Own Long Hair Evenly A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 17, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to save some money and time by cutting your own long hair, you might be intimidated by the thought of it. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can cut your hair evenly and achieve the look you want. In this step-by-step guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about cutting your own long hair.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You Need
  • Preparation
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Cutting the Back of Your Hair
  • Tips for a Professional-Looking Cut
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Cutting

Tools You Need

Before you start cutting your own hair, you need to make sure you have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • A pair of sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • Clips to section your hair
  • A handheld mirror
  • A large mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts. You can buy a pair of hair-cutting scissors at your local beauty supply store or online.

Preparation

Before you start cutting your hair, you need to prepare it by washing and drying it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

Additionally, you may want to wear a button-up shirt or a towel around your shoulders to protect your clothes from hair clippings.

Sectioning Your Hair

The key to cutting your own hair evenly is to section it properly. Start by separating your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use clips to hold each section in place.

Then, use your comb to create a part down the center of your head, separating the front and back sections. Use clips to hold each section in place.

Cutting Your Hair

Now it's time to start cutting your hair. Start with one of the front sections and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to cut the ends of your hair, using the length you want to achieve as a guide.

Once you've cut one side, repeat the process on the other side to ensure they're even. Then, move on to the back sections and cut them in the same way.

Cutting the Back of Your Hair

Cutting the back of your hair can be tricky, as you can't see what you're doing. To make it easier, use your handheld mirror to see the back of your head while you're cutting.

If you're struggling to cut the back of your hair evenly, you may want to ask a friend or family member to help you.

Tips for a Professional-Looking Cut

Here are some tips to help you achieve a professional-looking cut:

  • Make sure your scissors are sharp
  • Cut small sections at a time
  • Use a comb to guide your scissors
  • Take breaks to check your progress
  • Don't cut your hair too short - you can always cut more later

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if it's curly. However, keep in mind that curly hair can shrink up to an inch when it dries, so make sure you account for this when cutting your hair.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Should I cut my hair when it's wet or dry?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, make sure to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. This includes washing and conditioning your hair regularly, using a heat protectant when styling your hair, and avoiding harsh chemicals and heat tools.

With these tips and techniques, you can cut your own long hair evenly and achieve the look you want. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it!
