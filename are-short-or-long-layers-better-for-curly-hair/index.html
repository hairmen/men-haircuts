Are Short Or Long Layers Better For Curly Hair

Written by Christine Jul 31, 2023 · 4 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it looks absolutely gorgeous when styled correctly, it can also be a nightmare to manage. One of the most common ways to deal with curly hair is to get layers. But the question is, are short or long layers better for curly hair?

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Layers can help add volume, texture, and dimension to curly hair. They can also help make curls more manageable and easier to style. However, the length of the layers can make a big difference in how your curls look.

Short layers are typically cut closer to the crown of the head, while long layers are cut further down. Short layers can help add volume and lift to the roots, which can be especially beneficial for those with thin or fine hair. Long layers, on the other hand, can help create a more seamless, blended look and can help weigh down curls that are too bouncy or unruly.

How to

When deciding whether to get short or long layers, consider your hair type, face shape, and personal style. Short layers work well for those with round or heart-shaped faces, while long layers can complement those with oval or square-shaped faces.

If you have fine or thin curly hair, opt for short layers to add volume and lift to the roots. For those with thicker or coarser curly hair, long layers can help weigh down curls and create a more natural, effortless look.

Step by step

If you're getting layers for the first time, it's important to communicate your desired length and style with your stylist. Bring in pictures or inspiration to ensure that you're both on the same page.

When cutting short layers, your stylist will typically section off the top portion of your hair and cut it shorter than the bottom layers. This creates a layered, voluminous look. For long layers, your stylist will typically cut the layers further down the length of your hair, creating a more blended, natural look.

Tips

Regardless of whether you opt for short or long layers, it's important to maintain your curly hair with regular trims and deep conditioning treatments. This will help prevent split ends and dryness, which can make curly hair look frizzy and unruly.

Consider using a diffuser when blow-drying your hair, as this can help enhance your curls and reduce frizz. Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can break up your curls and create more frizz.

FAQ

Q: Can I get layers if I have naturally curly hair?

A: Yes, layers can help enhance and define your natural curls. However, it's important to find a stylist who is experienced in cutting curly hair to avoid any disasters.

Q: Will short layers make my hair look too poofy?

A: Short layers can add volume and lift to the roots, but this can be controlled with proper styling techniques and products. Consider using a volumizing mousse or spray to help define and hold your curls.

Hair Care

To keep your curly hair looking its best, it's important to use products specifically designed for curly hair. Look for sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners, as sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause dryness and frizz.

Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and hydrate your curls. This can be especially beneficial for those with dry or damaged hair.

When styling your curly hair, consider using a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define and hold your curls. Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

By following these tips and choosing the right length of layers for your hair type and face shape, you can achieve gorgeous, manageable curls that are the envy of all your friends.
