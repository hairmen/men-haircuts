How To Cut Ladies Hair In Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your long, dull hair and want to add some life and movement to it? The perfect solution is to cut your hair in layers. Not only does it give texture and volume to your locks, but it also adds dimension and shape to your face. However, going to a salon can be expensive, and you might not always get the desired results. So why not try cutting your hair at home? In this blog post, we’ll guide you on how to cut ladies hair in layers, with some tips and tricks along the way.

How to Cut Ladies Hair in Layers

Cutting hair in layers is a popular technique used by hairstylists to add depth and volume to hair. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to cut ladies hair in layers:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before starting, ensure your hair is clean and dry. This will help you see the natural fall of your hair and allow you to make more precise cuts.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Section your hair into four parts: the front, back, and two sides. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Start with the Back Section

Start with the back section, and take a small section of hair from the bottom. Hold it between your fingers and comb it straight down. Cut the hair in a straight line, perpendicular to the floor.

Step 4: Cut the Rest of the Back Section

After cutting the first section, take another section of hair above it, and align it with the previously cut hair. Cut it in the same way, making sure it's even. Continue doing this until you've cut the entire back section.

Step 5: Move on to the Sides

Unclip one of the side sections and comb it down. Cut the hair at a 45-degree angle, starting from the front and working towards the back. Make sure you cut the hair in a straight line, parallel to the floor. Repeat this on the other side.

Step 6: Cut the Front Layers

Take the front section and comb it straight down. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the chin and working towards the back. Make sure you cut the hair in a straight line, parallel to the floor.

Step 7: Blend the Layers

Once you’ve cut all the layers, comb through your hair to blend them. This will ensure that the layers flow seamlessly into each other.

Step 8: Trim the Ends

Trim the ends to your desired length. You can either cut them straight across or give them a slight curve for a softer look.

Step 9: Style Your Hair

Your hair is now ready to style. You can use a curling iron or straightener to create waves or straighten it.

Tips for Cutting Ladies Hair in Layers

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting ladies hair in layers:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure clean cuts.
  • Don’t cut too much hair in one go. It’s better to cut less and go back if needed.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting hair in layers can be time-consuming, but the results are worth it.

FAQs

1. Can I cut my hair in layers with regular scissors?

While you can use regular scissors, it’s better to use sharp hairdressing scissors. This will ensure clean cuts and prevent split ends.

2. How often should I cut my hair in layers?

You should trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the layers and prevent split ends.

3. Can I cut my hair in layers if it’s curly?

Yes, you can cut curly hair in layers. However, it’s best to leave this to a professional stylist who has experience with curly hair.

Hair Care after Cutting Layers

After cutting your hair in layers, it’s essential to take care of it. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the layers and prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you can now confidently cut ladies hair in layers at home. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and use sharp scissors. Happy cutting!
