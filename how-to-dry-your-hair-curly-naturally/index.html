How To Dry Your Hair Curly Naturally A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jun 30, 2023 · 4 min read

In the world of hair care, curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it’s beautiful and unique. On the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style. One of the biggest challenges for those with curly hair is drying it. Blow drying can lead to frizz and damage, but air drying can take forever. That’s why we’ve put together this step-by-step guide on how to dry your hair curly naturally. Read on for tips and tricks to get the perfect, frizz-free curls.

How to Dry Your Hair Curly Naturally

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, it’s important to understand the basics of drying curly hair. First and foremost, you should avoid using a towel to dry your hair. The friction can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess water. Secondly, it’s important to use the right products. A good leave-in conditioner or curl cream can help define your curls and keep them looking great.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that we’ve covered the basics, let’s get into the step-by-step guide for drying curly hair naturally.

  1. After washing your hair, gently squeeze out excess water with a microfiber towel or old t-shirt. Do not rub your hair dry.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  3. Divide your hair into sections. The number of sections will depend on the thickness and length of your hair. For shorter hair, you may only need two sections. For longer hair, you may need four or more.
  4. Twist each section of hair into a loose coil. You can use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to help define the curls.
  5. Allow your hair to air dry completely. This may take several hours, so be patient.
  6. Once your hair is completely dry, gently shake out the curls to add volume.

Tips for Drying Curly Hair

Drying curly hair naturally can be tricky, but there are a few tips and tricks that can help make the process easier and more effective.

  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to help define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Avoid touching your hair while it’s drying, as this can cause frizz.
  • Consider sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent frizz.
  • Refresh your curls throughout the day with a curl refreshing spray or water mixed with a leave-in conditioner.

FAQ

Can I dry my hair curly naturally overnight?

Yes, you can. Simply follow the same steps as outlined above and sleep with your hair in the loose coils. Be sure to use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz.

How often should I wash my curly hair?

This will depend on your hair type and lifestyle. Some people with curly hair can go a week or longer without washing, while others may need to wash every other day. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Can I use a hair dryer to dry my curly hair?

Yes, but be sure to use a diffuser attachment and keep the heat on low to reduce frizz.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Drying your hair curly naturally is just one part of a comprehensive hair care routine for those with curly hair. Here are a few tips to keep your curls looking their best:

  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, as they can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle.

By following these tips and using the right products, you can achieve beautiful, healthy curls without the need for harsh chemicals or damaging heat tools.
