Why Does My Hair Curl Outwards

Written by Christine Oct 27, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you tired of dealing with hair that constantly curls outwards? It can be frustrating to spend time styling your hair, only to have it curl in the opposite direction of your desired look. In this blog post, we’ll explore the reasons why your hair curls outwards and provide tips on how to manage this common issue.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

When we talk about hair curling outwards, we’re referring to hair that naturally curves away from the head instead of towards it. This can happen to all types of hair, from straight to curly. The reason for this phenomenon lies in the shape of the hair follicle. Hair that grows from a follicle that is more oval-shaped tends to curl more easily than hair that grows from a round follicle. Additionally, hair that is dryer or damaged is more prone to curling outwards.

How to

If you’re tired of dealing with hair that curls outwards, there are a few things you can do to manage it. First, make sure you’re using the right hair products. Look for products that are designed to smooth and straighten hair, such as a straightening serum or cream. Additionally, try blow-drying your hair with a round brush, pulling the hair inwards as you dry it. This can help to train the hair to curl inwards instead of outwards.

Step by Step

1. Wash your hair with a smoothing shampoo and conditioner.
2. Apply a straightening serum or cream to damp hair.
3. Blow-dry your hair with a round brush, pulling the hair inwards as you dry it.
4. Finish with a straightening iron or flat iron if desired.

Tips

- Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush, as brushes can cause hair to tangle and curl outwards.
- Avoid using heat-styling tools too often, as they can cause damage and make hair more prone to curling outwards.
- Consider getting a haircut with long layers, as this can help to weigh down the hair and prevent it from curling outwards.

FAQ

Q: Can genetics play a role in hair curling outwards?
A: Yes, genetics can play a role in the shape of your hair follicles, which can affect how your hair curls.

Q: Can humidity make hair curl outwards?
A: Yes, high humidity can cause hair to become frizzy and curl outwards.

Q: Can using too much hair product make hair curl outwards?
A: Yes, using too much product can weigh down the hair and cause it to curl outwards.

Hair Care

To prevent hair from curling outwards, it’s important to take good care of your hair. This means using products that are designed for your hair type and avoiding heat-styling tools too often. Additionally, consider getting regular haircuts to keep split ends at bay and prevent damage. With the right care and styling techniques, you can achieve the look you want for your hair.
