How To Cut Curly Girl Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Are you a curly girl looking to trim your own hair at home? It can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut curly girl hair at home.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Styling Your Hair
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

Tools You'll Need

The first step in cutting curly girl hair at home is making sure you have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Clips to section your hair
  • A handheld mirror or a mirror you can position behind you

Preparation

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Brush or comb your hair to remove any tangles. It's also a good idea to wear an old shirt or towel to avoid getting hair all over your clothes.

Sectioning Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use clips to keep each section separate. Begin with one of the back sections and unclip it. Take a small section of hair from the back and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and use your fingers as a guide to cut the hair. Cut about 1/2 inch to 1 inch at a time, and be sure to cut straight across.

Cutting Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting the back section, move on to the front sections. Starting with one of the front sections, unclip it and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and use your fingers as a guide to cut the hair. Be sure to cut straight across. Repeat on the other side.

Styling Your Hair

After you've finished cutting your hair, it's time to style it. Apply your favorite styling products, and use a diffuser to dry your hair. If you need to touch up any areas, do so carefully with your scissors.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

  • Remember to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink up to 2 inches when it dries.
  • Take your time and cut small sections at a time.
  • Be sure to cut straight across, as curly hair tends to bounce up when cut at an angle.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, start by trimming just the ends and work your way up to a full cut.

FAQs

Can I cut my curly hair with regular scissors?

No, it's important to use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean and even cut.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 3-4 months to maintain healthy ends.

Should I cut my hair when it's wet or dry?

You should always cut curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink up to 2 inches when it dries.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair is important to maintain healthy and beautiful locks. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to help define your curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as this can damage your hair. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and set your tools to a low heat setting.

Now that you know how to cut curly girl hair at home, you can save time and money by doing it yourself. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow our tips for the best results.
