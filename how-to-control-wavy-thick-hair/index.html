How To Control Wavy Thick Hair Tips And Tricks

Wavy thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it adds volume, texture, and movement to your hair, it can also be difficult to manage and style. In this post, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to control wavy thick hair and achieve the look you want.

How to Control Wavy Thick Hair

Controlling wavy thick hair starts with finding the right products and techniques that work for your hair type. Here are some steps to follow:

1. Wash your hair less often

Wavy thick hair tends to be dry, so washing it too often can strip it of its natural oils and make it frizzy. Aim to wash your hair no more than twice a week.

2. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner

When you do wash your hair, use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to help hydrate your hair and reduce frizz. Look for products that contain natural oils like argan or coconut oil.

3. Apply a leave-in conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help detangle and moisturize your hair. This will also help protect your hair from heat styling.

4. Use a wide-tooth comb

When combing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to avoid pulling and damaging your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up to avoid tangles and breakage.

5. Blow-dry with a diffuser

When blow-drying your hair, use a diffuser attachment to help reduce frizz and enhance your natural waves. Use a low heat setting and avoid over-drying your hair.

6. Style with a curling iron or wand

If you want to define your waves or create more curls, use a curling iron or wand. Wrap small sections of hair around the barrel and hold for a few seconds. Release and repeat until you've styled all of your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to control wavy thick hair:

  1. Wash your hair no more than twice a week with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to detangle and moisturize your hair.
  3. Use a wide-tooth comb to comb your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  4. Blow-dry your hair with a diffuser attachment on a low heat setting.
  5. If desired, style your hair with a curling iron or wand to define your waves or create more curls.

Tips for Managing Wavy Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing wavy thick hair:

  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol or sulfates, as they can dry out your hair.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to help reduce frizz and breakage.
  • Invest in a good quality hairbrush or comb to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners or curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep your hair healthy.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about managing wavy thick hair:

What are some good hairstyles for wavy thick hair?

Some good hairstyles for wavy thick hair include long layers, beachy waves, and messy buns. These styles work with the natural texture of your hair and require minimal styling.

How do I prevent my wavy thick hair from getting frizzy?

To prevent your wavy thick hair from getting frizzy, avoid over-washing your hair, use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and apply a leave-in conditioner after washing. You can also use a hair serum or oil to help tame frizz.

Hair Care for Wavy Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your wavy thick hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners or curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and tricks, you can learn how to control wavy thick hair and achieve the look you want. Remember to be patient and experiment with different products and techniques until you find what works best for you.
