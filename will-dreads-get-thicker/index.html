Will Dreads Get Thicker A Comprehensive Guide

Are you someone who's been rocking dreadlocks for a while now, but are wondering if they'll ever get thicker? Or maybe you're considering getting dreadlocks and want to know if they'll thicken up over time? Either way, you've come to the right place.

Description

Dreadlocks, also known as dreads or locs, are a hairstyle that involves tightly coiled strands of hair. They can be created through a variety of methods, such as backcombing, twisting, or simply allowing hair to mat together naturally over time. However, one common question that many people have is whether or not their dreads will get thicker over time.

The answer to this question is yes and no. While your dreads may appear thicker as they mature and lengthen, the thickness of each individual dread typically remains the same. That being said, there are steps you can take to help make your dreads appear thicker and fuller.

How to Make Your Dreads Thicker

If you're looking to make your dreads thicker, there are a few things you can do:

  • Use a thicker gauge of hair when creating your dreads
  • Backcomb your dreads to create more volume
  • Use a crochet hook to tighten your dreads and create a denser appearance
  • Avoid using conditioner or other products that can weigh down your hair

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to make your dreads thicker:

  1. Start by using a thicker gauge of hair when creating your dreads. This will help ensure that each dread is as thick as possible from the beginning.
  2. Once your dreads have been created, use a backcombing technique to create more volume. Take a small section of hair from your dread and comb it back towards your scalp using a fine-toothed comb. Repeat this process on each dread until you've achieved your desired level of volume.
  3. Next, use a crochet hook to tighten your dreads and create a denser appearance. Insert the hook at the base of your dread and pull it through to the tip, repeating this process until your dread is as tight as you want it to be.
  4. Avoid using conditioner or other products that can weigh down your hair. This will help ensure that your dreads stay as thick as possible.

Tips for Thicker Dreads

Here are a few additional tips for making your dreads appear thicker:

  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can cause your dreads to unravel and become thinner over time.
  • Consider using a sea salt spray to add texture and volume to your hair.
  • If you're struggling with thinning dreads, consider adding in extensions or using a dreadlock thickening product to help give your hair a boost.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about dreadlocks and thickness:

Will my dreads get thicker over time?

While your dreads may appear thicker as they mature and lengthen, the thickness of each individual dread typically remains the same.

Can I make my dreads thicker?

Yes, there are steps you can take to help make your dreads appear thicker and fuller. These include using a thicker gauge of hair, backcombing, using a crochet hook to tighten your dreads, and avoiding products that can weigh down your hair.

What causes dreads to thin out?

Several factors can contribute to thinning dreads, including over-washing, using products that weigh down your hair, and not properly maintaining your dreads.

Hair Care for Thick Dreads

Once you've achieved thicker dreads, it's important to take care of them properly to keep them looking their best. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can cause your dreads to unravel and become thinner over time. Instead, aim to wash your hair once or twice a week.
  • Use a residue-free shampoo specifically designed for dreadlocks to help keep them clean and healthy.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or oil to help keep your dreads moisturized and prevent them from drying out.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can cause damage to your dreads and make them thinner over time.

In conclusion, while your dreads may not necessarily get thicker over time, there are steps you can take to help make them appear fuller and more voluminous. By using a thicker gauge of hair, backcombing, and avoiding products that can weigh down your hair, you can achieve the thick, luscious dreadlocks you've always wanted.
