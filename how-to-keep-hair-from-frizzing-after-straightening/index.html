How To Keep Hair From Frizzing After Straightening Tips And Tricks

Written by William Oct 22, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, straightening it can be a great way to change up your look. However, one of the biggest challenges with straightening hair is keeping it from frizzing. In this article, we’ll share some tips and tricks to help you keep your hair smooth and sleek after straightening.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Keep Hair from Frizzing after Straightening

The key to keeping hair from frizzing after straightening is to seal the hair cuticle. When the hair cuticle is smooth and flat, it reflects light and looks shiny and smooth. When the cuticle is rough or raised, it can cause the hair to look dull and frizzy.

Here are some steps you can take to keep your hair smooth and frizz-free after straightening:

1. Use a heat protectant

Before you start straightening your hair, make sure to apply a heat protectant spray or serum. This will help protect your hair from damage and keep it looking healthy and shiny.

2. Use a high-quality straightener

Invest in a high-quality straightener that has ceramic or tourmaline plates. These materials help to distribute heat evenly, reducing the risk of damage and frizz.

3. Straighten small sections of hair

Instead of trying to straighten large sections of hair at once, divide your hair into small sections and work on each one individually. This will help you get a smoother finish and reduce the risk of frizz.

4. Use a cool shot

After straightening each section of hair, use the cool shot button on your hair dryer to cool down the hair and set the style. This will help to seal the cuticle and reduce frizz.

5. Finish with a lightweight oil

Once you’ve finished straightening your hair, apply a lightweight hair oil or serum to add shine and reduce frizz. Be careful not to use too much, as this can make your hair look greasy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you keep your hair from frizzing after straightening:

Step 1: Wash and condition your hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help to hydrate your hair and reduce frizz.

Step 2: Apply a heat protectant

Before you start straightening your hair, apply a heat protectant spray or serum to protect your hair from damage.

Step 3: Divide your hair into small sections

Divide your hair into small sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to straighten each section individually.

Step 4: Straighten each section of hair

Starting with the bottom sections of your hair, use a high-quality straightener to straighten each section of hair. Work from the roots to the ends, making sure to keep the straightener moving smoothly and evenly.

Step 5: Use a cool shot

After straightening each section of hair, use the cool shot button on your hair dryer to cool down the hair and set the style. This will help to seal the cuticle and reduce frizz.

Step 6: Apply a lightweight oil or serum

Once you’ve finished straightening your hair, apply a lightweight hair oil or serum to add shine and reduce frizz. Be careful not to use too much, as this can make your hair look greasy.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you keep your hair from frizzing after straightening:

  • Avoid touching your hair too much after straightening, as this can cause frizz.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can open the cuticle and cause frizz.
  • Consider using a silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz while you sleep.

FAQs

How often should I straighten my hair?

You should avoid straightening your hair every day, as this can cause damage and breakage. Instead, try to limit your straightening to once or twice a week.

Can I straighten my hair if it’s wet?

No, you should never straighten your hair if it’s wet. Wet hair is more prone to damage and breakage, and the heat from the straightener can cause steam to form, which can further damage your hair.

What should I do if my hair starts to frizz after straightening?

If your hair starts to frizz after straightening, try using a frizz-fighting hair spray or serum. You can also try running a dryer sheet over your hair to help smooth it down.

Hair Care for Straightened Hair

If you’re straightening your hair regularly, it’s important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips for caring for straightened hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and hot rollers, as these can cause damage and breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals like bleach or hair dye, as these can weaken your hair.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your hair looking smooth, shiny, and frizz-free after straightening. Remember to use a heat protectant, work in small sections, and finish with a lightweight oil or serum for best results.
