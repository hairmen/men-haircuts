Best Swimwear For Over 60S Uk A Guide For Relaxed Swimming

Welcome to our guide on the best swimwear for over 60s in the UK. As we age, we may face challenges when it comes to finding the right swimwear that meets our needs, but don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will provide you with a comprehensive guide on how to choose the best swimwear that is comfortable, stylish and fits your body type. Let's dive in!

Why is it Important to Choose the Right Swimwear?

Choosing the right swimwear is crucial as it can impact your confidence and comfort level while swimming. As we age, our bodies change, and we may have different needs when it comes to swimwear. Older adults may have specific concerns such as coverage, support, and ease of movement. The right swimwear can provide the necessary support, coverage, and comfort, making swimming an enjoyable and relaxing experience.

How to Choose the Best Swimwear for Over 60s?

Choosing the best swimwear for over 60s can be overwhelming, but with a few tips, you can make an informed decision. Here are some things to consider when choosing swimwear:

Body Type

Choose swimwear that flatters your body type. If you have a fuller figure, look for swimwear with tummy control and support for your bust. If you have a slimmer figure, you can opt for swimsuits with cutouts and bold prints to add some curves. Dark colors can also help create a slimming effect.

Comfort

Comfort is key when it comes to swimwear. Look for swimwear with soft and stretchy fabric that won't irritate your skin. Choose designs that allow ease of movement and have adjustable straps for a customized fit.

Coverage

If you prefer more coverage, look for swimsuits with higher necklines, longer hemlines, and sleeves. Swim dresses are also a great option for those who want more coverage. If you want to show some skin, opt for swimsuits with cutouts or mesh panels.

Sun Protection

Protecting your skin from the sun's harmful rays is crucial, especially for older adults. Look for swimwear with UPF protection or wear a rash guard over your swimsuit. Don't forget to wear a wide-brimmed hat and apply sunscreen with at least SPF 30.

Best Swimwear for Over 60s UK - Our Top Picks

Now that you know what to look for when choosing swimwear let's dive into our top picks:

1. Tankinis

Tankinis are a great option for those who want more coverage but still want the convenience of a two-piece swimsuit. They come in various styles and designs, and you can mix and match tops and bottoms to create your own look. Look for tankinis with tummy control and underwire support for your bust.

2. Swim Dresses

If you prefer a more modest look, swim dresses are a great option. They provide full coverage and come in various lengths and styles. Look for swim dresses with built-in bras and tummy control for added support.

3. One-Piece Swimsuits

One-piece swimsuits are a classic option that never goes out of style. They provide full coverage and support and are available in various designs, from simple to bold prints. Look for one-piece swimsuits with adjustable straps and built-in bras for added support.

4. Rash Guards

Rash guards are a great option for those who want to protect their skin from the sun's harmful rays. They come in various designs and provide UPF protection. You can wear them over your swimsuit or as a standalone top.

Tips for Maintaining Your Swimwear

Now that you've found your perfect swimwear, here are some tips to help you maintain it:

1. Rinse Your Swimwear

After swimming, rinse your swimwear with fresh water to remove chlorine or saltwater. This will help prevent fading and damage to the fabric.

2. Hand Wash Your Swimwear

Hand wash your swimwear with mild detergent and cold water. Avoid using hot water or a washing machine as they can damage the fabric.

3. Dry Your Swimwear Properly

Avoid wringing or twisting your swimwear. Instead, lay it flat to dry in a shaded area. Avoid using a dryer or hanging it in direct sunlight as it can cause fading and damage to the fabric.

4. Store Your Swimwear Properly

Store your swimwear in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Avoid folding or stacking your swimwear to prevent creases and damage to the fabric.

FAQs

1. Can I Wear a Bikini if I'm Over 60?

Yes, you can wear a bikini if you feel comfortable and confident in it. However, if you prefer more coverage, you can opt for tankinis or one-piece swimsuits.

2. Can I Wear a Swim Dress if I'm Petite?

Yes, you can wear a swim dress if you're petite. Look for swim dresses with a shorter hemline and avoid ones that are too long as they can make you look shorter.

Hair Care for Swimming

Swimming can be damaging to your hair, but with proper care, you can minimize the damage. Here are some tips:

1. Wet Your Hair Before Swimming

Wet your hair with fresh water before swimming. This will help prevent your hair from absorbing chlorinated water, which can cause damage.

2. Wear a Swim Cap

Wear a swim cap to protect your hair from chlorine and saltwater. Look for silicone or latex swim caps as they are more durable and comfortable.

3. Rinse Your Hair After Swimming

Rinse your hair with fresh water after swimming to remove chlorine or saltwater. This will help prevent damage to your hair.

We hope you found our guide on the best swimwear for over 60s in the UK helpful. Remember, the right swimwear can make your swimming experience more enjoyable and relaxing. Choose one that meets your needs and preferences and don't forget to maintain it properly. Happy swimming!
