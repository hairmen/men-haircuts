How to Style Shoulder Length Hair with Curtain Bangs

The first step in styling shoulder length hair with curtain bangs is to ensure that your hair is clean and dry. You can blow dry your hair or let it air dry, depending on your preference. Once your hair is dry, you can follow the steps below:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by applying a heat protectant spray to your hair. This will protect your hair from damage caused by heat styling tools. You can also apply some styling mousse to add volume to your hair.

Step 2: Create the Parting

To create the curtain bangs, you need to create a middle parting. Use a comb to create a straight line down the middle of your head. Then, comb your hair on either side of your head and let it fall naturally to create the curtain effect.

Step 3: Curl Your Hair

Using a curling iron, curl your hair in small sections. Start from the bottom and work your way up. Make sure to curl your hair away from your face to create a natural-looking curl. Once youâve curled all your hair, comb your fingers through your curls to loosen them up.

Step 4: Style Your Bangs

To style your curtain bangs, you can use a straightening iron to straighten them out or use a curling iron to curl them under. You can also leave them natural if you prefer. Use a hairspray to hold your bangs in place.

  2. Create a middle parting with a comb.
  3. Curl your hair in small sections, starting from the bottom.
  4. Style your curtain bangs with a straightening or curling iron.
  5. Finish with a hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips for Styling

Here are some tips to help you style your shoulder length hair with curtain bangs:

  • Apply styling mousse to add volume to your hair.
  • Use a curling iron to curl your hair away from your face.
  • Use a straightening iron to straighten out your curtain bangs.
