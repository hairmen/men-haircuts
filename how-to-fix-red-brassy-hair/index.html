How To Fix Red Brassy Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Nov 07, 2023 · 4 min read

Red brassy hair is a common problem that many people face. It is especially frustrating when you dye your hair to achieve a certain color, but it turns out brassy instead. Fortunately, there are ways to fix red brassy hair without having to go to a salon. In this article, we will guide you on how to fix red brassy hair in the comfort of your own home.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Fix Red Brassy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Red Brassy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Fix Red Brassy Hair

Before we dive into the solution, it’s important to understand what causes red brassy hair. Red hair dye tends to fade quickly, leaving behind a brassy color. This happens because the dye molecules are too big to penetrate the hair shaft, so they sit on top of the hair. Additionally, exposure to sunlight, chlorine, and hard water can cause red hair to turn brassy.

The best way to fix red brassy hair is by using a toner. A toner is a semi-permanent hair color that neutralizes brassy tones. It can be applied to both natural and dyed hair. Toners are available in various shades, so choose one that matches the color of your hair. If you’re unsure which toner to use, consult a hair color specialist.

Choosing the Right Toner

When selecting a toner, consider the color you want to achieve. If your hair is light red, choose a toner with a violet or blue base. If your hair is dark red, opt for a toner with a green or blue base. Keep in mind that toners do not lighten hair, so choose a toner that is darker than your hair color.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to fix red brassy hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup.
  2. Towel-dry your hair.
  3. Apply the toner to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  4. Leave the toner on your hair for the recommended time (usually 10-30 minutes).
  5. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water.
  6. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and leave it on for 5-10 minutes.
  7. Rinse your hair with cool water.
  8. Style your hair as usual.

Tips to Prevent Red Brassy Hair

Here are some tips to prevent red brassy hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid exposure to sunlight, chlorine, and hard water.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

FAQs

Can I fix red brassy hair without using a toner?

No, toner is the best way to fix red brassy hair. It neutralizes the brassy tones and restores the color of your hair.

Can I use a toner on natural red hair?

Yes, toner can be used on both natural and dyed hair. Choose a toner that matches the color of your hair.

How often should I use a toner?

Toners last for 4-6 weeks, so you can use them once a month to maintain the color of your hair.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to keep your hair healthy and vibrant:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair.
  • Avoid using hair ties with metal clasps.

And that’s it! Follow these steps to fix red brassy hair and keep your hair looking healthy and vibrant.
