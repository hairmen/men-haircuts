How To Make Thick Hair Look Less Puffy Tips And Tricks

How to Make Thick Hair Look Less Puffy - Tips and Tricks

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can look voluminous and full of life, it can also look puffy and difficult to manage. If you're struggling with puffy hair, don't worry! There are plenty of ways to make your hair look less puffy and more manageable. In this article, we'll discuss some tips and tricks for taming your thick hair and making it look its best.

Description

Thick hair is characterized by its density and volume. While this can be a great thing, it can also make your hair look puffy and unmanageable. Puffy hair is often caused by frizz, dryness, and lack of moisture. By following some simple tips and tricks, you can reduce the puffiness and make your hair more manageable.

How to Make Thick Hair Look Less Puffy

1. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner

Thick hair needs moisture to stay healthy and manageable. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are specifically designed for thick, coarse hair. These products will help to hydrate your hair and reduce frizz, making it less puffy and more manageable.

2. Don't over-wash your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to frizz. Try to wash your hair no more than every other day, and use dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb

When brushing out your hair, use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush. This will help to detangle your hair without causing breakage or frizz. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, gently combing through any knots or tangles.

4. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it look even more puffy. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, and if you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

5. Use a leave-in conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to hydrate your hair and reduce frizz. Look for a product that is specifically designed for thick, coarse hair, and apply it to your hair after washing and conditioning. This will help to keep your hair hydrated and reduce puffiness.

6. Blow dry your hair upside down

Blow drying your hair upside down can help to add volume and reduce puffiness. Start by flipping your head upside down and blow drying your hair in sections, using a round brush to add volume and shape. Once your hair is dry, flip your head back up and style as desired.

7. Get regular haircuts

Getting regular haircuts can help to keep your hair healthy and reduce puffiness. Ask your stylist to thin out your hair and add layers to help reduce bulk and add shape.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to make your thick hair look less puffy:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Gently towel dry your hair, being careful not to rub it too vigorously.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends and mid-lengths.
  4. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  5. Blow dry your hair upside down, using a round brush to add volume and shape.
  6. Once your hair is dry, flip your head back up and style as desired.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for reducing puffiness in your thick hair:

  • Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel, as this can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze excess water out of your hair.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as these can weigh down your hair and make it look even more puffy.
  • Consider getting a keratin treatment to help smooth and tame your hair.

FAQ

Q: Why is my thick hair so puffy?

A: Puffy hair is often caused by frizz, dryness, and lack of moisture. Thick hair is more prone to these issues, as it is more difficult for natural oils to travel down the hair shaft and hydrate the hair.

Q: Can I use oil on my thick hair to reduce puffiness?

A: Yes, oil can be a great way to hydrate your hair and reduce frizz. Look for lightweight oils like argan oil or jojoba oil, and apply a small amount to your hair after washing and conditioning.

Q: How often should I get my hair cut to reduce puffiness?

A: It's a good idea to get your hair cut every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and reduce puffiness. Talk to your stylist about the best haircut for your hair type and texture.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is key to reducing puffiness and keeping it looking healthy and manageable. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, like bleach or peroxide.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals, which can help to strengthen your hair.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using tight hair elastics or hair accessories that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage and frizz.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make your thick hair look less puffy and more manageable. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and don't be afraid to experiment with different products and styling techniques until you find what works best for you.
