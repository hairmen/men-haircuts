How To Cut Men s Hair Short On Sides A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of paying for haircuts every few weeks? Why not try cutting your own hair at home? With the right tools and techniques, you can create a stylish and professional-looking haircut without ever leaving your house. In this article, we'll show you how to cut men's hair short on the sides, including everything from the tools you'll need to the step-by-step process.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Blending Your Hair
  • Cleaning Up

  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few essential tools:

  • Scissors
  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Water spray bottle
  • Mirror

Once you've gathered your tools, you're ready to start!

Prepping Your Hair

The first step in cutting your hair is to wash it thoroughly. This will remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup that may interfere with the cutting process. Once your hair is clean, towel-dry it and comb it out.

Next, decide how short you want your hair to be on the sides. Use your clippers to set the guard to the desired length. For a shorter cut, use a smaller guard. For a longer cut, use a larger guard.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is essential for a clean and even cut. Use your comb to divide your hair into sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then dividing it into four sections: two on top and two on the sides.

Clip the top sections of your hair out of the way using hair clips. This will allow you to focus on cutting the sides.

Cutting Your Hair

Start by trimming the hair around your ears. Use your clippers to create a clean, even line. Work your way up the side of your head, using the same technique to trim the hair to the desired length.

Repeat the same process on the other side of your head.

Blending Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting the sides, it's time to blend your hair. Use your clippers without a guard to blend the hair between the top and the sides. Start at the top and work your way down, using a sweeping motion to create a smooth transition between the two sections.

Cleaning Up

Once you're done cutting and blending your hair, it's time to clean up. Use your clippers to trim any stray hairs around your ears and the back of your neck. Use your comb and scissors to make any final adjustments.

Helpful Tips

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard, if needed.
  • Take breaks as needed to avoid fatigue and mistakes.
  • Use your fingers to check for any uneven spots.
  • When using your clippers, make sure the blades are sharp and oiled.

FAQs

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the length and shape.

Can I cut my own hair if I have curly hair?

Cutting curly hair can be more challenging, but it's possible. It's important to cut curly hair when it's dry to avoid any shrinkage. Use a smaller guard and cut the hair in small sections.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's essential to take good care of it. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

With these tips and techniques, you can cut men's hair short on the sides like a pro. With a little practice, you'll be able to create a stylish and professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home.
