How To Make A Low Fade Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a classic yet modern haircut, a low fade might be the perfect choice. This style is versatile, easy to maintain, and suitable for all hair types. In this article, we'll show you how to make a low fade haircut in simple steps.

Description

A low fade haircut is a hairstyle that gradually fades from longer hair on top to short hair on the sides and back of the head. The hair is tapered, creating a smooth transition from the longer hair on top to the shorter hair at the bottom. This style is popular among men of all ages and can be customized to suit your preferences.

How to Make a Low Fade Haircut

Before you start, you'll need the following tools:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Cape or towel
  • Clipper guards

Now, let's get started:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a shampoo and conditioner. Dry your hair completely before you start cutting.

Step 2: Determine the Length of the Fade

Decide how long you want the hair on top to be and choose the appropriate clipper guard. The shorter the guard, the shorter the hair will be on the sides and back.

Step 3: Cut the Hair on the Sides and Back

Start by using the clippers without a guard to create a natural hairline. Then, use the selected guard to cut the hair on the sides and back, moving the clippers upwards from the bottom of the hairline. Be sure to blend the hair evenly for a smooth transition.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Switch to a longer guard and blend the hair between the shorter and longer parts. Use the clippers in an upward motion to create a gradual fade.

Step 5: Cut the Hair on Top

Use scissors to cut the hair on top to the desired length. You can use a comb to guide the scissors and create a clean, even cut.

Step 6: Blend the Hair on Top

Blend the hair on top with the shorter hair on the sides and back. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that stick out.

Step 7: Check for Any Unevenness

Take a few steps back and look at your hair in the mirror. Check for any unevenness or spots that need to be fixed.

Step 8: Clean Up

Use a brush or vacuum to clean up any loose hair. Remove the cape or towel from your shoulders and shake it out.

Tips

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a comb to help you blend the hair evenly.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting hair can be tiring, and you don't want to rush and make mistakes.

FAQ

How often should I get a low fade haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the fade grows out. As a general rule, you should get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain the style.

Can I make a low fade haircut at home?

Yes, you can. However, if you're new to cutting hair, we recommend going to a professional to avoid any mishaps.

Hair Care

To keep your low fade looking fresh, wash your hair regularly and use a conditioner to keep it soft and manageable. Avoid using too much styling product, as it can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Now that you know how to make a low fade haircut, why not give it a try? With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a stylish and timeless look that suits your personality.
