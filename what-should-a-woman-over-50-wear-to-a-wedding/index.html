What Should A Woman Over 50 Wear To A Wedding

Written by Christine Nov 21, 2023 · 4 min read

As a woman over 50, attending a wedding can be a fun and exciting event. However, it can also be a bit stressful trying to figure out what to wear. You want to look stylish and appropriate for the occasion, while still feeling comfortable and confident. In this article, we’ll provide some tips and suggestions on what to wear to a wedding, so you can enjoy the celebration without any wardrobe worries.

Description

Weddings are a time to celebrate love and commitment, and as a guest, it’s important to dress appropriately for the occasion. However, with so many different styles, colors, and dress codes to consider, it can be challenging to know where to start. This article will provide some guidance on what to wear to a wedding as a woman over 50, so you can look and feel your best.

How to

Step 1: Check the Dress Code

The first step in deciding what to wear to a wedding is to check the dress code. This will give you an idea of what type of attire is expected, and can help you narrow down your options. If the invitation specifies a dress code, be sure to follow it closely. If there is no dress code mentioned, take cues from the location and time of the wedding. A formal evening wedding will require a different dress than a casual daytime ceremony.

Step 2: Choose a Flattering Silhouette

When it comes to choosing a dress, focus on finding a silhouette that flatters your body type. A-line dresses, wrap dresses, and shift dresses are all great options for women over 50. Look for dresses that cinch at the waist to create an hourglass shape, or opt for flowy dresses that skim over any problem areas.

Step 3: Select the Right Fabric

The fabric of your dress can make a big difference in how comfortable and confident you feel. Look for fabrics that are lightweight and breathable, such as cotton or silk. Avoid heavy fabrics like velvet or wool, which can be uncomfortable in warm weather.

Step 4: Accessorize Wisely

Accessories can make or break an outfit, so choose them wisely. A statement necklace or earrings can add some sparkle to a simple dress, while a clutch or small bag can hold all of your essentials without overwhelming your look. Don’t forget to choose comfortable shoes that you can dance in all night long.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing what to wear to a wedding:

  • Stick to classic, timeless styles that will never go out of fashion.
  • Avoid anything too revealing or tight-fitting.
  • Consider wearing a jacket or shawl, especially if the wedding is outdoors or in a chilly venue.
  • Choose colors that complement your skin tone and hair color.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask the bride or groom for advice if you’re unsure about what to wear.

FAQ

Here are some common questions that women over 50 may have when it comes to dressing for a wedding:

What colors should I avoid?

Avoid wearing white, which is traditionally reserved for the bride. You may also want to avoid black, as it can be seen as too somber for a wedding. However, if you choose to wear black, you can add some color with accessories like a bright scarf or jewelry.

Can I wear pants to a wedding?

Yes, pants can be a great option for a wedding, especially if you choose a dressy pair made from a luxurious fabric like silk or satin. Pair them with a dressy blouse and heels to create a chic, sophisticated look.

What should I do with my hair?

Your hairstyle will depend on the dress code and formality of the wedding. If it’s a formal evening wedding, consider an updo or sleek blowout. For a more casual daytime wedding, you could opt for loose waves or a simple ponytail. Don’t forget to add some hairspray or other styling products to keep your hair in place all day and night.

Hair Care

When preparing for a wedding, it’s important to take care of your hair so it looks healthy and shiny. Here are some tips to keep your hair looking its best:

  • Wash your hair a day or two before the wedding to give it some time to settle.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment to nourish your hair and add shine.
  • Avoid using too many styling products, which can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Get a trim a few weeks before the wedding to get rid of any split ends.

With these tips and suggestions, you should now have a better idea of what to wear to a wedding as a woman over 50. Remember to choose a flattering silhouette, select the right fabric, and accessorize wisely to create a look that’s both stylish and appropriate for the occasion.
