How To Cut Men s Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Nov 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending money at the barber or hair salon every few weeks? Learning how to cut men's hair yourself can save you both time and money. Plus, it's a useful skill to have in case of emergencies or unexpected situations. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut men's hair at home, from choosing the right tools to finishing touches.

  2. Prepping the Hair
  3. Cutting the Hair

Tools You'll Need

Before you begin, you'll need to gather the necessary tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clipper guards
  • Cape or towel
  • Hand mirror
  • Regular mirror
  • Spray bottle
  • Haircutting spray or oil

Make sure your clippers are fully charged or plugged in and that your scissors are sharp.

Prepping the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair. This will remove any dirt or product buildup and make it easier to cut. Comb the hair in the direction it naturally falls, and use the clipper guards to choose the desired length. If you're not sure what length to use, start with the longest guard and work your way down.

Cutting the Hair

Begin at the sides and work your way up to the top. Use the clippers with the chosen guard to trim the hair, making sure to go against the grain for a closer cut. Use the comb to lift the hair and the clippers to trim any stray hairs. Use the scissors to trim any longer hairs or to create texture.

Fading the Hair

If you want to create a fade, switch to a shorter guard and begin at the bottom of the hairline. Slowly work your way up, using different guards to blend the hair until it reaches the desired length at the top. Use the scissor-over-comb technique to blend any harsh lines.

Detailing the Hair

Use the scissors to trim any longer hairs around the ears and neckline. Use the clippers to create a clean line at the sideburns and nape. Use the spray bottle and haircutting spray or oil to keep the hair damp and easier to work with.

Trimming the Beard

If the person you're cutting has a beard, use the clippers and scissors to trim it to the desired length. Use the comb and scissors to trim any stray hairs and create a clean line.

Cleaning Up

Once you're finished cutting, use a hand mirror and the regular mirror to check for any missed spots. Use the clippers and scissors to clean up any stray hairs. Remove the cape or towel and shake it out to remove any loose hair. Sweep or vacuum the area to clean up any remaining hair.

Tips for Success

  • Start with longer guards and work your way down.
  • Take your time and use a light touch.
  • Use the mirrors to check your work from different angles.
  • Practice on a willing friend or family member before attempting a more complicated cut.
  • Invest in quality tools to ensure a better cut.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What if I mess up?

    • If you make a mistake, don't panic. Use the scissors to create texture or blend in any harsh lines. If necessary, you can always go shorter or shave the head completely.

  • How often should I cut my hair?

    • This depends on your personal preference and hair type. Most men get a haircut every 4-6 weeks, but you can adjust this based on how quickly your hair grows.

  • What if I can't reach the back of my head?

    • Use the hand mirror to check the back of your head, or ask someone to assist you.

Hair Care After the Cut

After you've finished cutting the hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair clean and moisturized. Avoid using hot tools or harsh products that can damage the hair. Schedule regular trims to maintain the desired length and style.

Learning how to cut men's hair yourself takes practice, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done successfully. Use this guide as a starting point, and don't be afraid to experiment and try new things. Who knows, you may even discover a hidden talent for hair cutting!
