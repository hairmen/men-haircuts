How To Cut The Back Of A Mullet A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to rock a retro hairstyle, a mullet might just be the perfect option for you! The classic cut has made a comeback in recent years and is a popular choice among those looking for a bold and edgy look. However, getting the perfect mullet requires precision and skill, especially when it comes to cutting the back. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps of how to cut the back of a mullet, from start to finish.

Description

A mullet is a hairstyle in which the hair is cut short on the top and sides, while the back is left long. It's often associated with the 1980s and is considered a retro look. However, the mullet has made a comeback in recent years and is now a popular choice among those looking for an edgy and bold hairstyle.

How to Cut the Back of a Mullet

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. Once you have everything you need, follow these steps:

Step 1: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair. Take a comb and create a center part from the crown to the nape of the neck. Then, divide the hair into two sections, left and right.

Step 2: Cut the Bottom Layer

Next, take the bottom layer of the left section and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across, about two inches from the bottom. Repeat this process on the right section.

Step 3: Cut the Middle Layer

Take the middle layer of the left section and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at a slight angle, following the line of the bottom layer. Repeat this process on the right section.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, take the top layer of the left section and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at a sharper angle, following the line of the middle layer. Repeat this process on the right section.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all three layers on both sides, use your scissors to blend the layers together. This will create a seamless transition from the short top and sides to the longer back.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting the back of a mullet:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed
  • Use a mirror to check your progress as you go
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're unsure
  • Take breaks if your arms get tired or if you need to step back and assess your work

FAQ

How long should the back of a mullet be?

The length of the back of a mullet can vary depending on personal preference. However, it's typically longer than the top and sides, ranging from shoulder-length to mid-back.

Can I cut the back of a mullet myself?

While it's possible to cut the back of a mullet yourself, it can be challenging, especially if you're not experienced with cutting hair. It's always best to seek professional help if you're unsure.

Can I style my mullet in different ways?

Absolutely! A mullet is a versatile hairstyle that can be styled in many different ways. Experiment with different products, such as pomade or hairspray, to create your desired look.

Hair Care

Maintaining a mullet requires regular trims to keep the back at the desired length. It's also important to keep the hair healthy by using a nourishing shampoo and conditioner, as well as heat protectant products if you use hot tools. In addition, avoid over-styling with too much product or excessive heat, as this can damage the hair.

Congratulations, you now know how to cut the back of a mullet! Follow these steps, and you'll be rocking a retro look in no time.
